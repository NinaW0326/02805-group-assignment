<p><b>Godelot</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">dark wizard</a> who once owned the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">Elder Wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Described as an "<i>unpleasant character</i>", Godelot considered the Elder Wand to be an instructor, and used his knowledge of the wand's powers to write <i><a href="/wiki/Magick_Moste_Evile" title="Magick Moste Evile">Magick Moste Evile</a></i>, a book that contained information on <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">dark magic</a>. Despite his affinity towards dark magic, he refused to discuss <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>, deeming them and the ritual involved too dark even for him. Godelot perished in his own cellar, after he was locked in by his son, <a href="/wiki/Hereward" title="Hereward">Hereward</a>, who did this to gain ownership of the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">Elder Wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
