<p>The <b>Bloody Baron</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/11th_century" title="11th century">11th century</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_founders" title="Hogwarts founders">Founders</a>' time and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>.
</p><p>After Hogwarts, he fell in <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> with <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a>, but she did not love him in return. He was sent by <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw" title="Rowena Ravenclaw">Rowena Ravenclaw</a> to find Helena in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, but Helena refused to return with him and so he killed her in a rage. When he realised what he had done, the Baron stabbed himself with the same knife and died, committing suicide.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>, he is one of the ghosts that haunts <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, and the ghost of Slytherin House. Covered in Helena Ravenclaw's blood to this day, he earned himself the moniker of "Bloody Baron".<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>The baron was always a hot-tempered man.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a> relays the Baron's personality prior to his death<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>The Baron was born into nobility, no earlier than <a href="/wiki/First_millennium_A.D." title="First millennium A.D.">982</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-birthdate_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthdate-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the age of eleven, the Baron became part of one of the first generations of students at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, which, at that time, was still being run by its original Founders. He was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a> and was educated in the magical arts by <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a> for the seven years that followed.<sup id="cite_ref-House_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-House-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sometime around this time the Baron met <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw" title="Rowena Ravenclaw">Rowena Ravenclaw</a>, whom he fell in love with, though she always rejected his advances.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>He tracked me to the forest where I was hiding. When I refused to return with him, he became violent. The baron was always a hot-tempered man. Furious at my refusal, jealous of my freedom, he stabbed me... When he saw what he had done, he was overcome with remorse. He took the weapon that had claimed my life, and used it to kill himself. All these centuries later, he wears his chains as an act of penitence... as he should.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Helena tells <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> the story of her and the Baron's death<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Later on in his life, Helena, after stealing a <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem" class="mw-redirect" title="Rowena Ravenclaw&#39;s Diadem">diadem</a> belonging to her mother that increased the intelligence of the wearer, ran away to <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>. Around this same time, <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw" title="Rowena Ravenclaw">Rowena</a> fell fatally ill,<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> although legend has it that it was a broken heart,<sup id="cite_ref-WotM_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WotM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> possibly caused by her daughter abandoning her. Rowena ordered the Baron to go after her, which he did, eventually finding her hiding in a forest. After he had found her, the Baron tried to compel her to return with him, but she refused, and, in a fit of rage, he killed her. Overcome with grief, he proceeded to take his own life with the same weapon he used to stab Helena.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-mortem">Post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>Harry looked over at the Slytherin table and saw a horrible ghost sitting there, with blank staring eyes, a gaunt face, and robes stained with silver blood.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description of the Baron<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 242px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/99/Ghosts_of_Hogwarts.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160608191632" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/99/Ghosts_of_Hogwarts.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/240?cb=20160608191632" decoding="async" width="240" height="149" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Ghosts of Hogwarts.jpg" data-image-key="Ghosts_of_Hogwarts.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Ghosts_of_Hogwarts.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">The Bloody Baron in his blood-stained clothes standing next to the other House <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">Ghosts</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>The Baron, as well as Helena, now known as the <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Grey Lady</a>, both returned from the dead as ghosts, and returned to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>, where they became the house ghosts of their former Houses, <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Baron has been remorseful of his actions ever since, and carries heavy chains as a sign of penance.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Nobody else seems to know why he is covered in blood, not even the other two <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghosts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> For unspecified reasons, presumably related to his apparently violent, dangerous nature, the Baron has gained <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a>'s respect and is the only one, besides Professor Dumbledore, who can give him orders.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>

<p>As a ghost, the Baron was very pale,<sup id="cite_ref-PS10_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> pearly-white and slightly transparent.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He had wide, staring,<sup id="cite_ref-GF21_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> black eyes<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and a gaunt face.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He dressed in robes covered in silver bloodstains,<sup id="cite_ref-GF12_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF12-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and carried chains in a final act of penitence for having murdered <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the films, he sported a curly powdered wig, fashions closer to the 17th century than the 10th, and carried his sword.<sup id="cite_ref-PSf13_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSf13-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He spoke in a hoarse whisper.<sup id="cite_ref-PS16_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS16-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The most notable change was that the bloodstains were made red instead of silver, likely so that it would be visually noticeable and immediately identifiable (rather than having to differentiate silver on a pearly-white ghost).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix"><b>Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</b>: "<i>Well, now, you shouldn't take that attitude. Peaceful cooperation, that's the key. We ghosts, though we belong to separate houses, maintain links of friendship. In spite of the competitiveness between Gryffindor and Slytherin, I would never dream of seeking an argument with the Bloody Baron.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Ronald Weasley</b>: "<i>Only because you're terrified of him.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; An exchange between <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p>During his lifetime, the Baron was described by <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a> as a "hot-tempered man". He insisted on having his way in interactions, responding with violent uncontrolled anger when contradicted.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> As a ghost, he was frightening and most people did not seem to enjoy his presence,<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> not even his fellow <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghosts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>However, he seemed to have a cordial, if not friendly, relationship with the ghost of <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Sir Nicholas de Mimsy Porpington</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> despite <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a>'s allegations of the latter being "terrified" of him.<sup id="cite_ref-OP11_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP11-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The Baron seemed to be intolerant towards <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a>, who was scared of him,<sup id="cite_ref-PS16_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS16-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and could not bear his lack of civilisation, putting "his foot down" over the <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a>'s potential presence at the <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> <a href="/wiki/Start-of-Term_Feast" title="Start-of-Term Feast">Start-of-Term Feast</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF12_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF12-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He also seems to have a great deal of House pride; before <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s arrival at Hogwarts, <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> had won the House Cup for six years in a row, and the Baron had become "<i>unbearable</i>" according to <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li>In the Welsh translation, the Baron's name is given as Waldo.</li>
<li>The Bloody Baron is the only Hogwarts house ghost in the books without any on-'screen' dialogue.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, the Bloody Baron is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Terence_Bayler" title="Terence Bayler">Terence Bayler</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">films</a>, the Baron is depicted as being quite mirthful, in stark contrast to the solemn and quite terrifying character in the books. In the video games, however, the Baron resumes his "book" persona.</li>
<li>In all of his media appearances, the Bloody Baron appears in late seventeenth century attire. This wardrobe was chosen for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, when the Baron's death date was uncertain. This, of course, is not consistent with <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, in which he is revealed to have died in the eleventh century.
<ul><li>His epithet, the Bloody Baron, is also an anachronism, as feudalism was not introduced in the British Isles until after the Norman conquest in 1066, and therefore there were no barons until this time (unless he died shortly after the conquest). This is probably explained by it, along with Helena Ravenclaw's title of 'the Grey Lady', and the Fat Friar, being a nickname he acquired later on.</li></ul></li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, the Bloody Baron resembles Captain James Hook (as played by Dustin Hoffman) in the film <i>Hook</i>. Coincidentally, that film features <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Smith" title="Maggie Smith">Maggie Smith</a>, who plays <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a>, as well as a music score by <a href="/wiki/John_Williams" title="John Williams">John Williams</a>.</li>
<li>The Baron is encountered in the PC/Mac version of <i>Philosopher's Stone</i> walking the labyrinthine dungeon corridors where <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Professor Snape</a> sends <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> to find four crucial <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potion</a> ingredients. In the next generation version of Chamber of Secrets, the Baron can be seen walking the path from the dungeons through the entrance hall to the teacher's lounge corridor, every night, the chilling sound of his footsteps echoing through the hall each time as he approaches.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (video game)">video game version</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, it is mentioned he cannot speak.</li>
<li>In the credits of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, his name is misspelt as "Bloody Barron".</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (video game)">PS2 version of the video game adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, there is a large statue of the Bloody Baron on the <a href="/wiki/North_Wing" title="North Wing">North Wing</a>.</li></ul>
