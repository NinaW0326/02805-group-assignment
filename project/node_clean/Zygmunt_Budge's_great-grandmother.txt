<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Zygmunt_Budge" title="Zygmunt Budge">Zygmunt Budge</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Book_of_Potions" title="Book of Potions">Book of Potions</a></i><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Wonderbook:_Book_of_Potions" title="Wonderbook: Book of Potions">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>This <b><a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a></b> (fl. c. <a href="/wiki/16th_century" title="16th century">1500s</a>) was the great-grandmother of <a href="/wiki/Zygmunt_Budge" title="Zygmunt Budge">Zygmunt Budge</a>, one of the most accomplished <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">Potioneers</a> ever known. She was remembered by her great-grandson as a "<i>distressingly giggly</i>" persona, in sharp contrast to his own personality.<sup id="cite_ref-BSlaughingpotion_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BSlaughingpotion-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Budge's great-grandmother had an old recipe for the <a href="/wiki/Laughing_Potion" title="Laughing Potion">Laughing Potion</a> that she handed down to him as a child. In his adult years in reclusion in the <a href="/wiki/Hermetray" title="Hermetray">Isle of Hermetray</a>, Budge set himself the challenge of refining that recipe. He later used it on a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> neighbour from the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Harris" title="Isle of Harris">Isle of Harris</a> who thought himself funny and was determined to make fun of him â€” a drink from Budge's potion made him laugh for a week straight, after which he retired to a silent monastery, having developed an aversion for all things funny.<sup id="cite_ref-BSlaughingpotion_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BSlaughingpotion-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
