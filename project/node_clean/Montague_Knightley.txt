<b>Montague Knightley</b> (<a href="/wiki/1506" class="mw-redirect" title="1506">1506</a>-<a href="/wiki/1588" class="mw-redirect" title="1588">1588</a>) was a professional <a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_Chess" title="Wizard&#39;s Chess">Wizard's Chess</a> player. During his career, he eventually became the Wizard's Chess Champion. For his skill at Wizard's Chess, he became immortalised on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Montague_Knightley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Montague_Knightley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Montague" is from a surname meaning "pointed mountain" in French. "Knightly" is presumably a nod to the Knight chess piece.
</p>
