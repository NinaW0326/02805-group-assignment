<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tracey_Davis?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tracey_Davis?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Tracey" is from an English surname which was taken from a Norman French place name meaning "domain belonging to <i>Thracius</i>". It was used by Charles Dickens for a masculine character in his novel <i>The Pickwick Papers</i> (1837). It was later popularised as a feminine name by the main character Tracy Lord in the movie <i>The Philadelphia Story</i> (1940).
</p>
