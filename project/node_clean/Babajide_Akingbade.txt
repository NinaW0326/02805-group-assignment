<b>Babajide Akingbade</b> was an <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uagadou_School_of_Magic" title="Uagadou School of Magic">Uagadou</a> graduate who succeeded <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Mugwump" title="Supreme Mugwump">Supreme Mugwump</a> of the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards" title="International Confederation of Wizards">International Confederation of Wizards</a><sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, when the latter was <a href="/wiki/Campaign_to_discredit_Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter" title="Campaign to discredit Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter">voted out</a> by <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry</a> wizards after he made a speech announcing <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">return</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP5_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Babajide_Akingbade?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Babajide_Akingbade?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Babajide came from a Yoruba word "baba" meaning "father" and "dziade" meaning "raise from the dead." Babajide means "father has come to life again" or "father has returned."
</p>
