This <b>individual</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who, in <a href="/wiki/1544" class="mw-redirect" title="1544">1544</a>, held the post of <a href="/wiki/Chief_Warlock" title="Chief Warlock">Chief Warlock</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WonderbookGameDeubt_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WonderbookGameDeubt-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/16_July" title="16 July">16 July</a>, <a href="/wiki/1544" class="mw-redirect" title="1544">1544</a>, the Chief Warlock was invited by <a href="/wiki/Jarleth_Hobart" title="Jarleth Hobart">Jarleth Hobart</a> to witness his "maiden flight" — a public demonstration of his own revolutionary <a href="/wiki/Levitation_Charm" title="Levitation Charm">Levitation Charm</a> on himself. Hobart climbed onto the roof of his local church and, after several speeches and a rousing performance of the national anthem he leapt and, having cast the spell, was left hovering in mid-air. At first, he seemed to have succeeded<sup id="cite_ref-WonderbookGameDeubt_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WonderbookGameDeubt-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> but, after having spent nearly three minutes watching him hanging in mid-air, the crowd grew impatient to see him move somewhere, and booed him.
</p><p>In response to the catcalls, Hobart tried to move in midair, and started performing vigorous swimming movements, which produced no effect. Mistakingly believing that his clothes were making him heavier and impeding his movement, Hobart stripped thus making him fall ten feet onto the ground below, as it were the clothes that were holding him up in the air — <i>they</i> had been charmed by the Levitation Charm, not Hobart himself. He fell completely naked on the ground, breaking sixteen bones, and went on the receive a fine for "outrageous silliness" from the Chief Warlock. 
</p><p>Hobart returned home, humiliated, where he realised that his spell could make objects levitate for varying lengths of time, depending on the skill of the spellcaster and on the weight of the object. He also concluded that small animals or even children could be levitated, but that they had no control whatsoever of their movement once airborne.
</p><p>After realising that the spell was best used on inanimate objects (and small animals, but they do not have any control of their movement once airborne), Hobart made a second announcement, and an even larger crowd gathered to see his second demonstration of the spell (hoping for another laugh at his expense). Hobart's demonstration was, at first, by far more successful than the first one: he showed the onlookers how he could easily levitate objects ranging from small rocks to fallen trees. Hobart decided that, for a finale, we would levitate the Chief Warlock's hat — what he managed to levitate, however, was the Chief's wig, exposing his bald head to the gathered crowd. The Chief was not amused, and was determined to <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duel</a> Hobart, but the warlock levitated the Chief's robes over his head, and ran for it.
</p>
