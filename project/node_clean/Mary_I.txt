<b>Mary I</b> (<a href="/wiki/18_February" title="18 February">18 February</a>, <a href="/wiki/1516" class="mw-redirect" title="1516">1516</a> – <a href="/wiki/17_November" title="17 November">17 November</a>, <a href="/wiki/1558" class="mw-redirect" title="1558">1558</a>), also known by her sobriquet of "<b>Bloody Mary</b>", was <a href="/wiki/Queen" title="Queen">Queen</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> from <a href="/wiki/July" title="July">July</a> <a href="/wiki/1553" class="mw-redirect" title="1553">1553</a> until her <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> on <a href="/wiki/17_November" title="17 November">17 November</a>, <a href="/wiki/1558" class="mw-redirect" title="1558">1558</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Queen Mary was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and had a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> of her own. There was a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> of her hung at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MHP_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MHP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was a daughter of the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> king <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII" title="Henry VIII">Henry VIII</a> and his first wife  Catherine of Aragon and thus a half-sister to her successor, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I" title="Elizabeth I">Elizabeth I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_I?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_I?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Mary" is the usual English form of Maria, which was the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> form of the New Testament Greek names Μαριαμ (Mariam) and Μαρια (Maria) - the spellings are interchangeable - which were from the Hebrew name מִריָם (Miryam). The meaning is not known for certain, but there are several theories including "sea of bitterness", "rebelliousness", and "wished for child". However it was most likely originally an <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> name, perhaps derived in part from mry "beloved" or mr "<a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a>".
</p>
