<p>By <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, she had a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">Portrait</a> hung in <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAF_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1995-1996">1995-1996</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1995-1996">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1995-1996"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, she guarded a shortcut, leading from the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Staircase" title="Grand Staircase">Grand Staircase</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Clock_Tower_Entrance" title="Clock Tower Entrance">Clock Tower</a>, she was seen frequently complaining about her hunger or tiredness. By then, her password was "<i>Chops and Gravy</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-OotP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OotP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1996-1997">1996-1997</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1996-1997">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1996-1997"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">next school year</a>, Damara's portrait was moved to another place in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Staircase" title="Grand Staircase">Grand Staircase</a>. Despite this move, she still guarded a passage to the <a href="/wiki/Clock_Tower" title="Clock Tower">Clock Tower</a>, inaccessible to all, who had not got the new password: "<i>Dashing <a href="/wiki/Cadogan" title="Cadogan">Cadogan</a></i>".<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997-1998">1997-1998</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1997-1998">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1997-1998"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> ensued. Damara appeared to have fled from her portrait.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">"<i>Ohhhh, I'm too hungry. If you could ask Giffard Abbott — he's the portrait at the bottom of the Grand Staircase — to send some food along, then maybe I could help. Nothing much, just some chops, and a leg of lamb, some chicken and beef and maybe some cakes as well. Not too much. I'm so hungry.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Damara Dodderidge.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Damara was known being always very hungry and tired. Many times she would ask fellow portrait Giffard Abbott for food.<sup id="cite_ref-OotP_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OotP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her portrait at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> depicts her like so, but by <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> she seemed to stop constantly daydreaming about food and complaining about her tiredness. She said to be hungry once at breakfast time, though. Also by this time, she seemed to have developed a crush on fellow portrait <a href="/wiki/Cadogan" title="Cadogan">Sir Cadogan</a>, calling him "<i>dashing</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Damara is the Celtic goddess of fertility.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Damara_Dodderidge?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Damara is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Atkins" title="Rachel Atkins">Rachel Atkins</a> in the fifth video game while in the sixth game she is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Beth_Chalmers" title="Beth Chalmers">Beth Chalmers</a>.</li>
<li>Damara Dodderidge may have died sometime prior to <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, as many portraits hung at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts</a> depict deceased individuals.</li>
<li>It is possible that Damara is related to <a href="/wiki/Daisy_Dodderidge" title="Daisy Dodderidge">Daisy Dodderidge</a>, the famed founder and first landlady of <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">The Leaky Cauldron</a>.</li>
<li>Considering that she is named for the Celtic goddess of fertility, it may be that Damara Dodderidge's hunger and tiredness simply resulted from being <a href="/wiki/Pregnancy" title="Pregnancy">pregnant</a>.</li>
<li>Damara's portrait was based on a <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2f/Robert_Peake_-_Lady_Elizabeth_Pope_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg">c. 1615 portrait of Lady Elizabeth Pope</a>, by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Peake_the_elder" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Robert Peake the elder">Robert Peake the Elder (c. 1551–1619)</a>.</li></ul>
