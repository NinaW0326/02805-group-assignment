<b>Anthony Otterburn</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> student at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. He liked <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a>, and could often be found in the second <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_greenhouses" title="Hogwarts greenhouses">Herbology greenhouse</a>, as being around the plants made him feel calm. In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> he told <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> that he could use <i><a href="/wiki/Summoning_Charm" title="Summoning Charm">Accio</a></i> to collect the flower heads that he needed to make the <a href="/wiki/Draught_of_Living_Death" title="Draught of Living Death">Draught of Living Death</a> for <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a>.
</p><p>Later in the year he joined the <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a> run by <a href="/wiki/Maynard_Hatton" title="Maynard Hatton">Maynard Hatton</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Training_Grounds" title="Training Grounds">Training Grounds</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Anthony_Otterburn?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Anthony_Otterburn?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Anthony" is from the English form of the Roman family name Antonius, which is of unknown Etruscan origin. The most notable member of the Roman family was the general Marcus Antonius (called Mark Antony in English), who for a period in the 1st century BC ruled the Roman Empire jointly with Augustus. When their relationship turned sour, he and his mistress Cleopatra were attacked and forced to commit suicide, as related in Shakespeare's tragedy 'Antony and Cleopatra' (1606).
</p>
