<p><b>God</b> is the omniscient, omnipotent, omnipresent and omnibenevolent <a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">being</a> as defined by certain theistic and deistic religions (and other belief systems) who is most often conceived of as the supernatural creator and overseer of the universe. In many <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> societies, God is usually a central figure in their religions.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>So that's little <a href="/wiki/Scorpius_Malfoy" title="Scorpius Malfoy">Scorpius</a>. Make sure you beat him in every test, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Granger-Weasley" title="Rose Granger-Weasley">Rosie</a>. Thank God you inherited your <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">mother</a>'s brains.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> to his <a href="/wiki/Rose_Granger-Weasley" title="Rose Granger-Weasley">daughter</a> upon seeing Scorpius in <a href="/wiki/2017" title="2017">2017</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards, witches</a> and <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> often use terms such as <i>thank God</i> and <i>oh my God</i> to express excitement, shock, awe, dismay, or supplication; analogous to the wizard-used <a href="/wiki/Merlin%27s_beard" title="Merlin&#39;s beard">Merlin's beard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DH5_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DHE_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While many religions share the same basic conception of God, they may differ over the ideas of the dictates passed down by him and how one may best serve him. This can sometimes lead to conflicts, and the creation of organisations such as <a href="/wiki/Al-Hiraza" title="Al-Hiraza">Al-Hiraza</a>, which engage in actions designated as terrorism.<sup id="cite_ref-HBPF_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBPF-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
