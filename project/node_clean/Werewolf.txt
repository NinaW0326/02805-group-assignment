<dd>&#8212;Werewolf <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> regarding his affliction<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>A <b>werewolf</b>, also known as a <b>lycanthrope</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> <a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">being</a> who, upon the complete rising of the <a href="/wiki/Wolf_Moon" title="Wolf Moon">full moon</a>, becomes an uncontrollable, fearsome and deadly wolf. This condition is caused by infection with <a href="/wiki/Lycanthropy" title="Lycanthropy">lycanthropy</a>, also known as werewolfry.<sup id="cite_ref-PAf_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAf-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Werewolves appear in the form of a <a href="/wiki/Wolf" title="Wolf">wolf</a> but, there are distinctions between them and regular wolves.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Mixture_of_powdered_silver_and_dittany" title="Mixture of powdered silver and dittany">mixture</a> of powdered <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dittany" title="Dittany">dittany</a> applied to a fresh bite will seal the wound and allow the victim to live on as a werewolf, although tragic tales are told of knowing victims begging for <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> rather than becoming werewolves.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Wolfsbane_Potion" title="Wolfsbane Potion">Wolfsbane Potion</a>, invented by <a href="/wiki/Damocles_Belby" title="Damocles Belby">Damocles</a>, allows the werewolf to keep their human mind during transformation.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A werewolf cannot choose whether or not to transform and will no longer remember who they are and would kill even their best friend given the opportunity once transformed.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, they are able to recall everything they have experienced throughout their transformation upon reverting to their human form.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>To become a werewolf, it is necessary to be bitten by a werewolf in their wolfish form at the time of the full moon. When the werewolf’s saliva mingles with the victim’s blood, contamination will occur.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;How a wizard becomes a werewolf<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 174px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fa/FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160609022923" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="172" height="228" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="FenrirGreyback werewolf.jpg" data-image-key="FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fa/FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/172?cb=20160609022923" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fa/FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160609022923" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fa/FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/172?cb=20160609022923" decoding="async" width="172" height="228" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="FenrirGreyback werewolf.jpg" data-image-key="FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fa/FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/172?cb=20160609022923" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:FenrirGreyback_werewolf.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Savage werewolf Greyback in wolf form</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lycanthropy" title="Lycanthropy">Lycanthropy</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> illness known to be spread by contact between saliva and <a href="/wiki/Blood" title="Blood">blood</a>; thus, when a transformed werewolf bites a human, the bitten will become a werewolf themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Most <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>, however, will die from the extent of their injuries in the instance of a werewolf attack as noted by <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Marlowe_Forfang" title="Marlowe Forfang">Marlowe Forfang</a>, though some do survive to become werewolves themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> If a werewolf is in <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> form and bites the victim, they will merely gain lupine tendencies such as a fondness for rare meat.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Any bite or scratch obtained from a werewolf, whether in human or animal form, will leave permanent scars.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, the fresh wound can be sealed with a mixture of powdered silver and dittany.
</p><p>The only known human born to at least one werewolf parent (untransformed at time of conception) was <a href="/wiki/Edward_Lupin" title="Edward Lupin">Teddy Lupin</a>, son of werewolf <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus</a> and human metamorphmagus <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>. Teddy did not inherit his father's condition, however it is unknown if it definitively cannot be passed on in this manner or if Teddy did not inherit the condition from pure chance as other than Teddy, there was no documentation of a werewolf having a child in human form. In Teddy's case it was his father who was a werewolf, not his mother, therefore it is unknown if a pregnant female werewolf's transformations would affect the ability to carry the pregnancy to term.
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 169px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d5/FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160609023609" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="167" height="205" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="FenrirGreyback PM.jpg" data-image-key="FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d5/FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/167?cb=20160609023609" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d5/FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160609023609" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d5/FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/167?cb=20160609023609" decoding="async" width="167" height="205" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="FenrirGreyback PM.jpg" data-image-key="FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d5/FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/167?cb=20160609023609" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:FenrirGreyback_PM.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Greyback's appearance when in human form has a wolf like quality</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>If two werewolves mate at the full moon, in their animal forms, something very strange happens.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The result of their mating, which has only ever occurred twice throughout history, has been a pack of wolf cubs — actual <a href="/wiki/Wolf" title="Wolf">wolf</a> cubs — who grow to become very beautiful wolves and can only be distinguished from true wolves by their near-human intelligence.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Thus, rumours of werewolves living in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a> in the grounds at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> are actually about a <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest_wolf_pack" title="Forbidden Forest wolf pack">pack</a> of lupine werewolf offspring that was released into the woods with the kind permission of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">School</a>, and has lived there ever since.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Teachers have never tried to dispel these rumours because they felt that keeping students out of the forest was highly desirable.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatments">Treatments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Treatments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Treatments"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>The many Muggle myths and legends surrounding werewolves are, in the main, false, although some contain nuggets of truth. Silver bullets do not kill werewolves, but a mixture of powdered silver and dittany applied to a fresh bite will ‘seal’ the wound and prevent the victim bleeding to death (although tragic tales are told of victims who beg to be allowed to die rather than to live on as werewolves).</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Treatments that can be done in order to prevent death<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>Unfortunately, there is currently no cure for <a href="/wiki/Lycanthropy" title="Lycanthropy">lycanthropy</a>. However, some of the worst effects can be mitigated by consuming <a href="/wiki/Wolfsbane_Potion" title="Wolfsbane Potion">Wolfsbane Potion</a>, which allows a werewolf to retain his or her human mind while transformed, thus freeing him or her from the worry of harming other humans or themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is a very difficult potion to make, with many complicated ingredients.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>, it tastes disgusting but sugar makes it useless.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The high cost of the ingredients makes it virtually impossible for werewolves to brew the potion for themselves, as most are reduced to poverty and cannot taste the potion without revealing their statuses.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Because werewolves only pose a danger to humans, companionship with animals whilst transformed has been known to make the experience more bearable as the werewolf has no-one to harm and will be less willing to harm themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Homorphus_Charm" title="Homorphus Charm">Homorphus Charm</a> can force a werewolf back into human shape. However, due to Lockhart's reputation as a liar, and the many falsehoods he told to inflate his popularity, his information is highly suspect — as is the very existence of a Homorphus Charm in the first place. However, as many of Lockhart's claims are also based on the accounts of more trustworthy wizards (accomplishments he would claim for himself, following the disposal of the originating witch or wizard), there is a chance that the charm does, in fact, exist.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1980s <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Lee" title="Cecil Lee">Cecil Lee</a>, a wizard working for the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Werewolf_Capture_Unit" title="Werewolf Capture Unit">Werewolf Capture Unit</a>, told <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_Sibling" class="mw-redirect" title="Jacob&#39;s Sibling">Jacob's Sibling</a> that he had used the <a href="/wiki/Homorphus_Charm" title="Homorphus Charm">Homorphus Charm</a> during his job. However, for him, the charm "only" forced the werewolf to temporarily revert to human form rather than permanently curing them as Lockhart claimed. Though as Lee was a fan of Lockheart's, he believed that this was due to his own inability to perfectly cast the charm.<sup id="cite_ref-31oct_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31oct-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Contrary to what the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> world believes, werewolves are not affected by silver, except in that it can be used in the <a href="/wiki/Mixture_of_powdered_silver_and_dittany" title="Mixture of powdered silver and dittany">mixture of powdered silver and dittany</a> to prevent death and merely closes their wounds to prevent bleeding in a severe werewolf attack.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> There may certainly be several other ways and solutions to prevent and heal werewolf injuries, as <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> taught about the topic in first year Defence against the Dark Arts class. None of them, of course, could completely cure an afflicted person once they are bitten, but can merely prevent and close the physical wounds on the skin.
</p>

<p>The monthly transformation of a werewolf is extremely painful if untreated and is usually preceded and succeeded by a few days of pallor and ill health. The werewolf may display irritation towards friends.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While in his or her wolfish form, the werewolf loses entirely its human sense of right or wrong. However, it is incorrect to state (as some authorities have, notably <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Emerett_Picardy" title="Emerett Picardy">Emerett Picardy</a> in his book <i>Lupine Lawlessness: Why Lycanthropes Don’t Deserve to Live</i>) that they suffer from a permanent loss of moral sense.
</p><p>While human, the werewolf may be as good or kind as the next person. Alternatively, they may be dangerous even while human, as in the case of <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>, who attempts to bite and maim as a man and keeps his nails sharpened into claw-like points for the purpose. Though werewolves usually only infect their victims through biting, they sometimes take it too far and kill their victims.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Without any humans nearby to attack, or other animals to occupy it, the werewolf will attack itself out of frustration. This leaves many werewolves such as <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> with self-inflicted scars and premature ageing from the difficult transformations.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance_and_traits">Appearance and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Appearance and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Appearance and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Werewolves can be easily distinguished from regular wolves by their shorter snout, more human-like eyes, the tufted tail, and their mindless hunting of humans whilst in wolf form.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At all other times, they appear as normal humans, although they will age prematurely, and will gain a pallor as the moon approaches and then wanes.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The real difference between a wolf and a werewolf is in behaviour.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Genuine wolves are not very aggressive, and the vast number of folk tales representing them as mindless predators are now believed by wizarding authorities to refer to werewolves, not true wolves. A wolf is unlikely to attack a human except under exceptional circumstances. The werewolf, however, targets humans almost exclusively and poses very little danger to any other creature.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prejudice_and_discrimination">Prejudice and discrimination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Prejudice and discrimination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Prejudice and discrimination"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>The stigma surrounding werewolves has been so extreme for centuries that very few have married and had children.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The prejudice surrounding werewolves in the magical community<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 167px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/94/Dolores_Umbridge.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20101120104908" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="165" height="220" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Dolores Umbridge.jpg" data-image-key="Dolores_Umbridge.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/94/Dolores_Umbridge.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/165?cb=20101120104908" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/94/Dolores_Umbridge.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20101120104908" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/94/Dolores_Umbridge.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/165?cb=20101120104908" decoding="async" width="165" height="220" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Dolores Umbridge.jpg" data-image-key="Dolores_Umbridge.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/94/Dolores_Umbridge.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/165?cb=20101120104908" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Dolores_Umbridge.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, the drafter of the anti-werewolf legislation</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Werewolves are generally regarded with fear and disgust by <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding society</a>. People seem to think even when in human form, the werewolf may pose a danger. It is not uncommon for people known to be werewolves to be shunned by society and discriminated against within the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>. It is very difficult for a werewolf to get a job in the wizarding community, especially after the passing of restrictive <a href="/wiki/Anti-werewolf_legislation" title="Anti-werewolf legislation">anti-werewolf legislation</a> by the very prejudiced and hateful <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Umbridge drafted this legislation due to her irrational and vitriolic hatred for what she considered to be "<a href="/wiki/Half-breed" title="Half-breed">half-breeds</a>". Because of the difficulty in finding work in the wizarding world, many werewolves live in poverty. It also forced some, like <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> to take jobs far below their abilities. After <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s defeat in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, the anti-werewolf legislation was most likely repealed by the new Minister, <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>, in his effort to reform the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, therefore weeding out corruption and not tolerating prejudice and discrimination.
</p><p>As a result of the anti-werewolf legislation, many werewolves suffer poverty. Remus Lupin managed to get by with the aid of his friend <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and later by working as <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. Though in between he had to work at many jobs that are far below his level of abilities, resigning and moving on to another before his workmates notice his signs of lycanthropy. Lupin did this while living in a <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin%27s_cottage" title="Remus Lupin&#39;s cottage">tumbledown, semi-derelict cottage</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>. He decided to resign from this position after his condition was exposed, by Severus Snape, as most parents would not want their children being around a werewolf, despite the safety precautions Remus and <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> took; Remus stated that it would have been impossible for him to even attend Hogwarts as a child if it were not for Dumbledore's kindness, as other headmasters would not want a werewolf in the school.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a display of ignorance and arrogance, the Ministry expected werewolves would submit themselves to the department to sign various conducts and registries, which would also force the werewolves to promise to secure themselves from attacking others. No person would be prepared to walk into the Ministry to admit themselves as werewolves, thus showing the Ministry's lack of respect of werewolves' intelligence and dignity. <a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin" title="Lyall Lupin">Lyall Lupin</a>, in particular, regarded werewolves as "soulless, evil, deserving nothing but death", until his own son was infected as a result of his prejudicial comment.
</p><p>Given <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>'s friendship with Remus Lupin and the furthering of <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> and <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> rights after 1998, it is likely that the reforms of the Ministry under <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> Shacklebolt included less prejudicial treatment of werewolves.
</p>

<p>Due to the oppression and discrimination they face, some werewolves have come to hate the wizarding society, and as such have created their own society. Under Fenrir Greyback's leadership, this society works to infect as many people as possible, especially children, with the goal of one day having enough strength to take control of the wizarding community.
</p><p>The werewolves under Greyback's command served <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, believing that they would have a better life under his rule, though Remus Lupin spied on them for the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> looked down on them; for example, they were not permitted to have the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> They were used as a threat to ensure ordinary citizens' compliance with Voldemort; for example, five-year-old <a href="/wiki/Montgomery" title="Montgomery">Montgomery</a> was fatally attacked by Greyback after his mother refused to cooperate with the Death Eaters.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although only Greyback is explicitly mentioned as participating, werewolves under Greyback's command may have fought alongside the Death Eaters in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> They were most likely sentenced to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> for life for uniting with Lord Voldemort, or killed for resisting arrest. It is unknown whether this was the only occurrence of the society, though it is implied that it was a new idea of Greyback's. Likewise, it is unknown whether all werewolves in the society were caught, or — as it was "underground" — some of the members evaded capture and continued their efforts.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ministry_relations">Ministry relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Ministry relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ministry relations"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> attempts to regulate werewolves and thus the relationship that exists between them and the Ministry is a rocky one. According to <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newton Scamander</a>, werewolves have been shunted between the <a href="/wiki/Beast" title="Beast">Beast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">Being</a> divisions of the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Regulation_and_Control_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures">Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures</a> for years.
</p><p>At one point, the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf_Registry" title="Werewolf Registry">Werewolf Registry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Werewolf_Capture_Unit" title="Werewolf Capture Unit">Werewolf Capture Unit</a> were both in the <a href="/wiki/Beast_Division" title="Beast Division">Beast Division</a>, while at the same time the office for <a href="/wiki/Werewolf_Support_Services" title="Werewolf Support Services">Werewolf Support Services</a> was in the <a href="/wiki/Being_Division" title="Being Division">Being Division</a>. These regulations and services were ultimately a failure, as no one would be prepared to walk into the Ministry to admit themselves as werewolves, and thus none took the prescribed responsibilities of the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf_Code_of_Conduct" title="Werewolf Code of Conduct">Werewolf Code of Conduct</a>.
</p><p>The Werewolf Code of Conduct of 1637 was meant to give Werewolves a framework for coexisting safely and legally within the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>. Werewolves were required to sign a copy of the Code and promise to refrain from attacking and biting non-werewolves. They were also supposed to lock themselves away during their wolf transformation periods. As no werewolf was willing to sign it, the Ministry had huge difficulty in hunting down culprits of these attacks, such that <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> was able to act innocent in regards to him murdering two Muggle children.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Werewolves are classified as a XXXXX creature in their transformed state.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Dolores Umbridge herself incorrectly referred to werewolves as <a href="/wiki/Half-breed" title="Half-breed">half-breeds</a>, and has drafted an <a href="/wiki/Anti-werewolf_legislation" title="Anti-werewolf legislation">anti-werewolf legislation</a> that made it almost impossible for werewolves to find a job.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Even when the Ministry was taken over by the Death Eaters, relationship between the bureaucracy and werewolves remained strained. Death Eaters and their supremacist allies look down on werewolves, only using them as foot soldiers and to intimidate the rest of the wizarding world into submission. They did let the werewolves have more freedom of movement than before the takeover, but in no way showed they were equals; Greyback was not given the Dark Mark despite being permitted to wear the Death Eater's robe, and genuine Death Eaters looked down on him, while Greyback himself acknowledged that if he took <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> to the Ministry, he would be left out of any credits for capturing him.
It was not until after the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, in which <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a> took the position of Minster for Magic, did the Ministry's relationship with werewolves improve. Kingsley posthumously awarded his werewolf friend, <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merlin" title="Order of Merlin">Order of Merlin</a>, First Class, for his bravery in the war, the first time in history that a werewolf has been accorded this honour. The example of his life and death played an important role in lifting the stigma on werewolves among the wizarding society.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wizarding_education">Wizarding education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Wizarding education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Wizarding education"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wizarding children are educated about werewolves from a young age and information about them can be found in various <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> textbooks, for example <a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Forces:_A_Guide_to_Self-Protection" title="The Dark Forces: A Guide to Self-Protection">The Dark Forces: A Guide to Self-Protection</a>.
</p>

<p>Werewolves have been mentioned several times in connection with Harry Potter's <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professors</a>, as they are <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">dark</a> <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creatures</a> which are heavily studied throughout the class. <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> had encountered them in Black Forest, and at one point discussed in class how to treat werewolf bites.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>, supposedly, once defeated the <a href="/wiki/Wagga_Wagga_Werewolf" title="Wagga Wagga Werewolf">Wagga Wagga Werewolf</a>, something that may be discussed in his book <i><a href="/wiki/Wanderings_with_Werewolves" title="Wanderings with Werewolves">Wandering with Werewolves</a></i>. Lockhart eventually confessed to Ron and Harry that an 'ugly old Armenian warlock' had <i>actually</i> performed the rescue of a village from werewolves that he himself had taken credit for. Remus Lupin, of course, is a werewolf.
</p><p>Werewolves were discussed in a third year Defence Against the Dark Arts lesson, with an <a href="/wiki/Essay_on_werewolves" title="Essay on werewolves">essay</a> assigned by <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> when he once substituted for Lupin, although werewolves were not due to be covered until the last chapter of the third-year DADA textbook.<sup id="cite_ref-POA9_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA9-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Werewolf?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The word <i>werewolf</i> is from Old English <i>wer</i> ("man") and <i>wulf ("wolf")</i>.
</p><p>The term comes from Ancient Greek λυκάνθρωπος, λύκος being "wolf" and άνθρωπος translating to "human".
</p>

<ul><li>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/werewolf" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:werewolf">werewolf</a> is a creature found in the folklore of many European countries. Traditionally, a werewolf is a human who transforms into a wolf (as depicted in the <i>Harry Potter</i> books) but in some modern depictions the werewolf instead transforms into a man/wolf hybrid (as depicted in the <i>Harry Potter</i> films).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> had the first year <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> class copy notes about how to treat werewolf bites. Also, Professor <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> assigned an <a href="/wiki/Essay_on_werewolves" title="Essay on werewolves">essay</a> during the 1993-1994 school year when he substituted for Lupin, although werewolves were not due to be covered until the last chapter of the third-year DADA textbook. In Snape's case, this was an attempt to expose Lupin by having one of the students work out his secret (which <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> did, although she kept the secret rather than exposing it, as Snape had intended).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> once claimed that there were werewolves in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest_wolf_pack" title="Forbidden Forest wolf pack">wolf pack</a> actually lives in the forest, the cubs of two werewolves that mated during full moon, however they were just beautiful and highly intelligent wolves. Despite this, rumours of savage werewolves living in the forest spread amongst the student body of Hogwarts, rumours that the staff let spread in hopes to help keep students out of the forest.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During the detention in the Forbidden Forest, Harry Potter asked Rubeus Hagrid if it was possible that a werewolf could be killing the <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">unicorns</a> in the forest, but Hagrid stated that werewolves aren't fast enough.<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> once accused Rubeus Hagrid of raising "werewolf cubs" under his bed as a youngster. Since Riddle was trying to frame Hagrid, the veracity of his statement is questionable. Regarding this, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">Harry Potter</a></i> series author <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> stated in response to a fan question "Riddle was telling lies about Hagrid, just slandering him.".<sup id="cite_ref-barnesyahoo_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-barnesyahoo-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> referred to werewolf offspring as cubs another time. When at the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>, he ridicules <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">the Malfoys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix</a> about the marriage of Remus Lupin and <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, asking <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a> if he will "babysit the cubs." Given his purist attitude and disdain for 'half-breeds' this is far more likely to be a derogatory insult rather than an implication that lycanthropy is inheritable.</li>
<li>In the film <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a>, after Lupin is irritable with Harry, Mr Weasley says that "his condition takes its toll;" later on Tonks explains that "the first night of the cycle is always the worst." This could imply- though nothing like this was mentioned in the books- that werewolves suffer symptoms other than the transformation itself, which either directly or indirectly make them less patient and more harsh than they would normally be. What night the cycle begins with is unknown, though the full moon itself, the night after, or the new moon is most likely.
<ul><li>This could also be a side effect of the "illness" described during the week leading up to the full moon.</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Essential_Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="The Essential Defence Against the Dark Arts">A prop</a> made for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i> includes a large number of werewolf lore from the 1995 video game <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Beast_Within:_A_Gabriel_Knight_Mystery%7C">The Beast Within</a>, but as this text includes a number of ideas that contradict higher sources, it should not necessarily be considered <a href="/wiki/Canon" title="Canon">canon</a> to the Harry Potter universe.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The contradictory claims include:
<ul><li>It identifies several other ways to become a werewolf other than being bitten. These included being given <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">the power of shape shifting</a> via <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">sorcery</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lycacomia_Curse" title="Lycacomia Curse">Lycacomia Curse</a>, and being born to a werewolf.  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i>, however, clarifies that the only way to become a werewolf is via the bite of a werewolf at the full moon, and explicitly denies that inheriting the disease via birth is possible.</li>
<li>It states that werewolves can transform into their wolfish forms by a variety of means, including by will or when forced by various phases of the moon or hearing the howl of another werewolf. According to <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(companion_book)" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (companion book)">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i>, the full moon is the only thing that can transform a werewolf.</li>
<li>It claims that the <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a> of a werewolf is eternally damned if they have tasted human blood and cannot move on from the mortal plane upon <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>. However, Remus Lupin was successfully recalled from beyond the <a href="/wiki/Veil" title="Veil">Veil</a> using the <a href="/wiki/Resurrection_Stone" title="Resurrection Stone">Resurrection Stone</a>, and thus must have "passed on".</li></ul></li>
<li><span style="line-height:21px;">There is in the real world a very rare mental illness called lycanthropy, in which a patient believes he or she is an animal and behaves accordingly. This is sometimes referred to as clinical lycanthropy, to distinguish it from its meaning in folktales.</span></li>
<li>In the books it is mentioned that the werewolf resembles a normal wolf in appearance, save for a few distinguishing traits. This is not the case in the film, as Lupin in his werewolf form is shown as having a gaunt, humanoid, hairless appearance, with a coyote-like face and no tail.</li>
<li>Despite how the werewolf cannot be cured in Harry Potter books but in the real world, according to other myths it can be cured by medicine men and antidotes.</li></ul>
