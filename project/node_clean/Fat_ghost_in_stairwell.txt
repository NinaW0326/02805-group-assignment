<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fat_ghost_in_stairwell?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fat_ghost_in_stairwell?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This ghost's identity has been an ongoing matter of discussion for the Harry Potter community.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li>He may be <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</a> as he wears a ruff and looks like he was of aristocratic status before his death.</li>
<li>He may also looks similar to the <a href="/wiki/Fat_Friar" title="Fat Friar">Fat Friar</a>, being round and jolly, but the Fat Friar wears a monk's robe, and is a monk rather than a lord.</li>
<li>Given his appearance, he may be the <a href="/wiki/Portly_ghost" title="Portly ghost">portly ghost</a> that attended <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington%27s_five-hundredth_Deathday_Party" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington&#39;s five-hundredth Deathday Party">Sir Nick's Death Day Party</a>  on <a href="/wiki/31_October" title="31 October">31 October</a> <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.</li>
<li>He may be another unspecified ghost from the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">series</a> or simply invented by the illustrator <a href="/wiki/Giles_Greenfield" title="Giles Greenfield">Giles Greenfield</a> for decorative purposes.</li></ul></li>
<li>In earlier sketches done by illustrator for this scene, there was originally no ghost present<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>, but he was added subsequently in more finished sketches, although his depiction trended towards even more portly.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
