<p><b>Madam Poppy Pomfrey</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and the matron at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She was a very competent <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a>, having been in the position for, at least, the last quarter of the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.
</p><p>Madam Pomfrey was very possessive of the patients in her care while in the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a>, and their visitors upset her somewhat, as she frequently insisted they must rest and demanded the visitors leave. She was proficient in her area of expertise (<a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">healing magic</a>) and able to take care of some ailments "<i>in a heartbeat</i>". She fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, the final battle of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, during which she personally dispatched at least one Death Eater in a duel, and was seen after the battle treating the wounded and attending to the dead.
</p><p>Not much is known about Madam Pomfrey's life after the final battle. By <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a>, she had retired or was thinking of retiring, since <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Abbott" title="Hannah Abbott">Hannah Abbott</a> reportedly applied for the position of Matron at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-PM-QWC_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-QWC-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Regardless, either because she did not retire or because she was pulled out of retirement, Madam Pomfrey was working at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> in <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Act2Scene8_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Act2Scene8-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Matron_at_Hogwarts">Matron at Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Matron at Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Matron at Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Madam Pomfrey began her career at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> sometime before <a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a>, being trusted in that year by <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> to escort new <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> student <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>, who was infected with <a href="/wiki/Lycanthropy" title="Lycanthropy">lycanthropy</a> due to a bite from <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Whomping_Willow" title="Whomping Willow">Whomping Willow</a> every full moon.<sup id="cite_ref-POA18_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA18-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point before <a href="/wiki/1_November" title="1 November">1 November</a>, <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, Madam Pomfrey complimented Dumbledore on a set of <a href="/wiki/Earmuffs" title="Earmuffs">earmuffs</a> he had recently acquired.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The compliment made him blush in such a manner that would only be rivalled when <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> compared his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> prowess favourably against those of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="1985–1986_school_year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1985.E2.80.931986_school_year"><a href="/wiki/1985%E2%80%931986_school_year" title="1985–1986 school year">1985–1986 school year</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1985–1986 school year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1985–1986 school year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In addition to treating <a href="/wiki/Ben_Copper" title="Ben Copper">Ben Copper</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Khanna" title="Rowan Khanna">Rowan Khanna</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>, and <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>, all of whom at various times throughout the year was injured by the <a href="/wiki/Cursed_ice" title="Cursed ice">cursed ice</a> spreading throughout the castle, an assortment of injuries caused by <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duelling</a>, classroom accidents and general clumsiness kept Poppy very busy, including a student who was <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">transfigured</a> into a teapot.
</p><p>The same year, she enlisted <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> to be her assistant, impressed with their bedside manner when their friend Ben was injured. One of the things she tasked said assistant with was researching sleepwalking, and teaching them to <a href="/wiki/Episkey" title="Episkey">fix relatively minor injuries in a heartbeat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="1987–1988_school_year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1987.E2.80.931988_school_year"><a href="/wiki/1987%E2%80%931988_school_year" title="1987–1988 school year">1987–1988 school year</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1987–1988 school year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1987–1988 school year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In addition to caring for students stricken by the <a href="/wiki/Sleepwalking_curse" title="Sleepwalking curse">sleepwalking curse</a>, she took time to teach <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> the <a href="/wiki/Bandaging_Charm" title="Bandaging Charm">Bandaging Charm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Poppy Pomfrey dedicated much of her time treating two students who had been <a href="/wiki/Petrification" title="Petrification">petrified</a> as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Statue_curse" title="Statue curse">statue curse</a> as well as treating other students. She had help from a few students, including <a href="/wiki/Chiara_Lobosca" title="Chiara Lobosca">Chiara Lobosca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>. At some point during the school year, Madam Pomfrey was petrified.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Eventually, she was cured and continued to be the matron at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> was required to go to Madam Pomfrey after visiting <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> and being bitten by the <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragon</a> <a href="/wiki/Norberta" title="Norberta">Norberta</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> told her that he had been bitten by a dog, but Madam Pomfrey had doubts about his honesty due to the unusual colour of the purple, green, and black wound. <p>In 1992, Madam Pomfrey administered <a href="/wiki/Skele-Gro" title="Skele-Gro">Skele-Gro</a> on Harry to regrow the bones in his arm after <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Brackium_Emendo" title="Brackium Emendo">attempt</a> to fix a Quidditch-caused broken bone failed disastrously. Pomfrey was annoyed that Harry had not been brought straight to her in the first place, as re-growing bones is a much more difficult task than mending bones, which she claimed she could do "in a heartbeat".<sup id="cite_ref-cos10_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cos10-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the same year, Hermione Granger ended up in the hospital wing for a month after an accident in an unauthorised high-level <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potion-making</a> (<a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a>) left her half-feline; apparently the group felt comfortable letting Madam Pomfrey to treat her since she would not ask too many questions and just get on with the job in hand. Hermione was allowed visitors, but it bothered Madam Pomfrey somewhat.<sup id="cite_ref-COS13_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS13-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Hogwarts was also facing potential closure as a <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">monster</a> had been attacking Muggle-born students around the school and <a href="/wiki/Full_Body-Bind_Curse" title="Full Body-Bind Curse">Petrifying</a> them as a result. It was Madam Pomfrey's job to make sure that these students were found and treated to the best of her ability, though she could not cure students without <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Pomona Sprout</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mandrake_Restorative_Draught" title="Mandrake Restorative Draught">Mandrake Restorative Draught</a>, which took weeks to brew. She refused to allow any students to visit the victims, in fear of the attacker returning to finish them, but allowed Harry and Ron to visit Hermione with <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a>'s permission.<sup id="cite_ref-COS16_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS16-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Eventually, they managed to get the potion made and cured the victims - Hermione Granger, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Creevey" title="Colin Creevey">Colin Creevey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penelope_Clearwater" title="Penelope Clearwater">Penelope Clearwater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Finch-Fletchley" title="Justin Finch-Fletchley">Justin Finch-Fletchley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nearly Headless Nick</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a>. At the end of the year, Madam Pomfrey and Professor Sprout sat at the staff table and Albus Dumbledore asked the whole school to thank them both for their efforts in helping all petrified students, as well as applauding in Lockhart's departure and Hagrid's return.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In 1993, Hogwarts found itself under the questionable protection of the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> of <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>, horrible creatures whose mere presence was enough to induce the worst feelings and sensations in people. When one took it upon itself to inspect the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> for the escaped criminal <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, it caused many students great distress and actually caused Harry Potter to pass out due to recalling his mother's death. Madam Pomfrey was summoned to Minerva McGonagall's office to see if Harry was alright and administer treatment. While inspecting the boy, she exchanged a significant look with Professor McGonagall and expressed her disapproval of setting Dementors around the school, anticipating many more collapses all over the place due to the effect they had on people, particularly those who were 'delicate'. Madam Pomfrey recommended some <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolate</a>, but was surprised and very pleased to hear that he had already been given some by the new Defence Against the Dark Arts professor, <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA5_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA5-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_vs_Hufflepuff_Quidditch_match_(1993)" title="Gryffindor vs Hufflepuff Quidditch match (1993)">1993 Quidditch match</a> between <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a>, Madam Pomfrey tended to the injured, including Harry Potter, who fell over fifty feet due to Dementors being on the pitch, a Hufflepuff team member, and <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a> who was injured after the tail of her broomstick caught <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA9_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA9-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of 1994, Harry, Ron, and Hermione all had endured injuries following the events that took place when Sirius Black returned to Hogwarts and revealed <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> to be alive. The three all woke up in the hospital wing, Ron having broken his leg, and both Harry and Hermione having passed out from the Dementor attack, along with Sirius Black. Madam Pomfrey was not surprised to have the three in her care again.<sup id="cite_ref-POA21_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA21-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Whilst in the Hospital Wing, Hermione, at Dumbledore's hint, used her <a href="/wiki/Time-Turner" title="Time-Turner">Time-Turner</a> to go back in time with Harry to three hours previously. They rescued Sirius Black on <a href="/wiki/Buckbeak" title="Buckbeak">Buckbeak</a> the hippogriff to prevent him from being administered the <a href="/wiki/Dementor%27s_Kiss" title="Dementor&#39;s Kiss">Dementor's Kiss</a>. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a>, Madam Pomfrey had retired or was thinking of retiring, since <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Abbott" title="Hannah Abbott">Hannah Abbott</a> reportedly applied for the position of Matron at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-PM-QWC_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-QWC-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2019" title="2019">2019</a>, she assisted prospective members of the then recently established <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> seeking a career as <a href="/wiki/Magizoologist" title="Magizoologist">Magizoologists</a>, whom she taught a rudimentary variation of the <a href="/wiki/Mending_Charm" title="Mending Charm">Mending Charm</a>, hoping it would help them in the field. Eventually, she would later teach the same members some advanced concepts relating to this spell, allowing them to improve its effectiveness.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> She also instructed them how to refine the <a href="/wiki/Reviving_Spell" title="Reviving Spell">Reviving Charm</a> they learned from <a href="/wiki/Rosmerta" title="Rosmerta">Rosmerta</a> and upon being helped in tending to a student with a painful Quidditch injury, gave them practical advice over pain management to be applied to increase resilience. Regardless, either because she decided not to leave, or because she ended her retirement, she was working at Hogwarts in <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>.
</p><p>In an <a href="/wiki/Alternate_reality" title="Alternate reality">Alternate reality</a> created when <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a> travelled back in time to <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> and broke his arm in the process, Madame Pomfrey placed him in an induced sleep for twenty-four hours so that the arm could be reset. Unsure of what else to prescribe, she recommended eating lots of <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Act2Scene8_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Act2Scene8-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Madam Pomfrey was described by her students as being exceptionally kind, but even more so strict.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She was very obsessive, lining up her patients' sweet boxes in neat rows, and was intensely worried about all of the students under her care, believing even feasts to be risky.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Working in a school where magical accidents were almost an undeniable certainty, she appeared to have resigned herself to treating whoever came her way. Indeed, she never asked questions about how her patients received their ailments or questioning the sometimes ludicrous stories they told her, but instead immediately set to fixing them up.<sup id="cite_ref-PS14_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS14-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She had a tendency to burst into tears at moments where great sorrow was appropriate, but she was capable of controlling herself if need be.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP29_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP29-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> She also has her standards and loyalty, as she would have resigned in protest when <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> was shot down by four Stunning Spells from a cowardly Ministry attack, were she not worried for the students.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP31_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP31-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">Healing magic</a></b>: Being the Hogwarts Matron, Madam Pomfrey was a talented <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a> who was very well-versed in medical magic. She had a very high understanding of <a href="/wiki/Healing_spell" title="Healing spell">medical charms</a> and could mend broken bones in seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-cos10_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cos10-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a></b>: She was proficient in transfiguration, being able to quickly and <a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">wordlessly</a> <a href="/wiki/Conjuration" title="Conjuration">conjure</a> a <a href="/wiki/Chair-conjuring_spell" title="Chair-conjuring spell">chair</a> for <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> to sit on after hearing of the death of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP29_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP29-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Pomfrey was able to produce a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>, a feat that was noted to be extremely difficult given how advanced and powerful the said protective charm was.<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She was also a skilled enough <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellist</a> to survive the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, defeating <a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey%27s_Death_Eater_Opponent" title="Poppy Pomfrey&#39;s Death Eater Opponent">at least one witch</a> and surviving the onslaught of many more.<sup id="cite_ref-DHF2_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF2-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> She also clearly recognised a <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementor</a> attack and knew immediately to prescribe <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolate</a> as the relevant <a href="/wiki/Antidote" title="Antidote">antidote</a>, expressing grim approval of <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Professor Lupin</a> when she learned that he had already treated the student.<sup id="cite_ref-POA5_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA5-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: Pomfrey was very knowledgeable of healing potions and was skilled in their application.<sup id="cite_ref-cos10_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cos10-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GOF20_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF20-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She could also successfully brew healing potions such as <a href="/wiki/Pepperup_Potion" title="Pepperup Potion">Pepperup Potion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skele-Gro" title="Skele-Gro">Skele-Gro</a><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> and her own tonic <a href="/wiki/Pomfrey%27s_Pick-Me-Up" title="Pomfrey&#39;s Pick-Me-Up">Pomfrey's Pick-Me-Up</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-TCG_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TCG-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> showing her to have been proficient in the art of potion-making.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Poppy juice, better known as opium, is one of the oldest known medicines; it has been used as a painkiller and soporific for, at the very least, thousands of years. King Tutankhamun's tomb contained jars of poppy juice, presumably to relieve the pain he suffered from a head injury. Although it is mostly known today as an addictive drug, opium's derivatives, such as morphine and codeine, are vital to modern medicine for their anaesthetic properties.</li>
<li>Pomfrey sounds like <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/comfrey" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:comfrey">comfrey</a>, a plant in the borage family which can be made into a soothing salve. Meanings of her names are, therefore, both in some way medically related. Pomfrey or Pomfret cakes (named after mediaeval names for the Yorkshire town of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontefract" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Pontefract">Pontefract</a>, once a centre of liquorice cultivation) are small, sweet lozenges made from the roots of the licorice plant. Liquorice also has been a medicinal ingredient for hundreds of years.</li></ul>

<ul><li>Pomfrey is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> actress <a href="/wiki/Gemma_Jones" title="Gemma Jones">Gemma Jones</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Half-Blood Prince</a>,</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a>, Madam Pomfrey wears a cloth in her hair similar to caps worn by British Nurses at various points in history. Her uniform costume as seen in the second film is very similar and could be based on Victorian and Edwardian nurse's uniforms. Her uniform that we see in the 6th and 8th films is very similar and could be based on very early NHS nurse's uniforms from the 1940's and 50's.</li>
<li>In the German audio books, Madam Pomfrey speaks with a French accent.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Trio" title="Trio">Trio</a> seems to place a lot of faith in Madam Pomfrey, to the point of overly doing so. When Dumbledore's hand was <a href="/wiki/Curse_on_Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_ring" title="Curse on Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s ring">cursed</a> and the damage remained visible, when Katie was <a href="/wiki/Opal_Necklace" title="Opal Necklace">cursed</a>, and when Dumbledore was greatly weakened by the <a href="/wiki/Emerald_Potion" title="Emerald Potion">Emerald Potion</a>, Harry always expected that Pomfrey would be able to cure them, and even suggesting in those situations that she be summoned for assistance, instead of the more Dark Arts-experienced Snape, who was more proficient in staunching curses.</li></ul>
