<b>Timothy Blenkinsop</b> (born c. <a href="/wiki/1976" title="1976">1976</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> fan who supported <a href="/wiki/Puddlemere_United" title="Puddlemere United">Puddlemere United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wizardsb_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was twenty-three when he attended a match between Puddlemere United and the <a href="/wiki/Holyhead_Harpies" title="Holyhead Harpies">Holyhead Harpies</a> on <a href="/wiki/30_September" title="30 September">30 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1999" title="1999">1999</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wizardsb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> These two teams had long been rivals, and, anticipating the possibility of crowd violence, the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> confiscated <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> at the gates.<sup id="cite_ref-dp_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, many attendees turned over <a href="/wiki/Fake_wand" title="Fake wand">fake wands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dp_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Thus, when <a href="/wiki/Wilda_Griffiths" title="Wilda Griffiths">Wilda Griffiths</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a> for Puddlemere United who transferred from the Harpies earlier that year, disappeared mid-match, a riot broke out, and people were able to use their real wands.<sup id="cite_ref-dp_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Timothy got caught in the crossfire, and was <a href="/wiki/Tail-growing_hex" title="Tail-growing hex">left with a tail</a> because of it, presumably due to a <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spell</a> that hit him.<sup id="cite_ref-wizardsb_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Timothy_Blenkinsop?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Timothy_Blenkinsop?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Timothy" is from the Greek name Τιμοθεος (<i>Timotheos</i>) meaning "honouring God", derived from τιμαω (<i>timao</i>) "to honour" and θεος (<i>theos</i>) "god". As an English name, Timothy was not used until after the Protestant Reformation.
</p>
