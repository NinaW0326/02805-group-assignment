<b>Thomas Riddle</b> (<a href="/wiki/1880" class="mw-redirect" title="1880">1880</a>–<a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a>) was a very wealthy <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> who owned most of the town of <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a>. He and his wife <a href="/wiki/Mary_Riddle" title="Mary Riddle">Mary</a> had a son named <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle_Senior" title="Tom Riddle Senior">Tom</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Riddle_family" title="Riddle family">Riddles</a> were snobby and looked down on the poverty-stricken <a href="/wiki/House_of_Gaunt" title="House of Gaunt">House of Gaunt</a> who lived nearby. Although the Gaunts were also unpopular, frowned upon due to their squalour and ill manners by most of the village, the Riddle looked down on most of the families in the village as well, becoming very unpopular amongst the locals.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was murdered along with his wife and son by his own grandson, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Marvolo Riddle</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thomas was born in 1880. At some point he married Mary, who would give birth to their son, Tom, in 1905.
</p>
<h3><span id="Son's_marriage"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Son.27s_marriage">Son's marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Son&#039;s marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Son&#039;s marriage"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The elderly Riddle couple suffered a great scandal when Tom mysteriously eloped and <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_Tom_Riddle_Senior_and_Merope_Gaunt" title="Wedding of Tom Riddle Senior and Merope Gaunt">married</a> <a href="/wiki/Merope_Riddle" title="Merope Riddle">Merope Gaunt</a>, the daughter of the insane <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt" title="Marvolo Gaunt">Marvolo Gaunt</a>. The pair left for <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, but Tom soon returned to his <a href="/wiki/Riddle_House" title="Riddle House">parents' home</a>, claiming to have been "hood-winked" and "taken in" by Merope. This may have referred to her being a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and probably using a <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potion</a> on him to obtain his affections, or to her pregnancy.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Murder">Murder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Murder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Murder"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a>, Tom was confronted by his son from his forced marriage to Merope Gaunt—<a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Marvolo Riddle</a>, who had been left in an <a href="/wiki/Wool%27s_Orphanage" title="Wool&#39;s Orphanage">orphanage</a> after his mother's death and who was a very powerful <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>. Thomas was murdered by his grandson with the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a>, as were his wife and son, leaving the three with horrified faces at their deaths, and no medical reason for how three perfectly healthy persons died just like that. Riddle then framed his uncle <a href="/wiki/Morfin_Gaunt" title="Morfin Gaunt">Morfin Gaunt</a> for the crime, and Morfin was imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> by the Magical justice system. While the Muggle authorities could not figure out how the Riddles had died, many people suspected their gardener, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce" title="Frank Bryce">Frank Bryce</a>, of being the killer. However, the Muggle police never prepared charges against Bryce, due to lack of evidence. Thomas was buried in the <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton_graveyard" title="Little Hangleton graveyard">Little Hangleton graveyard</a> along with his son and wife. Due to their attitude towards the locals the only mourners for them were the parson, the mortician and the gravediggers. However, the murders did become the talk of the town, and residents of Little Hangleton did question what had happened and if it could reoccur.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, Albus Dumbledore refers to Riddle’s child, Tom (senior) as "the squire’s son." "Squire" is a British term referring to the leader of an English village, often a justice of the peace or a member of Parliament, indicating Thomas’s status in Muggle society.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Thomas" is the Greek form of the Aramaic name תָּאוֹמָא (<i>Ta'oma</i>’) which meant "twin".
</p>
