<p><b>King Ragnuk the First</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/11th_century" title="11th century">11th century</a>) was the king of the <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblins</a> during the lifetime of <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor" title="Godric Gryffindor">Godric Gryffindor</a>. He was the maker of the famous <a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor" title="Sword of Gryffindor">Sword of Gryffindor</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ragnuk?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ragnuk?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As the king of the goblins was also their finest silversmith, Ragnuk was commissioned by Gryffindor to forge <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor%27s_Sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Godric Gryffindor&#39;s Sword">a sword</a> of pure <a href="/wiki/Goblin%27s_silver" class="mw-redirect" title="Goblin&#39;s silver">goblin's silver</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ruby" title="Ruby">rubies</a> set into the hilt. By the time Ragnuk had finished the sword, however, he liked it so much that he sought to steal it back from Gryffindor. He sent a group of his subjects to retrieve the sword, but they were all fought off by Gryffindor, who was a skilled <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellist</a>. Gryffindor <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">bewitched</a> the goblins to return to Ragnuk and inform him that, if he tried such a thing again, Gryffindor would use the sword to kill him and all his subjects.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ragnuk made no further attempt to take the sword, however, a legend persisted in the goblin community that Gryffindor had stolen the sword, a myth perpetrated by the fact that goblins consider passing goblin-made possessions onto others without further payment to the maker, as Gryffindor would go on to do, as theft.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In fact, this myth was so prevalent in the <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a> community, that it was the cause for one of the bloody <a href="/wiki/Goblin_Rebellions" title="Goblin Rebellions">Goblin Rebellions</a> in the <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th</a> and <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th centuries</a>, around six-hundred years after the Sword was fashioned.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
