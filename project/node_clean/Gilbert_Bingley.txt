<b>Gilbert</b><sup id="cite_ref-firstname_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-firstname-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <b>P.</b><sup id="cite_ref-cb46_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cb46-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <b>Bingley</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">No-Maj</a> man who worked as a loan officer at the <a href="/wiki/Steen_National_Bank" title="Steen National Bank">Steen National Bank</a> branch in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FB9_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB9-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was described as "imposing" and "impeccably suited."<sup id="cite_ref-FB9_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB9-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Bingley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Bingley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Bingley conducted an in-person review of <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Kowalski" title="Jacob Kowalski">Jacob Kowalski</a>'s application for a loan to open <a href="/wiki/Kowalski_Quality_Baked_Goods" title="Kowalski Quality Baked Goods">a bakery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FB9_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB9-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FB11_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB11-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He summarily rejected the application when it became clear that Kowalski had insufficient income or assets to offer collateral.<sup id="cite_ref-FB11_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB11-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shortly thereafter, Bingley wandered down to the basement of the bank and found Jacob standing in front of an opened vault, in which <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a> was trying to recapture his escaped <a href="/wiki/Niffler" title="Niffler">Niffler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FB15_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB15-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Thinking that Jacob was trying to steal the money he needed, Bingley activated the alarm, but was swiftly paralysed with the <a href="/wiki/Full_Body-Bind_Curse" title="Full Body-Bind Curse">Full Body-Bind Curse</a> by Newt, allowing the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and his unlikely <a href="/wiki/No-Maj" class="mw-redirect" title="No-Maj">No-Maj</a> companion to escape.<sup id="cite_ref-FB15_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB15-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two days later, <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a> had <a href="/wiki/Frank" title="Frank">Frank</a> the <a href="/wiki/Thunderbird" title="Thunderbird">Thunderbird</a> disperse <a href="/wiki/Swooping_Evil" title="Swooping Evil">swooping evil</a> <a href="/wiki/Swooping_Evil_venom" title="Swooping Evil venom">venom</a> over <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> by creating a rainstorm, in order to erase the memories of a <a href="/wiki/Obscurial" title="Obscurial">recent magical catastrophe</a> from the minds of the city's No-Maj population.<sup id="cite_ref-FB118_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB118-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At the time, Bingley was taking a shower in the bathroom while <a href="/wiki/Bingley%27s_wife" class="mw-redirect" title="Bingley&#39;s wife">his wife</a> brushed her teeth at the sink, the water erasing his memories as it washed over him.<sup id="cite_ref-FB118_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB118-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He likely took a sudden retirement due to the activities at his bank.<sup id="cite_ref-FBF_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
