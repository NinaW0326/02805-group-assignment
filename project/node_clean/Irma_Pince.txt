<p><b>Madam Irma Pince</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a> – <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">librarian</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">library</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. She was highly possessive and protective of the school's books and was deemed highly unpleasant by most of the student body. 
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Irma Pince was born a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> sometime in or prior to <a href="/wiki/1974" title="1974">1974</a> and presumably purchased her <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> prior to attending school. She likely attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and proceeded with her studies into the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> arts until at least her <a href="/wiki/Sixth_year" title="Sixth year">sixth year</a>, since she could perform intricate <a href="/wiki/Non-verbal_spell" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-verbal spell">non-verbal magic</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> was able to place powerful and unpleasant <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hex" title="Hex">hexes</a> on objects<sup id="cite_ref-QTAforeword_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAforeword-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and could effectively remove a <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curse</a> from a student by administering the correct <a href="/wiki/Counter-curse" title="Counter-curse">counter-curse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSgbc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSgbc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<p>At some point in or before <a href="/wiki/1974" title="1974">1974</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HM_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Pince took up the position of Librarian at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. She was greatly disliked by the students for being very strict and unhelpful in finding books for them.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> An example of her strictness was the fact that she would not allow food in the library, for fear that the books would be damaged,<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and also disallowed talking, laughing, whispering, sneezing, scurrying, or any other behaviour that might seem at all suspicious in any way. She was also known to place odd jinxes and hexes on library books for their protection; for instance, one copy of <i><a href="/wiki/Theories_of_Transubstantial_Transfiguration" title="Theories of Transubstantial Transfiguration">Theories of Transubstantial Transfiguration</a></i>, on top of the usual library book spells, was jinxed to hit the reader around the head, should he ever doodle on it.<sup id="cite_ref-QTAforeword_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAforeword-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Pince cleaned the dust on the shelves of the library manually, with a feather duster she brandished at students whenever they looked suspicious.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>At some point, the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, visited the library with the goal of publishing <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages" title="Quidditch Through the Ages">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> to raise money. However, Madam Pince was extremely reluctant to let the book leave the confines of the library, as well as the wizarding world, and Dumbledore had to pry each of her fingers from the spine to take it.<sup id="cite_ref-QTAforeword_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAforeword-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Once he had taken the book, she wrote a warning, threatening the readers that the consequences of mistreating the book would be as horrible as it was in her power to make them.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Madam Pince was still librarian by the time Harry Potter had arrived at the school. As Harry, along with his friends <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, searched the library for a book on <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel" title="Nicolas Flamel">Nicolas Flamel</a>, Pince was deemed an obstacle, as she could warn Snape of their activities.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Once, in December 1991, she saw Harry edging towards the <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Section" title="Restricted Section">Restricted Section</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">Library</a> and asked him what he was looking for. When he answered "nothing" for fear of her suspicion, she sent him out.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On the next school year, Madam Pince was among the crowd who, on <a href="/wiki/31_October" title="31 October">31 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, read the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Heir of Slytherin's</a> message on the second-floor corridor of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSf13_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSf13-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A while later, Madam Pince overlooked the students at <a href="/wiki/Study_Hall" title="Study Hall">Study Hall</a> and was seen talking to a female student.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>It is unknown if this was a regular event that she chaperoned, or if it was a one-time event.
</p><p>Later, Hermione Granger arrived at the library with a note written by the new <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">teacher</a> allowing her to attain <i><a href="/wiki/Moste_Potente_Potions" title="Moste Potente Potions">Moste Potente Potions</a></i> from the <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Section" title="Restricted Section">Restricted Section</a>. She gave it to Madam Pince, albeit reluctantly, wanting to keep Lockhart's autograph, and Pince did a very long observation of the note, checking for forgery. She then went into the Restricted Section and handed over the book, despite still being very suspicious.<sup id="cite_ref-CS10_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS10-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pince attended the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Quidditch_team" title="Gryffindor Quidditch team">Gryffindor</a> vs <a href="/wiki/Slytherin_Quidditch_team" title="Slytherin Quidditch team">Slytherin</a> match. She was shocked to see that <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> was hurt.<sup id="cite_ref-CSf15_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSf15-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Madam Pince attended the End-of-Term Feast at the end of the school year, in which she sat by the <a href="/wiki/Potions_Master" title="Potions Master">Potions Master</a>, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>. She celebrated the downfall of <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Salazar Slytherin's Basilisk</a> and the departure of the less-than-liked <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSf36_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSf36-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During the next school year, Madam Pince kept her usual routine of prowling around the library, breathing down the necks of those touching the books.<sup id="cite_ref-OP24_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP24-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Early in 1995, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> was made <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_High_Inquisitor" title="Hogwarts High Inquisitor">High Inquisitor of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP15_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP15-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the Easter holidays, she was seen stamping out a pile of books for a frantic-looking <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Abbott" title="Hannah Abbott">Hannah Abbott</a>, who was presumably revising for her <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.s</a>. That day, she found Ginny Weasley and Harry Potter with chocolate in the library and bewitched their belongings to chase them out, also whacking them over the head for good measure.<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>At eight o'clock in the evening one day, Pince closed the library and told all of the students in there to return the books back to their shelves and to leave. When she told Harry and Hermione to leave, she noticed <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape%27s_copy_of_Advanced_Potion-Making" title="Severus Snape&#39;s copy of Advanced Potion-Making">the Half-Blood Prince's highly graffitied copy</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Advanced_Potion-Making" title="Advanced Potion-Making">Advanced Potion-Making</a></i>. Enraged, she screamed about how the book was "despoiled" and "befouled", and looked as if she may have a seizure.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, Madam Pince was seen prior to <a href="/wiki/Funeral_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="Funeral of Albus Dumbledore">the funeral</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Entrance_Hall" title="Entrance Hall">Entrance Hall</a>, standing by <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Argus Filch</a> and wearing a thick black veil that fell to her knees. It is likely she attended the <a href="/wiki/Funeral_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="Funeral of Albus Dumbledore">funeral</a>, as she was one of Dumbledore's colleagues.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP30_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP30-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The following year, Severus Snape was appointed <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> along with fellow Death Eaters <a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow" title="Amycus Carrow">Amycus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto Carrow</a> as professors of <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a> at Hogwarts. The Carrows imposed a reign of terror in Hogwarts as they enforced cruel punishments on students. Madam Pince, like the other members of the staff, protected the students from the Carrows. On 2 May, 1998, <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> was attacked by the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s forces and the faculty rose to defend it, Madam Pince most likely included.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> She survived the battle and continued to work for the school.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Having diligently carried out the duties of her office for more than twenty years following the battle, Madam Pince was still working at Hogwarts during the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%932020_school_year" title="2019–2020 school year">2019–2020 school year</a>, a member from the then recently established <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> pursuing a career as a <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> eventually joined their faculty. When said staff member later sought to improve their ability to fend off the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Forces</a>, Madam Pince would repeatably grant them access to the <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Section" title="Restricted Section">Restricted Section</a> of the library so they could peruse its shelves for books on the subject.<sup id="cite_ref-WU1_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU1-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> She was later handed a hand-written manuscript by the famous <a href="/wiki/Catullus_Spangle" title="Catullus Spangle">Catullus Spangle</a> by the newcomer, which presumably found its allotted place in the library thereafter, and she would sometimes recommend them certain reading material.<sup id="cite_ref-WU2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU2-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>One of Madam Pince's most striking physical attributes was looking like an underfed vulture.<sup id="cite_ref-CS10_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS10-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Her skin was parchment-like,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> her cheeks were sunken,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> her face was shrivelled<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and she had an unflattering hooked-nose.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> She was also very thin and irritable-looking.<sup id="cite_ref-CS10_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS10-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i> </i>WHAT DO YOU THINK YOU'RE DOING<i>?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Pince catches students eating in the library<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Madam Pince was a short-tempered woman who was overly protective of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">Library's</a> books<sup id="cite_ref-CS10_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS10-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and, as such, quite unpopular among students. She was mainly seen by students as an obstacle to research,<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> but she was professional nonetheless, helping students to check out books and by finding information for them.<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> While she did not appear to be overtly fond of the students, she did not, unlike <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Argus Filch</a>, appear to outright dislike them either, being strict but by no means demeaning or unpleasant, and only got genuinely angry when they failed to respect the sanctity of the school library by eating there, or by mistreating the books. Her protective nature of books did not just apply to the library's, as she looked deranged when she saw Harry's own textbook covered with self-written notes.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_20-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Likewise, she would not permit students eating in the library, and chased out anyone who violated this rule.<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While stern bordering on the extreme, she was fully capable of appreciating students who <i>did</i> respect the rules, behaved prudently and showed an interest in learning and reading. When she overheard <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> confide in his friends while visiting the library that the attack on <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Potter</a> was one of the reasons that Boggarts turned into Lord Voldemort, Madam Pince walked over and explained that she remembered the fondly, and told how they often used to sit on a specific spot in the library and study together, before awarding 20 points for helping to keep the memory of the Potters alive by talking about them. She later informed said student that she had also quite liked <a href="/wiki/Jacob_(Hogwarts_student)" title="Jacob (Hogwarts student)">Jacob</a> himself when he was a student, as he had always been well-behaved in the library and treated the books with respect.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also, despite her forceful insistence for the library to be silent, she was a huge fan of the <a href="/wiki/Frog_Choir" title="Frog Choir">Frog Choir</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM3_Frog_Choir_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM3_Frog_Choir-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>And whipping out her wand, she caused Harry’s books, bag, and ink bottle to chase him and Ginny from the library, whacking them repeatedly over the head as they ran.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Madam Pince jinxing Harry Potter's belongings<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Madam Pince was skilled with <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hex" title="Hex">hexing</a> books with nasty spells to make sure they were not mistreated, at least not without punishment for doing so.<sup id="cite_ref-QTAforeword_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAforeword-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She also jinxed <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry's</a> belongings to chase him and <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a> out of the library.<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Madam Pince was able to <a href="/wiki/Counter-curse_for_Curse_of_the_Bogies" title="Counter-curse for Curse of the Bogies">lift</a> the <a href="/wiki/Curse_of_the_Bogies" title="Curse of the Bogies">Curse of the Bogies</a> with ease, showing her to have been skilled with the casting of <a href="/wiki/Counter-curse" title="Counter-curse">counter-curses</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSgbc_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSgbc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It is also highly likely that, as a member of Hogwarts staff, that she participated and fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> on <a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, meaning she was likely a reasonably fierce and competent <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellist</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Non-verbal_magic" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-verbal magic">Non-verbal magic</a></b>: Madam Pince was also adept in casting intricate non-verbal magic with her wand. One example was when she saw Harry and Ginny with chocolate in the library, she jinxed their possessions to chase them out without any mention of a spoken incantation.<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This implies she was a highly skilled and powerful <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, as casting non-verbal spells is extremely difficult and advanced.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Madam Pince could also produce a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> a mark of superior <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability, given the immense difficulty of casting the charm.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Study_of_Ancient_Runes" title="Study of Ancient Runes">Study of Ancient Runes</a></b>: Madam Pince was very well versed and knowledgeable in the study of Runology, being able to understand and translate complex <a href="/wiki/Rune" title="Rune">runic</a> scriptures, alphabets and symbols correctly with the help of a highly useful and practical <a href="/wiki/Rune_Dictionary" title="Rune Dictionary">Rune Dictionary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Librarian skills</b>: As a librarian, Madam Pince was able to easily locate various pieces of information and books for students, as Harry was sure that she would be able to find information about <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel" title="Nicolas Flamel">Nicolas Flamel</a> in a second, but thought it might be too suspicious to ask her about it.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Loquaciousness</b>: Pince was very loquacious and voluble in her speech.<sup id="cite_ref-COS_PS2_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS_PS2-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> She was also fluent in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>, showing her to have been multilingual, a very impressive and intellectual talent.<sup id="cite_ref-COS_PS2_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS_PS2-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?veaction=edit&amp;section=18" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince?action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Irma could be a play on <i>firma</i>, which can mean both solid and frail. This makes her name mean something along the lines of "a frail woman with a pinched nose", or one who has a "firm pinch". Irma could also mean "the strong" or "she who is protected by Irm" (Irm is a Germanic god). Irma is from German irmen for "whole" or "complete". "Firma" also means "signature" in Spanish, which may or may not be a play of words about signed library cards.</li>
<li>The last name <i>Pince</i> seems to be in reference to the French word, which means "to pinch". The style of folding eyeglass, <i>pince-nez</i>, literally means "to pinch the nose".</li></ul>

<p class="mw-empty-elt"></p>
<ul><li>Pince is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> actress <a href="/wiki/Sally_Mortemore" title="Sally Mortemore">Sally Mortemore</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>. Mortemore would return over a decade later to provide snippets of dialogue for the character in <i>Hogwarts Mystery</i>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film</a>, she appears briefly; sitting next to <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> during the <a href="/wiki/End-of-Term_Feast" title="End-of-Term Feast">End-of-Term Feast</a> and can be seen in the background of several other scenes.</li>
<li>For various reasons, such as that Pince has a hooked nose and dark hair like <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus</a>, that her name is an anagram for "I am Prince", and that she is a rude and somewhat snappy personality like Snape, it is rumoured that Irma Pince is actually <a href="/wiki/Eileen_Prince" title="Eileen Prince">Eileen Prince</a> in hiding, though this has not been confirmed.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (video game)">video game adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Madam Pince is seen awarding house points. She never does so in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">books</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (video game)">video game adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, Madam Pince appears to have red hair, rather than black.</li>
<li>Despite the fact she appears to be old in the books and videogames, Sally Mortemore was 41 at the time of filming, and no notable effort was made to make her character appear older.</li>
<li>The Game Boy Color versions of the video games depict Pince as a helpful old woman with very thick glasses.</li>
<li>Irma Pince can produce a non-corporeal Patronus Charm in <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>, but oddly enough she is unable to cast it in the sequel <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>, despite her character being the same in both games. This was most likely a mistake made by the developers in the latter.</li></ul>
