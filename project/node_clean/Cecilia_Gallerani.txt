<b>Cecilia Gallerani</b> (<a href="/wiki/1473" class="mw-redirect" title="1473">1473</a>–<a href="/wiki/1536" class="mw-redirect" title="1536">1536</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Renaissance">Renaissance</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, and the favourite and most celebrated of the many mistresses of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludovico_Sforza" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Ludovico Sforza">Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan</a>. She is best known as the subject of one of <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Da_Vinci" title="Leonardo Da Vinci">Leonardo Da Vinci</a>'s portraits. She used to arrange meetings in which Milanese intellectuals discussed philosophy and other subjects, and Cecilia herself presided over these discussions.
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, there was a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> of Gallerani hung at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>, on the stairway leading to the <a href="/wiki/Dungeons" title="Dungeons">Dungeons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSps1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSps1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
