<p>A <b>wandmaker</b> is a person who constructs and sells <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> for <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches and wizards</a> and is thus an important occupation in <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding society</a>. The practise of making a wand is known as <b>wandcraft</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH24_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH24-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A related occupation is the <b><a href="/wiki/Wandkeeper" title="Wandkeeper">wandkeeper</a></b>, though it is unclear whether this is merely another term for wandmaker or a separate occupation. Regardless, one such example was an <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Ollivander%27s_associate" class="mw-redirect" title="Unidentified Ollivander&#39;s associate">associate</a> of <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>'s working at the shop's <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> branch.<sup id="cite_ref-WWHP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WWHP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wandmaker?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wandmaker?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A wandmaker use <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">cores</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a> to construct a magical instrument to conduct magic through. Wandmakers use a variety of wood and cores based upon their personal research and preference, and use wood and core found in their land. For example, wandmakers in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a> used <a href="/wiki/Thunderbird_tail_feather" title="Thunderbird tail feather">Thunderbird feather</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rougarou_hair" title="Rougarou hair">Rougarou hair</a><sup id="cite_ref-1920s_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1920s-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>, but wandmakers in Britain used <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_feather" title="Phoenix feather">Phoenix feather</a> and <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_hair" title="Unicorn hair">Unicorn hair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>It was considered a "complex and mysterious branch of magic".<sup id="cite_ref-DH24_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH24-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wandmaker?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wandmaker?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The earliest known wandmakers were <a href="/wiki/Druid" title="Druid">druids</a>, who thought any plant that had a woody stem could be used in wandmaking.<sup id="cite_ref-woods_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-woods-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In Britain at least, it was normal for the purchaser of a wand to provide their own <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">core</a> or request something specific, generally a traditional or well-liked substance.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Wandmaking shops are known to date back to the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders</a> in <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C</a>. The family had travelled from a Mediterranean country in Roman times and set up a stall in what would eventually be Diagon Alley. A descendant, <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a> became a renowed wandmaker and he forsook the concept of witches and wizards procuring their own wand core, because he thought their wands were usually unrefined.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>He claimed to have found more success in doing so. He researched the best wand cores and found the best wand wood to complement it, and rather have a purchaser select their wand, he let the wand choose the purchaser.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> From the <a href="/wiki/13th_century" title="13th century">13th century</a>, there was a story that <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">Death</a> had created the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">Elder Wand</a> for <a href="/wiki/Antioch_Peverell" title="Antioch Peverell">Antioch Peverell</a>. It was told in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">The Tales of Beedle the Bard </a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the United States of America, there were four renowned wandmakers using cores and wood native to their country. <a href="/wiki/Isolt_Sayre" title="Isolt Sayre">Isolt Sayre</a> was the earliest known wandmaker in America. <a href="/wiki/Shikoba_Wolfe" title="Shikoba Wolfe">Shikoba Wolfe</a> used <a href="/wiki/Thunderbird_tail_feather" title="Thunderbird tail feather">Thunderbird tail feather</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Jonker" title="Johannes Jonker">Johannes Jonker</a>  used <a href="/wiki/Wampus_cat_hair" title="Wampus cat hair">Wampus cat hair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thiago_Quintana" title="Thiago Quintana">Thiago Quintana</a> used <a href="/wiki/White_River_Monster_spine" title="White River Monster spine">White River Monster spine</a> but this went out of fashion, and <a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais" title="Violetta Beauvais">Violetta Beauvais</a> used <a href="/wiki/Rougarou_hair" title="Rougarou hair">Rougarou hair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1920s_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1920s-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>   
</p>
