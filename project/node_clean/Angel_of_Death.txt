<p>The <b>Angel of Death</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> personification of the concept of <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>, originating from biblical accounts of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archangel" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Archangel">archangels</a>. It can be considered similar to <a href="/wiki/Death_(The_Tale_of_the_Three_Brothers)" title="Death (The Tale of the Three Brothers)">Death</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding</a> fable <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tale_of_the_Three_Brothers" title="The Tale of the Three Brothers">The Tale of the Three Brothers</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/The_Riddles%27_grave" class="mw-redirect" title="The Riddles&#39; grave">The Riddle family gravestone</a> was accompanied of a large stone statue of the Angel, which was depicted as wearing long, flowing <a href="/wiki/Robes" title="Robes">robes</a> and carrying a scythe.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Angel_of_Death?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Angel_of_Death?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In Abrahamic faiths (Judaism, Christianity and Islam) the Angel of Death is named either <i>Azrael</i>, <i>Dumah</i>, <i>Puriel</i> or <i>Samael</i>.</li>
<li>In <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_mythology" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>, the Angel of Death was named <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thanatos" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Thanatos">Thanatos</a>, (θανατος) an angelic god who carried a sword. He was considered to be the counterpart of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eros" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Eros">Eros</a>, (Ερος) a god of love&#160;better known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cupid" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Cupid">Cupid</a>.&#160;In Latin, he was known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Letus">Letus</a>, the derivative of the English&#160;word <i>lethal</i>. Despite Thanatos' aggressive appearance, he was the god of nonviolent death. The Keres were the goddesses of violent death.</li></ul>
