<b>Herbert Burke</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who married the pure-blood witch <a href="/wiki/Belvina_Black" title="Belvina Black">Belvina Black</a>. Given that Belvina was not disowned by her <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">family</a>, who believed strongly in <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood purity</a>, it is likely that Herbert was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a>. The couple had <a href="/wiki/Belvina_Burke%27s_sons" title="Belvina Burke&#39;s sons">two sons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belvina_Burke%27s_daughter" title="Belvina Burke&#39;s daughter">one daughter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Herbert may be related to <a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke" title="Caractacus Burke">Caractacus Burke</a> of <a href="/wiki/Borgin_and_Burkes" title="Borgin and Burkes">Borgin and Burkes</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Herbert_Burke?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Herbert_Burke?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbert_(name)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Herbert (name)">Herbert</a>" is derived from the Germanic elements <i>hari</i>, "army" and <i>beraht</i>, "bright". The Normans introduced this name to England, where it replaced an Old English cognate <i>Herebeorht</i>. In the course of the Middle Ages it became rare, but it was revived in the 19th century.
</p><p>There was a real person with this name, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._Herbert_Burke" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:J. Herbert Burke">J. Herbert Burke</a>.
</p><p><i>Burke</i> means "fortified hill".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
