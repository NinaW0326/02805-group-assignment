</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Saul_Croaker?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Saul_Croaker?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Professor_Croaker%E2%80%99s_law" title="Professor Croaker’s law">Professor Croaker’s law</a> was named for him. Croaker attended the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a> final, either attending with or meeting fellow Unspeakable <a href="/wiki/Broderick_Bode" title="Broderick Bode">Broderick Bode</a>. Croaker was among the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> employees that passed by the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a>'s campsite.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF7_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Saul_Croaker?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Saul_Croaker?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <i>"Croaker"</i> is someone who habitually predicts evil; it has that sense because croaking is a noise made by <a href="/wiki/Crow" title="Crow">crows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raven" title="Raven">ravens</a>, who are considered <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> omens, as they are carrion eaters.
</p>
