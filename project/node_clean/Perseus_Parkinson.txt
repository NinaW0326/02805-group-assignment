<dd></dd></dl></div><p><b>Minister Perseus Parkinson</b> was <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">British Minister for Magic</a> from <a href="/wiki/1726" class="mw-redirect" title="1726">1726</a> to <a href="/wiki/1733" class="mw-redirect" title="1733">1733</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Perseus_Parkinson?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Perseus_Parkinson?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He attempted to pass a bill making it illegal to <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marry</a> a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a>. However, in doing so, he misread the public mood; the wizarding public was tired of <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_Supremacy" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizarding Supremacy">anti-Muggle sentiment</a> and wanted peace. Parkinson was therefore voted out during the next election for Minister.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Perseus Parkinson's attempts to ban mixed marriages were identical to <a href="/wiki/Rappaport%27s_Law" title="Rappaport&#39;s Law">Rappaport's Law</a> introduced by <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a> president <a href="/wiki/Emily_Rappaport" title="Emily Rappaport">Emily Rappaport</a>. However, unlike in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a>, anti-muggle sentiments were not as widespread and confined to wizarding supremacists. 
</p>
