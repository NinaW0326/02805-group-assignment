<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>A grim mood has gripped the country.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Prime Minister's political opponent.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>This <b>individual</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> politician who, in the summer of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, appeared on the news not only to enumerate all the dreadful calamities that had then struck the country (the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Brockdale_Bridge" title="Brockdale Bridge">Brockdale Bridge</a>, the nasty and well-publicised murders of <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones" title="Amelia Bones">Amelia Bones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emmeline_Vance" title="Emmeline Vance">Emmeline Vance</a> and the "freak hurricane" in the West Country), but also to explain why every one of them was the government's fault (they weren't spending enough funding on bridges, there was a lack of police officers against the murders, and the government somehow foresaw the hurricane).<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was running for the position of <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_(title)" title="Prime Minister (title)">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a> against <a href="/wiki/British_Prime_Minister_in_1996" title="British Prime Minister in 1996">current prime minister</a>, who was visited by then-<a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> and subsequent Minister <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a>, who attempted to explain to the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> community what was truly going on.
</p><p>In fact, all of those incidents were a result of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">rise to power</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Brockdale_Bridge" title="Brockdale Bridge">Brockdale Bridge</a> having been snapped in two by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>; <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones" title="Amelia Bones">Amelia Bones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emmeline_Vance" title="Emmeline Vance">Emmeline Vance</a> having been murdered because of their connections to the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, respectively; and the "freak hurricane" having been caused by <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">Giants</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li>This political opponent of the Prime Minister's is never named in the series, and may be intended to be a fictional character. Since the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_series" title="Harry Potter series"><i>Harry Potter</i> series</a> is set mostly in the 1990s the most likely real-life candidate would be <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Blair" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a>, the Leader of the Opposition from 1994 to 1997.</li></ul>
