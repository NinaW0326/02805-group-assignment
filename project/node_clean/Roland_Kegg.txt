<b>Roland Kegg</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1903" title="1903">1903</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was President of the <a href="/wiki/English_National_Gobstones_Team" title="English National Gobstones Team">English Gobstones Team</a>. For his achievement he was commemorated on a bronze <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Roland_Kegg?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Roland_Kegg?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Roland" means "famous land" from the Germanic elements <i>hrod</i> "fame" and land. Roland was a semi-legendary French hero whose story is told in the medieval epic 'La Chanson de Roland', in which he is a nephew of Charlemagne killed in battle with the Saracens. This name was introduced to England by the Normans.
</p>
