<b>Count Vlad Drakul</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1390" class="mw-redirect" title="1390">1390</a>) was a notorious <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a> who inspired the author <a href="/wiki/Bram_Stoker" title="Bram Stoker">Bram Stoker</a> to create his famous character <a href="/wiki/Count_Dracula" title="Count Dracula">Count Dracula</a>. Drakul had a son, <a href="/wiki/Vlad_the_Impaler" title="Vlad the Impaler">Vlad the Impaler</a>, who presumably shared his father's notorious reputation.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vlad_Drakul?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vlad_Drakul?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vlad_II_Dracul" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Vlad II Dracul">Vlad II Dracul</a>, (Vlad the Dragon) was a "voivode" (duke or prince) of Wallachia (a region that corresponds with modern-day Southern Romania), and reigned from 1436 to 1442, and again from 1443 to 1447.</li>
<li>In reality, there are <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dracula#Background" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dracula">various theories</a> as to Stoker's inspiration for Dracula. He was inspired by a Wallachian duke named Dracula (i.e. Dracul's son or Dragon's son), so it is Dracul's son Vlad the Impaler who is more commonly considered the source of inspiration.</li></ul>
