<b>Gondoline Oliphant</b> (<a href="/wiki/1720" class="mw-redirect" title="1720">1720</a>-<a href="/wiki/1799" class="mw-redirect" title="1799">1799</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> made famous for studying the life and habits of <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">trolls</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gondoline_Oliphant?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gondoline_Oliphant?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1799" class="mw-redirect" title="1799">1799</a>, she was clubbed to <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cotswolds" title="Cotswolds">Cotswolds</a> while sketching. For her achievements in <a href="/wiki/Magizoology" title="Magizoology">Magizoology</a>, she was featured on a silver <a href="/wiki/Famous_Witches_and_Wizards_Cards" title="Famous Witches and Wizards Cards">Famous Wizard card</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> series.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-POAG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was a bust of her at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>, on a secret passage leading from the fourth floor to the second floor.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gondoline_Oliphant?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gondoline_Oliphant?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The surname Oliphant is derived from an archaic English word for "<a href="/wiki/Elephant" title="Elephant">elephant</a>." J.R.R. Tolkien included huge, elephant-like creatures known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%BBmak" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:MÃ»mak">oliphaunts</a> in his novel <i>The Lord of the Rings.</i></li>
<li>The name Gondoline is perhaps a further nod to Tolkien: in the <i>Silmarillion</i>, there was an Elven city called Gondolin, which remained hidden for centuries before being destroyed.</li></ul>
