<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Randolph_Keitch?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Randolph_Keitch?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1929" title="1929">1929</a>, he established the <a href="/wiki/Comet_Trading_Company" title="Comet Trading Company">Comet Trading Company</a> with fellow player <a href="/wiki/Basil_Horton" title="Basil Horton">Basil Horton</a>. Together they also invented the <a href="/wiki/Horton-Keitch_Braking_Charm" title="Horton-Keitch Braking Charm">Horton-Keitch Braking Charm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Randolph_Keitch?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Randolph_Keitch?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: Keitch was an accomplished and skilful <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> flyer, as he was a professional <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Falmouth_Falcons" title="Falmouth Falcons">Falmouth Falcons</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Keitch was highly talented with charm-work, given how he invented the <a href="/wiki/Horton-Keitch_Braking_Charm" title="Horton-Keitch Braking Charm">Horton-Keitch Braking Charm</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Spell_creation" title="Spell creation">Spell creation</a></b>: Keitch successfully created the <a href="/wiki/Horton-Keitch_Braking_Charm" title="Horton-Keitch Braking Charm">Horton-Keitch Braking Charm</a>, a very impressive <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> accomplishment, given the immense complexity and difficulty of inventing new <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Randolph_Keitch?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Randolph_Keitch?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From the Germanic elements rand meaning "rim (of a shield)" and wulf meaning "wolf". The Normans brought this name to England, where there existed already an Old Norse cognate Rand√∫lfr, which had been introduced by Scandinavian settlers. Randolf became rare after the Middle Ages, though it was revived in the 18th century (usually in the spelling Randolph).
</p>
