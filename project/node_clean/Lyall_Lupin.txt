<p><b>Lyall Lupin</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1929" title="1929">1929</a> – <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was a world-renowned authority on <a href="/wiki/Non-being" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-being">Non-Human Spirituous Apparitions</a>. During the prelude to the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> called on his expertise of <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark</a> creatures to help contain the threat. He eventually joined the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Regulation_and_Control_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures">Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures</a>. Lyall married <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Hope_Howell" title="Hope Howell">Hope Howell</a> and had one son, <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus John Lupin</a>, who was bitten by the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> in retaliation for a negative comment about lycanthropes that Lyall made.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lyall attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in his youth.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_career">Marriage and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Marriage and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage and career"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By the age of thirty, Lyall had become a world-renowned expert on Non-Human&#160;Spiritous Apparition, which included <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">poltergeists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lyall went on an expedition to a remote <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> woodland to investigate reports of a very fierce Boggart, and heard a woman scream. He rushed to her aid and banished the Boggart, <a href="/wiki/Boggart-Banishing_Spell" title="Boggart-Banishing Spell">turning</a> it into a <a href="/wiki/Field_mushroom" title="Field mushroom">field mushroom</a> with a wave of his wand. The woman was <a href="/wiki/Hope_Howell" title="Hope Howell">Hope Howell</a>, a Muggle who worked for an insurance company in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>. He tried to explain it was a Boggart, but she didn't understand so he acted like she had been confronted by a big and scary man. Afterward, he escorted her home wishing to ensure her safety.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A few months later, he admitted to Hope that the Boggart had not posed any serious danger to her. This admission did not negatively impact her opinion of him, however — by this time the pair had fallen in <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a>. Eventually, Lyall gathered the courage to propose and Hope accepted, enthusiastically committing herself to planning their nuptials. Their wedding took place in early <a href="/wiki/1959" title="1959">1959</a> and included a cake featuring a Boggart-shaped <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/wedding_cake_topper" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:wedding cake topper">topper</a>. One year later on <a href="/wiki/10_March" title="10 March">10 March</a>, <a href="/wiki/1960" title="1960">1960</a> the couple's first and only child, <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus John Lupin</a> was born.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By the mid-<a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">1960s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort's</a> power was growing, and <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">his followers</a> were recruiting a wide variety of Dark creatures to further his cause.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> called upon experts on Dark creatures in the hope of better understanding the threat and keeping it in check.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Lyall was asked to join the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Regulation_and_Control_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures">Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures</a> and did so happily.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point during his career at the Ministry, Lyall got rid of the <a href="/wiki/Screaming_Bogey_of_Strathtully" title="Screaming Bogey of Strathtully">Screaming Bogey</a> of <a href="/wiki/Strathtully" title="Strathtully">Strathtully</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a> that had been feeding on the fears of local <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> to the point that it had become an elephantine black shadow with glowing white eyes, by trapping it in a matchbox.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Incurring_a_werewolf's_wrath"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Incurring_a_werewolf.27s_wrath">Incurring a werewolf's wrath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Incurring a werewolf&#039;s wrath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Incurring a werewolf&#039;s wrath"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early <a href="/wiki/1965" title="1965">1965</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> was brought in for questioning at the Ministry of Magic following the deaths of two Muggle children. Although he was apparently caught with a <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> pack, the Ministry was not aware that Greyback was himself a werewolf because the existing <a href="/wiki/Werewolf_Registry" title="Werewolf Registry">Werewolf Registry</a> was not well-maintained. Greyback insisted to the questioning committee that he was only a Muggle tramp, which nearly all of the members fell for. Lyall, however, recognised characteristic signs of <a href="/wiki/Lycanthropy" title="Lycanthropy">lycanthropy</a> in Greyback's appearance and behaviour and voiced his concerns to the committee, recommending that Greyback be detained until the next full moon, which was only twenty-four hours away.
</p><p>Regardless, the other committee members ridiculed Lyall and he lost his temper, declaring werewolves to be "soulless, evil, deserving nothing but death." Consequently, he was expelled from the room, and Greyback was released. While being seen out by a Ministry wizard, who intended to <a href="/wiki/Memory_Charm" title="Memory Charm">erase his memory</a> of having been brought in for questioning, Greyback escaped with the help of two accomplices.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon after, Greyback hatched a plan to get revenge on Lyall for his derogatory remark.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Shortly before his son Remus's fifth birthday, Greyback forced open the boy's bedroom window and attacked him.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Lyall reached his son in time to save his life, repelling Greyback with powerful <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a>, but Remus from then on had become a werewolf.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Raising_a_lycanthropic_son">Raising a lycanthropic son</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Raising a lycanthropic son">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Raising a lycanthropic son"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With his son Remus becoming a werewolf, Lyall's perception for such individuals changed forever. Lyall and his wife tried all manner of treatments for their son's lycanthropy, but in those days, before <a href="/wiki/Damocles_Belby" title="Damocles Belby">Damocles</a>'s invention of the <a href="/wiki/Wolfsbane_Potion" title="Wolfsbane Potion">Wolfsbane Potion</a>, there were none that were actually effective.<sup id="cite_ref-poa18_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poa18-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Thus, the couple was forced to take drastic measures to conceal Remus's condition, moving to a new village or town whenever their neighbours began to take notice of the boy's strange behaviour.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They forbade Remus from playing with other children, and consequently, despite having the love of both his parents, his early childhood was marked by loneliness.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Initially, Lyall was able to keep Remus under control during the full moon by placing him in a locked room and casting several <a href="/wiki/Silencing_Charm" title="Silencing Charm">Silencing Charms</a>, but as the child grew, he became more difficult to contain.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By the time he was ten, Remus was capable of knocking down doors and breaking windows during his monthly transformation, requiring Lyall to use ever more powerful spells.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Lyall and his wife grew thin worrying for their son, whom they loved dearly, knowing that the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding community</a> would react severely if they learned of the existence of an uncontrolled werewolf.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Lupins feared their son would not be able to attend <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> due to his condition.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-poa18_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poa18-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Lyall tried to provide his son with a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> education at home as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, shortly before Remus's eleventh birthday in <a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of Hogwarts, showed up unannounced at the Lupins' door. The couple tried frantically to prevent him from entering their home, but their magical defences, although strong enough to keep their werewolf son from getting out of control, were useless in front of the greatest wizard of the time and he easily entered their house without them realising it until five minutes later when Dumbledore played Gobstones with their son.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Dumbledore told the couple that he knew of how their son had been attacked by Fenrir Greyback (Greyback had boasted of his actions to spies that Dumbledore had planted among the Dark creatures).<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He assured them that Remus would be able to attend Hogwarts, since he had already seen to it that the boy would have a safe, secure location to spend his monthly transformations.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sometime prior to Voldemort's fall on <a href="/wiki/31_October" title="31 October">31 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, Hope died, leaving Lyall a widower.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Although Remus was unable to find stable employment to support himself following the end of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, he refused to move back home with Lyall, fearing that doing so would threaten his father's "peaceful" life.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, he still saw Lyall from time to time, which made his father happy.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Eventually, Remus died in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, leaving Lyall with only a <a href="/wiki/Edward_Lupin" title="Edward Lupin">grandson</a>; whether Lyall was dead by <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> or whether he ever met his grandson is unknown.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lyall was described as a shy and clever man.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Though usually mild-mannered, he had a hidden fierce temper which could assert itself when he was sufficiently upset by a situation, and was not afraid to challenge his professional superiors when he knew them to be in the wrong on an important matter.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Though he did not bear any prejudice against <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>, he subscribed to the negative view of <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolves</a> that was prevalent in the wizarding community, until his eyes were opened when his own son was left a werewolf as a result of an attack carried out in retaliation for an anti-werewolf remark he made.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Presumably, his beliefs on werewolves changed once his son was infected, given Remus became a caring individual, not evil and soulless like Lyall believed werewolves to be. Lyall proved himself to be a devoted father who loved his son unconditionally and was willing to go to great lengths to protect him despite his condition.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lyall sometimes found it difficult to reveal things to people if he thought it would make them think less of him. He refrained from telling Hope that the <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a> wasn't a real danger for several months after meeting her, presumably because he didn't want her to think that he had simply been playing the hero to try to win her affections.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also refrained from telling Remus the true circumstances of his attack and the identity of his attacker for many years out of fear of his son's condemnation.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_2-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lyall_Lupin?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>"Lyall"</i> may be from a Scottish surname which was derived from the Old Norse given name "<i>Liulfr"</i> (which was derived in part from "<i>úlfr"</i>, meaning "wolf"). "<i>Lyall"</i> is also a common surname (most common in America).</li>
<li>"<i>Lupin</i>" is derived from "<i>lupus (/lupa)</i>", Latin for "wolf". <i>Lupin</i> thus means "wolf-like" in Latin, and is also the name of a flower. "<i>Canis lupus"</i> is the scientific name for wolf. To be described as "lupine" means to "resemble a wolf". This alludes to&#160;<a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">lycanthropy</a>. The surname may also be derived from the character of Arsène Lupin, a fictional&#160;gentleman thief&#160;and master of disguise, who was created by French writer&#160;Maurice Leblanc in 1905. In Leblanc's novels, Arsène Lupin is often a force for good, while operating on the wrong side of the law. Leblanc's Lupin was said to have later inspired the character of "the Lone Wolf",&#160;a jewel thief-turned-private detective in a series of novels written by Louis Joseph Vance, beginning in 1914.</li></ul>
