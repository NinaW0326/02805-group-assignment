<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, unlike his <a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_father_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizard&#39;s father (The Wizard and the Hopping Pot)">father</a>, felt those without <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> were worthless. Upon his death, his father left him his "<a href="/wiki/The_Hopping_Pot" title="The Hopping Pot">lucky one-legged cooking pot</a>" with a slipper inside, instead of the <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> he had been expecting. His father had wrote a note to his son, hoping that "he would never need the pot". The son was very angry at this and decided to use it as a rubbish <a href="/wiki/Bin" title="Bin">bin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Hopping_Pot">The Hopping Pot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Hopping Pot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Hopping Pot"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As his father, a compassionate wizard, had helped out the local <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> by using his "magic pot" to <a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">cure</a> people's diseases, the villagers turned to his son for the same services. However, the son turned out to be a cold-hearted man who turned away anybody who sought his help. As such, the pot began sprouting manifestations of the forms of untreated diseases that villagers were suffering from. The pot also began to continuously hop on its foot, resulting in an endless clamour.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Change_as_a_character">Change as a character</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Change as a character">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Change as a character"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>The young wizard's conscience awakes and he agreed to use his magic for the benefit of his non-magical neighbours.</i>"</span></dd>

<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The son finally gave up in the end and ran through the town, crying out to the villagers to come see him to remedy their ailments. As he used his magic to heal them, the horrific manifestations on the pot begun to disappear. Finally, the pot was back to its former appearance and the son could slip the slipper onto the pot's foot. Once that was done, he and the pot walked away from the village to go and help other people.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Unlike his father, this wizard was a cold-hearted man who turned away anybody who sought his help. He felt that <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">people who could not perform magic</a> were worthless. He also did not care about his father's death, and was angry that his father did not leave him any money, showing him to have been extremely selfish and uncaring. In the end, however, he decided to help the people who needed his help.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Father">Father</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Father">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Father"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>In the fond hope, my son, that you will never need it.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;His father's note that was left in the Hopping Pot.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>The Wizard had a difficult relationship with his father as they had two different views of how things are. The son thought that those who could not do <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> were worthless, a thought shared by wizard fanatics some years later when Muggle persecution of wizards and witches began.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When his father only left him an old cooking pot and a slipper, he cursed his father's age-softened mind as he had expected gold. However, after he was given the taste of the Muggles' misery, his conscience awoke and he agreed to use his magic to help his Muggle neighbours as his father did before him.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Muggle_neighbours">Muggle neighbours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Muggle neighbours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Muggle neighbours"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>A kind old wizard decides to teach his son a lesson by giving him the taste of the local Muggles' misery. The young wizard's conscience awakes and he agreed to use his magic for the benefit of his non-magical neighbours.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s notes.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The Wizard felt that muggles were worthless, and thus he refused to help them, when they asked him for help. Unlike his father, who was a good person who always helped people, the wizard turned away anybody who sought his help. In the end, the wizard was given a taste of the muggles' misery, and started to use his magic in order to help them.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
