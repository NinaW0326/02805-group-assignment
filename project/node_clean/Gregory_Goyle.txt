<p><b>Gregory Goyle</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>/<a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> in the same year as <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. He was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>. Goyle was the son of <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle%27s_father" class="mw-redirect" title="Gregory Goyle&#39;s father">a Death Eater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle%27s_mother" title="Gregory Goyle&#39;s mother">his wife</a>. It is unknown if he was a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> as well.
</p><p>Goyle, along with fellow Slytherin <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a>, seemed to already be best friends with <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> before their first year at Hogwarts. Both wizards were shown to be more lackeys than friends, often following Malfoy around and doing his bidding.
</p>

<h3><span id="Early_life_(1980-1991)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_.281980-1991.29">Early life <span class="template-c">(1980-1991)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life (1980-1991)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life (1980-1991)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gregory Goyle was born into a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> magical family in late 1979 or early 1980. His father, <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle%27s_father" class="mw-redirect" title="Gregory Goyle&#39;s father">Mr Goyle</a>, was a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> and a loyal supporter of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> until his downfall in 1981. Several Death Eaters avoided imprisonment in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> by claiming that the Dark Lord had controlled them with the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a>. It is likely that Mr Goyle did this.
</p><p>Due to all of their fathers being Death Eaters, Goyle probably knew <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a> before his first year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Like many new students, he might have purchased his wand from <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders</a> in <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="First_year">First year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">

<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Goyle started his education at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>. He, Draco, and Crabbe met <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and offered him acceptance into their clan. Harry rejected this offer and left Draco Malfoy, the leader of the group, confused as to why he would choose to be friends with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> instead. <p>When he arrived at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, he was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into the House of <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> with his friends Draco Malfoy and Vincent Crabbe. Throughout the year, Goyle was mean to Harry Potter and Ron Weasley and at the end of the year final exam results were to be given out and Harry Potter (who disliked Goyle) hoped that he was as stupid as he was mean and would fail his exams. However, Goyle just managed to scrape through them and passed his first year of education.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<dd></dd></dl></div><p>In his <a href="/wiki/Second_year" title="Second year">second year</a> of education, the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> was opened for the second time, having opened 50 years previously when <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> was in education. <p>In their seventh year, Goyle and Crabbe were among the favoured students of the new <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> professors <a href="/wiki/Alecto_and_Amycus_Carrow" title="Alecto and Amycus Carrow">Alecto and Amycus Carrow</a>. </p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Goyle, Crabbe, and Malfoy did not flee with the other <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherins</a>; instead, they tried to ambush <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> in the Room of Requirement. In the ensuing fight, Goyle was less aggressive than Crabbe, who defied Draco's orders and tried multiple dark curses. Goyle was <a href="/wiki/Disarming_Charm" title="Disarming Charm">disarmed</a> by Harry and then <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">stunned</a> by Hermione. When Crabbe's <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">cursed fire</a> got out of control, Goyle was saved by Ron and Hermione, who flew him out on their broom while Harry saved Draco. Crabbe was engulfed by the flames and killed. Harry and his friends then left Draco and Goyle to mourn Crabbe.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Later_life_(post_1998)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_.28post_1998.29">Later life <span class="template-c">(post 1998)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life (post 1998)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Later life (post 1998)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Very little is known of what happened to Goyle after the Second Wizarding War or his later life, or if he even survived the Battle of Hogwarts and thus the Second Wizarding War. If he did survive, his friendship with Draco apparently ended sometime after they completed school. It is even possible that he was arrested, tried and sent to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> for using the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> on other students at Hogwarts, along with all other <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> and their allies. 
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>, while talking with Harry at the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, Draco revealed that he was always jealous about Harry having Ron and Hermione as friends while he had Goyle and Crabbe.<sup id="cite_ref-CC_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Goyle was generally less intelligent than Crabbe, always causing trouble and nearly failing his exams in his <a href="/wiki/1991-1992_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1991-1992 school year">first year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was unable to give <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> a simple answer after being questioned about <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>'s lessons, only being able to grin foolishly.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In his <a href="/wiki/1995-1996_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1995-1996 school year">fifth year</a> Goyle (along with Crabbe) failed his <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L</a>., showing his weak academic ability and overall laziness.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>I’ll make Goyle do lines, it’ll kill him, he hates writing. I... must... not... look... like... a... <a href="/wiki/Baboon" title="Baboon">baboon</a>’s... backside....</i>"</span></dd>

<p>Goyle seemed to lack intelligence, <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> talent, and independent thought, thus used only his size and strength to bully other students or scare off anyone who threatened Malfoy. Goyle was also very sadistic and had no qualms about using the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> to torture fellow students.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> However, while he did poorly in Defence Against the Dark Arts,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> he excelled in the actual <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts</a> class, being top of the class due to his sadistic tendency to use fellow students as practise subjects for curses.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Goyle often followed Malfoy around.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Simply put, he was nothing more than his lackey. However, Draco showed concern for him sometimes; he immediately reported Goyle's injury to Snape in their fourth year.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> When Crabbe's <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">Fiendfyre</a> destroyed the Room of Requirement, Draco went out of his way to save Goyle from the flames, dragging him to safety.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Harry, Ron, and Hermione disliked Goyle greatly. He was also one of their four most disliked people at Hogwarts, even going as far as expressing their hope that Goyle might fail his exams in his first year and be forced to leave the school.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, Ron and Hermione did assist Draco in saving Goyle's life during the Battle of Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?veaction=edit&amp;section=20" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle?action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregory" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Gregory">Gregory</a> is derived from Greek meaning "watchful, alert". It is also associated with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grigori" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Grigori">Grigori</a>, "the Watchers", a group of fallen angels who mated with mortal women to spawn a race of giants. This may allude to Goyle frequently acting like a bodyguard to Draco Malfoy and to his large frame.</li>
<li>His last name is probably from "gargoyle," small monsters used to decorate buildings. The Gargouille also was a legendary water monster living in the River Seine in Paris, France. Gargoyles served as gutters/downspouts on gothic buildings. The word Gargoyle is derived from the French gargouille and the Latin <i>gargula</i> which translate to <i>throat </i>in contemporary English. Related words include gargle and gurgle.</li>
<li>Interestingly, gargoyles' supposed jobs are to watch over the building they are perched on. They are portrayed as grim and monstrous characters.</li></ul>

<ul><li>Goyle is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Josh_Herdman" title="Josh Herdman">Josh Herdman</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptions</a> of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_books" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter books"><i>Harry Potter</i></a>, making him one of fourteen characters to appear in all eight.</li>
<li>In the books, Goyle is generally portrayed as being less intelligent than Crabbe, but in the films it seems to be the other way around.</li>
<li>In the the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, Goyle was absent during the scene at Hogsmeade, being replaced by the original character <a href="/wiki/Pike" title="Pike">Pike</a>. This was due to Josh Herdman being incapable of performing such a scene due to a then-recent injury.</li>
<li>Goyle is among the cast of characters who appear in all eight films, though he hardly has any lines at all.</li></ul>

<ul><li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, Goyle takes the place of Crabbe, who was written out due to drug charges against <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Waylett" title="Jamie Waylett">Jamie Waylett</a>. Goyle dies in Crabbe's place; when Goyle (mirroring Crabbe in the book) tries to kill Hermione with the Killing Curse, Ron chases after him, and Goyle casts Fiendfyre in an attempt to kill him, after which (Goyle) attempts to climb to safety with Malfoy and <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Zabini" title="Blaise Zabini">Blaise Zabini</a> (who plays roughly the same role as Goyle does in the book), but grabs onto a loose chair and falls into the fire. Also, at the beginning of the scene, when Harry asks Malfoy to turn back from Voldemort, Goyle insists that he stay on Voldemort's side. In addition, it is not Goyle who destroys the diadem, but Harry, who stabs it with a Basilisk Fang before Ron kicks it into the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>, Goyle is portrayed as being more intelligent (and even supportive of Harry at times); when Malfoy and Crabbe confront Harry after <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a> gets petrified, Goyle is disapproving of their behaviour, and at the end of the same school year, he cheers for Harry until Malfoy knocks him out. Something similar happens in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, where Malfoy simply pulls <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a> back down when about to cheer for Hagrid.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>, unlike the film, Goyle doesn't grab onto a loose chair and climbs to safety successfully like Draco and Blaise. However, when Harry and Ron rescue Draco and Blaise, Hermione is unable to reach Goyle, who remains standing on the high ground until the Fiendfyre charges at him, killing Goyle offscreen. Contrary to canon, Goyle's in-game character is unable to perform <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">dark magic</a>.</li>
<li>Gregory Goyle's LEGO minifigure in set 4735 features Harry Potter's face on the flip side to show that it is actually Harry, disguised by Polyjuice Potion.<sup id="cite_ref-lhpcharacters_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lhpcharacters-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
