<b>Ivor Dillonsby</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> interviewed by <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> for her book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></i>. In his interview, Dillonbsy claimed to have discovered eight of the twelve uses of <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Dragon_blood" title="Dragon blood">blood</a> before <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a>, and accused Dumbledore of stealing his papers.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He also expressed the opinion that <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot" title="Bathilda Bagshot">Bathilda Bagshot</a> had gone senile, remarking of her, "<i>The fire's lit, but the <a href="/wiki/Cauldron" title="Cauldron">cauldron</a>'s empty.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ivor_Dillonsby?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ivor_Dillonsby?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Ivor" is from the Old Norse name <i>√çvarr</i>, which was derived from the elements <i>yr</i> "yew, bow" and <i>arr</i> "warrior". During the Middle Ages it was brought to Britain by Scandinavian settlers and invaders, and it was adopted in Ireland, Scotland and Wales.
</p>
