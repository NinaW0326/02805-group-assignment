The Right Honourable <b>Sir Thomas More</b>, also known by Catholics as <b>Saint Thomas More</b> (<a href="/wiki/7_February" title="7 February">7 February</a>, <a href="/wiki/1478" class="mw-redirect" title="1478">1478</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/6_July" title="6 July">6 July</a>, <a href="/wiki/1535" class="mw-redirect" title="1535">1535</a>), was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who acted as a lawyer, social philosopher, author, statesman and noted Renaissance humanist within the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> world. He was an important counsellor to <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII" title="Henry VIII">King Henry VIII of England</a>, and for three years toward the end of his life, he was Lord Chancellor.
</p><p>He was executed for high treason after denying the validity of the Act of Succession. By the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, there was a portrait of More hung at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSps1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSps1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
