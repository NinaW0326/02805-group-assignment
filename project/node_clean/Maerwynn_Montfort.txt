<p><b>Maerwynn Montfort</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and author whose books were sold at <a href="/wiki/Flourish_and_Blotts" title="Flourish and Blotts">Flourish and Blotts</a>. By the <a href="/wiki/1980s" title="1980s">1980s</a>, Montfort had been largely forgotten.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5.10_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5.10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Villanelle" title="Villanelle">Madam Villanelle</a> told <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> that their brother <a href="/wiki/Jacob_(Hogwarts_student)" title="Jacob (Hogwarts student)">Jacob</a> had been very interested in Montfort's works before his disappearance. She remarked how Jacob was one of her favourite customers as she found it nice to talk to someone else who had also read those ancient texts.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5.10_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5.10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Maerwynn_Montfort?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Maerwynn_Montfort?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Maerwynn is derived from two old English elements: "maer", meaning "famous", "renowned", or "one who Illuminates" and "wynn", meaning "friend," "white," or "fair." It is the name of a <a href="/wiki/First_millennium_A.D." title="First millennium A.D.">10th century</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%A6rwynn" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Mærwynn">nun and saint</a> who served as a foster mother to Edgar the Peaceable's daughter Ælfflæd.
</p><p>The surname "Montfort" is of Norman origin, coming from Old French mont 'hill' (see Mont 1) + fort 'strong', 'impregnable' (Latin fortis). It is also a habitational name from any of the numerous places called Montfort,
</p>
