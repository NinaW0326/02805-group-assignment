<b>Hereward</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who mastered the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">Elder Wand</a> after killing his own <a href="/wiki/Godelot" title="Godelot">father</a> to acquire it.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He succeeded in doing this by locking him in his own cellar to die, after stealing <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">his wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It's unknown what Hereward did after obtaining the wand. He ultimately lost possession of the Elder Wand at some point, presumably by being defeated in a duel, killed, or disarmed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hereward?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hereward?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Hereward" is derived from the Old English elements <i>here</i> "army" and <i>weard</i> "guard", from which the magial kind of "ward" (as a protection) is also derived. This was the name of an 11th-century Anglo-Saxon leader who rebelled against Norman rule.
</p>
