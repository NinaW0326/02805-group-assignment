<b>Mr Goldstein</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who lived in the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb44_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb44-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was the father of <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Porpentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Queenie_Goldstein" title="Queenie Goldstein">Queenie Goldstein</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb44_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb44-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He and <a href="/wiki/Goldstein_sisters%27_mother" title="Goldstein sisters&#39; mother">his wife</a> died of <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Pox" title="Dragon Pox">Dragon Pox</a> when their daughters were young.<sup id="cite_ref-fb44_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb44-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This left the girls to "raise each other," which lead them to forge a life-long bond.<sup id="cite_ref-fb44_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb44-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-collider_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-collider-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pottermorevideo_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorevideo-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After being sentenced to <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> in <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Tina Goldstein had her happy <a href="/wiki/Memory" title="Memory">memories</a> extracted from her head by <a href="/wiki/Bernadette" title="Bernadette">Bernadette</a>, who then cast them into the <a href="/wiki/Death_potion" title="Death potion">death potion</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Death_Cell" title="Death Cell">Death Cell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb67_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb67-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> To keep Tina sedated, the potion replayed the memories for her, which included "happy images" of her mother and father.<sup id="cite_ref-fb67_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb67-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fb69_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb69-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
