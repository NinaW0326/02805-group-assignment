<b>Alistair Ballcocke</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> whose name was carved into a wood panel on the wall of Professor <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Trelawney's</a> <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> <a href="/wiki/Divination_Classroom" title="Divination Classroom">classroom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alistair_Ballcocke?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alistair_Ballcocke?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Ballcocke</i> is an alternate spelling of the English surname <i>Balcock</i>, which derives from the diminutive suffix -<i>cock</i> combined with either <i>Bald</i>, a pet form of the Germanic male given name <i>Baldwin</i>, or the Old English <i>beald</i> ("brave," "bold").<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A ballcock is a mechanism for filling water tanks (such as in flush toilets) designed to prevent overflow and backflow.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alistair is the anglicised form of Alasdair. Alasdair is the Scottish form of Alexander, which means "defending men".
</p>
