<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She and her husband met at <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons Academy of Magic</a> in their youth.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Perenelle and her husband drank the <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a> produced by the stone, and were <a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">immortal</a>, until the stone was destroyed in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, after which she and Nicolas would later die. She was at least six hundred and eighty three years old at the time of her death.<sup id="cite_ref-birthdate_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthdate-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She may have had a friendship with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> like her husband. Having lived for so long, both Perenelle and her husband were glad to finally embrace death, which to them was like "going to bed after a very long day".<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nicolas_Flamel">Nicolas Flamel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Nicolas Flamel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nicolas Flamel"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Perenelle Flamel was the wife of <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel" title="Nicolas Flamel">Nicolas Flamel</a>. She, along with her husband, drank the <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a> created by the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a> making her <a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">immortal</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel" title="Nicolas Flamel">Nicolas</a> and Perenelle lived over 600 years happily together.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a> was nearly stolen by Lord Voldemort, the couple agreed that it should be destroyed. Despite their immortality, they were not afraid of dying and they both died thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Perenelle</i> is the Old French form on the name <i>Petronilla</i>, a diminutive of the name <i>Petronia</i>, which is the feminine form of <i>Petronius</i> - a Roman family name which is possibly derived from Latin petro, petronis "yokel".
</p><p>Petro-, of both Latin and Greek origin, is a prefix meaning "of or pertaining to stone."
</p><p>The name <i>Flamel</i> means in Old French "flame". Such meaning can be associated to Flamel's work as an alchemist.
</p>
