<b>Wendy Slinkhard</b> was a British <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who wrote at least one book.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> While at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, she studied under <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, and was selected to join the <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a>. Slughorn kept a picture of the two, showing Wendy signing a copy of her book to Slughorn.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wendy may be related to <a href="/wiki/Wilbert_Slinkhard" title="Wilbert Slinkhard">Wilbert Slinkhard</a>, the author of <i><a href="/wiki/Defensive_Magical_Theory" title="Defensive Magical Theory">Defensive Magical Theory</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wendy_Slinkhard?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wendy_Slinkhard?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Wendy appears only in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i> and is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Nina_Aimer_Fox" title="Nina Aimer Fox">Nina Aimer Fox</a>.</li>
<li>It is possible, as a former <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a> member, she attended the <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club_Christmas_party" title="Slug Club Christmas party">Christmas party</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wendy_Slinkhard?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wendy_Slinkhard?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the case of the character from J. M. Barrie's play <i>Peter Pan</i> (1904), the name "Wendy" was created from the nickname fwendy "friend", given to the author by a young friend. However, the name was used prior to the play (rarely), in which case it could be related to the Welsh name Gwendolen and other names beginning with the element gwen meaning "white, fair, blessed".
</p>
