<p><b>Professor Galatea Merrythought</b> was a British <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from around <a href="/wiki/1895" class="mw-redirect" title="1895">1895</a> until her retirement in <a href="/wiki/1945" title="1945">1945</a>. From some point between the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1940s" title="1940s">1940s</a>, she taught <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>. 
</p><p>Before her retirement, she worked alongside <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, her former students; after her retirement, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>, another one of her pupils, was refused the chance to take her place, and so he vengefully <a href="/wiki/Jinx_on_the_post_of_Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts_teacher" title="Jinx on the post of Defence Against the Dark Arts teacher">jinxed the post</a> so no professor could hold the occupation for longer than a year.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nothing is known of Galatea Merrythought's past, although it is likely that she attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and would have purchased her <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> from <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders Wand Shop</a> at <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a> at the age of eleven. She would have graduated at the age of seventeen and it is likely that she had high <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> marks — almost definitely a <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a> in the field.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_at_Hogwarts">Career at Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career at Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career at Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;http&#58;//www.accio-quote.org/articles/2005/0705-tlc_mugglenet-anelli-3.htm/">"<i>Dumbledore, in his time had access to superb teachers at Hogwarts…</i>"</span></dd>

<p>Merrythought returned to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> to teach sometime around <a href="/wiki/1895" class="mw-redirect" title="1895">1895</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> though what subject is unclear. Among her pupils was a young <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> named <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>; other students she taught included <a href="/wiki/Elphias_Doge" title="Elphias Doge">Elphias Doge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> during her early years was presumably <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Phineas_Nigellus_Black" title="Phineas Nigellus Black">Phineas Nigellus Black</a>, although she also went on to work under <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet" title="Armando Dippet">Armando Dippet</a>.
</p><p>Having authored at least two books on the subject (<i><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts_(Book)" class="mw-redirect" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts (Book)">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Advanced_Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Advanced Defence Against the Dark Arts">Advanced Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></i>, required reading when <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> was teaching the subject in the 1910s),<sup id="cite_ref-MinaLima_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MinaLima-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> at an unknown point in her teaching career, certainly between 1927 and 1945, Professor Merrythought became the <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, taking ownership of <a href="/wiki/Classroom_3C" title="Classroom 3C">Classroom 3C</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn%27s_second_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Horace Slughorn&#39;s second office">an office on the sixth floor</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Chamber_of_Secrets_and_retirement">The Chamber of Secrets and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Chamber of Secrets and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Chamber of Secrets and retirement"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">"<i>Sir? Is it true that Professor Merrythought is retiring?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> questioning <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Potions_Master" title="Potions Master">Potions Master</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Many years later, when <a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet" title="Armando Dippet">Professor Dippet</a> was Headmaster, Galatea became the teacher of a brilliant student named <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>. Her fellow colleagues, and possibly Galatea herself, had been impressed by Riddle's skills and determination, and taught him all they knew. During her fifth year of teaching Riddle, the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> was opened and many of her students were petrified. Eventually, a third-year named <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> was accused of releasing <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Slytherin's monster</a> from the Chamber and he was expelled. It was during this year that Galatea decided to retire, whether it was because of these events or not, and she left the school the following year.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Professor Merrythought was an expert in fighting the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>, having been a highly respected Professor of this subject at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> for many years. She authored numerous books in the field, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts_(book)" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts (book)">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Advanced_Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Advanced Defence Against the Dark Arts">Advanced Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></i>, meaning she was highly knowledgeable and accomplished in this branch of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The surname Merrythought could imply that she was adept at conjuring the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>, which would fit with her having taught Defence Against the Dark Arts at Hogwarts.</li>
<li>Her given name Galatea may well be derived from Greek mythology. In Ancient Greek, the root forms would be <i>gala(-ktos)</i>, meaning "milk", and <i>-tea</i>, "white". There are three notable women in Greek mythology named Galatea:
<ol><li>Galatea of Sicily, one of the fifty <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nereid">Nereids</a>  (daughters of Nereus and Doris), was courted by both Polyphemus and Acis.</li>
<li>Galatea of Cyprus was the wife of King Pygmalion. Pygmalion was famous for having created a statue of Aphrodite, the Goddess of Love, that the goddess brought to life at his request. After receiving the gift of life, Galatea married the king.</li>
<li>Galatea was the name of the wife of King Lamprus. Galatea prayed to the Greek goddess Leto to transform her daughter into a son so she would not have to sacrifice her according to the command of her husband. Leto granted Galatea's request.</li></ol></li></ul>
