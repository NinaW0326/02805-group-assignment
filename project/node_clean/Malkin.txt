<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosophers Stone">"<i>Hogwarts, dear? Got the lot here -- another young man being fitted up just now, in fact.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Malkin graciously greeting Harry Potter into her shop in 1991<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosophers_Stone" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter and the Philosophers Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Malkin became the proprietor of <a href="/wiki/Madam_Malkin%27s_Robes_for_All_Occasions" title="Madam Malkin&#39;s Robes for All Occasions">Madam Malkin's Robes for All Occasions</a> sometime before <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>. In <a href="/wiki/August" title="August">August</a> 1991, when <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> first visited <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a> with <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> to purchase his school supplies for his first year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, Malkin greeted him kindly and found some robes to fit him. It was then, that Harry first met his long-term adversary <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> in her shop, who was also buying his school robes.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, Madam Malkin went to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Which_Witch%3F" title="Which Witch?">Which Witch?</a> fashion show, and left her shop closed with a notice outside.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, Harry Potter returned to her shop when shopping in Diagon Alley, to purchase new school robes for the <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%931994_school_year" title="1993–1994 school year">upcoming academic year</a>, along with other needed school supplies from other shops.<sup id="cite_ref-POA4_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, Madam Malkin had a sale in her shop, for which an advertisement was published in the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP14_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP14-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Malkin was unsuccessfully trying to fit new robes on a particularly disgruntled <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>, who was in the shop with his mother <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>. When Harry Potter, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> entered her shop to purchase their own robes, Draco nastily insulted Granger as a "<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudblood</a>", much to her shock and disgust for such inflammatory language being used in her shop. Whilst the <a href="/wiki/Trio" title="Trio">trio</a> argued bitterly and traded insults with the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoys</a>, Malkin became very anxious of the escalating tension, and even tried to tweak the robe he was trying on, but ended up accidentally pinching him with her needles. Malfoy and his mother then left the shop in contempt, with Narcissa Malfoy disdainfully telling her she would rather do business with rival tailors <a href="/wiki/Twilfitt_and_Tattings" title="Twilfitt and Tattings">Twilfitt and Tattings</a>, because of the "scum" that she served, leaving Malkin highly insulted. She picked up all the robes which had fallen to the floor, cleaned them with a wave of her wand, and fitted and sold robes to the trio. She seemed to be relieved when everyone had left, as it meant no more confrontation in her shop.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP6_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP6-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is unknown what happened to Malkin during the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, although it is possible she survived it, as she was not mentioned to have died at any point during the war.  
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Madam Malkin was described as being a squat, smiling witch who was dressed in mauve clothing.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>I don’t think there’s any need for language like that! And I don’t want wands drawn in my shop either!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Malkin's hatred of poor behaviour in her establishment<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Madam Malkin was generally friendly, helpful and personable towards her customers,<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> as well as fair; most notably, she scolded both Draco Malfoy for using the highly insulting term '<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudblood</a>' in her presence despite him being a wealthy customer, and Harry Potter and Ron Weasley for drawing their <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> in response. However, she was not above being irritated or offended, as she was when Narcissa Malfoy implied her shop was of lower class for catering to <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a>, and could become very easily distracted if in this mood. She was also shown to care for the welfare of her customers, as she was greeted Harry Potter generously into her shop in 1991 and was very willing with their purchases, and even reminded Draco to be careful when wandering around Diagon Alley.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP6_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP6-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>Snatching up the fallen robes and moving the tip of her wand over them like a vacuum cleaner, so that it removed all the dust.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Malkin performing non-verbal magic<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Malkin could successfully use a <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_cleaner_spell" title="Vacuum cleaner spell">cleaning charm</a> which removed dust from objects like a <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_cleaner" title="Vacuum cleaner">Vacuum cleaner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP6_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP6-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Malkin was capable of conjuring at least a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> an incredibly advanced and powerful piece of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Nonverbal magic</a></b>: Malkin was able to instantly clean up dust which had gathered on several robes which had fallen on the floor merely by running the tip of her wand across them in 1996,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP6_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP6-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> meaning she could cast <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a> without speaking their <a href="/wiki/Incantation" title="Incantation">incantations</a> aloud, which is highly advanced magic.</li>
<li><b>Business</b>: Malkin was evidently an accomplished and successful businesswoman who managed her own shop <a href="/wiki/Madam_Malkin%27s_Robes_for_All_Occasions" title="Madam Malkin&#39;s Robes for All Occasions">Madam Malkin's Robes for All Occasions</a> for many years, and presumably earned a comfortable living from her profits.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Possessions">Possessions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Possessions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Possessions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">Wand</a></b>: Madam Malkin owned a wand, of unknown length, <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">core</a> materials.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP6_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP6-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Magical_tape_measure" title="Magical tape measure">Magical tape measure</a></b>: Madam Malkin owned a measuring device capable of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magically</a> moving on its own accord, in order to measure the necessary clothing lengths of her customers.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Madam_Malkin%27s_Robes_for_All_Occasions" title="Madam Malkin&#39;s Robes for All Occasions">Madam Malkin's Robes for All Occasions</a></b>: Madam Malkin was the owner of her own shop in <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>, which sold dress robes, travelling cloaks and even <a href="/wiki/Invisibility_Cloak" class="mw-redirect" title="Invisibility Cloak">Invisibility Cloaks</a> for <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a>, along with school uniforms for <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> students.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Harp" title="Harp">Self-playing harp</a></b>: Madam Malkin owned an <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">enchanted</a> harp, that could play by itself.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malkin?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Malkin" may be derived from "Malkin Tower" which was a key element in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pendle_witches" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Pendle witches">Pendle witches</a> trial.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Graymalkin was the name of a <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cat</a> that belonged to one of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Witches" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Three Witches">Three Witches</a> (the Weird Sisters) in <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macbeth" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Macbeth">Macbeth</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A malkin is also an old name for a <a href="/wiki/Scarecrow" title="Scarecrow">scarecrow</a>, dressed to frighten away <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. Malkin-trash was someone who was poorly or frighteningly dressed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It could also be of early medieval English origin, deriving from a female given name first recorded as "Malkyn" in 1297, a diminutive form of "Malle", itself a nickname form of "Mary". To Malle has been added the diminutive suffix "kyn or kin", meaning "close relative of". "Mary" was an extremely popular medieval female given name, being the claimed name of the mother of Christ in the New Testament. The original name is thought to have been derived from the Aramaic "Maryan", translating as the "wished-for-child", but others claim that it means "the star of the sea".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
