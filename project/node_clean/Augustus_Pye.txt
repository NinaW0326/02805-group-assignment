<b>Augustus Pye</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and trainee <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a> at <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a> in the Dai Llewellyn Ward.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP22_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP22-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was interested in <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> healing techniques, and used <a href="/wiki/Stitches" title="Stitches">stitches</a> on <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> when he was admitted to the hospital after he was <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Arthur_Weasley" title="Attack on Arthur Weasley">attacked</a> by <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>. Presumably, <a href="/wiki/Nagini%27s_venom" title="Nagini&#39;s venom">Nagini's venom</a> made Arthur's wounds impossible to heal in this manner.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP23_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a>, Pye taught trainee <a href="/wiki/Magizoologist" title="Magizoologist">Magizoologists</a> how to master the <a href="/wiki/Stamina_Charm" title="Stamina Charm">Stamina Charm</a>, to help them restore stamina to themselves in combat.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Pye?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Pye?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Augustus" means "great" or "venerable", derived from Latin augere "to increase". Augustus was the title given to Octavian, the first Roman emperor. He was the adopted son of Julius Caesar who rose to power through a combination of military skill and political prowess. This was also the name of three kings of Poland.
</p>
