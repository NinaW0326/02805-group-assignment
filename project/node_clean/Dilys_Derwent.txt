<b>Professor Dilys Derwent</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/1768" class="mw-redirect" title="1768">1768</a><sup id="cite_ref-OP22_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP22-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who worked as a <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a> at <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a> and was a celebrated <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmistress</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Her <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> hangs not only in the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's Office</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster_portraits" title="Headmaster portraits">portraits of previous headmasters and headmistresses</a>, but also in <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a>' main admitting area.<sup id="cite_ref-OP22_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP22-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_a_Healer_and_Headmistress">As a Healer and Headmistress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: As a Healer and Headmistress">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: As a Healer and Headmistress"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Derwent started working at <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a> in <a href="/wiki/1722" class="mw-redirect" title="1722">1722</a>.
</p><p>Derwent left the job in <a href="/wiki/1741" class="mw-redirect" title="1741">1741</a> and became the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmistress</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> that same year. She proved very popular among students and teachers, gaining her the status of one of the "most celebrated <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Heads</a>" among Dumbledore and Everard. She died in the post in 1768.<sup id="cite_ref-OP22_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP22-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-mortem">Post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> was attacked by Voldemort's snake <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a> in late <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, then-<a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a>, asked Derwent to go to her portrait at <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a>'s main admitting area in order to keep Dumbledore apprised on Mr Weasley's condition.<sup id="cite_ref-OP22_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP22-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> went to the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's office</a>, shortly after <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s defeat and the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Dilys Derwent joined the ovation made by the previous Hogwarts Headmasters, sobbing unashamedly.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dilys_Derwent?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Dilys" means "genuine" in Welsh. Derwent may be from the name of an English lake in Cumbria, in the Lake District called "Derwent Water".
</p>
