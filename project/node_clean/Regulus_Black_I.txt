<b>Regulus Black</b> (<a href="/wiki/1906" title="1906">1906</a> – <a href="/wiki/1959" title="1959">1959</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> wizard, the youngest child of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black_II" title="Sirius Black II">Sirius Black II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hesper_Gamp" title="Hesper Gamp">Hesper Gamp</a> and brother of <a href="/wiki/Arcturus_Black_III" title="Arcturus Black III">Arcturus Black III</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lycoris_Black" title="Lycoris Black">Lycoris</a>. Regulus never married and never had children.<sup id="cite_ref-BFT_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black_I?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black_I?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Based on a statement by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, Regulus may have been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>. However, he attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> c. <a href="/wiki/1917" title="1917">1917</a> - <a href="/wiki/1924" title="1924">1924</a>, when Slughorn may not have been Potions master.</li>
<li>Regulus was the granduncle of <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">another Black called Regulus</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black_I?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black_I?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Members of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">the Black family</a> and their history seem to have made a tradition of naming their children after stars and constellations. In Latin, <i>regulus</i> means "little king" and was one term for a <a href="/wiki/Basilisk" title="Basilisk">basilisk</a>. As a name, it has also been applied to the star <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Regulus">Alpha Leonis</a>, which is situated at the heart of the lion in the constellation <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_(constellation)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Leo (constellation)">Leo</a>. Regulus was also the family name of a Roman general, Marcus Atilius Regulus, who was known for his honour and self-sacrifice.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
