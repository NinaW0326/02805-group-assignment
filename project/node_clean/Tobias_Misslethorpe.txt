<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tobias_Misslethorpe?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tobias_Misslethorpe?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During his lifetime, Misslethorpe founded <i><a href="/wiki/Witch_Weekly_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Witch Weekly Magazine">Witch Weekly</a></i>, a popular magazine for <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a>, serving as its first editor.<sup id="cite_ref-portrait_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-portrait-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tobias_Misslethorpe?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tobias_Misslethorpe?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Misslethorpe died sometime in or prior to <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-death_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-death-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> of him, painted by the artist <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cartwright" title="Oliver Cartwright">Oliver Cartwright</a>, was hung at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-portrait_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-portrait-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tobias_Misslethorpe?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tobias_Misslethorpe?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Tobias" is the Greek form of Tobiah. This is the name of the hero of the apocryphal Book of Tobit in many English versions of the Old Testament. It relates how Tobias, with the help of the archangel Raphael, is able to drive away a demon who has been plaguing Sarah, who subsequently becomes his wife. It is also known that he shares the same first name as that of <a href="/wiki/Tobias_Snape" title="Tobias Snape">Tobias Snape</a>, who is the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> father of <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>.
</p>
