<b>Erich Ludendorff</b> (<a href="/wiki/9_April" title="9 April">9 April</a> <a href="/wiki/1865" class="mw-redirect" title="1865">1865</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/20_December" title="20 December">20 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1937" title="1937">1937</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> general during the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He lead the German war effort during the latter part of the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>, and afterward became a prominent nationalist figure, engaging in an unsuccessful coup with Adolf Hitler in <a href="/wiki/1923" title="1923">1923</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> interview with the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Chronicle" title="New York Chronicle">New York Chronicle</a></i>, former <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> prime minister <a href="/wiki/Georges_Clemenceau" title="Georges Clemenceau">Georges Clemenceau</a> revealed some of the things he planned to discuss in a talk scheduled for that evening, including what he saw as the post-war emergence of a "new triple alliance" that threatened the <a href="/wiki/Allies" title="Allies">Allied powers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> According to him, this alliance consisted of "<a href="/wiki/Kemalism" title="Kemalism">Kemalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, the reactionary Germany of Ludendorf [sic], and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">Soviets</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
