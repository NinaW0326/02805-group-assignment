<b>Mopsy Fleabert</b><sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was an eccentric elderly <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who lived at the edge of <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a>. She was especially fond of <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dogs</a>, and kept several, much to the chagrin of her neighbours.<sup id="cite_ref-jkrofficial_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkrofficial-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mopsy wrote a series of books on <a href="/wiki/Animal_ghost" title="Animal ghost">animal ghosts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A copy of one of the books in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_Ghosts_of_Britain" title="Animal Ghosts of Britain">Animal Ghosts of Britain</a></i>, was kept on the mantle in <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mopsy_Fleabert?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mopsy_Fleabert?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has stated that she initially wished to include a dog-lover in the story to contrast with the cat-loving <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a>. Mopsy would take <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> into her home while he was on the run, thinking he was a stray dog. However, Rowling's editor asked her to cut Mopsy out of an early draft of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>. Rowling chose to have fugitive Sirius stay inside a cave instead, where he could freely discuss important matters with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a> without Mopsy's interference.<sup id="cite_ref-jkrofficial_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkrofficial-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>It is never explicitly stated that Mopsy the dog lover from <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling%27s_official_site" title="J. K. Rowling&#39;s official site">J. K. Rowling's official site</a> and Mopsy Fleabert the author of <i><a href="/wiki/Animal_Ghosts_of_Britain" title="Animal Ghosts of Britain">Animal Ghosts of Britain</a></i> from  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> are the same individual; however, given the unique name with a tie to their similar interests, it is believed here these both refer to the same individual.</li></ul>
