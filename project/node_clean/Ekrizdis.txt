<b>Ekrizdis</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">15th century</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a> of unknown nationality and the original inhabitant of the fortress of <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ekrizdis?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ekrizdis?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alone on Azkaban's island, which was at the time uncharted by both <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizardkind</a>, Ekrizdis practised the worst kinds of <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Magic.</a> He would often lure passing Muggle sailors to his fortress only to torture and murder them. He was believed to have <a href="/wiki/Insanity" title="Insanity">lost his sanity</a> during his years of solitude. After Ekrizdis's death, the <a href="/wiki/Concealment_Charm" title="Concealment Charm">Concealment Charms</a> he placed on the fortress and island faded, and the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>, suddenly aware of the island's existence, investigated. Other than the fact that the building was infested with <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>, investigators refused to discuss what other evidence of Ekrizdis's activities they found within Azkaban.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
