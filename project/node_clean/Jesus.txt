<b>Jesus</b>, also known as <b>Jesus of Nazareth</b> or <b>Jesus Christ</b>, was the central figure of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>, regarded by most Christian denominations as the son of <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a>, born of a human mother, <a href="/wiki/Mary" title="Mary">Mary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was considered an important prophet in <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was a stained glass window in the <a href="/wiki/St_Jerome%27s_Church" title="St Jerome&#39;s Church">St Jerome's Church</a> in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a> which depicted Jesus along with the authors of the four books of the Gospel (<a href="/wiki/Matthew" title="Matthew">Matthew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark" title="Mark">Mark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luke" title="Luke">Luke</a> and <a href="/wiki/John" title="John">John</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One of the known works by artist <a href="/wiki/Haniball_Carathi" title="Haniball Carathi">Haniball Carathi</a> was a "little piece" depicting "our Ladie" (i.e., Mary) with the presumably infant Jesus "upon her right hand."<sup id="cite_ref-lc_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lc-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A catalogue listing for this work was incorporated into the label of the <a href="/wiki/Belch_Powder" title="Belch Powder">Belch Powder</a> box.<sup id="cite_ref-lc_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lc-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
