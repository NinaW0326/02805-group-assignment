<b>Mark Constantine</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> entrepreneur and the co-founder of the <a href="/wiki/Lush" title="Lush">Lush</a> cosmetics company. In July of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a> <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Darling" title="Alistair Darling">Alistair Darling</a> announced "controversial changes" to the <a href="/wiki/Capital_gains_tax" title="Capital gains tax">capital gains tax</a>. Mark Constantine told <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> that the measure would alienate the business community that <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a>, as Chancellor, had fought to attract to the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party" title="Labour Party">Labour Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
