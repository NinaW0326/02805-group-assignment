<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Loch_Ness_Monster?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Loch_Ness_Monster?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its favourite shape is that of a <a href="/wiki/Sea_serpent" title="Sea serpent">Sea serpent</a>. Wizard observers from the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards" title="International Confederation of Wizards">International Confederation of Wizards</a> discovered it was a Kelpie when they observed it changing into an otter on the approach of a team of <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> investigators, then back into a Sea Serpent. The <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Misinformation" title="Office of Misinformation">Office of Misinformation</a> has worked diligently, convincing the Muggles that all photographic evidence of "Nessie" is actually fake.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> once offered the use of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lake" title="Great Lake">lake</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> to house the <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creature</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Loch_Ness_Monster?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Loch_Ness_Monster?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loch_Ness_Monster" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Loch Ness Monster">Loch Ness Monster</a> is a creature believed by some to live in Loch Ness, Scotland. Though it is most commonly identified as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plesiosauria" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Plesiosauria">plesiosaur</a>-like creature, sea serpent depictions are not uncommon either. The reference to the Office of Misinformation discounting photographic evidence may be in reference to the "sturgeon's photograph", which was once considered plausible evidence of the Loch Ness Monster's existence but is in more recent years believed to have been a hoax.</li>
<li>It is possible that the kelpie seen in <i><a href="/wiki/Wonderbook:_Book_of_Spells" title="Wonderbook: Book of Spells">Wonderbook: Book of Spells</a></i> is intended to be the Loch Ness Monster, as it is rather large, lives in a Scottish loch, and takes the form of what appears to be a sea serpent, rather than the horse form the species generally takes.</li></ul>
