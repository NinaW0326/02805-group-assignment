<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlus_Potter?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlus_Potter?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>Charlus</i> could refer back to the name <i>Charles</i>. It is derived from the Germanic <i>karl </i>or <i>hari</i>. It can mean "man", "army" or "warrior". This can relate to many members of the Potter family.</li>
<li>Potter is a very common surname of English origin. It traditionally refers to the occupation of making pottery. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beatrix_Potter" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Beatrix Potter">Beatrix Potter</a> was a well-known English children's author. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potter%27s_field" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Potter&#39;s field">Potter's field</a> is the name for a burial ground for the unknown or unclaimed dead, particularly soldiers and orphans. The Potter family had many members that fit that description. The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potter_Box" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Potter Box">Potter Box</a> is a model for making ethical decisions developed at Harvard University. J. K. Rowling has also commented that she has liked the name since childhood, as a family that had lived near her carried this surname.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlus_Potter?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlus_Potter?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>There was some speculation that Charlus Potter was the father of <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a> and the paternal grandfather of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. Later,  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> has revealed that <a href="/wiki/Fleamont_Potter" title="Fleamont Potter">Fleamont Potter</a>, not Charlus, is Harry's grandfather. In spite of this, it is still more than likely that Charlus was an uncle or cousin of James Potter and his descendants, or perhaps a more distant relative of <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Potter" title="Abraham Potter">Abraham Potter</a>.</li>
<li>Charlus was not Featured on the Potter family tree on Pottermore. So it is possible he is not closely related to any of the Potters that are featured on that tree.</li>
<li>Rowling has stated that Harry had no family remaining other than the Durselys at the time of Lord Voldemort's attack on Godric's Hallow, suggesting that Charlus, and any descendants he might have had otherwise, were dead by this time.</li></ul>
