<b>Pyrites</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and a servant of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> at around the time of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. He was a dandy wizard who often wore white silk gloves, which would sometimes be stained with <a href="/wiki/Blood" title="Blood">blood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jkr_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pyrites?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pyrites?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pyrites is named after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrite" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Pyrite">the mineral</a>, which is more commonly known as fool's gold.<sup id="cite_ref-jkr_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pyrites?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pyrites?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Pyrites only appears in early drafts of the first chapter <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>. In these drafts, he would have met up with <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> in front of the <a href="/wiki/Potters%27_Cottage" class="mw-redirect" title="Potters&#39; Cottage">Potter cottage</a>. He was cut, however, with the switch in perspective to <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">Vernon Dursley</a>'s day rather than the scene at <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jkr_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>It is likely he was an actual <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> as well.</li>
<li>Despite being cut from the series as a whole, it's possible that this character still exists as there is nothing to contradict his existence or lack thereof.</li>
<li>It's also possible that he took part in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, if so he was likely either one of the Death Eaters at <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a> or one of the Death Eaters to escape during the mass breakout of <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. Regardless, he was probably killed or arrested following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.</li>
<li>He may have been related to <a href="/wiki/Argo_Pyrites" title="Argo Pyrites">Argo Pyrites</a>, who authored <i><a href="/wiki/Alchemy,_Ancient_Art_and_Science" title="Alchemy, Ancient Art and Science">Alchemy, Ancient Art and Science</a></i>.</li></ul>
