<b>Barker</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard or witch</a> who co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a>-making company <a href="/wiki/Flyte_and_Barker" title="Flyte and Barker">Flyte and Barker</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Flyte" title="Flyte">Flyte</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Barker_(wizard)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Barker_(wizard)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The surname Barker derives from an English occupational name for a leather tanner, from Middle English <i>bark(en)</i>, "to tan."<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-surnamedb_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-surnamedb-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Tree bark was once used in the process of tanning leather.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-surnamedb_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-surnamedb-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It may also derive from an occupational name for a shepherd, from the Old French word <i>bercher</i>/<i>bercher</i>, "shepherd," from the Late <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>berbicarius</i>, from the Latin <i>berbex</i>, "ram."<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-surnamedb_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-surnamedb-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Barker" is an English word for a person whose job involves calling out to people walking by in the hope of soliciting customers for a particular business or attraction (such as at a carnival).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
