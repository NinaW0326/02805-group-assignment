The two<sup id="cite_ref-newyorker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newyorker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <b>children</b> of <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley Dursley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_wife" title="Dudley Dursley&#39;s wife">his wife</a> were <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jkr_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They would occasionally be visited by their <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a>&#160;second cousins, the two sons and daughter of their father's cousin <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.
</p><p>While the two of them played with <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_II" title="James Potter II">James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lily_Potter" title="Lily Potter">Lily</a>, the children's fathers, Harry and Dudley, would sit silently together as&#160;they were on <i>"Christmas Card"</i> terms.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_children?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_children?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Rowling once considered having Harry helping Dudley with a magical child of his own <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_Station" title="King&#39;s Cross Station">Platform 9 3/4</a> in the epilogue of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, but later changed her mind, stating that "any latent wizarding genes would never survive contact with <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">Uncle Vernon</a>’s DNA."<sup id="cite_ref-jkr_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>It is possible that Dudley raised his children to be better than he was during his childhood, given that the encounter with the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> made him change his ways.
<ul><li>It should be noted that Harry's children get along well with Dudley's own, supporting this.</li></ul></li></ul>
