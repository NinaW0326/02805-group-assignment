<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Merlin was born sometime during the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval era</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chocolate_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chocolate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> During his formative years, he attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is possible that he was taught by <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a> himself, given the time period Merlin lived in. It is said that Merlin's <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> was of <a href="/wiki/English_oak" title="English oak">English oak</a> (though his grave has never been found, so this cannot be proven).<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Either while or after he attended the school, Merlin studied the five secret rooms at the school that would one day come to be known as the fabled <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vaults</a> and, not only the purpose for their existence, but also the ancient magic surrounding them. Later on in his life, as an old man, a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> was painted by him, which he educated to speak and act like himself, and taught about the vaults so that once hung at Hogwarts, it could advise future residents about them, although its warnings were cryptic and, thus, of little use in regard to finding the vaults and breaking the curses.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At some point in his lifetime, Merlin became a part of the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_King_Arthur" title="Court of King Arthur">Court</a> of <a href="/wiki/Arthur" title="Arthur">King Arthur</a>, assisting him during his reign.<sup id="cite_ref-chocolate_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chocolate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is also widely believed by the wizarding community that he was friends with <a href="/wiki/Cadogan" title="Cadogan">Sir Cadogan</a>, which helped him secure a position on the <a href="/wiki/Knights_of_the_Round_Table" title="Knights of the Round Table">Knights of the Round Table</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was the enemy of Arthur's half-sister, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_le_Fay" title="Morgan le Fay">Morgan le Fay</a> (otherwise known as Morgana), a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">dark witch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-morganlefay_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-morganlefay-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Merlin believed that wizardkind should help <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and live peacefully with them. To this end, he founded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merlin_(organisation)" title="Order of Merlin (organisation)">Order of Merlin</a>, an organisation which promoted Muggle rights, creating rules against using <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> on them.<sup id="cite_ref-chocolate_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chocolate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Merlin was also an expert user of <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charms</a>. His exceptional skill earned him the epithet "Prince of Enchanters".<sup id="cite_ref-chocolate_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chocolate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At some point, either during Merlin's lifetime or after his death, the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merlin" title="Order of Merlin">Order of Merlin</a> switched from a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> rights organisation to an award bestowed upon witches and wizards who performed a great deed at personal risk or contributed to the betterment of wizarding society in some way, a sign of the estrangement of the Muggle community from the Wizarding one.
</p><p>Merlin's renown led his name to become a part of everyday <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_idioms" title="Wizarding idioms">wizarding vernacular</a>. The popular exclamation, "<i><a href="/wiki/Merlin%27s_beard" title="Merlin&#39;s beard">Merlin's beard</a>!</i>", and the more unusual "<i>Merlin's pants!</i>" are examples of such.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> students at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> took special pride in Merlin — "the most famous wizard in history" — having been <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into their <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Houses" title="Hogwarts Houses">House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chocolate_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chocolate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, this fact is one that the other houses do not like to recognise.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b3/Merlin_Portrait_2.png/revision/latest?cb=20180914162437" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b3/Merlin_Portrait_2.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180914162437" decoding="async" width="250" height="348" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Merlin Portrait 2.png" data-image-key="Merlin_Portrait_2.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Merlin_Portrait_2.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A portrait of Merlin at Hogwarts</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Merlin had at least one portrait at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. It was located one of the walls by the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Staircase" title="Grand Staircase">Grand Staircase</a>, next to the the portrait of the old friend of his real-life counterpart and fellow courtier of King Arthur, <a href="/wiki/Cadogan" title="Cadogan">Sir Cadogan.</a> This portrait offered the residents of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> advice on the <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vaults</a> while they were trying to free <a href="/wiki/Beatrice_Haywood" title="Beatrice Haywood">Beatrice Haywood</a> from a painting in <a href="/wiki/1988" title="1988">1988</a>, explaining that in order to understand the nature of <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a> placed upon each of them, they first needed to know what the vaults had been built to hold; warning that this curse was their punishment for abandoning ancient magic and to the vaults, the people in Hogwarts were the curse.<sup id="cite_ref-HM52_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM52-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On a later occasion, Merlin's portrait discussed the <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vaults</a> with his neighbouring portrait, <a href="/wiki/Cadogan" title="Cadogan">Sir Cadogan</a>, and the House Ghost of <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Sir Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</a>, whom he confided that the <a href="/wiki/Portrait_curse" title="Portrait curse">Portrait curse</a> were nothing more than a "trap guarding the mouth of <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Horntail" title="Hungarian Horntail">an all-consuming beast</a>", and that a <a href="/wiki/R%27s_messengers" title="R&#39;s messengers">faceless</a> <a href="/wiki/Ben_Copper" title="Ben Copper">interloper</a> had been tampering with the portraits.<sup id="cite_ref-HM53_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM53-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also expressed the opinion that if a member of <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a> had been running the school, their current predicament would never have happened.<sup id="cite_ref-HM55_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM55-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Later still, when questioned about whether he knew anything about a painting that was linked to any of the Cursed Vaults, Merlin's portrait recalled "<a href="/wiki/Vault_Portrait" title="Vault Portrait">a portrait</a> of an <a href="/wiki/Buried_Vault" title="Buried Vault">underground chamber</a> choked with shadow", and explained that in the case of the vaults, "one curse begets the next."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> At a later point, when <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> had to wreak havoc at the school as per the instruction of <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> the poltergeist in exchange for the <a href="/wiki/Vault_Portrait" title="Vault Portrait">Vault Portrait</a> by pretending that they were convinced that it was Merlin, and not Cadogan who defeated the <a href="/wiki/Wyvern_of_Wye" title="Wyvern of Wye">Wyvern of Wye</a>, Merlin's portrait did not correct them. Instead, when Sir. Cadogan called upon his painted companion to defend his honour and to help him correct the student, Merlin's portrait amusingly pretended to be unable to defend anything other than ancient magic, and told Sir. Cadogan to fight his own battles.<sup id="cite_ref-HM522_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM522-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magical</a> mastery</b>: Arguably the most powerful <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> of all time,<sup id="cite_ref-chocolate_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chocolate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> it can be assumed that Merlin was rich in learning and experience and had more than considerable skill in magic.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Merlin's exceptional ability in this field of magic is noted, to the point he has been dubbed the "Prince of Enchanters".<sup id="cite_ref-chocolate_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chocolate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">Wandless magic</a></b>: Merlin was also capable of performing magic without his wand, something that only wizards of superior magical ability are able to accomplish.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Spell_creation" title="Spell creation">Spell creation</a></b> (possibly): Merlin probably had skill in spellcraft, since some posit the <a href="/wiki/Disarming_Charm" title="Disarming Charm">Disarming Charm</a> was a creation of Merlin's; although some (like <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Goshawk" title="Miranda Goshawk">Miranda Goshawk</a> wrote in the <i><a href="/wiki/Book_of_Spells" title="Book of Spells">Book of Spells</a></i>) maintain that that <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spell</a> was created in the <a href="/wiki/14th_century" title="14th century">14th century</a> by <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Smudgling" title="Elizabeth Smudgling">Elizabeth Smudgling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WBS_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBS-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Study_of_Ancient_Runes" title="Study of Ancient Runes">Study of Ancient Runes</a></b> (possibly): Merlin may have been skilled at writing and reading in <a href="/wiki/Rune" title="Rune">runic</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Merlin%27s_book" title="Merlin&#39;s book">a book</a> that was hand-written in unfamiliar <a href="/wiki/Rune" title="Rune">runes</a> and believed to have belonged to him was said to have been kept at the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_Office" title="Headmaster&#39;s Office">Headmaster's Office</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Merlin?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/68/Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png/revision/latest?cb=20160606202336" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="180" height="180" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png" data-image-key="Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/68/Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20160606202336" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/68/Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png/revision/latest?cb=20160606202336" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/68/Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20160606202336" decoding="async" width="180" height="180" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png" data-image-key="Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/68/Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20160606202336" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Merlin-01-chocFrogCard.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Merlin's bronze wizard card</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<ul><li>Merlin is a wizard featured in many British legends. Though he first appeared under the name Myrddin Wyllt (Merlin the wild) in Welsh literature as a half-crazed prophet of the forest, he is perhaps most famous as a central figure in the Arthurian legend. He is most commonly depicted as the adviser of King Arthur.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>As the legends and tales of Merlin is known both to <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> alike, and Muggles have written about how Merlin is said to have been the child of a <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> woman who was impregnated by a <a href="/wiki/Demon" title="Demon">demon</a>, many fans have speculated that Merlin may have been a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">Half-blood</a> wizard, and that his father may have been a fearsome <a href="/wiki/Warlock" title="Warlock">Warlock</a> who used <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">dark magic</a> against Muggles and were both so widely feared by Muggles that he was recorded in myth as an evil entity. However, as mentioned, this is just speculation.</li>
<li>In some myths about Merlin, he proved capable of making <a href="/wiki/Prophecy" title="Prophecy">prophecies</a>, change his appearance at will and <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">predict the future</a>, and if this indeed is true for his <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding</a> counterpart, Merlin would have been both a <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a> and either very skilled at human transfiguration or a <a href="/wiki/Metamorphmagus" title="Metamorphmagus">Metamorphmagus</a>.</li>
<li>Some myths claim Merlin to be a <a href="/wiki/Druid" title="Druid">Druid</a> and/or raised among them. While yet to be confirmed, this may very well be the case of his in-universe counterpart, as the Druids' respect and veneration of nature and all of its beings fits very well with the fact that Merlin not only reportedly was chosen by a wand made of English oak, a wood known to have "an affinity with the magic of the natural world", in which he would be likely to have already revered already before to his Hogwarts years, but it would also explain his desire to promote Muggle rights, as learning about the philosophy of the Druids from a young age would have shielded him from being influenced by the common belief in the inherent superiority of pure-bloods found in Slytherin house, which would have been especially notable if he indeed was taught by Salazar Slytherin himself.</li>
<li>One of the possible questions in the <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorting</a> quiz at  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> mentions that Merlin marked his possessions with <a href="/wiki/Mark_of_Merlin" title="Mark of Merlin">a mysterious rune</a>. Since this is just a possible answer to a hypothetical question, it is unclear if Merlin actually used this symbol as his mark in historical canon.</li>
<li>A  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> "Features" article about what the portraits at Hogwarts do when no one is looking, asks us to "<i>imagine an ancient portrait of Merlin stopping a student for a chat because the boy she likes will be coming down the corridor any minute now</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> While this is just a hypothetical scenario, there is at least one portrait of Merlin at Hogwarts as shown in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_Hogwarts_Mystery" title="Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery">Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, an extraordinarily talented and powerful wizard, resembled Merlin in many ways, such as the iconic long beard and prodigious magical skill. Both were <i>"Muggle sympathizers"</i></li>
<li>It is unusual that Merlin, one of the most famous proponents of <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> rights and tolerance, would be placed in <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, a house strongly associated with anti-Muggle views, although since he was such an ambitious person with all his plans for the Order of Merlin, it is not entirely surprising. Slytherins are not necessarily evil or malicious individuals, nor is there any reliable information on whether or not the House of Slytherin actually held the same views in Merlin's time.</li>
<li>In fact, the entire placement of Merlin at Hogwarts is paradoxical. Hogwarts was founded in the 9th/10th century, and while the early 9th century was the time the first records of the Arthur-myth were written, the actual myth plays around the year 500 A.D., meaning that the 'historical' Merlin in fact predates Hogwarts by roughly five-hundred years.</li>
<li>Coincidentally, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colin_Morgan" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Colin Morgan">Colin Morgan</a>, who plays Merlin in BBC One's <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merlin_(TV_series)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Merlin (TV series)">Merlin</a></i>, was sorted into Slytherin in  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i>.<sup class="noprint">&#91;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Wikipedia:Citing sources"><i><span title="The material in the vicinity of this tag needs references to reliable sources." style="white-space: nowrap;">citation needed</span></i></a>&#93;</sup></li></ul>
