<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mason?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mason?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/31_July" title="31 July">31 July</a>, Mr Mason and his <a href="/wiki/Mason%27s_wife" title="Mason&#39;s wife">wife</a> were dinner guests at the <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">Dursley home</a>. The evening was nearly ruined when the dessert <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia Dursley</a> had made crashed to the floor (an incident blamed on <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, though it was actually <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a>'s fault). The evening could have been rescued were it not for the barn <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a> that flew through the open window and dropped a letter on Mrs Masonâ€™s head. Mr Mason then informed the <a href="/wiki/Dursley_family" title="Dursley family">Dursleys</a> that his wife was mortally afraid of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. He then left their home quickly following after his screaming wife.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mason?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mason?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The English word "<i>mason",</i> meaning a craftsmen, normally a stoneworker, and originally leaders of teams of builders. This is likely a reference to Mr Mason's position as head of a building company.
</p>

<ul><li>Mr Mason was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Norton" title="Jim Norton">Jim Norton</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>.</li>

Also, instead of the Ministry owl dropping a letter on Mrs Mason, Petunia said: "Would you like some ice cream, Mr and Mrs Mason?"</li>
<li>In a deleted scene, Mr Mason was said that he was a wonderful golfer and he says that he plays occasionally. This was his only line in the film but was cut out from the theatre version.</li></ul>
