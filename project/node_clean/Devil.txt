The <b>devil</b>&#160;is&#160;an entity believed in many religions (notably <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>) to be the embodiment of evil and the enemy of <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humankind</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In certain Christian traditions, the devil was said to have once been an <a href="/wiki/Angel" title="Angel">angel</a> named <b>Lucifer</b>, but was cast out of heaven for disobeying <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The story of Lucifer's fall was covered in the book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hierarchie_of_the_Blessed_Angells" title="The Hierarchie of the Blessed Angells">The Hierarchie of the Blessed Angells</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Fat_Lady" title="Fat Lady">Fat Lady</a> described <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> as having "eyes like the devil" after he attacked her <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> in <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
