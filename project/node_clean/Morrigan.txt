<b>Morrigan</b> was a famous <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, notable for being an <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> who could turn into a <a href="/wiki/Crow" title="Crow">crow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown if she was a registered <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>
</p><p>Morrigan was an ancestor of <a href="/wiki/William_Sayre" title="William Sayre">William Sayre</a>, who nicknamed his daughter, <a href="/wiki/Isolt_Sayre" title="Isolt Sayre">Isolt</a>, "Morrigan" due to her affinity for all things natural when she was young.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_skills_and_abilities">Magical skills and abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Morrigan?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical skills and abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Morrigan?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Magical skills and abilities"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a></b>: Morrigan was exceptionally proficient with the art of <a href="/wiki/Human_Transfiguration" title="Human Transfiguration">Human Transfiguration</a>, that she learned how to become an <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>, allowing her to transform into a <a href="/wiki/Crow" title="Crow">crow</a> at will.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: In order to fully became an Animagus, one must brew the highly advanced, difficult and time-consuming <a href="/wiki/Animagus_potion" title="Animagus potion">Animagus potion</a> to complete the process, meaning she was very talented in this <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> field as well.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Morrigan?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Morrigan?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irish_mythology" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Irish mythology">Irish mythology</a>, the Morrígan ("phantom queen") or Mórrígan (moe-ree-gan) ("great queen") was a figure associated with fate, especially with foretelling doom and death in battle — in this capacity, she would take the shape of a crow, flying above the battlefield.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
