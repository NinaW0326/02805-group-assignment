<b>Sir Menzies</b> was a British politician and the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party" title="Liberal Party">Liberal Party</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. In July of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Sir Menzies resigned from his leadership post out of frustration and irritation at the media's obsession with trivia and his age, feeling that he could not escape the "<i>cloying blanket of speculation</i>" over his position. After he resigned, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Huhne" title="Chris Huhne">Chris Huhne</a>, the party's environment spokesman, (whose supporters briefed against Sir Menzies) announced his candidacy to the leadership of the party, as did <a href="/wiki/Nick_Clegg" title="Nick Clegg">Nick Clegg</a>, home affairs spokesman. These events were covered by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
