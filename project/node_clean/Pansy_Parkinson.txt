<p><b>Pansy Parkinson</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>/<a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. She was born into the <a href="/wiki/Parkinson_family" title="Parkinson family">Parkinson family</a>, members of the <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Twenty-Eight" title="Sacred Twenty-Eight">Sacred Twenty-Eight</a>. Pansy was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a> and in her fifth year became a <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefect</a>. In the same year she became a member of the short-lived <a href="/wiki/Inquisitorial_Squad" title="Inquisitorial Squad">Inquisitorial Squad</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">final battle</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> broke out at Hogwarts on 2 May, 1998 Pansy spoke up in favour of turning Harry Potter over to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. This reaction caused the majority of her schoolmates to draw their wands upon her in Harry's defence.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It can be assumed that Pansy survived the Second Wizarding War, as she left the Great Hall with the other Slytherins before the Battle of Hogwarts broke out and most likely did not return to the castle to fight. Also sometime after the war, Pansy apparently fell apart with her former friend <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Life">Early Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early Life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pansy was born in Great Britain or Ireland. She was a member of the Parkinson family and a possible descendent of <a href="/wiki/Perseus_Parkinson" title="Perseus Parkinson">Perseus Parkinson</a>, a former Minister for Magic. Her family is listed as one of the <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Twenty-Eight" title="Sacred Twenty-Eight">Sacred Twenty-Eight</a>. As Pansy was born after the directory was published, it is unknown if she was pure-blood herself.
</p>

<p>Pansy Parkinson began her education at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> and was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>. She soon became good friends with fellow Slytherin boys <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Zabini" title="Blaise Zabini">Blaise Zabini</a>. She was also popular with the girls, possibly including <a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bulstrode" title="Millicent Bulstrode">Millicent Bulstrode</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tracey_Davis" title="Tracey Davis">Tracey Davis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Daphne_Greengrass" title="Daphne Greengrass">Daphne Greengrass</a>. Both she and Draco were like leaders amongst their friends, and used this position to bully other students.<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Pansy's first <a href="/wiki/Flying_(class)" title="Flying (class)">flying class</a>, which Slytherin shared with its rival house, <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>, she teased <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a> for defending <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, after Draco Malfoy threw away his <a href="/wiki/Remembrall" title="Remembrall">Remembrall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was the start of a long-lived mean streak for Pansy. Throughout her education, she would belittle countless other students whom she disliked.<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

During this infiltration, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> fainted in the presence of a Dementor and Pansy was one of the Slytherin students who took to mocking him for this throughout the rest of the school year.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During a <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> lesson, Draco Malfoy was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Buckbeak" title="Buckbeak">Buckbeak</a> the <a href="/wiki/Hippogriff" title="Hippogriff">hippogriff</a> after he approached it without caution and insulted it, even after Hagrid had stated that this was an extremely dangerous thing to do. She was in tears after the attack and was concerned enough to follow him to the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a>. Afterwards, she frequently fawned over Malfoy's injury, which he exaggerated in order to get attention and in an attempt to get <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> into trouble with the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, which evidently he did, as it resulted in the planned execution of Buckbeak, much to the delight of Pansy and her friends.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During Pansy's fourth year, Hogwarts hosted the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, a special wizarding event that attracted a lot of media attention. Pansy was among the students who gave <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> journalist <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> false information on <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>, and Hermione Granger. One piece was the making up of false rumours which detailed that Harry and Hermione were romantically involved. She was highly amused by the <a href="/wiki/Potter_Stinks_badge" title="Potter Stinks badge">Potter Stinks badges</a> passed around by her fellow Slytherins, and laughed when Draco's <i><a href="/wiki/Densaugeo" title="Densaugeo">Densaugeo</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hex" title="Hex">hex</a> accidentally hit Hermione when he and Harry started a <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duel</a> in a school hallway, leaving Hermione with temporarily over-sized front teeth.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Pansy accompanied Draco to the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> that <a href="/wiki/25_December" title="25 December">Christmas</a>, wearing frilly pink robes. While there, she expressed outrage upon seeing famous <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> player and Triwizard Champion <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a> escort Hermione Granger to the ball, and again later when a <i>Daily Prophet</i> article commented on how stunning Hermione looked at the event. Also that year, a slightly softer side of Pansy was shown when she clearly expressed (although she tried to conceal this) a fondness for <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">Unicorns</a> during a <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> class.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>In her fifth year, Pansy became a Slytherin <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefect</a> along with Draco. At the start of the school year she shared a <a href="/wiki/Thestral" title="Thestral">Thestral</a> drawn-carriage with Draco for the ride up to the castle. This school year would see Ministry official <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> appointed as the school's <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor. Later on in her regime, Umbridge created a group called the Inquisitorial Squad, of which Pansy was a member. While Hagrid was away, Professor <a href="/wiki/Grubbly-Plank" class="mw-redirect" title="Grubbly-Plank">Grubbly-Plank</a> stood in for his position as <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> teacher. During one lesson Draco imitated Hermione by raising his hand over-enthusiastically, which caused Pansy to shriek with laughter, only then to be startled by the pile of sticks they were studying as they turned out to be Bowtruckles. When Hagrid returned to his teaching post, Pansy crudely remarked to Dolores Umbridge that it was difficult to understand him when he spoke, and that everyone hated his subject, while giggling herself.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> matches that year, Pansy directed her fellow Slytherins in singing <i><a href="/wiki/Weasley_is_Our_King" title="Weasley is Our King">Weasley is Our King</a></i> to mock <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, as well as other Gryffindor players. Pansy attempted to unsettle Harry before a game by telling him that <a href="/wiki/Cassius_Warrington" title="Cassius Warrington">C. Warrington</a> planned on knocking him off his broom. Harry was unfazed and responded that Warrington's aim was so pathetic, he would be more worried if he was aiming for the person next to him. Later on in the year when she saw <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a> out on a date on <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine&#39;s Day">Valentine's Day</a>, Pansy insulted them both.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> members attempted to escape from the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a> later in the year, Pansy searched for female D.A. members in the girls' bathrooms and grabbed the list of names as evidence. Pansy and other Squad members were <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hex" title="Hex">hexed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">cursed</a> during a student rebellion after <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Fred and George Weasley</a>'s departure from Hogwarts; Pansy missed a day of classes while recovering from a <a href="/wiki/Anteoculatia" title="Anteoculatia">spell</a> that made her sprout a pair of antlers.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>

Draco had his head in Pansy's lap, and she stroked his hair while smirking, like anyone would have loved to be in her place. Although upset by Draco's suggestion that he might not return to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> for his seventh year, Pansy seemed impressed by his implication that he had taken the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a> and joined the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. He told her to go ahead without him, as he wanted to check to see if Harry really was spying on him, as he thought he saw Harry's trainers.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

Pansy, along with the all students in the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>, roared with applause at this, save for Draco who was still brewing over his decision to become a Death Eater. Also in Potions that year <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> would be filling in for Snape after he took on his new position as Defence Against the Dark Arts Professor. Pansy was not among the few students who advanced to <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a>-level Potions. Later on in the year, after <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> discovered that it was Draco behind <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a>'s injury, Harry injured Draco with the <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curse</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Sectumsempra" title="Sectumsempra">Sectumsempra</a></i>. Upon hearing the news, Pansy rushed to visit him in the hospital wing, and "<i>lost no time in vilifying Harry far and wide</i>". At the end of the year Albus Dumbledore was killed by Severus Snape and students attended his funeral service in which he was buried next to the school lake.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="quote">

<p>Pansy was present on 1 September, 1997 aboard the Hogwarts Express when Death Eaters stormed the train in search of Harry Potter. During Pansy's final year at Hogwarts, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> was in indirect control of the school through <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> as headmaster and <a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow" title="Amycus Carrow">Amycus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto Carrow</a> as the Dark Arts and Muggle Studies professors. As a Slytherin, Pansy was probably one of the favoured students and would not have been on the receiving end of their brutal and unfair punishments.
</p>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/1_May" title="1 May">1 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Lord Voldemort approached the school with his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> army, and offered those inside the Hogwarts castle a chance to turn <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> over to him in exchange for sparing them all. When in the Great Hall, Pansy was the only person to stand up in favour of doing so. The students of Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, and Ravenclaw houses rose as one and drew their <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> on her as a result, and <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> dismissed Slytherin house from the Great Hall first, starting with Pansy.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pansy left Hogwarts with her house-mates, and likely was not one of the few to return as reinforcements during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> with <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Professor Slughorn</a>. Instead, she may have been among the Slytherin students that Voldemort told <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> had come to join him. However, this is unlikely as Pansy seemed terrified of the thought of Voldemort attacking Hogwarts, so it is more likely that she didn't take either side.
</p>
<h3><span id="Later_life_(Post_1998)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_.28Post_1998.29">Later life <span class="template-c">(Post 1998)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life (Post 1998)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Later life (Post 1998)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Very little is known of what happened to Pansy after the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> or her later life. Her friendship with Draco apparently ended sometime after they completed school.
</p>

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">"<i>Hey, Potter! Potter! The Dementors are coming, Potter! Woooooooo!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Pansy cruelly taunting Harry Potter<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Sadistic, mean-spirited, and petty, Pansy often bullied and teased <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> students, as well as other students in general. She might have targeted them in particular because of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s aforementioned hostile relationship with <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> (and also his general unpopularity among Slytherins), but it seems that by all appearances, she just had a fondness for tormenting others in general. Pansy apparently had some sort of friendship with the Gryffindor <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a> at one point, or at least respected her to an extent, though this possible friendship appeared to have fully evaporated by the time of their fifth year. During their first <a href="/wiki/Flying_(class)" title="Flying (class)">Flying</a> lesson in the <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%931992_school_year" title="1991–1992 school year">1991–1992 school year</a>, Pansy disapproved of Parvati defending <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> (after he injured himself during the lesson), saying she "<i>never thought Parvati would</i> <i>like fat little cry-babies</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 246px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/5/50/PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG/revision/latest?cb=20131017030336" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="244" height="134" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG" data-image-key="PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/5/50/PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/244?cb=20131017030336" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/5/50/PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG/revision/latest?cb=20131017030336" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/5/50/PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/244?cb=20131017030336" decoding="async" width="244" height="134" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG" data-image-key="PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/5/50/PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/244?cb=20131017030336" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:PansyParkinsonMocking.PNG" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Pansy and others mocking Harry Potter about his fear of <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Similarly, Pansy and her fellow Slytherins joined Hufflepuff in supporting <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>, whom they considered to be "<i>the real Hogwarts champion</i>" for the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> during the 1994-1995 school year, but this was likely done less out of kindness or respect for Cedric, and more out of a desire to denigrate and antagonise Harry Potter (who Pansy and most Slytherins looked down upon). However, she <i>did</i> later refer to Cedric as "good-looking" when she saw his former girlfriend <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a> with Harry, indicating that she liked his handsomeness if nothing else.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> once described Pansy as "<i>thicker than a concussed <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">troll</a></i>".<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This may not have been totally inaccurate, as Hermione typically showed respect for genuine talent and skill in people, even if said people had upset her, as Professor Snape did on a few occasions. Therefore, if Hermione had no such praise to give, it indicates that Pansy probably did lack talent or intelligence, though the fact that she was made a <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefect</a> and got a high score in at least her <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a> should be considered.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Pansy most likely lacked common sense and rational thought, as her extremely unwise suggestion of handing over Harry Potter to Voldemort resulted in the entirety of Gryffindor, Hufflepuff and Ravenclaw threatening to magically assault her in retaliation, meaning she was likely unable to consider the consequences of her selfish actions. Her actions also made her appear very cowardly, since Pansy did appear to be very frightened and anxious about the situation, but failed to demonstrate any bravery or decency in response.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Judging by the amusement Pansy expressed when <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Zabini" title="Blaise Zabini">Blaise Zabini</a> made an offensive comment about <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> being a "<i><a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">filthy blood traitor</a></i>",<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> her association with the then-prejudiced Draco Malfoy as well as the stance she expressed just before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, she was most likely a prejudiced <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacist</a> herself.
</p>

<p>Pansy got on seemingly well with Draco Malfoy during their school years. They were both sorted into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> house and each had dominant personalities, both being leaders in their house. By her third year, Pansy had developed tender feelings for Draco, appearing quite concerned by his injury to his arm caused by <a href="/wiki/Buckbeak" title="Buckbeak">Buckbeak</a> during a <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> lesson, after which she rushed out of class to the hospital wing to visit him. She was often seen fawning over him and Draco seemed to greatly enjoy her attention.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By 1994, possible romantic feelings between the two had developed, as they attended the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> together.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In their fifth year, Pansy and Draco were both made the <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">Prefects</a> of Slytherin, and both had joined the <a href="/wiki/Inquisitorial_Squad" title="Inquisitorial Squad">Inquisitorial Squad</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> journey to school for their sixth year, Pansy and Draco sat together, with Draco's head in her lap as she stroked his hair. They were clearly affectionate. Draco did not confide in Pansy about the mission <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> had given him that year. However, this may have been due to the Dark Lord's command that Draco and his mother not speak of the mission to anyone. After Draco was sent to the hospital wing because of Harry's <i><a href="/wiki/Sectumsempra" title="Sectumsempra">Sectumsempra</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curse</a>, Pansy immediately visited him.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sometime after Pansy and Draco had finished their education, their friendship ended. Draco's changed views on pure-blood supremacy, caused by the events during his last year of school, may have contributed to this. He eventually married <a href="/wiki/Astoria_Malfoy" title="Astoria Malfoy">Astoria Greengrass</a>, who had a more tolerant stance about <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muggle-borns" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle-borns">Muggle-borns</a>, much to his parents' chagrin. Draco and Astoria also had a son together, whom they named <a href="/wiki/Scorpius_Malfoy" title="Scorpius Malfoy">Scorpius</a>.
</p>

<p>Like other members of Slytherin, Pansy respected their <a href="/wiki/Head_of_House" title="Head of House">Head of House</a>, Snape, and enjoyed his teasing and taunting of Gryffindor students. She joined the other Slytherins in applauding him when he was finally given the job of teaching <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He favoured students in his own House but still had high expectations of them. As mentioned previously, it is possible that he was pleased with Pansy and selected her as a prefect in 1995, unless prefects were chosen by the Headmaster.
</p><p>Pansy was unkind to at least one of her teachers, <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Hagrid</a>. When Dolores Umbridge inspected his <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> class, she questioned Pansy about the lessons. Pansy took the opportunity to openly criticise him, and laughed spitefully when Umbridge openly made disparaging remarks about him.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?veaction=edit&amp;section=20" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>A <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/pansy" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:pansy">pansy</a> is a type of violet. Its name is derived from the French word <i>pensée</i>, meaning "thought" or "remembrance."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In William Shakespeare's <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:A Midsummer Night&#39;s Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>, the juice of the pansy flower is used as a <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potion</a>. In folklore, the pansy represents separated lovers. The slang term <i>pansy</i> can also refer to a cowardly person, or a weak and effeminate male.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The face of a Persian cat, flat and squashed, is sometimes described as a "pansy face",<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and may refer to Pansy Parkinson's looks. Pan was the Greek god of the wild, forests, nature, etc. His name means "all"; however the literal meaning in Ancient Greek of "Pan" is "rustic".</li>
<li>Parkinson is an English surname meaning "son of Parkin", with Parkin itself being a diminutive form of the given name Peter, which comes from the Greek <i>petros</i>, meaning "rock" or "stone". The idea behind giving an antagonistic character the name Parkinson may derive from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parkinson%27s_disease" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Parkinson&#39;s disease">Parkinson's disease</a>, a brain disease that causes degradation of motor skills and speech.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?veaction=edit&amp;section=21" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson?action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has said that Pansy did not end up marrying Draco because Rowling always hated her: <i>"I loathe Pansy Parkinson. I don't love Draco but I really dislike her. She's every girl who ever teased me at school. She's the Anti-<a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a>. I loathe her."</i><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Pansy has been portrayed by four different actresses in the film series. According to a forum, Katherine Nicholson was listed to portray Pansy as a background extra in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (film)</a></i> and in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, Pansy Parkinson was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Genevieve_Gaunt" title="Genevieve Gaunt">Genevieve Gaunt</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, she was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Shotton" title="Lauren Shotton">Lauren Shotton</a>; however, the role was uncredited and was a non-speaking cameo. In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, she was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Scarlett_Byrne" title="Scarlett Byrne">Scarlett Byrne</a>.</li>
<li>Pansy was not in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, where, instead of Pansy, a <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons</a> student appeared at the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> dancing with <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>.</li>
<li>Pansy's name continues a pattern of flower names in the series. For instance, in the generation of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, there was <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia Evans</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>, the first names of whom are all known to be flowers. In the generation of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, there was Pansy, <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>, again all flowers. In the <a href="/wiki/21st_century" title="21st century">generation</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">war</a>, there was <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>'s daughter, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Granger-Weasley" title="Rose Granger-Weasley">Rose</a> and also <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>'s daughter, <a href="/wiki/Lily_Potter" title="Lily Potter">Lily</a>. Also, Madam Pomfrey's first name is Poppy, another flower.</li>
<li>In the film version of Deathly Hallows, Pansy gets sent to the dungeons along with all the other Slytherins as punishment for wanting to give Harry to Voldemort. This part is controversial with the fandom, as it is not consistent with McGonagall's character to send students to the dungeon.</li>
<li>There has recently been a fandom theory questioning Pansy's motives for wanting Voldemort to take Harry during the Battle; whether it is the obvious idea that she is a cruel person, as the series often shows, or whether she is genuinely hoping that if he is handed over, Voldemort will not attack the school.</li>
<li>For all of her many obvious character failings, Pansy was still made a prefect in her fifth year. Her Head of House, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Snape</a>, had high expectations, and he might not have selected her if he was disappointed with her (he later gave two of her fellow Slytherins, <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Crabbe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Goyle</a>, detention for not passing their Defence Against the Dark Arts <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L. exams</a>). However, it is unclear whether a Head of House always chooses prefects, and in fact it might not be so; <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> asked <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> why he did not make Harry a prefect, which Dumbledore replied that he felt Harry already had enough weight on his shoulders, indicating that Dumbledore had taken notice that <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> were made prefects, which then implies that Dumbledore had been the one to make them prefects, but not Harry. Thus, it is possible that someone else made Pansy a prefect in lieu of Severus Snape (though which teacher would wish to do so and why is a mystery).</li>
<li>Pansy's role in the films is minor, compared to her appearances in the books.</li></ul>
