<p><b>Vincent Crabbe</b> (<a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>/<a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a> <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a><sup id="cite_ref-HPM_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-blood_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blood-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, and the son of <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> <a href="/wiki/Crabbe" title="Crabbe">Crabbe Snr</a>. He came from a line of <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, with his <a href="/wiki/Crabbe" title="Crabbe">father</a> and possibly grandfather before him.
</p><p>Vincent Crabbe was also a student at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> and a member of <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>. Crabbe and <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a> were rarely seen without their ringleader <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>. While <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> was <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmistress</a>, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Inquisitorial_Squad" title="Inquisitorial Squad">Inquisitorial Squad</a> with Goyle and Malfoy. In his last year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, he became a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>. He learned to cast powerful <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">dark</a> <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curses</a>. He accidentally killed himself using <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">Fiendfyre</a> on <a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a> <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>

<p>Vincent Crabbe was born into the <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Crabbe_family" title="Crabbe family">Crabbe family</a>. His father, <a href="/wiki/Crabbe" title="Crabbe">Mr Crabbe</a>, was a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>. This likely influenced his son's <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">prejudice</a> against <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a> and other non-pure-bloods. Vincent was related, in some way, to <a href="/wiki/Irma_Crabbe" title="Irma Crabbe">Irma Black</a> <span class="template-c">(nÃ©e Crabbe)</span>.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For his disobedience of orders during their final reunion which nearly cost Goyle's life and his own, Draco held the deceased Crabbe in low esteem.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>, while talking with Harry at the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, Draco revealed that he was always jealous about Harry having Ron and Hermione as friends while he had Crabbe and Goyle.<sup id="cite_ref-CC_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_description">Physical description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Physical description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Crabbe was large and very fat, with a thick neck. He had gorilla-like arms, a flat nose, and wore a pudding bowl-style haircut. He was very mean and very stupid, which makes him little more than a "follower" of Malfoy. However, in spite of his overweight, fat physique, he was an avid runner, as shown when he, Harry, Ron, Hermione, Malfoy and Goyle were running away from the Fiendfyre. Crabbe is told to have outstripped the rest of them, even Harry, who was very quick.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix"><b>Angelina Johnson</b>: "<i>Well, they donâ€™t look bright enough to tell one end of a broom from another, but I was always surprised <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_Derrick" title="Peregrine Derrick">Derrick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucian_Bole" title="Lucian Bole">Bole</a> managed to find their way onto the pitch without signposts.</i>"</span></dd>

</div>
<p>Crabbe was entirely lacking in introspection or inquisitiveness, and was very greedy and gluttonous, eating at the feasts for longer than anyone and leaving with large loads of cakes to still be eaten. He could be described as enormously fat and pig-like, like <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley Dursley</a>, but even more thuggish and stupid. Despite his large frame, Crabbe had a surprisingly soft voice, though he speaks so rarely that Harry never noted his tone of voice until Crabbe's last battle. Crabbe seemed to be incapable of independent thought; he was generally told what to do by <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a>, and both he and <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Goyle</a> did so poorly in their O.W.L.s that they had to repeat their fifth year, further showcasing their low intelligence. Nonetheless, Crabbe was still depicted as slightly more intelligent than Goyle and in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> he seemed to quarrel with Malfoy for the first time, for a reward.
</p><p>Slytherins are typically described as "cunning", though given Crabbe and Goyle's demonstrated low intelligence, it is likely that they were sorted into Slytherin due to their desire for power and their belief in <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood purity</a>, which are often considered to be other common Slytherin traits. It is also possible that they did not fit into the other houses as well as they fit into Slytherin, or that they requested the <a href="/wiki/Sorting_Hat" title="Sorting Hat">Sorting Hat</a> to specifically place them there as Harry and Hermione did for <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>.
</p><p>Crabbe also displayed poor sportsmanship as a Quidditch player, as when playing Beater for the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin_Quidditch_team" title="Slytherin Quidditch team">Slytherin Quidditch team</a>, he spitefully sent a Bludger at Harry after the Snitch had already been caught during the 1995-1996 Quidditch match between Slytherin and Gryffindor.
</p><p>Near the end of his life, Crabbe proved to be a deadly <a href="/wiki/Dark_wizard" class="mw-redirect" title="Dark wizard">dark wizard</a>, being able to cast the <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">Unforgivable Curses</a> with clear talent. While he failed his <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> classes, he excelled in the actual <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts class</a>, his thuggish sadism giving him the motivation to perform powerful curses on his fellow students and even becoming top of the class. He also seemed to change for the worse, as he defied Draco knowing that the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a> had lost its influence and prestige in both the wizarding world and Voldemort's eyes, and that nothing could be gained from being affiliated with them anymore. Furthermore, he was not above torturing and even attempting to kill fellow students. Crabbe attempted to murder <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> for being a Muggle-born and blood traitor, respectively, also hurling the highly offensive "Mudblood" at them. This differentiates him from Draco, who had realised in his sixth year that he had delved deeper into the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> than he wanted. However, Crabbe also remained reckless and stupid, and neglected the potency of the Dark Arts, releasing <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">cursed flames</a> to incinerate his opponents that he could not control, which led to his death.
</p>

Fortunately, he missed both Hermione and Ron when he used the Killing Curse but his ability to successfully cast such a lethal spell at all cemented him as a deadly duellist.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Crabbe was able to perform the <i><a href="/wiki/Descendo" title="Descendo">Descendo</a></i> charm, which causes any targeted object to move downwards. He was also able to cast the <a href="/wiki/Disillusionment_Charm" title="Disillusionment Charm">Disillusionment Charm</a> to disguise himself, showing he had a reasonable degree of skill in charm-work.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li>Vincent Crabbe was portrayed in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of the <i>Harry Potter</i> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">series</a> by <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> actor, <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Waylett" title="Jamie Waylett">Jamie Waylett</a>.</li>
<li>In the books, Crabbe is generally portrayed as being more intelligent than Goyle but in the films, it seems to be the other way around.</li>
<li>He is portrayed as a more comedic character in the films, the shorter and fatter of Malfoy's bodyguards, but as of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">fourth film</a>, he is the taller of Malfoy's henchmen. The more comedic punishments for the Slytherins' wrongdoing seem to go to him. In the fourth film, when Malfoy is turned into a ferret by Bartemius Crouch Jnr, masquerading as Mad-Eye Moody, the ferret is thrown into Crabbe's trousers, clawing away at his legs. Jamie Waylett who plays Crabbe had to wear two pairs of trousers so that the ferret would not cause any injury, earlier in the film the spider was thrown on to Crabbe's head and he groans out of disgust and fear. Indeed, in the film adaptation of the third novel, Harry is messing with Malfoy, Crabbe, and Goyle while under his invisibility cloak, under the pretence of being a vicious ghost, he pulls down Crabbe's trousers and kicks him on his backside when Crabbe tries and fails to pull them back up. Malfoy runs away screaming, and he pushes Crabbe down again, who is struggling to get to his feet and pull up his trousers. He eventually gets up and has to crawl away from the scene, trousers forgotten, and his underpants and legs showing. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">original novel</a>, Harry had thrown mud at the threesome and thrown a tree branch at Crabbe. This scene was relived somewhat in the fifth film, albeit that it was Fred and George Weasley who caused the mayhem. The fireworks set off by the Weasley twins target the Slytherin threesome. One particularly overambitious firework goes after Goyle, who pushes it away. It chases after Crabbe, who is running, yelling, away. The firework is ultimately too fast, and it shoves his backside, reminiscent of the kicking scene. In the second film, when the school is cheering for Rubeus Hagrid, Crabbe gets up to cheer, only to be pulled down by Malfoy, looking embarrassed. Another somewhat comedic portrayal of Crabbe is in the third film, in which Malfoy, Goyle, Pansy Parkinson, Pike and other Slytherins imitate Dementors with hoods and robes. Crabbe imitates a Dementor like the others, but has neither hood nor robe on, and therefore doesn't even slightly resemble a Dementor.</li>
<li>Crabbe does not appear in either part of the film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (film)">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (film)</a></i>, and his role is taken by Goyle, who casts the Fiendfyre and dies in his place. Goyle's role in the book is replaced by <a href="/wiki/Blaise_Zabini" title="Blaise Zabini">Blaise</a>'s. This is because on <a href="/wiki/7_April" title="7 April">7 April</a> <a href="/wiki/2009" title="2009">2009</a> Crabbe's actor (<a href="/wiki/Jamie_Waylett" title="Jamie Waylett">Jamie Waylett</a>) and another man <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Waylett#Legal_issues" title="Jamie Waylett">were arrested for cannabis possession</a> and later tried and sentenced to community work. There is no mention of him or his fate in Part 2 (instead of Crabbe, Goyle is the one dying in the movie) although it may be possible that in case he attended that year, he was sent to the dungeons like Pansy Parkinson or evacuated to Hogsmeade along the underage students. This was the same in the video game adaptation and <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, Crabbe is seen in Slughorn's Potions class. However, it is stated in the book that Crabbe and Goyle failed to advance to N.E.W.T.-level potions.</li>
<li>Vincent Crabbe's LEGO mini-figure in set 4735 features Ronald Weasley's face on the flip side to show that it is actually Ron, disguised by Polyjuice Potion.<sup id="cite_ref-lhpcharacters_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lhpcharacters-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
