<p><b>Veronica Smethley</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, who was a fan of <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>. She wrote to him at least once, in the early <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS7_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the course of a detention in the <a href="/wiki/1992-1993_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1992-1993 school year">1992-1993 school year</a>, while Lockhart was the <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> was tasked with helping him answer his fan mail. As he was writing out Veronica Smethley's address on an envelope, he heard a <a href="/wiki/Serpent_of_Slytherin" title="Serpent of Slytherin">disembodied voice</a> calling "<i>Come… come to me… Let me rip you… Let me tear you… Let me kill you…</i>". Lockhart did not hear the voice, but Harry gave a huge jump on his seat and smeared a large lilac blot across the envelope and it covered her street name.<sup id="cite_ref-COS7_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
