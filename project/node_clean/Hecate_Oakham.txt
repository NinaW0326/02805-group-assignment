<b>Hecate Oakham</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, born in or prior to <a href="/wiki/1982" title="1982">1982</a>, somewhere in either <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> or <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. When she turned eleven she began attending <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/1993-1994_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1993-1994 school year">1993-1994 school year</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> saw her roaming the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">castle</a> alone rather often.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hecate_Oakham?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hecate_Oakham?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hecate gets her name from <i>Hecate,</i> in Latin, or <i>Hekate, in</i> Greek. They both refer to the same goddess. In Greek mythology, she is an embodiment of the dark side of the moon and is the deity of magic. She is a foggy figure of unclear allegiances, sphere and parentage. She is chthonic and often is associated with sorcery, being the mother of angels and poisonous plants.
The Goddess <i>Hecate</i> in turn has verious etymology behind her name. Her name may originally come from the Greek word for will. Various Greeks have also translated her name to mean "operates from afar", "she removes or drives off", "the far reaching one" or "the far-darter". Another suggested root of the name is from the Egyptian goddess of childbirth, however, evidence for this is lacking.
</p>
