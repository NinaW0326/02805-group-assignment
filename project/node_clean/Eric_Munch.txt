<b>Eric Munch</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, who was employed as a <a href="/wiki/Watchwizard" title="Watchwizard">watchwizard</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-op14_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-op14-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was poorly-shaven and wore peacock blue robes when on duty.<sup id="cite_ref-op7_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-op7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eric_Munch?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eric_Munch?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Much was working for the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> by <a href="/wiki/August" title="August">August</a> <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. During the day, Munch sat at the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic_Security_Desk" title="Ministry of Magic Security Desk">security desk</a> on the left side of the Atrium, checking visitors with a <a href="/wiki/Probity_Probe" title="Probity Probe">Probity Probe</a> and registering their <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> before giving them clearance to enter. Munch seemed to find this portion of his job very tedious. When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> stopped by the security desk on the way to a hearing on <a href="/wiki/12_August" title="12 August">12 August</a> 1995, Munch went through the check-in protocols mechanically, only noticing the famous <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_scars#The_lightning-bolt_scar" title="Harry Potter&#39;s scars">lightning-bolt scar</a> when he had finished. Realising who Harry was, Munch asked him to wait, but <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> dragged Harry away from the security desk.<sup id="cite_ref-op7_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-op7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Munch apparently also worked the night shift on occasion. He caught <a href="/wiki/Sturgis_Podmore" title="Sturgis Podmore">Sturgis Podmore</a> trying to break into <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a> at one o'clock in the morning on <a href="/wiki/31_August" title="31 August">31 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a> and arrested him on charges of trespass and attempted robbery.<sup id="cite_ref-op14_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-op14-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sometime in the <a href="/wiki/2010" title="2010">2010s</a>, Munch had to be rescued from the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>' <a href="/wiki/Brain_Room" title="Brain Room">Brain Room</a> by the more recently hired <a href="/wiki/Grim_Fawley" title="Grim Fawley">Grim Fawley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eric_Munch?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eric_Munch?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Munch was featured on a conceptual art for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>. He was not, however, featured in the actual film.</li>
<li>Eric Munch's conspicuous absence during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> is noted by Harry when he arrives, apparently not being on duty that night. Alternatively, unless he was incapacitated by the Death Eaters and put somewhere out of sight as not to arouse the suspicion of those who might pass through the Atrium after their arrival,  it is possible that Lucius Malfoy or one of the other Death Eaters present with ties to the Ministry was able to arrange a distraction that would keep him occupied elsewhere so that the Death Eaters could enter the Department of Mysteries unseen.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eric_Munch?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eric_Munch?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Eric" comes from the Old Norse name Eiríkr, derived from the elements ei "ever" and ríkr "ruler". A notable bearer was Eiríkr inn Rauda (Eric the Red in English), a 10th-century navigator and explorer who discovered Greenland. This was also the name of several early kings of Sweden, Denmark and Norway.
</p>
