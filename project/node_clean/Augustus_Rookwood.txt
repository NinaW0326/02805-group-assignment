<p><b>Augustus Rookwood</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> loyal to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> he was a spy who worked as an <a href="/wiki/Unspeakable" title="Unspeakable">Unspeakable</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>. After the height of the first war he was named by his comrade <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a>, Rookwood was arrested and imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.
</p><p>Rookwood escaped during the <a href="/wiki/Azkaban_escapes#1996" title="Azkaban escapes">1996 mass break-out</a> and served as a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> once more. He fought in several battles of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> after which he was imprisoned again, but escaped in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Rookwood was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>. His fate after Voldemort's final defeat is unknown. It is possible that he was imprisoned in Azkaban once again for his various crimes.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Wizarding_War">First Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rookwood worked as an <a href="/wiki/Unspeakable" title="Unspeakable">Unspeakable</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>, but became a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> and spy for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. He had an information network inside and outside of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> with a number of well-connected wizards. Whether or not these wizards knew they were being used for information by Rookwood is unknown.
</p><p>What is known is that <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Bagman" title="Ludovic Bagman">Ludo Bagman</a> gave Rookwood information. Bagman was likely ignorant of the fact of Rookwood's real affiliations, since Rookwood dangled the prospect of a Ministry position before him. Bagman's testimony revealed that Augustus Rookwood was friends with his <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Bagman%27s_father" title="Ludovic Bagman&#39;s father">father</a>. He also referred to Rookwood rather affectionately as "Old Rookwood", despite the man being a Death Eater, during his own court case.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> From that information, it can be deduced that Rookwood was outgoing and perhaps affable during his years as a spy, breaking the mould of so many other Death Eaters to serve as a cover.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, Rookwood was captured and convicted on testimony from <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a> as to his Death Eater activities and imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. This was one of many names that Karkaroff surrendered in order to achieve his own freedom, and the first one to be viable, as the ones before were already caught or killed.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Wizarding_War">Second Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 242px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Augustus_Rookwood_in_Azkaban.png/revision/latest?cb=20161104192217" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Augustus_Rookwood_in_Azkaban.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/240?cb=20161104192217" decoding="async" width="240" height="139" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Augustus Rookwood in Azkaban.png" data-image-key="Augustus_Rookwood_in_Azkaban.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Augustus_Rookwood_in_Azkaban.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Augustus Rookwood during his imprisonment in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Rookwood <a href="/wiki/Azkaban_escapes#1996" title="Azkaban escapes">escaped</a> Azkaban along with nine other Death Eaters in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, when the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> defected to the recently returned <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This was Azkaban's largest and most notorious breakout and helped set the stage for the Second Wizarding War.
</p><p>Rookwood was responsible for getting Voldemort on track with stealing the <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_first_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s first prophecy">prophecy</a> which concerned the Dark Lord and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, who saw Rookwood in one of his dreams. Obviously, his experience in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a> made him a valuable asset in this function.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Battle of the Department of Mysteries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Battle of the Department of Mysteries"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After numerous attempts at stealing the prophecy failed, Voldemort lured Harry to the Ministry by sending him visions of his godfather <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> being tortured there. Rookwood was one of the Death Eaters who participated in the ambush of Harry and his five friends, and in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">battle</a> which ensued when the teenagers resisted. He was the only one not assigned a partner as the Death Eaters pursued the six <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">D.A.</a> members.
</p><p>When Order of the Phoenix members arrived he was duelling with <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a> without his mask on. It is presumed that Rookwood, along with all the other Death Eaters aside from <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>, was captured and imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> after the battle.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, he was again at large the <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">following year</a>, after another <a href="/wiki/Azkaban_escapes#1997" title="Azkaban escapes">Azkaban break-out</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rookwood was described, judging by his mugshot seen in the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>, as a tall, pock-marked and grey greasy-haired <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As Ludo Bagman described him as "<i>old</i> Rookwood" and given his status as a friend of Bagman's father, he was likely an older man by the time the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> ended.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As a Death Eater, he bore the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a> on his left inner forearm.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Judging by his mugshot seen in the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>, Rookwood seemed bored, contrasting from the other <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, who were sneering and glaring. He also appeared being outgoing and perhaps affable, being a friend of <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Bagman%27s_father" title="Ludovic Bagman&#39;s father">Ludovic Bagman's father</a>. This is further supported by everyone's surprise when <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a> sold him out as a Death Eater.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was also shown to be rather fearful of Lord Voldemort when delivering bad news to him. However, during the Battle of Hogwarts, Rookwood showed his power in combat, as he did not hesitate to attack students who remained in the battlefield.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Despite joining Voldemort out of fear, Rookwood was highly loyal to his master.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Rookwood was highly accomplished with dark magic, being a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a>, he attempted to kill Kingsley Shacklebolt with the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He was presumably skilled in casting the other <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">Unforgivable Curses</a>, along with other <a href="/wiki/Dark_charm" title="Dark charm">dark charms</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">Martial magic</a></b>: Rookwood was proficient at <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duelling</a> as he fought Kingsley Shacklebolt head on in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a>. It should be noted however that he was defeated by Kingsley during the same duel.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, invading the school and attacking students. He was also shown chasing after a group of students, implying that even together they were unable to subdue Rookwood. He was however <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">stunned</a> defeated during the final battle by <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magical</a> knowledge</b>: Due to his past employment in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>, Rookwood came to amass certain important information from the various research there, such as the consequence of an unauthorised person trying to remove a <a href="/wiki/Prophecy" title="Prophecy">prophecy</a>. He relayed these valuable information to Voldemort. This also indicated good spying skills, as he was convicted only when one of his fellow Death Eaters betrayed him.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Rookwood was named as Algernon Rookwood in the first UK editions of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, but his name reverted to Augustus again later on. This mistake was corrected in the U.S. edition of the text.</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustus_(honorific)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Augustus (honorific)">Augustus</a> is Latin for "majestic" or "venerable", derived from the Latin <i>augere</i>, "to increase". It was the title given to the first emperor of Rome and subsequently to make other Roman emperors.</li>
<li>Rooks are known to be extremely sociable birds, and also have a kind of caw that sounds like a croak.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Rookwood is indicated to be a sociable wizard, and when he was seen speaking to Voldemort, he was hoarse and his tone was described as a croak.</li>
<li>The surname "Rookwood" may also come from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambrose_Rokewood" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Ambrose Rokewood">Ambrose Rookwood</a>, one of the conspirators in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gunpowder_Plot" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Gunpowder Plot">Gunpowder Plot</a> of 1605.</li>
<li>Rookwood is also a family in the 1834 romance of the same name written by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Ainsworth" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William Ainsworth">William Harrison Ainsworth</a>. In the novel, the Rookwood family are described quite negatively: <i>No mate ever brook would a Rook of the Rookwood! A merry saying it is, and true. No woman ever stood in a Rookwood's way but she was speedily removedâ€”that's certain.</i></li>
<li>Other possible references from <i>Rookwood</i> are the surname <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Turpin" title="Lisa Turpin">Turpin</a> (Lisa Turpin), from the famous highwayman, and the <a href="/wiki/Hand_of_Glory" title="Hand of Glory">Hand of Glory</a> that is the subject of a poem within the book.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
