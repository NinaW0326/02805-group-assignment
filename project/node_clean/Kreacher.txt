<p><b>Kreacher</b> was a male <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> who served the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a> with fanatical loyalty for most of his life, until he was left to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> upon the death of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>. 
</p><p>He was particularly fond of "his mistress" <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black" title="Walburga Black">Walburga Black</a>, who died in <a href="/wiki/1985" title="1985">1985</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black%27s_portrait" title="Walburga Black&#39;s portrait">whose portrait</a> still shrieked orders from the wall in the front hall of the <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">Black house</a>. After Mrs Black's death, Kreacher let <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">the home</a> fall into disarray, doing little, if any, cleaning.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He was also fiercely loyal to <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus Black</a>, and later to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> whom he fought for in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> alongside many other house-elves. Kreacher survived the final battle of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>; it is unknown what happened to him later in life, though he died at some point after <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a> went to Hogwarts.
</p>

<h3><span id="Serving_the_Black_family_(Pre_1979-1996)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Serving_the_Black_family_.28Pre_1979-1996.29">Serving the Black family <span class="template-c">(Pre <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>-<a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Serving the Black family (Pre 1979-1996)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Serving the Black family (Pre 1979-1996)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Under_Master_Regulus_Black">Under Master Regulus Black</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Under Master Regulus Black">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Under Master Regulus Black"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> told <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus Black</a> that he needed a house-elf, Regulus volunteered Kreacher, saying it would be an honour to serve the Dark Lord. Voldemort took Kreacher to the <a href="/wiki/The_Cave" title="The Cave">Crystal Cave</a> and forced him to drink the potion in the basin so that Voldemort could place <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a> in the basin. The potion, (the same one <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> drank) caused Kreacher to have nightmarish delusions, intense stomach pains and made him dehydrated. Voldemort then left, leaving Kreacher to drink from the lake (as any water conjured evaporates) and die at the hands of the <a href="/wiki/Inferius" title="Inferius">Inferi</a>. However, Voldemort, in his arrogance, had never cared to learn that House Elves can apparate out of locations that Wizards and Witches cannot, so Kreacher, using his own <a href="/wiki/House-elf_magic" title="House-elf magic">special type of magic</a> and not contained by the enchantments on the cave, was able to <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Disapparate</a> from the cave and follow his master's orders, to "come home when he finished his task".
</p><p>Regulus later had Kreacher take him back to the <a href="/wiki/The_Cave" title="The Cave">Crystal Cave</a>. Regulus drank the potion himself and ordered Kreacher to switch Slytherin's Locket with a fake locket and then go home and destroy the "real" locket, leaving Regulus to die in the cave. For the last few seconds Kreacher was there, he saw the <a href="/wiki/Inferius" title="Inferius">Inferi</a>'s hands pulling Regulus into the <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>. Regulus also ordered Kreacher to never tell his family what had happened to him. Kreacher left as ordered but was unable to destroy the locket, despite his many attempts. This inability to complete an order of his master may have driven him to insanity, explaining his muttering and devotion to his <a href="/wiki/Portrait_of_Walburga_Black_at_12_Grimmauld_Place" class="mw-redirect" title="Portrait of Walburga Black at 12 Grimmauld Place">mistress' painting</a>.
</p>

<p>Kreacher was loyal only to <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> in general, <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black" title="Walburga Black">Walburga Black</a> in particular. After her death, however, <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> became Kreacher's master, despite having been considered a <a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitor</a> and disowned from his family. Kreacher was hostile to Sirius and to the entire <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> when they used 12 Grimmauld Place as their Headquarters, but was forced to serve Sirius. He was known to steal Black family heirlooms during the Order's cleaning efforts and hide them in his den in the kitchen cupboard. In turn, Sirius took the items and threw them away, and berated Kreacher in harsh and cruel words.

<p>Kreacher was considered somewhat of a liability by the Order of the Phoenix, who used the Blacks' house as headquarters; as a house-elf, Kreacher overheard sensitive information, but was forbidden to disclose it. However, in December 1995, when he witnessed Harry and the Weasley children arrive by <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">Portkey</a> after <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> was attacked, Sirius told him to get out of the kitchen. Kreacher took this to mean that he was allowed to leave the house and went to the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a>, who were close relatives of the Blacks, and told <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> about Harry and Sirius' close relationship. Later, when <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> used the <a href="/wiki/Floo_Network" title="Floo Network">Floo Network</a> to ask if Sirius was there, Kreacher lied. This convinced Harry that his vision of Sirius being tortured in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a> was real, and led him and his friends to attempt to rescue him. This resulted in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> in which Sirius was murdered by Bellatrix.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like all house elves, Kreacher was considerably smaller than a human. He was described as having a bulbous, snout-like nose, bloodshot eyes, many folds of skin, and white hair growing out of his bat-like ears. Dobby also knocked out half of his teeth with a punch in 1997, and it is unclear as to whether he had them magically grown back.
</p>

<p>Kreacher was very loyal to all the members of the Black family except <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, whom he hated because he treated Kreacher poorly. He had a strong bond with Sirius' brother, <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus Black</a> and was devastated by his death. Kreacher was usually hostile, bitter and miserable and had a habit of insulting people, including his masters. He frequently called <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> a <a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudblood</a>. He loathed taking orders from those he hated, often giving them a dirty look or a biting remark.
</p><p>Like most members of the Black family, Kreacher believed that <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitors</a> were scum. Because of this, he frequently insulted Hermione even though she tried to be nice to him. However, he gradually grew more respectful towards both Muggle-borns and blood traitors, as is seen by his giving Hermione a respectful salute prior to the <a href="/wiki/Infiltration_of_the_Ministry_of_Magic" title="Infiltration of the Ministry of Magic">infiltration of the Ministry of Magic</a>. Kreacher was also very loyal to people who treated him with kindness and respect, particularly Regulus Black. He later grew loyal to Harry Potter and started to be friendly towards Harry, Ron and Hermione. However, he was disloyal to those who treated him poorly. This ultimately led to him being partially responsible for the death of Sirius Black. Had Sirius treated Kreacher the same way his brother had, his death could have been avoided.
</p><p>While he assisted Regulus in trying to bring Voldemort down, this did not stop him from assisting the dark lord through Bellatrix and the Malfoys some fifteen years later. Despite this he likely did not see a connection between the two events and merely saw both actions as service to the Black family. He also didn't seem to hold any resentment towards Voldemort for trying to kill him and showed no hesitation in helping him indirectly by luring Sirius out of hiding. This is presumably due to the way house-elves are generally treated which, as Hermione says, is not much better than what Voldemort did to him.
</p><p>Because of years of solitude, Kreacher hadn't cleaned the Black family home for a long time and when the Order of the Phoenix used it as a headquarters, he was rarely seen cleaning. He also had a habit of stealing items that reminded him of his loyalty to the Black family. Kreacher was also considered to be slightly mad, probably from being stuck in the Blacks' house alone for so long. His misery and insanity was most probably a combination of drinking the <a href="/wiki/Emerald_Potion" title="Emerald Potion">Drink of Despair</a>, Regulus Black's death, not being able to complete his master's order of destroying the locket, living alone with the locket as well as Walburga's portrait, and Sirius Black's cruelty to him.
</p><p>A tradition for the Black family house elves was for their heads to be cut off and stuck on a plaque. Kreacher and Ron both expressed the wish for this to be done to Kreacher. Ron later regretted this statement.
</p><p>Eventually, after being given back the locket that Regulus had switched for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s real Horcrux, Kreacher grew less hostile and was happier than he had been in many years, resuming most of his expected duties. He also led the house elves into the fray during the battle of Hogwarts.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/House-elf_magic" title="House-elf magic">House-elf magic</a></b>: Being a <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a>, Kreacher would have possessed his own very powerful form of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, which he would have been able to perform both <a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">non-verbally</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">without</a> a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>, as can all house elves. This magic would have likely been very for his house-keeping duties serving the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a> and as an <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_kitchens" title="Hogwarts kitchens">kitchens</a> employee. For example, he was very capable of <a href="/wiki/Levitation" title="Levitation">levitating</a> objects.<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOHP_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOHP-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a>:</b> Like all house elves, Kreacher was able to apparate even in areas that forbade apparition such as the <a href="/wiki/The_Cave" title="The Cave">cave</a> which contained <a href="/wiki/Slytherin%27s_Locket" title="Slytherin&#39;s Locket">one</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>, and on the grounds of Hogwarts itself. He was also able to perform Side-Along Apparition (Apparition with another person in tow).</li>
<li><b>Longevity:</b> Kreacher lived for over six centuries, a tremendous feat for a <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a>, or by the standards of most species.<sup id="cite_ref-age2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-age2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Black_family">Black family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Black family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Black family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Kreacher was extremely devoted to all members of the Black family except Sirius Black, whom he hated. He especially held <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black" title="Walburga Black">Walburga Black</a> in high regard and spoke to her portrait with devoted affection. Kreacher often attempted to calm her portrait down when it flew into a rage and referred to it as his mistress even though it wasn't truly Walburga (who had long since perished). Sirius claimed that he was slightly less devoted to <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black" title="Orion Black">Sirius' father</a> than he was to Walburga, though this did not stop Kreacher from trying to save <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black%27s_ring" title="Orion Black&#39;s ring">Orion's old ring</a> and a pair of his <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black%27s_old_trousers" title="Orion Black&#39;s old trousers">trousers</a>. It's likely that he held the other members of the family with similar affection and devotion. Considering Kreacher was disloyal to people who treated him in a horrible manner, the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black family</a> must have treated him with kindness.
</p>

<p>Out of all members of the Black family, Kreacher's bond with <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus</a> appeared to be the strongest. In fact, it was partially because of Kreacher that Regulus turned against Voldemort; after discovering the cruel way his master treated Kreacher, and realising that his immortality came from creating Horcruxes, Regulus and Kreacher went to the cave to steal Voldemort's locket. Regulus sacrificed himself to retrieve the locket, even though he could have ordered Kreacher to drink the Drink of Despair for him, showing that he truly did care about Kreacher. Despite the fact that Regulus died trying to bring down Voldemort, Kreacher still assisted the dark lord through Bellatrix and the Malfoy family years later, though he likely didn't associate the two events together. The elderly house elf was still devastated by Regulus' death, nearly two decades after it happened. He felt he had failed Regulus by being unable to destroy Voldemort's locket, and partly warmed up to Harry Potter when he learned that he hoped to finish the job Regulus sought to do. He later led his fellow House Elves into battle in Regulus' name during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Narcissa_Malfoy_and_Bellatrix_Lestrange">Narcissa Malfoy and Bellatrix Lestrange</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Narcissa Malfoy and Bellatrix Lestrange">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Narcissa Malfoy and Bellatrix Lestrange"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Kreacher called them 'Miss Cissy' and 'Miss Bella' and in 1997, when Harry, Ron and Hermione were staying in <a href="/wiki/Grimmauld_Place" title="Grimmauld Place">Grimmauld Place</a> , Hermione immediately realised that Kreacher was very fond of them. She told Harry and Ron that since 'Miss Cissy' and 'Miss Bella' must have been lovely to him, Kreacher must have been willing to do a job for them, especially against <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius,</a> the man who treated him horribly. Harry, who at first hated Kreacher, for good reason since it was Kreacher's lie to Harry that made him go to the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>, resulting in Sirius's death, took a while to come to terms with the fact that <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix</a>'s tactics must have been a better idea than Sirius's pure hatred. Despite this he was willing to fight against them along with the other Death Eaters during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>

<p>At first, Harry and Kreacher had an antagonistic relationship. When Harry attempted to discover if Lord Voldemort had captured Sirius, Kreacher lied to him and claimed he was alone in Sirius' house. After Sirius' death, Harry placed part of the blame on Kreacher and part on Severus Snape, but put most of the blame on himself. Under Harry's ownership, Kreacher was ordered to work in the Hogwarts kitchens. Harry then ordered him and <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> to spy on Draco Malfoy and made sure there were no loopholes in the instructions he'd given Kreacher. Harry then gave Kreacher the fake Slytherin's Locket with the note from <a href="/wiki/Regulus_(disambiguation)" title="Regulus (disambiguation)">Regulus</a>, saying Regulus would have wanted him to have it, as Kreacher adored having a <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black family</a> heirloom for himself. </p>

<p>During the Order of the Phoenix's time at 12 Grimmauld Place, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> was the only person who made an effort to be kind to Kreacher. Despite this Kreacher was disdainful towards her because she was a "Mudblood". Hermione insisted that Kreacher wasn't in his right mind, and despite his insults she continued to treat him kindly, giving him a present at Christmas which no one else had bothered to do.
</p><p>When Hermione discovered that Kreacher's <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">master</a> had been killed during his and Kreacher's mission to retrieve one of Voldemort's Horcruxes, she tried to comfort him with a hug but Kreacher appeared revolted. However, Hermione's kindness towards Kreacher eventually started to change his attitude towards her, and before going on his search for Mundungus Fletcher he gave her a little spasm, which was supposed to be a respectful salute. Because of Hermione, Kreacher grew more respectful towards Muggle-borns.
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> thought that Kreacher was a nutter and thus they didn't have a close relationship. Ron felt disturbed at the thought of Kreacher wandering around his room in the middle of the night and said that his life's ambition was to have his head chopped off and put on a plaque, like his mother before him. Kreacher thought of Ron and the rest of the Weasley family as "blood traitors". However, as with Harry and Hermione, Kreacher grew more friendly towards Ron during the trio's time in Grimmauld Place. Ron even displayed some affection for Kreacher and said that he no longer dreamed of chopping off his head.
</p><p><br clear="all" />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dobby">Dobby</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=20" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Dobby">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Dobby"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kreacher had a bad relationship with <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> mainly because they had, at first, opposite opinions of Harry Potter. The two house elves even got into a brawl at one point after Kreacher insulted Harry. However, they worked together to spy on <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?veaction=edit&amp;section=22" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kreacher?action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kreacher's name is most likely derived from the word "creature." Reminiscent of the German "Kriecher" derived from "kriechen," meaning "to creep, crawl, cringe, grovel, tuckle, or fawn upon."
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Timothy_Bateson" title="Timothy Bateson">Timothy Bateson</a> voiced Kreacher in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">video game</a> adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Simon_McBurney" title="Simon McBurney">Simon McBurney</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Bateson" title="Timothy Bateson">Timothy Bateson</a> as Kreacher in the film and video game adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, after <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Bateson" title="Timothy Bateson">Bateson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/2009" title="2009">2009</a>.</li>
<li>The character of Kreacher was written in the first few drafts of the film of <i>Order of the Phoenix</i>, but later cut from the film in order to decrease the page count. He was added back after <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> series</a> author <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> read that draft and insisted the character be featured, due to his later importance.<sup id="cite_ref-pts153_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pts153-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s role in inheriting Kreacher through Sirius's will and having him work in Hogwarts' kitchens was omitted from the films.</li>
<li>Kreacher's role in the Harry Potter series has been compared to the role of Gollum in the <i>Lord of the Rings</i> trilogy, in that he is the unwilling servant of the story's protagonist. His desire to protect all the heirlooms of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a> can be linked to Gollum's desire for the One Ring.</li>
<li>In the books, Kreacher is the only named house-elf whose name that does not end with the letter "y". Some assume that Kreacher was never a proper name, but rather a disdainful nickname by his Black masters ("Creature") which Kreacher was so servile as to accept as his name from then on.</li>
<li>Unlike in the book and video game adaption, Kreacher was not given the fake locket by Harry in the first part of the film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>.</li>
<li>J.K Rowling mentions that Kreacher died at the age of 666. This number is known as the Number of the Beast found in the Book of Revelation. However, this may not be officially canon.</li>
<li>Though Voldemort left Kreacher to die in the <a href="/wiki/The_Cave" title="The Cave">Horcrux Cave</a>, he did not seem suspicious when Kreacher later helped him through Bellatrix and Narcissa. It is possible that he did not realise that Kreacher was the same elf, as Voldemort did underestimate the species.</li></ul>
