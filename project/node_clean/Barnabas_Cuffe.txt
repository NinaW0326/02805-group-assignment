<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Barnabas_Cuffe?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Barnabas_Cuffe?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While studying at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, Cuffe must have either been a very good student, or possibly come from money, power, or has good stories to tell, as <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> chose Barnabas to join his <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a>. As a child, he was very interested in the news, and enjoyed Slughorn's exaggerated outakes on the <i>Daily Prophet</i>. He maintained a good relationship with Slughorn as he stated that Cuffe was always ready to take his <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a> on the day's news. He also enjoyed receiving <a href="/wiki/Vibe" title="Vibe">vibes</a> as a means of communication.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Cuffe's leadership, the <i>Daily Prophet</i> was under a strong influence by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, which highly relied on the newspaper to assure the support of the British wizarding community.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aside from being the paper's editor, Cuffe occasionally wrote articles for the <i>Prophet</i>. One such article, entitled "The Wizarding World in Mourning", covered the then-recent death of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Barnabas_Cuffe?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Barnabas_Cuffe?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Barnabas" is the Greek form of an Aramaic name. In Acts in the New Testament the byname Barnabas was given to a man named Joseph, a companion of Paul on his missionary journeys. The original Aramaic is unattested, but it may be from בּר נביא (bar naviya') meaning "son of the prophet", though in Acts 4:36 it is claimed that the name means "son of encouragement". As an English name, it came into occasional use after the 12th century.
</p>
