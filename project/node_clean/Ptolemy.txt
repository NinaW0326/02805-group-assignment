<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ptolemy?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ptolemy?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>There are several historical figures who bore the name Ptolemy, the most famous being <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claudius_Ptolemaeus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Claudius Ptolemaeus">Claudius Ptolemaeus</a> (c. 90 AD – c. 168 AD), a mathematician, <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_(class)" title="Astronomy (class)">astronomer</a>, geographer, and astrologer who lived in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> while it was under Roman rule. Another famous historic figure was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy_I_Soter" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Ptolemy I Soter">Ptolemy I</a>, who was one of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>'s generals. He was the first Pharaoh of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemaic_dynasty" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Ptolemaic dynasty">Ptolemaic dynasty</a>.</li>
<li>Despite being mentioned in the first novel, Ptolemy's <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> doesn't appear in any of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_video_games" title="Harry Potter video games"><i>Harry Potter</i> games</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Khanna" title="Rowan Khanna">Rowan Khanna</a> mentioned obtaining his card.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ptolemy?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ptolemy?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Ptolemy" comes from the Greek name Πτολεμαιος (Ptolemaios), derived from Greek πολεμηιος (polemeios) meaning "aggressive" or "warlike".
</p>
