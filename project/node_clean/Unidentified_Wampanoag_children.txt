<div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>The Unidentified <a href="/wiki/Wampanoag" title="Wampanoag">Wampanoag</a> children</b> were two <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans" title="Native Americans">Native American</a> boys who became one of the first students of <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th century</a>. They were part of the Wampanoag tribe. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Wampanoag_children?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Wampanoag_children?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along with a mother and her two daughters of the <a href="/wiki/Narragansett" title="Narragansett">Narragansett</a> tribe, they were given <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> made by <a href="/wiki/Isolt_Sayre" title="Isolt Sayre">Isolt Sayre</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Steward" title="James Steward">James Steward</a> and instructed in the use of them and in return they shared their own magical knowledge.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
