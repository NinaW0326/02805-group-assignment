<b>Curupiras</b> are red-haired, forest-dwelling <a href="/wiki/Dwarf" title="Dwarf">dwarves</a> native to <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. Curupiras are fiercely protective of any other magical <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creature</a>, and will defend them when they feel they are being harmed by <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a>. The feet of the Curupiras face backwards relative to their bodies.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Curupira_hair" title="Curupira hair">Curupira hair</a> is a notoriously unstable <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">wand core</a> associated with powerful and unpredictable wizards.<sup id="cite_ref-twitter_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twitter-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Curupira?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Curupira?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A group of Curupiras are used as the mascot for the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_National_Quidditch_team" title="Brazilian National Quidditch team">Brazilian National Quidditch team</a>. During the opening ceremony of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="2014 Quidditch World Cup">2014 Quidditch World Cup</a>, the Curupiras believed that the handlers attempting to stop a fight between the <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fijian</a> <a href="/wiki/Dukuwaqa" title="Dukuwaqa">Dukuwaqa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> <a href="/wiki/Selma" title="Selma">Selma</a> were hurting the creatures and thus attacked, adding to the (already extreme) chaos and bloodshed.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Curupira?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Curupira?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curupira" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Curupira">Curupira</a></i> is a creature from Brazilian folklore. They have backwards-facing feet to confuse hunters into following them the wrong direction and will attack any poachers they feel take more from their forests than necessary.</li>
<li>Curupiras were not included in the fifty-second edition of <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(real)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (real)"><i>Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</i></a>.</li></ul>
