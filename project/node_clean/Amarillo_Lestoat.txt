<p><b>Amarillo Lestoat</b> (<a href="/wiki/1776" class="mw-redirect" title="1776">1776</a>—<a href="/wiki/1977" title="1977">1977</a>) was a flamboyant <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a>. He wrote a book, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Vampire%27s_Monologue" title="A Vampire&#39;s Monologue">A Vampire's Monologue</a></i>, which was intended to bore readers out of their minds and render them easier to bite. For his infamy, he was immortalised onto a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amarillo_Lestoat?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amarillo_Lestoat?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<i>Amarillo</i>" is <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> for "<i>yellow</i>", "<i>le</i>" is <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> for "<i>the</i>" and a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoat" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Stoat">stoat</a> is an animal related to the weasel. Consequently, his name appears to translate to "<i>the yellow stoat</i>".
</p>
