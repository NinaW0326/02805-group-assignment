<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Jocunda_Sykes?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Jocunda_Sykes?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was the first <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> ever to <a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">fly</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1935" title="1935">1935</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This made her, and the broomstick she flew across the Atlantic with, the <a href="/wiki/Oakshaft_79" title="Oakshaft 79">Oakshaft 79</a>, famous throughout the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was a bust of Sykes at the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Entrance_Hall" title="Entrance Hall">Entrance Hall</a>, near the stairway leading to the <a href="/wiki/Dungeons" title="Dungeons">Dungeons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Jocunda_Sykes?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Jocunda_Sykes?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: Sykes was an exceptionally accomplished and gifted <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> flyer, being the first <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> in wizarding history to fly across the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> on a broom, a truly remarkable <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> achievement which would have taken immense skill, determination and effort. For her renowned flying talent, she was included on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> amongst other <a href="/wiki/Famous_Witches_and_Wizards_Cards" title="Famous Witches and Wizards Cards">famous witches and wizards</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Jocunda_Sykes?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Jocunda_Sykes?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Jocunda is possibly the wizard counterpart of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amelia_Earhart" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Amelia Earhart">Amelia Earhart</a>, who is famous for flying across the Atlantic on a plane, the first woman to do so.</li>
<li>In the Danish version of the second game her name is misspelt "Jocunca Sykes".</li>
<li>The etymology of her first name, from the Latin for "congenial" or "delighful", is the source of the English <i>jocund, </i>generally suggesting a cheerful and carefree approach to life. Also, her last name is an anagramm for "skies", though the "i" has been replaced by a "y" in order to make it look more like a well-respected name.</li></ul>
