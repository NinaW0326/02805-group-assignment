<b>Kennilworthy Whisp</b> was an author and <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> expert/fanatic, known for having written a number of <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>-related works, including the best-selling book <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages" title="Quidditch Through the Ages">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i>. Whisp resided in <a href="/wiki/Nottinghamshire" title="Nottinghamshire">Nottinghamshire</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, and divided his time between his home and "wherever the <a href="/wiki/Wigtown_Wanderers" title="Wigtown Wanderers">Wigtown Wanderers</a> were playing". His hobbies included playing <a href="/wiki/Backgammon" title="Backgammon">backgammon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vegetarian_cookery" title="Vegetarian cookery">vegetarian cookery</a>, and collecting vintage <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomsticks</a>. Despite liking vegetarian cookery, he was not a vegetarian.<sup id="cite_ref-JKRtwitter_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRtwitter-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kennilworthy_Whisp?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kennilworthy_Whisp?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The author's surname may be derived from the Old English word "Wisp", meaning a small bundle of straw (such as might be found at the end of a broom). "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenilworth" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Kenilworth">Kenilworth</a>" is also the name of a town in Warwickshire, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.
</p>
