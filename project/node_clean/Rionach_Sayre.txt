<p><b>Rionach Sayre</b> (née <b>Gaunt</b>) (d. <a href="/wiki/1608" class="mw-redirect" title="1608">1608</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>. She was the wife of <a href="/wiki/William_Sayre" title="William Sayre">William Sayre</a> and the mother of <a href="/wiki/Isolt_Sayre" title="Isolt Sayre">Isolt Sayre</a>. Isolt was one of the founders of <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Rionach was sympathetic towards her <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> neighbours and because of this she was murdered by her prejudiced sister <a href="/wiki/Gormlaith_Gaunt" title="Gormlaith Gaunt">Gormlaith Gaunt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rionach was a descendant of <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>, one of the founders of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. She was born into the <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> branch of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Gaunt" title="House of Gaunt">Gaunt family</a>. By <a href="/wiki/1603" class="mw-redirect" title="1603">1603</a>, she had married a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Sayre" title="William Sayre">William Sayre</a>, and was living at <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_Cottage" title="Ilvermorny Cottage">Ilvermorny Cottage</a> in <a href="/wiki/Coomloughra" title="Coomloughra">Coomloughra</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Kerry" title="County Kerry">County Kerry</a>, Ireland. Their daughter, <a href="/wiki/Isolt_Sayre" title="Isolt Sayre">Isolt</a>, was born that year.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She and her husband <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">loved</a> their daughter very much and were quietly helpful to their&#160;<a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a>&#160;neighbours, producing <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> cures for <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> and livestock alike. She did not seem to share her family's belief in their <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_2-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, when Isolt was five, William and Rionach were killed in a <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a> started by her estranged and deranged sister, <a href="/wiki/Gormlaith_Gaunt" title="Gormlaith Gaunt">Gormlaith Gaunt</a>, a malevolent <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark witch</a> who "saved" Isolt with the intention of raising her with a belief in <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood superiority</a>. Isolt eventually fled Gormlaith and moved to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">New World</a> where she befriended a <a href="/wiki/Pukwudgie" title="Pukwudgie">Pukwudgie</a> who she named <a href="/wiki/William_(Pukwudgie)" title="William (Pukwudgie)">William</a> in honour of her father. Rionach had two granddaughters through Isolt's marriage to <a href="/wiki/James_Steward" title="James Steward">James Steward</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martha_Steward_II" title="Martha Steward II">Martha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rionach_Steward" title="Rionach Steward">Rionach Steward</a>, as well as two grandsons by adoption, <a href="/wiki/Chadwick_Boot" title="Chadwick Boot">Chadwick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Webster_Boot" title="Webster Boot">Webster Boot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_2-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rionach was evidently a very kind-hearted and compassionate witch, who <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">loved</a> her daughter and husband dearly, and cared immensely for the well-being of others. She was known to be very tolerant towards <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>, as she often helped them using <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, showing despite being a pure-blood, she viewed them as equals to <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">Wizardkind</a>, and was not a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacist</a>, unlike many others, such as her <a href="/wiki/Gormlaith_Gaunt" title="Gormlaith Gaunt">sister</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">Healing magic</a></b>: Rionach was proficient in the art of medical magic, as she was capable of producing <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> cures for the illnesses and health conditions of her <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> neighbours and livestock alive, meaning with <a href="/wiki/Healing_spell" title="Healing spell">healing spells</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a> and other magical remedies.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_2-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rionach_Sayre?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Rionach is a variant of the name Ríoghnach, derived from Irish ríoghan meaning "queen". In Irish legend this was a wife of the Irish king Niall.</li>
<li>Sayre is a derivative of the pre-medieval personal name&#160;<i>Saher</i>&#160;or&#160;<i>Seir</i>, which itself is a short form of the Norman name&#160;<i>Sigiheri</i>&#160;introduced into England after the Conquest of 1066, and that can be translated as "victory, army". It can also be originated from the medieval occupational name for a wood cutter,&#160;<i>sayhare</i>, although the usual surname is now&#160;<i>Sawyer</i>. The third orgin is from Middle English&#160;<i>say(en)</i>&#160;or&#160;<i>seycen</i>, to say, and literally means "professional reciter".The fourth origin is from the medieval occupation of assaying metals or tasting food, derived from the Old French&#160;<i>essay</i>, meaning "trial, test"</li></ul>
