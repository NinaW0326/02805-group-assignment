<b>Mary</b>, also known as <b>Saint Mary</b> or the <b>Virgin Mary</b>, was the mother <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a>, the son of <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a> according to the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> and the central figure of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She was venerated as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/saint" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:saint">saint</a> in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholicism" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Catholicism">Catholic</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglicanism" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Anglicanism">Anglican</a>, and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox</a> churches.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s_Church_Hall" title="St. Mary&#39;s Church Hall">St. Mary's Church Hall</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Emanuel_Hurwitz" title="Emanuel Hurwitz">Emanuel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kay_Hurwitz" title="Kay Hurwitz">Kay Hurwitz</a> once performed along with the <a href="/wiki/Dollis_Chamber_Ensemble" title="Dollis Chamber Ensemble">Dollis Chamber Ensemble</a>, was presumably named after her.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 165px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/6a/MaryMedallion.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20161101031810" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/6a/MaryMedallion.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/163?cb=20161101031810" decoding="async" width="163" height="170" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="MaryMedallion.jpg" data-image-key="MaryMedallion.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:MaryMedallion.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A medallion depicting Mary, mother of <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>One of the known works by artist <a href="/wiki/Haniball_Carathi" title="Haniball Carathi">Haniball Carathi</a> was a "little piece" depicting "our Ladie" (i.e., Mary) with the presumably infant Jesus "upon her right hand."<sup id="cite_ref-lc_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lc-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A catalogue listing for this work was incorporated into the label of the <a href="/wiki/Belch_Powder" title="Belch Powder">Belch Powder</a> box.<sup id="cite_ref-lc_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lc-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A medallion depicting Mary was among the many objects scattered about the interior of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Bell_Tower" title="Old Bell Tower">old bell tower</a> near the <a href="/wiki/Chudley_Cannons" title="Chudley Cannons">Chudley Cannons</a>' <a href="/wiki/Chudley_Cannons%27_training_grounds" class="mw-redirect" title="Chudley Cannons&#39; training grounds">training grounds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-medallion_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-medallion-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Mary" is the usual English form of Maria, which was the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> form of the New Testament <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> names Μαριάμ (Mariam) and Μαρία (Maria) - the spellings are interchangeable - which were from the Hebrew name מִרְיָם (Miryam). The meaning is not known for certain, but there are several theories including "sea of bitterness", "rebelliousness", and "wished for child". However it was most likely originally an <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> name, perhaps derived in part from <i>mry</i>, "beloved" or <i>mr</i>, "<a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a>".
</p>
