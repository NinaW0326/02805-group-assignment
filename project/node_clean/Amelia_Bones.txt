<p><b>Amelia Susan</b><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><b> Bones</b> (d. mid-July, <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-death_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-death-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and celebrated Head of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP7_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP7-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Born to <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones%27_father" title="Amelia Bones&#39; father">Mr</a>. and <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones%27_mother" title="Amelia Bones&#39; mother">Mrs Bones</a>, she likely attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> before joining the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>.
</p><p>Madam Bones, having a seat on the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a>, presided over the <a href="/wiki/Disciplinary_hearing_of_Harry_Potter" title="Disciplinary hearing of Harry Potter">disciplinary hearing</a> of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. She was fair, unlike <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>, the prosecutor, and was astounded and impressed by Harry's ability to conjure a corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>. She voted for the clearing of all of his charges and Harry was allowed to leave.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A year after in the second week of July <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Amelia was murdered, likely by Lord <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>, though she put up a valiant fight.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Little is known about Amelia's early life, other than that the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> took a heavy toll on her family: her <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones%27_father" title="Amelia Bones&#39; father">father</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones%27_mother" title="Amelia Bones&#39; mother">mother</a> were killed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>: Amelia's brother <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Bones" title="Edgar Bones">Edgar</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Bones%27_wife" title="Edgar Bones&#39; wife">his wife</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Bones%27s_children" class="mw-redirect" title="Edgar Bones&#39;s children">their children</a> were murdered by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP25_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP25-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She had at least one surviving sibling, however, as she and Edgar shared a niece, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Bones" title="Susan Bones">Susan</a>, who was born to another brother. As she was a partial namesake of her niece, it is likely that she was close to that brother. It is also likely <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> during her youth, although the nature of her education is unknown.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Department of Magical Law Enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Department of Magical Law Enforcement"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sometime after her schooling, Amelia joined the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> and was there-on known as "Madam Bones". She became the Head of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a> and had a seat on the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP7_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP7-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Second_Wizarding_War_(1995-1996)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Wizarding_War_.281995-1996.29">Second Wizarding War <span class="template-c">(1995-1996)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War (1995-1996)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War (1995-1996)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">"<i>My father reckons your aunt's a troublemaker, someone will teach her a lesson!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a> foreshadows what happens to Amelia<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Respected and celebrated by most, the <a href="/wiki/Goyle" title="Goyle">father</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a> called her a "<i>troublemaker</i>" and that someone should teach her a lesson,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTPG_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTPG-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> presumably disliking her due to her unbiasedness and his association with the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/ea/Bones_at_hearing.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160607182621" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/ea/Bones_at_hearing.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20160607182621" decoding="async" width="250" height="110" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Bones at hearing.jpg" data-image-key="Bones_at_hearing.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Bones_at_hearing.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Amelia at <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s disciplinary hearing</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Madam Bones sat on the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> and presided over Harry Potter's <a href="/wiki/Disciplinary_hearing_of_Harry_Potter" title="Disciplinary hearing of Harry Potter">disciplinary hearing</a> in 1995, which was initially supposed to take place in her office but was instead moved to Courtroom Ten in the depths of the Ministry. There, she sat on the bench along with <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> who acted as "Interrogators". <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> was the "witness for the defence". Despite poor treatment of Harry by Fudge, Madam Bones managed to keep the trial fairly unbiased and in defiance of Fudge's recent threats to fire anybody loyal or friendly to Dumbledore, allowed him to speak.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She also confirmed that under the rules laid down by her department, the accused had the right to provide witnesses and evidence in their defence and to use magic in front of Muggles if circumstances threatened the safety of all present, something which Fudge was actively trying to deny. Thanks to this, Harry was found innocent by a large majority of the court, including Madam Bones herself. During the trial, Madam Bones expressed admiration for Harry's ability to produce a corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a> at such a young age, and believed <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a>'s testimony when Fudge stubbornly didn't.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Amelia was murdered during the second week of July in the <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> summer holiday,<sup id="cite_ref-death_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-death-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> presumably for her anti-<a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> activities, in her own home. In the opinion of Fudge, who had recently been dismissed as <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, she had likely been murdered by Voldemort himself, as she was known to be a very skilled witch and all the evidence left proved that considerable effort was required to kill her. Her murder was also reported in the Muggle newspapers, the nature of which baffled Muggle police officers as she was murdered in a room which was locked from the inside; only the <a href="/wiki/British_Prime_Minister_in_1996" title="British Prime Minister in 1996">Muggle Prime Minister</a> was notified by Fudge the true reason of her demise.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Afterward, some members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> referred to her as one of the greatest witches of her time, and that her absence would be keenly felt by many.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Pius_Thicknesse" title="Pius Thicknesse">Pius Thicknesse</a> replaced Madam Bones as Head of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a> after her death.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is possible that she was targeted by Voldemort as she was seen as a likely replacement for Fudge after his removal (the position of Head of the Department of Magical Law Enforcement is often used as a stepping stone to the Ministership) and would have been impossible to manipulate or control in that regard. Another possibility would be that she was acknowledged to be one of the greatest and most powerful witches of her time, and therefore Voldemort would certainly have regarded her as a significant threat.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Calamity">The Calamity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Calamity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Calamity"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Volunteer members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> were able to free her, allowing Madam Bones to return to her original time.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amelia Bones was described as being a square-jawed witch with close-cropped grey hair and a monocle. This gave her an appearance of being a serious person, which matched her personality perfectly. She also had a loud, booming voice.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amelia Bones was a serious, but fair person who did not overlook obvious points. She gave <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and Mrs Figg a chance to testify, and was also one to be impressed with remarkable feats, given her admiration of Harry's production of a corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a> at such a young age. Unbiased and firm, she did not cave into Fudge's intimidation and corrupt authority, and even openly expressed her friendliness with Arthur Weasley, a known supporter of Dumbledore and Harry, even in Fudge's presence, whereas many other coworkers avoided him out of fear of the Minister threatening to fire anyone in league with them.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Besides this, Amelia was a courageous woman, putting up a valiant fight against Voldemort himself despite knowing that she would more than likely perish, though she was ultimately killed.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is also very likely that she was kind, given the fact that <a href="/wiki/Susan_Bones" title="Susan Bones">Susan Bones</a>, her niece, was upset when she died.
</p>

<p>Amelia was a member of the Bones family, a family of many great witches and wizards. The Bones family supported the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coworkers">Coworkers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Coworkers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Coworkers"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Amelia commanded great respect from her fellow coworkers at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, and she respected them in return. During <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Campaign_to_discredit_Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter" title="Campaign to discredit Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter">campaign to discredit Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter</a>, in which he ensured anyone who affiliated themselves with either of the two would be dismissed, Amelia remained fair during the <a href="/wiki/Disciplinary_hearing_of_Harry_Potter" title="Disciplinary hearing of Harry Potter">disciplinary hearing of Harry Potter</a>, and followed Dumbledore's opinion of following the proper protocols, while accepting <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a>'s testimony without bias. She also remained in friendly terms with <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>, a known supporter of Dumbledore and Harry, and did not let Fudge's tyrannical bias distort her job, which Fudge could not seem to circumvent.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> When she died in battle against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, Fudge sadly regarded her death as tragic.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Albus_Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Albus Dumbledore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Albus Dumbledore"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Amelia's effectiveness and unbiased personality has earned the respect of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>. During the hearing, he appealed to her to follow the protocols of the Wizengamot, in which she agreed, and she permitted him to speak in Harry's defence and bring in witnesses.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP8_5-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> When Amelia was murdered the following year, Albus noted she was a great witch, and mourned her loss deeply.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bones?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amelia is a female given name. It is a variant of Amalia, derived from the Germanic word amal meaning "work" and means "industrious" and "fertile". The diminutive is usually Amy, Lia, Mia, Mel, or Mimi.
</p><p>Etymologists believe that the name Amelia/Amalia is unrelated to the Latin gens name Aemilia, which was translated into English as Emily. Equivalents of Aemilia/Emily in romance languages do sound similar to Amelia (e.g. Italian Emilia) but have a different origin from this Germanic name.
</p>
