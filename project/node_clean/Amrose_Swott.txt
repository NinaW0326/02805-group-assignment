<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifetime">Lifetime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Lifetime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lifetime"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Professor Swott lived in the early <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He became the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> at some point.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-mortem">Post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Professor Swott died sometime in or prior to <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DOD_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOD-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Two <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portraits</a> of him hung at Hogwarts: one depicted a <a href="/wiki/Glasses" title="Glasses">bespectacled</a> young man,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and another, painted in <a href="/wiki/1724" class="mw-redirect" title="1724">1724</a><sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and hung in the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's office</a>, depicted an older wizard with a long ginger beard.<sup id="cite_ref-cosdvd_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cosdvd-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Swott's name was also carved into a wood panel on the wall of the school's <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> <a href="/wiki/Divination_Classroom" title="Divination Classroom">classroom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-poapanel_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poapanel-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the winter of <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, Swott's name appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Marauder%27s_Map" title="Marauder&#39;s Map">Marauder's Map</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> presumably indicating that he lingered at Hogwarts as a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a> after his <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> or that there is a student named after him.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Ambrose" is a name, which ultimately derives from the Greek name <i>Ambrosios</i>, meaning "immortal".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Swott may have been based on <i>swot</i>, a British slang term, meaning "to study hard" or "one who studies hard."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amrose_Swott?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>It is possible that Swott's portrait is a reference to <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/de/JKepler.png">a portrait</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannes_Kepler" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Johannes Kepler">Johannes Kepler</a>, a German mathematician, astronomer and astrologer.</li>
<li>The portrait depicting a younger Swott seems to have been based on a <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9e/Godfrey_Kneller_Selfportrait.jpg">1685 self-portrait</a> of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Godfrey_Kneller" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Godfrey Kneller">Sir Godfrey Kneller (1646-1723)</a>.</li>
<li>The motto inscribed on Swott's portrait, "<i>Ora et Labora</i>" (meaning "Pray and Work") was the motto of the Benedictine monks.</li></ul>
