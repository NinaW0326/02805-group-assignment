<b>Eunice Murray</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Montrose_Magpies" title="Montrose Magpies">Montrose Magpies</a>. Murray once petitioned for a <i>"faster <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Snitch</a> because this is just too easy."</i> Murray died in <a href="/wiki/1942" title="1942">1942</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eunice_Murray?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eunice_Murray?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Eunice" is the Latinized form of the Greek name Ευνικη (Eunike) which meant "good victory". Eunike is derived as well from the name of the goddess of victory in Greek mythology <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nike_(mythology)">Nike</a>.
</p>
