<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some may appear as large and hairy humanoids, while others resemble humongous-sized people, and some may even have bestial features (i.e. protruding sharp molars). Giants generally live in tribes, although as their numbers dwindle, the tribes have merged into larger groups. A Giant tribe is led by the strongest giant, known as the <a href="/wiki/Gurg" title="Gurg">Gurg</a>. Giants average between twenty and twenty five feet in height, however two nota exceptions include Grawp, who was considered small at sixteen feet high, and Goliath, under ten feet tall.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_traits">Behaviour and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behaviour and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behaviour and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>Harry, they're just vicious, giants. It's like Hagrid said, it's in their natures, they're like trolls... they just like killing, everyone knows that.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Ron Weasley regarding giants<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 157px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e0/Grawp_%28Conceptual_Artwork_for_the_HP5_movie_05%29.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20091021085559" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e0/Grawp_%28Conceptual_Artwork_for_the_HP5_movie_05%29.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/155?cb=20091021085559" decoding="async" width="155" height="230" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Grawp (Conceptual Artwork for the HP5 movie 05).jpg" data-image-key="Grawp_(Conceptual_Artwork_for_the_HP5_movie_05).jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Grawp_(Conceptual_Artwork_for_the_HP5_movie_05).jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a>, a giant on the smaller side</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Giants are not as intelligent as <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> but definitely more so than <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">trolls</a>, given that they seem to be able to comprehend <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and are actually fond of its use (provided it isn't being used against them, of course). They have their own language, culture, and societal structure. They are capable of learning <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a>, and presumably other languages as well, and read.<sup id="cite_ref-DHG2_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHG2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> When breeding, they pride themselves on having large babies, and disappointment would arise if the child was smaller than expected.
</p><p>Unfortunately, most giants don't have the patience for long discussions and seem to prefer <i>simplifying</i> matters by killing their audience if the topic they brought proved too complicated. Because of this, it is imperative that one must have gifts at the ready when trying to appease giants and take one's time, giving the giants the privilege of deciding what to do with what was offered.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e6/Giant%27s_Helm_WU.png/revision/latest?cb=20200721122651" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e6/Giant%27s_Helm_WU.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20200721122651" decoding="async" width="180" height="226" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Giant&#39;s Helm WU.png" data-image-key="Giant&#39;s_Helm_WU.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Giant%27s_Helm_WU.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A Giant's large helmet, used for battle</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Due to their lack of patience for resolving disputes verbally, they are not used to being cramped in small areas, and would fight against each other over the smallest matters, such as sleeping places and food. Even being a <a href="/wiki/Gurg" title="Gurg">Gurg</a> does not guarantee the complete loyalty of the tribe, as others may end up killing them to obtain the position, such as when <a href="/wiki/Golgomath" title="Golgomath">Golgomath</a> killed <a href="/wiki/Karkus" title="Karkus">Karkus</a>, summarising their characteristics of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/might_makes_right" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:might makes right">might makes right</a>.
</p><p>Even without significant magical abilities, Giants are immensely powerful creatures. Gifted with overwhelming raw strength proportionate to their prodigious size, they are difficult to detain by wizards since most <a href="/wiki/Spell_resistance" title="Spell resistance">spells tend to have little to no effect upon them</a> and may very well be even stronger than dragons, as seen when six ministry wizards came to subdue the half-giant, Hagrid. All of the stunning spells they sent at him only seemed to just bounce off. This is particularly noteworthy, since Sirius claimed that nothing less of at least half a dozen wizards casting said enchantments at the same time can stun a full-grown dragon into submission. However, similar to dragons, they may have certain weaker spots on their bodies, such as their eyes, as <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Olympe Maxime</a> managed to subdue some of Golgomath's subordinates with the <a href="/wiki/Conjunctivitis_Curse" title="Conjunctivitis Curse">Conjunctivitis Curse</a> (which attacks the eyes). It also seems apparent that, while giants may be resistant to many spells, their skin appears to be no more resistant to non-magical trauma than regular wizards, as evidenced by the fact that <a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a> - a full-blooded giant - was subdued and injured by the centaurs' arrows in the spring of 1996, despite there being no indication that their bows and arrows have any magical properties. The arrows may have been the equivalent of thumb tacs, considering Grawp's size, but they still provoked blood and forced Grawp to step back.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Giants were once plentiful across the world, with at least a hundred different tribes.
</p><p>Giants were active in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>, even frequenting wizarding speakeasies such as <a href="/wiki/The_Blind_Pig" title="The Blind Pig">The Blind Pig</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Wizarding_War">First Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire"><b>Ron Weasley</b>: "<i>There aren't any left in Britain now, though.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Harry Potter</b>: "<i>What happened to them?</i>"</dd>

</div><p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, they allied with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and were responsible for some of the War's worst atrocities against the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> community. <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a> from the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> targeted and killed many giants and forced them into hiding in remote regions of the world.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> All of this left a deep loathing and fear from the wizard community.
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Between_the_wars">Between the wars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Between the wars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Between the wars"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The remaining giants retreated into a remote mountainous region in Northern Europe, and being cramped in such areas resulted in them killing each other for the most trivial matters or sometimes for nothing but sheer violence, reducing their numbers to around eighty in total. Muggle mountaineers who approach the giants' colony would meet their demise at the hands of these fearsome beings, but they were always written off as some kind of climbing accident.
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">Wizard</a> kind, however, seemed to be satisfied with the giants out of their community as a whole and did not bother to monitor or exterminate them as long as they remained so.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, following the return of Voldemort, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> dispatched two <a href="/wiki/Half-giant" title="Half-giant">half-giants</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> gamekeeper, <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>, accompanied by the headmistress of <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Olympe Maxime</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GF_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> to make an alliance with giants and deny their forces to Voldemort when <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> refused to make an official approach on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. The pair travelled to a tribe north-east of <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a> to try and gain the friendship of the tribe, however, they were not alone in their efforts. Two <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Walden_Macnair" title="Walden Macnair">Walden Macnair</a>, had also arrived to try and convince the giants to rejoin Voldemort's forces.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hagrid and Maxime were doing well and were on good terms with the <a href="/wiki/Gurg" title="Gurg">Gurg</a> called <a href="/wiki/Karkus" title="Karkus">Karkus</a>. The Death Eaters, though, courted the favour of <a href="/wiki/Golgomath" title="Golgomath">Golgomath</a> and engineered an uprising. Golgomath killed Karkus, took his place as Gurg, and allied the Death Eaters. Hagrid and Maxime were forced to retreat after being attacked by the giants.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Wizarding_War">Second Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>They've brought giants of their own!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>, Battle of Hogwarts<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>In the opening days of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, giants were responsible for an attack on <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> in the West Country of England, although the official explanation was that a hurricane had hit the area. The Ministry of Magic covered up the incident but informed the Muggle <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> of the true cause behind the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-HP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, many giants fought on the side of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> against the defenders of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. They mainly battled Grawp, a small giant who had fled the colony two years earlier, and the <a href="/wiki/Hippogriff" title="Hippogriff">hippogriffs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thestral" title="Thestral">Thestrals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It is not known what happened to them after the battle. The most likely theories are that the survivors returned to the northern colony, came to be accepted by wizard kind from <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s attempt to offer them the hand of friendship, or were subsequently executed by Aurors and The Committee for Disposal of Dangerous Creatures for the part they played in the war. If it is the latter, then the race of giants would be near extinct, with <a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a> and two half-giants remaining, but given <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>'s loyalty to Dumbledore, as well as the insistence of Harry, Ron and Hermione (and likely eventually others after the Battle of Hogwarts) it is far more likely that wizardkind eventually wanted and managed to make peace and friendship with the surviving giants and let them live in remote habitats. However, considering their long life cycle and extremely small population size, it is not likely under any circumstances that the giant species would have remained extant for long after the end of the war, unless they were given more space on their own.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Giants_within_the_Wizarding_World">Giants within the Wizarding World</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Giants within the Wizarding World">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Giants within the Wizarding World"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire"><b>Albus Dumbledore</b>: "<i>Extend them the hand of friendship, now, before it is too late, or Voldemort will persuade them, as he did before, that he alone among wizards will give them their rights and their freedom!</i>"</span></dd>

<dd>&#8212; Wizarding opinion regarding the giants<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div><figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 162px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/46/Giant_bell_towers.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110427102836" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="160" height="239" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Giant bell towers.jpg" data-image-key="Giant_bell_towers.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/46/Giant_bell_towers.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20110427102836" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/46/Giant_bell_towers.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110427102836" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/46/Giant_bell_towers.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20110427102836" decoding="async" width="160" height="239" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Giant bell towers.jpg" data-image-key="Giant_bell_towers.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/46/Giant_bell_towers.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20110427102836" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Giant_bell_towers.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A giant fighting for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> at Hogwarts</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Due to giants being dangerously violent, they are feared and hated amongst the Wizarding World. This was further compounded when they joined forces with Voldemort and caused some of the most devastating catastrophes. As such, wizards and witches tend to reject any connections with such fearsome creatures in general. <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, however, see the giants as valuable assets and recruited them for both wars. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> attempted to bring them over as an attempt to strengthen inter-species bonding (as well as preventing Voldemort from utilising their immense strength for his army), despite <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Fudge</a>'s absolute refusal of the idea.
</p><p>It is partly because of giants that fear of "part-humans" in the Wizarding World exist. Half-giants are looked down upon as outcasts of society and are singled out as a subject of ridicule. When <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> was outed by <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> as a half-giant, many became frightened of him despite having known him for years, and some demanded his resignation. <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Olympe Maxime</a> refused to publicly admit her heritage, even to a fellow <a href="/wiki/Half-giant" title="Half-giant">half-giant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Not all giants are "bad." <a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a> was taken from the mountain tribe by his half-brother Rubeus Hagrid and lived in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a>. Hagrid managed to civilise his half-brother to a degree and taught him some English.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Grawp attended Dumbledore's funeral in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a><sup id="cite_ref-HP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and assisted the defenders of Hogwarts Castle during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> There were also some other giants who grew interested when Hagrid and Madame Maxime went to negotiate with them to live in harmony with wizards.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Unfortunately, when the <a href="/wiki/Gurg" title="Gurg">Gurg</a> that they did not agree with raided the area looking for them, they refused to have anymore to do with Hagrid and Madame Maxime.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Giant?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Giant">Gigantic humanoids</a> exist in the legends of many cultures worldwide. The giants of <i>Harry Potter</i> are most likely predominately based on the giants of English folklore, such as those in the fairy tale "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_the_Giant_Killer" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Jack the Giant Killer">Jack the Giant Killer</a>".</li>
<li>According to <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">W.O.M.B.A.T.</a>, giants may be cannibals, or giants may have very poor eyesight when compared with humans. Support for the latter is provided by the fact that <a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a> needed to move his head close to see Harry and Hermione, and later to see the centaurs while in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Andros_the_Invincible" title="Andros the Invincible">Andros the Invincible</a> was able to conjure a <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a> the size of a giant, although the shape it took was unclear.</li>
<li>Given the considerable size discrepancy between giants and humans, it is unknown how they are able to mate with the latter. A possible explanation is the <a href="/wiki/Shrinking_Charm" title="Shrinking Charm">Shrinking Charm</a>.</li></ul>
