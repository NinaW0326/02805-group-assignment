<b>Gordon Brown</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> British politician and <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a> sometime before the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. In July of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, chancellor <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Darling" title="Alistair Darling">Alistair Darling</a> announced "controversial changes" to the <a href="/wiki/Capital_gains_tax" title="Capital gains tax">capital gains tax</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Constantine" title="Mark Constantine">Mark Constantine</a> told <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> that the measures would alienate the business community that Gordon Brown, as Chancellor, had fought to attract to the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party" title="Labour Party">Labour Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
