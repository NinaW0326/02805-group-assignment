<b>Anna Hill-Hall</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> who worked as the data retrieval officer for the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police" title="Metropolitan Police">Metropolitan Police</a> of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Her name appeared in a July <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> cover story in the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, having been quoted as saying that "nothing of value" had been found in all of the retrieved <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/closed-circuit_television" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:closed-circuit television">CCTV</a> footage of the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">collapse</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Bridge" title="Millennium Bridge">Millennium Bridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
