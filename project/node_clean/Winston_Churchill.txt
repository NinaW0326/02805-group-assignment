<b>Sir Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill</b> (<a href="/wiki/30_November" title="30 November">30 November</a>, <a href="/wiki/1874" class="mw-redirect" title="1874">1874</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/24_January" title="24 January">24 January</a>, <a href="/wiki/1965" title="1965">1965</a>) was the Muggle <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_(title)" title="Prime Minister (title)">Prime Minister</a> from <a href="/wiki/10_May" title="10 May">10 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1940" title="1940">1940</a> to <a href="/wiki/26_July" title="26 July">26 July</a>, <a href="/wiki/1945" title="1945">1945</a> and again from <a href="/wiki/26_October" title="26 October">26 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1951" title="1951">1951</a> to <a href="/wiki/6_April" title="6 April">6 April</a>, <a href="/wiki/1955" title="1955">1955</a>. Churchill was widely regarded as one of the greatest wartime leaders, due to Britain's involvement in the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Winston Churchill enjoyed a good working relationship with the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Spencer-Moon" title="Leonard Spencer-Moon">Leonard Spencer-Moon</a>, as they oversaw a period of great international turmoil in both the wizarding (<a href="/wiki/Global_wizarding_war" title="Global wizarding war">global wizarding war</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> world (<a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-PMministers_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMministers-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>To this day, six different actors who have appeared in the <i>Harry Potter</i> film series have portrayed Winston Churchill:
<ul><li>The late <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hardy" title="Robert Hardy">Robert Hardy</a> (who played <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>) played Churchill in the 1981 drama <i>Winston Churchill: The Wilderness Years</i>, the television-film <i>The Woman He Loved</i>, the mini-series <i>War and Remembrance</i> (both released in 1988) and in the <i>Agatha Christe's Marple</i> episode 'The Sittaford Mystery'.</li>
<li>The late <a href="/wiki/David_Ryall" title="David Ryall">David Ryall</a> (who played <a href="/wiki/Elphias_Doge" title="Elphias Doge">Elphias Doge</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>) portrayed Churchill in the 2002 television film <i>Bertie &amp; Elizabeth</i>, the 2002 film <i>Two Men Went to War</i> (in which Robert Hardy played US President Franklin D. Roosevelt) and in the 2006 French miniseries <i>Le Grand Charles</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Brendan_Gleeson" title="Brendan Gleeson">Brendan Gleeson</a> (who played <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody</a>) played Churchill in the 2009 biopic <i>Into the Storm</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Timothy_Spall" title="Timothy Spall">Timothy Spall</a> (who played <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>) portrayed Churchill in the 2010 film <i>The King's Speech</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Michael_Gambon" title="Michael Gambon">Michael Gambon</a> (who played <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>) played Churchill in the 2016 television film <i>Churchill's Secret</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gary_Oldman" title="Gary Oldman">Gary Oldman</a> (who played <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>) portrayed Churchill in the 2017 biopic <i>Darkest Hour</i>. Oldman's performance, which was considered by many critics as one of the best in his career, earned him an Academy Award for Best Actor.</li></ul></li></ul>
