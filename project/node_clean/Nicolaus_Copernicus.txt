<p><b>Nicolaus Copernicus</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/19_February" title="19 February">19 February</a>, <a href="/wiki/1473" class="mw-redirect" title="1473">1473</a>—<a href="/wiki/24_May" title="24 May">24 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1543" class="mw-redirect" title="1543">1543</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymath" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Polymath">polymath</a> who lived during the Renaissance era.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was a contemporary of <a href="/wiki/Phillipus_von_Hohenheim" title="Phillipus von Hohenheim">Paracelsus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Da_Vinci" title="Leonardo Da Vinci">Leonardo Da Vinci</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSpc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSpc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Copernicus is best known for his <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_(class)" title="Astronomy (class)">astronomical</a> work. This included coming up with the first comprehensive heliocentric cosmology, which displaced the <a href="/wiki/Orrery" title="Orrery">Earth</a> from the centre of the universe.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In addition, he was a mathematician, astronomer, physician, quadrilingual polyglot, classical scholar, translator, artist, Catholic cleric, jurist, governor, military leader, diplomat and economist.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
