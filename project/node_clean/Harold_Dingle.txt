<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Harold_Dingle?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Harold_Dingle?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, he tried to sell a substance that he claimed was powdered <a href="/wiki/Dragon_claw" title="Dragon claw">dragon claw</a> to students studying for their <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.s</a>. In truth, the substance was dried <a href="/wiki/Doxy" title="Doxy">Doxy</a> droppings, which <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> confiscated as a school <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">Prefect</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP31_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Harold later offered to sell <a href="/wiki/Firewhisky" title="Firewhisky">Firewhiskey</a> to <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP32_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP32-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Harold_Dingle?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Harold_Dingle?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name "Harold" is from the Old English name <i>Hereweald</i> meaning "leader of the army", derived from the elements <i>here</i> "army" and <i>weald</i> "leader, ruler". The Old Norse cognate Haraldr was also common among Scandinavian settlers in England. This was the name of five kings of Norway and three kings of Denmark. It was also borne by two kings of England, both of whom were from mixed Scandinavian and Anglo-Saxon backgrounds, including Harold II who lost the Battle of Hastings (and was killed in it), which led to the Norman conquest. After the conquest the name died out, but it was eventually revived in the 19th century.</li>
<li>A "dingle" is a small wooded valley; a dell.</li></ul>
