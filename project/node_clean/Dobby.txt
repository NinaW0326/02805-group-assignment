<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Dobby has no master! Dobby is a free elf, and Dobby has come to save Harry Potter and his friends!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Dobby's pride of being a free elf, and his loyalty towards <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Dobby</b> (<a href="/wiki/28_June" title="28 June">28 June</a> <span class="template-c">(year unknown)</span> – <a href="/wiki/March" title="March">March</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>) was a male <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> who served the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a>. His masters were <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizards</a> who treated him cruelly. In <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a> he disobeyed his masters and tried to warn <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> of the plot to have the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> reopened. He came to know this via the contact between his masters and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>.  At the end of that school year, after Harry had vanquished the memory of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> and slayed the <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">basilisk</a> in the Chamber of Secrets, he <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_sock" title="Harry Potter&#39;s sock">tricked</a> <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> into freeing Dobby. Harry accomplished this by placing <a href="/wiki/T._M._Riddle%27s_Diary" title="T. M. Riddle&#39;s Diary">T. M. Riddle's Diary</a> inside his own sock.<sup id="cite_ref-sock_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sock-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Lucius disgustedly ripped the sock off the book and threw the sock into the air, where Dobby caught it.
</p><p>Dobby suffered a period of unemployment, during which he was often ostracised for having no "proper shame" in regards to being out of work. He was generally pleased to be free, although he did enjoy working as a hobby. Dobby was ecstatic to be out of the abusive hands of the Malfoys, but equally pleased to join the Hogwarts staff. At Hogwarts he could come and go as he liked, did not have many tasks, and those he did have were fairly simple. Dobby requested some compensations for his work and eventually he and Dumbledore came to an agreement. Dobby went on to work in the kitchens at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, for which he was paid one <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">Galleon</a> a week and got one day off each month. He had been accompanied to Hogwarts by his friend <a href="/wiki/Winky" title="Winky">Winky</a> (who had recently been dismissed) and so began Dobby's new happy life nearby his closest friends: Harry, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> Dobby helped Harry spy on <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> he went on <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>'s orders to save the lives of Harry and his companions from <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> at <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>. During this rescue he was fatally wounded by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange%27s_knife" title="Bellatrix Lestrange&#39;s knife">Bellatrix Lestrange's knife</a>, but successfully apparated Harry and Griphook to safety at Shell Cottage. Harry dug Dobby's grave without magic, in the gardens of <a href="/wiki/Shell_Cottage" title="Shell Cottage">Shell Cottage</a> and carved into the headstone of the grave "<i>HERE LIES DOBBY, A FREE ELF.</i>"
</p>

<p>Dobby was the resident <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>, serving <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a>, his wife <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa</a>, and their only son <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> They treated Dobby with unkindness and cruelty, often reminding him to perform extra punishments on himself when he did something disagreeable to <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">them</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Malfoys were harsh and abused Dobby often.  Dobby was threatened many times during his time serving the Malfoy family and told <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> that he was accustomed to death threats, because he received them 'five times a day' from the Malfoys.<sup id="cite_ref-COS10_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Though he always did as he was told, he knew that their behaviour was wrong and he longed to be free of them.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/1985%E2%80%931986_school_year" title="1985–1986 school year">1985–1986 school year</a>, Dobby befriended <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Near the end of Harry's second year, Lucius Malfoy came to the school with Dobby to see if it was true that <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> had returned. In the book, Lucius opened the diary and found Harry's sock inside. He then threw it only for it to be caught by Dobby.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This act freed Dobby from the Malfoys.<sup id="cite_ref-sock_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sock-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> When Lucius tried to attack Harry in retaliation, Dobby blasted him backwards down a flight of stairs. Harry's only request in return for granting Dobby freedom was for Dobby never to try to save his life again.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Dobby continued to work in the kitchens at Hogwarts, but was an outsider among the other elves in the castle. Over the course of his employment at Hogwarts, Dobby became close friends with <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a> and spent much time at Aberforth's home in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a>. <p>Captured by a group of <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatchers</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a>, and the goblin <a href="/wiki/Griphook" title="Griphook">Griphook</a> were taken to <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>. Keeping Hermione upstairs for interrogation and torture, Bellatrix had the others imprisoned in the locked cellar, where they encountered <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, who were also being held there. Though the new arrivals were tied up, Luna found a nail with which she was able to cut their bonds.<sup id="cite_ref-DH23_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

Ron grabbed Hermione and they Disapparated to Shell Cottage. Meanwhile Harry similarly grabbed Griphook and Disapparated.<sup id="cite_ref-DH23_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<dd>&#8212;Dobby's physical appearance<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Dobby was a house-elf, much shorter in stature than humans, standing only about 3 and a half feet (1.06 m). He had large, tennis-ball-like green eyes,<sup id="cite_ref-eyes_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eyes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> a pencil-like nose and long,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> bat-like pointed ears.<sup id="cite_ref-eyes_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eyes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Before he was given freedom, he wore a body-covering pillowcase. Later at Hogwarts, he wore a mismatched variety of jumpers, shorts, and socks.
</p><p>Socks became his favourite clothing after he was freed by Harry's sock. He preferred colourful, mismatched socks. When <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> began hiding clothes she made around the Gryffindor Common Room in an attempt to free the Hogwarts house-elves, Dobby kept the clothes and began wearing them, as the other elves thereafter refused to clean Gryffindor Tower.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dobby?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dobby?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Harry Potter, Dobby has come to rescue you.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Dobby's loyalty to Harry Potter<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Dobby was a brave, loyal house-elf, willing to put himself in dangerous situations when he knew it to be the right thing to do.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DH23_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was also very loyal to the few friends he had. He considered himself to be a good house-elf, though other house-elves seemed to find his desires and proclamations of being a free house-elf to be shameful. Despite this, he was still a hard worker. When he went to work at Hogwarts he refused Dumbledore's original offer of ten Galleons a week and weekends off, opting instead for one Galleon a week and one day off per month. He explained to Harry, Ron, and Hermione that he liked freedom very much, but he liked work better. Dobby thought of Harry as his best friend.
</p>

<p>Because of his loyalty to his friends, Dobby tried everything he could to save them if they were in danger, even if it hurt them (this led to Harry asking Dobby to never try to save him again after setting him free), and once attempted to seriously injure Harry Potter, hoping that if he was hurt badly enough he would be sent home. He did not make the rogue bludger chase Harry out of malicious intent, but because he was trying to save him from Lucius Malfoy's plot to open the Chamber of Secrets.<sup id="cite_ref-COS10_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> When Harry found out, he was furious but Dobby explained why he had tampered with the bludger,<sup id="cite_ref-COS10_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and by the end of the year Harry had forgiven him.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One most noticeable trait was his capacity to self-harm whenever he believed he had done something wrong, usually using something to hit his head or going as far as to iron his own hands. Dobby clearly had a strong conscience, and punished himself even when he had done "wrong" things that house elves should not do, even if they were for the right reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dobby's favourite gift to receive was socks, likely because he was freed from his enslavement to the Malfoys with a sock.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He would get confused when he would get two of the same style as he preferred to have two brightly coloured or patterned mismatched socks and would often give socks as gifts as well.
</p><p>Dobby referred to himself in the third person and tended to get his verb conjugations mixed up. Examples of his strange speech pattern include "I has seven now sir" or "Dobby must punish himself most grievously for coming sir". Dobby also referred to Ron as Harry's "Wheezy."
</p><p>He was extremely devoted to Harry Potter after Harry freed him in <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> helping him out on several occasions, ultimately at the cost of his own life.<sup id="cite_ref-DH23_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also tried to help Winky after she was dismissed by <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Crouch</a>, getting her a job at Hogwarts as well, and looking after her whenever she became drunk on <a href="/wiki/Butterbeer" title="Butterbeer">butterbeer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a></b>: Because he was a house-elf, Dobby was able to apparate even inside places that normally forbade Apparition such as Hogwarts.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Levitation" title="Levitation">Levitation</a></b>: Dobby displayed the ability to levitate a cake when trying to stop Harry from returning to Hogwarts. The Ministry referred to this as the <a href="/wiki/Hover_Charm" title="Hover Charm">Hover Charm</a>, mistakenly believing that Harry cast the charm.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Tampering magic</b>: Dobby also displayed a talent for magically tampering with objects. This was shown when he tampered with a bludger forcing it to chase Harry hoping that if it hurt him badly enough he'd be sent home. Naturally Dobby was not intending to kill Harry; in fact he was trying to save him from Lucius Malfoy's plan to open the Chamber of Secrets. Also, he tampered with the barrier that leads to Platform Nine and Three-Quarters, a very powerful magical barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-COS10_11-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b>Magical blasts</b>: By clicking his fingers, Dobby can release powerful blasts of magic at other creatures or wizards and witches. He performed this ability in <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, when he magically blasted his former master <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> away from attacking <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, sending him flying down several flights of stairs.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Disarming</b>: Dobby displayed the ability to disarm wizards and witches of their wands with a snap of his fingers, much like the <a href="/wiki/Disarming_Charm" title="Disarming Charm">charm</a> itself.<sup id="cite_ref-DH23_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b>Overall ability</b>: Overall, Dobby had a <i>very</i> great amount of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> skill. Although house-elves are generally treated very poorly as slaves, they <i>do</i> have very <a href="/wiki/House-elf_magic" title="House-elf magic">powerful magic</a>, perhaps even beyond that of witches and wizards. House-elves do not need possession of a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> to cast <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a> or perform magical actions, as most wizards and witches do. In addition, boundaries are put on the apparition of witches and wizards in certain areas, but this does not apply to house-elves, so in this sense, they have much greater power than that of wizards (as far as apparition goes). They also can do magic <a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">without wands</a>, something only the most powerful witches and wizards can do. Dobby could disarm with a snap of his fingers, send strong blasts to stun and disable opponents, and very efficiently perform <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charms</a> like those of witches and wizards such as levitation and also tamper with magical objects. Dobby even managed to save Garrick Ollivander, Griphook the Goblin, Luna Lovegood, Dean Thomas, Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger, and Harry Potter from a mansion full of some of the most powerful Death Eaters, even if it was at the cost of his own life—Dobby sacrificed himself in order to get Harry and his friends out of a situation in which they surely otherwise would have died. Without Dobby, Harry would not have lived, and Voldemort never would have been defeated. That being said, Dobby played a very significant role in the defeat of Voldemort, although sadly he was hardly ever recognised, probably partially due to the fact that he is a house-elf. Although quite small and humble, when put into perspective, Dobby was extremely powerful and was very heroic in his contributions to the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>.</li></ul>

<p>Dobby and Harry Potter had a very strong relationship. Dobby loved and respected him very much to the point where he considered Harry to be his best friend. When Harry first met Dobby, he was a bit confused by the elf's devotion to keeping him safe from harm.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_3-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Harry was furious when he found out Dobby was intercepting all of his friends' letters to him, and demanded them back while refusing to promise him to stay out of Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-COS10_11-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Throughout the year, when Dobby made attempts to drive Harry away from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, causing some rather unpleasant results on the way, Harry made some threats that were never followed through.<sup id="cite_ref-COS10_11-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Near the end of the <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%931993_school_year" title="1992–1993 school year">1992–1993 school year</a>, Harry freed Dobby from the slavery of the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a>. Lucius became angry and intended to attack Harry but Dobby protected him by using a defensive spell. Dobby may have realised that he underestimated Harry Potter, when Harry managed to stop Lucius' plot and survive the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Dobby also asked if it would be alright if he visited Harry from time-to-time, with Harry responding with an of course. Dobby visited Harry on Christmas day and presented him with a present, socks and when Harry tried them on Dobby's eyes misted with tears. In 1994, Dobby also provided Harry with gillyweed for the second task of the Triwizard Tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Dobby was the <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> of the Malfoy family.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> treated Dobby cruelly and were very sadistic to him.<sup id="cite_ref-COS2_3-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He frequently had to punish himself and received death threats several times a day.<sup id="cite_ref-COS10_11-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS10-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Dobby feared them, but did everything they ordered him to. In <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> freed Dobby by tricking Lucius into unknowingly giving Dobby a sock. Lucius became angry and attempted to attack Harry, but Dobby blasted him down a flight of stairs and forcefully told him to leave, with Dobby finally getting revenge on his former cruel master.<sup id="cite_ref-COS18_6-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The last time Lucius saw Dobby was in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, when Dobby came to <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a> to rescue Harry Potter and his friends. Dobby was eventually killed by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH23_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown how the Malfoys reacted when they learned of it.
</p>

<p>Dobby first met Ron Weasley in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> in the Hogwarts kitchens, where Dobby worked. When Dobby talked with Harry and Ron about his life as a free elf, Ron started to like Dobby, and offered him a maroon sweater <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">his mother</a> had made, making the house-elf excited. When Ron was used as a "hostage" for the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, Dobby who had learned of the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament#The_Second_Task" title="Triwizard Tournament">Second Task</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior#Disguised_as_Alastor_Moody" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">the fake Mad-Eye Moody</a>, became fearful of Harry losing his "Wheezy" (Dobby's name for Ron) to the <a href="/wiki/Merpeople" title="Merpeople">merpeople</a> and was manipulated by the fake Moody into stealing <a href="/wiki/Gillyweed" title="Gillyweed">gillyweed</a> for Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_7-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ron and Dobby met again three years later, in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, when both Harry and Ron were at the hospital wing; Harry wanted Dobby to spy on <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> because he believed he was a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>. However, Ron didn't agree with this. In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Harry, Ron, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> and others were imprisoned at Malfoy Manor and Dobby came for their rescue. However, he was killed during the battle by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>. Ron then respected Dobby very much and thanked him for saving his life. He also gave Dobby his shoes and socks to be buried in.<sup id="cite_ref-DH24_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH24-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His death seemed to finally drive home to Ron the sorry life that house-elves lived, as during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Ron voiced the opinion that they should evacuate the house-elves, or else risk another death like Dobby's. Dobby, as an inadvertent result, thus helped Ron and Hermione begin their relationship. Overcome by his thoughtfulness towards elves, Hermione kissed Ron for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Hermione Granger also met Dobby for the first time at the Hogwarts kitchens, where the latter worked. Hermione appeared to like Dobby, because he loved his freedom and because he wanted to make the other house-elves want freedom too. During her fifth year at Hogwarts, Hermione spent most of her time making clothes and hiding them in many places of the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> Common Tower. However, the house-elves stopped cleaning those places once they found the first of the clothes, as they didn't want to take them. Thus, Dobby cleaned Gryffindor Tower by himself, taking and wearing all of Hermione's knitted clothes.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_7-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dobby and Hermione didn't meet again until <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, when Harry had sent Dobby to spy on Draco Malfoy. When Dobby came to the Malfoy Manor to rescue them, Hermione was being tortured by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>. Dobby then brought them to <a href="/wiki/Shell_Cottage" title="Shell Cottage">Shell Cottage</a> but was killed by Bellatrix by her throwing a knife and stabbing Dobby during the Apparition.<sup id="cite_ref-DH23_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Dobby was first seen with Winky, a female <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. Earlier that same year, Harry had mistaken Winky for Dobby at the Quidditch World Cup. Winky excitedly told Harry that she knew Dobby, too; they evidently were friends previous to <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> even though their relationship was only first displayed that year. It is likely that they met in the company of their masters, as Winky's master <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Barty Crouch</a> was a high-ranking ministry official and Dobby's former masters the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a> were well-connected and influential socialites.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_7-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unlike Dobby, who loved his freedom, Winky was depressed and thought she was useless. When Dobby expressed his opinion that his life as a free elf was better than as a servant, he made her believe that he was a “bad house-elf”. So, Dobby tried in vain to convince her that she could live without serving the <a href="/wiki/Crouch_family" title="Crouch family">Crouch family</a> by finding them both jobs at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_7-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It is known that Winky eventually managed to get over the fact that she was fired, although Dobby had died by then.
</p>

<p>Dobby presumably met Kreacher, another <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a>, in late 1996 when Harry sent Kreacher to work in the kitchens of Hogwarts, and they did not have a good relationship. Firstly, Kreacher strongly disliked Harry because he was a friend of many Muggle-borns and “blood-traitors" and because Harry initially wasn't very nice to Kreacher. Dobby loved and respected Harry very much for a variety of reasons; these differing viewpoints on Dobby's hero/best friend was mostly the reason why the two house-elves often fought with each other.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, they fought in the hospital wing because Kreacher had badmouthed Harry. Harry attempted to make them stop, but then <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> appeared and encouraged them to hit each other. During the scuffle, Dobby managed to hit Kreacher in the mouth hard enough to knock out half his teeth– it is unknown how Kreacher grew them back or if he ever did. Furthermore, Harry told both Dobby and Kreacher to go and spy on <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>; even then, they argued: When they reappeared to give a report, Dobby was angry that Kreacher hadn't informed him that he was going to Harry. Dobby didn't want it to seem like he hadn't been helping, as Kreacher was trying to make it out.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> However, Kreacher later started to respect Harry and be less prejudiced in general. It is possible that, since Kreacher had contact with the Hogwarts kitchen house-elves, he had contact with Dobby, too, and their common ground caused a release of the bad blood, but it is unknown. Dobby might not have learned about Kreacher's change before he was killed in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>. Kreacher looked like Dobby in appearance, but was angry and antagonistic in character.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hogwarts_Kitchens_House-elves">Hogwarts Kitchens House-elves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dobby?veaction=edit&amp;section=22" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hogwarts Kitchens House-elves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dobby?action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Hogwarts Kitchens House-elves"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dobby started his work at the Hogwarts Kitchens in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, a year after he was freed by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. He loved his life as a free elf, something that the other house-elves who worked at the kitchens did not. They believed Dobby was mad and there was a great difference between their ideology and Dobby's, about a house-elf's life. Thus, he didn't have such a good relationship with his co-workers, as they believed that a house-elf should not have any personal life when they had to work and serve their masters. However, their opinion about Dobby might have changed during their later years at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_7-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> all the house-elves, with Kreacher as a leader, fought against the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, without anyone ordering them to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> This indicates that they learned a house-elf can have a personal opinion, and that they should have as many rights as the humans. Unfortunately, Dobby had died by then.
</p>

<p>Albus Dumbledore, the Headmaster of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, was considered by many people (including Dobby) as the greatest wizard of all time. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dobby?veaction=edit&amp;section=26" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dobby?action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Oxford English Dictionary">Oxford English Dictionary</a>, a "dobby" is an old English word for a benevolent elf that would perform household tasks secretly in the night, like the elves in tales of the elves and the shoemaker. However, just as those elves disappeared when the shoemaker made them shoes, so the dobbies were said to disappear when offered clothing. Also called a Brownie. A dobby is also a small mechanical part in a loom that enables the weaver to create small geometric patterns.
</p>

<ul><li>Dobby appears in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-Dobby_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dobby-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>, being voiced by <a href="/wiki/Toby_Jones" title="Toby Jones">Toby Jones</a> in both films.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Dobby's favourite article of clothing appears to be socks. This may be because Dobby was freed when Harry tricked Lucius Malfoy into giving Dobby a sock.</li>
<li>The magic Dobby used to fend off <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> to protect Harry resembles the <a href="/wiki/Knockback_Jinx" title="Knockback Jinx">Knockback Jinx</a>.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, it appears that instead of a rock to mark Dobby's grave, there is a more conventionally-shaped tombstone.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>, Dobby throws Harry's school books in the bin, because he thinks this will prevent Harry from returning to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, Dobby's eyes are bright green. However, in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, Dobby's eyes appear to be darker blue-green.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, Dobby is still wearing his pillow case instead of the clothes he wears in the books but he is also wearing shoes.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> Dobby speaks in first person, even though he and Kreacher had always spoken in the third person.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, during the <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_at_Malfoy_Manor" title="Skirmish at Malfoy Manor">Skirmish at Malfoy Manor</a>, Dobby snaps his fingers and disarms <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>.</li>
<li>In the second book, Harry asks Dobby to promise to not save his life again, but 5 books later Dobby does because he is the last hope of getting out of Malfoy Manor. He loses his life by doing so because of Bellatrix Lestrange throwing a knife into his chest. According to Harry, Dobby also saved his life with the Gillyweed.</li>
<li>The first and last thing Dobby ever said to Harry was, "<i>Harry Potter</i>." In the film they expanded this line, but still making "<i>Harry Potter</i>" the last words said.</li>
<li>Dobby appears in five of the books but only appeared in two of the films.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> Dobby appears with Kreacher when he is bringing <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher" title="Mundungus Fletcher">Mundungus Fletcher</a> to the trio, something he doesn't do in the book.</li>
<li>In the the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, Dobby is wrinkled and is barefoot, and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, he looks smooth and wears brown shoes on his feet (instead of the bunches of mismatched clothing he is described to wear in the novels).</li>
<li>Series author <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> owned a model of Dobby as he appears in the <i>Harry Potter</i> films. She gifted it to the Marcliffe at Pitfodels in <a href="/wiki/October" title="October">October</a> of <a href="/wiki/2013" title="2013">2013</a>, along with a personalised letter, as a thank-you present for the service they had provided her during the several months she stayed in the hotel while her father was undergoing treatment at Aberdeen Royal Infirmary.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i>, there was a feature: "5 friendship lessons we learned from Dobby".</li>
<li>Series author <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has hinted that she picked Dobby's birthday date to match with her sister's, Dianne Rowling, who is fond of the character.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Dobby is possibly one of the few characters known to be killed with a non magical item, as the <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange%27s_knife" title="Bellatrix Lestrange&#39;s knife">knife</a> <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> threw at him was not known to have any magical properties.</li>
<li>In the TV series <i>Blindspot</i>, recurring antagonist Rich Dotcom pretends that his lover has recently died when trying to convince the protagonists in the FBI to save him from an assassin, but his lie is seen through as the death scene Dotcom described was copied from Dobby's demise, prompting his lover to express disbelief at Rich's choice of lie when the FBI reveal how they uncovered the truth.</li></ul>
