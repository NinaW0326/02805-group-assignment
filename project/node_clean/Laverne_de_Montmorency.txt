<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Laverne_de_Montmorency?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Laverne_de_Montmorency?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laverne de Montmorency was born in <a href="/wiki/1823" class="mw-redirect" title="1823">1823</a>, somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British Isles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSg_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSg-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In her youth, she attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from around <a href="/wiki/1834" class="mw-redirect" title="1834">1834</a>-<a href="/wiki/1841" class="mw-redirect" title="1841">1841</a>, and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After her graduation from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a>, Laverne de Montmorency presumably studied further into <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potion-making</a>, becoming an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">potioneer</a> and an expert in <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">love potions</a>. She became famous for her invention of many such potions.<sup id="cite_ref-PSg_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSg-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ms. de Montmorency died in <a href="/wiki/1893" class="mw-redirect" title="1893">1893</a>, aged around 70. In her honour, her invention of many <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">love potions</a> was recorded on a Chocolate Frog Card.
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">Prefect</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hilliard" title="Robert Hilliard">Robert Hilliard</a> welcomed the <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> <a href="/wiki/First_year" title="First year">first years</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a> with a short speech, in which he mentioned <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">potioneer</a> Laverne de Montmorency as one of the many notable Hogwarts students, who had been <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a>, like them, into Ravenclaw.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Laverne_de_Montmorency?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Laverne_de_Montmorency?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: Laverne was a highly accomplished professional <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">Potioneer</a> and was thus highly proficient in the art and <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> of potion-making, having successfully invented a number of <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potions</a> in her lifetime. Given how Love Potions are particularly advanced brews, and that she was even featured on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> for her impressive achievements, this shows her great level of expertise and talent in this branch of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Laverne_de_Montmorency?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Laverne_de_Montmorency?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Laverne" is derived from a French place-name that includes the Gaulish word <i>vern, </i>meaning "<a href="/wiki/Alder" title="Alder">alder</a>." It is sometimes associated with <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laverna">Laverna</a>, the Roman goddess of thieves, or the Latin adjective <i>vernus, </i>meaning "of spring."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Montmorency" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:House of Montmorency">House of Montmorency</a> was one of the oldest and most distinguished noble families in France. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montmorency,_Val-d%27Oise" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Montmorency, Val-d&#39;Oise">Montmorency</a> was also the name of the family's fiefdom. The <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montmorency_cherry">Montmorency cherry</a> is a popular variety of sour cherries, cultivated in the United States.</li></ul>
