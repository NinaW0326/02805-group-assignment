<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Goliath%27s_slayer?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Goliath%27s_slayer?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In the book <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Books_of_Samuel" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Books of Samuel">1 Samuel</a> of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Testament" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Old Testament">Old Testament</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_(disambiguation)" title="David (disambiguation)">King David</a> slays the giant Goliath with a slingshot as a child, but the <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> for Goliath only refers to the giant's slayer as a "young boy" and does not explicitly name David. This may have to do with the fact that in 2 Samuel, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elhanan,_son_of_Jair" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Elhanan, son of Jair">Elhanan, son of Jair</a> is credited for killing Goliath, rather than David. Alternatively, the producers of the game in which the card appears may have wished to avoid naming David to avoid offending religious sensibilities.</li></ul>
