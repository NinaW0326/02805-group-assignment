<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifetime">Lifetime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Lifetime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lifetime"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Professor Skanderberg lived in the <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">fifteenth century</a>. In her lifetime, she was a social historian, specialising in <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> society.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She was appointed <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmistress</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, and had a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> painted in <a href="/wiki/1503" class="mw-redirect" title="1503">1503</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-mortem">Post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After her death, her <a href="/wiki/Headmaster_portraits" title="Headmaster portraits">portrait</a> was hung in the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's office</a>, so that she could advise the current Headmaster.<sup id="cite_ref-cosdvd_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cosdvd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She had more portraits around the castle: in the <a href="/wiki/East_Wing" title="East Wing">East Wing</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-PoAVG_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PoAVG-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Grand_Staircase" title="Grand Staircase">Grand Staircase</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and other halls such as the <a href="/wiki/Glanmore_Peakes%27_Corridor" title="Glanmore Peakes&#39; Corridor">Glanmore Peakes' Corridor</a><sup id="cite_ref-PoAVG_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PoAVG-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Edessa" (probably originally from a Phrygian or Illyrian word meaning ‘tower in the water’) is the name of a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edessa,_Greece" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Edessa, Greece">Greek city</a> and, in Ancient Greece, also a province.</li>
<li>Her surname may be the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albania" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Albania">Albanian</a> name <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skanderbeg" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Skanderbeg">Skanderbeg</a> influenced by German names ending in <i>-en-berg</i>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Edessa_Skandenberg?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Edessa Skanderberg's portrait was based on a <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.nationalgalleries.org/media/5/artworks_other/the_hen_wife.jpg">1706 portrait of the Henwife of Castle Grant</a> by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Waitt" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Richard Waitt">Richard Waitt</a>.</li>
<li>The phrase on Skandenberg's portrait (<i>mirabile visu</i>) is a Latin expression, meaning "wonderful to behold."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On a wood panel in the Divination Classroom of Hogwarts, her last name seems to be Skanderberg.</li>
<li>On the cover of the <i><a href="/wiki/Home_Life_and_Social_Habits_of_British_Muggles" title="Home Life and Social Habits of British Muggles">Home Life and Social Habits of British Muggles</a></i> prop that was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, Edessa Skanderberg is credited to have authored the book. This is canonically wrong, as we know the book was written by <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Wigworthy" title="Wilhelm Wigworthy">Wilhelm Wigworthy</a> in <a href="/wiki/1987" title="1987">1987</a>, but nevertheless we can conclude from the text of the back cover blurb that Edessa was a "<i>social historian and Muggle specialist</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
