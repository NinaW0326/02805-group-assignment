<b>Half-giants</b> are <a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">beings</a> with some, but not a total, amount of <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a> heritage or blood, as well as part <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> blood. For instance, a <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> might have a Giant mother and a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> father or vice versa.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics_and_appearance">Characteristics and appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Characteristics and appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-giant?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics and appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All known Half-giants had magical blood and were capable of producing <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, traits they received from their <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch or wizard</a> ancestors.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF24_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF24-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> From their <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a> family they usually retained a large height and build,<sup id="cite_ref-PS4_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS4-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> they were usually considered extremely tall by <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> standards and short by <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a> standards. They also retained the <a href="/wiki/Spell_resistance" title="Spell resistance">natural resistance</a> to most types of magical attacks from their giant blood.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP31_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP31-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP28_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP28-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The large physical build of half-giants lends itself easily to great physical strength and stamina that could rival many, if not most magical creatures, with endurance dependant on how active an individual's lifestyle was. They were also gifted with human intelligence, enabling them to learn the use of magic at a <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_school" title="Wizarding school">wizarding school</a> and the right to own a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>.
</p><p>Despite their immense stature, half-giants appear otherwise indistinguishable from humans, as their proportions relative to their size are not mentioned to be abnormal, meaning that they simply appear as oversized humans.
</p><p>It is unknown as to whether a half-giant would still have magic if the human parent was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a>; it is possible they could receive magic from the giant parent. In any case, the entire question would be more academic theory than a practical concern. The very act of a Muggle even <i>meeting</i> a giant, let alone having offspring with one, would be a breach of Clause 73 of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a>. Such a union after the events of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> became even less likely; Muggle mountaineers who approach the colony of the last giants would meet their demise at the hands of these fearsome beings.
</p><p>Half-giants cannot use any potion that is only designed for human physiology, such as <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH5_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Oppression">Oppression</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-giant?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Oppression">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-giant?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Oppression"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many half-giants have been discriminated against, as with <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaurs</a>, and other half-humans. This prejudice was notoriously demonstrated by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, some <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">Pure-bloods</a>, and others like&#160;<a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP21_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP21-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> One example of this prejudice is then-<a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> accusing <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Olympe Maxime</a> of murdering <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Bartemius Crouch Senior</a> during his conversation with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> because of Crouch's disappearance taking place near the <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Carriage" title="Beauxbatons Carriage">Beauxbatons Carriage</a>, despite having no conclusive evidence to back up this claim and instead basing this suspicion around the violent nature that giants commonly have.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF29_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF29-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Pure giants also look down on half-giants due to their inferior sizes, as it is a giant's pride to give birth to large children.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF24_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF24-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Half-giants are rare, as Hagrid never thought he would meet another one before Madame Maxime. This suggests that the bonding between humans and giants is indeed rare, seeing animosity for giants as it is. Maxime desperately kept her heritage hidden to protect herself from prejudice.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF23_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF23-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
