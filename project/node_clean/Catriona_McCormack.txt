<b>Catriona McCormack</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who was a <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Pride_of_Portree" title="Pride of Portree">Pride of Portree</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">1960s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-QTA7_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTA7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Catriona_McCormack?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Catriona_McCormack?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She captained the team to two <a href="/wiki/League_Cup" title="League Cup">League Cups</a>. She played for the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_National_Quidditch_team" title="Scottish National Quidditch team">Scottish National Quidditch team</a> thirty-six times.<sup id="cite_ref-QTA7_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTA7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Catriona had two children, <a href="/wiki/Meaghan_McCormack" title="Meaghan McCormack">Meaghan McCormack</a> (current <a href="/wiki/Keeper" title="Keeper">Keeper</a> for the Pride of Portree), and <a href="/wiki/Kirley_Duke" title="Kirley Duke">Kirley Duke</a> (lead guitarist of the <a href="/wiki/Weird_Sisters" title="Weird Sisters">The Weird Sisters</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-QTA7_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTA7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Catriona McCormack was on the cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Times" title="Quidditch Times">Quidditch Times</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/29_February" title="29 February">29 February</a> <a href="/wiki/1964" title="1964">1964</a>, the magazine's 3000th issue, having been interviewed about her League wins and on "how to wear purple with flair".<sup id="cite_ref-QTAIE_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAIE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
