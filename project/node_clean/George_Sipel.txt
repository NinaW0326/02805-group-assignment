<b>George Sipel</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> <a href="/wiki/Pig" title="Pig">pig</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cattle" title="Cattle">cattle</a> dealer who lived in <a href="/wiki/Middlebush" title="Middlebush">Middlebush</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> in the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Sipel was subpoenaed to testify in the  the trial of <a href="/wiki/Hall%27s_wife" title="Hall&#39;s wife">Mrs Hall</a> for the <a href="/wiki/14_September" title="14 September">14 September</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> murders of <a href="/wiki/Hall" title="Hall">Rev. Mr Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mills" title="Mills">Mrs Mills</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Gibson" title="Gibson">Mrs Gibson</a> had reported that she had seen Mrs Hall in <a href="/wiki/De_Russey_Lane" title="De Russey Lane">De Russey Lane</a> with a male companion on the night of the murders.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Sipel was to be questioned as to whether he was driving the <a href="/wiki/Car" title="Car">automobile</a> that Mrs Gibson claimed illuminated the faces of Mrs Hall and her companion.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Mrs Gibson claimed that she heard that Sipel had told his friend, <a href="/wiki/John_Garry" title="John Garry">John Garry</a>, that he had seen her that night.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Sipel denied this claim and reportedly showed a "frantic eagerness" to avoid testifying.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The testimony of Sipel was said to call into question alternate testimony from <a href="/wiki/Nellie_Lo_Russell" title="Nellie Lo Russell">Nellie Lo Russell</a> on the whereabouts of Mrs Gibson on the night of the murders.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The trial was reported on in the <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> 1926 issue of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Chronicle" title="New York Chronicle">New York Chronicle</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
