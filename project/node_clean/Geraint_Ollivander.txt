<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Geraint Ollivander was born in the Middle Ages, somewhere in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. He was born into the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> <a href="/wiki/Ollivander_family" title="Ollivander family">Ollivander family</a>, who had been in the <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">wand-making business</a> since <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_a_wandmaker">Career as a wandmaker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Geraint took up the <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">family business</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, studying further into <a href="/wiki/Wandlore" title="Wandlore">wandlore</a> and becoming an accomplished wandmaker. He considered it an honour to match a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch or wizard</a> with a wand made from the <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wood</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Cypress" title="Cypress">cypress</a> tree because it indicated he had met someone who was going to die heroically, and left that fact in his writings.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Geraint_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Geraint" is a name of unknown origin, possibly a Welsh form of the Late Roman name "Gerontius" deriving from Greek γερων (geron), meaning "old man". <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geraint" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Geraint">Geraint</a> was the name of a figure various Welsh legends. He was also incorprorated into later Arthurian tales as one of the knights of the Round Table and the husband of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enide" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Enide">Enid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BTN_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTN-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
