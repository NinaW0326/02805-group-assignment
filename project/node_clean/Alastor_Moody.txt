<p><b>Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/27_July" title="27 July">27 July</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a><sup id="cite_ref-DH1_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, considered to be the most famous <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF11_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF11-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was a pivotal member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First</a> and <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding Wars</a>. Moody served with distinction during the first conflict, gaining a reputation as a skilful battler against the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> and sacrificing an eye, a leg, and part of his nose during the conflict.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As an Auror, he played a role in the imprisonment of many of the inhabitants of <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. This activity led to his gaining many enemies and resulted in his becoming overly-cautious and paranoid about his security later in life.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF11_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF11-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, he agreed to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s request to become the new <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> Professor at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-GOF11_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF11-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> however, he was kidnapped just prior to taking up the position by <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a>, who subsequently disguised himself as Moody as part of a plan to kidnap <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> on the instructions of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following his rescue from Barty Crouch Jnr, Moody joined the second Order of the Phoenix and fought in a number of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>'s battles, including the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP35_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP35-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a> in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> dying during the latter.<sup id="cite_ref-DH5_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His magical eye was later used by <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> to spy on her subordinates in the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a>. It was later retrieved by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> when he <a href="/wiki/Infiltration_of_the_British_Ministry_of_Magic_(1997)" title="Infiltration of the British Ministry of Magic (1997)">infiltrated</a> the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic_Headquarters" title="British Ministry of Magic Headquarters">Ministry of Magic</a> in 1997,<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and was given a proper burial by Harry in the forest close to where the <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">422nd Quidditch World Cup</a> had been held three years prior.<sup id="cite_ref-DH14_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH14-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_life_(pre_1960s)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_.28pre_1960s.29">Early life <span class="template-c">(pre 1960s)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life (pre 1960s)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life (pre 1960s)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alastor Moody was born into the <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Moody_family" title="Moody family">Moody family</a>. The precise date is unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-DH1_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_father" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s father">father</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_mother" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s mother">mother</a> were both <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-MoodyFactFile_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MoodyFactFile-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> as were many of his ancestors - the Moody family gained a reputation for producing many "renowned Aurors."<sup id="cite_ref-DH1_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moody bought or inherited his wand at some point before his formal magical education began. He attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, but his house is unrecorded in Ministry records.<sup id="cite_ref-DH1_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was known to be an excellent student however, gaining at least an <a href="/wiki/Exceeds_Expectations" title="Exceeds Expectations">'Exceeds Expectations'</a> mark in five <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a> subjects.<sup id="cite_ref-Auror_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Auror-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He eventually became an Auror and had three years of training like every traditional Auror.
</p>

<p>Moody, during his time as an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>, served the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, fighting <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> under Lord Voldemort's command. He was a key member of the Order of the Phoenix, led by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, in the struggle to defeat Voldemort, and his efforts were said to have led to half the cells in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> being populated by Death Eaters he captured.
</p><p>As the war continued, and the situation grew more fraught, the Ministry of Magic authorised their Aurors to use <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">Unforgivable Curses</a> against Death Eaters, a practice which continued for a time after Voldemort's fall from power. </p><p>It is believed that his activities during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> and his time as an Auror that led to Moody losing a leg (replaced by a wooden prosthesis). His work also led to significant facial scarring, including losing a piece of his nose in his fight with Rosier.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_Career">Further Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Further Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Further Career"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Moody likely continued hunting down and straggler Death Eaters. He in the post years after the war had some kind of battle that made him lose his left eye and it was replaced with magic prosthetic. He eventually retired from active Auror service and would take Nymphadora Tonks under his wing and helped in her Auror training.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span id="Meeting_Jacob's_sibling"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Meeting_Jacob.27s_sibling">Meeting Jacob's sibling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Meeting Jacob&#039;s sibling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Meeting Jacob&#039;s sibling"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/June" title="June">June</a> <a href="/wiki/1989" title="1989">1989</a>, he <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparated</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Three_Broomsticks_Inn" title="Three Broomsticks Inn">Three Broomsticks Inn</a> and told <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> they had much to talk about. He then Disapparated with the student, and swore them to secrecy. The two returned to the Three Broomsticks several hours later, when everyone except <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Khanna" title="Rowan Khanna">Rowan Khanna</a> had left to the graduation.<sup id="cite_ref-HM533_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM533-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Summer_holidays" title="Summer holidays">summer holidays</a>, Moody and Jacob's sibling met up at <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head_Inn" title="Hog&#39;s Head Inn">Hog's Head Inn</a>, where Moody revealed more information about <a href="/wiki/R" title="R">R</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Rakepick" title="Patricia Rakepick">Patricia Rakepick</a>'s in it and the group's goal to locate the final <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vault</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM61_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM61-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Later, Moody heard about the student's plan to sneak into the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> with an <a href="/wiki/Invisibility_cloak" title="Invisibility cloak">invisibility cloak</a> to get to Rakepick's artefacts, and went after them to stop them. Moody found Jacob's sibling in Hog's Head Inn under an invisibility cloak, and informed them that it would not be enough to fool the Ministry. Moody told Jacob's sibling to prepare to fight for their life.<sup id="cite_ref-HM612_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM612-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Moody was convinced to come out of retirement by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and serve as <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>'s latest Defence Against the Dark Arts teacher. Around this time, Moody's violently tore apart a birthday present paranoid that it may be a cleverly disguised <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_egg" title="Basilisk egg">Basilisk egg</a>; it turned out only to be a carriage clock.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF17_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF17-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%931995_school_year" title="1994–1995 school year">school year</a> began, however, Moody was attacked in his home by Barty Crouch Jnr and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s order. Moody proved more difficult to overcome than initially thought, and put up a significant fight which alerted his Muggle neighbours, but was overpowered and imprisoned in his own magical trunk. This was done as part of Voldemort's plan to regain power.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>To this end, Barty Crouch Jnr used Polyjuice Potion to take on Moody's form to replace him at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, successfully fooling staff and pupils alike. Barty Crouch Jnr drank Polyjuice Potion out of Moody's hipflask regularly, in imitation of the real Moody, and expressed many of Moody's normal habits. Doing this allowed him to impersonate Moody for almost the whole year without raising suspicion. All the while, Crouch did his part in Voldemort's plan. Moody's life was spared due to Crouch needing his hair to create the Polyjuice Potion, and wanting to question Moody for his behaviour, patterns and such, so Crouch could successfully imitate him, all the while keeping Moody captive under the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Working hard and using all of his cunning, Crouch-as-Moody ensured that <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> would get into the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> and ascend to the Third Task. Upon doing so, Voldemort used Harry Potter to <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">return</a> to life in a body of his own with all its powers once again. When Crouch learned of this upon Harry's return, he tried to murder him, but Dumbledore, <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> stopped him. The professors waited patiently for the Polyjuice Potion to wear off, revealing Crouch's true form. Under the influence of <a href="/wiki/Veritaserum" title="Veritaserum">Veritaserum</a>, he admitted everything and Moody was freed from his trunk.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After recuperating in the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a>, Moody returned to health in time to sit at the staff table for the End of Term Feast as he was the designated DADA teacher, but obviously never taught.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF37_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF37-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He was, if possible, even more, paranoid for his safety than before;<sup id="cite_ref-GOF37_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF37-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and his eye occasionally got stuck as a result of Crouch having worn it.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>As it turned out, Moody's over-protectiveness of Harry Potter would be his doom. Shortly before Harry's seventeenth birthday, Moody and a team of seven <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> members tried to move Harry away from <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a> to the<a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow"> Burrow</a>, using broomsticks, <a href="/wiki/Thestral" title="Thestral">Thestrals</a> and a flying motorcycle to reach a network of carefully-placed <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">Portkeys</a>, when the original plan proved useless following <a href="/wiki/Pius_Thicknesse" title="Pius Thicknesse">Pius Thicknesse</a>'s subversion. Moody was partnered with a reluctant <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher" title="Mundungus Fletcher">Mundungus Fletcher</a> who didn't want to participate in the move out of fear.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>

Fletcher panicked, crying out, and Moody tried to stop him, but Mundungus <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Disapparated</a>, leaving Moody vulnerable to attack. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-mortem_and_legacy">Post-mortem and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-mortem and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Post-mortem and legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Harry could not quite comprehend it. Mad-Eye dead; it could not be... Mad-Eye, so tough, so brave, the consummate survivor.</i>"</span></dd>

<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The Ministry, meanwhile, did not report Mad-Eye's death, as <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a> did not want to admit the truth about Voldemort's power, or the recent Azkaban breakout (which had resulted in the large numbers of Death Eaters attacking the Order).<sup id="cite_ref-DH6_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH6-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moody's broken body was quickly retrieved by the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, who were prompt in tidying up after themselves. Bill and <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> left to recover it but failed, as it had been difficult to know where Moody might have fallen, given the darkness and the confusion of the battle. As a result, the Order could not hold a funeral for Moody. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> held out hope for Moody's survival, but Harry and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a> promptly pointed out the flaws in his logic, and so all, in the end, accepted the truth, but the shock and sadness were great for all.<sup id="cite_ref-DH6_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH6-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 253px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/db/Moody-eye-ministry.png/revision/latest?cb=20101223011654" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="251" height="121" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Moody-eye-ministry.png" data-image-key="Moody-eye-ministry.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/db/Moody-eye-ministry.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/251?cb=20101223011654" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/db/Moody-eye-ministry.png/revision/latest?cb=20101223011654" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/db/Moody-eye-ministry.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/251?cb=20101223011654" decoding="async" width="251" height="121" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Moody-eye-ministry.png" data-image-key="Moody-eye-ministry.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/db/Moody-eye-ministry.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/251?cb=20101223011654" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Moody-eye-ministry.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Moody's eye on Umbridge's office door</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Mad-Eye's magical eye was salvaged by the Death Eaters and then, after the fall of the Ministry, given to the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Head_of_Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Head of Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, possibly as a gift. It was set in the wood of her office door and used to aid her in spying on her workers. When Harry, Hermione and Ron <a href="/wiki/Infiltration_of_the_British_Ministry_of_Magic_(1997)" title="Infiltration of the British Ministry of Magic (1997)">infiltrated</a> the Ministry to find the <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">locket Horcrux</a>, Harry discovered the eye and, disgusted by how it was being used, stole it. Doing so, however, raised the alarm.<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> After escaping, Harry, early the next morning and before the other two were awake, left the tent they were staying in to search the woods around them for the oldest, most gnarled and resilient-looking tree he could find. Then, in its shadow, he buried the eye and marked the spot by gouging a small cross in the bark with his wand. While it wasn't much, Mad-Eye Moody was given a proper burial.<sup id="cite_ref-DH14_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH14-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of his influence, the members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order</a> seemingly adopted Moody's paranoia-based caution, as Lupin asked Harry and others a security question after they made it to <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH5_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and Harry later did the same on Lupin in <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH11_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH11-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Ron also thought there was nobody else they could trust when <a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood" title="Xenophilius Lovegood">Xenophilius Lovegood</a> betrayed them.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ironically, after the defeat of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> followed in Mad-Eye's footsteps by becoming an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-JKRS_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRS-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Calamity">The <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Calamity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: The Calamity"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, Mad-Eye appeared as a <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundable</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a>. Volunteer members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> were able to free him, allowing Mad-Eye to return to his original time.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_description">Physical description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Physical description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 191px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c8/Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20111008214149" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="189" height="185" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Mad Eye Moody.jpg" data-image-key="Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c8/Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/189?cb=20111008214149" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c8/Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20111008214149" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c8/Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/189?cb=20111008214149" decoding="async" width="189" height="185" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Mad Eye Moody.jpg" data-image-key="Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c8/Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/189?cb=20111008214149" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Mad_Eye_Moody.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody's mangled face</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Moody was described as having a growling voice and looking as though his face was roughly carved from wood. Due to injuries from his long career as an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>, it was covered with scars, and a chunk of his nose was missing. He had dark grey, grizzled hair. He also had a wooden leg that was described as having a clawed foot.
</p><p>His eyes, however, were described as his most shocking feature: one was small and dark while the other was a vivid, <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_magical_eye" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s magical eye">electric blue magical eye</a> that moved around independently from his normal eye, giving him the nickname of "Mad-Eye Moody", or simply "Mad-Eye". This eye could see through objects, <a href="/wiki/Invisibility_cloak" title="Invisibility cloak">Invisibility cloaks</a> and the back of his own head.
</p>

<p>Alastor Moody was tough, brave and a consummate survivor, as well as an excellent fighter. He had a gruff, surly disposition. Having encountered many dangerous experiences throughout <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">his career</a>, he was hyper-paranoid and thought of by many as being delusional.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF11_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF11-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GOF17_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF17-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moody prepared all of his own meals and drank only from his <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_hip_flask" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s hip flask">personal flask</a> in case someone tried to <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poison</a> him.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He was also very distrusting of most people, being very careful of whom he relayed facts about his personal life to. He kept what he considered to be important information back even from his colleagues and superiors in the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, such as his birthplace and which house he was sorted into during his attendance at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. The latter probably with keeping in mind that the house could identify some of his more essential personality traits, making it easier for the enemy to find weaknesses in his psychological defences.
</p><p>Despite his paranoia, Moody was a brave individual. He never hesitated to go up against powerful dark wizards, including Lord Voldemort himself. Indeed his plan for getting Harry to safety involved using himself as a decoy knowing that Voldemort would likely go after him. This demonstrates both confidence in his own skill and a willingness to protect the rest of the Order members from Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It's likely that he knew that he could easily be killed during this encounter suggesting that he didn't fear death. Indeed he seemed to see being killed in battle as something to be proud of, as shown by his proud descriptions of the deaths of members of the previous Order of the Phoenix. At the same time, he was far from being devoid of attachment towards his allies and comrades: He was visibly saddened by the insanity of <a href="/wiki/Alice_and_Frank_Longbottom" title="Alice and Frank Longbottom">Alice and Frank Longbottom</a>, noting how being dead would have be better than having to live the rest of their lives insane, and openly acknowledged he was never able to find it easy to get over the loss of a comrade and only by turning the sadness he felt into motivation to get justice was he able to move on, as seen by his expressing sympathy towards <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> after they lost <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Khanna" title="Rowan Khanna">Rowan Khanna</a> and even acknowledging he should have trained them well enough.
</p><p>He also had a well-known habit of being set off by sudden noises or movements in his presence and even attacked a witch who shouted “boo” at him on April Fool’s Day. He even destroyed a birthday present carriage clock, believing it was a cleverly disguised <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_egg" title="Basilisk egg">basilisk egg</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GOF17_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF17-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and was described in a Daily Prophet article as having left the Ministry when deemed "<i>Unable to tell the difference between a handshake and attempted murder.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-GOF13_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF13-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> This only intensified after he was freed from nine months of being imprisoned inside <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Trunk" title="Moody&#39;s Trunk">his own trunk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moody was a great wizard in his day and the most effective <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a> hunter that the Ministry of Magic ever had. Nearly half of the cells in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> were full because of Moody.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF11_10-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF11-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He had the ability to think like a Dark wizard, which helped him track so many down. This also allowed him to outwit Voldemort during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a> where he accurately predicted that Voldemort would believe Harry to be guarded by the most powerful members.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He was also a staunchly loyal friend and supporter of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and one of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>'s most powerful and reliable allies.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP30_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP30-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He despised those who practised the Dark Arts, labelling such people as "filth", and relished the idea of Dark wizards being left at the mercy of the Dementors.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alastor Moody was said to be the most skilled <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>. Despite his skills being somewhat diminished by the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, he was still a highly valued and respected member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>. Even <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> acknowledged Moody as one of the most powerful members of the Order following Dumbledore's death.
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magical</a> mastery</b>: As a <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> student, Moody had excelled in his studies, and developed an extraordinary skill in many disciplines of magic, qualifying for <a href="/wiki/Auror_training" title="Auror training">Auror training</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>. This speaks for his versatility and his extensive expertise in many magical disciplines, which is a very big accomplishment for a sorcerer. After all, while most wizards are skilled in different areas of magic, but are best at one specific kind, Moody reportedly had fully mastered several. Ultimately, through his work as both an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> member, Moody became widely known as one of the greatest wizards of modern times.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> skills</b>: Alastor Moody completed the <a href="/wiki/Concealment_and_Disguise" title="Concealment and Disguise">Concealment and Disguise</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Stealth_and_Tracking" title="Stealth and Tracking">Stealth and Tracking</a> portions of <a href="/wiki/Auror_training" title="Auror training">Auror training</a> with excellence, with him eventually becoming considered by many to be the most powerful Auror of all time. Moody was thus extremely capable in both offensive and defensive magic, the hallmarks of the Auror's trade, which he employed so effectively that Moody personally ensured that half the cells of <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> were filled with <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, and was trusted by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> with the most important missions. Also, Moody somehow managed to keep his birthplace and Hogwarts house a secret even from his colleagues and Ministry superiors. Additionally, Moody was an expert on <a href="/wiki/Dark_Detector" title="Dark Detector">Dark Detectors</a>, owning a <a href="/wiki/Foe-Glass" title="Foe-Glass">Foe-Glass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Secrecy_Sensor" title="Secrecy Sensor">Secrecy Sensor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sneakoscope" title="Sneakoscope">Sneakoscope</a>, among others. These skills also allowed Moody to be by far one of the most prolific members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> in both the First and <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding Wars</a>. In fact, Moody's Auror reputation was enough to intimidate even <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, who avoided bullying (who he thought was) Moody like he did to all other <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professors. Moreover, Moody was capable of placing immensely powerful protective enchantments and curses on the headquarters of the Order as a means of warding off Death Eaters, wards of such strength, that not even <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> himself could penetrate them. Moody's immense skills allowed him to later mentor <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, helping her become a highly skilled Auror in her own right.</li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: Alastor Moody was an extremely skilled duellist, since in the midst of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> and in its aftermath, Moody utilised <a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">martial magic</a> to fight and defeat dozens of skilled <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a> being among them.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Moody's ability to switch from offence to defence made him an immensely formidable opponent. He was also known to cast spells with his <a href="/wiki/Walking_stick" title="Walking stick">Walking stick</a>, which gave Moody an extra advantage in case he was deprived of his wand. Moody often restrained himself from inflicting fatal injuries, and tried to take his opponents alive, which may have resulted in his permanent physical injuries: mutilated face, loss of an eye, and loss of a leg. Indeed, Moody's reputation as a duellist was enough to intimidate even <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, himself a master duellist. After years of retirement, Moody's duelling skills had apparently grown rusty, which diminished his effectiveness when he once again began participating in magical combat during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. Hence, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> were able to subdue Moody at his home, despite his owning several <a href="/wiki/Dark_Detector" title="Dark Detector">Dark Detectors</a> that could warn him of danger, and they beat Moody before <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> agents could arrive on the scene. Despite this, Moody evidently managed to put up a considerable fight against the two Death Eaters, as Crouch later stated that he and Pettigrew had difficulty subduing Moody, and only managed to do it just before the Ministry agents arrived. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a>, Moody fought two <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> simultaneously, defeated one of them with a single nonverbal spell, and later survived a duel with <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a>, among the most powerful of Death Eaters, although Moody was injured and beaten by him. Despite this, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> himself considered Moody to be the most skilled Auror during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>, and the one most likely guarding the real <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. He personally targeted Moody, and killed him, before pursuing <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>. It should be noted, however, that the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> Voldemort cast only hit Moody because <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher" title="Mundungus Fletcher">Mundungus Fletcher</a>, the intended target, had <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Disapparated</a>. Given that he seemed certain that Voldemort would go after him suggests that he felt confident enough in his abilities to hold off the most powerful dark wizard at least long enough to get himself and Fletcher to safety.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Being considered the greatest Auror of all time, Moody would have received either an "<a href="/wiki/Outstanding" title="Outstanding">Outstanding</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Exceeds_Expectations" title="Exceeds Expectations">Exceeds Expectations</a>" in his <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a> for this subject,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> with Moody's capability and knowledge in defending against the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> being extreme. This mastery can be seen by his ability to defeat many <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizards</a> and witches as an Auror. He may also have been considerably capable in breaking and countering curses, so as to be able to negate the dark spells performed by his enemies. Even though Moody's duelling abilities had grown rusty during his retirement, his mastery of defending against the Dark Arts was still exceptional, as Dumbledore (himself formerly a professor of Defence Against the Dark Arts) held Moody's abilities in high enough esteem that he hired Moody to teach <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> for one year, and Moody was skilled enough in defensive magical wards that he personally set up the defences on <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>, defences powerful enough that even <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> could not penetrate them. Moreover, Moody was an expert at fending off dark creatures, with him volunteering to get rid of a <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a> for <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP9_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: While Moody usually refrained from using dark magic, inflicting fatal injuries on his opponents, and killing them if it could be avoided throughout his time as an Auror, since the use of <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">Unforgivable Curses</a> and other forms of Dark Magic were legalised by <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Bartemius Crouch Snr</a>, to torture or kill <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> rather than capture them in the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, Moody was capable of practising dark magic, despite not being a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>. Although the extent of his abilities in this field is unknown, there are pieces of evidence to prove that Moody had a rather considerable amount of ability and knowledge, as shown by the fact that he killed <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a> when the latter resisted capture, likely by either the use of the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> or another curse which could inflict fatal injuries on his opponent,<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and by the fact that Moody attacked a witch who startled him during an April Fool’s Day celebration (although the type of spell he used is unknown). Moody may even be powerful enough in the field of magic to be capable of performing the Unforgivable Curses, considering that <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> while <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">disguised</a> as Moody, was able to demonstrate the Unforgivable Curses while teaching <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> and practise the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> on his students to build up their resistance, without arousing suspicion from even <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>. This also hints that Moody was willing to demonstrate the effects of Curses on people if he taught them about the dangers of dark magic, as long as the curses did not harm people seriously in the process. However, this is ultimately debatable, as Crouch Jnr managed to temporarily fool Dumbledore of his true identity, thereby possibly deceiving Dumbledore into believing that he just taught the Unforgivable Curses, rather than directly demonstrating them, and practising the Imperius Curse on his students. Nevertheless, Moody's knowledge of dark magic was likely great, which aided Moody in his time as an Auror to counteract curses from opponents, as well as setting up <a href="/wiki/Dark_Detector" title="Dark Detector">Dark Detectors</a> at his home. Furthermore, Moody was capable of using dark magic in a protective capacity, as he used the <a href="/wiki/Tongue-Tying_Curse" title="Tongue-Tying Curse">Tongue-Tying Curse</a> as one of the protections held on <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>, which worked successfully on anyone who entered the house.<sup id="cite_ref-DH9_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH9-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: As an Auror, Moody was highly proficient in charm-work, since as an Auror, he would received at least an <a href="/wiki/Exceeds_Expectations" title="Exceeds Expectations">Exceeds Expectations</a> in his O.W.L. and N.E.W.T. for this <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">subject</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and was capable of performing the <a href="/wiki/Disillusionment_Charm" title="Disillusionment Charm">Disillusionment Charm</a>, a highly advanced and powerful charm which would camouflage the target.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Moreover, Moody did not require an <a href="/wiki/Invisibility_cloak" title="Invisibility cloak">Invisibility cloak</a> to make himself and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> invisible when acting as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Advance_Guard" title="Advance Guard">Advance Guard</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and he was an expert on the <a href="/wiki/Boggart-Banishing_Spell" title="Boggart-Banishing Spell">Boggart-Banishing Spell</a>, given his offer to take care of the <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a> in <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP9_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Moody was skilled enough in charms to cast them from his <a href="/wiki/Walking_stick" title="Walking stick">Walking stick</a>, which gave Moody an extra advantage, in case he was deprived of his wand. It's also likely that he himself applied the powers to his <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_magical_eye" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s magical eye">magical eye</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Moody%27s_Trunk" title="Moody&#39;s Trunk">magical trunk</a> as they seem exclusive only to him. In addition, Moody could also conjure a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> a mark of superior <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability and testament to his expertise and talent in charms, given the immense difficulty of casting such an advanced and powerful protective spell.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a></b>: As an Auror, Moody was proficient in transfiguration, as he had to have received at least an Exceeds Expectations in his O.W.L. and N.E.W.T. for this <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">subject</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> When Barty Crouch Jnr easily <a href="/wiki/Human_to_ferret_spell" title="Human to ferret spell">transfigured</a> Malfoy into a ferret, <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">McGonagall</a> was not suspicious, meaning Moody would have likely been capable of doing the same feat.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF13_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF13-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: As an Auror, Moody was proficient in potion-making, as he had to have received at least an Exceeds Expectations in his O.W.L. and N.E.W.T. for this <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">subject</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Indeed, Moody was capable of brewing the highly complex <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a>, making enough of it to disguise all seven of the willing participants of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a> as <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, to ensure the latter's safety, showing his great talent in this field.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a></b>: As an Auror, Moody was skilled in Herbology, as he had to have received at least an Exceeds Expectations in his O.W.L. and N.E.W.T. for this subject.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a></b>: Moody, like most accomplished adult wizards, could Apparate at will. He originally intended to bring Harry away from the Dursley's through side-along apparition.</li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: Moody was an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> flyer, as he flew his broomstick while acting as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Advance_Guard" title="Advance Guard">Advance Guard</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and fought in mid-air during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>, which required a high level of flying skill.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, Moody was ultimately not skilled (or fortunate) enough to escape <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, and was blasted off his broom by the Dark Lord's <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a>, though this could've partially been due to Mundungus Fletcher's desertion.<sup id="cite_ref-DH5_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">Wandless</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">nonverbal magic</a></b>: Despite being somewhat rusty from years of not applying his magic in his retirement, Moody had evidently remained an extremely powerful and skilled wizard, as he could effectively perform nonverbal magic, was able to summon brooms, duel <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, and even cast the highly advanced <a href="/wiki/Disillusionment_Charm" title="Disillusionment Charm">Disillusionment Charm</a> without saying the <a href="/wiki/Incantation" title="Incantation">incantation</a>. Moody also demonstrated powerful wandless magic with his <a href="/wiki/Walking_stick" title="Walking stick">Walking stick</a>, as it gave Moody an extra advantage, in case he was deprived of his wand.</li>
<li><b>Leadership skills</b>: Moody had exceptional leadership skills, evident from his status as a very prominent Auror in the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>, from the fact that Moody lead the <a href="/wiki/Advance_Guard" title="Advance Guard">Advance Guard</a>, and from Moody being the one to lead the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> following <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s death.</li>
<li><b>Teaching skills</b>: While Moody never had the chance to teach <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> since <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Bartemius Crouch Junior</a> did his best to convincingly imitate Moody's behaviour and habits, it can be deduced that Moody would have been a very effective professor, since <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> learned from the fake Moody to resist the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a> noted that the fake Moody had taught them a great deal, and Dumbledore was not suspicious of Crouch's success in teaching the students. Moreover, Moody mentored his protégée <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> in becoming a highly skilful <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> in her own right, showing him to have been an excellent teacher himself.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Indomitable willpower</b>: Moody possessed immense willpower and bravery, being considered the greatest Auror of all time, having great respect in the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>, and fighting in both Wizarding Wars on the front lines, thus showcasing an unbreakable spirit. As such, Moody excelled at intimidating others, notably <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">Vernon Dursley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a>, and even <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> also admitted that (who he thought was) Moody "scared him slightly." However, this would also induce Moody to come off as a hyper-paranoid consummate survivor, scanning his surroundings with his magical eye, preparing all of his own meals, drinking only from his personal flask,<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> keeping his birthplace and Hogwarts house a secret even from his colleagues and Ministry superiors, and being set off by sudden noises or movements. Indeed, Moody even attacked a witch for shouting "boo" at him during April Fools Day, and destroyed a birthday carriage clock when he thought it might have been a disguised <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_egg" title="Basilisk egg">Basilisk egg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF17_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF17-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_magical_eye" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s magical eye">Magical Eye</a></b>: Moody's most notable possession was his magical eye, which was electric blue and earned him his nickname "Mad-Eye". This eye allows him to see through anything, such as wood, invisibility cloaks (including Harry Potter's invisibility cloak which was one of the <a href="/wiki/Deathly_Hallows" title="Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a>) and the back of his head. Ever since it was worn by <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch</a> the eye tended to get stuck in its socket.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_Wand" class="mw-redirect" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s Wand">Wand</a></b>: Like most wizards and witches, Moody owned and carried a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> If he was ever deprived of his wand he would use his staff instead.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTPF_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTPF-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Invisibility_cloak" title="Invisibility cloak">Invisibility cloaks</a></b>: Moody possessed two invisibility cloaks, though he lent one to <a href="/wiki/Sturgis_Podmore" title="Sturgis Podmore">Sturgis Podmore</a>, which was never returned.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP9_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It is also possible that Moody's travelling cloak was one of his invisibility cloaks.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_trunk" class="mw-redirect" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s trunk">Magical trunk</a></b>: Moody possessed a magical trunk, with seven compartments (one in which Barty Couch Jnr hid the real Moody during his year at Hogwarts), along with the keys for each compartment.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Foe-Glass" title="Foe-Glass">Foe-Glass</a></b>: Alastor's Foe-Glass showed the images of foes when they approached him.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sneakoscope" title="Sneakoscope">Sneakoscope</a></b>: Moody's Sneakoscope was large, and Barty Crouch, impersonating Moody, said that he disabled it because it was constantly spinning and whistling due to the large number of students who were practising untrustworthy behaviour in the vicinity. In reality, it was constantly signalling that Barty Crouch was committing a deception.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Secrecy_Sensor" title="Secrecy Sensor">Secrecy Sensor</a></b>: Moody's Secrecy sensor would detect secrets and lies.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_walking_stick" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s walking stick">Walking Stick</a></b>: Moody's walking stick had magical capabilities and if he was ever deprived of his wand he would use his staff instead.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTPF_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTPF-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_hip_flask" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s hip flask">Silver Drinking Flask</a></b>: Moody's paranoia led to him believing an enemy may be poisoning his food behind his back, so he drank from his personal flask to prevent that from happening. <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> filled it with <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a> during his year in disguise and was able to drink without arousing suspicion.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_broomstick" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s broomstick">Broomstick</a></b>: Moody flew his broomstick while acting as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Advance_Guard" title="Advance Guard">Advance Guard</a><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and fighting during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Wooden Leg</b>: At some point during his career, Alastor lost one of his legs and therefore relied on a wooden leg to replace his lost one.</li></ul>

</p><p>When Tonks was injured during her duel with Bellatrix Lestrange in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>, Mad-Eye crawled his way to her side in an effort to help her regain consciousness, despite having been injured himself by <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a>. <p>Alastor Moody was one of Albus Dumbledore's closest friends. They fought alongside each other in many battles, but Moody teased Dumbledore for hating <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>. In 1994, Moody accepted Dumbledore's offer to teach Defence Against the Dark Arts for a year, but was captured and imprisoned by Barty Crouch Jnr, who impersonated him during the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%931995_school_year" title="1994–1995 school year">1994-1995 school year</a>, in order to send Harry Potter to Lord Voldemort. Dumbledore was able to deduce the impersonation once Crouch lost his composure from over-excitement. Dumbledore and Moody fought together in the Battle of the Department of Mysteries. Moody was the next member of the order to die after Dumbledore.

Upon first meeting the real Moody (which made Harry felt strange due to having spent so much time with an impostor), Harry thought that Moody was a little mad and overprotective, as he suspected everybody, and didn't even let Harry carry his wand in his back pocket, saying that better wizards than him had lost buttocks.

While it wasn't much, Harry felt that Mad-Eye Moody was given a proper burial and would have appreciated this act of kindness. Harry avenged Moody when he killed Voldemort at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p><p>While Harry's first impression of the real Moody was that he was overly paranoid with the extra security, he would later adopt this mentality during the rising stakes of the Second Wizarding War, questioning those around him to ensure they are not impostors.
</p>

<p>Alastor Moody was always on good terms with the Weasley family. He was friendly with <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>, whom he had met through the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. Arthur had a high opinion of Moody and thought he was a little mad, but he was a great wizard and the most famous Auror of his time. Arthur and Moody fought together in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>. When Moody was killed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, Arthur was very sad.
</p><p>Moody also seemed to be friendly with <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a>. Although they didn't have a close relationship, Molly knew about him and knew Moody was a great Auror. It could well be that, as a member of the original Order of the Phoenix, he had at least known her brothers, <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Prewett" title="Fabian Prewett">Fabian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gideon_Prewett" title="Gideon Prewett">Gideon Prewett</a>, reasonably well, whether or not they were truly friends. <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Fred and George</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> also thought Moody was a great wizard and thought he taught them great magic. However, in fact, it wasn't Moody, but <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a>, who was disguised as Moody. It is also possible that it was the kind of magic that the real Moody would teach.
</p><p>He was also friendly with <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charlie</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a>. After he was killed, the Weasley family was devastated. Bill, along with <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>, went to search for Moody's dead body.
</p>

<p>Moody was a member of the Order of the Phoenix in both wizarding wars. He was friendly with his most fellow members of the orders, despite his overprotective behaviour and his madness. Except <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, Albus Dumbledore, Harry Potter, and the Weasley family, Moody was also a friend of <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>. Lupin and Moody met each other during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, but they also fought in several battles of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. When Moody was killed, Remus was devastated and attempted to search for his dead body.
</p><p>Moody was also a friend of <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>. They fought together at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> and the Battle of the Seven Potters. Kingsley was also devastated by Moody's death and became the leader of the order after the battle.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> also spoke highly of Moody, respecting him for his abilities and for not killing Death Eaters unless necessary. The two fought together in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> where Sirius was killed. Moody was likely saddened by his death.
</p><p>He was also friendly with <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>. Hermione and Moody fought at several battles together, but, when Moody was killed, Hermione was devastated.
</p><p>He might have been also a friend of: <a href="/wiki/Dedalus_Diggle" title="Dedalus Diggle">Dedalus Diggle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emmeline_Vance" title="Emmeline Vance">Emmeline Vance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hestia_Jones" title="Hestia Jones">Hestia Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sturgis_Podmore" title="Sturgis Podmore">Sturgis Podmore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elphias_Doge" title="Elphias Doge">Elphias Doge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher" title="Mundungus Fletcher">Mundungus Fletcher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a>. Sirius was murdered by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> during the Battle of the Department of Mysteries, after he, Moody and three other attempted to battle the Death Eaters, in order to save Harry and his friends.
</p><p>Even though <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> was a member of the Order of the Phoenix after defecting from his role as a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> and Dumbledore trusted him completely, Moody was sceptical over Snape's true loyalties to Dumbledore and the Order. Moody was visibly displeased when Dumbledore defended Snape during <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a>'s trial but due to his friendship and loyalty with Dumbledore, never questioned Dumbledore directly about his trust for Snape. Due to Moody's distrust of Snape, <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Bartemius Crouch Jnr</a> used that knowledge while impersonating Moody to intimidate Snape and told Snape that while Dumbledore trusted him there were "spots that don't come off", presumably referring to Snape's Dark Mark and his former position as a Death Eater. After Snape killed Dumbledore, Moody's distrust and hatred for Snape increased, unaware that Dumbledore had secretly planned for Snape to kill him and that Snape was still loyal to the Order, and he was responsible for setting up protective enchantments against Snape in <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a> which lasted even after his death.
</p><p>Vance was killed by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, while Podmore was imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> for attempting to open the door of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>. Moody didn't know it and was angry with Podmore, because he had taken his Invisibility Cloak.
</p><p>Mundungus was paired along with Moody during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_11-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> When Voldemort attacked them, Mundungus was frightened and disappeared, abandoning Moody, who was killed by Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-DH5_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some of the members of the order were killed during the First Wizarding War. These included <a href="/wiki/Marlene_McKinnon" title="Marlene McKinnon">Marlene McKinnon</a>, who was killed along with her family, <a href="/wiki/Caradoc_Dearborn" title="Caradoc Dearborn">Caradoc Dearborn</a>, who was murdered by Death Eaters and his body was never found, <a href="/wiki/Benjy_Fenwick" title="Benjy Fenwick">Benjy Fenwick</a>, who was killed and mutilated, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Bones" title="Edgar Bones">Edgar Bones</a>, who was killed along with his family, <a href="/wiki/Dorcas_Meadowes" title="Dorcas Meadowes">Dorcas Meadowes</a>, who was murdered by Voldemort himself, <a href="/wiki/Gideon_Prewett" title="Gideon Prewett">Gideon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Prewett" title="Fabian Prewett">Fabian Prewett</a>, who were killed by five Death Eaters, and <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Potter</a>, who were killed by Voldemort. However, <a href="/wiki/Alice_Longbottom" title="Alice Longbottom">Alice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Longbottom" title="Frank Longbottom">Frank Longbottom</a> were tortured into insanity by four Death Eaters; <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> and Rodolphus and Rabastan Lestrange. Moody was evidently saddened by their fates and noted that death would have been better and much easier to handle than the lack of sanity with which they would have to live until they die.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP9_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Most members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> were devastated after Moody was killed,<sup id="cite_ref-DH5_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and many of them were killed, too, by the end of the Second Wizarding War.
</p><p>Although originally some members of the Order thought Moody was being overly paranoid with his extra security, and simply humoured him passively, during the rising stakes of the Second Wizarding War, especially after Alastor was killed, the other members took after his methods, mainly asking questions that only other members would be aware of; Lupin even acted violently and cautiously when asking Harry such a security question.
</p>

<p>During his years as an Auror, Moody captured and defeated many <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a>. According to <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, Moody was very cruel with the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> he fought against, but he never killed if he could avoid it.
</p><p>Moody captured Karkaroff after Voldemort's downfall. He also participated in Karkaroff's trial and told Dumbledore that they should take information from Karkaroff and then send him back to the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_12-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> However, Dumbledore refused. Moody and Karkaroff met again in 1994, during the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. In fact, he wasn't the real Alastor Moody, but <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a>, a Death Eater. After <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> was chosen for the Tournament, Karkaroff got very angry, because Hogwarts had two champions. When Barty (disguised as Moody) told him how the Goblet of Fire might have been fooled, Karkaroff told him that he thought like a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>. Barty answered that it was his job to capture Dark Wizards and perhaps he remembered, meaning that Moody and Karkaroff knew each other well.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF17_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF17-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown how the real Moody reacted when he learned that Karkaroff was killed by Death Eaters.
</p><p>Moody also held a grudge against <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> for abducting, imprisoning and impersonating him, and was furious that Crouch ruined his magical eye by wearing it, causing it to get stuck from time to time.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_5-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> was another Death Eater who attacked and subdued Moody.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Both Peter and Moody fought against the Death Eaters together during the First Wizarding War, though it's unknown what kind of relationship they had. It's also not known how Moody reacted upon learning that Peter was alive and actually a Death Eater.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?veaction=edit&amp;section=27" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody?action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Alastor" is Ancient Greek for "he who does not forget", thus (1) "avenger", "persecutor", "tormenter", (2) "one who suffers from divine vengeance". It gives him, therefore, certain similarities to <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto Carrow</a>, whose name is that of the first of the Furies, or Erinyes, and means "implacable anger".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> It is similar to "Alastair", which is the Scottish form of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Alexander">Alexander</a>, which means "protector of mankind", and also evolved into "Alistair".</li>
<li>Moody means temperamental or tending to quickly change moods.</li></ul>

<ul><li>Moody is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> actor <a href="/wiki/Brendan_Gleeson" title="Brendan Gleeson">Brendan Gleeson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">Order of the Phoenix</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>. His son <a href="/wiki/Domhnall_Gleeson" title="Domhnall Gleeson">Domhnall Gleeson</a> played <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Part 2</a></i>.</li>
<li>In the films, Moody is portrayed considerably different from his literary counterpart. Differences between his description in the book and his portrayal on film include his magical eye being held in place by an eye patch-like strap and brass ring, having a less mutilated face, including a full nose instead of a piece of it missing.</li>
<li>In the film adaptation, the magical eye replaced Moody's left eye. The illustration in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a> shows Moody's magical eye replaced his right eye and his nose is intact (see drawing under physical description above). The back cover illustration of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> also shows it in the right eye socket, while the bridge of his nose looks damaged. There's no written description of which leg and which eye were lost.</li>
<li>In the films he has hair that is blonde but greying rather than hair that is completely grey, and is somewhat overweight. His artificial leg in the films is made of metal rather than wood.</li>
<li>He also speaks with Gleeson's natural Irish accent, although his nationality appears to be Scottish, seeing as his impostor wears a kilt in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i> during the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>. His nationality is never identified in the book, although Moody is a surname common in both Ireland and Scotland, while Alastor (sometimes spelt Alistair) is a Scottish first name.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, Moody has a <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_broomstick" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s broomstick">specialised broom</a> and his wand is either concealed in his <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody%27s_walking_stick" title="Alastor Moody&#39;s walking stick">walking stick</a> or is the stick itself.</li>
<li>Also in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> scene, Alastor Moody can be momentarily glimpsed in the background duelling two Death Eaters wielding not only his staff in his right hand, but what appears to be a wand in his left.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>(Exact time in reference 1:53-1:57)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Noble_Collection" title="Noble Collection">Noble Collection</a> also made a replica of Alastor's wand, as seen in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>.</li>
<li>Alastor Moody is the only Professor of Defence Against the Dark Arts never seen before the start of the academic year, as Barty Crouch Jnr, disguised as Moody, first appears on 1 September, and the <i>real</i> Mad-Eye Moody appears only at the end of the year.</li>
<li>A keychain replica of Moody's magical eye was also released.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, it is Sirius, not Moody, who gives Harry the picture of the original <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> members.</li>
<li>"Constant Vigilance!" is Moody's supposed catchphrase. However, the phrase is never actually uttered by Moody, only by Barty Jnr when impersonating him. In the film adaptions, the phrase is omitted altogether (aside from one use that was edited out).</li>
<li>The scenes of Moody's magical eye being taken and used by <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, as well as Harry recovering and properly burying it have been omitted from <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> (footage of this may have been filmed, however, seeing as it is seen on Umbridge's door with no mention of it being used.)</li>
<li>According to his file, Alastor Moody was 6' 2" and weighed 183 lbs. Whether this weight accounted for his prosthetic leg (which, in the films where the file is seen, is made of metal and thus would presumably be significantly heavy) or not is unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-DH1_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>He says in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> that <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a> "tastes like <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a> piss" before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>.
<ul><li>To this one of the <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Weasley twins</a> says "Have a lot of experiences with that, do you Mad-Eye?", either referring to their 6th year and well aware that this was not the real Moody or joking that he knew what "goblin piss" tasted like, or both.</li></ul></li>
<li>It is possible that Moody took part in the capture of the Lestranges and Barty Crouch Jnr following their attack on Frank and Alice Longbottom. During the trial for the former, Moody is conspicuously absent despite being present during both Igor Karkaroff and Ludovic Bagman's trials. It may be during this skirmish that he lost his eye as he had both eyes during the former trials and he is said to have lost it in a battle against Death Eaters. It's possible that his absence during the Lestrange trials was due to suffering an injury to his eye, forcing him to replace it with his magical one.</li>
<li>Moody may have been currently the only wizard to know a <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a>'s true form: in fact, in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, he was asked by Molly Weasley to check if there was one inside a drawer. Since he used his magical eye, which can see through objects, it is possible the Boggart did not realise his presence and thus did not transform.</li>
<li>On  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i>, in the Moment <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.pottermore.com/writing-by-jk-rowling/moment1">"Grimmauld Place"</a>, the image of Alastor Moody is incorrectly identified in the page source as <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://assets.pottermore.com/site/html5/images/b5c4m1/items/dumbledore.png">"dumbledore.png"</a>, though this filename could possibly refer to the fact that Moody is giving Harry a note from Albus Dumbledore in this scene.</li></ul>
