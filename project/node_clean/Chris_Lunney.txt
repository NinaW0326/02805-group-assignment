<b>Chris Lunney</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> photographer who worked for the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Express" title="Daily Express">Daily Express</a></i>. Two of his photos were featured on the front page of an issue of the paper in the summer of <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>: the first was of heat-exhausted spectators at a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> show being tended by a nurse, in an article about an unprecedented heat wave that was then affecting <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> titled "Killer Heat," and the second was of a soap star with her hand raised to shield her face from the camera.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
