<p><b>Naasz</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and celebrity <a href="/wiki/Magizoologist" title="Magizoologist">Magizoologist</a>, noted for her relentless ego. She always wanted to be a headmistress and so opened the unaccredited Naaszcademy of Magizoology in Brussels.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Naasz?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Naasz?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, Naasz took members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> who pursued a <a href="/wiki/Magizoologist" title="Magizoologist">Magizoologist</a> profession on a journey to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> to find a wild <a href="/wiki/Phoenix" title="Phoenix">Phoenix</a> in its natural habitat. The experience of it, however, ended up being a test of patience due to the endless interviews they agreed to give, and her egotistical demeanour.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Naasz once tricked a <a href="/wiki/Calamity_Investigator" title="Calamity Investigator">Calamity Investigator</a> into transporting her store of personally-designed (and almost completely useless) magical artefacts to the Ministry of Magic for safekeeping. At one point she created or obtained a trinket described as a "green-dyed Hippogriff feather with tiny baubles hanging equidistantly around the spine." It was sent to a member of the Statute of Secrecy Task Force, who thought it looked like a rather cheap souvenir. Naasz insisted that it was a valuable artefact, and carrying it seemed to increase the Task Force volunteer's chances of keeping a Magizoological Trace in their sights.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In America, Naasz performed a one-woman, wordless stage play titled '<i>The Calamitous Calamity</i>'. It was emotionally stirring. She also hosted a relentless and sophomoric lecture about theoretical magic regarding harmonic attuning and energy.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Naasz once attempted to sell her own blend of tea, but the taste was revolting. She was convinced to sell it as a solvent instead, to the relief of teashop owners.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She once released a long-winded 21-page pamphlet detailing techniques for handling creatures commonly deployed by the Calamity as Confoundables, but only four things within were actually useful. Naasz also opened a school in Brussels on a whim, desiring the position of headmistress. It had no faculty or staff.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
