<p><b>Madam Professor Griselda Marchbanks</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, who served as Governor of the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_Examinations_Authority" title="Wizarding Examinations Authority">Wizarding Examinations Authority</a> that ran the <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">W.O.M.B.A.T.</a> examinations from the late <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a> to, at least, the early <a href="/wiki/21st_century" title="21st century">21st century</a>. Before resigning in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, Madam Marchbanks was one of the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> elders.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP31_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Griselda Marchbanks was born no later than the <a href="/wiki/1870s" title="1870s">1870s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP31_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She presumably attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a> in her youth, where she would have received her <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> education.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_life">Professional life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Professional life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Professional life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>Examined </i>[Dumbledore]<i> personally in Transfiguration and Charms when he did N.E.W.T.s… Did things with a wand I'd never seen before.</i>"</span></dd>

</p><p>At some point in her life, Marchbanks became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> and was, until 1995, one of its oldest members.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP15_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP15-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She was also a friend of <a href="/wiki/Augusta_Longbottom" title="Augusta Longbottom">Augusta Longbottom</a>, and was very much like her in terms of personality.
</p>
<h4><span id="1995–1996_school_year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1995.E2.80.931996_school_year">1995–1996 school year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1995–1996 school year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1995–1996 school year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>Hogwarts is a school, not an outpost of Cornelius Fudge's office. This is a further disgusting attempt to discredit Albus Dumbledore.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Griselda Marchbanks to the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Before the year began, Harry Potter (whom the Ministry had actively been trying to discredit along with Dumbledore) was brought before the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> to stand trial for using the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a> in the presence of a Muggle; the assembly likely included Madam Marchbanks. When Dumbledore appeared to defend Harry, two witches (possibly Marchbanks and another) waved at him in welcome, and when the verdict was to be given, she likely voted to clear Harry of all charges given her support for Dumbledore.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP10_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/8_September" title="8 September">8 September</a> <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, with the passage of <a href="/wiki/Educational_Decree_Number_Twenty-Three" title="Educational Decree Number Twenty-Three">Educational Decree Number Twenty-Three</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> was appointed <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_High_Inquisitor" title="Hogwarts High Inquisitor">High Inquisitor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> by then-<a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP15_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP15-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Professor Marchbanks, along with <a href="/wiki/Tiberius_Ogden" title="Tiberius Ogden">Tiberius Ogden</a>, resigned from the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> in protest of Umbridge's appointment, declaring to the <i>Daily Prophet</i> that "Hogwarts is a school, not an outpost of Cornelius Fudge's office" and cited it as a "further disgusting attempt to <a href="/wiki/Campaign_to_discredit_Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter" title="Campaign to discredit Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter">discredit</a> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>". Marchbanks was also accused of having ties to "<i>subversive <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a> groups</i>" on <a href="/wiki/9_September" title="9 September">9 September</a> by the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>. By this time, she was apparently hard of hearing and possibly somewhat senile.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP15_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP15-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/25/MarchbanksSubversiveGoblinGroups.png/revision/latest?cb=20130213012647" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/25/MarchbanksSubversiveGoblinGroups.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20130213012647" decoding="async" width="180" height="217" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="MarchbanksSubversiveGoblinGroups.png" data-image-key="MarchbanksSubversiveGoblinGroups.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:MarchbanksSubversiveGoblinGroups.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> article: "<i>Madam Marchbank</i> [sic] <i>Denies Links to Subversive Goblin Groups</i>"</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Later that school year, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Umbridge</a> became <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmistress</a> of Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> When Marchbanks arrived at the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Castle</a> with her colleagues from the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_Examinations_Authority" title="Wizarding Examinations Authority">W.E.A.</a> to administer the <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">Ordinary Wizarding Level</a> Examinations, she showed no signs of respect for Umbridge, dismissing her attempts at flattery and expressing scorn at the belief that the Ministry could catch <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP31_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She examined a variety of students in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s year, including <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a> practical, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> practical and all the fifth-years in the <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_(class)" title="Astronomy (class)">Astronomy</a> practicals; during the latter, she likely witnessed the failed assault on <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> and the near-fatal injury of Minerva McGonagall by Umbridge and several corrupt <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a>, including <a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish" title="John Dawlish">John Dawlish</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP31_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is not known whether she was reinstated in the Wizengamot after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> and subsequent sacking of <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Umbridge</a> or not.
</p><p>Marchbanks was said to be friends with <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> for many years, and in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, she went to <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a> for dinner. From the way Draco boasted about "it's who you know" and not "what you know", it was likely the dinner was arranged to win favour with Marchbanks and persuade her to award Draco a higher O.W.L. grade in the exam.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_war">After the war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: After the war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: After the war"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, for which she remained alive through, Marchbanks continued to work in the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_Examinations_Authority" title="Wizarding Examinations Authority">W.E.A.</a> in her post of Governor.<sup id="cite_ref-JKR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prof. Marchbanks was described as a short and very elderly witch.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She took her role as an examiner very seriously and conducted her tests fairly, not being swayed by close relationships or bribes despite what Draco Malfoy thought. She was a friend and supporter of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> having known him since testing him during his time as a student where she was, and continues to be, impressed by his incredible <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> talents. She was not afraid to vocalise her disdain for the Ministry's treatment of Dumbledore, and showed confidence that they would not have a chance at capturing him. According to <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, she was a tough and strict woman much like his grandmother <a href="/wiki/Augusta_Longbottom" title="Augusta Longbottom">Augusta Longbottom</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Professor Marchbanks was ancient in age by the year <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, as she examined <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> during his <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.s</a> in <a href="/wiki/1899" class="mw-redirect" title="1899">1899</a>. <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">W.O.M.B.A.T.</a> claimed she was still working placing her approximate age today at least in her 130s, and likely even older.</li>
<li>Along with the other <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a> examiners, Professor Marchbanks was omitted from the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>. However, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> is seen supervising the <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a> theory exam.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTPF_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTPF-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://atrossbooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Harry-Potter-and-the-Order-of-the-Phoenix-2nd-draft.pdf">an early draft</a> of the fifth film, Professor Marchbanks was male.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Marchbanks?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Griselda" is possibly derived from the Germanic elements gris "grey" and hild "battle". It is not attested as a Germanic name. This was the name of a patient wife in medieval tales by Boccaccio and Chaucer.
</p>
