<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>Of the Horcrux, wickedest of magical inventions, we shall not speak nor give direction —</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<i><a href="/wiki/Magick_Moste_Evile" title="Magick Moste Evile">Magick Moste Evile</a></i><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>A <b>Horcrux</b> was an object in which a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark wizard or witch</a> has hidden a fragment of his or her <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a> in order to become <a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">immortal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Horcruxes could only be created after committing murder, the supreme act of evil. The process for the creation of a Horcrux involved <a href="/wiki/Horcrux-making_spell" title="Horcrux-making spell">a spell</a> and a horrific act is performed soon after the murder has been committed. Given that Horcruxes were precious to those who made them, there were usually protective measures made to prevent them from being stolen or destroyed, such as <a href="/wiki/Counter-charm" title="Counter-charm">Counter-Charms</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a>. The Horcrux was considered to be the darkest art and the most terrible of all <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">dark magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Herpo_the_Foul%27s_Horcrux" title="Herpo the Foul&#39;s Horcrux">first known Horcrux</a> was created by <a href="/wiki/Herpo_the_Foul" title="Herpo the Foul">Herpo the Foul</a> in <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">Ancient</a> <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. The only other known creator of a Horcrux was <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, who was likely the only person to have successfully created more than one Horcrux.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottercast_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottercast-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The nature and concepts of Horcruxes were so terrible, they were kept secret from most of the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, and very few people were ever made aware of their nature. <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> banned any study of the subject of Horcruxes; <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> was particularly adamant in enforcing this rule.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Very few books, even those revolving around the Dark Arts, would mention Horcruxes even in the slightest detail: <i><a href="/wiki/Magick_Moste_Evile" title="Magick Moste Evile">Magick Moste Evile</a></i>, a book that contained much information of advanced dark arts, barely skimmed the subject of Horcruxes as a subject so evil that it should not be spoken of, showing that even some Dark Wizards were horrified by them. In fact, only one known book, <i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art" title="Secrets of the Darkest Art">Secrets of the Darkest Art</a></i>, had detailed information on the method and consequences of creating a Horcrux. No document in existence has information regarding a single individual creating multiple Horcruxes, as no wizard before Voldemort even thought of attempting such a feat; he only dared to when he asked the experienced <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> for an expert opinion, under the guise of creating more than one Horcrux in theory.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature_and_creation">Nature and creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Nature and creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nature and creation"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>A Horcrux is the word used for an object in which a person has concealed part of their soul... Well, you split your soul, you see, and hide part of it in an object outside the body. Then, even if one's body is attacked or destroyed, one cannot die, for part of the soul remains earthbound and undamaged.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The nature of Horcruxes<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 162px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fe/Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170118060422" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fe/Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20170118060422" decoding="async" width="160" height="231" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Secrets of the Darkest Art.jpg" data-image-key="Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art" title="Secrets of the Darkest Art">Secrets of the Darkest Art</a></i> gives clear instructions on making a Horcrux</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>The only known book that provided specific instruction on the creation and nature of a Horcrux was <i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art" title="Secrets of the Darkest Art">Secrets of the Darkest Art</a></i>, which was once held in <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">Hogwarts' library</a> (and later in Dumbledore's office). Due to the book's extremely dark and dangerous nature, Albus Dumbledore hid it away in his <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">office</a> when he became the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. It was believed that Tom Riddle had found the information necessary to make a Horcrux in this book before Dumbledore managed to get hold of it. While a Horcrux could be anything at all, including a living being, it was most advisable to create a Horcrux out of an inanimate object to decrease the chances of it being destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Horcruxes by their nature appeared to be extraordinarily durable, as only very destructive magics and processes could truly destroy them.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>To create a Horcrux, a wizard first had to deliberately commit murder. This act, said to be the most supreme act of evil, would result in the murderer metaphysically damaging their own soul. A wizard who wished to create a Horcrux would then use that damage to their advantage by casting <a href="/wiki/Horcrux-making_spell" title="Horcrux-making spell">a spell</a> which would sever the damaged portion of the soul and encase it in an object.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> If the maker was later killed, they would continue to exist in a non-corporeal form,<sup id="cite_ref-GOF33_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF33-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> although there were methods of regaining a physical body.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF32_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF32-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, according to <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, few would want to live in such a form, and death would be preferable to most, and he found the concept so dreadful that he wished not to know the process of creating such a vile object.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 247px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fd/First_time.gif/revision/latest?cb=20180904181155" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="245" height="159" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="First time.gif" data-image-key="First_time.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fd/First_time.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20180904181155" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fd/First_time.gif/revision/latest?cb=20180904181155" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fd/First_time.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20180904181155" decoding="async" width="245" height="159" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="First time.gif" data-image-key="First_time.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/f/fd/First_time.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20180904181155" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:First_time.gif" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> creates his first Horcrux at the age of sixteen after murdering Myrtle Warren</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>As a fragment of soul, a Horcrux seemed to retain the identity of its creator at the time of its creation. Voldemort, for instance, created a Horcrux (his diary) during what was presumably his fifth year at Hogwarts. As such, the fragment of soul contained within the Horcrux took on the appearance and mannerisms of Voldemort as he had been when he was sixteen years old.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Horcruxes were said to be essentially the opposite of a person. Where a person's container, their body, could be destroyed without any damage to their soul, the fragment of soul contained inside a Horcrux was dependent on the container for its existence. If the container was destroyed, so too would be the fragment of soul within it.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Horcruxes were originally conceived of as being a singular act. As such, it was unknown for many centuries what the attempted creation of multiple Horcruxes would result in, as nobody thought it possible to accomplish such an act. However, Voldemort planned to create six Horcruxes in the hope that it would make him stronger than just creating one, due to his belief in the power of the number seven.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was stated at one point that Voldemort had already "pushed his <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a> to the limit"<sup id="cite_ref-DH14_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH14-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> in creating his Horcruxes. This implied a finite number of Horcruxes any one person may create before the process became too dangerous to attempt again. It also implied that the creation of a Horcrux used a set amount of soul and that this amount was the same every time the process was undertaken. Creating multiple Horcruxes rendered the soul unstable and liable to break apart if the creator of the Horcruxes was killed.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> For instance, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> explicitly stated that Voldemort's soul had become so unstable that it simply "broke apart" when Voldemort tried to murder <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> for the first time on <a href="/wiki/31_October" title="31 October">31 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a> in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Destruction">Destruction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Destruction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Destruction"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince"><b>Harry Potter</b>: "<i>So if all of his Horcruxes are destroyed, Voldemort could be killed?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Albus Dumbledore</b>: "<i>Yes, I think so. Without his Horcruxes, Voldemort will be a mortal man with a maimed and diminished soul.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; A discussion on how to go about killing Voldemort<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>

<p>Destroying a Horcrux required that the object containing the soul fragment be damaged to a point beyond any and all physical or magical repair. When a Horcrux was damaged to that point, it may appear to "bleed" (ink in the case of Tom Riddle's diary<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and a "dark blood-like substance" in the case of Ravenclaw's Diadem)<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and a scream may be heard as the soul fragment perished. However, as a safety measure to protect one's immortality and precious soul fragment, the creator would usually place powerful <a href="/wiki/Protective_enchantment" title="Protective enchantment">enchantments</a> onto the artefact to prevent damage.
</p><p>It was unknown if the creator of the Horcrux would be able to sense that their soul fragment was destroyed, although Dumbledore stated that in the particular case of Voldemort, he would not feel their loss because his soul had been split too many times and had been in that state for too long.
</p>

<p>All known methods of Horcrux destruction required the "receptacle" to be damaged beyond repair<sup id="cite_ref-Pottercast_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottercast-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (which, in the case of living receptacles, meant that they must be killed).<sup id="cite_ref-Pottercast_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottercast-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JKRtwitter6fev2015_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRtwitter6fev2015-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For example, methods were <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">Fiendfyre</a> (as evidenced by the destruction of <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem" class="mw-redirect" title="Rowena Ravenclaw&#39;s Diadem">Rowena Ravenclaw's Diadem</a>), which required extreme skill to control,<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> on living Horcruxes.<sup id="cite_ref-DH32_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH32-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> However, Harry Potter was never an intentional Horcrux and so it may not work on a proper animate Horcrux (like <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>) and probably had unforeseen side effects, along with <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_venom" title="Basilisk venom">Basilisk venom</a><sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the touch of <a href="/wiki/Sacrificial_protection" title="Sacrificial protection">sacrificial love</a> (on pseudo-Horcruxes). This was seen on Professor Quirrell when he was destroyed by Harry Potter's touch.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry Potter and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> both used the fangs of <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Salazar Slytherin's Basilisk</a><sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> to destroy <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_Diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s Diary">Tom Riddle's Diary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Helga Hufflepuff's Cup</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> respectively. This was only achievable as <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_venom" title="Basilisk venom">basilisk venom</a> was an extremely destructive substance capable of destroying Horcruxes. Although beyond repair when exposed to this venom, living Horcruxes could potentially be saved through the speedy administration of <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_tears" title="Phoenix tears">phoenix tears</a>, an extremely rare substance. <a href="/wiki/Fawkes" title="Fawkes">Fawkes</a> demonstrated this ability for Harry when he was bitten by the basilisk in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Because he was a living Horcrux himself, Harry was not destroyed then because the tears saved his life.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottercast_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottercast-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH19_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH19-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a><sup id="cite_ref-DH36_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> all used <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor%27s_Sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Godric Gryffindor&#39;s Sword">Godric Gryffindor's Sword</a> to destroy <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_Ring" title="Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s Ring">Marvolo Gaunt's Ring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>, respectively. This was only achievable as <a href="/wiki/Goblin-wrought_silver" title="Goblin-wrought silver">goblin-wrought silver</a> is a destructive enough metal capable of absorbing qualities that strengthen it. The sword demonstrated this ability for Harry Potter when he used it to slay the basilisk in the Chamber of Secrets. Because it was made of goblin-wrought silver itself, the sword was imbued with basilisk venom and became capable of destroying Horcruxes.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers">Powers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Powers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Powers"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows"><b>Ron Weasley</b>: "<i>The bit of soul in that diary was possessing <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a>, wasn’t it? How does that work, then?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Hermione Granger</b>: "<i>While the magical container is still intact, the bit of soul inside it can flit in and out of someone if they get too close to the object. I don’t mean holding it for too long... I mean close emotionally. Ginny poured her heart out into that diary, she made herself incredibly vulnerable. You’re in trouble if you get too fond of or dependent on the Horcrux.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; A Horcrux's ability to possess and eventually control a person<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div><p>The main purpose and power of a Horcrux is to anchor the creator to the mortal realm for the sake of achieving immortality. As long as at least one Horcrux exists, the creator's soul will be bound to the world of the living. Hence, if the body is destroyed, the soul will remain as a lingering spirit instead of passing through to the other side. Though being in such a state of existence is what very few would prefer over death, the soul can possess others to regain physical form, which shortens the host's lifespan drastically.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is also possible (through a second party) to use certain potions to create a <a href="/wiki/Rudimentary_body_potion" title="Rudimentary body potion">rudimentary body</a><sup id="cite_ref-GOF1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> or even the <a href="/wiki/Regeneration_potion" title="Regeneration potion">true body</a> of the Horcrux creator's soul to possess.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF32_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF32-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Because of this purpose, a Horcrux is usually kept hidden and dormant, and its other powers are rarely explored or utilised.
</p><p>The fragment of a person's soul within a Horcrux was capable of thinking for itself and had certain magical abilities, including the ability to influence those in their vicinity and affect them mentally. When Harry, Ron, and Hermione were carrying Salazar Slytherin's locket around their necks in 1997, it brought out the worst in the trio by making them moodier, more prone to fighting, and slowly darker aspects of them began to appear, especially Ron as he carried the locket much longer than his friends.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF15_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF15-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, when away from a Horcrux its influence and effects would begin to wane and then disappear and those influenced would return to their normal selves, as soon after leaving Ron came to his senses and regretted his actions. They were also unable to summon their <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronuses</a> while wearing the locket since the soul fragment inside was darkening their thoughts. A person with an affinity for the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>, on the other hand, would be strengthened by the influence of a Horcrux, as <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> was when wearing Salazar Slytherin's locket.<sup id="cite_ref-online_bloomsbury_chat_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online_bloomsbury_chat-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 247px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/00/Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140407011351" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="245" height="100" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Ginevra Weasley Writing on the Wall.gif" data-image-key="Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/00/Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20140407011351" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/00/Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140407011351" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/00/Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20140407011351" decoding="async" width="245" height="100" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Ginevra Weasley Writing on the Wall.gif" data-image-key="Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/00/Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20140407011351" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Ginevra_Weasley_Writing_on_the_Wall.gif" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">The diary Horcrux takes possession of <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>If a person is more emotionally vulnerable, it is possible for the soul inside the Horcrux to take control of him or her, as Ginny Weasley using her. In fact, Voldemort took advantage of this possessive power over Ginny to reopen the Chamber of Secrets, using the diary as a weapon rather than a safeguard.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Voldemort himself was able to possess Harry Potter and take control of his mind when Harry was heartbroken over <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s death and angry enough to perform the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> on <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> and almost perform the Killing Curse on her. This quasi-sentient entity that is capable of sapping life-force to create a physical form differs from a "mere <a href="/wiki/Memory" title="Memory">memory</a>", which the diary's manifestation claimed itself to be, as no mere charmed object can achieve such a feat, though <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> thought that was what the diary simply was.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In this way, a Horcrux can gradually feed on another person's life or negative emotions to strengthen itself and increase the ability of the soul fragment within to act independently in the physical world. The best example of this is in the case of <a href="/wiki/T._M._Riddle%27s_Diary" title="T. M. Riddle&#39;s Diary">Tom Riddle's diary</a>. For decades, the diary lay dormant in <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a>'s possession, doing nothing other than safeguarding the soul fragment of Tom Riddle. When Ginny Weasley began to transcribe her fears and insecurities into the pages of the diary, the fragment of Tom Riddle's soul contained within was not only able to write back to Ginny but eventually drained enough life out of her to actually manifest itself in a semi-corporeal form and work magic with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s wand.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Likewise, <a href="/wiki/Slytherin%27s_Locket" title="Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's locket</a> slowly gained power when it was in the possession of Harry, Ron, and Hermione in the months prior to its destruction. It can be theorised that the locket gained somewhat less power from them (not enough for Riddle to fully manifest but still enough to speak and create illusions) because Harry, Ron, and Hermione were actively resisting the locket's influence instead of embracing it as Ginny had done with the diary.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On the other hand, Horcruxes which have been isolated for long periods of time (such as <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" title="Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Helga Hufflepuff's cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem" title="Ravenclaw&#39;s Diadem">Rowena Ravenclaw's diadem</a>) were very passive by comparison and took no real measures to protect themselves. Even Slytherin's locket was fairly inert when it was initially discovered in a cabinet in the drawing-room at 12 Grimmauld Place. It displayed no powers and gave no indication that it possessed superior magical properties.<sup id="cite_ref-DH10_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH10-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c9/Slytherin%27s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110407205455" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="250" height="104" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Slytherin&#39;s Locket taunting Ron.jpg" data-image-key="Slytherin&#39;s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c9/Slytherin%27s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110407205455" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c9/Slytherin%27s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110407205455" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c9/Slytherin%27s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110407205455" decoding="async" width="250" height="104" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Slytherin&#39;s Locket taunting Ron.jpg" data-image-key="Slytherin&#39;s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c9/Slytherin%27s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110407205455" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Slytherin%27s_Locket_taunting_Ron.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Sensing imminent destruction, the locket Horcrux taunted Ron as its last defence</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Horcruxes also possessed some last line of defence against destruction. The fragment of soul within the Horcrux seems to be able to sense impending threats and can act to defend itself. For instance, Slytherin's locket viciously taunted Ron Weasley with visions of his deepest fears in the hopes of preventing him from stabbing it with <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor%27s_Sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Godric Gryffindor&#39;s Sword">Godric Gryffindor's sword</a>, and even attempted to strangle Harry Potter when he was close to obtaining the Sword with the intent of piercing the locket.<sup id="cite_ref-DH19_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH19-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> However, this form of defence may not be viable if the soul fragment in question is not given enough strength or chance to retaliate, as while Harry opened the locket with <a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parseltongue</a> before allowing Ron to attack it, both Hufflepuff's Cup and Ravenclaw's Diadem were swiftly destroyed the moment the trio were given an opportunity.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aside from its self-defence mechanism from the soul fragment, a Horcrux is usually enchanted by the creator to have other forms of defences to prevent destruction. <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_ring" class="mw-redirect" title="Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s ring">Marvolo Gaunt's ring</a> contained a <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_Ring%27s_curse" class="mw-redirect" title="Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s Ring&#39;s curse">deadly curse</a> that would kill anyone who touched it.<sup id="cite_ref-DH33_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH33-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> At least Salazar Slytherin's Locket was unbreakable by even house-elf magic.<sup id="cite_ref-DH10_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH10-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s diary">Tom Riddle's diary</a> was completely <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">waterproof</a> and impervious to spilled ink.<sup id="cite_ref-COS13_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS13-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Side_effects">Side effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Side effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Side effects"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Tamper with the deepest mysteries — the source of life, the essence of self — only if prepared for consequences of the most extreme and dangerous kind.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The first of the <a href="/wiki/Fundamental_Laws_of_Magic" title="Fundamental Laws of Magic">Fundamental Laws of Magic</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>To create a Horcrux is to divide one's soul — the "<i>essence of self</i>" — and it is therefore in the creation of a Horcrux that one falls prey to <a href="/wiki/Adalbert_Waffling" title="Adalbert Waffling">Adalbert Waffling</a>'s first <a href="/wiki/Fundamental_Laws_of_Magic" title="Fundamental Laws of Magic">Fundamental Laws of Magic</a>, which essentially states that tampering with one's soul inevitably results in grave side effects. Creating Horcruxes is considered perhaps the most dreadful act possible.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dehumanisation">Dehumanisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Dehumanisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dehumanisation"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>One of these such side-effects is the "dehumanising" effect the mutilation of one's <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a> is said to have. The more Horcruxes one creates, the less human they become, both emotionally and physically; for example, in the <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> <a href="/wiki/Hokey" title="Hokey">Hokey</a>'s memory Tom Riddle was initially shown to be hollow-cheeked but otherwise normal,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> though ten years later his features look as if they have been burned and blurred, and his skin was extremely white.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_9-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 247px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Voldemort2.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140907192548" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="245" height="102" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Voldemort2.gif" data-image-key="Voldemort2.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Voldemort2.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20140907192548" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Voldemort2.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140907192548" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Voldemort2.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20140907192548" decoding="async" width="245" height="102" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Voldemort2.gif" data-image-key="Voldemort2.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Voldemort2.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20140907192548" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Voldemort2.gif" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> after his transformation</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>One should note that it is unclear whether the red eyes and slit-like nostrils that Voldemort had after he was reborn were caused by having more Horcruxes than he did than when he applied for the <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> post a second time,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> whether they are characteristics of a person who has been resurrected with the help of serpents (which have continued to play key roles in his revival), or whether due to any other alterations he had made to himself. Dumbledore speculated that Voldemort underwent other dangerous transformations, as well as creating Horcruxes which resulted in Voldemort's hideous appearance.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Instability">Instability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Instability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Instability"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A third side effect of Horcrux creation is that the master <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a> itself becomes unstable (even with creating just one Horcrux).<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For example, the creation of Voldemort's sixth "Horcrux" (of seven) — Harry Potter — is known to be the direct result of this.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottercast_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottercast-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> When Voldemort was hit by the back-fired <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> at Potters' home in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a>, it caused Voldemort's soul to split, with one fragment immediately seeking out the only other living thing in the room and latching onto it — <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. The rest of Voldemort's mutilated soul fled.<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However, this parasitic fragment of Voldemort's soul that attached to Harry did not make him a true Horcrux, since it was not created intentionally and the necessary parts of the Horcrux creation process were not carried out.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Limbo">Limbo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Limbo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Limbo"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Something that is beyond either of our help...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The fate of those who create a Horcrux<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>The final known side-effect of Horcrux creation is a possible inability to move on from <a href="/wiki/Limbo" title="Limbo">limbo</a> after death. This was seen when Voldemort's <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> destroyed the part of his soul that resided in Harry Potter. This broken and mangled piece of <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> was forced to exist in the stunted form of a flayed and mutilated baby that Harry saw in King's Cross during his visit to limbo, unable to return to the land of the living, unable to become a ghost, and unable to go to the land of the dead because his soul was maimed and corrupted.<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The same fate was implied to have been suffered by Voldemort's "main" soul piece, the one that inhabited his body; it is unknown if this was a standard fate meted out for all Horcrux creators, or if it was unique to Voldemort due to the number of his Horcruxes. Regardless, reconciliation apparently cannot occur after death, as the soul's state at death remains forever, so the greatest of all consequences incurred by Horcrux creation may be the possibility of eternal limbo of the soul. Voldemort's soul fragments also appeared to possess only the awareness and intelligence of the infant they appeared to be.<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Lord_Voldemort's_Horcruxes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Lord_Voldemort.27s_Horcruxes">Lord Voldemort's Horcruxes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Lord Voldemort&#039;s Horcruxes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Lord Voldemort&#039;s Horcruxes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>I, who have gone further than anybody along the path that leads to immortality....</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Lord Voldemort to his gathered <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> after his <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">rebirth</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p><a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, obsessed with immortality and unable or unwilling to understand the importance of the soul's well-being, went further than any wizard known to history to create seven — although he wanted six Horcruxes to have a seven part soul. He accidentally created a pseudo-Horcrux when he failed to murder <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in 1981,<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and later made his pet snake <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> into his seventh Horcrux. As a student named Tom Riddle at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> in the 1940s, he learned of Horcruxes through books in the <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Section" title="Restricted Section">Restricted Section</a>, including <i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_of_the_Darkest_Art" title="Secrets of the Darkest Art">Secrets of the Darkest Art</a></i>, and sought out <a href="/wiki/Potions_Master" title="Potions Master">Potions Master</a> <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> for further information about creating more than one, of which no book would have any record. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> removed those books from the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">Hogwarts Library</a> soon afterwards, although later he suspected that Slughorn had given information to Riddle about Horcruxes, as Slughorn refused to reveal the true account of what really happened. Voldemort did understand that his soul had a limit to how many times he could split it, as he ceased creating any more Horcruxes once he intentionally reached his desired six and refused to create any replacements for the ones that were destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dumbledore later assigned Harry to retrieve the <a href="/wiki/Pensieve" title="Pensieve">stored memory</a> of it during the 1996-1997 school year, in which Harry was able to get it through the use of <a href="/wiki/Felix_Felicis" title="Felix Felicis">Felix Felicis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Although Voldemort had been warned that the usage of Horcruxes would render one's soul to live a miserable existence should their living flesh be destroyed, and Voldemort even experienced this first hand for fourteen years, he would regardless prefer to live in such a state when the alternative was death, which he feared the most, since he was willing to continue his creation of his <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">sixth true Horcrux</a> even in his crippled form.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF33_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF33-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Voldemort's streak of independence made it intolerable for him to utilise any other methods to obtain his immortality (such as the <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a>); he chose to rely on Horcruxes because they were magical extensions of himself.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Having split his soul multiple times, it became extremely unstable that upon his Killing Curse's first rebound on him, Voldemort's already maimed soul split apart and attached to the only living being in the room at the time: Harry Potter. It was also the creation of so many Horcruxes that led Voldemort's humanity to deteriorate severely. As Voldemort's soul fragments have been separated from him for so long, he could no longer feel them should they be destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was part of Dumbledore and Harry's quest to determine, locate, and destroy, in secret, what they believed to be as many as four of Voldemort's remaining Horcruxes (two had already been destroyed). After Dumbledore's death, Harry took up the quest with his friends <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>. Hermione was able to <a href="/wiki/Summoning_Charm" title="Summoning Charm">summon</a> the books on the subject to her from the Headmaster's office at the end of the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">1996–1997 school year</a> to aid them in their research on Horcruxes.<sup id="cite_ref-DH6_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH6-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although Voldemort had six true Horcruxes (not including Harry) in total, no more than five existed at one time because <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s diary">Tom Riddle's diary</a> was destroyed before Nagini was turned into a Horcrux. Concerning Nagini, it is not known if she had to have been killed by <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor%27s_Sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Godric Gryffindor&#39;s Sword">Godric Gryffindor's Sword</a> or if any other means, such as a regular sword killing her, would have destroyed the Horcrux. However, as Albus Dumbledore stated, using a living being as a Horcrux was a risky business, due to it being able to move around and think for itself.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown if any method that would normally kill Nagini would also destroy the Horcrux. It is also unknown what would happen if Nagini had died a natural death, or if that were possible, since Horcruxes have mechanisms in place to prevent destruction.
</p><p>All of Voldemort's Horcruxes were made from objects that had extreme value, in his desire to secure his position as the greatest sorcerer in history, and that only noteworthy items could live up to his standards and have the honour of housing a fragment of his precious <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a>. As such, he had originally made it his desire to collect four items of the four founders of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>; he found only three, and gave up after failing to find Gryffindor's sword, but made Horcruxes out of other items that had sentimental value to himself, if not as a priceless artefact of the Wizarding world. Believing that the number seven is the most powerful number when it comes to magic, Voldemort intended to split his soul into seven pieces, with six Horcruxes housing one fragment each and his body containing the seventh. He hid these Horcruxes in special locations and kept their existence and purposes from absolutely everyone. However, his arrogance had also inadvertently let him leave behind subtle hints of their whereabouts, leading to their destruction. Because Voldemort was the first (and by far, the only) known wizard to have created more than one Horcrux, both Dumbledore and Voldemort himself believed that he was the closest to true immortality than any other wizard or witch before him.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A secondary reason why Voldemort chose to create multiple Horcruxes is to utilise the effects of having a soul fragment to weaponize some of these artefacts, as opposed to keeping them as hidden safeguards. Voldemort felt more comfortable placing these few Horcruxes in the risk of destruction knowing he had more hidden safely to maintain his immortality. It was for this reason he chose to use <a href="/wiki/T._M._Riddle%27s_Diary" title="T. M. Riddle&#39;s Diary">his diary</a> to reopen the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>, and sending <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> on missions from time to time.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although Voldemort created multiple Horcruxes so that if one was destroyed, it would not be detrimental to him, he would nevertheless be furious if any one of them were destroyed. He was wrathful at <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> for indirectly causing the destruction of the diary Horcrux,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> despite intending for it to be a weapon instead of a safeguard, due to Lucius acting without instruction. When Voldemort found out that Hufflepuff's cup was taken, he massacred all the Gringotts employees within the bank that failed to protect the cup. Discovering that Slytherin's locket and the Gaunt family ring were taken from their hiding places infuriated him even more, though it became more understandable as he was aware that he was losing more Horcruxes as time went on.<sup id="cite_ref-DH27_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH27-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> His arrogance prevented him from adding more defences for the Lost Diadem of Ravenclaw, which led to its destruction. He went out of his way to protect his last Horcrux, Nagini, and was horrified and angry beyond words when she was killed by <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> with <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor%27s_sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Godric Gryffindor&#39;s sword">Gryffindor's sword</a>, thus stripping him of his immortality.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an <a href="/wiki/Alternate_reality" title="Alternate reality">alternate reality</a> where <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> became a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> and killing Neville during <a href="/wiki/Voldemort%27s_Last_Stand" title="Voldemort&#39;s Last Stand">Voldemort's Last Stand</a>, Nagini was not killed as a result, thereby allowing Voldemort to maintain his immortality and kill Harry Potter while also conquering the Britain wizarding community.<sup id="cite_ref-CC_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pseudo-Horcruxes">Pseudo-Horcruxes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Pseudo-Horcruxes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Pseudo-Horcruxes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>While he does not fit the definition of a Horcrux, as he was not created intentionally using the <a href="/wiki/Horcrux-making_spell" title="Horcrux-making spell">Horcrux-making spell</a> for the purpose of obtaining immortality, Harry Potter essentially became a Horcrux. After Voldemort's curse rebounded on him, a piece of his mangled soul split off and latched onto Harry. Dumbledore explained that for simplicity's sake, Harry was a Horcrux.<sup id="cite_ref-Harry_not_Horcrux_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harry_not_Horcrux-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The word <i>Horcrux</i> may be comprised of <i>"hor"</i> or <i>"hore"</i> (old English/middle-English) meaning <i>"dirt, evil, impurity"</i> and <i>"crux"</i> or <i>"crúce"</i> (old English) meaning <i>"container, pitcher(ful), jar"</i> which would therefore mean "container of evil".
</p><p>Alternatively, <i>Horcrux</i> can be seen as a combination of a shortening of <i>"horrible"</i> and <i>"crux" </i>(meaning "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_cross" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Christian cross"><i>the Cross</i></a>" in Latin)<i>. </i>In this sense, a Horcrux would be something that a follower of the Cross would regard as horrible.
</p><p>On the other hand, "<i>hor"</i> could be derived from the French "<i>hors"</i>, which means "<i>outside"</i>. Thus, "Horcrux" would mean something that is "outside what is permitted under the Cross".
</p><p>The Greek "<i>hor</i>" means "<i>boundary</i>", which could refer to the boundary between life and death. Latin "<i>crux</i>" also lends way to words such as "<i>crucify</i>" and "<i>excruciating</i>", meaning "pain/torture", which may describe either the experience of ripping one's soul apart, or the terrible deeds that must be performed in order to create a Horcrux.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Horcrux?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> stated in  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> that Professor <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> served as a temporary Horcrux when Voldemort's soul possessed his body during Harry Potter's <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">first year</a> at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-quirrell_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quirrell-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> A notable difference, however, is that the piece of soul within Quirrell was able to exist without its container, as it abandoned Quirrell and left him to die in the <a href="/wiki/Underground_Chambers" title="Underground Chambers">Underground Chambers</a>. This is due to it being the "master" soul that serves as the awareness and consciousness of Voldemort's psyche.
<ul><li>Based on this, the animals that Voldemort inhabited during his years of exile, such as rats and snakes, can also be considered temporary Horcruxes.</li></ul></li>
<li>J. K. Rowling knows exactly what the process for the creation of a Horcrux is, but is not telling — yet. All she will say is that a spell is involved, and a horrific act is performed.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottercast_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottercast-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The information was initially planned to be revealed in the <a href="/wiki/Encyclopaedia_of_Potterworld" title="Encyclopaedia of Potterworld">Harry Potter Encyclopaedia</a>. However, since the encyclopaedia may have been cancelled, the information may eventually be revealed on <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i>.
<ul><li>The likelihood of the incantation to this spell being revealed is very low, as Rowling said that she could not "possibly tell [us]. Some things are better left unsaid." in an interview, on July 30th, 2007.</li></ul></li>
<li>J. K. Rowling described the invention of the Horcrux as comparable to the splitting of the atom: "<i>Something that people imagined might be able to be done, but couldn't quite bring it off, and then... and then people started doing it with sometimes catastrophic effects.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-Pottercast_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottercast-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In an interview, J. K. Rowling was asked why the Horcrux within Harry wasn't destroyed after he was poisoned by the basilisk in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>. She replied that <a href="/wiki/Fawkes" title="Fawkes">Fawkes</a>'s tears "mended" him before he could be destroyed beyond repair.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The only Horcrux that Harry personally destroyed was Tom Riddle's Diary, even though he was the only one clearly stated to be sent for the mission. Also, he did not even know that it was a Horcrux at the time. The <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_Ring" title="Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s Ring">Ring</a> was destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Locket</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Cup</a> by <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem" class="mw-redirect" title="Rowena Ravenclaw&#39;s Diadem">Diadem</a> with <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">Fiendfyre</a> that was conjured by <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> was killed by <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, and the fragment within Harry was inadvertently destroyed by Voldemort himself. In the film adaptation, however, the Diadem is stabbed with a basilisk's fang by Harry and then kicked by Ron into the oncoming Fiendfyre.</li>
<li>Of the seven Horcruxes Voldemort created (intentionally and unintentionally), four of them were destroyed during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, along with Voldemort himself. In fact, aside from <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a>, all Horcruxes were destroyed within Hogwarts grounds, either during or before the aforementioned battle.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, the only Horcruxes mentioned are the diary, the ring, and the locket, leaving Harry with no direction in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (film)">film adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a> hides Harry's potion book while Harry closes his eyes (as well as kissing him for the very first time), and there is no indication that Harry sees the diadem.</li>
<li>There has been controversy of the fact that, in the films, Harry, Ron and Hermione do not have any leads to find the remaining Horcruxes, apart from the locket. However, in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, it is shown that a Horcrux will leave a <i>trace</i> of Dark magic - this gives the person who touches the Horcrux visions of associated events and other related Horcruxes. A scene in the sixth film shows Harry touching <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_Ring" title="Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s Ring">Marvolo Gaunt's Ring</a> and experiencing a flow of high speed visions including <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> screaming in agony (possibly due to the method of ripping his soul) and <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>, one of Lord Voldemort's Horcruxes. This is also stated in Steve Klove's script for the film. This would ultimately lead Harry, Ron, and Hermione to know most (if not all) of the Horcruxes in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (film)">film versions</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>.</li>
<li>A person who is a Horcrux also seems to possess some of the creator's abilities such as <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> being able to use Parseltongue which is one of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s inherited abilities. It also creates a mental link between the two the strength of which seems to depend upon the strength of the creator. For example, when Voldemort was weak and only in a spiritual form, Harry could only sense his presence when he was close by and feel his anger, but after he returned to somewhat of a body, this expanded a bit into the occasional dream vision of things happening with Voldemort. After Voldemort returned to full power, this link expanded so that Harry got full visions in his dreams of what Voldemort was dwelling upon, but the link can also be two-way and Voldemort was able to use it and Legilimency to implant a false vision in Harry's mind. The link can be shut off with Occlumency on the part of one of the two with the link, but if the Occlumency slips, the link can reopen. The only way to sever the link completely and remove the abilities the Horcrux gives is to destroy the Horcrux itself.</li>
<li>It is debatable if the Killing Curse can destroy inanimate Horcruxes, although given the extreme measures Harry, Ron, and Hermione had to go through to obtain Basilisk venom to destroy the Horcruxes they found during their <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter%27s_Horcrux_Hunt" title="Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter&#39;s Horcrux Hunt">search</a>, it seems unlikely. It is also possible that none of them were capable of performing the curse, as successfully executing an unforgivable curse requires dark intentions, making it nearly impossible for a non-dark wizard to do.</li>
<li>According to Dumbledore, Voldemort was preserving the creation of his Horcruxes for significant deaths. However, this stands to be another one of Dumbledore's deductions being wrong, as Voldemort has used a <a href="/wiki/Muggle_tramp" title="Muggle tramp">Muggle tramp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albanian_peasant" title="Albanian peasant">Albanian peasant</a> to create two of his Horcruxes, with no known significance for the two. However, Dumbledore only knew for certain of two of the murders committed to create Voldemort's Horcruxes: the murder of <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren" title="Myrtle Warren">Moaning Myrtle</a> (his very first killing) to create the diary, and murder of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle_Senior" title="Tom Riddle Senior">Tom Riddle Snr</a>, his father, for the Gaunt Ring Horcrux. Both of these murders were very significant, but it turns out later that Dumbledore was incorrect that all Horcrux-related deaths were important ones.</li>
<li>J.K. Rowling's exact definition of a Horcrux is "a receptacle prepared by dark magic in which a Dark wizard has intentionally hidden a fragment of his soul for the purpose of attaining immortality."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The concept of a "soul container" is not original to the world of <i>Harry Potter</i>. See <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/SoulJar">this page</a> for more information.
<ul><li>Most notably, the concept of storing ones soul in an external vessel is similar to the idea of a Lich, popularised by the role playing game Dungeons &amp; Dragons. A lich is a spell-caster who has stored their soul in a vessel called a phylactery to prevent death.</li></ul></li>
<li>It is unknown whether a Dark Wizard protected by a Horcrux could still be killed by being thrown through the <a href="/wiki/Veil" title="Veil">Veil</a>. It's also unknown whether a Horcrux could be <a href="/wiki/Dementor%27s_Kiss" title="Dementor&#39;s Kiss">kissed</a> by a <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementor</a>, thereby presumably destroying it.</li>
<li>Interestingly, no one destroyed more than two Horcruxes (counting Quirrell) and half of them were destroyed by someone other than Harry, Ron, and Hermione. (This does not apply to the films, however, as Harry (and Ron, to an extent) destroy the diadem, which is destroyed by Crabbe in the book.) The majority of the people who destroyed Voldemort's Horcruxes didn't know what they were destroying at the time. Neville didn't know what Horcruxes were when he killed Nagini, Harry didn't know about Horcruxes when he destroyed the diary, Voldemort didn't know he was destroying a Horcrux when he attempted to kill Harry Potter, and Vincent Crabbe didn't know what he was destroying when he destroyed the diadem. Hermione, Ron, and Dumbledore all knew that they were destroying Horcruxes, which was exactly what they were trying to do.</li>
<li>The books previously established that Voldemort can't feel when a Horcrux is destroyed. However, this was completely reversed in the movies. That is the reason he starts on the journey of seeing if all the Horcruxes are safe in the books, but in the movies it is unclear if he even checks his hiding spots. When Hermione destroys the Hufflepuff Cup, Voldemort, Harry and Nagini all seem to be momentarily incapacitated by pain. This also happens when the Ravenclaw Diadem and Nagini are destroyed, clearly showing that all Horcruxes and the Master Soul feel the destruction of the others.
<ul><li>Interestingly enough, this only starts to happen when the cup is destroyed, as Harry does not react to the locket's destruction and Voldemort remains oblivious when both it and the ring are destroyed. The reason for this is unclear, but it may be because the cup marked the destruction of half of Voldemort's soul.</li></ul></li>
<li>In the fairy tale <i><a href="/wiki/The_Warlock%27s_Hairy_Heart" title="The Warlock&#39;s Hairy Heart">The Warlock's Hairy Heart</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Warlock_(The_Warlock%27s_Hairy_Heart)" title="Warlock (The Warlock&#39;s Hairy Heart)">main character</a> of the story stores his <a href="/wiki/Hairy_Heart" title="Hairy Heart">own heart</a> outside of his body via dark magic, similar to Horcruxes in both function and consequences. It was a feat considered impossible outside of the storybook, and was probably a metaphor for Horcrux-making, altered so that misguided readers may not try to imitate the exact process, but still similar enough for virtuous reader to understand the story's Æsop. This in turn resembles the Slavic tales of <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koschei">Koschei the Deathless</a>.</li>
<li>According to Dumbledore, even when Voldemort's Horcruxes were destroyed his brain and his magical power would remain intact.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_1-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> both never learned that they were each entrusted with a Horcrux despite being told to keep them under the strictest security. Furthermore, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> was unable to figure out Voldemort had created Horcruxes despite being told that a fragment of Voldemort's soul resides within Harry. In fact, despite Voldemort surviving the first rebounding Killing Curse and telling his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> that he alone went further than anyone on the path of immortality, none of them were able to understand that he utilised Horcruxes to achieve this. This all suggests that even among the most devoted Dark Arts practitioners such as them, they could not fathom that their master would dare mutilate his own soul for the sake of immortality. The only Death Eater who successfully learned Voldemort had created a Horcrux was <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus Black</a>.</li>
<li>In the season 12 episode <i><a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://supernatural.fandom.com/wiki/The_One_You%27ve_Been_Waiting_For">The One You've Been Waiting</a></i> of the American TV series <i>Supernatural</i> <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://supernatural.fandom.com/wiki/Nazi_Pocket_Watch">a mystical artefact</a> containing the soul of <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://supernatural.fandom.com/wiki/Adolf_Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> was described as a Horcrux by <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://supernatural.fandom.com/wiki/Christoph_Nauhaus">Christoph Nauhaus</a> while explaining how it worked which protagonist Sam Winchester recognised as a <i>Harry Potter</i> reference. Like a Horcrux in <i>Harry Potter</i>, it saved the soul of the creator to ensure their survival and eventual return.</li></ul>
