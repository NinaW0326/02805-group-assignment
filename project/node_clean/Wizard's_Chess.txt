<p><b>Wizard's Chess</b> was the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> variant of the classic board game Chess, in which the pieces were <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">enchanted</a> to move of their own accord when commanded by the player. When a piece was taken, it was removed by the attacking piece, often in a barbaric manner where the losing piece was smashed violently by the winning piece.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The chessboard was a type of checkerboard that consisted of 64 squares (eight rows and eight columns) arranged in two alternating colours (light and dark). The colours were called "black" and "white" (or "light" and "dark"). The Chess pieces, or chessmen, consisted of one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The chessboard and chessmen were exactly like <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> chess pieces, except they were <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magically</a> animated,<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> likely using <i><a href="/wiki/Piertotum_Locomotor" title="Piertotum Locomotor">Piertotum Locomotor</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Instructions">Instructions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_Chess?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Instructions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_Chess?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Instructions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Players moved their pieces by speaking the name of the piece and the square it was to move to by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algebraic_chess_notation" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Algebraic chess notation">algebraic notation</a>. For example, "Knight to E5". Aside from the self-moving pieces, the rules of Wizard's Chess were exactly the same as <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> chess. The moving pieces seemed to be reasonably sentient, as seen when <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> began learning how to play the game, he used <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a>'s pieces, which offered him conflicting advice because they knew that he was not a good or experienced player.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> owned a Wizard's Chess set which he inherited from his grandfather. Unlike many of his hand-me-downs possessions, the second-hand chess set was advantageous as the pieces trusted him. When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> played with <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a>'s chess men, they did not trust his judgement and offered him advice.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Feast" title="Christmas Feast">Christmas Feast</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%931992_school_year" title="1991–1992 school year">1991–1992 school year</a>, Harry obtained his very own Wizard's Chess set in a number of <a href="/wiki/Wizard_Cracker" title="Wizard Cracker">Wizard Crackers</a>, among other things. He later broke it in by losing, once again, to Ron (although Harry saw this more as the fault of Ron's brother, <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a>, who stood over his shoulder and gave him advice).<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In addition, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> lost at the game much to her annoyance, for which Harry and Ron believed was a good experience for her.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1995-1996_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1995-1996 school year">1995-1996 school year</a>, there were three Wizard's Chess challengers in Hogwarts: the Gryffindor champion (easiest level) at Gryffindor common room, the Slytherin champion at the Great Hall and the Ravenclaw champion (hardest) at the Library.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTPG_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTPG-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Giant Wizard's Chess kings appeared as <a href="/wiki/Confoundable" title="Confoundable">Confoundables</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a> which affected the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_World" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizarding World">Wizarding World</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, guarding the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>. Volunteer members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> had to destroy the kings with the use of the <a href="/wiki/Exploding_Charm" title="Exploding Charm">Exploding Charm</a> in order to return the Stone to its rightful place with <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel" title="Nicolas Flamel">Nicolas Flamel</a> in 1927.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> chose to place an enormous Wizard's Chessboard in the third of the <a href="/wiki/Underground_Chambers" title="Underground Chambers">Underground Chambers</a> acting as security for the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>. When Harry, Ron and Hermione encountered the Chessboard while trying to beat <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> whom they believed) to the Stone, Harry played as one of the black bishops, Hermione as a rook, and Ron as a Knight. Using his extensive knowledge of chess, Ron managed to get himself, Harry, and Hermione across, ultimately sacrificing himself to the White Queen so that Harry could checkmate the King. Fortunately, Ron later recovered.<sup id="cite_ref-PS16_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS16-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_Chess?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_Chess?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, the game was depicted with the chess pieces destroying each other (presumably to be fixed with the <a href="/wiki/Mending_Charm" title="Mending Charm">Mending Charm</a> at the end of the game), but in the book the pieces simply knock each other out and drag captured pieces off the edge of the board.</li>
<li>In the film the captured pieces were, in order: Black Pawn, White Bishop, Black Queen, White Knight, Black Pawn, Black Bishop, White Knight, Black Rook, Black Knight, White King.</li>
<li>Ron's chess set depicted in the film is a replica of the 12th century <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewis_Chessmen" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lewis Chessmen">Lewis Chessmen</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> student <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Markham" title="Kenny Markham">Kenny Markham</a> was a Wizard's Chess fan and collected chess pieces.</li>
<li>A similar concept is featured in Mikhail Bulgakov's novel <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Master_and_Margarita" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Master and Margarita">The Master and Margarita</a></i>, where Professor Woland (alias Satan) and his retinue play with a supernatural chess set whose pieces are animate and move by themselves.</li></ul>
