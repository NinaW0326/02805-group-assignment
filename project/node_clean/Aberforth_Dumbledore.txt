<p><b>Aberforth Dumbledore</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1883" class="mw-redirect" title="1883">1883</a>/<a href="/wiki/1884" class="mw-redirect" title="1884">1884</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, the second son of <a href="/wiki/Percival_Dumbledore" title="Percival Dumbledore">Percival</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kendra_Dumbledore" title="Kendra Dumbledore">Kendra Dumbledore</a>, younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus</a>, and older brother of <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Dumbledore" title="Ariana Dumbledore">Ariana</a>. He attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> between <a href="/wiki/1895" class="mw-redirect" title="1895">1895</a> and <a href="/wiki/1902" title="1902">1902</a>. His father — who had been sentenced to prison for attacking three <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> — died in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> when Aberforth was young, and his mother and sister were later accidentally killed. Aberforth blamed his brother Albus for the <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> of their sister, Ariana and thus their relationship was rather strained.
</p><p>Aberforth was the owner and barman of the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head_Inn" title="Hog&#39;s Head Inn">Hog's Head Inn</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a>, and was a member of the original <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, he assisted <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> from a distance during their <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter%27s_Horcrux_Hunt" title="Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter&#39;s Horcrux Hunt">Horcrux hunt</a>, sending the <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">House-elf</a> <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> to help them escape from <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>. He later allowed them to use a secret tunnel that connected the Hog's Head to Hogwarts when the <a href="/wiki/Trio" title="Trio">trio</a> arrived in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a>. He subsequently fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> and survived the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. He continued to live in Hogsmeade, tending to his <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goats</a>.
</p>

Albus wanted his younger brother to complete his education at Hogwarts, although he bitterly resented having to abandon his grand plans.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> By this time, Aberforth was described as a "wayward" young man, known to chuck goat dung at their neighbours and inclined to get into <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duels</a> with those he had disagreements with.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C18_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C18-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

When it was time for Aberforth to return to Hogwarts, he decided to finally confront Albus and Grindelwald on the impossibility of such a scheme and angrily pointed out on Albus's neglect of Ariana and that he could not be the heroic leader of the revolution with Ariana in tow, which Albus later said to have "brought him back to reality" despite not wanting to hear the truth of the words Aberforth shouted at him.

Albus tried to protect his brother and a three-way <a href="/wiki/Duel_at_Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Duel at Godric&#39;s Hollow">duel</a> broke out. The exchange was observed by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, who attempted to confront Mundungus following Aberforth's departure.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_C12_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP_C12-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Astronomy_Tower" title="Battle of the Astronomy Tower">Battle of the Astronomy Tower</a> took place at Hogwarts, culminating with the death of Albus Dumbledore at the hands of Severus Snape who (although this was unknown to everyone else at the time besides the deceased Dumbledore) was working under Albus's orders. A great number of people attended his funeral, including Aberforth, Hogwarts students, professors, old friends, Hogsmeade residents, Order of the Phoenix members and Ministry of Magic officials.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_C30_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP_C30-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> After his brother's death, Aberforth had no family left.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">next year</a>, since Dumbledore had died and could not protect the school from Death Eaters, Lord Voldemort managed to take over Hogwarts. Snape became Headmaster, and <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow" title="Amycus Carrow">Amycus Carrow</a>, two Death Eaters, became Deputy Headmaster and Deputy Headmistress. The Carrows enjoyed punishing students, and were cruel and sadistic. They used their power to torture the students who opposed them. Aberforth's pub was used as passageway into Hogwarts to the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>, he supplied the students in hiding with food and <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

Harry was unaware of the details, but occasionally looked at the mirror, thinking the eyes might belong to Albus.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> When Harry desperately requested help from the mirror whilst trapped at <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>, Aberforth sent <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> to the rescue.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C23_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C23-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He was sad to learn the <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> died in action.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Deciding to look for the next of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a> in the Death Eater-controlled <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>, Harry, Hermione, and Ron Apparated into <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> village, setting off a <a href="/wiki/Caterwauling_Charm" title="Caterwauling Charm">Caterwauling Charm</a> that alerted Death Eaters to their arrival. As they were hidden under the Invisibility Cloak, the Death Eaters decided to send <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> to find Harry. The Dementors, sensing fear, rapidly closed in, and Harry cast a <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a> to repel them. A door creaked open behind, and Aberforth waved the trio in. Aberforth then convinced the Death Eaters that he set off the alarm by letting his <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cat</a> out, and it was his goat Patronus, not Harry's <a href="/wiki/Deer" title="Deer">stag</a>, that they actually saw.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry recognised the man as Aberforth Dumbledore, Albus' younger brother. Aberforth wanted them to escape the village the next morning, believing the Order of the Phoenix finished, Voldemort had already won the war, and those still fighting were only deluding themselves. When Harry refused, saying he must complete Dumbledore's mission, Aberforth berated Harry for following his brother, and asked if he knew the entire truth about him. Harry, already doubting Dumbledore's intentions, was unable to respond. With little prodding, Aberforth indicated he knew <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">book</a>, and related the true story about their childhood and was shocked when Harry told him afterwards of Albus taking the <a href="/wiki/Emerald_Potion" title="Emerald Potion">Drink of Despair</a>, which revealed how much he still suffered great remorse for his past actions.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</div><p>Aberforth was regarded by most people who were familiar with him as being odd and gruff. <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>, who met him at the taking of the photo of the original Order, only described him as a "strange bloke".<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_C9_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP_C9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He had a curious obsession with <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goats</a>, which was seemed by some to be an inappropriate affection,<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_C24_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF_C24-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and dealt with a great many dodgy customers at the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head_Inn" title="Hog&#39;s Head Inn">Hog's Head Inn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_C9_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP_C9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also had a long memory; he once placed a lifetime ban from the Hog's Head on <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher" title="Mundungus Fletcher">Mundungus Fletcher</a> for offending him.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_C17_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP_C17-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Aberforth had a sharp wit and was a highly talented <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellist</a>, though he had no interest in academic matters. It is suggested that Aberforth was illiterate, hence why he did not let the scandal of him practising inappropriate <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charms</a> on a goat upset him. He was a very terse person, commenting that the <a href="/wiki/Trio" title="Trio">trio</a> were being "<i>Bloody Fools</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DH_C31_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C31-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aberforth generally seemed cynical and bad-tempered, such as when he sarcastically pointed out to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> how little <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> had really told him even while putting an enormous responsibility on his shoulders. However, despite this, Aberforth proved himself worthy of being a <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>, displaying an astonishing amount of bravery and remarkable amount of morality that held firm even when it came to his family despite his cynical and rough personality, with Albus describing Aberforth as ultimately an <i>infinitely better</i> man than he ever was. For example, although it was <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> who traumatised his sister, Aberforth never discriminated against them and found the thought of Albus and Grindelwald's quest for Muggle domination as being absolutely disgusting, not only because of the danger Ariana and himself would be under but also because of the overall amount of damage that would be caused by such quest, even going as far as to not hesitate to duel both of them at once, despite knowing fully well they were superior duelists who he would certainly not stand a chance against, with his magic seemingly being amplified by the anger and determination he felt to hold them at bay, a feat made even more impressive by the fact that he previously had been subjected to the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> by Grindelwald. In addition, Aberforth would join the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> despite it being led by the older brother who he so disliked for his glory-seeking, and blaming him for their younger sister's tragic <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>, indicating he was willing to overcome his grudge towards his brother to fight for the whole wizarding world, further showing his morality. Although he never forgave Albus for years, Aberforth still considered Albus his brother and maintained contact with him, with their relationship being deemed cordial by <a href="/wiki/Elphias_Doge" title="Elphias Doge">Elphias Doge</a>, and Aberforth joined the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, which Albus founded and led, and attended Albus's funeral. Also, in spite of claiming earlier that "<i>You-Know-Who's won, it's over, and anyone who's pretending different's kidding themselves</i>" and also showing still his resentment of Albus's true nature, Aberforth was shocked into silence as he contemplated what Harry had revealed: that Albus had never forgiven himself for Ariana's death and was always tortured by the fact that he was responsible for it. Ultimately forgiving his brother, Aberforth then fought bravely and selflessly at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, which may be out of a desire to honour his older brother's memory by protecting the school Albus had led for so long, possibly sparked by the regret of never even considering Albus had regretted Ariana's death and also that he never managed to reconcile with his brother. In addition, he appeared to consider the possibility that his brother would never resort to having Slytherin students held as ransom against the Death Eaters before sneering at the idea. His unwillingly courageous nature shows itself in one of his last conversations with Harry; after Harry thanked him for saving his and his friends' lives twice, Aberforth gruffly retorted, "<i>Look after 'em, then. I might not be able to save 'em a third time</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aberforth was rather willing to use underhanded tactics against his foes, as he suggested to Harry to hold <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> students hostage against their parents who also served as <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.
</p>

<p>Though he did not have his brother Albus' prestigious gifts, Aberforth was nonetheless a very talented and powerful <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> in his own right, given his participation in and subsequent survival of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First</a> and <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding Wars</a>, the latter of which included the legendary <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Aberforth Dumbledore once used a <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore%27s_goat_charm" title="Aberforth Dumbledore&#39;s goat charm">charm to keep a goat clean and its horns curly</a>. Though he was prosecuted because that charm was considered inappropriate, it was still a testament of sorts to his being skilled in charms.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_C24_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF_C24-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Aberforth was also able to conjure a corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>, which is incredibly advanced and powerful <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> as well as proof of superior magical ability. His Patronus took the corporeal form of a <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: During his school years, Aberforth was said to prefer to settle things by duelling instead of negotiating,<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C2_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> which shows that, even as a student, he was already an active and experienced duelist, although <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">his brother</a> was still superior to him, noted to have the ability to put Aberforth in his place had he wanted to. Despite this, by the age of fifteen, his duelling skills were already sufficient to stand his ground in the three-way duel between himself, Albus, and <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> when they were teenagers, both of whom eventually became arguably the most powerful duellists to ever live.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Aberforth also noted the reason he did not face <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Rakepick" title="Patricia Rakepick">Patricia Rakepick</a>, an infamously powerful individual, in battle when she came to his pub was not because he was afraid of facing her but because she would have disappeared without any traces for him to inform the Auror Office about, showing he was assured of his ability to at least fight against the dark witch long enough for reinforcements to come. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> he fought courageously and defeated the very skilled Death Eater <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood" title="Augustus Rookwood">Augustus Rookwood</a><sup id="cite_ref-DH_C31_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C31-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and emerged as one of the survivors,<sup id="cite_ref-DHF_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> showing him to have been remarkably proficient in <a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">martial magic</a> to have survived such a bloody battle.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Legilimency" title="Legilimency">Legilimency</a> (possibly)</b>: Harry once described him as having the same piercing look as his brother.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Given that Albus usually had this look when performing Legilimency, it's possible that Aberforth did the same.</li></ul>

<p>Aberforth's relationship with his parents is not very well known. His <a href="/wiki/Percival_Dumbledore" title="Percival Dumbledore">father</a> was imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> after he used illegal spells on three Muggle boys who attacked Ariana. Because of this, it's likely that, like Albus, Aberforth did not hold Percival in high regard. His <a href="/wiki/Kendra_Dumbledore" title="Kendra Dumbledore">mother</a> was described as a good woman, but Aberforth knew her secretive nature and bitterly described himself and Albus as having learned lies and secrets from her. Nevertheless, when Kendra was accidentally killed by Ariana, Aberforth's bitterness did not prevent him from being devastated by her death, judging from the grief and sadness with which he described the event.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Aberforth Dumbledore was <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Dumbledore" title="Ariana Dumbledore">Ariana Dumbledore</a>'s older brother. She seemed to have preferred him rather than their eldest brother, Albus Dumbledore. After her attack, Aberforth was the only one who could calm her down when she flew into a rage, something even their <a href="/wiki/Kendra_Dumbledore" title="Kendra Dumbledore">mother</a> could not always accomplish. The two often fed goats together in their free time. Aberforth was not present when Ariana lost control of her <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and accidentally killed their mother. He believed that he would have been able to prevent the death of his mother. After the unfortunate incident, he wanted to take care of Ariana, but their older brother <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus</a> insisted that Aberforth complete his education at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> first.
</p><p>Soon after, Albus became friends with <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, and Aberforth stepped in when they began making plans for world domination and <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> subjugation, pointing out that Ariana was in no state to be dragged along with them. This resulted in a three-way <a href="/wiki/Duel_at_Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Duel at Godric&#39;s Hollow">duel</a> between Ariana's brothers and Grindelwald. Ariana tried to interfere, and was inadvertently killed by one of them, which devastated Aberforth and caused an unending rift between him and Albus. It is unknown who actually cast the spell which led to her death. At Ariana's funeral, Aberforth broke his brother's nose and shouted at him that her death was all his fault. Long after Ariana's death, Aberforth continued to keep a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> of his late sister in the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head_Inn" title="Hog&#39;s Head Inn">Hog's Head Inn</a>, symbolising his affection for her. It concealed a secret passage to the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Aberforth was resentful and bitter about the fact that he was living under Albus's shadow due to Albus's superior magical capabilities and spent most of his time caring for his sick younger sister, <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Dumbledore" title="Ariana Dumbledore">Ariana</a>. After the death of <a href="/wiki/Kendra_Dumbledore" title="Kendra Dumbledore">Kendra Dumbledore</a>, Albus was left to care for his brother and sister and the two remained as strained as ever, as while Albus insisted that Aberforth complete his education, Aberforth disliked the way Albus was treating Ariana. When Albus and his friend, Gellert Grindelwald, began planning to start a revolution, Aberforth and Albus grew even more estranged as Aberforth was disgusted by his older brother's ambitions and this soon escalates to the point that Aberforth angrily confronted Albus and bluntly told him that Ariana would not be accompanying and his plans are impossible, resulting in Grindelwald initiating a duel that resulted in Ariana's death, which finally caused Albus and Aberforth to become fully estranged, as Aberforth felt extreme amount of anger at Albus and he fully blamed him for Ariana's death,<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> shouting at him at Ariana's funeral and breaking his nose.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C8_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <span>In his later years, </span><span>Albus became the longest and most noted </span><a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a><span> of </span><a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a><span> and was considered to be the greatest wizard of all time. During</span> this time, the two had relatively little contact, although Aberforth and Albus had managed to establish a cordial relationship according to Elphias Doge and Aberforth did join the original <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_C9_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP_C9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> as well as attended his brother's <a href="/wiki/Funeral_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="Funeral of Albus Dumbledore">funeral</a> in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_C30_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP_C30-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although Albus initially was resentful towards Aberforth for dragging him down from fame and glory with the responsibility of being head of the family, Albus loved his brother dearly, and came to respect and admire him greatly for his devotion to Ariana.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, despite Albus being known to feel sadness for his estrangement with his younger brother, he never attempted to change Aberforth's opinions of him, likely as he recognised that he fully deserved Aberforth's resentment and accepted it as punishment for his part in the tragic death of Ariana. But he did have a desire to reconcile with Aberforth, as whenever he looked at the Mirror of Erised, he would see Aberforth reconciled with him. While he still bitterly resented Albus even after the death of his older brother, Aberforth eventually forgave his late brother after Harry explained Albus' drinking of the <a href="/wiki/Emerald_Potion" title="Emerald Potion">Drink of Despair</a>, and fought in the defence of his brother's school, the first of the Hogsmeade residents to do so.
</p>

<p>Aberforth detested Gellert Grindelwald immensely. Grindelwald was Albus's friend after <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. While Aberforth was taking care of Ariana, Albus and Grindelwald were planning to enslave the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>. As the two boys were planning to travel around the world, they also planned to take Ariana with them. Aberforth opposed them, telling them that they were being irresponsible and that he would be able to take care of Ariana once more. Grindelwald became very angry and used the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> on Aberforth; Albus then defended his brother and the three boys began to duel. None of the three ever knew who had cast the fatal spell, but by the end of the duel, Ariana lay dead on the floor. Grindelwald immediately fled the country; the two brothers both lived the rest of their lives with the fear that <i>they</i> had cast the curse that killed their sister. Aberforth retained bitter feelings towards Grindelwald for many years after their fateful encounter, and when he recounted the events that led to his sister's death Harry noted the "positively dangerous" look that came over his face when Grindelwald's name was mentioned.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C28_7-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C28-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Although he had a good relationship with some members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, Aberforth was described as a "strange bloke" by <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_C9_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP_C9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>. Aberforth seemed to know <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Fred and George Weasley</a>. He saw Lupin fighting with <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a> and he told Tonks so. He met many allies of the Order during the Battle of Hogwarts, when many people were getting into <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> through a portrait-hole in a room of his pub.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_C31_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH_C31-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aberforth had a mixed relationship with <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher" title="Mundungus Fletcher">Mundungus Fletcher</a>. While Aberforth banned Mundungus for life from the bar for offending him,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_C17_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP_C17-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> the two remained in touch, with Mundungus selling stolen goods to Aberforth.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_C12_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP_C12-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He did not seem to have a very high opinion of <a href="/wiki/Elphias_Doge" title="Elphias Doge">Elphias Doge</a> for his hero worshipping Albus both as students and later in life.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore?veaction=edit&amp;section=22" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore?action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Welsh, <i>Aberforth</i> means "from the river".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Also, in Old English, <i>Aber</i> means "the river mouth", and would look like Æbber when written in Old English.
</p><p>Dumbledore is Old English for "bumblebee". When breaking down the etymology of Dumbledore, <i>dora</i> is the Old English word for humble-bee, which became <i>dore </i>or <i>dorre </i>in Middle English.
</p>

<ul><li>Aberforth appears briefly in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i> played by <a href="/wiki/Jim_McManus" title="Jim McManus">Jim McManus</a> and is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Ciar%C3%A1n_Hinds" title="Ciarán Hinds">Ciarán Hinds</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (film)">two-part film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>.</li>
<li>Though in the books his Patronus is a goat, in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i> he produces a non-corporeal Patronus while fending off hundreds of <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>.</li>
<li>The character first appeared as the innkeeper at the Hog's Head in <i>Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</i>, though he was not revealed to be Aberforth in the books until the final instalment. Many fans, however, made the connection through the hints provided. When asked about the various hints, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> series</a> author <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> at first coyly stated "I was quite proud of that clue," but then admitted "Well yes, obviously."<sup id="cite_ref-edinburhg_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edinburhg-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In a 2005 interview, when asked who her favourite member of the Order of the Phoenix was, J. K. Rowling stated that "...there is one member of the Order of the Phoenix that you have not yet met properly and you will, well, you know that they are a member, but you haven't really met them properly yet and you will meet them in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">seven</a>, so I am looking forward to that." It is likely that Rowling was referring to Aberforth, as readers had "met" him as the owner of the Hog's Head, but at the time did not yet know him for who he was.<sup id="cite_ref-cubreporter_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cubreporter-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In response to Twitter user <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://twitter.com/WeWillQuackYou">Tasha Florentz-Clift</a>'s comment "<a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://twitter.com/WeWillQuackYou/status/616133852428374016">"It does not do to dwell on goats and forget to live." J.K. @jk_rowling #AddGoatRuinAQuote</a>", J.K. Rowling stated "<a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://twitter.com/jk_rowling/status/616159443378638848">That just turns Albus's motto into Aberforth's.</a>"</li></ul>
