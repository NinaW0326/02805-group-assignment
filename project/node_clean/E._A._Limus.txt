<b>E. A. Limus</b> was an American <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard or witch</a> who worked as the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Identity_Commissioner" title="Federal Identity Commissioner">Federal Identity Commissioner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chief_Auror" title="Chief Auror">Chief Auror</a><sup id="cite_ref-CB_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CB-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-macusaid_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macusaid-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Federal Identity Commissioner, their job involved keeping records of MACUSA employees and certifying employee identification.<sup id="cite_ref-macusaid_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macusaid-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As such, their signature appeared on the identification card of <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Porpentina Goldstein</a>, a worker in the <a href="/wiki/Wand_Permit_Office" title="Wand Permit Office">Wand Permit Office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-macusaid_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macusaid-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Chief Auror, they were presumably partly responsible for overseeing the work of American Aurors. Their signature appeared on wanted posters as certification.<sup id="cite_ref-CB_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CB-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
