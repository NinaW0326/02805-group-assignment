<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Macdonald?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Macdonald?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1970s" title="1970s">1970s</a> with <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Evans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH33_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH33-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sometime during her time at school, she was viciously assaulted by <a href="/wiki/Mulciber_(Marauder-era)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mulciber (Marauder-era)">Mulciber</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> and a future <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH33_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH33-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Evans</a> heard about this incident and thought it proved that Mulciber was "creepy" with an "evil" sense of humour<i>, </i>but Lily's friend <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> callously described the incident as "a laugh."<sup id="cite_ref-DH33_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH33-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mary later told Lily that Snape was planning to spend the night outside the <a href="/wiki/Fat_Lady" title="Fat Lady">entrance</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a> to apologise to her after he called her a "<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudblood</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-DH33_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH33-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Macdonald?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Macdonald?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Mary" is the usual English form of Maria, which was the Latin form of the New Testament Greek names Μαριαμ (Mariam) and Μαρια (Maria) - the spellings are interchangeable - which were from the Hebrew name מִרְיָם (Miryam). The meaning is not known for certain, but there are several theories including "sea of bitterness," "rebelliousness," and "wished for child." However it was most likely originally an Egyptian name, perhaps derived in part from mry ("beloved") or mr ("love").
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Macdonald?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Macdonald?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>It is unknown what <a href="/wiki/Mulciber_II" title="Mulciber II">Mulciber</a> tried to do to Mary or why she was chosen as his target. She might have been a <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">muggle-born</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitor</a>, or else she might have been a random target. <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a> claimed during his trial that Mulciber specialised in the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which could suggest Mary was the victim of something along those lines, but Karkaroff might have meant <a href="/wiki/Mulciber_I" title="Mulciber I">Mulciber's father</a> and not the Marauders' contemporary.</li>
<li>While it is not directly stated in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, Mary must have been a Gryffindor. She was able to tell Lily Evans that Severus Snape was waiting outside Gryffindor Tower, which means Mary was <i>inside </i>the tower at the time; few students have managed to access another house's common room, and those who do keep it secret.</li>
<li>Fans have speculated that there may be a connection between Mary Macdonald and <a href="/wiki/Mary_Cattermole" title="Mary Cattermole">Mary Cattermole</a>, a muggle-born witch who is known only by her married name, after both were introduced in <i>Deathly Hallows</i>. If they are the same person, Mary Macdonald would be a muggle-born and an obvious target for a future Death Eater; however, there is no evidence besides their shared first name and the chance that they share a <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood status</a>.</li></ul>
