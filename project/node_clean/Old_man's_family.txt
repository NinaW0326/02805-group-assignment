<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)"><b>Old man</b>: "'<i>Tis my old donkey, sir. Lost, she is, or stolen, and without her I cannot take my wares to market, and my family will go hungry tonight.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Wizard</b>: "<i>And I am hungry now!</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; The old man and the <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)" title="Wizard (The Wizard and the Hopping Pot)">Wizard</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>

<p>The <b>old man's family</b> were characters in the tale <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot" title="The Wizard and the Hopping Pot">The Wizard and the Hopping Pot</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Beedle_the_Bard" title="Beedle the Bard">Beedle the Bard</a>. Their <a href="/wiki/Old_man%27s_donkey_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)" class="mw-redirect" title="Old man&#39;s donkey (The Wizard and the Hopping Pot)">donkey</a> either ran away or was stolen, and the <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Old_Man" title="Unidentified Old Man">old man</a> asked the <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)" title="Wizard (The Wizard and the Hopping Pot)">Wizard</a> to help him, for without the donkey to carry his wares to the market, his family would starve. The Wizard rudely refused to help him.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
