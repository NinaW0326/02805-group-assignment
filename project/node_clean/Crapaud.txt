<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Quidditch Through the Ages (real)"><b>Grenouille</b>: "<i>I cannot go with you to the market today, Crapaud.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Crapaud</b>: "<i>But Grenouille, I cannot carry the cow alone.</i>"</dd>
<dd><b>Grenouille</b>: "<i>You know, Crapaud, that I am to be Keeper this morning.  Who will stop the Quaffle if I do not?</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Extract from <i>Hélas, Je me suis Transfiguré Les Pieds</i><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages_(real)" title="Quidditch Through the Ages (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p><b>Crapaud</b> is a character in the <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">15th century</a> play <i><a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9las,_Je_me_suis_Transfigur%C3%A9_Les_Pieds" title="Hélas, Je me suis Transfiguré Les Pieds">Hélas, Je me suis Transfiguré Les Pieds</a></i> by the French writer, <a href="/wiki/Malecrit" title="Malecrit">Malecrit</a>.
In the play, Crapaud asks <a href="/wiki/Grenouille" title="Grenouille">Grenouille</a> for help to carry a cow to the market, but Grenouille has to refuse since he is due to play <a href="/wiki/Keeper" title="Keeper">Keeper</a> for his local <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_team" title="Quidditch team">team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-QA8_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QA8-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The play's main conflict occurs when Crapaud performs the titular foot-transfiguration, an act traditionally accompanied by a puff of yellow smoke (which is conjured by a <a href="/wiki/Special_effects_wizard" title="Special effects wizard">special effects wizard</a> concealed beneath the stage).<sup id="cite_ref-Wonderbook_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wonderbook-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The play concludes with Crapaud's final rousing monologue.<sup id="cite_ref-Wonderbook_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wonderbook-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
