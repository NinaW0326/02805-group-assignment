<p><b>Mary Lou Barebone</b><sup id="cite_ref-EW2016-08-10_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW2016-08-10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> (d. <a href="/wiki/7_December" title="7 December">7 December</a>, <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">No-Maj</a> who lived in the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-everything_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-everything-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She was born into the <a href="/wiki/Barebone_family" title="Barebone family">Barebone family</a>, a family that descended from one of the&#160;<a href="/wiki/Scourers" title="Scourers">Scourers</a>&#160;who evaded justice in the late&#160;<a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th century</a> by integrating into No-Maj society.
</p><p>Mary Lou was marked by a profound belief in the existence of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and an equally deep hatred of it. She was the leader of the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">New Salem Philanthropic Society</a>, also known as the Second Salemers, an extremist group whose goal was to expose and eradicate <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witchcraft</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-everything_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-everything-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-snitchseeker1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snitchseeker1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-entertainmentweekly2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entertainmentweekly2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She had three adopted children:<sup id="cite_ref-EW2016-08-10_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW2016-08-10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chastity_Barebone" title="Chastity Barebone">Chastity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Modesty_Barebone" title="Modesty Barebone">Modesty</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-EW2016-08-10_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW2016-08-10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-entertainmentweekly1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entertainmentweekly1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mary Lou died on <a href="/wiki/7_December" title="7 December">7 December</a>, <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> at the hands of Credence's unleashed <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">Obscurus</a>. She was violently flung against the rafters in the chapel of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Salem_Church" title="Second Salem Church">Second Salem Church</a> and fell dead with formidable marks covering her face.
</p>

<p>Mary Lou Barebone was born into the <a href="/wiki/Barebone_family" title="Barebone family">Barebone family</a>, which was a family that descended from one of the&#160;<a href="/wiki/Scourers" title="Scourers">Scourers</a>&#160;who evaded justice in the late&#160;17th century&#160;by integrating into <a href="/wiki/No-Maj" class="mw-redirect" title="No-Maj">No-Maj</a> society. The Barebones were infamous for their profound belief in <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and their equally deep hatred of it.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point, Mary Lou became the leader of the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">New Salem Philanthropic Society</a>, an American No-Maj anti-witchcraft group located in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>. By December of <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Mary Lou had three adopted children, named <a href="/wiki/Chastity_Barebone" title="Chastity Barebone">Chastity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modesty_Barebone" title="Modesty Barebone">Modesty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Credence was given to her by the half-<a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a>/<a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">elf</a> <a href="/wiki/Irma_Dugard" title="Irma Dugard">Irma Dugard</a>, who saw necessary to have Credence raised by Mary Lou, likely unaware of Mary Lou's disciplinary and abusive attitude.<sup id="cite_ref-FB:TCoG_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB:TCoG-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point before December of <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Mary Lou was magically assaulted by Auror <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Porpentina Goldstein</a> because she was physically abusing Credence.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On the steps of the <a href="/wiki/Steen_National_Bank" title="Steen National Bank">Steen National Bank</a> on <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Mary Lou, along with her children, preached to the public in a rally supporting the New Salem Philanthropic Society. After she praised the marvellous man-made inventions of New York, she insisted that the public heed her warnings of the presence of a mysterious <a href="/wiki/Obscurial" title="Obscurial">force</a> that's wreaking havoc in the city, and she implored the audience to join the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">Second Salemers</a> in the fight against wizardkind. From the group of spectators, and unaware that he was a wizard, Mary Lou spoke directly to <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a>, asking if he was a seeker of truth, before he ran into the bank.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Langdon_Shaw" title="Langdon Shaw">Langdon Shaw</a>, a believer in magic, brought Mary Lou and her children to his father, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Senior" title="Henry Shaw Senior">Henry Shaw Senior</a>'s, <a href="/wiki/Shaw_Tower" title="Shaw Tower">office</a> in an attempt to garnish the support of his <a href="/wiki/Shaw_News" title="Shaw News">newspaper company</a> and his elder son's reelection platform. The meeting escalated quickly as <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior" title="Henry Shaw Junior">Henry Shaw Junior</a> called her son <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence</a> a freak. Understanding that the Shaw family was not going to support the Second Salemers, Mary Lou politely bid them a good-bye.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The next day, Mary Lou's son Credence found a seemingly real <a href="/wiki/Modesty_Barebone%27s_wand" title="Modesty Barebone&#39;s wand">wand</a> under her daughter Modesty's bed. As soon as the girl arrived to protest that it was just a toy, Mary Lou entered and was appalled to see what her son was holding. As <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence</a> removed his belt so she could punish him, Mary Lou snapped the wand in half and <a href="/wiki/Modesty_Barebone" title="Modesty Barebone">Modesty</a> confessed saying the wand was hers. But Mary Lou assumed it was his. The belt suddenly flung out of Mary Lou's grasp, injuring her hand, and as she went to pick it back up again, it again magically flew away. After she turned to confront her children, Mary Lou was attacked by Credence's <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">Obscurus</a>. She was flung against the rafters of the chapel and fell dead with formidable marks covering her face.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, while speaking to his allies about Credence, <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> bitterly remarked that Mary Lou, the woman who raised Credence, was nearly the same one who destroyed him. Sometime afterwards, when Credence met Irma Dugard, she explained to him why she left him in Barebone's charge, reiterating that it was necessary.<sup id="cite_ref-FB:TCoG_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB:TCoG-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mary Lou Barebone was described as a handsome mid-western woman who wore 1920s versions of <a href="/wiki/Puritanism" title="Puritanism">Puritanical</a> dresses.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She had brown hair in a styled bob haircut with blue eyes. She always carried a charismatic and earnest disposition.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mary Lou Barebone was a fanatical bigot against the magical community, and would raise her adopted children to hate witchcraft. Her utter intolerance against them bordered on the pathological, as she regularly physically, verbally, and emotionally abused and tormented her adopted children out of paranoia that they too, were witches or wizards (in fact, one of her "children", Credence, actually <i>was </i>magical). Mary Lou was also a sadistic disciplinarian; the slightest infraction of her strict rules by her children would result in abuse.<sup id="cite_ref-EW2016-08-10_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW2016-08-10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, Mary Lou also knew how to speak to a crowd, where she presented herself as composed, well-spoken, and inquisitive in a friendly manner, such as when she calmly asked Newt Scamander for his opinion on the subject of witches living amongst New York City's people. She also showed an array of good mannerisms and an understanding of defeat as she displayed these qualities to <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior" title="Henry Shaw Junior">Henry Shaw Jr</a> when he decided not to support the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">Second Salemers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FBaWtFT_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBaWtFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Mary Lou Barebone is portrayed by English actress <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Morton" title="Samantha Morton">Samantha Morton</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(film)" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-businesswire_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businesswire-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Mary Lou seemed to be the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">non-magical</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> counterpart of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacists</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>. While <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s followers and Umbridge felt that the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> wizards must be wiped out or separated from the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding world</a> or subjugated, Mary's ideology was the polar opposite because she felt that <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">Wizardkind</a> must be destroyed to save the world as a whole.</li></ul>
