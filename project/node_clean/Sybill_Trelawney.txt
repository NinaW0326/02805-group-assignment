<p><b>Professor Sybill Patricia</b><sup id="cite_ref-Patricia_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patricia-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><b> Trelawney</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/9_March" title="9 March">9 March</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pottermorebirthday_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorebirthday-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">pre 1962</a><sup id="cite_ref-birth_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birth-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a><sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. She is the great-great-granddaughter of the celebrated <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney" title="Cassandra Trelawney">Cassandra Trelawney</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> who was also a <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a>.
</p><p>It was Trelawney herself who made <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_first_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s first prophecy">the prophecy</a> concerning <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and the one with the power to vanquish him (Voldemort took this to mean <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>) during her job interview with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%931994_school_year" title="1993–1994 school year">1993–1994 school year</a>, she accurately <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_second_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s second prophecy">predicted</a> the escape of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">return</a> of Lord Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%931996_school_year" title="1995–1996 school year">1995–1996 school year</a>, Trelawney was greatly distressed by <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, who dismissed her and attempted to banish her from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>, though Dumbledore allowed her to remain at the school,<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> with Umbridge being greatly offended when she later had to teach alongside the <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaur</a> <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%931998_school_year" title="1997–1998 school year">1997–1998 school year</a>, she participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, dropping <a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">crystal balls</a> on <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>' heads<sup id="cite_ref-DH_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and tending to the wounded and dead along with <a href="/wiki/Padma_Patil" title="Padma Patil">Padma Patil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHP2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Professor Trelawney continued to teach well into the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, continuing her tradition of predicting the <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">deaths</a> of her students.<sup id="cite_ref-PI_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PI-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Since Firenze was welcomed back to <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest_Centaur_colony" title="Forbidden Forest Centaur colony">his herd</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Firenze_gone.3F_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Firenze_gone.3F-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> it is possible that she taught Divination on her own again.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix"><b>Dolores Umbridge</b>: "<i>And you are a great-great-granddaughter of the celebrated seer Cassandra Trelawney?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Trelawney</b>: "<i>Yes.</i>"</dd>
<dd><b>Dolores Umbridge</b>: "<i>But I think — correct me if I am mistaken — that you are the first in your family since Cassandra to be possessed of the Second Sight?</i>"</dd>
<dd><b>Trelawney</b>: "<i>These things often skip — er — three generations.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Sybill Trelawney being questioned by <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p>Sybill was born on <a href="/wiki/9_March" title="9 March">9 March</a> in an unknown year, to a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> mother and <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> father,<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> into the <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Trelawney_family" title="Trelawney family">Trelawney family</a>. There were three generations between her and <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney" title="Cassandra Trelawney">Cassandra Trelawney</a>, who was a renowned <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a>. No one in the family since Cassandra had possessed the <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Second Sight</a>.
</p><p>Presumably purchasing or inheriting <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_wand" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s wand">her wand</a> at the age of eleven, Sybill attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, which she referred to as her home. Being <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> by <a href="/wiki/1973" title="1973">1973</a> at the latest, she may have been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a><sup id="cite_ref-Slug_Club_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slug_Club-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> because of her ancestry. In her <a href="/wiki/Third_year" title="Third year">third year</a>, Sybill would have taken <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> as one of her elective <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_subjects" title="Hogwarts subjects">subjects</a> in her <a href="/wiki/Third_year" title="Third year">third year</a>, and achieved high grades in her <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T</a>.
</p><p>She married early, but it ended in unforeseen rupture due to Sybill's refusal to adopt the surname <a href="/wiki/Higglebottom" title="Higglebottom">Higglebottom</a>. They had no children.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After graduating, Sybill drifted through the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, trying to gain employment based on her ancestry but scorning those who did not offer what she felt was the rightful treatment of a true Seer.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Either late in <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a> or early in <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Sybill, who by this point had begun making false predictions, arranged a meeting with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head" class="mw-redirect" title="Hog&#39;s Head">Hog's Head</a> where she was staying in the hopes of acquiring the position of <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a>. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a>, who was not planning on continuing divination at Hogwarts, agreed to give her a chance because of her ancestry.
</p><p>Sybill displayed none of <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney" title="Cassandra Trelawney">Cassandra</a>'s divinatory skills, which disappointed <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> even though he was planning on discontinuing the subject anyway. After courteously telling her she was not fit for the position, Sybill entered a real trance and made the <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_first_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s first prophecy">prophecy</a> about <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Attack_at_Godric%27s_Hollow_(1981)" title="Attack at Godric&#39;s Hollow (1981)">defeat</a>. It so happened that the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> was eavesdropping at the door, which placed Trelawney in great danger from Voldemort. Hoping to protect Sybill, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> hired her; despite this fact, she did not join the faculty until 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As a professor, Trelawney taught in the <a href="/wiki/Divination_Classroom" title="Divination Classroom">Divination Classroom</a> on the seventh floor of the <a href="/wiki/North_Tower" title="North Tower">North Tower</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She presumably also ran the <a href="/wiki/Divination_Workshop" title="Divination Workshop">Divination Workshop</a>. Sybill made many <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_predictions" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s predictions">predictions</a> over her time at Hogwarts. Minerva McGonagall once claimed that she predicted the death of one of her students every year, none of whom had yet died.
</p><p>Knowing her talents pale in comparison to her colleagues,<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Professor Trelawney very rarely attended meals in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a> with the rest of the school, preferring to remain in her tower. She appeared to be acquainted with a certain colleague of hers, but frequently clashed with Professor McGonagall.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trelawney attended the <a href="/wiki/Start-of-Term_Feast" title="Start-of-Term Feast">Start-of-Term Feast</a> in <a href="/wiki/1989" title="1989">1989</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During the <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%931994_school_year" title="1993–1994 school year">1993–1994 school year</a>, Professor Trelawney gained <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> as a student, who had only chosen to take <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> because his friend, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a>, had.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> She opened her lesson with normal fortune-teller practises and vague predictions, including that <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> would break at least one teacup, something <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a> was dreading would happen on 16 October, and that, around Easter, one of her students would leave the class forever. During a class on tea leaves, Trelawney saw the <a href="/wiki/Grim" title="Grim">Grim</a> — a well-known omen of death — in Harry Potter's cup. This visibly disturbed the class, who went rather shaken to <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a> with <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>After Neville broke two teacups, Lavender's rabbit <a href="/wiki/Binky" title="Binky">Binky</a> was killed, and Hermione Granger, after being greatly insulted by Professor Trelawney, stormed out of a lesson, Lavender and <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a> became great supporters of Trelawney.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OTPG_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTPG-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Christmas, Trelawney decided to join the small dinner in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>. However, once she saw the amount of people at the table she tried to refuse, claiming that if she joined, they would become a party of the unlucky number thirteen and the first to rise would be the first to die. An impatient <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">McGonagall</a> stated that they would risk it. Two hours later, both Harry and Ron bolted up from their seats, causing Trelawney to shrilly ask which one of them got up first. McGonagall dryly joked that unless there was a mad axe-man waiting in the <a href="/wiki/Entrance_Hall" title="Entrance Hall">Entrance Hall</a> to slaughter the first person he saw, it made little difference. However, Ron was accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew#Scabbers" title="Peter Pettigrew">Scabbers</a> at the time, who unbeknownst to all was actually <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>, thus meaning that thirteen dined at the table. As Dumbledore stood to welcome Trelawney, he was the first to rise from a table of thirteen, and also the first to die, meaning that Trelawney predicted the death of Dumbledore years before the event occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>While conducting the third year exams with the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindors</a>, Professor Trelawney, while invigilating Harry Potter, entered a trance and made a <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_second_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s second prophecy">prediction</a> that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">servant</a> would be freed, returning to him that night. <p>The following year, the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> took place at Hogwarts and Harry Potter was one of the champions. Trelawney continued to annoy him with increased predictions of his death. She guessed that Harry had lost his parents because he was born during midwinter, heralding a tragic life. However, Harry told her he was born in July. Unbeknownst to Harry, who was the final Horcrux, Voldemort was born in December. So, Trelawney’s prediction of a tragic life was in fact accurate.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Once, <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>, in the form of a beetle, eavesdropped upon Harry during Trelawney's lesson when Harry had a crisis caused by his scar. Trelawney was very excited and pressed Harry to tell her what had happened, saying that she had experience in these matters and that the clairvoyant vibrations of her room could allow him to see further than he had ever seen before. Unfortunately for Trelawney, Harry refused.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of the year, Trelawney learned about the death of <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> and the return of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> had begun.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Early on in the <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%931996_school_year" title="1995–1996 school year">1995–1996 school year</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> was made <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_High_Inquisitor" title="Hogwarts High Inquisitor">Hogwarts High Inquisitor</a> by <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> edict, and appeared in Trelawney's classroom with a clipboard, making notes. Trelawney saw this as an intrusion, and was most upset and angered by it. Umbridge asked Trelawney to justify her teaching techniques and required a prediction from her. Trelawney at first was indignant, and haughtily claimed that the Inner Eye did not See upon command. Unimpressed, Umbridge made to leave. Panicked, Trelawney attempted to persuade Umbridge that she could suddenly see great danger in her future, but the High Inquisitor remained unmoved -- whether Trelawney <i>had</i> actually seen Umbridge in danger in the forest at the end of the year or whether she just made it up based on the Defence Against the Dark Arts curse is unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During free time that year, Professor Trelawney was visited in her tower by Lavender Brown and the <a href="/wiki/Padma_Patil" title="Padma Patil">Patil</a> twins, who had additional sessions of Divination with her.<sup id="cite_ref-OTPG_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTPG-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Umbridge later put Professor Trelawney on <a href="/wiki/Probation" title="Probation">probation</a>, which greatly distressed and insulted her. She became uncharacteristically brisk and started drinking a large amount of <a href="/wiki/Sherry" title="Sherry">cooking sherry</a>, which she hid in the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Harry Potter noted that Trelawney became more harassed and bedraggled in appearance, like she was falling apart at the seams. Umbridge would stalk Trelawney during every one of her lectures, interrupting her increasingly hysterical talks with difficult questions regarding <a href="/wiki/Heptomology" title="Heptomology">Heptomology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ornithomancy" title="Ornithomancy">Ornithomancy</a>, insisting that Sybill predict students' answers before they gave it to her, and demanding she demonstrate her skills at the <a href="/wiki/Crystal-gazing" title="Crystal-gazing">crystal ball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tessomancy" title="Tessomancy">tea leaves</a> and rune stone in turn. Umbridge states that she saw no improvement in Trelawney's performance to the point that she could not predict tomorrow's weather.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Harry Potter stood up to Professor Umbridge by giving an interview to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>, edited by <a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood" title="Xenophilius Lovegood">Xenophilius Lovegood</a>, Trelawney showed her support by breaking her usual pattern of predicting horrible deaths for Harry, instead predicting that he would have a long life, become <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, and have twelve children, a prediction that made Umbridge unhappy.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Eventually, Trelawney was <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Dismissal" title="Order of Dismissal">dismissed</a> by Umbridge, who almost threw her bodily from the castle, gleefully relishing the fact that Trelawney was in paroxysms of grief over her dismissal. Before she could be chased off the premises, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> came to her aid and insisted that she stay at Hogwarts, as Umbridge had no authority to banish her from the school grounds. This was partially to protect Trelawney from Voldemort who would target her for making the prediction concerning Harry and himself. Despite not having much respect for Trelawney, McGonagall comforted her as best she could and helped <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Filius Flitwick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Pomona Sprout</a> get her back inside to her tower.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trelawney was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaur</a> <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a>, appointed by Dumbledore, much to Umbridge's shock and fury; she hated both the fact that Firenze was what she called a "half-breed" and that Dumbledore appointed someone without consulting her (in spite of the fact that <a href="/wiki/Educational_Decree_Number_Twenty-Two" title="Educational Decree Number Twenty-Two">Educational Decree Number Twenty-Two</a> does not let her deny such an action). Firenze taught in <a href="/wiki/Classroom_Eleven" title="Classroom Eleven">Classroom 11</a> rather than the <a href="/wiki/North_Tower" title="North Tower">North Tower</a>, where Trelawney remained living.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was finally reinstated to her teaching position after Umbridge was suspended. This meant that, since Dumbledore could not ask him to return to the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest_Centaur_colony" title="Forbidden Forest Centaur colony">herd</a> that tried to kill him, she would have to share classes with Professor Firenze, a fact she was not pleased with.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Battle of Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Battle of Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>And with a move like a tennis serve, she heaved another enormous crystal sphere from her bag, waved her wand through the air, and caused the ball to speed across the hall and smash through a window.</i>"</span></dd>

<p>During the 1997–1998 school year, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> had taken over <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> and made Snape headmaster. <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto Carrow</a> replaced the murdered <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a> teacher, <a href="/wiki/Charity_Burbage" title="Charity Burbage">Charity Burbage</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow" title="Amycus Carrow">Amycus Carrow</a> took Snape's position as <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> teacher, which became simply the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts</a>. It is possible that she, like all of the other staff members, tried to protect the students from the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. Professor Trelawney, being <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>, was permitted to remain teaching at the school. It is unknown why Voldemort did not attempt to capture her. It is possible that since Voldemort had since turned his attention to finding the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">Elder Wand</a>, he believed that any prophecy concerning him and Harry would be rendered irrelevant upon its retrieval, of which he was wrong.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

She fought by dropping <a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">crystal balls</a> on enemies' heads, successfully knocking out <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> as he attacked <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Professor Trelawney survived the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> and continued to teach well into the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, continuing her tradition of predicting the deaths of her students.<sup id="cite_ref-PI_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PI-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Since <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> was welcomed back to <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest_Centaur_colony" title="Forbidden Forest Centaur colony">his herd</a><sup id="cite_ref-Firenze_gone.3F_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Firenze_gone.3F-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>, it is possible that she taught <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> on her own again. Trelawney wrote a book entitled <i><a href="/wiki/My_Eyes_and_How_to_See_Past_Them" title="My Eyes and How to See Past Them">My Eyes and How to See Past Them</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Calamity">The Calamity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Calamity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: The Calamity"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the 2010s, Professor Trelawney appeared as a <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundable</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a>. Volunteer members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> were able to free her, allowing Trelawney to return to her original time.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/August" title="August">August</a> <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>, during another surge of the Calamity, fragments were found of a <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_possible_third_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s possible third prophecy">prophecy</a> allegedly made by Professor Trelawney in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. In it, she predicted the Calamity. She also claimed that the “Ministry of Fools” will blame “the Grim man” for at least a year. However, since <a href="/wiki/Grim_Fawley" title="Grim Fawley">Grim Fawley</a> learned to manipulate the Calamity, this prophecy could have been falsified.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Professor Trelawney was described as being a thin woman, usually draped in gauzy shawls, and cloaks and bangles all covered with shining sequins and glittering strings of beads.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She had thick glasses, which hugely magnified her eyes and caused them to appear about ten times their normal size. She spoke in a soft, misty, ethereal voice, though she would become brisk and snappy when she was angry or upset.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Umbridge's constant harassment, Trelawney's appearance became increasingly bedraggled from the stress, with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> describing her as though she was falling apart at the seams.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">"<i>Welcome. How nice to see you in the physical world at last. Welcome to <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a>. My name is Professor Trelawney. You may not have seen me before. I find that descending too often into the hustle and bustle of the main school clouds my <a href="/wiki/Inner_Eye" title="Inner Eye">Inner Eye</a></i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Professor Trelawney greets her third-year students<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Trelawney was extremely eccentric; she had a theatrical presence and frequently spoke in misty, ethereal tones. She saw herself as being a great <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a>, although her predictions rarely came true. Much like <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>, Trelawney was sometimes unable to understand who liked and who disliked her. She was, however, conscious of her low status amongst the staff, which led to her spending most of her time apart from her colleagues in her office.
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a>, seeing death omens was a favourite way for Trelawney to greet her class, something that greatly annoyed <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> as he was her frequent target. One of her hobbies was practising doom-laden prophecies in front of the mirror.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She became angered when she believed that students were not impressed by nor interested in her subject, such as <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>.
</p><p>She took her appearance as a Seer seriously, putting on showy airs of mysticism and veiling her classroom in scented vapours. She could be very defensive in the face of those disbelieving in her abilities, attempting to impress them with false predictions of impending danger. She would claim that those unbelievers had "lack of Sight" or that they were simply scared of Seers.
</p><p>Trelawney was implied to have had a mild drinking problem; a number of times Harry detected the smell of old sherry on her, and at one point, she was seen stowing away empty <a href="/wiki/Alcohol" title="Alcohol">alcohol</a> bottles in the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>. She was especially fond of <a href="/wiki/Sherry" title="Sherry">sherry</a>. This escalated during the reign of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_High_Inquisitor" title="Hogwarts High Inquisitor">Hogwarts High Inquisitor</a>, in which Sybill's stress at being monitored so closely led her to appear more drunk during work hours.
</p>

<p>She was very sensitive, most notably when <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> placed her on probation and then dismissed her. Trelawney expressed on how she believed that all of the High Inquisitor's questionings and demands were an insult to a self-proclaimed Seer, but her own inability to cope with the pressure led to a spiralling emotional breakdown that Umbridge considered to be an embarrassment to the rest of the school. Trewlaney's poor control over her emotions led her to behave more unprofessionally when she turned to alcoholism to such an extent that Harry could catch the smell of sherry on her; she was actually holding a bottle of sherry at the time of her dismissal. Sybill was, however, loyal to the school and defended it during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire"><b>Trelawney</b>: "<i>I was saying that Saturn was surely in a position of power in the heavens at the moment of your birth... your dark hair... your mean stature... tragic losses so young in life... I think I am right in saying, my dear, that you were born in midwinter?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Harry Potter</b>: "<i>No, I was born in July.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Trelawney's often inaccurate abilities as a seer<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a></b>: Sybill Trelawney was a <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a>, meaning she could receive genuine <a href="/wiki/Vision" title="Vision">visions</a> of the future with her <a href="/wiki/Inner_Eye" title="Inner Eye">Inner Eye</a>. Despite this, Trelawney was generally dismissed as a fraud, but there is evidence that this is not true. Almost every prediction she made, including the ones not made under the influence of Second Sight, came to pass. The only possible exceptions to this were the ones where she foreshadowed Harry's death (though he did technically die in 1998) and the ones she blatantly fabricated for the <a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">Quibbler</a> interview simply to annoy <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Umbridge</a>. While Trelawney had the gift of sight, she lacked the gift of interpretation which is why when she saw a <a href="/wiki/Grim" title="Grim">grim</a> (a large black <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dog</a>) in the cup, she interpreted it as an omen of death rather than the return of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">a wizard in animagus form</a>. Her most notable non-second sight prediction was probably the foretelling of the <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Astronomy_Tower" title="Battle of the Astronomy Tower">Battle of the Astronomy Tower</a> via <a href="/wiki/Tarot_cards" title="Tarot cards">Tarot cards</a>. As noted prior, she did also possess the Second Sight like her ancestor, <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney" title="Cassandra Trelawney">Cassandra</a>. This caused her to go into trances, where she would speak true <a href="/wiki/Prophecy" title="Prophecy">prophecies</a> before returning to normal. However, she would have no memory of making the prediction afterwards. All things considered, Professor Trelawney could be said to be a rather accurate Seer in spite of her reputation.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Nonverbal magic</a></b>: Trelawney was able to cast <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charms</a> non-verbally. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, she launched <a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">crystal balls</a> at enemies using <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_wand" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s wand">her wand</a> without saying any <a href="/wiki/Incantation" title="Incantation">incantations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This actually testifies to her being a powerful witch, as non-verbal magic is very difficult.</li></ul>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">"<i>There's more to me than incense and tea leaves, Mr Finnigan.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Trelawney regarding her duelling skills<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: Trelawney was shown to have a remarkable amount of skill in duelling and in <a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">martial magic</a>, since she participated in and survived the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> For example, she blasted a Death Eater off a tower with significant force, to which members of Dumbledore's Army regard her with utter amazement.<sup id="cite_ref-SK_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SK-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Trelawney was capable of producing a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> which is a sign of superior <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> skill, given how advanced and difficult the immensely powerful <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spell</a> is to perform.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prophecies">Prophecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=18" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Prophecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Prophecies"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Chosen_One">The Chosen One</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Chosen One">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: The Chosen One"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dd><div class="noprint"><i>Main article&#58; <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_first_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s first prophecy">Sybill Trelawney's first prophecy</a></i></div></dd></dl>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>The one with the power to vanquish the Dark Lord approaches... born to those who have thrice defied him, born as the seventh month dies… and the Dark Lord will mark him as his equal, but he will have power the Dark Lord knows not… and either must die at the hand of the other for neither can live while the other survives… the  one with the power to vanquish the Dark Lord will be born as the seventh month dies.…</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The first Prophecy<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Her first true prediction was made in the presence of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head" class="mw-redirect" title="Hog&#39;s Head">Hog's Head</a> inn.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Trelawney was in the middle of an interview for a teaching job at Hogwarts when it was made. Dumbledore reported that he had decided not to give Trelawney the job, but changed his mind when he heard <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_first_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s first prophecy">the prophecy</a>. Trelawney herself is not aware that she made the prophecy, merely remembering that she came over faint, which she attributed to not having eaten that day.
</p><p>This prediction is believed to refer to Voldemort ("The Dark Lord") and Harry Potter, who was born on <a href="/wiki/31_July" title="31 July">31 July</a>. At the time of its prophesying, the identity of the child was not known as he had not been born yet. The time of year the prophecy was made is unknown. 
</p>

<p>It later became apparent that the prophecy could have applied to either Harry or <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, who was also born at the end of the seventh month on <a href="/wiki/30_July" title="30 July">30 July</a>. Part of the prediction came to pass on the night of <a href="/wiki/31_October" title="31 October">31 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, when Voldemort's body was destroyed after Harry survived an attack from him, using the killing curse, <i><a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Avada Kedavra</a></i>. The attack left Harry with an ability to speak <a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parseltongue</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_scars" title="Harry Potter&#39;s scars">lightning-shaped scar</a> on his forehead, and a mental connection to Voldemort. It was Dumbledore's view that it was Voldemort's choice of Harry as his target from the two possible boys, that caused Harry to have the power to vanquish the Dark Lord. In other words, the only reason Harry had the power to defeat Voldemort is that Voldemort believed he did. By attacking Harry, Voldemort caused the prophecy to become true.
</p><p>The prophecy as interpreted by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> to Harry in June <i><a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a></i> read: "Someone (Harry or Neville) with the power to vanquish Voldemort is coming... born to those who have defied Voldemort three times (the Potters or the Longbottoms), born at the end of July... and Voldemort will attempt to kill Harry, but Harry will unexpectedly survive... either Harry or Voldemort must be killed by the other because one of them must die... Harry will be born at the end of July..."
</p><p>When Harry and the others arrive in the <a href="/wiki/Hall_of_Prophecy" title="Hall of Prophecy">Hall of Prophecy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>, Ron found a <a href="/wiki/Prophecy_Record" title="Prophecy Record">record of the prophecy</a> that had a tag which read:
</p>
<dl><dd><i>S.P.T. to A.P.W.B.D.</i></dd>
<dd><i>Dark Lord</i></dd>
<dd><i>and (?) Harry Potter</i></dd></dl>
<p>S.P.T. stands for <b>S</b>ybill <b>P</b>atricia <b>T</b>relawney and A.P.W.B.D. stands for <b>A</b>lbus <b>P</b>ercival <b>W</b>ulfric <b>B</b>rian <b>D</b>umbledore. The question mark before the Harry Potter is because at the time it was unclear who the prophecy was written about, but when Voldemort sought to murder Harry, the prophecy record was changed to include his name.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_predictions">Other predictions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=21" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Other predictions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Other predictions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Professor Trelawney has made other minor predictions, too, some of which came true, though it may be a coincidence.
</p>
<ul><li>She told <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> to take a blue cup after breaking his first one, because she likes the pink ones. Neville immediately took a cup, broke it, and then later broke his second cup as well.</li>
<li>She also asked Neville about his grandmother, saying she may not be as well as he thought. Depending on your interpretation, this could arguably have come true. This may be Sybill misinterpreting Neville's first Defence Against the Dark Arts lesson, when Neville's boggart (taking the form of Snape) is forced to don his grandmother's clothes. Perhaps Sybill merely saw Mrs Longbottom's clothes with a mannish face, and mistakenly believed it was the real Mrs Longbottom with a distorted face.</li>
<li>She predicted Neville would be late for his next <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">divination</a> class.</li>
<li>She told <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a> to beware of a red-headed man. Parvati immediately suspected <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a>, who sat behind her. At the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> and Ron, Parvati's and her sister Padma's dates, ignored them both. Two years later, Ron started going out with Parvati's best friend <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>, who consequently started to ignore Parvati.</li>
<li>She predicted a bout of <a href="/wiki/Flu" title="Flu">flu</a> in <a href="/wiki/February" title="February">February</a>, and that she herself would lose her voice.</li></ul>

<ul><li>She predicted that the thing Lavender was dreading would happen on <a href="/wiki/16_October" title="16 October">16 October</a>. On that day, Lavender got a letter that her pet rabbit, Binky, had been killed by a fox. <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a> was sceptical, indicating that Lavender could hardly have dreaded her new rabbit dying, and that the death could not have happened on the day the news arrived. (Though this can be argued, as Lavender was dreading something horrible on that day.)
<ul><li>It is worth noting that everyone at Hogwarts - including Lavender - was perpetually afraid of Sirius Black breaking into the castle. <li>On the beginning of <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%931994_school_year" title="1993–1994 school year">1993–1994 school year</a>, she told in the whole class that, near <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>, one of their number will leave them forever. <figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 231px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Pdahq1_%281%29.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110716152328" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="229" height="153" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Pdahq1 (1).jpg" data-image-key="Pdahq1_(1).jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Pdahq1_%281%29.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/229?cb=20110716152328" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Pdahq1_%281%29.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110716152328" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Pdahq1_%281%29.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/229?cb=20110716152328" decoding="async" width="229" height="153" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Pdahq1 (1).jpg" data-image-key="Pdahq1_(1).jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3f/Pdahq1_%281%29.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/229?cb=20110716152328" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Pdahq1_(1).jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Sybill Trelawney - an experienced forecaster, though she does not remember her <a href="/wiki/Prophecy" title="Prophecy">prophecies</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure> It seems that her prediction was fulfilled when Hermione quit the class in disgust near Easter holidays.</li>
<li>At the <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> lunch, Trelawney at first refused to join the table, since <i>when thirteen dine together, the first to rise will be the first to die</i>. Harry Potter and Ron Weasley got up from the table first together, but Albus Dumbledore was the first among those at the table to die. Also, she did not take into account of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> (who was at the time disguised as Scabbers) which would in fact count as fourteen people. However before she was seated, there <i>were</i> thirteen people at the table, counting Scabbers. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> was the first to rise and greet her, and he was the first person to die.
<ul><li>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, thirteen members of the order dined together, and the first to rise was <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius</a>, who was the first to die of those thirteen people.</li>
<li>Also, after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>, thirteen people (Mr and Mrs Weasley, Bill, Fleur, Fred, George, Ron, Ginny, Harry, Hermione, Lupin, Tonks and Hagrid) gathered in <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a> to mourn the death of <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>. Afterwards, Lupin stood up and offered to look for Moody's dead body. Later in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Lupin was killed by <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a>. Fred and Tonks were also killed, but as the time of Lupin's death is unknown, it is possible he was first.</li></ul></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/1994-1995_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1994-1995 school year">Harry's fourth year</a> Trelawney, while discussing star charts, commented that given his "dark hair, mean stature, [and] tragic losses so young in life..." Saturn was likely in a position of power when he was born, and suggested that he was born in mid-winter. Interestingly (and unknown to both at that time), Harry was a <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>, who was born on New Years Eve.</li>
<li>When <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> was appointed <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_High_Inquisitor" title="Hogwarts High Inquisitor">Hogwarts High Inquisitor</a> during <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%931996_school_year" title="1995–1996 school year">Harry's fifth year</a>, she insisted that Trelawney demonstrate her abilities by prophesying something. Trelawney claimed that she saw dark events ahead for <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Umbridge</a>, which Umbridge immediately dismissed as nonsense. Later, Umbridge was dragged off and imprisoned by centaurs in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a>.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">Harry's sixth year</a> he saw Trelawney coming and hid from her. As she passed, he saw that she was examining a pack of cards and muttered, "A dark young man, possibly troubled, one who dislikes the questioner." She dismissed her own words as nonsense, unaware of Harry's presence or his dislike of her. Later in the year, Harry saw <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a> arguing with <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Snape</a> as he questioned Draco on the progress of his mission for Voldemort. Later Trelawney again met Harry in the corridors, and this time complained that Dumbledore had been repeatedly dismissing her warnings of impending disaster. She looked at some Tarot cards and saw, "... the lightning-struck tower... Calamity. Disaster. Coming nearer all the time...". This seems to predict the events of Dumbledore's death on the <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_Tower" title="Astronomy Tower">Astronomy Tower</a>. The Lightning-Struck Tower tarot card (The Tower) is generally considered representative of disaster or life-altering change.</li>
<li>According to Professor McGonagall, Trelawney had predicted the death of one student each year since her arrival at Hogwarts (which was in 1980). As of 1993, not a single one of those had yet died.<sup id="cite_ref-PA6_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA6-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li>To be fair, her predictions of death do not give any time frames. Since all people must die eventually, her predictions of death are necessarily accurate 100% of the time, as long as there is no time frame for these predictions to come to pass.</li></ul></li>
<li>In 1996, however, she retracted her prediction regarding Harry Potter as a means of attempting to annoy Dolores Umbridge, stating that he would live to a ripe, old age, become <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> and have twelve children; a prediction that also, as of now, has not come to pass as of the present day.<sup id="cite_ref-OP26_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP26-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_wand" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s wand">Wand</a></b>: Sybill Trelawney, presumably since the age of eleven, possessed a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> made of <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_hair" title="Unicorn hair">unicorn hair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hazel" title="Hazel">hazel</a>. She wielded it in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, launching <a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">crystal balls</a> at <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> in a tennis-serve motion.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">Crystal balls</a></b>: As one of the <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> teachers, Professor Trelawney owned several crystal balls. She used them as weapons during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, and used one to knock out the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatcher</a> <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_cat" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s cat">Pet cat</a></b>: Professor Trelawney owned a pet <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cat</a> who lived in the <a href="/wiki/Divination_Classroom" title="Divination Classroom">Divination Classroom</a> at the top of the <a href="/wiki/North_Tower" title="North Tower">North Tower</a>. It was sitting on a chair during her lessons and when Trelawney made her <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney%27s_second_prophecy" title="Sybill Trelawney&#39;s second prophecy">second prophecy</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Tarot_cards" title="Tarot cards">Tarot cards</a></b>: Professor Trelawney owned a pair of tarot cards for the means gaining insight into future events. During the <a href="/wiki/1996-1997_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1996-1997 school year">1996-1997 school year</a>, she made frequent visits to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> to inform him about bad omens she was seeing in her cards.</li></ul>

<p>Sybill took great pride in her lineage of the great Seer <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney" title="Cassandra Trelawney">Cassandra Trelawney</a>, and tend to ride on that fact when trying to gain employment, despite showcasing none of her great-great-grandmother's talent.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sybill married a man by the surname <a href="/wiki/Higglebottom" title="Higglebottom">Higglebottom</a>, but her refusal to take his surname caused their <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a> to end unexpectedly and rather acrimoniously.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<p><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a> seemed to have an exceptionally close relationship with Sybill Trelawney; they had an unwavering faith in the reliability of Trelawney's predictions, and were often seen comforting her and seeking out her company. The two girls often spent lunch periods in the North Tower with Trelawney, returning to the other Gryffindors with smug looks as though they knew something the others did not. Both of them even became offended on the Professor's behalf when Hermione made clear her disdain for Trelawney and the subject she taught in 1993. When she was temporarily sacked by Dolores Umbridge, Lavender and Parvati brought Trelawney some nice daffodils to cheer her up.
</p><p>For her part, Trelawney seemed to return their genuine affection for her. For example, she told Parvati that she had the makings of a true Seer,<sup id="cite_ref-PA_13-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and tried in vain to save Lavender from <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> by dropping a crystal ball on his head during the Battle of Hogwarts. Sadly, Lavender died from her injuries caused by the werewolf.
</p>

<p>Sybill Trelawney strongly disliked <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor and later <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_High_Inquisitor" title="Hogwarts High Inquisitor">Hogwarts High Inquisitor</a> because of the way the pink nightmare treated her during her insulting inspections of Sybill's Divination classes as well as being asked to predict on command. It was during Umbridge's reign at Hogwarts when Harry, who did not like Trelawney very much, felt pity for her.
</p><p>Umbridge interfered in Trelawney's lessons and soon put the Divination teacher on probation. After receiving this news, Trelawney sobbed and furiously proclaimed dramatically that she had been insulted by the establishment, those whose eyes were clouded by the Mundane, and did not possess the Inner Eye, and that it was, alas, the fate of all Seers to be feared by society and persecuted. Trelawney, very distressed, then began to drink copious amounts of <a href="/wiki/Sherry" title="Sherry">cooking sherry</a>. She became somewhat brisk in her lessons, looking very bedraggled and harassed much of the time, and would sob uncontrollably while teaching.
</p><p>Umbridge believed that Trelawney was an old fraud and, true to her duties of High Inquisitor to weed out any unsatisfactory teachers, eventually sacked her in a rather unceremonious — and even cruel — way. When Dumbledore defended Trelawney and let her stay at Hogwarts, Umbridge was furious with Dumbledore because he had already picked out a replacement teacher, <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a>. Firenze was a <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaur</a>, a creature Umbridge was prejudiced against because of her hatred of "half-breeds". Umbridge left Hogwarts in 1996 after the <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_in_the_Forbidden_Forest" title="Skirmish in the Forbidden Forest">Skirmish in the Forbidden Forest</a> at the end of the school year, and Trelawney resumed teaching Divination along with Firenze, who could not go back to his herd in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forest</a> as they had banished him for working with humans. Trelawney viewed this as a great insult.
</p>

<p>Professor Trelawney and <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> were colleagues as professors at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. McGonagall had no use for Trelawney's subject, <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a>, politely describing it as an 'imprecise' branch of magic. When Dolores sacked Trelawney, McGonagall comforted her and spoke out against Umbridge's behaviour.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_6-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=31" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Both her name and her surname are related to her future telling ability. "Sibyl" was a priestess in ancient Roman mythology. A sibyl (one "l") is a woman who could look into the future. A sibyl also can be any female prophet. The name is from the Latin sibylla, seer. Rowling chose to spell it "Sybill", because she did not feel Trelawney really qualified as a "Sibyl".<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Sybil" could also originate from the Sybilline Books, a Roman collection of oracular occurrences.
</p><p>Her middle name, "Patricia", comes from Latin and means "noble woman".
</p><p>"Trelawney" is derived from a famous cry of defiance from the south-west of England, where Rowling grew up, often shouted at football matches:
</p><p>"And shall Trelawney live? And shall Trelawney die? Here's ten thousand Cornishmen who ask the reason why!"
</p><p>The cry is a line from <i>The Song of the Western Men</i>, written in <a href="/wiki/1833" class="mw-redirect" title="1833">1833</a> by the poet and parson Robert Stephens Hawker and the unofficial Cornish national anthem. It concerns the march to London in <a href="/wiki/1688" class="mw-redirect" title="1688">1688</a> in protest at the incarceration in the Tower of Jonathan Trelawney, Bishop of Bristol. This could be a reference to Trelawney predicting the death of one of her students every year. It is also interesting to note that Bishop Trelawney also was released and went on to live for another thirty-three years, just as Professor Trelawney's famous death predictions rarely came true.
</p><p>The name "Trelawney" comes from the Cornish phrase <i>tre-lonow</i>, homestead of groves, or <i>tre-launow</i>, farm in clearing. J. K. Rowling claimed that she picked the name because of her love of Cornish surnames, which she had not used in her books before. The age of the name also made it suitable, because of Trelawney's over-reliance on her ancestry when seeking to impress.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Author's_comments"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Author.27s_comments">Author's comments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=32" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has stated that she does not believe in <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, and does not intend that her readers should believe in it either. She has hinted that the portrayal of Trelawney (a mystic and fortune-teller, perhaps the kind of "magic" that children are most likely to come across) as something of an ineffectual old fraud may be related to this. However, her name and the reference to a grandmother named Cassandra is somewhat contradicting. In Greek mythology, Cassandra was a Trojan <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">seer</a> who received the gift of prophecy from Apollo, but when she spurned his romantic advances, the god in his wrath cursed her that she would be ever correct but never believed. This suggests that perhaps some of Trelawney's pronouncements may be more accurate than is often believed.
</p><p>She said that she imagined that Trelawney's pre-Hogwarts existence consisted of drifting through the wizarding world, trying to trade on her ancestry to secure employment, and scorning those who did not offer her what she felt was the status due to a Seer.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, Trelawney's first name is misspelled as <i>Sybil</i>.</li>
<li>Her middle name Patricia is said to be Priscila in some of the Brazilian editions.</li>
<li>Sybill could possibly be derived from the name of a group ancient Roman oracles known as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sybilline_oracles" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Sybilline oracles">Sybilline oracles</a>, which is appropriate considering her ability to tell the future and make accurate prophecies.</li>
<li>Trelawney is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Emma_Thompson" title="Emma Thompson">Emma Thompson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">Order of the Phoenix</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>. Thompson does not appear in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i> because she was filming for <i>Nanny McPhee and the Big Bang</i>, despite the fact that <i>Harry Potter</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Smith" title="Maggie Smith">Maggie Smith</a> also appears in the film (although, as supporting cast, and thus not having as many scenes as Thompson, who portrayed the lead character, Nanny McPhee.) Thompson would later appear alongside <a href="/wiki/Emma_Watson" title="Emma Watson">Emma Watson</a> as Mrs Potts in Disney's live-action <i>Beauty and the Beast</i>, in which Watson plays the leading role of Belle, the Beauty of the film's title.
<ul><li>As well as having the same first name, Thompson and Watson share the same birthday - 15 April.</li></ul></li>
<li>Emma Thompson was married for several years to Kenneth Branagh (<a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>) who directed and starred alongside her in <i>Henry V</i>, <i>Dead Again</i>, <i>Peter's Friends</i>, and <i>Much Ado About Nothing</i>. Interestingly enough, Branagh was offered the chance to direct <i>Prisoner of Azkaban</i>, which marks the first appearance of Professor Trelawney in the series, before Alfonso Cuaron got the job.</li>
<li>Branagh broke up with Thompson after falling for Helena Bonham Carter (<a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>), whom he had met while directing <i>Mary Shelley's Frankenstein.</i></li>
<li>Trelawney predicting the prophecy and Snape overhearing it are not included in the films. Indeed, Trelawney plays a far smaller part in Harry's destiny in the films than in the books.</li>
<li>Trelawney told <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a> that the worst thing for her would come true on Friday, <a href="/wiki/16_October" title="16 October">16 October</a>. However, 16 October, <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a> was actually a Saturday.</li>
<li>Emma Thompson had originally stated that she would not return as Trelawney in the last films.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> However, she later confirmed that she would reprise her role as Trelawney and had already filmed her scenes.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Emma Thompson has appeared in several films with <a href="/wiki/Imelda_Staunton" title="Imelda Staunton">Imelda Staunton</a>; Much Ado About Nothing, Peters Friends, Sense and Sensibility and Nanny McPhee. <a href="/wiki/Alan_Rickman" title="Alan Rickman">Alan Rickman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hardy" title="Robert Hardy">Robert Hardy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gemma_Jones" title="Gemma Jones">Gemma Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Spriggs" title="Elizabeth Spriggs">Elizabeth Spriggs</a> also starred in <i>Sense and Sensibility</i>. <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Smith" title="Maggie Smith">Maggie Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" title="Ralph Fiennes">Ralph Fiennes</a> also appear in <i>Nanny McPhee and the Big Bang</i>.</li>
<li>Professor Trelawney was also referenced in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (video game)">video game adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i> by a locked door on the seventh floor that states if Harry tries to open it "Do not disturb, crystal gazing".</li>
<li>Oddly enough, many of the predictions made by Trelawney and others during the third year come true. Ron does in fact go through trials and suffering during the hunt for the Horcruxes, and ends up happy from the experience since he becomes closer to Hermione. Harry does come into a great sum of unexpected gold; his prize money for winning the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> (though later it was transferred to the Weasley twins), and his inheritance from Sirius after his death. Harry also ends up working for the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, although it is unknown if he ever became Minister. Though Trelawney seems to have been making it up at the time, seeing a death omen in Harry's cup, Harry did face his own death in 1998, although he did not actually die. However, the fact the death omen was in the shape of a dog may have been misinterpreted and may have truly been symbolising Sirius entering his life as an animagus.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, Trelawney appears for only a fleeting moment, respectfully covering one of the fallen battle casualties. She can also be seen from behind battling Death Eaters in the courtyard; look for her curly hair and distinctive green sparkling coat right after Hermione runs between the legs of the troll on the way to the boathouse. You can also hear a <i>Crucio</i> said in her voice.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, Sybill incorrectly states Harry Potter's birthday of as being during the winter. Interestingly enough, Voldemort's birthday is 31 December and Harry was later revealed to be one of Voldemort's Horcruxes. In addition, the other descriptions in Sybill's statement ("<i>your dark hair...your mean stature...tragic losses so young in life...</i>") could apply to Voldemort as well. So she may have made the prediction based on the piece of Voldemort's soul within Harry.</li>
<li>In book's Finnish edition Sybill's name is <i>Sibylla Punurmio</i>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, there is a deleted scene that portrays Trelawney dinning in the Great Hall at the opening feast of 1995, where she clumsily eats and makes a mess of her food.</li>
<li>A collector character wand has been made for Trelawney. However, her name is misspelled as <i>Sybil</i> on the box.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the <i>Harry Potter</i> Glossary on the <a href="/wiki/Bloomsbury_Harry_Potter_website" title="Bloomsbury Harry Potter website">Bloomsbury Harry Potter website</a>, her middle name is incorrectly given as "Phyllis."<sup id="cite_ref-bloomsglossary_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomsglossary-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>It is somewhat notable that <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> did not attempt to oust Professor Trelawney from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> when <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Professor Dumbledore</a> was temporarily removed from his post as <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a>. It is possible that Umbridge was so overwhelmed with the chaotic status of the school during her brief spell as Headmistress that she simply forgot. Another possibility is that Trelawney was either forcibly removed from the school or helped into hiding by Dumbledore, and <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> merely did not mention this in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">story</a>.</li>
<li>Trelawney bears a resemblance in both appearance and personality to the <i>American Horror Story: Coven</i> character Myrtle Snow, who is also a witch. Ironically, the show also focuses on a school for witches.</li>
<li>Trelawney is similar in name and characteristics to <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sybil_Leek">Sybil Leek</a>, an English witch, astrologer, occult author, antiques dealer, and self-proclaimed psychic. Similarities include:
<ul><li>Nearly identical first names.</li>
<li>Leek's claim to have been descended from the historical <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molly_Leigh">Molly Leigh</a>, who was accused of being a witch during the <a href="/wiki/Salem_Witch_Trials" title="Salem Witch Trials">Salem Witch Trials</a>.</li>
<li>Many people thought Leek as "either a bit of a joke or a fraud."</li>
<li>Leek's common disagreements or quarrels with other witches over differences in beliefs.</li>
<li>One of Leek's students admitted that Leek "mixed truths with untruths liberally, causing great harm as she went."</li></ul></li></ul>
