<b>Blodwyn Bludd</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1923" title="1923">1923</a>), also known as the <b>Vampire from the Valleys</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a> singer from the <a href="/wiki/Valleys" title="Valleys">Valleys</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. He was famous for his bass-baritone voice and for singing to his victims before sinking his fangs into their necks.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Blodwyn_Bludd?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Blodwyn_Bludd?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This character's name was clearly constructed primarily as a play on the word "blood," since Blodwyn is actually a girl's name, and means "white flowers" in Welsh.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The surname, using correct Welsh pronunciation, would lose its vampiric connotations, as it would be pronounced "Bleeth" (th as in 'the', not as in 'teeth').</li></ul>
