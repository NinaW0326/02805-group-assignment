<b>Eduardo Lima Filho</b> (b. sun in <a href="/wiki/Pisces" title="Pisces">Pisces</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">moon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pisces" title="Pisces">Pisces</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who worked in the <a href="/wiki/Runes_%26_Symbols" title="Runes &amp; Symbols">Runes &amp; Symbols</a> department of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Lima_Filho?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Lima_Filho?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This character was named after <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Lima" title="Eduardo Lima">Eduardo Lima</a>, a graphic artist who worked on every <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_films" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter films"><i>Harry Potter</i> film</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i> onward.</li>
<li>In accordance with the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_name" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Portuguese name">Portuguese naming conventions</a>, the suffix "Filho" is added to the name with initial upper case and without a separating comma, whenever someone is named after their father (in a similar way as "Jnr." is used in the English-speaking world). This practise is most common in Brazil.</li></ul>
