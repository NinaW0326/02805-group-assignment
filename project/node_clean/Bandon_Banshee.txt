<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Bandon" title="Bandon">Bandon</a> Banshee</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Banshee" title="Banshee">banshee</a> who was supposedly defeated by <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a><sup id="cite_ref-CS6_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>, but it was actually defeated by a <a href="/wiki/Witch_with_a_hairy_chin" title="Witch with a hairy chin">witch with a hairy chin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS16_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS16-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The book <i><a href="/wiki/Break_with_a_Banshee" title="Break with a Banshee">Break with a Banshee</a></i> is most likely centred around this Banshee's defeat.
</p><p>This achievement seemed to have been one of Lockhart's personal favourites, as he often discussed it, and when <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> was speaking disdainfully of Lockhart to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, he complained how Lockhart was "bangin’ on about some banshee he banished", when he visited him previously. Hagrid also correctly suspected then that Lockhart was indeed a dishonest con artist, as he stated afterwards that " If one word of it was true, I’ll eat my <a href="/wiki/Kettle" title="Kettle">kettle</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-COS7_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
