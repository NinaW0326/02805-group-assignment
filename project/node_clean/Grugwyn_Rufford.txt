<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Grugwyn_Rufford?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Grugwyn_Rufford?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Rufford complained to the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> because they did not cover the <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Welsh</a> victory over <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_National_Gobstones_Team" title="Hungarian National Gobstones Team">Hungary</a>. On <a href="/wiki/31_July" title="31 July">31 July</a> that year, the editor replied to him via an editorial saying that people found that <a href="/wiki/Gobstones" title="Gobstones">Gobstones</a> were "<i>deeply boring</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-DP_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Grugwyn_Rufford?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Grugwyn_Rufford?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is possible that this name 'Grugwyn' comes from Welsh <i>grug gwyn</i> "white heather".
</p>
