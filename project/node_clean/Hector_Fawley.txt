<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Fawley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Fawley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minister Fawley was voted in because of his marked difference to his predecessor, <a href="/wiki/Lorcan_McLaird" title="Lorcan McLaird">Lorcan McLaird</a> — while the latter was taciturn and an unlikely politician, Fawley was ebullient and flamboyant in character.
</p><p>His term in office coincided with the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Global_wizarding_war" title="Global wizarding war">"For the Greater Good" revolution</a>. Fawley did not take Grindelwald's threat to the world wizarding community sufficiently seriously, and his lack of response was openly questioned. The <a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a> once asked in <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, whether "Flamboyant Fawley" was doing enough.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>He was re-elected at least once, but the consequence of his lack of response to Grindelwald came in <a href="/wiki/1939" title="1939">1939</a>, when he was forced from his office, being replaced with the more proactive <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Spencer-Moon" title="Leonard Spencer-Moon">Leonard Spencer-Moon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Fawley?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Fawley?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Hector Fawley is the sitting Minister for Magic in Britain during the time setting of the films <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(film)" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts:_The_Crimes_of_Grindelwald" title="Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald">Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald</a></i>.</li>
<li>While the "British Envoy" portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Clothier" title="Richard Clothier">Richard Clothier</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(film)" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i> was speculated to be Fawley as Newt addresses him as Minister, Clothier's agency lists his role as <a href="/wiki/Hector_Podmore" title="Hector Podmore">Hector Podmore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Hector Fawley's inability to properly respond to the threat Grindelwald posed seems to mirror real-life British Prime Minister <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Neville Chamberlain">Neville Chamberlain</a>, who gave in to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> by signing the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Munich_Agreement" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Munich Agreement">Munich Agreement</a> and announced "<i>peace in our time</i>" in 1938, shortly before the outbreak of World War II. Regardless of Chamberlain's other achievements, he went down in history as a bumbler.</li>
<li>The Fawleys (Aubrey and Julia) are characters in <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Casual_Vacancy" title="The Casual Vacancy">The Casual Vacancy</a></i>. In that novel, they are a rich aristocratic family and the owners of Sweetlove House, an historic manor in the outskirts of Pagford, whose ancestors were responsible for selling the nearby city of Yarvil a piece of Pagford land — one of the catalysts for the plot.</li></ul>
