<b>Lady Wimborne</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1899" class="mw-redirect" title="1899">1899</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who was president of the <a href="/wiki/Witches%27_League" title="Witches&#39; League">Witches' League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lady_Wimborne?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lady_Wimborne?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This character is mentioned only on an award certificate seen in the "<a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Lockhart's Office</a>" feature on Disc 2 of the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i> DVD. The prop award certificate appears to be partially constructed from a membership certificate for a real historical organisation called the Ladies' League for the Defence of the Reformed Faith of the Church of England. This group was founded in 1899 by a Lady Wimborne and advocated against <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ritualism" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Ritualism">ritualism</a> in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_England" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Church of England">Anglican Church</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
