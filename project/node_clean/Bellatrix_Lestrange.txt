<p><b>Bellatrix Lestrange</b> (née <b>Black</b>) (<a href="/wiki/1951" title="1951">1951</a> – <a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark witch</a>, the eldest daughter of <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_III" title="Cygnus Black III">Cygnus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Druella_Black" title="Druella Black">Druella Black</a>, cousin of <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, and the elder sister of <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_Tonks" title="Andromeda Tonks">Andromeda Tonks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>. She was a member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>, an old wizarding family and one of the <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Twenty-Eight" title="Sacred Twenty-Eight">Sacred Twenty-Eight</a>. Bellatrix started her education at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the early sixties (either <a href="/wiki/1962" title="1962">1962</a> or <a href="/wiki/1963" title="1963">1963</a>), and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>.
</p><p>After graduating from Hogwarts, Bellatrix became a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>. She was fanatically loyal to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and was among the most dangerous and sadistic of his followers. All the Black sisters married pure-blood wizards, except for Andromeda who married <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> <a href="/wiki/Edward_Tonks" title="Edward Tonks">Ted Tonks</a> and was disowned by the family. After Voldemort's fall from power, Bellatrix, her husband <a href="/wiki/Rodolphus_Lestrange" title="Rodolphus Lestrange">Rodolphus Lestrange</a>, her brother-in-law <a href="/wiki/Rabastan_Lestrange" title="Rabastan Lestrange">Rabastan Lestrange</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> captured and <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Frank_and_Alice_Longbottom" title="Attack on Frank and Alice Longbottom">tortured</a> the <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a> <a href="/wiki/Frank_Longbottom" title="Frank Longbottom">Frank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alice_Longbottom" title="Alice Longbottom">Alice Longbottom</a>, seeking information about Voldemort's whereabouts which eventually drove the two Aurors insane. The four Death Eaters were caught and sentenced to life imprisonment in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. Voldemort eventually returned to full strength in 1995 and Bellatrix, Rodolphus, and Rabastan escaped from Azkaban in the <a href="/wiki/Azkaban_escapes#1996" title="Azkaban escapes">1996 mass break-out</a>, along with seven other Death Eaters.
</p><p>Bellatrix participated in several battles of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, making it a particular goal to murder any relatives who were members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>. She successfully murdered two, her cousin <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> and niece <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>. She also murdered the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a>'s former house-elf, <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a>, who had become a firm ally of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">final battle</a>, Bellatrix was the last Death Eater standing. She was eventually killed in a duel by <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a> after her attempted murder of <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>.
</p><p>Before her death, Bellatrix secretly gave birth to an illegitimate daughter named <a href="/wiki/Delphini" title="Delphini">Delphini</a>, whom she conceived with her beloved master, Lord Voldemort. Delphini was conceived sometime after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> in 1996 and before Bellatrix's death at the Battle of Hogwarts in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

However, he never returned her feelings as he was incapable of understanding <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> and disliked the idea of being emotionally attached to anyone.

Her fifteen years in a maximum security cell took a toll on her already unstable mentality, as well as hollowing out her appearance until only the faintest traces of her familial beauty remained.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<p>When Voldemort <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">returned to power</a> in June 1995, he said that Bellatrix, Rodolphus, and Rabastan Lestrange were faithful members of his inner circle and should be rewarded, as they didn't denounce him after they were captured and tried.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/January" title="January">January</a> <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, a mass breakout from Azkaban set ten long-imprisoned Death Eaters loose. Bellatrix was one of the ten who <a href="/wiki/Azkaban_escapes#1996" title="Azkaban escapes">escaped</a> in the mass breakout and rejoined Voldemort, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> whom <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> correctly predicted would side with Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The <i>Daily Prophet </i>(probably with encouragement from the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>) reported that the escaped criminal <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> must be responsible for the incident. These ten were considered to be some of the most dangerous and highly guarded convicts due to their crimes being brutal and legendary. As a result, Bellatrix and her allies became wanted witches and wizards, living in hiding to avoid being captured by the Ministry.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Later that very same year, Bellatrix, Rodolphus, and Rabastan were all members of a Death Eater task force led by Lucius Malfoy. They participated in the Battle of the Department of Mysteries, a plot to steal a prophecy regarding <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and the Dark Lord. She was enraged when Harry spoke Voldemort's given name and mentioned his true status as a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>. This enraged her so much that she actually tried to <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">stun</a> him before Lucius deflected her spell with a <a href="/wiki/Shield_Charm" title="Shield Charm">Shield Charm</a>, not wanting to inadvertently harm the prophecy they were sent to retrieve. She was very condescending when dealing with the six <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">D.A.</a> members. Bellatrix tried unsuccessfully to retrieve the prophecy from Harry Potter via a <a href="/wiki/Summoning_Charm" title="Summoning Charm">Summoning Charm</a>, as her efforts were stopped by Harry's quick use of his own Shield Charm. However, she was willing to do whatever it took to get the prophecy from Harry, including gleefully and sadistically threatening to torture fourteen year old <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> while he was forced to watch.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> members arrived, Bellatrix duelled and defeated her niece, <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>. She then began fighting her cousin <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> who proceeded to taunt her during the course of their duel but she then struck him with a <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange%27s_curse" class="mw-redirect" title="Bellatrix Lestrange&#39;s curse">curse</a> that sent him through the <a href="/wiki/Veil" title="Veil">veil</a> in the Death Chamber, killing him. After Sirius's death, Bellatrix defeated <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a> before deciding to flee. 
</p>

<p>Driven into a rage at the loss of his only living relative, Harry pursued her all the way to the Atrium in an attempt to avenge his godfather. After hideously mocking his relationship with Sirius, Harry attempted to use the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> on her but only knocked her over causing her a few seconds of pain. Dropping her jeering manner, Bellatrix disdainfully lectured him on his failure to execute the curse effectively (inadvertently teaching him how to do it properly in the future) before resolving to torture him herself with it in order to demonstrate the full potency of the curse. After unsuccessfully attempting to attack Harry, she discovered to her horror that the prophecy had been destroyed and proceeded to panic, repeatedly attempting to summon it in vain from Harry who laughed at her efforts and dismissed her pleadings for leniency from her master due to being unaware that the latter had just arrived at the Ministry.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Once Harry realised that Voldemort was indeed present, Bellatrix pleaded with her master not to be punished and tried to warn him that Dumbledore was nearby, but he ignored her and concentrated on attempting to kill Harry. As the Killing Curse blasted from the end of his wand towards Harry, Dumbledore arrived and <a href="/wiki/Piertotum_Locomotor" title="Piertotum Locomotor">enchanted</a> the statues of the Fountain of Magical Brethren to protect Harry, act as reinforcements, and to ensure that Bellatrix couldn't intervene as she was then pinned to the ground by the newly sentient witch statue. However, she was the sole Death Eater to escape being captured by effectively <a href="/wiki/Shield_Charm" title="Shield Charm">blocking</a> the spell that he had attempted to cast upon her.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before duelling Voldemort, Dumbledore summoned <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> and several Aurors to the Ministry using the statues as messengers via the fireplaces along the Atrium wall. After a failed attempt at possessing Harry, Voldemort grabbed Bellatrix before Apparating away but not before being seen by Fudge and the Aurors. With the rest of the Death Eaters immobilised and caught by the Ministry only moments later, Bellatrix was the only Death Eater not to be apprehended due to Voldemort intervening on her behalf, a testament to his acknowledgement of her loyalty to him.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In July of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Bellatrix reluctantly accompanied her younger sister, <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>, to the house of <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> at <a href="/wiki/Spinner%27s_End" title="Spinner&#39;s End">Spinner's End</a>. Once there, Bellatrix interrogated Snape and voiced her distrust of him. Snape's calm, reasonable, and logical answers to her aggressive questioning rendered her dissatisfied, but temporarily without retort. However, Narcissa proceeded to beg Snape to look after her son <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a>'s welfare while he attended Hogwarts and tried to fulfil Voldemort's orders to kill <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a>. She showed her obsessive loyalty to her master's cause in telling her sister that she should be proud, instead of being worried, that her son was not "<i>shrinking away from his duty</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

<p>By the next year, Bellatrix was living with Narcissa at <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>. She was the only resident of the manor who was actually pleased that Voldemort was staying there, telling him that there was "<i>no higher pleasure</i>". Despite her unwavering loyalty, her master humiliated her and the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoys</a> by mentioning the marriage of <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, the daughter of their sister, to the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>. However, Bellatrix insisted that she and Narcissa didn't consider Nymphadora to be their niece, as they had disowned her mother for marrying a Muggle-born. Voldemort eventually stopped the teasing and advised Bellatrix to continue to "prune" her "family tree", which she eagerly agreed to do as soon as possible. Despite her proclaimed loyalty towards her master, even Bellatrix hesitated when Voldemort said that he would need to borrow a wand from them in order to circumvent the problem of his own wand refusing to kill Harry, looking much like her colleagues "<i>as though he had asked to borrow one of their arms</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the Death Eaters subsequently <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">attacked</a> the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> while they were moving <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> from <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a>, Bellatrix and her husband pursued a disguised <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> and Tonks, whom Bellatrix tried very hard to kill. However, Rodolphus was injured in the pursuit and Tonks escaped. Soon afterwards, a copy of <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor%27s_Sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Godric Gryffindor&#39;s Sword">Godric Gryffindor's Sword</a> was secured in Bellatrix's <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Vault" title="Lestrange Vault">family vault</a> at <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank" title="Gringotts Wizarding Bank">Gringotts Wizarding Bank</a> with Bellatrix and the Death Eaters unaware that it was merely a copy. The goblins who ran the bank noticed at once that it was not the genuine article, but neglected to mention it to Bellatrix owing to their disdain at the new wizard control of their bank.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

When she spotted Godric Gryffindor's Sword amongst the trio's captured possessions, however, she at once flew into a panic because the sword (as far as she knew) was hidden in her vault. This led Harry to deduce that there could be something of great importance in her vault which ended up being one of Voldemort's Horcruxes: <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Helga Hufflepuff's Cup</a>. He then informed her that the sword had been found in the Trio's tent. She then took charge of the situation by resorting to torturing Hermione into revealing how the sword was in the Trio's possession and demanded that the rest of the prisoners be taken into the cellar temporarily, while ordering the Stunned Snatchers to be placed in the courtyard to be disposed of by her personally at a later time.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="Legacy_(1998-)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy_.281998-.29">Legacy <span class="template-c">(1998-)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy (1998-)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Legacy (1998-)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although it is unknown what happened to her body following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, it is possible that if Bellatrix was not cremated or buried in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, she might have been sent to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> to be buried and laid to rest alongside the countless other members of the Lestrange family in the family's <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Mausoleum" title="Lestrange Mausoleum">mausoleum</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Cimeti%C3%A8re_du_P%C3%A8re-Lachaise" title="Cimetière du Père-Lachaise">Père Lachaise Cemetery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. 
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a> which occurred in <a href="/wiki/2010" title="2010">2010s</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding world</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">Portrait</a> of Bellatrix escaped <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a> as one of the many <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundables</a>, for unknown and mysterious reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Bellatrix had the classic <i>"great good looks"</i> and arrogant bearing common for members of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>: She was a tall woman of white skin; and long, thick, shiny dark hair. Her face had a strong jaw, thin lips, and heavily-lidded eyes with long eyelashes. As a Death Eater, Bellatrix bore the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a> on her inner left forearm. However, her lengthy imprisonment in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> took a toll on her appearance - her face became gaunt and skull-like, but she still managed to retain some vestige of her great beauty.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>There was a remarkable resemblance between Bellatrix and her sisters - at first glance, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> mistook <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_Tonks" title="Andromeda Tonks">Andromeda Tonks</a> for Bellatrix, being was only upon closer observation did he notice the differences (Andromeda had lighter brown hair, a kinder face, and wider eyes). Besides this, <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a> was as fair as Bellatrix was dark.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>Let him watch while we torture the little girl. I’ll do it.</i>"</span></dd>

<p>Bellatrix was an intensely sadistic witch with brutal, violent tendencies.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She possessed a fierce and dangerous temper, a trait that she shared with her cousin <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius</a> and her aunt <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black" title="Walburga Black">Walburga</a> and which was likely a result of the inbreeding that plagued her birth family. Bellatrix was one of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s cruellest <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, always eager to inflict the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> on enemies. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> noted that she was as "<i>mad as her master</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Likely for these reasons, Bellatrix was slavishly devoted to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and was willing to do anything for his cause. She believed to have proven her loyalty by her time in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>, an event which had seemingly furthered her mental decline.
</p>

<p>Overall, Bellatrix was obsessed with blood purity and took immense pride in the aristocracy of her family since a young age. Like all the rest of Lord Voldemort's followers, she was vehemently against all <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-bloods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Half-breeds" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-breeds">half-breeds</a>. She had a particular disdain for <a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitors</a>, not being above killing members of her own family who displeased her, including her cousin Sirius and her younger sister's daughter <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora</a>, whom she sought to kill with great determination. Given her reaction to Harry's words that Voldemort is a half-blood, it is likely that she didn't know that her master wasn't a pure-blood or at least was in denial. 
</p>

<p>Bellatrix was intelligent and a natural leader, taking charge in tricky situations. These traits were shown during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_at_Malfoy_Manor" title="Skirmish at Malfoy Manor">Skirmish at Malfoy Manor</a>. The latter event was a mark of her decisiveness and ruthlessness, as she took no unnecessary prisoners (preferring to kill them instead), as she planned to do with a group of <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatchers</a> who attempted to defy her.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She was the only known Death Eater to have never trusted <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> in spite of her master doing so. However, Bellatrix could be easily distracted and reckless, especially in battle with opponents that appeared to be (or were) weaker than herself. 
</p><p>Bellatrix was also egotistical and arrogant, described by Harry as "<i>treat[ing] everyone like scum"</i> in a rather taunting way. She had immense confidence in her skills and never hesitated to jump into battle against any auror or Order of the Phoenix member. Furthermore, she was immensely proud of her status as the most loyal and devoted follower of Lord Voldemort. However, Bellatrix chose to flee when <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> arrived, suggesting that, like her master, she held a degree of fear and caution for the powerful wizard.
</p><p>Despite her cruel personality, Bellatrix was not incapable of caring for a few people. She was obsessively in <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> with her master, Lord Voldemort. She had a close relationship with her younger sister <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>, grudgingly going with her to Snape's house to ask his help and didn't tell her master of her sister's deception. She also showed affection for her nephew and student <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>, but didn't seem to be too concerned about his safety as she would "<i>be glad to give [her own sons] up to the service of the Dark Lord</i>".
</p>

<p>Bellatrix Lestrange was an extremely powerful and dangerous <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, trained by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> himself. She was a mistress of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>, an incredibly skilful <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellist</a>, and an experienced <a href="/wiki/Occlumency" title="Occlumency">Occlumens</a>. Her unbalanced mental state and emotional instability seemed to feed her power and her prodigious <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> skill was proven by her many victories over numerous talented wizards and witches, including experienced <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a>. 
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Bellatrix claimed to have been trained in the Dark Arts by Voldemort himself, boasting to Harry Potter that she had knowledge of "spells of such power that you, pathetic little boy, can never hope to compete!"&#160;;<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> a claim supported by her status as Voldemort's "best lieutenant". Bellatrix displayed particular proficiency with the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a>, which was her signature spell. She preferred to torture her victims rather than killing them straight away, doing so with such skill her victims would pass out or would lose their sanity after prolonged exposure to the curse. Following her first duel against Nymphadora Tonks, the latter had to spend some time at <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mungo&#39;s">St Mungo's</a>, suggesting that her aunt cursed her in such a way that couldn't be cured easily.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Like her master, Bellatrix was capable of casting the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> with no regard for the life she wished to extinguish. She effectively killed a <a href="/wiki/Fox_near_Spinner%27s_End" title="Fox near Spinner&#39;s End">fox</a> on her way to Spinner's End in 1996 with this curse,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and nearly killed Ginny Weasley with it in the Battle of Hogwarts.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: Bellatrix Lestrange was an incredibly accomplished and lethal duellist, noted for her "prodigious skill".<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She presumably took part in many battles during the First Wizarding War and duelled against A<a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">urors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> members alike. She was able to capture the notoriously powerful <a href="/wiki/Frank_Longbottom" title="Frank Longbottom">Frank</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alice_Longbottom" title="Alice Longbottom">Alice Longbottom</a>, though with help from <a href="/wiki/Rodolphus_Lestrange" title="Rodolphus Lestrange">three</a> <a href="/wiki/Rabastan_Lestrange" title="Rabastan Lestrange">other</a> <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Death Eaters</a>. She defeated and seriously injured <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a> in <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duels</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> and killed her cousin Sirius Black. She was the only Death Eater to escape from <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> himself, though she was later dispatched by the headmaster's animated statues in the latter's duel against her master.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Despite her years of captivity within <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>, Bellatrix displayed incredible reflexes that outmatched the much younger and physically-fit <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, repelling and redirecting his Stunning Spell back towards him with a swiftly cast shield charm. She later participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of Seven Potters</a>, where she viciously hunted down Tonks and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, though they were able to hold her off and escape albeit with great difficulty. She quickly and single-handedly defeated four <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatchers</a> at Malfoy Manor who were all described to be "no match for her", including the notoriously savage werewolf <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Bellatrix survived the first part of the battle with no injuries and finally succeeded in murdering Tonks. She also duelled <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> all at once, being able to stalemate their combined efforts. She seemed to be getting the upper hand when she attempted to murder Ginny, given her Killing Curse missed Ginny by "an inch". Her death at the hands of <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a> was due to her vastly underestimating the witch, who was fuelled by grief over the murder of her <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">son</a>, and was nonetheless a skilled witch in her own right.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She was the last <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> standing apart from Lord Voldemort, despite wielding a wand which she had most likely not won the allegiance of, which is further evidence of her extraordinary duelling skills.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Non-verbal magic</a></b>: Bellatrix was shown to have been highly adept at casting non-verbal <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Dark_charm" title="Dark charm">dark charms</a>. She fired several destructive <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a> at Harry Potter without speaking their <a href="/wiki/Incantation" title="Incantation">incantations</a> aloud in the Atrium at the Ministry of Magic. During her duel with <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a>, the intensity was such that the floor around them became "hot and cracked",<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> all without uttering a single incantation.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Bellatrix displayed a high amount of proficiency in charms, given how she was able to cast the <a href="/wiki/Summoning_Charm" title="Summoning Charm">Summoning Charm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">Stunning Spell</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Shield_Charm" title="Shield Charm">Shield Charm</a> effortlessly and successfully. She was able to block Harry's stunning spell using a Shield Charm with lightning speed, and furthermore using it to block an attack from <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> (an incredibly powerful wizard) with great success, showing her considerable talent in this field.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Occlumency" title="Occlumency">Occlumency</a></b>: Bellatrix was an experienced Occulmens,<sup id="cite_ref-PM_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and she trained her nephew <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> in Occlumency as part of his mission to kill <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>. This training was effective enough that Draco was able to block out <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> on at least one occasion. It is also likely that Bellatrix could perform <a href="/wiki/Legilimency" title="Legilimency">Legilimency</a> as well, as she may have penetrated Draco's mind in order to train him to block his thoughts.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a></b>: Bellatrix was proficient in Apparition, although her sister <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa</a> may be even more skilled than her. This was shown when they both apparated into <a href="/wiki/Spinner%27s_End" title="Spinner&#39;s End">Spinner's End</a>, with Bellatrix's pop being somewhat louder and Narcissa making only a slight "popping" sound. However, Bellatrix may simply have been too desperate in chasing her sister to do it more quietly.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: Bellatrix was a skilful flyer, as she participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a> via <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">Broomstick</a>. She fiercely pursued and fiercely duelled with her niece Nymphadora Tonks, who was also proficient in flying. Her ability to duel whilst flying shows her very impressive talent in this field.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Teaching skills</b>: Bellatrix taught Draco Occlumency and (non-verbal spells, the <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">Unforgivable Curses</a> and other branches of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>) in furtherance of his objective to kill Dumbledore.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She also unwittingly taught Harry how to properly use the Unforgivable Curses, given how he acknowledged that his success on the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> was actually from her taunting.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Indomitable willpower</b>: Bellatrix had a very powerful will, remaining adamant in her loyalty to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> even when facing a life sentence in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. She endured the prison for approximately fourteen years without losing her will to live, in spite of the fact that many prisoners there would have preferred death instead of suffering the depressive states they had been reduced to. She maintained her formidable magical powers and her keen intelligence, although her emotional temperament seemed to have become more unstable due to her years in captivity.</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows"><b>Lord Voldemort</b>: "<i>I'm talking about your niece, Bellatrix. And yours, Lucius and Narcissa. She just married the werewolf, <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>. You must be so proud.</i>"</span></dd>

</div><p>Bellatrix took the <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> values of her family very seriously, as indicated by her marriage into a known pure-blood wizarding family and her zealous belief in <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> ideology. She had no feelings of affection for those in her family deemed <a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitors</a> by disgracing and dishonouring her family by not fanatically honouring the prejudiced values she did, and readily attacked and killed them on many occasions.

<p>Bellatrix seemed to have had a fairly close and affectionate relationship with her younger sister Narcissa, whom she called "Cissy." Despite distrusting <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> and attempting to dissuade her sister from approaching him, Bellatrix accompanied Narcissa to <a href="/wiki/Spinner%27s_End" title="Spinner&#39;s End">Spinner's End</a> to request Snape's help in seeing <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> through the task assigned to him by Lord Voldemort in 1996. After Snape agreed to make the <a href="/wiki/Unbreakable_Vow" title="Unbreakable Vow">Unbreakable Vow</a>, Bellatrix acted as the bonding agent, ensuring that Snape would fulfil his promises to Narcissa.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Narcissa and Bellatrix occasionally clashed over the former's protectiveness towards her son; She argued that Narcissa should be proud that her son was a Death Eater, while Narcissa consistently demonstrated that the safety of her son and husband were her first priorities. At some point, Bellatrix trained Draco in <a href="/wiki/Occlumency" title="Occlumency">Occlumency</a> to aid him in his mission.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Delphini">Delphini</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=21" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Delphini">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Delphini"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Bellatrix had an illegitimate daughter with Lord Voldemort, named <a href="/wiki/Delphini" title="Delphini">Delphini</a>, who was born in secret at <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a> in the late 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-p2-a4-s11_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p2-a4-s11-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Bellatrix didn't live long enough to watch her daughter grow up; this left the infant Delphi an orphan as her father had been. <a href="/wiki/Euphemia_Rowle" title="Euphemia Rowle">Euphemia Rowle</a> took Delphi in and raised her, allegedly only because she was offered a substantial amount of <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-p2-a3-s16_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p2-a3-s16-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Malfoy_family">Malfoy family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=22" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Malfoy family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Malfoy family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Bellatrix's relationship with Draco seemed to have some affection; though she seemed to disapprove of his inability to kill and his dislike for torture. She taught him <a href="/wiki/Occlumency" title="Occlumency">Occlumency</a>, non-verbal spells, and several branches of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>, making her already talented nephew a powerful wizard. His blood relationship with her was probably the only reason he was remained alive; due to his father's failure and Snape taking over his 1996-1997 task.
</p>

<p>Her brother-in-law Lucius was antagonistic with Bellatrix due to usurping his place within the Death Eater ranks despite the fact she had been imprisoned in Azkaban for fourteen years, unlike Lucius. Their differences in loyalty in the Dark Lord fuelled their antagonism for each other even more. When Bellatrix wanted to kill Harry Potter during the Battle of the Department of Mysteries, Lucius briefly restrained her. After the battle, she told Narcissa that it was Lucius' fault that she couldn't give Voldemort the Prophecy. However, the two fought together during the skirmish at Malfoy Manor. Seeing Lucius's extreme failure and the confiscation of his wand, Bellatrix treated him with disdain and contempt, showing him little to no respect during their argument of summoning Voldemort for capturing Harry.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rodolphus_Lestrange">Rodolphus Lestrange</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=23" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Rodolphus Lestrange">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Rodolphus Lestrange"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Bellatrix attended Hogwarts with <a href="/wiki/Rodolphus_Lestrange" title="Rodolphus Lestrange">Rodolphus Lestrange</a> and were part of a <a href="/wiki/Gang_of_Slytherins" title="Gang of Slytherins">gang of other Slytherin students</a> which also included <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, all of whom became <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She later married him in order to make a "<i>respectable <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> marriage</i>",<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> as it was expected of her.<sup id="cite_ref-leakychat_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leakychat-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> There is no indication that their relationship went beyond this. She never expressed any love or concern for him. No children appear beneath their names on the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black family tapestry</a>. It could be surmised that she made no attempts to produce children with her official husband, as she adored her master only and concentrated on pleasing only him.
</p><p>However, Bellatrix and Rodolphus did seem to work effectively together as Death Eaters, suggesting their partnership is strictly professional. Both were some of the most loyal and valued Death Eaters, so it is likely that they would've felt a connection from their mutual devotion to their master. Rodolphus <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">tortured</a> <a href="/wiki/Alice_Longbottom" title="Alice Longbottom">Alice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Longbottom" title="Frank Longbottom">Frank Longbottom</a> alongside his wife, <a href="/wiki/Rabastan_Lestrange" title="Rabastan Lestrange">brother</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He fought with Bellatrix during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a><sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> mentioned cursing him and being pursued by Bellatrix.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

This was likely for a combination of reasons, including Tonks' being a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>, and member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, the fact that she had married the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>, and Voldemort's comments to her about "pruning her family tree". <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulus_Black">Regulus Black</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=26" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Regulus Black">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Regulus Black"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>It is possible that Bellatrix was on good terms with her other cousin, <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus</a>, who was also a Death Eater during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. Regulus initially espoused the pure-blood supremacist line associated with his family, and like Bellatrix, was sorted into the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> house during his time at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. It is unlikely that he revealed his change of heart to Bellatrix, or anyone else, prior to his death in 1979.
</p>

Unlike many of the other <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, Bellatrix never attempted to deny her loyalties when her master fell in 1981. Instead, she proudly claimed that she was his most loyal and trusted servant, and stated that he would rise again. She seemed to view <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> prison as a mere place to wait for his return.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bellatrix was described as being slavishly devoted to her master, regarding him with "worshipful fascination" and speaking to him in a romantic way.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On at least one occasion, during the 1997 meeting of Death Eaters at <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>, her cheeks flushed and her eyes welled up with tears when she thought he was praising her. Satisfying him was of the utmost importance to her. She was perfectly willing to kill her niece, <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, as a way of "pruning" her "family tree", though she would most likely have done it without his orders, anyway.
</p><p>Bellatrix was outraged by anyone showing her master even the slightest disrespect. When Harry called him by his name in 1996, she became enraged, feeling that he was unworthy of speaking the name. She was also furious when Harry spoke of Voldemort's <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> status and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle_Senior" title="Tom Riddle Senior">Muggle father</a>. Despite agreeing with her master's ideology of blood purity, she apparently didn't care that he himself was a half-blood, if she believed this at all. Voldemort might have falsely told his subordinates that he was a pure-blood, something which Harry Potter suspected. If so, she believed her master's lie over the truth from the enemy.
</p><p>Voldemort respected her talents and skills and considered her of greater importance than most of his other servants. At the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a>, he grabbed her and <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparated</a> away with her. She was the sole Death Eater whom he bothered to help, though this may have been done out of convenience, as she was much closer in proximity than the other captured Death Eaters. However, Voldemort didn't reciprocate her romantic feelings, as he didn't know <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a>. Due to his egotistical and self-centred nature, it is possible that he was at least pleased with her desire for him. Their sexual relationship and the honour of bearing of his child could have also been a reward to Bellatrix for her loyalty to him, as he believed that he had a found a way to cheat death and didn't need to leave an heir behind. When she was killed by <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">final battle</a>, Voldemort's fury was said to "<i>explode like a bomb</i>". Her death was the only one that he attempted to avenge before <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> intervened. It is unlikely, though, that he truly cared about her as a person, rather than being angry about losing someone who was very useful to him.
</p>

<p>Bellatrix was acquainted with Severus Snape, a fellow Death Eater prior to the end of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, changing sides shortly before <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a> downfall, and becoming a double agent for <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>. While their relationship prior to Snape's defection is unknown, Bellatrix was highly suspicious of him due to Severus's alliance with Dumbledore and his spotty record of fervent loyalty to the Dark Lord. Although she was genuine in her mistrust of Snape, she was possibly also envious of the position of trust and respect Snape enjoyed under Voldemort. It is also possible that Snape's <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> status is a point of derision on Bellatrix's part.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Bellatrix followed <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa</a> to Snape's house, trying to dissuade her from seeking his help in guiding and guarding <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a> in the task of killing Albus Dumbledore. She was taken aback by Snape's conformance in making the <a href="/wiki/Unbreakable_Vow" title="Unbreakable Vow">Unbreakable Vow</a>, for which she acted as a bond-maker. Both she and Snape fought and died at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>. However, Bellatrix's death didn't allow her to discover that her initial mistrust of Snape had been valid after all.
</p>

<p>Bellatrix wasn't likely to have viewed many of the other <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> as her equals, as she believed both her skill, devotion, and family status exceed that of others. While it is unclear exactly how much genuine respect the other Death Eaters had for Bellatrix, it was clear they were familiar with her view of herself, as <a href="/wiki/Travers" title="Travers">Travers</a> became less suspicious of <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, disguised as Bellatrix via <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, only after Hermione took on a much haughtier personality. Undoubtedly, she considered Voldemort's most loyal Death Eaters to be those who, like her, were imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> at some point: namely, <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rodolphus_Lestrange" title="Rodolphus Lestrange">her husband</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rabastan_Lestrange" title="Rabastan Lestrange">his brother</a>.
</p><p>While in school Bellatrix was part of a gang that included her husband, Severus Snape, <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wilkes" title="Wilkes">Wilkes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avery_(disambiguation)" title="Avery (disambiguation)">Avery</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mulciber_(disambiguation)" title="Mulciber (disambiguation)">Mulciber</a>. It's unknown how well she got along with these individuals, both as students and Death Eaters. Rosier was likely related to her on her mother's side. She and Mulciber both were sent to Azkaban at the end of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> and both fought in the Battle of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> seemed pleased to see her as well as Narcissa when they visit him and Snape, though Bellatrix didn't acknowledge him. It's possible that she appreciates Peter for his role in bringing back their master or else dislikes him for not attempting to help him sooner.
</p><p>Bellatrix had a disdainful attitude towards the <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatchers</a>. Despite their role in capturing Harry, she treated them like lowly servants and was quick to attack them the moment one of them disobeyed her. <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> showed fear and resentment towards her, though Bellatrix did acknowledge his role in capturing Harry and agreed that he did deserve to be rewarded.
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a> was the last <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>, who shared his family's <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacist</a> values. Like <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black" title="Walburga Black">Walburga Black</a>, he was disappointed with Sirius for not sharing those same values, thus favouring the latter's cousins, Bellatrix and Narcissa. He kept pictures of Bellatrix and some other Black family members in his sleeping area at <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>. Bellatrix's picture was his favourite, as he had attempted to repair it with <a href="/wiki/Spellotape" title="Spellotape">Spellotape</a> and placed it in front of the others. In December of <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%931996_school_year" title="1995–1996 school year">1995</a>, when Sirius yelled at Kreacher to "get out" of a room, Kreacher interpreted the words as a command to leave his master's service and exit the house altogether. Because Bellatrix was currently in Azkaban and Andromeda had been disowned, Narcissa was the next family member whom he could serve. He went to <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">her home</a> and gave her information about Harry's godson relationship with Sirius.
</p><p>After Bellatrix escaped from Azkaban, he helped her by injuring <a href="/wiki/Buckbeak" title="Buckbeak">Buckbeak</a>, Sirius's <a href="/wiki/Hippogriff" title="Hippogriff">hippogriff</a>. He even lied to Harry about Sirius being gone from the house one day, when he had actually only gone upstairs to tend to Buckbeak's injury. Harry went to the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> to "save" Sirius, who in turn went there to save Harry. After she killed Sirius in a duel,<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Kreacher hoped to officially move into her possession, but Sirius's will transferred ownership to Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_15-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Kreacher eventually gave his loyalty to Harry in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, fighting against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, Bellatrix included, during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Bellatrix Lestrange saw Harry Potter as her master's most hated enemy and despised him for the inexplicable threat he posed to the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Dark Lord</a>, wanting him dead very badly. She thought of him as someone lesser than herself due to his status as a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> and for having less experience than her. Harry had hated Bellatrix ever since he first saw her in the <a href="/wiki/Pensieve" title="Pensieve">Pensieve</a> due to learning that she was responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Frank_and_Alice_Longbottom" title="Attack on Frank and Alice Longbottom">attack</a> on <a href="/wiki/Alice_Longbottom" title="Alice Longbottom">Alice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Longbottom" title="Frank Longbottom">Frank Longbottom</a>. He seemed to have an odd fascination with her caused by possibly fear, being immediately drawn to her picture when the news of the ten escaped Death Eaters appeared in the Daily Prophet and experienced "a little jolt in his stomach" after seeing her picture in Kreacher's bedroom.
</p><p>After she killed his godfather in the Battle Of The Department Of Mysteries, Harry aggressively pursued Bellatrix after she fled from Dumbledore and attempted to cast the Cruciartus Curse on her and taunted her for her failed efforts in achieving the prophecy for Voldemort. At the onset of the <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_at_Malfoy_Manor" title="Skirmish at Malfoy Manor">Skirmish at Malfoy Manor</a>, the mere sound of her voice when she appeared in the fray was enough to make his fear reach a new high. Bellatrix killed his close friends, <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> the house-elf and <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>. When she attempted to murder <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> (the girl he loves) during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Harry was diverted from his goal of destroying Voldemort in the hopes of attacking her; but he was stopped only by the sudden intervention of <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a>.
</p>

<p>After the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Dark Lord</a> in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, Bellatrix along with three her husband Rodolphus, the latter's brother and Bartemius Crouch Junior set out to attack members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, hoping to interrogate them for information on Voldemort's whereabouts. She personally attacked Alice and Frank Longbottom, torturing them by means of the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> in her attempt at forcing them to yield the information she wanted. The event rendered the <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a> clinically and permanently insane, therefore condemning them to <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a> for the remainder of their lives.
</p>

He was left with parents incapable of even recognising him let alone caring for him, and so he was raised by his <a href="/wiki/Augusta_Longbottom" title="Augusta Longbottom">grandmother</a>. Bellatrix seemed to find the fact that she had caused Neville's family tragedy highly amusing. Upon realising who Neville was, she proceeded to mock his loss, extremely angering him in the process. Ever since he was old enough to understand why his parents needed to be hospitalised, Neville had despised Bellatrix Lestrange, but given his benign nature, he never sought vengeance against her. Instead, he honoured his parents by showing more bravery than anyone could have expected of him, eventually retaliating when Bellatrix taunted him, rebelling against the tyranny of the <a href="/wiki/Carrow_family" title="Carrow family">Carrows</a> during his seventh year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, and subsequently fighting during the <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> against Voldemort's forces.

<p>Albus Dumbledore knew Bellatrix to be a highly dangerous Death Eater since the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, having witnessed her trial and imprisonment after she <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">tortured</a> <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a> to insanity and beyond. Dumbledore seemed to understand her very well; he admitted to <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> that he would prefer to not fall prey to Bellatrix Lestrange in his weakened state (caused by Voldemort's cursed <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a> ring), as she liked to "<i>play with her food before she eats it</i>"; in other words, inflict unbelievable pain before finally delivering the killing blow.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_12-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Though Bellatrix was fanatically loyal to Voldemort and believed that he was greater and more powerful than Dumbledore, she would not brave Dumbledore in a duel, as she recognised him as a far more superior wizard than she was a witch. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a>, Bellatrix opted to flee rather than engage Dumbledore in battle, being the only Death Eater in the <a href="/wiki/Death_Chamber" title="Death Chamber">Death Chamber</a> who succeeded in escaping from him.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_13-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is possible that Dumbledore knew Bellatrix while she was a student, either being her headmaster or teacher. It's unknown what kind of relationship, if any, the two had at the time.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?veaction=edit&amp;section=37" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange?action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like many members of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>, Bellatrix's name is derived from that of a star. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bellatrix" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Bellatrix">Bellatrix</a> is the third brightest star in the constellation Orion. It is also known as the Amazon Star, which is one of Orion's seven brightest stars. In fact, it's the twenty-seventh brightest star in the night sky. The Amazons were warrior women in Greek mythology, which is presumably an allusion to Bellatrix being among the most dangerously skilled <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. Her name likely has Latin, French and English origins: <i>Bella</i> is Latin both for the plural of <i>bellum</i>, meaning war, and the feminine singular adjective meaning "beautiful;" <i>trix</i> is Latin feminine suffix (i.e. Bellatrix is the Latin word for a female warrior or a personification of feminine beauty). 'Bellatrix' also translates directly to 'war-like'.
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Lestrange_family#Etymology" title="Lestrange family">Lestrange</a></i> is likely a play on <i>l'étrange</i>, which is French meaning "the strange one". Her name thus alludes to her own mental instability, although technically <i>Lestrange </i>came from Bellatrix's husband Rodolphus.
</p>

<ul><li>Originally, <a href="/wiki/Helen_McCrory" title="Helen McCrory">Helen McCrory</a> was going to play the role of Bellatrix in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, but due to her pregnancy, she opted out of the film and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Helena_Bonham_Carter" title="Helena Bonham Carter">Helena Bonham Carter</a>.
<ul><li>Ironically, Bonham Carter herself learned she was pregnant shortly after completing filming. McCrory was eventually cast as Bellatrix's sister <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>. In the film, Bonham Carter is noticeably wearing looser clothing, as opposed to her form-fitting costume of the previous film. With Bonham Carter's casting, she is roughly the same age as Bellatrix during the books (early-mid 40s), but she is younger than all of the Marauders, whom she pre-dates in her Hogwarts education.</li>
<li>On the tapestry depicted in the film, the face of Bellatrix pictured is a face of Helena Bonham Carter. On the DVD there is a different picture of Bellatrix on the tapestry, where she has a purple hat and looks stylistically similar to her sisters.</li></ul></li>
<li>In an interview, <a href="/wiki/Helena_Bonham_Carter" title="Helena Bonham Carter">Helena Bonham Carter</a> mentions that the scene where she holds <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville</a> with her wand to his throat contains an injury. She ad-libbed pushing her wand into his ear canal, thinking it was good torture. Unfortunately Matthew Lewis moved his head towards her and ruptured his ear drum. After fighting <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>, Bellatrix's corpse separates in three pieces (her head, her torso, and her legs) and later explodes, killing her in the process.</li>
<li>Helena Botham Carter, Bellatrix's actress, previously worked with Ralph Fiennes, Voldemort's actor, in <i>Wallace &amp; Gromit: The Curse of the Were-Rabbit</i>, portraying Lady Campanula Tottington and Lord Victor Quartermaine, respectively. Ironically, given Bellatrix and Voldemort's relationship, Tottington had the exact opposite feelings to Quartermaine.</li></ul>
