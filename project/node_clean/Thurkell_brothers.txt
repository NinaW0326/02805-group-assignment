The <b>Thurkell brothers</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th century</a>) were the seven sons of <a href="/wiki/Thaddeus_Thurkell" title="Thaddeus Thurkell">Thaddeus Thurkell</a>. They all turned out to be <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squibs</a>. This made their <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> father so angry that he <a href="/wiki/Human_to_hedgehog_spell" title="Human to hedgehog spell">transfigured</a> them into <a href="/wiki/Hedgehog" title="Hedgehog">hedgehogs</a> out of disgust.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-POAG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is rare for a single Squib to be born into a <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding</a> family. The fact that all seven of the Thurkell brothers were non-magical seems to indicate that there was something out of the ordinary about their case.
</p>
