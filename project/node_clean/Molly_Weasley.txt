<p><b>Molly Weasley</b> (née <b>Prewett</b>) (b. <a href="/wiki/30_October" title="30 October">30 October</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BD_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BD-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/1949" title="1949">1949</a> or <a href="/wiki/1950" title="1950">1950</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-YOB_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YOB-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a><sup id="cite_ref-blood_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blood-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and matriarch of the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a> after marrying <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>. She was born into the <a href="/wiki/Prewett_family" title="Prewett family">Prewett family</a> and was sister to <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Prewett" title="Fabian Prewett">Fabian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gideon_Prewett" title="Gideon Prewett">Gideon Prewett</a>, members of the original <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>. 
</p><p>Molly attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and was Sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a>. Sometime after she graduated, both her brothers were murdered by Death Eaters in the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. Molly and Arthur ended up having seven children, consisting of six sons named <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charlie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Fred, George</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> and only one daughter named <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>. She also played a large part in the life of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, acting as a motherly figure towards him. 
</p><p>Molly was a member of the revived Order of the Phoenix following the return of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">final battle</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. Her actions during this battle caused her to be known as the witch that defeated and killed the notorious <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>.
</p><p>Molly survived the final battle to see her family expand even further, with twelve grandchildren by <a href="/wiki/2017" title="2017">2017</a>. Percy and his wife named their <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley_II" title="Molly Weasley II">eldest daughter</a> in honour of her.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire"><b>Molly</b>: "<i><a href="/wiki/Fat_Lady" title="Fat Lady">She</a> gave me such a telling-off one night when I got back to the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">dormitory</a> at four in the morning —</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Bill Weasley</b>: "<i>What were you doing out of your dormitory at four in the morning?</i>"</dd>
<dd><b>Molly</b>: "<i>Your father and I had been for a night-time stroll.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Molly discussing her time at Hogwarts<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p>Molly was born on <a href="/wiki/30_October" title="30 October">30 October</a><sup id="cite_ref-BD_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BD-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/1949" title="1949">1949</a> or <a href="/wiki/1950" title="1950">1950</a><sup id="cite_ref-YOB_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YOB-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_father" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s father">Mr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_mother" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s mother">Mrs Prewett</a>. She had two brothers: Gideon and Fabian. Molly began attending <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the early 1960s and was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a>. She once said that in her youth she brewed a <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA5_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA5-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was Hogwarts where Molly met <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>, whom she began dating. One night, they went out for a night-time stroll and whilst Arthur got caught by <a href="/wiki/Apollyon_Pringle" title="Apollyon Pringle">Apollyon Pringle</a>, she managed to get back to Gryffindor Tower at four in the morning and was told off by the <a href="/wiki/Fat_Lady" title="Fat Lady">Fat Lady</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Wizarding_War">First Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">

It was the same last time he was powerful, people eloping left, right, and centre —</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Ginny Weasley</b>: "<i>Including you and Dad.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Molly reflecting on her marriage in 1996<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>

<p>Molly graduated from Hogwarts in the late 1960s. She and Arthur wed soon after.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The family lived at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a> on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Ottery_St_Catchpole" title="Ottery St Catchpole">Ottery St Catchpole</a> in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>. Molly and Arthur's first child, <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">William</a>, was born <a href="/wiki/29_November" title="29 November">29 November</a>, <a href="/wiki/1970" title="1970">1970</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BD_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BD-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> More children followed: <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charlie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy</a>, and twins <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George</a>. After having her first five sons, she longed to have a daughter but had another son, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald</a>, before finally having a daughter, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginevra</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, Molly wasn't mentioned as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, something that was confirmed by <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>. This is likely because she was having and taking care of many young children at the time. However, her brothers were members of the Order, but were murdered during the war by five <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> in 1981, including <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a>, who was subsequently imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> for the crime. Given the way she speaks about Gideon and Fabian, Molly was distraught at their deaths and may have inherited their things, as was seen by her keeping <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Prewett%27s_watch" title="Fabian Prewett&#39;s watch">Fabian's watch</a> for several years.
</p><p>In 1987, Fred burnt a hole in Ron's tongue by feeding him an <a href="/wiki/Acid_Pops" title="Acid Pops">Acid Pop</a>. This made Molly outraged enough to wallop the former with <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_broomstick" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s broomstick">her broomstick</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Between_the_wars">Between the wars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Between the wars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Between the wars"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>Now, behave, won't you? Won't you, Fred? And you, George?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Molly telling her sons to behave<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>By 1991, all her children from Bill to Fred and George had been accepted to Hogwarts and <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a>, and Ron's first year at Hogwarts was to begin. Molly and Ginny accompanied Percy, Fred, George, and Ron to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_Station" title="King&#39;s Cross Station">King's Cross Station</a>, although her daughter was too young to begin school herself. After Percy, Fred, and George got onto <a href="/wiki/Platform_Nine_and_Three-Quarters" title="Platform Nine and Three-Quarters">Platform 9&#190;</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">another young boy</a> approached her and politely asked how to get onto the Platform. She kindly gave him instructions, and told him to go ahead before Ron.<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>After getting onto the platform with Ginny, Molly learned that the boy she had just talked to was Harry. When Fred and George wondered if he remembered what <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> looked like, she became stern and forbade them from asking him. When last calls for the train came, Molly ushered her children onto the train and called for them to have a good term. She addressed the twins, telling them that she didn't want to receive any more owls that they had blown up a toilet, and George quipped that they would send her a <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_lavatory_seat" title="Hogwarts lavatory seat">Hogwarts lavatory seat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Having become friends with Harry, Ron contacted Molly shortly before the Christmas holidays to tell her that Harry wasn't expecting any presents. She came to the rescue, knitting him an emerald green <a href="/wiki/Weasley_jumper" title="Weasley jumper">Weasley jumper</a> and making him a box of home-made fudge.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> For the Christmas holidays, Molly, Ginny, and Arthur also went to <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> to visit their son <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charlie</a>.
</p><p>When Harry and the others returned from Hogwarts for the summer, he thanked Molly for the jumper and fudge, to which she insisted it was no trouble. When Harry's <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">uncle Vernon</a> barked at him to get moving, she tried to engage him in conversation, which ended up going nowhere.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>One night, Molly was scared to death when she found Fred, George, and Ron's beds empty, the <a href="/wiki/Flying_Ford_Anglia" title="Flying Ford Anglia">family car</a> gone, and no note left behind explaining what the boys were doing. When the trio returned with Harry the next morning, having flown to <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">Surrey</a> and back, her anger exploded and she yelled at them for what, to Harry, seemed like hours. <p>On <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a>, Molly, Bill, and Charlie accompanied the <a href="/wiki/Trio" title="Trio">trio</a> to King's Cross Station for the return to Hogwarts, wishing all of them a happy term and gave hugs goodbye. The three of them all implied that <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">something special</a> was happening at the school, but wouldn't elaborate. This left the trio and others highly curious as to what it could possibly be, but they didn't find out until the term started what the surprise was.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to Charlie, when Harry's name came out of the <a href="/wiki/Goblet_of_Fire" title="Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a> as a surprise fourth champion, Molly was against letting him compete, believing that Harry was too young. She was "in floods" after reading an "interview" with <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> in which Skeeter claimed Harry still cried about his parents, and she happened to believe every word that <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> penned. Considering his mother's already fragile state, Charlie didn't have the heart to tell her what Harry had to do for the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament#The_First_Task" title="Triwizard Tournament">First Task</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Molly was horrified when Skeeter reported that <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> was messing with Harry. As a result, on Easter she sent Hermione chicken-sized Easter eggs containing homemade <a href="/wiki/Toffee" title="Toffee">toffee</a>, whereas the ones she sent to Harry and Ron were the size of dragon eggs.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In June, Molly arrived at Hogwarts with Bill to watch Harry in the Third Task of the Triwizard Tournament, in place of his family. While whittling down the hours until the task started, Harry gave her and Bill a tour around the grounds, showing them the <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons</a> carriage and the <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang</a> ship. Molly was intrigued by the <a href="/wiki/Whomping_Willow" title="Whomping Willow">Whomping Willow</a>, which had been planted after she left school. She also reminisced about <a href="/wiki/Ogg" title="Ogg">Ogg</a>, the gamekeeper before Hagrid.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the trio returned to the castle for lunch, Ron was surprised to see his mother and eldest brother there. Fred, George, and Ginny came over to join them, something which Harry enjoyed. When Hermione appeared at the table, having come from the library, she greeted her more stiffly than usual, as Skeeter had been writing articles about the prodigy student supposedly toying with Harry and <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>'s affections. After Harry explained that the stories were false and that he and Hermione had never been romantically involved, she warmed up to her son's friend once again. Molly later defended Harry with a fierce passion against <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">Amos Diggory</a>'s accusations of not correcting Rita Skeeter in her original article about Harry, which was supposed to be about the Triwizard Tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

When Dumbledore acted to reactivate the Order of the Phoenix, Molly was shocked to learn Sirius Black was in the room, still believing Sirius to be a mass murderer like most of the wizarding world. However, Ron was able to convince her otherwise. Molly and the other members learnt about Lord Voldemort's return, which she believed Harry about. She also comforted Harry after his horrific experience in the Little Hangleton graveyard.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Wizarding_War">Second Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A week into the summer holidays, Molly had her heart broken when <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy</a> broke contact with the family after a row with <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">his father</a>. Percy insulted his parents, believing them to be "idiots" for following Dumbledore, and moved out to <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley%27s_London_residence" title="Percy Weasley&#39;s London residence">a flat in London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> She went to London to try and talk with Percy, but he simply slammed the door in her face.
</p>

<p>Molly and Arthur moved their family to 12 Grimmauld Place in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order</a>. Under Dumbledore's orders, the Weasleys kept Harry Potter in the dark about the Order's goings-on. When the <a href="/wiki/Advance_Guard" title="Advance Guard">Advance Guard</a> delivered Harry to Grimmauld Place, she was pleased to see him, but quickly ushered him upstairs so he wouldn't interrupt the Order's meeting. When the meeting ended, Molly brought the children downstairs for dinner. Against her wishes, Sirius and Remus gave Harry the general picture of what the Order was doing in the fight against Voldemort, which lead to an intense row between Molly and Sirius involving Harry's welfare.
</p>

<p>For the following weeks, Molly led the charge in making the house suitable for living in, which was not an easy task considering how long it had been without inhabitants or proper housekeeping.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> When Harry had to go to the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> for a hearing regarding his illegal use of under-age magic, Molly was among those who worried about the result. Like the rest of the family, she was relieved when Harry was cleared of all charges, although she did scold Fred, George, and Ginny for parading around chanting "He got off."
</p>

<p>When Ron was made a Gryffindor <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefect</a>, Molly was overjoyed and offered to get him a reward, to which he asked for a new broom, but quickly added that it didn't need to be a <i>good</i> broom upon seeing his mother's face fall. She returned later that night with the children's school supplies and prepared a party to celebrate Ron and Hermione's appointments. When <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">"Mad-Eye" Moody</a> arrived, Molly asked him to look at what was in the writing desk in the drawing room. Using his "mad" eye, Moody confirmed it was a <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a> and offered to get rid of it, but she declined, commenting she would do it after dinner. After Harry witnessed her inability to vanquish it, she tearfully expressed her concern over her family's safety. Lupin comforted her, explaining that the Order had a better understanding of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> than they did the first time, and that while he couldn't promise no one would get hurt, the Order would do their best to protect her family and Harry from the upcoming danger.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP9_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP9-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On the morning of 1 September, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George</a> again got on Molly's nerves when they bewitched their trunks to zoom down the stairs, to save the trouble of carrying them. The trunks managed to knock Ginny down two flights of stairs, to which her mother screamed at the pair that they could have seriously injured her. When Sirius came bounding downstairs in his dog form to see Harry off, Molly reiterated that it was a bad idea, but felt that it was up to him if he wanted to risk his safety.
</p><p>When she got wind of the <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">secret group</a> Harry, Ron, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a> were planning to form in order to teach practical <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">defensive magic</a>, Molly was firmly against the idea, believing that they would all have time to learn to defend themselves after they left school. She asked Sirius to pass on a message to Ron, which was that he was in no way to continue the group. She also wanted the message passed on to Harry and Hermione, due to accepting that she had no control over the pair's actions, but having their best interests at heart.
</p>

<p>During the Christmas holiday, Molly was contacted by Dumbledore's phoenix <a href="/wiki/Fawkes" title="Fawkes">Fawkes</a> to warn her that Arthur had been attacked by <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> while doing guard duty for the Order. While the Weasley children stayed with Sirius in <a href="/wiki/Grimmauld_Place" title="Grimmauld Place">Grimmauld Place</a>, they wanted to set off for St Mungo's at once. In response, Sirius had to convince them they couldn't until their mother had been notified, as it would look too suspicious otherwise. This caused issues with Fred and George whom wanted to see their father immediately. Before setting off for <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="St Mungo&#39;s">St Mungo's</a>, Molly sent a note to Grimmauld Place, which stated that Arthur was still alive and warned them to stay put. At 5:10 a.m.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> she arrived at Grimmauld Place to tell them Arthur would be all right and that they could all visit him later, making George and Ginny very relieved before they got up and hugged her. When Sirius began making breakfast, Molly hugged Harry and expressed her gratitude that if not for him Arthur might have died. She proceeded to tell Sirius to go and relax and that she would take over breakfast.

<p>When the Weasleys and Harry visited Arthur in St Mungo's, Molly became alarmed when Arthur mentioned one of his room-mates was a <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a>, but he calmed her by pointing out the full moon wasn't for another fortnight. When the twins tried to get information from Arthur about what he had been doing, she got angry and told them that the topic wasn't up for discussion. Molly later fretted over Harry's supposed illness when he was acting strangely after their visit. In fact, Harry was concerned and scared for the well-being of others because he thought he might have been possessed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, but she did not know this.
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas Day</a>, Molly's concern about Percy reached a breaking point when he returned his Christmas jumper unopened. Fred and George "attempted" to console her, but soon afterwards Lupin took over. When the family and Harry returned to visit Arthur again, Molly exploded after learning that for his wound, Arthur had tried using <a href="/wiki/Stitches" title="Stitches">stitches</a>, a Muggle remedy for similar Muggle wounds.

<p>In June, Molly and Arthur arrived at King's Cross Station to pick their children up. The couple also made plans with Dumbledore to take Harry to <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a> two weeks into the summer holidays, so he wouldn't have to spend the whole summer with the <a href="/wiki/Dursley_family" title="Dursley family">Dursleys</a>, probably guessing that Harry didn't want to spend his entire summer holidays grieving his godfather's death at <a href="/wiki/Privet_Drive" title="Privet Drive">Privet Drive</a> alone, with only his negative thoughts and unpleasant relatives for company.
</p><p>When Harry arrived in July, Molly revealed that Arthur had been promoted to head a <a href="/wiki/Office_for_the_Detection_and_Confiscation_of_Counterfeit_Defensive_Spells_and_Protective_Objects" title="Office for the Detection and Confiscation of Counterfeit Defensive Spells and Protective Objects">new department</a> for <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a>. From the tone of her voice, Harry deduced she had been "bursting" to tell someone. She made him a bowl of onion soup and remarked on how much he had grown since she saw him last, stating that it looked as though he had a <a href="/wiki/Stretching_Jinx" title="Stretching Jinx">Stretching Jinx</a> placed upon him. Molly and Harry then discussed <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> and his return to Hogwarts. When Arthur arrived home from work, he made her ask him their security questions before allowing himself in, as to set an example and in the process revealed that Arthur's private nickname for her was "Mollywobbles."

<p>The next morning, Molly expressed her disdain at Bill's engagement to <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a>, whom was staying at the house as Bill had brought her to the Burrow so she could get to know his family better before the wedding. Molly had a low opinion of Fleur and disliked being alone with her, thinking that she was snobbish and rude. She also felt that the engagement was rushed and believed it was due to the fear brought on by Voldemort's return to power. According to Ginny, her mother kept trying to break up the engagement by inviting <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> to dinner, hoping that Bill would fall for her instead.
</p><p>When Hermione got a black eye from one of Fred and George's <a href="/wiki/Boxing_telescope" title="Boxing telescope">punching telescopes</a>, she went to Molly to try and get rid of it to no avail; but she was unable to remove the bruise even with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Healer%27s_Helpmate" title="The Healer&#39;s Helpmate">The Healer's Helpmate</a></i>. When Ron's <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a> results arrived, Molly was pleased to learn he had earned seven "pass" grades, commenting that it was more than Fred and George both earned, put together. When the school book lists arrived, she refused to go to <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a> without Arthur; when Ron made a joke about Voldemort possibly hiding in <a href="/wiki/Flourish_and_Blotts" title="Flourish and Blotts">Flourish and Blotts</a>, his mother threatened not only to leave him behind and get his things herself, but hold him back from returning to Hogwarts too, so Ron quickly recanted as he wanted to see <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wizard_Wheezes" title="Weasleys&#39; Wizard Wheezes">Fred and George's shop</a>.

<p>During the Christmas holidays, Molly had her hands full, as she entertained many quests that Christmas, some including Harry, Fleur, and Remus. She masked <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy</a>'s continued defection from the family by saying he was busy at the Ministry. When the pair arrived in the hospital wing, she hugged Harry very tightly, pointing out how many of the family members he had saved. She was very happy that Ron was no longer in danger and would make a full recovery.

<p>When Harry and Hagrid arrived at the <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">Burrow</a> after the Battle of the Seven Potters, Molly was relieved that they managed to arrive safely. She hurried from the inside of the Burrow and proceeded to pull Harry into a hug that he felt he didn't deserve. She was worried because a lot of the others had missed their <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">portkeys</a> and Harry and Hagrid were the first to arrive, even thought they were suppose to arrive third. <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Hagrid</a> then asked if Molly had any brandy, for "medicinal purposes", as he was in pain from the battle. Instead of <a href="/wiki/Summoning_Charm" title="Summoning Charm">summoning</a> it by magic, she went into the house to collect it herself. Harry knew that this was because she wanted to hide her worry and the blotchiness of her face.
</p>

<p>A few moments later, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a> alerted Molly to the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Lupin</a> and George. Seeing there was obviously something wrong with her son, she soon learned that he was missing his left ear. Molly managed to <a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">staunch the bleeding</a>, but was unable to repair the damage. However, she was simply relieved that he was alive. When <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> returned safely, Molly cried harder than ever when George turned the injury into a punch line. George then commented that at least from then on, she would be able to differentiate the pair. A few minutes later, Ron and Bill both returned safely. Hugging his mother, Bill told the group that "Mad-Eye" Moody had been killed.
</p>

<p>Within a few days, Molly approached <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> under the pretence of determining the owner of a sock, about why he, Ron, and Hermione were planning to leave Hogwarts. Harry refused to say explicitly why, but dropped hints that it was because of something <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> wanted him to do. She argued that she and Arthur deserved to know, and was sure <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger%27s_father" title="Hermione Granger&#39;s father">Mr</a>. and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger%27s_mother" title="Hermione Granger&#39;s mother">Mrs Granger</a> would agree, continuing that Dumbledore had the whole Order at his command if he wanted something done and the three of them were hardly of age. Harry countered that there had been no misunderstanding: the mission Dumbledore left for him was for him alone, and Ron and Hermione were coming along by choice. Seeing she was fighting a losing battle, Molly the changed the subject by asking if he would help prepare the house for Bill and Fleur's wedding. He agreed, but she made sure to give the trio jobs that kept them away from each other, believing that she could delay their departure if she could prevent them from getting together and planning, as <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a> commented to Harry. When the <a href="/wiki/Delacour_family" title="Delacour family">Delacours</a> arrived the following morning, Molly was somewhat flustered, but pleased to have them there. She and Arthur fervently insisted that Monsieur and Madame Delacour take their bedroom, while she and Arthur slept in the sitting room. 
</p>

<p>The following day, which was also Harry's birthday, Molly gave him <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Prewett%27s_watch" title="Fabian Prewett&#39;s watch">a watch</a> that had belonged to her late brother Fabian. In the middle of explaining the tradition of giving children a watch when they came of age, her speech was interrupted by a hug from Harry with which he hoped to convey his deep feelings of gratitude and care, which worked as she was taken-aback but very touched. When Charlie arrived that evening, she forced him into a chair at wandpoint and gave him a haircut. Molly then made a scrumptious dinner and made Harry's birthday cake into the shape of a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a>. Just before the cutting of the cake, she learned that Rufus Scrimgeour was accompanying Arthur home. After the pair arrived, Scrimgeour revealed the reason for his visit: he required a private word with Harry, Ron, and Hermione. 
</p>

<p>The next day was <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_Bill_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" class="mw-redirect" title="Wedding of Bill Weasley and Fleur Delacour">Bill's wedding to Fleur</a>. Molly was once again in tears as Percy didn't show up. During the ceremony, she sat with <a href="/wiki/Apolline_Delacour" title="Apolline Delacour">Apolline Delacour</a> and the pair cried into their tissues. At the reception that followed, she danced with Monsieur Delacour. Soon afterwards, Molly learned with everyone else that the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> had fallen to the Death Eaters' control and that Scrimgeour was dead, shortly before the reception was ransacked by arriving Death Eaters. After the Death Eaters left, she and Arthur sent word to Harry, Ron, and Hermione that the rest of the family was safe.
</p><p>In March, the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> identified Ron as travelling with Harry Potter. Bill was able to get word to his family, and Molly and the rest of the Weasley family vacated the Burrow. They lived with her aunt <a href="/wiki/Muriel" title="Muriel">Muriel</a> during this time.
</p>

<p>On 2 May, 1998 Molly and Arthur arrived at the school to take part in the Battle of Hogwarts. Along with several other individuals, she cast <a href="/wiki/Protego_Maxima" title="Protego Maxima">protective charms</a> over Hogwarts before the battle. As the combination of the Order and <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> left to gather in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>, she was adamant about Ginny going home since she was under-age. Ginny insisted she should be there in addition to the rest of her family and because she was a member of Dumbledore's Army. During this argument, Percy entered the room, having learned of the pending battle from <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>. He made peace with his family, much to her relief.
</p><p>
When the family left to join the others, Molly caught Ginny trying to sneak out with them. Lupin then proposed that Ginny could stay in the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>; that way she could remain close to her family while simultaneously being out of harm's way. During a break in the battle, as ordered by Voldemort, Molly grieved Fred's death in the Great Hall. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the years following Voldemort's death, Molly's family grew with the marriages of her children and births of numerous grandchildren. She also served as the namesake of <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley_II" title="Molly Weasley II">Percy's oldest daughter</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=20" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Molly was frequently described as a short, slightly plump, and kindly-looking woman. Like the rest of her immediate family, she had flaming fiery red hair and her eyes were the same shade of warm brown as her daughter's. Harry was shocked to see that she could so easily look "like a sabre-tooth tiger" when she was angry with her sons. 
</p><p>Molly was described as "porky," by <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a><sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and "dumpy" by <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">Vernon Dursley</a>, despite the fact that he and his <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">son</a> were much larger than her.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Between June and August of 1995, she lost a good deal of weight and appeared paler, though she seemed to get a healthier look back by the next year. 
</p>

<p>Molly was a kind-hearted, gentle, loving, and very motherly woman. It was comfortable to be around such a sympathetic and warm person. Being in the same room with her was very reassuring. Appalled at the mistreatment that Harry suffered at the <a href="/wiki/Dursley_family" title="Dursley family">Dursleys</a>, Molly was delighted to have <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> stay with the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasleys</a> at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a>, where she could offer food, advice, and general mothering. Starved for affection from his own aunt <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia</a>, Harry found warmth from Molly, who considered to be his closest mother figure.  
</p><p>Molly could have even her twin sons cowering before her if she was angry enough. She was fiercely proud of her children's accomplishments, but was also scandalised by their misbehaviour, being particularly frustrated for the twins' antics. She wasn't above of sending a <a href="/wiki/Howler" title="Howler">Howler</a> to the school to humiliate one of them publicly for stepping out of line, as evidenced by her reaction to the use of the unauthorised <a href="/wiki/Flying_Ford_Anglia" title="Flying Ford Anglia">Flying Ford Anglia</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> to reach <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> at his second school year. Ironically, it was high achiever <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy</a> who caused her the most pain, breaking with the family for their unwavering allegiance to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.  
</p><p>Harry occasionally found Molly's over-protectiveness somewhat smothering. Along with her eldest son <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill</a>, she came to watch Harry in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> and after his ordeal in the graveyard, visited him in Hogwarts' hospital wing, where she hugs him 'how Harry imagined a mother would'. However, Molly sometimes found it difficult to cope with her children heading out in their own, separate directions. Not surprisingly, she considered <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a> wrong for Bill when they become engaged until the latter showed her true feelings. When Harry asked about what the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> was doing against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>, she continually insisted he shouldn't be told more than he needed to know under Dumbledore's orders. However, his godfather <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius</a> believed that he had to dealt with more than the Order could imagine and thus deserved to have the whole picture. Though Harry was touched by Molly's actions, he felt Sirius was right in that he wasn't a small child and should be treated as a maturing adult, something which she understood but still worried about.      
</p>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">Love</a></b>: Molly was a very loving person and the epitome of a good mother, loving all her children dearly and often came to regard close friends as family too. She grew so fond of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> that her <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a> changed accordingly to include his dead body along with her family. Motivated by her love for her family, Molly bravely took on <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> and killed her to defend her daughter <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Nonverbal magic</a></b>: During the Battle of Hogwarts, Molly could cast spells without saying them verbally while she duelled. Since non-verbal magic is very difficult, this testifies that she was actually a powerful witch in her own right.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: Molly was shown to be a surprisingly skilful and even lethal <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellist</a>, especially when her family was threatened. In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, she took part in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> where she single-handedly killed Bellatrix, a dangerous member of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">followers</a>, after the Death Eater had nearly killed her <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">daughter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Molly was normally a good-natured woman who did not actively practise dark magic, but she was actually capable of casting <a href="/wiki/Dark_charm" title="Dark charm">Dark charms</a> with lethal effect. During her duel against Bellatrix, she effectively cast a wide variety of <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a> and ultimately killed her opponent with a <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_curse" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s curse">curse</a> that struck her over her heart.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> She also confidently threatened to either <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinx</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hex" title="Hex">hex</a> Ron, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a>, and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George</a> on numerous occasions. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=24" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=25" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prewett_family">Prewett family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=26" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Prewett family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Prewett family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Molly was born into the <a href="/wiki/Prewett_family" title="Prewett family">Prewett family</a>. Her relationship with her <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_mother" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s mother">mother</a> and <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_father" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s father">father</a> is unknown, but it is possible that, like the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasleys</a>, the Prewetts didn't buy into values of pure-blood supremacy held by so many other wizarding pure blood families.
</p><p>Her brothers, <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Prewett" title="Fabian Prewett">Fabian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gideon_Prewett" title="Gideon Prewett">Gideon Prewett</a>, were both members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, but were brutally killed by five <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. It is likely that Molly was close to her brothers, as she kept Fabian's watch until she gave it to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> for his seventeenth birthday. Their deaths influenced her later protectiveness of her family during the Second Wizarding War.
</p><p>Molly had an uncle, <a href="/wiki/Ignatius_Prewett" title="Ignatius Prewett">Ignatius Prewett</a>, whose name became her son Percy's middle name, and aunt by marriage <a href="/wiki/Lucretia_Black" title="Lucretia Black">Lucretia Prewett</a> (née <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black</a>). Her irritable great-aunt <a href="/wiki/Muriel" title="Muriel">Muriel</a> was still alive in the 1990s and associated with her family.
</p>

<p>Molly pampered her seven children as much as she could afford to, making up for the lack of funds for new books, brooms and robes by showering her loved ones with affection and providing them with abundant and delicious home-cooking and handmade gifts. However, Molly had no qualms about speaking her mind and enforcing discipline when her children put a "toe out of line"; the only thing <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> could do to stop <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George</a> testing their <a href="/wiki/Skiving_Snackbox" title="Skiving Snackbox">Skiving Snackboxes</a> on first years prior to their departure from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> was threaten to write to their mother, something which genuinely scared them. She constantly tried to pressure <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill</a> into cutting his hair and not dressing as flashily as he liked to. Molly had a strong desire for her children to work at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> like their father, causing her to scold Fred and George on numerous occasions for their dream of managing a joke shop. In the end, she clearly wanted her children to be happy in their careers, being impressed when Fred and George proved to be skilled businessmen.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Though she was frequently exasperated with her husband's quirky fascination with <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> artefacts and his seeming inability to act as a firm disciplinarian when it came to many of their sons' shenanigans, Molly clearly loved <a href="/wiki/Arthur" title="Arthur">Arthur</a> dearly and was fiercely proud of all he had accomplished in his career.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Molly's love and sense of protection for her family - especially the youngest members - was apparent in the form her boggart took: Her greatest fear was her family, including Harry, lying dead on the floor. Her fear of seeing her family dead was partially realised when Fred was killed during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, especially since one of the forms that her boggart took was the dead bodies of the twins. 
</p><p>Molly loved all of her children, but she seemed especially fond of her youngest child and only daughter, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a>, who she passed most moments with. When she saw Ginny being fought by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>, the mere possibility of losing her only daughter was enough to make her openly challenge and kill the most dangerous Dark witch of that age. Also, Molly was notable for extending her familial affections to those who weren't related to her, including <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a>'s best friends.
</p><p>Molly was heartbroken when the "high achiever", <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy</a>, turned his back on his family in favour of pursuing his career at the Ministry. She tried to get him to return several times but to no avail, often crying over his departure and continued to send him gifts like her hand-knitted Christmas jumpers, which he would coldly return without a note. Despite his aloof departure, Molly remained affectionate towards her third son, using his good behaviour as reference when scolding the twins for overusing their magic for the simplest matter and masking his defection by making excuses for him such as being too busy at the Ministry to return home for Christmas. After Percy apologised to the whole family, Molly was not shown to have any problems in forgiving him.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Molly's family expanded even further, having many grandchildren through her sons and daughter: <a href="/wiki/Victoire_Weasley" title="Victoire Weasley">Victoire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Weasley" title="Dominique Weasley">Dominique</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Weasley" title="Louis Weasley">Louis</a>, Bill and <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a>'s children; <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley_II" title="Molly Weasley II">Molly</a> (who was named after her) and <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Weasley" title="Lucy Weasley">Lucy</a>, Percy and <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Weasley" title="Audrey Weasley">Audrey's</a> daughters; <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley_II" title="Fred Weasley II">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roxanne_Weasley" title="Roxanne Weasley">Roxanne</a>, George and <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a>'s children; <a href="/wiki/Rose_Granger-Weasley" title="Rose Granger-Weasley">Rose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Granger-Weasley" title="Hugo Granger-Weasley">Hugo</a>, Ron and Hermione's children; and <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_II" title="James Potter II">James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_Potter" title="Lily Potter">Lily</a>, Ginny and Harry's children. Based on her affection for her family, it is likely that Molly was a devoted grandmother.
</p>

<p>Molly first met Harry while taking her sons to the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a>. She took pity on him for being alone, as he seemed to be a sweet and polite boy. Upon being told by Fred and George who he was, Molly was adamant that her children not pester Harry about his past, stating that he would probably get enough attention and instructing them not to add to it. After getting to know more about Harry through Ron, Molly began treating him as another member of the family, whether it be by letting him stay at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">the Burrow</a> for the school holidays or fussing over the state of his health and making sure he had additional helpings during meals, although she tended to avoid the subject of the Dursleys when Harry brought them up even briefly. She also sent Harry a <a href="/wiki/Weasley_jumper" title="Weasley jumper">Weasley jumper</a> and a large box of fudge or mince pies every <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>, even after the two of them had only spoken briefly at King's Cross during Harry's first year, simply because Ron had mentioned that Harry wasn't expecting any presents.
</p>

<p>Molly would occasionally forget she wasn't Harry's mother and tended to be as protective of him as if he was one of her own children, a sentiment Harry himself shared. She was deeply affected when Harry was forced to compete in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, to the point that her attitude towards Hermione temporarily became almost hostile because she assumed <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>'s claims that Hermione was toying with Harry's emotions until Harry himself assured her otherwise. Molly pushed particularly hard for Harry not to be "burdened" with the knowledge of certain aspects of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>'s business involving <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>, which caused her to come into conflict with Harry's godfather, <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> and even her own husband. She showed her over-protective nature again upon discovering that Harry, Ron, and Hermione weren't intending to return to Hogwarts during their <a href="/wiki/Seventh_year" title="Seventh year">final year</a>, demanding to be informed of their intentions and even did everything in her power to prevent them having any opportunity to discuss their plans in secret. The form of Molly's <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">boggart</a>, which reflected her greatest fear of seeing her family dead by appearing as their dead bodies, included Harry's own corpse. In return, Harry was grateful to her for the love and protection he needed and wanted. After Molly killed Bellatrix, Voldemort prepared to attack her in revenge, prompting Harry to reveal his continued existence rather than continuing his attempts to to attack from under his Invisibility Cloak.
</p><p>Sometime after the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Harry married Ginny, becoming Molly's son-in-law and officially joining the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a>. He is also the father of three of Molly's grandchildren: <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_II" title="James Potter II">James Sirius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Severus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_L._Potter" title="Lily L. Potter">Lily Luna Potter</a>.
</p>

</p><p>After the Second Wizarding War, Molly had three grandchildren through Fleur.

<p>Molly had a rather close relationship with <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> and fellow <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order</a> member, Nymphadora Tonks. Molly often invited Tonks to dine with the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a> and the young witch was always a welcome guest at the events held by the Weasley matriarch. Molly would at times get annoyed at Tonks due to her clumsiness, but never out of dislike for her. Rather it was mostly due to the fact that Tonks' attempts at helping resulting in her causing minor annoyances, such as setting off the <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black%27s_portrait" title="Walburga Black&#39;s portrait">Portrait of Walburga Black at 12 Grimmauld Place</a>. During these times, Molly would attempt to dissuade Tonks from helping, so as to avoid such accidents.
</p><p>Though there was a significant difference in age between the two, Molly appeared to be Tonks's closest adult friend: She appeared to be the only person in whom Tonks confided her love of <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>. Several people suspected that Molly hoped her son <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> would fall in love with Tonks, rather than marry <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a> and Harry Potter noted Molly was acting somewhat frosty toward Lupin during the <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> Christmas holidays, though he misinterpreted the reason why. Molly reacted heatedly to disparaging remarks made by Fleur in regards to Tonks, which is indicative of the high opinion she held for the young Auror. At the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, it was Molly who duelled and killed Tonks' murderer and aunt Bellatrix, therefore avenging Tonks' death.

<p>Molly considered <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> to be the greatest wizard of all time. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, she was technically the first new recruit of the Order of the Phoenix, an organisation both founded and led by Dumbledore, having been asked to help battle against Voldemort almost immediately after his return was discovered. Along with most of the wizarding world, Molly was devastated by Dumbledore's death, and attended his funeral in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> was close with the Weasley family, joined the re-instated Order, and saw Molly on a fairly regular basis. Lupin helped Molly get rid of a boggart that took the form of her family and Harry Potter dead; confessing to him that she was afraid of what would happen to her children and husband. Lupin comforted her, saying that he and the other members of the Order would take care of her family. After Dumbledore's death, Molly expressed her opinion that Lupin and Tonks should be together, as they loved each other, despite Lupin's protests that he was too old, too poor, and too dangerous for Tonks. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>, Lupin saved George's life from Death Eaters, for which Molly was incredibly grateful. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Lupin and Tonks were killed, showing Molly grieving near their bodies.
</p><p>Molly initially believed <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> (her "cousin by marriage", as his aunt Lucretia married her uncle Ignatius) was a murderer, who had betrayed the <a href="/wiki/Potter_family" title="Potter family">Potter family</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, and didn't learn of his innocence until <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. While both Sirius and Molly lived at <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>, they argued over the latter's tendency to be overprotective of Sirius's godson Harry: Sirius reminded her she wasn't Harry's mother, while Molly considered Sirius to be too reckless and reminded him that Harry wasn't his father James. Nonetheless, they got along well for the most part, as Sirius was thrilled at having the Weasley family and Harry Potter stay at Grimmauld place over the 1995 winter holidays. Molly was greatly saddened by Sirius's death in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> and was ultimately the one to kill Bellatrix, Sirius's cousin and murderer.
</p><p>Molly worked fairly closely with the late <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>, being on good terms with both of them. She was shocked by Moody's death in 1997. Kingsley was responsible for saving countless lives when he sent his <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a> to warn the attendees of Bill and Fleur's <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">wedding</a> that the Ministry had fallen in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> and later became <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, employing a number of the members of Molly's family.
</p><p>Molly was friendly with <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a> professor and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_Gryffindor_House" title="Head of Gryffindor House">Head of Gryffindor House</a>, the house into which all the members of the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a> had been sorted. The two discussed Dumbledore's death in 1997. McGonagall would have been Headmistress while several of Molly's grandchildren attended the school.
</p><p>The Weasley family were all fond of <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>, the gamekeeper and later <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> professor at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. He fought alongside Molly at the Battle of Hogwarts and they both survived the war. He appeared to be friendly with Molly's grandchildren, as seen by his inviting Albus to his house for tea.
</p><p>Molly seemed to be on good terms with <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, despite the fact that he disliked her children. However, she and her family obviously turned against him when they learnt he killed Dumbledore, but it was most likely that this changed when she learned of Snape's true loyalties from Harry. In addition, Harry and Ginny would later name <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">one of Molly's grandsons</a> after Snape.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=34" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Molly</i> is a diminutive form of <i>Mary</i>, the English form of the Hebrew name <i>Miryam</i>, the meaning of which is unknown, though theories include "sea of bitterness", "rebelliousness", "wished for child", and "beloved". In the case of Molly Weasley, however, the name is most likely derived from the term "mollycoddle," meaning to "to pamper or overprotect."
</p><p>The surname <i>Prewett</i> is Welsh in origin. It is derived from the Middle English word <i>prou</i>, meaning "brave" or "valiant", and the suffix "-et", which implies smallness. Thus, it is believed to be derived from a nickname for someone small, but brave.
</p><p><i>Weasley</i> is derived from the weasel, an animal <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> is fond of. The term is especially appropriate for Molly, as a mother weasel is particularly protective of her young to the point that she will willingly take on animals several times bigger than her in defence of her offspring.
</p>

<ul><li>Contrary to rumours among fans that <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> would be the one to do it, <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> stated in an interview that she had always intended that Molly would be the one to kill <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>, for two main reasons: firstly, so that Molly could "have her moment" and show that simply because she had dedicated herself to her family did not mean that she was not a powerful witch, and secondly, to show the clash of Molly's "maternal love" against Bellatrix's "obsessive love"<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>.</li>
<li>Her husband's pet name for her, "Mollywobbles", may be a pun on "the collywobbles", British slang for a queasy stomach, especially when caused by anxiety. Possibly in reference to her constant fretting over the welfare of her loved ones.</li>
<li>Molly is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> actress <a href="/wiki/Julie_Walters" title="Julie Walters">Julie Walters</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Half-Blood Prince</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Part 2</a></i>.</li>
<li>In 1999, when Warner Brothers was casting for the first film, Rosie O'Donnell had admitted to loving the books so much, she called the studio and asked to play Mrs Weasley without receiving payment. Chris Columbus called her afterwards, declining her offer as they wanted a strictly British cast. The aftermath resulted in Molly Weasley's signature in the books being hand-written by Rosie O'Donnell in order to fulfil her wish in some small way.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the credits of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a>,</i> she is mentioned as Mrs Molly Weasley, while in the credits of the other films, she is just mentioned as Molly Weasley.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Julie_Walters" title="Julie Walters">Julie Walters</a>' favourite line in the films is "Not my daughter, you bitch!" from <i>Deathly Hallows: Part 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> and Molly Weasley <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duel</a> on the top of a table after Bellatrix attacks <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a>. Molly kills her using an unknown freezing spell followed by a <a href="/wiki/Disintegration_curse" title="Disintegration curse">Disintegration curse</a>.</li>
<li>Molly is a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 (video game)">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 (video game)</a></i>.</li></ul>
