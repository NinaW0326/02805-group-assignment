<b>Malcolm Baddock</b> (born c. <a href="/wiki/1982" title="1982">1982</a>-<a href="/wiki/1983" title="1983">1983</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> student at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> three years below <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. His arrival at the Slytherin table immediately after being <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> was met with applause from <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a> hissed at him, and Harry privately wondered if Malcolm was aware of Slytherin House's sinister reputation.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF12_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF12-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Baddock?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Baddock?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Malcolm means "disciple of St Columba," a sixth century Irish saint who established a convent on the island of Iona. This was the name of four kings of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> starting in the 10th century, including Malcolm III, who became king after killing Macbeth, the usurper who had murdered his father.
</p>
