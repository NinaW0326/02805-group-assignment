<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eldritch_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eldritch_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister</a> Diggory was born into the <a href="/wiki/Diggory_family" title="Diggory family">Diggory family</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> family of unknown <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood status</a>. At an unknown point (though probably at age eleven like most children) he bought <a href="/wiki/Eldritch_Diggory%27s_wand" title="Eldritch Diggory&#39;s wand">a wand</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders</a>. According to <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, he owned a "fine" <a href="/wiki/Poplar" title="Poplar">poplar</a> <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wands_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wands-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Diggory was later appointed as the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>. He was a popular Minister and first established an Auror recruitment programme in Great Britain.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point in his career, Diggory visited <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. This proved to be a shock for him, since he'd had no idea of the awful conditions inside the prison. Many within the prison died of despair and the rest were mostly <a href="/wiki/Insanity" title="Insanity">insane</a>. When he returned to London Diggory established a committee to explore alternatives to Azkaban, or at least remove the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> as guards. Experts argued that if the Dementors were deprived of prisoners' souls on which they could feed, they might abandon the prison and head for the mainland. However, Diggory, being so horrified at what he had seen, continued his search for alternatives nonetheless.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1747" class="mw-redirect" title="1747">1747</a>, Diggory died in office due to a case of <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Pox" title="Dragon Pox">Dragon Pox</a>. This disabled the committee from reaching a decision concerning Azkaban.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eldritch_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eldritch_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eldritch is an English word of uncertain origin meaning "alien, supernatural". The term is most closely related with the works of American horror author <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H._P._Lovecraft" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:H. P. Lovecraft">H. P. Lovecraft</a>, which launched a type of monsters known as "eldritch abominations", of which <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> are sometimes seen as a low-key example.
</p>
