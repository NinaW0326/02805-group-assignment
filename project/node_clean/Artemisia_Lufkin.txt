<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Artemisia was born in <a href="/wiki/1754" class="mw-redirect" title="1754">1754</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-COS_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British Isles</a>, to at least one magical parent. In her youth, she attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from around <a href="/wiki/1765" class="mw-redirect" title="1765">1765</a> to <a href="/wiki/1772" class="mw-redirect" title="1772">1772</a>, where she was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Minister_for_Magic">As Minister for Magic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: As Minister for Magic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As Minister for Magic"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1798" class="mw-redirect" title="1798">1798</a>, Ms. Lufkin rose to the position of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, succeeding <a href="/wiki/Unctuous_Osbert" title="Unctuous Osbert">Unctuous Osbert</a>, and became the first woman ever to have held the office.<sup id="cite_ref-COS_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Minister for Magic, she established the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_International_Magical_Cooperation" title="Department of International Magical Cooperation">Department of International Magical Cooperation</a> and lobbied hard and successfully to have a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a> tournament held in Britain during her term.<sup id="cite_ref-PMministers_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMministers-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She served as Minister for thirteen years, being succeeded in the post by <a href="/wiki/Grogan_Stump" title="Grogan Stump">Grogan Stump</a> in <a href="/wiki/1811" class="mw-redirect" title="1811">1811</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_post-mortem">Death and post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death and post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death and post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Ms. Lufkin passed away in <a href="/wiki/1825" class="mw-redirect" title="1825">1825</a>, aged around 71. She was featured in a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> by the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">Prefect</a> <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Truman" title="Gabriel Truman">Gabriel Truman</a> welcomed the <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> <a href="/wiki/First_year" title="First year">first years</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff_Basement" title="Hufflepuff Basement">Hufflepuff Basement</a> with a short speech in which he mentioned former Minister Artemisia Lufkin as one of the many notable Hogwarts students who had been <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a>, like them, into Hufflepuff.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore2_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_Lufkin?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Artemisia is a feminine form of Artemisios,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> a Greek name which derived from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artemis" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Artemis">Artemis</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> the name of the Greek maiden daughter of Zeus and Leto, and the twin sister of Apollo. She is the goddess of the moon, hunting, the wilderness, and the gift of taming wild animals.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artemisia_(genus)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Artemisia (genus)">Artemisia</a> is also the name of a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Genus">genus</a> of plants that includes <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sagebrush" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Sagebrush">sagebrush</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wormwood" title="Wormwood">wormwood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artemisia_Gentileschi" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Artemisia Gentileschi">Artemisia Gentileschi</a> was a pioneering Italian painter of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baroque" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Baroque">Baroque</a> movement, the first woman to join the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accademia_di_Belle_Arti_Firenze" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Accademia di Belle Arti Firenze">Accademia di Belle Arti Firenze</a>, as well as one of the first to handle religious and historical subjects.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Lufkin is a pet form of the Middle English given name Love,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> which derived from either the Old English girl's name <i>Lufu</i>, meaning "<a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a>", or its masculine equivalent <i>Lufa</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li>The meaning of her surname is rather curious and ironic, considering "<i>Lufa-Lufa</i>" is the Portuguese name for Hufflepuff, the house she was sorted in, and the suffix 'kin' is often used to refer to a person's children. In other words, her surname can be rather fittingly interpreted as "child of Hufflepuff".</li></ul></li></ul>
