<b>Elizabeth II</b> (full name <b>Elizabeth Alexandra Mary</b>; born <a href="/wiki/21_April" title="21 April">21 April</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>) was the reigning <a href="/wiki/Queen" title="Queen">queen</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">United Kingdom</a> and other <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Commonwealth">Commonwealth</a> countries during the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. She was still queen as of <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a> and became the longest serving monarch of Britain in <a href="/wiki/September" title="September">September</a> <a href="/wiki/2015" title="2015">2015</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corgi_incident">Corgi incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Corgi incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Corgi incident"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, one of the Queen's <a href="/wiki/Corgi" title="Corgi">corgwn</a> was <a href="/wiki/Corgi_to_hamster_spell" title="Corgi to hamster spell">transformed</a> into a <a href="/wiki/Hamster" title="Hamster">hamster</a> by an unknown <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch or wizard</a>, prompting the <a href="/wiki/Improper_Use_of_Magic_Office" title="Improper Use of Magic Office">Improper Use of Magic Office</a> to launch an investigation and question several suspects.<sup id="cite_ref-ps-prophet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ps-prophet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The motive behind this attack was unknown at the time,<sup id="cite_ref-ps-prophet_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ps-prophet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and it is not known if the culprit was ever found.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> authorities, on the other hand, concluded that the Queen's corgi had been stolen and replaced with a hamster as a "cruel" prank.<sup id="cite_ref-ps-prophet_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ps-prophet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They launched a "full scale" search to attempt to locate the <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dog</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ps-prophet_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ps-prophet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The incident was reported in the <a href="/wiki/6_September" title="6 September">6 September</a> 1991 issue of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ps-prophet_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ps-prophet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The Queen's likeness was featured on the obverse face of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Muggle_money" title="Muggle money">banknotes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-banknotewiki_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-banknotewiki-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A £50 note was among several objects left on a table at the <a href="/wiki/Siren%27s_Rest" title="Siren&#39;s Rest">Siren's Rest</a> pub in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cob4_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cob4-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Queen%27s_Handbag" title="The Queen&#39;s Handbag">The Queen's Handbag</a></i>, Queen Elizabeth II lost her handbag and the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> sent the task of finding it to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>. The events surrounding this short skit cannot, however, be considered canon, as it contradicts canon in several points.</li>
<li>It is unknown if, as Queen of the United Kingdom in the second half of the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>, Elizabeth II was ever made aware of the existence of the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a> or the wizarding goings-on like the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First</a> or <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, like her <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> was.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "<a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_(disambiguation)" title="Elizabeth (disambiguation)">Elizabeth</a>" comes from Ελισαβετ (<i>Elisabet</i>), the <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> form of the Hebrew name אֱלִישֶׁבַע (<i>Elisheva</i>) meaning "my <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Unbreakable_Vow" title="Unbreakable Vow">oath</a>" or perhaps "my God is abundance".
</p>
