<p><b>Madam Eloise Mintumble</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/1899" class="mw-redirect" title="1899">1899</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Unspeakable" title="Unspeakable">Unspeakable</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a> who died as a result of an experiment to travel back in time for more than a few hours.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eloise_Mintumble?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eloise_Mintumble?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Madam Mintumble was trapped, for a period of five days, in the year <a href="/wiki/1402" class="mw-redirect" title="1402">1402</a>. When she was finally retrieved to the present, her body had aged five centuries, and, irreparably damaged, she died in <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a>. Her excursion to the past provoked a great disturbance to the <a href="/wiki/Life_path" title="Life path">life paths</a> of all those she met, changing the present so dramatically that no fewer than twenty-five of their descendants vanished in the present, having been "<a href="/wiki/Un-born" title="Un-born">un-born</a>". Moreover, there were a few more alarming signs that time itself had been disturbed: Tuesday following her reappearance lasted two and a half days, whereas Thursday shot by in the space of four hours.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
