<b>Elladora Ketteridge</b> (<a href="/wiki/1656" class="mw-redirect" title="1656">1656</a> - <a href="/wiki/1729" class="mw-redirect" title="1729">1729</a>) was the first <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> to discover the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> properties of <a href="/wiki/Gillyweed" title="Gillyweed">Gillyweed</a>, having done so by accident. She nearly suffocated after eating the plant, but saved herself by sticking her head into a bucket of <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PSG_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Elladora_Ketteridge?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Elladora_Ketteridge?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Despite the fact that Elladora Ketteridge discovered the effects of Gillyweed, it is said that <a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks" title="Beaumont Marjoribanks">Beaumont Marjoribanks</a> discovered the existence of the plant about a century later. This error would be fixed if the dates were switched, or if Ketteridge was alive at the same time as, or after Majoribanks, as Majoribanks only discovered the existence of Gillyweed, while Ketteridge discovered its magical effects.</li>
<li>While she discovered the properties of Gillyweed, Ketteridge did so in an accidental manner, and as no other information is known about Ketteridge's career, it is unknown if she regularly studied plants as a <a href="/wiki/Herbologist" title="Herbologist">Herbologist</a>, or if this was just a one-time fluke.</li></ul>
