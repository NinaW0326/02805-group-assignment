<p>A <b>Centaur</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creature</a> whose head, torso, and arms appear to be <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> and are joined to a <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horse</a>'s body. They are, however, their own individual species, and thus are not <a href="/wiki/Half-breed" title="Half-breed">half-breeds</a>. They also have a rich history and are known for being naturally talented in <a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">Healing magic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> and <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_(class)" title="Astronomy (class)">Astronomy</a>.
</p><p>Despite possessing 'human intelligence,' centaurs are classified as <a href="/wiki/Beast" title="Beast">Beasts</a> by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>, at their own request, as they were unhappy at having to share <a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">Being</a> status with <a href="/wiki/Hag" title="Hag">Hags</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">Vampires</a>, known <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">dark</a> creatures.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Like horses, centaurs can come in a variety of colours, ranging from deep black to white. A centaur by the name of <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> had white-blonde hair, a body light in colour, and possessed astonishingly blue eyes. <a href="/wiki/Magorian" title="Magorian">Magorian</a> on the other hand possessed black hair and was chestnut in colour.<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Centaurs have the body of a horse, and the torso, arms, and head of a human. Also <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a> found the centaur Firenze physically attractive.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Centaurs are not in fact half-breeds, but an entirely different species on their own. They are not very pleased to be insulted, particularly where their lineage is concerned, as <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> found out the hard way. <sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Centaurs are also natural born <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seers</a>, in particular the Centaur <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> who taught alongside <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Sybill Trelawney</a> in <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination class</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mannerisms">Mannerisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Mannerisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mannerisms"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>Never try an' get a straight answer out of a centaur. Ruddy stargazers. Not interested in anythin' closer'n the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">moon</a>.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Rubeus Hagrid<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Centaurs are known to be gifted in <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>. Indeed, the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest_Centaur_colony" title="Forbidden Forest Centaur colony">centaurs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a> regularly made vague allusions to seeing the future, as when the centaur <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> told <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> that they were fated to meet again, and when he and <a href="/wiki/Bane" title="Bane">Bane</a> spoke of the "intermission" of sorts, between the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First</a> and <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding Wars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also mentioned to his class at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, that centaurs spend years trying to master their particular art of divining the fates.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Centaurs on the whole seem also to be rather proud of their species, which was evident in their testiness whenever anyone even accidentally alluded to them being "used" or in any way employed by humankind, as when <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> was chastised for allowing <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> to ride to safety on his back, after the young wizard encountered <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a> — his fellow centaurs considered allowing such a thing tantamount to becoming a 'common' mule.<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 253px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Centaurs.JPG/revision/latest?cb=20170106064534" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="251" height="105" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Centaurs.JPG" data-image-key="Centaurs.JPG" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Centaurs.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/251?cb=20170106064534" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Centaurs.JPG/revision/latest?cb=20170106064534" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Centaurs.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/251?cb=20170106064534" decoding="async" width="251" height="105" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Centaurs.JPG" data-image-key="Centaurs.JPG" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Centaurs.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/251?cb=20170106064534" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Centaurs.JPG" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A herd of centaurs armed with bows and arrows, their primary weapons</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, they attempted to murder Firenze for his decision to "enter into human servitude," which was seen as a great and base betrayal.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Centaurs also decided to be placed in the <a href="/wiki/Beast_Division" title="Beast Division">Beast Division</a> instead of the <a href="/wiki/Being_Division" title="Being Division">Being Division</a> in the British Ministry of Magic archives, because they wished to not be associated with hags and vampires, whom the Ministry designates "Beings".<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Centaurs also seem to favour nature over technology, the extent of their tools visibly in use being their bows and arrows. Instead, they cast their attention upon reading signs in nature, and living in forests appropriated by the Ministry of Magic.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They are territorial creatures by nature, as <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> mentioned in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, upon seeing a herd of centaurs in the Forbidden Forest, whose land was being consistently reduced, and that they might revolt against the Ministry as a result. Later the same year, they proclaimed that even Hagrid was "no longer welcome" within "their" forest, as he had "forfeited the friendship of the centaurs," and that their "tolerance was waning" (in reference to Hagrid's half-brother, <a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a>, whom Hagrid was hiding in the forest at the time).<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Centaurs do have a sense of honour, being unwilling to harm the young, as the herd within the Forbidden Forest refused to harm Hagrid due to the young Harry Potter and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> accompanying him at the time. However, this patience and generosity can wane, as later they saw Harry and Hermione deliberately lead <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> into the forest to "use" the centaurs to eliminate her; this, along the fact that both teenagers are nearing their adulthood, led the herd to attempt to attack the two.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Abilities">Abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Abilities"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Centaurs do not use wands for magic, but are known to be well-versed in <a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">magical healing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>, archery, and <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_(class)" title="Astronomy (class)">Astronomy</a>. Centaurs burn herbs such as <a href="/wiki/Mallowsweet" title="Mallowsweet">mallowsweet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sage" title="Sage">sage</a> to refine their stargazing findings. In fact, the centaur <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> taught <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> at Hogwarts, starting in the 1995–1996 school year.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> They also can perform non-verbal magic while duelling, as shown when <a href="/wiki/Torvus" title="Torvus">Torvus</a> duelled against <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> without a wand or an arrow.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Habitat"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Centaurs are forest-dwelling, and, in each country where colonies exist, the wizarding authority has set aside land exclusively for their use.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> One such <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest_Centaur_colony" title="Forbidden Forest Centaur colony">colony</a> exists in the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. However, centaurs have their own methods of concealment and avoid contact with both wizards and witches and <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>, as they are not fond of humans. They generally live in herds of ten to fifty individuals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_wizardkind">Relationship with wizardkind</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationship with wizardkind">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Centaur?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Relationship with wizardkind"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>You have a human on your back! Have you no shame? Are you a common mule?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Bane and other centaurs consider helping humans beneath them<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Starting in <a href="/wiki/1811" class="mw-redirect" title="1811">1811</a>, centaurs were offered "<a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">Being</a>" status, when <a href="/wiki/Grogan_Stump" title="Grogan Stump">Grogan Stump</a> decreed that a "being" was "any creature that has sufficient intelligence to understand the laws of the magical community and to bear part of the responsibility in shaping those laws." However, they declined because of the associations with other creatures, such as <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampires</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hag" title="Hag">hags</a>, that "being" status would give them. The <a href="/wiki/Merpeople" title="Merpeople">merpeople</a> made the same decision a year later. Subject teaching material thus notes on why they are classed as 'Beasts' and that they should be treated with great respect.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite being highly intelligent creatures, centaurs were still officially classified as of "near-human intelligence" by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> in the mid-1990s, per the text of <a href="/wiki/Law_Fifteen_B" title="Law Fifteen B">Law Fifteen B</a>, as laid down by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Regulation_and_Control_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures">Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures</a>. Centaurs take this phrase to be a great insult, as <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> found out the hard way, as they find that "[their] <i>intelligence, thankfully, far outstrips</i> [humans']."<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Centaur_Liaison_Office" title="Centaur Liaison Office">Centaur Liaison Office</a> still exists in the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Regulation_and_Control_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures">Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures</a>, though no centaur has ever used it. In fact, the phrase "Being sent to the Centaur Office" has become an in-joke at the Ministry, meaning that the person in question is about to be sacked. A centaur was also represented in the <a href="/wiki/Fountain_of_Magical_Brethren" title="Fountain of Magical Brethren">Fountain of Magical Brethren</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, at least before the fountain's destruction during the battle between Albus Dumbledore and Lord Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Centaurs have also been known to save wizards from harm, one instance being <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> saving <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> was in the forest, though this was highly looked down upon as centaurs were not supposed to interfere with "what is to come."<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> ended up leading <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> into the Forbidden Forest, a group of centaurs appeared with some of them carrying off Dolores while the rest were scared away by <a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a> in an event known as the <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_in_the_Forbidden_Forest" title="Skirmish in the Forbidden Forest">Skirmish in the Forbidden Forest</a>. It was mentioned that Albus negotiated for Dolores' release when he was rehired by the Ministry of Magic.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The centaur herd in the Forbidden Forest, along with Firenze, also fought against the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DH36_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li>In the books, Centaurs are described as they are in traditional Greek myths, with the back-end of a horse, but an ordinary human face and torso in their upper parts. In the films, however, their human faces are more equine-like, like an exact cross between horse and human. They are covered in hair, the same as their horse halves, with long protruded faces, and long pointy ears.</li>
<li>Despite the fact that the <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">Wizards' Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test</a> establishes them to exist, female centaurs have yet to be physically seen in any <i>Harry Potter</i> media.</li>
<li>In Greek mythology, female centaurs are called centaurides, and though they are not mentioned in early Greek literature and art, they do appear occasionally in later antiquity.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The word <b>Centaur</b> (Κένταυρος, <b>Kentauros</b> in Ancient Greek), is generally regarded as being of obscure origin.</li>
<li>A  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> riddle consisting of an early  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> concept art of a Centaur was added to the  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> insider on <a href="/wiki/16_October" title="16 October">16 October</a> <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a>. It is still unknown who this centaur is.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a> describes that Centaurs refuse to wear any clothing. In the films, <a href="/wiki/Magorian" title="Magorian">Magorian</a> wears pieces of jewellery, shown in detail in the book <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_The_Creature_Vault" title="Harry Potter: The Creature Vault">Harry Potter: The Creature Vault</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rowan_Khanna" title="Rowan Khanna">Rowan Khanna</a>, a history buff, wanted to ask a centaur about the 1811 controversy of not accepting the Being status as they were fascinated by it, but neither Jacob's sibling, <a href="/wiki/Barnaby_Lee" title="Barnaby Lee">Barnaby</a> nor <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Tonks</a>, understood their obsession.</li></ul>
