<b>Iola</b><sup id="cite_ref-JKR_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKR-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <b>Hitchens</b> (née <b>Black</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and the sister of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black_I" title="Sirius Black I">Sirius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phineas_Nigellus_Black" title="Phineas Nigellus Black">Phineas Nigellus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elladora_Black" title="Elladora Black">Elladora Black</a>. She married a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> man named <a href="/wiki/Bob_Hitchens" title="Bob Hitchens">Bob Hitchens</a>, and was struck off the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black Family tree</a> and disowned as a result. Her dates are unknown, but she was born after Elladora (<a href="/wiki/1850" class="mw-redirect" title="1850">1850</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-BFT_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BFT-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Iola_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Iola_Black?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Several sources mistakenly list this character's name as "Isla Black". This seems to stem from an <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://www.hp-lexicon.org/wizards/blackfamilytree.html">incorrect transcription of J.K. Rowling's handdrawn family tree</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_Lexicon" title="Harry Potter Lexicon">Harry Potter Lexicon</a>, based on observation of the original during its auction, on 20 February, 2006 (and which was, for a time, the only available source online on the matter). A scan of the document has, however, since surfaced, having been included in the book <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_Film_Wizardry" title="Harry Potter Film Wizardry">Harry Potter Film Wizardry</a></i>, a behind-the-scenes look at the making of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> film series</a>. The scan clarified that Iola's name had been incorrectly transcribed by the Lexicon (and, indeed, the name "Iola" had already appeared on the tapestry featured in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>).</li>
<li>Based on a statement by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, Iola may have been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>. However, she attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> in the 1860s, at a time when Slughorn was not even born yet.</li>
<li>Iola was the second earliest known disowned person in the family. The earliest known was <a href="/wiki/Eduardus_Limette_Black" title="Eduardus Limette Black">Eduardus Limette Black</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Iola_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Iola_Black?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Iola</i> is probably a female variant of the Ancient Greek name Iole, meaning "violet". In Greek mythology <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iole" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Iole">Iole</a> was a beautiful young princess who was claimed by Heracles for a bride, but her father, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurytus#King_Eurytus_of_Oechalia" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Eurytus">King Eurytus of Oechalia</a>, refused her hand in marriage,<sup id="cite_ref-BTN_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTN-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> which seems to suit Iola Black's story of a morganatic marriage and subsequent disowning.
</p>
