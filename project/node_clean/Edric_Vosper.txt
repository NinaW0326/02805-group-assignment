<b>Edric Vosper</b> was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a> on the <a href="/wiki/English_National_Quidditch_team" title="English National Quidditch team">English National Quidditch team</a> during the four-hundred and twenty-second <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Edric_Vosper?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Edric_Vosper?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Edric" comes from Old English <i>ead</i>, meaning "rich", "blessed" and <i>ric,</i> "ruler". After the Norman conquest this Old English name was not commonly used. It has occasionally been revived in modern times.
</p>
