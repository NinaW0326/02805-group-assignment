<p><b>Gerbold Octavius Ollivander</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmaker</a> who came from a <a href="/wiki/Ollivander_family" title="Ollivander family">long line</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> who began <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">selling wands</a> in <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C</a>. He was the father of <a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander" title="Gervaise Ollivander">Gervaise Ollivander</a>, and, through him, the paternal grandfather of <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gerbold Octavius was born in or prior to the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">nineteenth century</a>, somewhere in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. He was born into the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> <a href="/wiki/Ollivander_family" title="Ollivander family">Ollivander family</a>, who had been in the <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">wand-making business</a> since <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_a_wandmaker">Career as a wandmaker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>My august grandfather, Gerbold Octavius Ollivander, always called wands of </i><a href="/wiki/Fir" title="Fir">fir</a><i> 'the survivor's wand,' because he had sold it to three wizards who subsequently passed through mortal peril unscathed.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Gerbold took up the <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">family business</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, studying further into <a href="/wiki/Wandlore" title="Wandlore">wandlore</a> and becoming an accomplished wandmaker.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gerbold believed that <a href="/wiki/Fir" title="Fir">fir</a> produced "the survivor's wand", as he had sold wands of this wood to three different wizards, each of whom found himself in mortal danger and came out unharmed. He was also fond of saying, "If you seek integrity, search first among the <a href="/wiki/Poplar" title="Poplar">poplars</a>," which his grandson Garrick found correct based on his own experience with poplar wands and their owners.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">nineteenth century</a>, at a time when wands made of <a href="/wiki/Silver_lime" title="Silver lime">silver lime</a> were in vogue, the wandmaker <a href="/wiki/Arturo_Cephalopos" title="Arturo Cephalopos">Arturo Cephalopos</a> claimed that the <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wood</a>'s reputed connection with powers of <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> was "a falsehood circulated by merchants like Gerbold Ollivander, who have overstocked their workshops with silver lime and hope to shift their surplus". According to Gerbold's grandson <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick</a>, however, Cephalopos was "an ignoramus" who predictably went out of business.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point, he fathered a son, <a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander" title="Gervaise Ollivander">Gervaise</a>, and by the late <a href="/wiki/1910s" title="1910s">1910s</a> he had a grandchild, <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick</a>. Both his descendants continued the family tradition of <a href="/wiki/Wandlore" title="Wandlore">wandmaking</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
