<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>Born in the mid-<a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">eighteenth century</a> Gulliver Pokeby was an expert on magical birds. Acknowledged as the first wizard to identify the meaning of Augurey song, Pokeby penned </i><a href="/wiki/Why_I_Didn%27t_Die_When_the_Augurey_Cried" title="Why I Didn&#39;t Die When the Augurey Cried">Why I Didn't Die When the Augurey Cried</a><i>.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Pokeby's <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Gulliver Pokeby</b> (<a href="/wiki/1750" class="mw-redirect" title="1750">1750</a> - <a href="/wiki/1839" class="mw-redirect" title="1839">1839</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magizoologist" title="Magizoologist">Magizoologist</a>, expert on <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> and the author of <i><a href="/wiki/Why_I_Didn%27t_Die_When_the_Augurey_Cried" title="Why I Didn&#39;t Die When the Augurey Cried">Why I Didn't Die When the Augurey Cried</a></i>. He was the first to identify that the <a href="/wiki/Augurey" title="Augurey">Augurey</a>'s cries meant <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">rain</a> was coming, rather than impending <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was immortalised on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which featured him wearing his <a href="/wiki/Gulliver_Pokeby%27s_pocket_watch" title="Gulliver Pokeby&#39;s pocket watch">golden pocket watch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gulliver_Pokeby?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gulliver_Pokeby?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Magizoology" title="Magizoology">Magizoology</a></b>: Pokeby was an expert of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> Ornithology, the study of magical <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, having famously discovered the true explanation of the <a href="/wiki/Augurey" title="Augurey">Augurey</a>'s cries meaning impending <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">rain</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>, a very important <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">scientific</a> discovery in this field. He wrote the book on the subject <i><a href="/wiki/Why_I_Didn%27t_Die_When_the_Augurey_Cried" title="Why I Didn&#39;t Die When the Augurey Cried">Why I Didn't Die When the Augurey Cried</a></i>, showing his experience and knowledge of his research.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
