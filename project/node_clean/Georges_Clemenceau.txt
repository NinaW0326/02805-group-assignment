<b>Georges Clemenceau</b> (<a href="/wiki/28_September" title="28 September">28 September</a> <a href="/wiki/1841" class="mw-redirect" title="1841">1841</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/24_November" title="24 November">24 November</a> <a href="/wiki/1929" title="1929">1929</a>), popularly known by the nickname <b>Tiger</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> politician, physician, and journalist.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He served as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_France" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Prime Minister of France">Prime Minister of France</a> from <a href="/wiki/1906" title="1906">1906</a> to <a href="/wiki/1909" title="1909">1909</a> and again from <a href="/wiki/1917" title="1917">1917</a> to <a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Clemenceau came to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a> to deliver a talk, sequestering himself in the home of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Dana_Gibson" title="Charles Dana Gibson">Charles Dana Gibson</a> on <a href="/wiki/East_73rd_Street" title="East 73rd Street">East 73rd Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> to prepare his speech for that evening.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Many admirers flocked to the house, hoping to secure interviews or autographs, but only a reporter for the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Chronicle" title="New York Chronicle">New York Chronicle</a></i> was granted access.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Clemenceau told the reporter that he had only slept five hours, but "was all ready for the fray," and planned to talk for an half-and-a-half.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He revealed that he had cut out a part of his speech dealing with "figures and statistics" establishing that <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> had not honoured the conditions of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Treaty of Versailles">Treaty of Versailles</a>, but would likely re-add it for a future, planned appearance in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He also stated that he planned to discuss what he saw as the emergence of a "new triple alliance" that posed a threat to the <a href="/wiki/Allies" title="Allies">Allied powers</a>, consisting of "<a href="/wiki/Kemalism" title="Kemalism">Kemalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, the reactionary Germany of <a href="/wiki/Erich_Ludendorff" title="Erich Ludendorff">Ludendorf</a> [sic], and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">Soviets</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The interview was published in the 6 December 1926 issue of the <i>New York Chronicle</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
