<b>Cassiopeia Black</b> (<a href="/wiki/1915" title="1915">1915</a> – <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, the eldest daughter of <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_II" title="Cygnus Black II">Cygnus Black II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Violetta_Bulstrode" title="Violetta Bulstrode">Violetta Black</a> (née Bulstrode) and sister of <a href="/wiki/Pollux_Black" title="Pollux Black">Pollux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marius_Black" title="Marius Black">Marius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dorea_Black" title="Dorea Black">Dorea</a>. Cassiopeia never married or had any children.<sup id="cite_ref-BFT_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_Black?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Based on a statement by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, Cassiopeia may well have been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>.  She attended Hogwarts c. <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> - <a href="/wiki/1933" title="1933">1933</a>. Slughorn became Potions Master in 1931 when she would likely have been in her fifth or sixth year.</li>
<li>Since Cassiopeia and <a href="/wiki/Callidora_Black" title="Callidora Black">Callidora</a> were born in the same year, it is likely that they were classmates at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>.</li>
<li>Cassiopeia is a queen in Greek mythology, and her daughter is Andromeda. Another relative was <a href="/wiki/Andromeda_Tonks" title="Andromeda Tonks">Andromeda Tonks</a>, a grand-niece of Cassiopeia Black.</li>
<li>Cassiopeia died two years after her brother Pollux and one after their <a href="/wiki/Arcturus_Black_III" title="Arcturus Black III">cousin</a>, and the same year as <a href="/wiki/Lucretia_Black" title="Lucretia Black">two</a> <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_III" title="Cygnus Black III">other</a> members of the Black family. At that time, Cassiopeia's grand-nephew (<a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black&#160;III</a>) was in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.</li>
<li>On the family tree featured in the film, her first name is misspelt "Cassiapeia".</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_Black?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like many members of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>, Cassiopeia is named after a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cassiopeia_(constellation)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Cassiopeia (constellation)">constellation</a>. The constellation is in turn named after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cassiopeia_(mythology)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Cassiopeia (mythology)">Queen Cassiopeia</a> of Greek mythology, a beautiful but vain woman whose arrogant boasting led to her downfall. The name is derived from the Greek Κασσιόπη, meaning "she whose words excel".
</p>
