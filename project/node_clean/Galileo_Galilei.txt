<b>Galileo Galilei</b> (<a href="/wiki/15_February" title="15 February">15 February</a> <a href="/wiki/1564" class="mw-redirect" title="1564">1564</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/8_January" title="8 January">8 January</a> <a href="/wiki/1642" class="mw-redirect" title="1642">1642</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> astronomer, physicist, and engineer.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was famous for his improvements to the <a href="/wiki/Telescope" title="Telescope">telescope</a> and for the role he played in making <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/heliocentrism" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:heliocentrism">heliocentrism</a> (the concept that the <a href="/wiki/Orrery" title="Orrery">Earth</a> and other planets revolve around the Sun) the widely-accepted scientific model.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The work of Galileo was among the many topics covered in the book <i><a href="/wiki/A_Brief_History_of_Time" title="A Brief History of Time">A Brief History of Time</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" title="Stephen Hawking">Stephen Hawking</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-poa-film_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poa-film-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
