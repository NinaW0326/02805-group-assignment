<div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>opposition</b> is the political party (or the parties) opposed to the party currently in office in countries with <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westminster_system" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Westminster system">Westminster</a>-based <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Parliament">parliamentary</a> systems such as <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the predecessor of the British <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> was visited by <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>, he thought he was a hoax perpetrated by the opposition, and thus tried to throw him out a window.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
