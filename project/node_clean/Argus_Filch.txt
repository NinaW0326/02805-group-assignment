<dd>&#8212;Filch's sadistic enjoyment when punishing students<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Argus Filch</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Caretaker" title="Hogwarts Caretaker">caretaker</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> since around <a href="/wiki/1973" title="1973">1973</a>. Filch usually wandered around the school corridors with his cat <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a>, trying to catch students breaking the school rules and complaining about <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> the <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, after Mrs Norris was <a href="/wiki/Petrification" title="Petrification">petrified</a> by <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Salazar Slytherin's Basilisk</a>, Filch was furious; however, his cat was healed by the end of the year. In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> became High Inquisitor at Hogwarts, Filch gladly supported her. The next year, he was responsible for recognising <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark</a> objects that were brought into Hogwarts.
</p><p>Filch helped the students evacuate during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> and participated in the battle himself. He kept his job of caretaker after the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. Filch was also the only one of the Hogwarts staff to stay at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> during the summer holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Little is known about Filch's early life. He was born to at least one <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> parent sometime before <a href="/wiki/1951" title="1951">1951</a><sup id="cite_ref-birth_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birth-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British Isles</a>. Despite his magical parentage, Filch was a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>, meaning he could not perform <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS9_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_at_Hogwarts">Career at Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career at Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career at Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Haven't you been complaining about <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> for a quarter of a century?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, in 1998, relays for how long Filch has been working at Hogwarts<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In around <a href="/wiki/1968" title="1968">1968</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HM_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Filch joined the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> succeeding <a href="/wiki/Apollyon_Pringle" title="Apollyon Pringle">Apollyon Pringle</a> as <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Caretaker" title="Hogwarts Caretaker">Caretaker</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF31_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF31-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Filch waged a constant war against the students and <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a>, the school's resident <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">poltergeist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He disliked the students and constantly hassled them over the littlest infraction and took delight in the doling out of detention-worthy write-ups.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> By the end of the <a href="/wiki/1970s" title="1970s">1970s</a>, Filch had already filled at least one thousand and fifty-six boxes with punishment records.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Filch frequently patrolled the hallways at night-time, hunting down students wandering out of bed and looking for excuses to give them <a href="/wiki/Discipline_at_Hogwarts#Detention" title="Discipline at Hogwarts">detentions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In his years as Caretaker, Filch learned all about the Castle's <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_secret_passages" title="Hogwarts secret passages">secret passageways and hidden doors</a> better than anyone but the <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Weasley twins</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Marauders" title="Marauders">Marauders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Filch lived in a dingy and windowless <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch%27s_Office" class="mw-redirect" title="Argus Filch&#39;s Office">office</a> containing filing cabinets with detailed records of all the students Filch had disciplined. A highly polished collection of chains and manacles hung on the wall behind Filch's desk. He always begged Headmaster <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> to let him suspend students by their ankles from the ceiling.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 1970s, Filch did many write-ups of <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, occasionally accompanied by the names of <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the 1977-1978 school year, Filch, probably on a tip-off from <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, whose obsession it had become to expose his arch-rival, <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a> in wrongdoing, confiscated the <a href="/wiki/Marauder%27s_Map" title="Marauder&#39;s Map">Marauder's Map</a> after the Marauders over-reached themselves and were cornered.<sup id="cite_ref-PottermoreMarauder.27sMap_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PottermoreMarauder.27sMap-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Filch stored the map in a cabinet marked "<i>Confiscated and Highly Dangerous</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-PA10_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA10-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Although Filch was a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a> and could not make the map work properly,<sup id="cite_ref-PA10_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA10-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> he did know that it was called the Marauder's Map.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5CH4_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5CH4-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1980s"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/1984" title="1984">1984</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>, Filch had <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a>, his cat, following <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> every time he went up into the Castle.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1984%E2%80%931985_school_year" title="1984–1985 school year">1984–1985 school year</a>, Filch and Mrs Norris found the <a href="/wiki/Cursed_ice" title="Cursed ice">cursed ice</a>. Suspecting it was linked to the <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vaults</a>, he was about to report to Dumbledore, but spotted Professor <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> first and told him instead. He brought the Professor to the corridor where he found the ice.<sup id="cite_ref-HM16_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM16-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During this time, the Marauder's Map was stolen from his office by a thief that he suspected to be Peeves.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5CH4_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5CH4-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Map was later brought back to Filch's office and kept there until <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a> 'nicked' it during their first year (1989-1990 school year) at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry after being hauled to his office for letting off a <a href="/wiki/Dungbomb" title="Dungbomb">Dungbomb</a> in a corridor.<sup id="cite_ref-PA10_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA10-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Harry used it that night to sneak into the <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Section" title="Restricted Section">Restricted Section</a> and research about <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel" title="Nicolas Flamel">Nicolas Flamel</a>, which he knew was related to the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>. After he opened <a href="/wiki/Shrieking_book" title="Shrieking book">a book</a> that screamed, Filch arrived on the scene almost immediately but was unable to see Harry. Filch warned Snape at once and Harry, while trying to avoid them, hid in an <a href="/wiki/Disused_classroom" title="Disused classroom">unused classroom</a> and stumbled into the <a href="/wiki/Mirror_of_Erised" title="Mirror of Erised">Mirror of Erised</a> for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>During the school year, Filch tried to improve his status as a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a> using a <a href="/wiki/Kwikspell" title="Kwikspell">Kwikspell</a> course. Of course, as Filch had no inherent magical powers, this was of little use. After the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Quidditch_team" title="Gryffindor Quidditch team">Gryffindor Quidditch team</a>'s practice on 24 October, Mrs Norris saw Harry Potter who was covered in soil and dirt. Soon, Filch discovered Harry and took him to his office; however, before he could punish Harry, Filch heard a noise and realised that <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> had damaged a very valuable <a href="/wiki/Vanishing_Cabinet" title="Vanishing Cabinet">Vanishing Cabinet</a>. He left Harry and ran to find Peeves, excited that he might finally be able to pursuade Dumbledore to kick Peeves out of Hogwarts. However, when he returned to his office, he learned that Harry had read his letter about Kwikspell courses. Filch was furious and kicked Harry out off his office, trying to convince him that the letter was not his, and ordering him not to tell anyone.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<p>During the school year, a mysterious, dangerous monster roamed around the Castle and tried to attack muggle-born students. The first individual to be attacked was Mrs Norris, Filch's cat, whom he had a very close relationship with. The cat was found <a href="/wiki/Petrification" title="Petrification">petrified</a> under a message written on the wall saying that "<i>THE CHAMBER OF SECRETS HAS BEEN OPENED. ENEMIES OF THE HEIR, BEWARE</i>", causing Filch tremendous stress and concern: he believed that Harry Potter was the one who petrified her, as he knew that Filch was a Squib. <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> agreed with Filch, not because he had the same opinion, but because he wanted Harry to be punished. Filch shouted in a hysterical voice about all the work he would have mopping up all night, and ran to Headmaster Dumbledore slamming a door behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-CS13_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS13-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>

<p>In early February, <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Professor Sprout</a> kindly assured Filch she would have <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a> back in no time, as the <a href="/wiki/Mandrake" title="Mandrake">Mandrakes</a>, who were maturing for the Mandrake Restorative Draught, were already moody, secretive, and with acne.<sup id="cite_ref-CS13_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS13-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Filch was also present when in a corridor with all the other staff when a new message from the Heir of Slytherin appeared on the wall stating that Ginny had been taken into the chamber and that her skeleton will lie there forever.
</p><p>At the end of the year, Filch attended the End-of-Term Feast, reunited with his cat.<sup id="cite_ref-CSf36_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSf36-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> His cat was cured via the administration of Professor Sprout’s  <a href="/wiki/Mandrake_Restorative_Draught" title="Mandrake Restorative Draught">Mandrake Restorative Draught</a>, brewed with Professor Sprout's Mandrakes.<sup id="cite_ref-CS18_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS18-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He learned that the one who petrified Mrs Norris was the memory of Tom Riddle, the one who controlled <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Salazar Slytherin's Basilisk</a>.
</p>

<p>During the next school year, the alleged mass-murderer <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> escaped from <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> and the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> stationed <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> around the entrances to the grounds in order to protect the school and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, which was thought to be Black's target.<sup id="cite_ref-PA5_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA5-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 September, 1993, Filch attended the Start-of-Term Feast, and stood in the raised platform by the High Table while the school choir and orchestra led a presentation of <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Trouble" title="Double Trouble">Something Wicked This Way Comes</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-PAf8_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAf8-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 31 October, Filch stood by the <a href="/wiki/Entrance_Hall" title="Entrance Hall">Entrance Hall</a> checking the names of those going to the first <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade_weekend_trips" title="Hogsmeade weekend trips">Hogsmeade trip</a> of the term. He later told Harry Potter off for wandering around on his own, which he thought suspicious.<sup id="cite_ref-PA8_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA8-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Later in the evening of that same day, Sirius Black broke into the Castle and tried to enter <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a>; this resulted in the destruction of the Fat Lady's portrait.<sup id="cite_ref-PA8_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA8-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Filch helped secure the Castle on <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Professor Dumbledore</a>'s instruction, lowering the portcullis and bolting the front doors of the Clock Tower.<sup id="cite_ref-PAf14_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAf14-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Filch searched both the dungeons,<sup id="cite_ref-PA8_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA8-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_Tower" title="Astronomy Tower">Astronomy Tower</a> and the Owlery,<sup id="cite_ref-PAf14_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAf14-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> but found no sign of him. Filch was also charged with restoring the Fat Lady's portrait,<sup id="cite_ref-PA8_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA8-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and he did so expertly.<sup id="cite_ref-PA14_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA14-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>That same year, Harry Potter was given the <a href="/wiki/Marauder%27s_Map" title="Marauder&#39;s Map">Marauder's Map</a> by Fred and George Weasley, using it to sneak into <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade Village</a> and walk throughout the castle, without Filch noticing him.<sup id="cite_ref-PA10_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA10-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December, Filch attended the Christmas feast, in which he departed with his usual brown coat and wore a very old and rather mouldy-looking tailcoat.<sup id="cite_ref-PA11_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA11-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Sirius Black's second break-in in the evening of 5 February, Filch bustled around the corridors boarding up everything from tiny cracks in the walls to mouse holes.<sup id="cite_ref-PA14_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA14-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Before the start of the school term, Filch extended the list of forbidden objects within the school premises so that it included four hundred and thirty-seven items, including <a href="/wiki/Screaming_Yo-yo" title="Screaming Yo-yo">Screaming Yo-yos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fanged_Frisbee" title="Fanged Frisbee">Fanged Frisbees</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ever-Bashing_Boomerang" title="Ever-Bashing Boomerang">Ever-Bashing Boomerangs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF12_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF12-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>

<p>That school year, the Triwizard Tournament took place at Hogwarts. Delegations of students from <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons Academy of Magic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang Institute</a> came to Hogwarts in order to watch the Tournament and sign up to be chosen as one of the champions.<sup id="cite_ref-GF12_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF12-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GF15_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF15-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October, Filch underwent an extra-thorough cleaning of the Castle and acted ferociously to any students who forgot to wipe their shoes. On one occasion, he terrified a pair of first-year girls into hysterics.<sup id="cite_ref-GF15_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF15-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Hallowe%27en_feast" class="mw-redirect" title="Hallowe&#39;en feast">Hallowe'en feast</a> on 31 October, Filch wore his mouldy old tailcoat and added chairs to accommodate <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Olympe Maxime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Bagman" title="Ludovic Bagman">Ludovic Bagman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Bartemius Crouch Senior</a> at the Staff Table. Filch, later, brought the jewel-encrusted casket with the <a href="/wiki/Goblet_of_Fire" title="Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a> inside.<sup id="cite_ref-GF16_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF16-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On several occasions in December, Filch had to take <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> from inside suits of armour, where he had taken to hide, singing Christmas carols with very rude lyrics of his own invention.<sup id="cite_ref-GF22_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF22-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>In the night of 21 January, 1995, Filch almost caught Harry Potter while he was on his way back to <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a> after having been in the Prefects' bathroom trying to solve the <a href="/wiki/Golden_egg" title="Golden egg">golden egg</a> riddle for the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. Harry sank into one of the trick steps and got stuck in it, making his golden egg break open and wail. Hearing this, Filch thought that Peeves stole the egg. This made him even more hopeful that Dumbledore would kick Peeves out of Hogwarts for it. Filch was then joined by <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> when the two got hold of Harry's egg and the <a href="/wiki/Marauder%27s_Map" title="Marauder&#39;s Map">Marauder's Map</a>. </p><p>At the end of the year, champion <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> was murdered, while everyone learned from Harry about <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s return. The <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> began.<sup id="cite_ref-GF35_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF35-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span id="1995–1996_school_year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1995.E2.80.931996_school_year">1995–1996 school year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1995–1996 school year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 1995–1996 school year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>After <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s return, and as part of its campaign to discredit Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter, the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> instated Dolores Umbridge as <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor.<sup id="cite_ref-OP11_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP11-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the start of the school term, Filch fastened the list of forbidden items to his office door in an attempt to make students read it. He also told <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Professor Dumbledore</a>, in what he said was the four-hundred-and-sixty second time, to remind the students that magic was not permitted in corridors between classes.<sup id="cite_ref-OP11_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP11-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Filch attended the <a href="/wiki/Start-of-Term_Feast" title="Start-of-Term Feast">Start-of-Term Feast</a> in the evening of 1 September, 1995 in which he stood by the double doors leading to the <a href="/wiki/Entrance_Hall" title="Entrance Hall">Entrance Hall</a>, and listened attentively to Dolores Umbridge's speech. He apparently realised the Ministry of Magic was going to interfere at Hogwarts that school year, and clapped enthusiastically.<sup id="cite_ref-OPf_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OPf-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Umbridge was a sadistic woman who, like Filch, enjoyed torturing and punishing students. Thus, the caretaker started to support Umbridge and follow her orders.
</p><p>On 7 September, Filch got a tip-off that Harry Potter was sending in a massive order of Dungbombs. When he got to the <a href="/wiki/Owlery" title="Owlery">Owlery</a>, he found Harry Potter (who had just sent a letter for <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a> (who was sending her mother a parcel for her birthday).<sup id="cite_ref-OP14_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP14-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> later speculated that this was Umbridge's doing, as she probably needed an excuse to read Harry's post.<sup id="cite_ref-OP18_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP18-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> But Cho vouched for Harry and stated that he was not ordering Dungbombs.
</p>

<p>With the passing of <a href="/wiki/Educational_Decree_Number_Twenty-Three" title="Educational Decree Number Twenty-Three">Educational Decree Number Twenty-Three</a>, which appointed Dolores Umbridge to the post of High Inquisitor of Hogwarts, the Ministry effectively started its interference at the school.<sup id="cite_ref-OP15_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP15-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Umbridge started passing Educational Decrees to suppress and outlaw behaviour of which the Ministry did not approve.<sup id="cite_ref-OP17_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP17-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the morning before the first school trip to <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> of the school year, Filch matched the names of those visiting the village to the long list of students who had permission from their parents or guardian to do so. When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> reached Filch, the caretaker gave a great sniff as though to detect a whiff of <a href="/wiki/Dungbomb" title="Dungbomb">Dungbombs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP16_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP16-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On 7 October, Filch hung a copy of the <a href="/wiki/Educational_Decree_Number_Twenty-Four" title="Educational Decree Number Twenty-Four">Educational Decree Number Twenty-Four</a> on the Entrance Hall.<sup id="cite_ref-OPf_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OPf-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He was also probably the one to affix the notices with the Decree on the noticeboards of the Common Rooms.<sup id="cite_ref-OP17_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP17-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Filch also assisted Umbridge when she had Trelawney sacked, something he most likely took pleasure in, as it caused Trelawney great stress and reduced her to tears.
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine&#39;s Day">Valentine's Day</a>, 1996, Harry Potter and Cho Chang departed for Hogsmeade together. Filch signed the students out in a queue in the Entrance Hall.<sup id="cite_ref-OP25_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP25-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Later that same month, after <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Quidditch_team" title="Gryffindor Quidditch team">Gryffindor</a>'s abysmal performance in their <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff_Quidditch_team" title="Hufflepuff Quidditch team">Hufflepuff</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> sang <i>Weasley is Our King</i> so loudly and frequently that Filch banned the song from the corridors out of sheer irritation.<sup id="cite_ref-OP26_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP26-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In the evening of 20 April, 1996, <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a> denounced the unsanctioned Dumbledore's Army to Dolores Umbridge. Umbridge and her Inquisitorial Squad then managed to catch the students leaving the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a> and Harry Potter, head of the organisation, was taken to the Headmaster's office while joined by Umbridge, Edgecombe, Deputy Headmistress <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, the Minister for Magic <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> and his Junior Assistant <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a>, and two <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a>.
</p><p>Professor Dumbledore took full responsibility for the group, and was to be escorted to the Ministry, where he would be formally charged, and then sent to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> to await trial for conspiracy and sedition. Dumbledore then knocked down the Ministry officials, and left the school undetected.<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The Ministry passed the <a href="/wiki/Educational_Decree_Number_Twenty-Eight" title="Educational Decree Number Twenty-Eight">Educational Decree Number Twenty-Eight</a> overnight, making Dolores Umbridge Headmistress of Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Under Umbridge, Filch walked around in an extremely good mood. He hummed creakily under his breath, under the prospect of the changes Umbridge had announced him. He was going to be permitted to bring back the "old punishments" and give students whippings, and she asked the Minister for Magic to sign an order for the expulsion of Peeves, the resident <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> However, the counterattack against Umbridge was now in full force: much of the student body opposed Umbridge and Filch.
</p><p>On the former's first afternoon as Headmistress, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a> set off an enormous crate of Weasleys' Wildfire Whiz-bangs which spread all over the school. At the end of the day, Umbridge was dishevelled, sweaty-faced and soot-blackened from trying to extinguish the fireworks, and Filch set one of his brooms ablaze when he tried to swat at the fireworks in midair.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Harry Potter helped <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a> mess up the clock in the Clock Tower, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Creevey" title="Colin Creevey">Colin Creevey</a> sent the Inquisitorial Squad several rude Howlers in chocolate boxes, and <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati</a> and <a href="/wiki/Padma_Patil" title="Padma Patil">Padma Patil</a> poured <a href="/wiki/Babbling_Beverage" title="Babbling Beverage">Babbling Beverage</a> into the loudspeakers.<sup id="cite_ref-OPvg_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OPvg-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sometime after the Easter holidays, Fred and George Weasley set off a <a href="/wiki/Portable_Swamp" title="Portable Swamp">Portable Swamp</a> on the Fifth Floor Corridor, and left the school in a blaze of glory.<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> They did not leave instructions on how to remove the swamp and as such, Umbridge and Filch tried fruitlessly different means of removing it. The other teachers, who could have removed the swamp in an instant, preferred instead to watch Umbridge struggle. The area was roped off, and Filch was given the task of getting students across it to their classrooms in a small punt.<sup id="cite_ref-OP30_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP30-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Inspired by the twins' legendary flight to freedom, a great number of students wreaked havoc in the school: <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jordan" title="Lee Jordan">Lee Jordan</a><sup id="cite_ref-OP31_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP31-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> put <a href="/wiki/Niffler" title="Niffler">Nifflers</a> into Umbridge's office, which promptly tore the place apart while searching for shiny objects and leapt on Umbridge when she entered, trying to gnaw the rings off her fingers. Dungbombs and <a href="/wiki/Stink_Pellets" title="Stink Pellets">Stink Pellets</a> were frequently dropped in the corridors, so that students performed <a href="/wiki/Bubble-Head_Charm" title="Bubble-Head Charm">Bubble-Head Charms</a> on themselves to get a supply of fresh air.<sup id="cite_ref-OP30_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP30-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Due to all those misdeeds, Filch prowled the corridors with a horsewhip ready in his hands, desperate to catch students in the act, but the problem was that there were now so many of them, he never knew which way to turn. He was aided by the Inquisitorial Squad, but odd things like antlers sprouting from their heads kept happening to its members.<sup id="cite_ref-OP30_53-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP30-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>No one, however, could compete with the master of chaos, <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a>. On two occasions, he shut <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a> inside a suit of armour, from which she was rescued by a furious Filch, yowling loudly.<sup id="cite_ref-OP30_53-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP30-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_in_the_Forbidden_Forest" title="Skirmish in the Forbidden Forest">skirmish</a> between Umbridge and the Forbidden Forest centaur herd and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> on 18 June, Umbridge was confined to the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a> in what <a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey" title="Poppy Pomfrey">Madam Pomfrey</a> said was a case of shock. Umbridge left <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts</a> the day before the end of term, having crept out during dinnertime in hope to depart undetected. She, however, met Peeves on the way, who chased her from the premises, whacking her alternately with Minerva McGonagall's walking stick and a sock full of chalk as the students and teachers watched.<sup id="cite_ref-OP38_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP38-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Filch brought her her luggage, as she was stopped by a mob of <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> reporters who took pictures of her escape.<sup id="cite_ref-OPf_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OPf-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Filch was reportedly miserable, as he thought Umbridge was the best thing that ever happened to Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-OP38_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP38-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Umbridge's Educational Decrees were abolished, as well as her <a href="/wiki/Approval_for_Whipping" title="Approval for Whipping">Approval for Whipping</a>, as Albus Dumbledore was reinstated as Headmaster, meaning that he never got the opportunity to inflict corporal punishment on the students. 
</p>

<p>Now in open war, upon the students' arrival at the school on 1 September, 1996, Filch was charged with checking the students and their possessions for dark objects with Secrecy Sensors. Filch confiscated a shrunken head from <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a>. Filch also had the responsibility of checking all the owls to prevent potentially harmful objects from entering the premises.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP11_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP11-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> During the Start-of-Term Feast, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> informed the students that Filch had asked him to remind the them that there was a blanket ban on any joke items bought at <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a>'s shop, <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wizard_Wheezes" title="Weasleys&#39; Wizard Wheezes">Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP8_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP8-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Filch, however, was hoodwinked by Fred and George's Owl order service, in which their products came disguised as perfumes and cough potions.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s discovery of the Half-Blood Prince's <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape%27s_copy_of_Advanced_Potion-Making" title="Severus Snape&#39;s copy of Advanced Potion-Making">copy</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Advanced_Potion-Making" title="Advanced Potion-Making">Advanced Potion-Making</a></i>, he learnt several new spells such as <i><a href="/wiki/Langlock" title="Langlock">Langlock</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinx</a> that glued the victim's tongue to the roof of the mouth. Harry cast this jinx twice, to general applause, on an unsuspecting Argus Filch.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October, Filch stood as usual in the Entrance Hall checking the names of students who had permission to go in a Hogsmeade weekend trip. The process took longer than normal as Filch triple-checked everybody with his Secrecy Sensor. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> demanded to know what did it matter if the students were smuggling Dark objects out of the premises, and his cheek earned him a few jabs with the Sensor.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> When the students arrived after the attack on <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a>, who had touched a cursed necklace, Filch held his secrecy sensor aloft, but was dismissed by <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> who told him to take the necklace, wrapped in a scarf, to <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, the school's expert on <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_59-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 19 December, Harry Potter and Hermione Granger argued and speculated whether or not Filch and <a href="/wiki/Irma_Pince" title="Irma Pince">Madam Pince</a> were secretly in love with each other.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In the evening of the Slug Club Christmas party, on 20 December, Filch caught <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> lurking in an upstairs corridor, most likely going to the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a> to repair the <a href="/wiki/Vanishing_Cabinet" title="Vanishing Cabinet">Vanishing Cabinet</a>. In an excuse to escape punishment, Draco told Filch he had been invited to <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Professor Slughorn</a>'s party, and Filch dragged him by the ear to Slughorn's office to see if his claim was true. Once there, Slughorn said that he had not issued him an invitation. Draco then told Filch that he was trying to gatecrash. Filch said that this did not make him happy, but the look on his face seemed to say otherwise, but as it was Christmas, Slughorn let Draco stay with no punishment.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_58-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the evening of 1 March, 1997, as <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> was escorting Harry Potter and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a> where <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> stayed after ingesting poisoned mead, Filch came across them threatening the students with detention. Hagrid vouched for them, but Filch rudely undermined Hagrid's authority as a teacher, with Hagrid insulting Filch for being a Squib in retaliation. and the two argued loudly as Harry and Hermione hurried off on Hagrid's instruction.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 March, Filch did his usual prodding act with the Secrecy Sensor as some of the sixth years departed the school for an <a href="/wiki/Apparition_(class)" title="Apparition (class)">Apparition</a> lesson at <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP21_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP21-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>Filch had been looking for someone to clear out his old punishment files, and when Severus Snape put Harry Potter in detention for hurting Draco Malfoy, he put Harry to work by making him copying out the misdeeds and punishments afresh and making sure that they were in alphabetical order without the aid of magic.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 30 June, 1997, a small gang of <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> managed to penetrate the magical protections around the Castle with the help of <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> and an assault took place at the <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_Tower" title="Astronomy Tower">Astronomy Tower</a> and the corridors beneath it. Severus Snape joined them, and killed Albus Dumbledore atop the Tower<sup id="cite_ref-HBP27_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP27-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> (in fact, Snape was a double agent and was working on Dumbledore's orders, meaning since Dumbledore was already slowly dying, Snape's killing of him was a mercy killing).<sup id="cite_ref-DH33_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH33-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>

<p>On 1 August, 1997, the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> was taken over by Lord Voldemort's <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> By 1 September, 1997, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> was made Headmaster of Hogwarts while his fellow Death Eaters, <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto Carrow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow" title="Amycus Carrow">Amycus Carrow</a> became the <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> teachers respectively, as well as Deputy Heads.<sup id="cite_ref-DH12_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH12-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Carrows were put in charge of all discipline and punishments, which were violent, including beatings and placing students under the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH29_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH29-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> Filch was kept working at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> in his capacity of caretaker. It is unknown what was made of Filch, or even if he was mistreated by the Carrows simply for being a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>. Apparently, Filch lost some of his duties, as punishment seemed to be given out exclusively by the Carrows and Severus Snape.
</p>

<p>In the evening of 1 May, Argus Filch was patrolling the corridors while <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> went to <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH29_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH29-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> Later, after <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> gained knowledge from <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto Carrow</a> that Harry Potter was in the school premises, the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_House" title="Head of House">Heads of House</a> started conjuring protective enchantments around the school, preparing to fight and waking up students. Filch, unaware of what was happening, came to Professor McGonagall shouting that students were out of bed. Frustrated, McGonagall sent him to do something constructive and find <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a>, for the <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a> would be helpful against the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. Though clearly thinking that Professor McGonagall had gone insane, Filch complied.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Later on, Filch and <a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey" title="Poppy Pomfrey">Madam Pomfrey</a> oversaw the evacuation procedure of underage students and of those not willing to fight, via the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> The caretaker stayed in the Castle during the battle,<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> although it is unknown how, as a Squib, he participated in the battle; perhaps he stayed to tend to the fighters' injuries or to aid the Hogwarts forces with his extensive knowledge of the Castle layout and secret passages. During the first stage of the battle, Filch was left with a deep gash in his left arm.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_70-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> During the one hour-long interruption in the battle, Filch was at the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>, where his injuries were treated by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_70-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>

<p>When the Death Eaters marched to Hogwarts Castle with Harry Potter's "dead body", Filch stood by <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Pomona Sprout</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey" title="Poppy Pomfrey">Poppy Pomfrey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> with a gloomy expression on his face.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_70-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> The battle recommenced shortly afterwards, and it all ended when Lord Voldemort was finally defeated and killed by Harry Potter in the Great Hall.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the battle, he returned to his position of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Caretaker" title="Hogwarts Caretaker">caretaker</a>, as he was immediately seen sweeping near the Great Hall.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_70-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> He presumably kept doing what he was used to: punishing the students and arguing with <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> the <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a>, even under the new <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmistress</a> <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>.
</p>

<p>Argus Filch was a <a href="/wiki/Rheumatism" title="Rheumatism">rheumatic</a> man<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> with hunched-shoulders<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyphosis" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Kyphosis">hunchback</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> He had a horrible, pouchy<sup id="cite_ref-GF25_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF25-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> and pasty face<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and bulging, pale eyes,<sup id="cite_ref-PS12_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS12-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> along with sunken, veined cheeks.<sup id="cite_ref-OP14_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP14-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He had long hair with a bald spot on the top of his head and quivery jowls.<sup id="cite_ref-OP14_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP14-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Skinny-ankled,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_60-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Filch would usually wheeze<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and shuffle when walking.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He had knobbly hands and whenever he got the <a href="/wiki/Flu" title="Flu">flu</a>, a bulbous and unusually purple nose.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Filch usually wore a brown coat,<sup id="cite_ref-PA11_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA11-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> although on special occasions he would wear a mouldy-looking tailcoat<sup id="cite_ref-PA11_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA11-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> or an ancient black suit and tie reeking of mothballs.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP30_65-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP30-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> At night, while not patrolling the corridors, Filch wore an old flannel dressing gown.<sup id="cite_ref-GF25_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF25-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">"<i>If Filch’s trying to learn magic from a Kwikspell course, I reckon he must be a Squib. It would explain a lot. Like why he hates students so much. He’s bitter.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>'s explanation of Filch's grouchy personality<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Very foul-tempered, cantankerous, and easily annoyed, Filch had an extremely unpleasant personality. Despite working at a school, Filch deeply resented the students and was greatly disliked by them in return,<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and some members of the staff, as well. However, when called on to oversee the evacuation of the majority of the students before the Battle of Hogwarts, Filch led the children to safety without hesitation.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In keeping with his dislike for students and their inevitable tendency to create messes and disorder (which he, as caretaker, would get stuck cleaning up), Filch idolised "the old ways" of discipline and constantly lamented that he was no longer allowed to use corporal punishment.<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Filch was obsessed with keeping the Castle neat and tidy, and was infuriated when students made a mess with potion ingredients, or forgot to wipe their feet.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was also very domineering, controlling and paranoid, as demonstrated by the numerous times he asked students what they were doing when they were simply wandering around in what he thought was a suspicious manner.<sup id="cite_ref-PA8_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA8-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He seemed to be bitter and embarrassed about not being able to use <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, as was demonstrated when <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> accidentally discovered his <a href="/wiki/Kwikspell" title="Kwikspell">Kwikspell</a> letters in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> also suspected that Filch was always in such a foul mood due to his status as a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>, and thus resented young wizards and witches as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-CS9_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Filch also had a difficulty in recognising <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a>, due to his lack of magical knowledge as he did not notice the <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potions</a> disguised as perfumes and <a href="/wiki/Cough_potion" title="Cough potion">cough potions</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wizard_Wheezes" title="Weasleys&#39; Wizard Wheezes">Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_58-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> nor did he notice the <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoned</a> <a href="/wiki/Mead" title="Mead">mead</a> sent in by <a href="/wiki/Rosmerta" title="Rosmerta">Madam Rosmerta</a> (while under the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> cast by <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>), showing he was not particularly observant despite his obsessive personality. He also did not suspect anything that was from Rosmerta, another factor that would have permitted the necklace and poisoned mead to enter the castle as well as a flaw that Draco attempted to take advantage of.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP27_63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP27-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> His obsession with the castle's cleanliness led him to neglect actual security at times, as he forgot to lock the doors at least one time even in the more dangerous hours, something Slughorn reprimanded him for.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP22_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP22-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Possibly the only being in Hogwarts that Filch actually liked was his beloved <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS9_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She had dust-coloured fur and yellow, lamp-like eyes. Mrs Norris, like her master, was infamously bad-tempered with anyone but Filch himself. The two shared a strong bond that linked them somehow; anytime Mrs Norris spotted someone breaking a rule, Filch would arrive soon afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Filch talked to her and called her "my sweet".<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> When she was <a href="/wiki/Petrification" title="Petrification">Petrified</a> by <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Salazar Slytherin's Basilisk</a>, Filch was devastated and started sobbing, which made <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> feel slightly sorry for him, though he felt a lot more sorry for himself, fearing he might be expelled.<sup id="cite_ref-CS9_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Filch fought a running war with Peeves, the <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a>, and the two were sworn enemies for more than twenty-five years.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Filch never ceased his futile efforts to get Peeves expelled from the castle, and Peeves never stopped taunting Filch. To annoy Filch, Peeves would trap Mrs Norris in <a href="/wiki/Suit_of_armour" title="Suit of armour">suits of armour</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OP30_53-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP30-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> drop chandeliers,<sup id="cite_ref-OP30_53-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP30-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> play tennis against the Castle walls in the middle of the night,<sup id="cite_ref-PS14_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS14-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> stick wads of gum in keyholes<sup id="cite_ref-PA7_72-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA7-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> and caused general mayhem, which Filch was later responsible for fixing and cleaning up. It is presumed that this on-going war between them continued after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>

<p>The twins <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a> had as bad a relationship with Filch as Filch had had with Peeves ever since their first year. Sometime in <a href="/wiki/1989" title="1989">1989</a> or <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>, Fred and George let off a <a href="/wiki/Dungbomb" title="Dungbomb">Dungbomb</a> and were caught by Filch, who took them to his office and threatened them with his usual unpleasant punishments he always had in mind. The twins noticed a filing cabinet marked <i>Confiscated and Highly Dangerous</i> so, curious, they let off another Dungbomb in his office and "nicked" the <a href="/wiki/Marauder%27s_Map" title="Marauder&#39;s Map">Marauder's Map</a>, which was stored inside that drawer, from him.<sup id="cite_ref-PA10_17-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA10-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The caretaker had punished them many times during their Hogwarts years, for their trouble-making. Among his many file cabinets documenting the punishments he handed out over the years, Filch had an entire drawer dedicated to the twins.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Upon the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wizard_Wheezes" title="Weasleys&#39; Wizard Wheezes">Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes</a>, Filch has placed a blanket ban on all the joke items that could be purchased at their shop, but the twins managed to sneak past security by disguising all their <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potions</a> as <a href="/wiki/Cough_potion" title="Cough potion">cough potions</a> and perfumes.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_58-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Although Filch disliked most students, Harry Potter was one of those he disliked most. In 1991, Harry and Ron managed to get on Filch's bad side on their very first day at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a>, when he and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> got lost and inadvertently tried to enter the out-of-bounds <a href="/wiki/Third-floor_corridor" title="Third-floor corridor">third-floor corridor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The next school year, Filch was furious when Harry learnt that he was a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS8_13-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS8-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Then, he believed that Harry was the one who petrified <a href="/wiki/Mrs_Norris" title="Mrs Norris">Mrs Norris</a>, Filch's cat, as he was one of the few people who knew he was a Squib.<sup id="cite_ref-CS9_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During the next years, Harry disliked Filch even more, especially when the caretaker took the side of Dolores Umbridge, during Harry's fifth year.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Due to Harry's dislike of Filch, Harry <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxed</a> him with <i><a href="/wiki/Langlock" title="Langlock">Langlock</a></i> jinx in his <a href="/wiki/Sixth_year" title="Sixth year">sixth year</a>, humiliating Filch much to the amusement of everyone who witnessed it.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_59-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, Filch, in a way, helped Harry Potter and the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> defeat the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, as he helped McGonagall call Peeves during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> During the Battle, when the Death Eaters marched towards the Castle with Harry's "dead body", he was clearly solemn as he stood by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Pomona Sprout</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey" title="Poppy Pomfrey">Poppy Pomfrey</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_70-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> despite all their differences, as his death meant the victory of the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.
</p>

<p>As Dolores Umbridge was a sadist who enjoyed torturing students, Filch could relate to her views on a number of levels.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Also, as Umbridge <a href="/wiki/Approval_for_Whipping" title="Approval for Whipping">granted</a> Filch permission to torture misbehaving students,<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> whereas Dumbledore would never even think of such things, he had loyalty to her.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He was one of the few people who actually liked her at the school.<sup id="cite_ref-OP30_53-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP30-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Umbridge had gone through great lengths to gain Filch's loyalty, such as giving him permission to torture, and to have Peeves dismissed, due to Filch's knowledge around the school's secret passageways being second only to the Weasley twins.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Otherwise, she did have a low opinion of him, telling him not to <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">Stun</a> some <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wildfire_Whiz-bangs" title="Weasleys&#39; Wildfire Whiz-bangs">enchanted fireworks</a> and having it explode all over the place, when it was in fact her own doing.<sup id="cite_ref-OP28_50-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP28-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> was a very strict and somewhat cruel teacher, Filch respected that and acted a loyal subordinate to him.<sup id="cite_ref-PS11_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS11-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> They both very much liked to give students a hard time, unfair punishments, and cruel aspects of such punishments.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_59-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Snape, however, did not show much respect to the caretaker, and simply treated him as a minion.<sup id="cite_ref-GF25_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF25-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, while Snape gave some leniency to students of <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, Filch saw no such mercy with any of the students.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_58-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Filch was also caretaker when Snape was a student at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  The one thing that the two absolutely agreed on was a mutual dislike of Harry Potter.
</p><p>During his tenure as <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a>, Snape kept Filch working at Hogwarts despite his status as a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH29_68-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH29-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown whether this was for his loyalty to Snape, his long years of service to the school, or his ability to catch troublemakers through his extensive knowledge of the Castle's secrets.
</p>

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hogwarts_staff">Hogwarts staff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=24" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hogwarts staff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Hogwarts staff"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Filch's relationship with most professors was unknown. The only thing that is known is that he respected many of them, and did what they say without question.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_58-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_59-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Most of the staff appeared to dislike Filch's grumpy and antipathetic personality, such as <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, who could often be irritated by his behaviour,<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, who intended to report him for being more concerned about cleaning up than with the students' security.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP22_62-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP22-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Slughorn seemed to have, however, put their differences aside when he tended to Filch's injuries during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt2_70-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt2-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> Others, like <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Pomona Sprout</a>, were sympathetic and kind towards him.<sup id="cite_ref-CS13_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS13-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Filch also appeared to respect Headmaster <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> very much. Dumbledore appeared to like Filch, although the latter was unpleasant. When Filch's cat, Mrs Norris, was petrified, Dumbledore seemed to care about Filch and his cat.<sup id="cite_ref-CS9_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, Filch did always plead to Dumbledore to permit him to use corporeal punishments on the students, a request that was never granted, which became a primary reason why Filch preferred Umbridge as head of the school.
</p><p>Filch disliked <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>, the Gamekeeper and later <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> professor.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_60-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Filch thought that Hagrid was an oaf, and he also told Mrs Norris, his cat, to watch Hagrid when he entered the castle.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Filch also did not care for Hagrid as a professor and treated him as if he were inferior, possibly because he was half-giant.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_60-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Hagrid, on his side, insulted Filch, calling him "<i>that old git</i>",<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_12-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and a "<i>sneakin' Squib</i>", when the latter insulted him.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_60-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?veaction=edit&amp;section=25" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch?action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Argus" comes from Greek mythology, where it was the name of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argus_Panoptes" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Argus Panoptes">a giant with one hundred eyes</a> who served the goddess <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hera" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hera">Hera</a> as a watchman (much like Mr Filch's role in patrolling the hallways of Hogwarts). His surname, "Filch", is an English word meaning "to steal", usually in a sneaky manner.
</p>

<ul><li>Filch is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> actor <a href="/wiki/David_Bradley" title="David Bradley">David Bradley</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">Goblet of Fire</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Half-Blood Prince</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>.</li>
<li>Filch's role in the films is much the same as his role in the books, though in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">fourth movie</a>, he was used more as a comic relief character. He was seen setting off the <a href="/wiki/Cannon" title="Cannon">cannon</a> to signal the start of a zarre manner. He was also present with Mrs Norris as Professor McGonagall conducted a dance lesson, and played the tune "<a href="/wiki/Neville%27s_Waltz" title="Neville&#39;s Waltz">Neville's Waltz</a>" from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Wizard_Waltz" title="Wizard Waltz">Wizard Waltz</a></i> for her. Similarly, in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">fifth movie</a>, Filch is constantly making unsuccessful attempts to uncover <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, and is jinxed with <a href="/wiki/Fever_Fudge" title="Fever Fudge">exploding boils</a> by <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Fred and George Weasley</a>. In the final film instalment, Filch is once again a more comedic role: screaming that students are out of bed shortly after the <a href="/wiki/Ousting_of_Severus_Snape" title="Ousting of Severus Snape">ousting of Severus Snape</a>, and attempting to sweep a huge pile of rubble with his broom immediately after the Battle.</li>
<li>The films omit Peeves the Poltergeist, and so Filch's ongoing war with him is omitted also.</li>
<li>Filch's status as a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a> is not mentioned for most of the films — the scene when Harry discovers it in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i> was deleted from the final cut. In the sixth film, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, Draco refers to the caretaker's Squib status when Filch catches him in the corridor and brings him into <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Slughorn's Christmas party</a>.</li>
<li>In the early <i>Harry Potter</i> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">films</a>, Filch is seen supporting the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin_Quidditch_team" title="Slytherin Quidditch team">Slytherin Quidditch team</a> during matches.</li>
<li>Filch plays a minor role in <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>. He is generally just an obstacle, introduced in "The Restricted Section" level. In most levels involving Filch, he would walk around waving his lantern, unless Mrs Norris, his cat, meowed at the player. This would cause Filch to run up to the player who attracted Mrs Norris and strangle them. While being attacked, the player must switch characters and jinx the cat, causing Filch to run away and search the nearest exit. In one bonus level, Filch and Ron must work on a team to make a <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a>, and get a Gold Brick from a <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>-Only Door. Being a Squib, Filch cannot do any magic and has only two actions: mêlée with his lantern, and unleash Mrs Norris, who can dig and jump over far areas.</li>
<li>Despite the fact that Filch never directly participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> in the book, he appears to have participated in some measure in the film. In a deleted scene, he was seen locking up all of the Slytherin students in the dungeons, although they were all freed when the dungeons were damaged in an explosion. He is seen later being tended by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, where Filch apparently suffered a significant gash to his arm.</li>
<li>In the PC version of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (video game)">video game adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, when Harry is escaping from the library after seeing the Mirror of Erised, he claims to have "locked the only door out of there". Said door (actually a secret passage concealed behind a bookcase) is obviously magically locked. How Filch accomplished this is unclear, given his status as a Squib.</li>
<li>Interestingly, Filch's love of medieval punishments would finally be achieved when the actor who played him in the films, David Bradley, would go on to play Lord Walder Frey in the famous <i>Game of Thrones</i> television series. In the show, Frey was a cruel, grouchy, temperamental man (much like Filch), and committed one of the most violent acts on television, the Red Wedding, where he oversaw the brutal murders of Robb Stark and his army. He also had Catelyn Stark killed, whose actress, <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Fairley" title="Michelle Fairley">Michelle Fairley</a>, incidentally also played Hermione Granger's <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger%27s_mother" title="Hermione Granger&#39;s mother">mother</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>.
<ul><li>This role is also referenced in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_Hogwarts_Mystery" title="Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery">Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery</a></i>, where Filch muttered to himself that in another life, he would be a Lord.</li></ul></li></ul>
