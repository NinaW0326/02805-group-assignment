<b>Georgios Xenakis</b> was a Greek wizard and a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_referee" title="Quidditch referee">referee</a>. He officiated in the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_National_Quidditch_Stadium" title="Bulgarian National Quidditch Stadium">Bulgaria</a> versus <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_National_Quidditch_team" title="New Zealand National Quidditch team">New Zealand</a> first round encounter at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="2014 Quidditch World Cup">2014 Quidditch World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Georgios_Xenakis?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Georgios_Xenakis?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>If this individual's name is indication of anything, he is probably <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> or of Greek descent — the name "Georgios" is the Greek form of George.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The surname Xenakis is also Greek, from <i>ξένος ['ksenos]</i> ("stranger", "foreigner", but also "friend from far away"). Greek artist <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantin_Xenakis" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Constantin Xenakis">Constantin Xenakis</a> (born 1931), Greek composer and architect <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iannis_Xenakis" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Iannis Xenakis">Iannis Xenakis</a> (1922–2001), and Greek gymnast <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Xenakis" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Thomas Xenakis">Thomas Xenakis</a> (1875–1942) are some notable people who bore this surname.</li></ul>
