<dd>&#8212;Xenophilius Lovegood's bizarre appearance <sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Xenophilius "Xeno" Lovegood</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who worked as the editor of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i> magazine, the husband of <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Lovegood" title="Pandora Lovegood">Pandora</a>, and the father of <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>. His wife died when their only child was nine years old, and he raised his daughter by himself. He was likely the source of his daughterâ€™s eccentric beliefs, although he was often correct in his assumptions. He believed in the existence of the Deathly Hallows and supported <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> in <i>The Quibbler</i> when few others would.
</p><p>Xenophilius and Luna lived in a <a href="/wiki/Lovegood_House" title="Lovegood House">house</a> shaped like a rook chess piece near the village of <a href="/wiki/Ottery_St_Catchpole" title="Ottery St Catchpole">Ottery St Catchpole</a> and were friends with the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a>. In the summer of <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, Xenophilius and Luna attended <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">wedding</a> at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">the Burrow</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Luna was kidnapped by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. To save her, Xenophilius betrayed Harry Potter to Death Eaters. Harry, accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> managed to escape. Lovegood was temporarily imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> by the Death Eaters.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, he left the prison and continued editing <i>The Quibbler</i>. When Luna married <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Scamander" title="Rolf Scamander">Rolf Scamander</a>, Xenophilius had two grandsons, <a href="/wiki/Lorcan_Scamander" title="Lorcan Scamander">Lorcan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lysander_Scamander" title="Lysander Scamander">Lysander</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/4f/Xenophilius_Lovegood_standing_on_stairs_in_house.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170719202952" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/4f/Xenophilius_Lovegood_standing_on_stairs_in_house.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20170719202952" decoding="async" width="250" height="116" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Xenophilius Lovegood standing on stairs in house.jpg" data-image-key="Xenophilius_Lovegood_standing_on_stairs_in_house.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Xenophilius_Lovegood_standing_on_stairs_in_house.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Xenophilius Lovegood standing inside his unusual home</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Xenophilius was born into the <a href="/wiki/Lovegood_family" title="Lovegood family">Lovegood family</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> where he was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>. At an unknown time he married <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Lovegood" title="Pandora Lovegood">Pandora Lovegood</a> with whom he had a daughter, <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>. He raised Luna himself after an accident during magical experimentation took Pandora's life.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP38_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP38-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Xenophilius created a magazine called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>, in which he wrote about his obscure beliefs, including the <a href="/wiki/Crumple-Horned_Snorkack" title="Crumple-Horned Snorkack">Crumple-Horned Snorkack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP10_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP13_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP13-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point during his life, Xenophilius attended one of <a href="/wiki/Eldred_Worple" title="Eldred Worple">Eldred Worple</a>'s book signings. He presumably shared his bizarre beliefs with Worple, not to mention his choice of clothing and accessories, as the latter remembered him as a "seriously <i>unbalanced</i> man".<sup id="cite_ref-script_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-script-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quidditch_World_Cup">Quidditch World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Quidditch World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Quidditch World Cup"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Xenophilius and Luna watched the final of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a>; <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> vs <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF6_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF6-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Quibbler">The Quibbler</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Quibbler">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Quibbler"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Xenophilius published <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s story about the <a href="/wiki/24_May" title="24 May">night</a> he saw <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">return</a> and sent Harry a free copy.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP25_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP25-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This was the fastest-selling edition of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>, even being reprinted at least once before Mr Lovegood sold the story to the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Lovegood_family" title="Lovegood family">The Lovegoods</a> used the money received from selling the story to the <i>Prophet</i> to travel to <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> over the summer to search for a <a href="/wiki/Crumple-Horned_Snorkack" title="Crumple-Horned Snorkack">Crumple-Horned Snorkack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP38_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP38-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a> became <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, Xenophilius planned to print a story in <i>The Quibbler</i> stating that Scrimgeour was in fact a <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a>. However the Ministry stepped in and prevented him from printing the story; Xenophilius believed that this was because the Ministry did not want the truth to get out.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As an adult, Luna would soon come to realise the truth about her father, and accepted that some of his creatures did not exist. She married <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Scamander" title="Rolf Scamander">Rolf Scamander</a>, grandson of the famous <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a>, and the couple had twins, <a href="/wiki/Lorcan_Scamander" title="Lorcan Scamander">Lorcan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lysander_Scamander" title="Lysander Scamander">Lysander</a>, thus making Xenophilius a grandfather.
</p><p>It is not known what happened to Xenophilius beyond this, except that when his daughter travelled to the <a href="/wiki/Patagonian_Desert" title="Patagonian Desert">Patagonian Desert</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, to attend the final of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="2014 Quidditch World Cup">427th Quidditch World Cup</a>, in July <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a>, Lorcan and Lysander were left at home "with grandpa", which may have meant Xenophilius or the twins' paternal grandfather (<a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newton Scamander</a>'s son or son-in-law).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xenophilius was an eccentric-looking <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, who looked slightly cross-eyed and had shoulder length hair that was the texture of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Candyfloss" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Candyfloss">candyfloss</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a>â€™s <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">wedding</a>, he wore a cap with a tassel that dangled in front of his nose, and wore a golden chain around his neck, that bore the sign of the <a href="/wiki/Deathly_Hallows" title="Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a>, sometimes mistaken as the <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald%27s_sign" class="mw-redirect" title="Gellert Grindelwald&#39;s sign">mark</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>. He also wore robes of an eye-watering shade of egg-yolk yellow, which <a href="/wiki/Muriel" title="Muriel">Muriel</a> said made him look like an omelette.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Xenophilius cared a great deal about his daughter <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna</a>, and shared her bizarre beliefs; he was likely the cause of them. He thought the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> was an awful paper, and only published stories that he thought were important and needed to be known by the public. He was extremely firm in his beliefs and felt those who disbelieve in them were "painfully limited", "narrow" and "close-minded ". Every time a sceptic demanded the evidence for his beliefs, he in turn asked them to prove them untrue themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to his bizarre views, many in the wizarding world thought he was senile. <a href="/wiki/Eldred_Worple" title="Eldred Worple">Eldred Worple</a> even remembered him as a "seriously unbalanced man" after meeting him at one of his book signings.<sup id="cite_ref-script_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-script-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> According to Luna, her father did not care about making money and did not pay people to write for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>; she claimed they did so because it was an honour to see their name in print. Luna also said that her father was supportive of any anti-<a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> action. Mr Lovegood fully supported <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and encouraged others to do so as well, until his daughter was kidnapped by Death Eaters. Xenophilius cared about Luna so much that he was willing to hand Harry, Ron and Hermione over to the Death Eaters in exchange for his daughter. Because of his predicament, Xenophilius was rather nervous and reclusive when the Trio visited him, leading Ron and Hermione to accuse him of cowardice and hypocrisy, and later the former even calling Xenophilius treacherous, only reluctantly retracting that claim when Harry reminded of Luna's abduction.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Xenophilius believed in the <a href="/wiki/Deathly_Hallows" title="Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a>, (which actually proved to be real), as well as numerous magical cryptids such as <a href="/wiki/Crumple-Horned_Snorkack" title="Crumple-Horned Snorkack">Crumple-Horned Snorkacks</a>. He also believed that <a href="/wiki/Gnome" title="Gnome">gnomes</a> had powerful magic,<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and that the <a href="/wiki/Dirigible_plum" title="Dirigible plum">dirigible plum</a> would enhance one's ability to accept the extraordinary. Although he sought the Deathly Hallows, given his personality, he was likely interested in them more as a matter of curiosity about such unusual objects, rather than a desire to actually possess or use them.
</p><p>Given his tendency to believe in anything dubious and ridiculous, Xenophilius sometimes became the subject of pranks, such as when a young wizard gave him an Erumpent horn and claimed that it belonged to a Crumple-Horned Snorkack. Unbeknownst to Lovegood, the Erumpent horn would explode if given even the slightest touch. This also shows that he was ignorant about some magical <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creatures</a>, as he was unable to identify the horn at first glance.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_17-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">Love</a></b>: Xenophilius loved his daughter Luna and cared about her safety and welfare immensely, as he was distraught when the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> kidnapped her for ransom in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>. He was even willing to betray <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> to them, in the hopes of them releasing Luna and being reunited with her. This shows what drastic and desperate lengths Xenophilius was willing to go to keep his daughter safe from harm, by going as far as betraying his own beliefs and cause.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_17-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He would have been heart-broken following the tragic <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> of his wife <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Lovegood" title="Pandora Lovegood">Pandora</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP38_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP38-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> showing him to have been an incredibly loving and caring father and husband indeed.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a></b>: Xenophilius grew various <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> plants in his garden, such as <a href="/wiki/Dirigible_plum" title="Dirigible plum">Dirigible plums</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snargaluff" title="Snargaluff">Snargaluffs</a>, showing he had knowledge of magical plants and how to grow and handle them.<sup id="cite_ref-DH20_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH20-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Xenophilius could cast a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which is a mark of superior <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability, given the immense difficulty of casting the charm. He could also cast simpler charms such as the <a href="/wiki/Hover_Charm" title="Hover Charm">Hover Charm</a> successfully, and was also capable of casting a particularly powerful <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">Stunning Spell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_17-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Study_of_Ancient_Runes" title="Study of Ancient Runes">Study of Ancient Runes</a></b>: In the <a href="/wiki/July" title="July">July</a> or <a href="/wiki/August" title="August">August</a> edition in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, the <a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">Quibbler</a> published a segment written in ancient <a href="/wiki/Rune" title="Rune">Runes</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP10_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> meaning he had some understanding of ancient runic languages, and that he may have even studied the subject at Hogwarts.</li>
<li><b>Journalism</b>: Xenophilius was the editor of the Quibbler, and in the tabloid he published various bizarre and often unproven conspiracy theories.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP10_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Despite the often ridiculous nature of the Quibbler's content, he was still implied to be a skilled journalist and writer nonetheless, as they were those who truly believed his wild tales and theories.</li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood%27s_wand" title="Xenophilius Lovegood&#39;s wand">Wand</a></b>: Xenophilius's was of an unknown length, <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">core</a> materials. He or his parents likely purchased it prior to his <a href="/wiki/First_year" title="First year">first year</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Loevgood%27s_dress_robes" class="mw-redirect" title="Xenophilius Loevgood&#39;s dress robes">Dress robes</a></b>: Xenophilius Lovegood wore brightly-coloured dress robes to the <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour</a>, along with the symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Deathly_Hallows" title="Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a>. He explained that wearing sun colours to a wedding was believed to be good luck. His daughter, <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna</a>, wore the same colours to the wedding.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood%27s_necklace" title="Xenophilius Lovegood&#39;s necklace">Necklace</a></b>: This was an ornament that he wore to Bill Weasley and Fleur Delacour's wedding. Some, like <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a> suspected him of being a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>, but actually the necklace simply bore the symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Deathly_Hallows" title="Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_15-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Lovegood_House" title="Lovegood House">House</a></b>: The Lovegood House was a black-coloured cylindrical house on top of a hill with a stream at the base of it, located near the village of <a href="/wiki/Ottery_St_Catchpole" title="Ottery St Catchpole">Ottery St Catchpole</a>. It was the home of Xenophilius and and his daughter Luna during Luna's childhood.<sup id="cite_ref-DH20_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH20-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Erumpent_horn" title="Erumpent horn">Erumpent horn</a></b>: Xenophilius owned an Erumpent horn that he claimed to be the horn of a <a href="/wiki/Crumple-Horned_Snorkack" title="Crumple-Horned Snorkack">Crumple-Horned Snorkack</a>. He proudly displayed it and accidentally blew it up, which resulted in his house being torn apart.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_17-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Bust_of_Rowena_Ravenclaw" title="Bust of Rowena Ravenclaw">Bust of Rowena Ravenclaw</a></b>: This bust of <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw" title="Rowena Ravenclaw">Rowena Ravenclaw</a> was created by Xenophilius Lovegood, who was trying to recreate the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> properties of her diadem.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_17-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Xenophilius' name comes from two Greek words: <i>Xenos</i> "strange" and <i>-Phile "</i>love".<i> </i>As opposed to xenophobe, one who fears the strange and foreign, his name means "one who loves the strange."
</p>
