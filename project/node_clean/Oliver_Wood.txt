<p><b>Oliver Wood</b> (born c. <a href="/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a> or <a href="/wiki/1976" title="1976">1976</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was the <a href="/wiki/Keeper" title="Keeper">Keeper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Captain#Hogwarts_Quidditch_Captains" title="Quidditch Captain">Captain</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Quidditch_team" title="Gryffindor Quidditch team">Gryffindor Quidditch team</a>. He attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from <a href="/wiki/1987" title="1987">1987</a> to <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. Oliver was known for his determination and skill on the playing field, and his long lectures before practices. He led the Gryffindor team to victory in the <a href="/wiki/Inter-House_Quidditch_Cup" title="Inter-House Quidditch Cup">Quidditch Cup</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Seventh_year" title="Seventh year">seventh year</a>.
</p><p>After leaving school, Oliver joined <a href="/wiki/Puddlemere_United" title="Puddlemere United">Puddlemere United</a> as a reserve player (presumably as Keeper). In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Oliver returned to Hogwarts to fight in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, alongside his former school friends and teammates.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Oliver was born in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> in <a href="/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a> or <a href="/wiki/1976" title="1976">1976</a>, likely into a wizarding family.<sup id="cite_ref-blood_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blood-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Oliver_DADA_class.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160524160821" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Oliver_DADA_class.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20160524160821" decoding="async" width="250" height="104" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Oliver DADA class.jpg" data-image-key="Oliver_DADA_class.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Oliver_DADA_class.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Oliver during a <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> lesson</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Oliver Wood attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from <a href="/wiki/1987" title="1987">1987</a> to <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. He was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> and presumably shared a <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Boys%27_Dormitory" title="Gryffindor Boys&#39; Dormitory">dormitory</a> with <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a>. He joined the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Quidditch_team" title="Gryffindor Quidditch team">Gryffindor Quidditch team</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Keeper" title="Keeper">Keeper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> During his first game he took a <a href="/wiki/Bludger" title="Bludger">Bludger</a> to the head two minutes in, and woke up a week later in the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a>. He mentioned this to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> when Oliver was in his fifth year, as Harry was nervous for his first <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> match. Oliver also mentioned that he didn't really remember his first match because of this.
</p><p>He also attended Hogwarts with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charlie Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> for four years before they graduated.  Oliver would frequently talk about Charlie's superb Quidditch skills and showed appreciation for them, as he was a huge Quidditch fan himself.
</p><p>Sometime before 1991, Oliver was given an "award for effort".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Quidditch_Captain">Quidditch Captain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Quidditch Captain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Quidditch Captain"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">

</div>
<p>Oliver became Captain of the Gryffindor Quidditch team in either <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>, his fourth year<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, his fifth year.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%931992_school_year" title="1991–1992 school year">1991–1992 school year</a>, despite assembling a good team, he was unable to find anyone skilled enough to play as <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> for the team after the departure of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charles Weasley</a> in the previous year. However, his search was ended when his <a href="/wiki/Head_of_House" title="Head of House">Head of House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a>, observed <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> catch <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Remembrall" title="Remembrall">Remembrall</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> from a fifty foot dive. McGonagall claimed that it was a move that even Charlie Weasley couldn't have pulled off, convincing Oliver that Harry was indeed something special. He immediately saw that Harry's build was perfect for a Seeker, and impressed upon McGonagall the need for Harry to have a decent <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broom</a> to fly.
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/93/Oliver_teaching_Harry.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20180526003153" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/93/Oliver_teaching_Harry.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180526003153" decoding="async" width="250" height="104" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Oliver teaching Harry.jpg" data-image-key="Oliver_teaching_Harry.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Oliver_teaching_Harry.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Oliver gave Harry his first Quidditch lesson in private</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Oliver gave Harry his first Quidditch lesson in private, immediately seeing that Harry was a natural flyer on a broomstick.<sup id="cite_ref-PS10_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Wanting to use Harry as a secret weapon during the first match of the season against <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, Oliver tried to keep his presence on the team under wraps. Despite his best efforts, however, word got out about Harry's presence, although his skill level was a secret from everyone outside the team.
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/bb/Oliver_wood_ps.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170827021428" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/bb/Oliver_wood_ps.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20170827021428" decoding="async" width="250" height="103" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Oliver wood ps.jpg" data-image-key="Oliver_wood_ps.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Oliver_wood_ps.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Oliver and his team preparing for the first match of the Quidditch season against Slytherin</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Captaining the side against their nemesis, Slytherin, in the first game of the season, Oliver was delighted when Harry captured the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a> in his first match, winning the game for Gryffindor. However, the second game of the season, against <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a>, gave Oliver cause for concern, when he found out that <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Professor Snape</a> would be refereeing the match in place of <a href="/wiki/Rolanda_Hooch" title="Rolanda Hooch">Madam Hooch</a>. Fearing that Snape would not be fair to Gryffindor following their defeat of Slytherin, he urged Harry to catch the Snitch as soon as possible. When he caught it within five minutes, Oliver was overjoyed. If Gryffindor could beat <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> in the final match of the season, then they would surely capture the <a href="/wiki/Inter-House_Quidditch_Cup" title="Inter-House Quidditch Cup">Quidditch Cup</a>.
</p><p>Shortly after, Gryffindor House lost one hundred and fifty house points due to the actions of three first year students, including Harry Potter. Harry offered his resignation to Oliver, who declined it, his reasoning being that if they didn't win at Quidditch, they had no chance to recoup the lost points. Despite this, Oliver likely felt somewhat irked towards Harry, as, during team practice sessions, his angry teammates simply referred to Harry as "the Seeker".
</p><p>The final match of the season against <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> was a disaster. Harry was in the hospital wing after facing <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Professor Quirrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, and recovering the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>. Without Harry and his Nimbus 2000 broomstick, the Gryffindor team suffered its biggest defeat<sup id="cite_ref-PS_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in three hundred years.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Oliver spent the summer of <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a> devising an entirely new training schedule that included early morning practice sessions. He was determined to win the Cup, and his enthusiasm for regular training sessions knew no limits. He was annoyed that his team did not seem to be taking it as seriously as he was, but that may have been a result of getting them all up earlier than they expected on the day of their first training session.
</p><p>On the first Saturday back at school, he booked the Quidditch pitch to begin training earlier than the other teams. His plan was cut short by the arrival of the Slytherin team, who had been given permission to use the pitch by Professor Snape in order to train their new Seeker, <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>.
</p>

<p>Gryffindor won their first match of the season against Slytherin, but the unexplained attacks on <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> students (later found to be caused by the reopening of the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> and the freeing of the <a href="/wiki/Serpent_of_Slytherin" title="Serpent of Slytherin">monster</a> within) forced the cancellation of the year's remaining matches. When <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> announced the good news that the school would not be closing, many students shouted "<i>Dumbledore's back</i>" or "<i>The heir of Slytherin has been caught</i>". Wood instead showed his intense love for Quidditch by shouting "<i>Quidditch matches are back on</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-CS_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Seventh_year">Seventh year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Seventh year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Seventh year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">"<i>This is our last chance — my last chance — to win the Quidditch Cup. I'll be leaving at the end of this year. I'll never get another shot at it.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Oliver's last shot at winning the cup<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In his <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%931994_school_year" title="1993–1994 school year">final year</a>, Oliver Wood was especially desperate to win the Quidditch Cup. He knew that he had the best team in the whole school, and he felt that the Cup would have been Gryffindor's for the past two years if events hadn't so conspired against them. Training his team hard for the first match against <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, he was shocked to find out that Slytherin had indeed managed to postpone the match owing to an injury to their Seeker, <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>. Malfoy, injured by a <a href="/wiki/Hippogriff" title="Hippogriff">hippogriff</a> after insulting it during a <a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a> lesson, was overplaying his injury to get out of playing the match during such extremely bad weather. Gryffindor were to then play Hufflepuff instead.
</p><p>During the match, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> was overcome by <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>, who were attracted to the large gathering. He fell from his <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broom</a> while chasing the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a> and it was captured by the Hufflepuff Seeker, <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>. When he realised what had happened, Cedric immediately offered Oliver a rematch, but Oliver declined it, knowing that Cedric had caught the Snitch fair and square, and assuring Harry that he didn't blame him for failing to catch the Snitch despite his previously impeccable record.
</p><p>However, Oliver knew they still had a chance to win the Cup if they won their remaining matches and other results went their way. He urged Harry to buy a new broom, since his had been destroyed by the <a href="/wiki/Whomping_Willow" title="Whomping Willow">Whomping Willow</a> after the last match, and was also worried about the Dementors appearing again and affecting Harry. Harry assured him that <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Professor Lupin</a> was teaching him to deal with Dementors, and that he had received a <a href="/wiki/Firebolt" title="Firebolt">Firebolt</a> broomstick for Christmas. Oliver was amazed, then disappointed to learn that <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> had confiscated it to check for signs of tampering or sabotage, as she feared that <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> may have sent it to harm Harry. Oliver tried to appeal to McGonagall, telling her that having the Firebolt would greatly enhanced their chances of winning the Quidditch Cup. McGonagall, however, accused Oliver of placing victory above Harry's life in his order of priorities. Finally, though, the Firebolt was given a clean bill of health, and returned to Harry, much to Oliver's joy.
</p>

<p>In the run up to the second match of the season against <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>, Oliver upped the training sessions to five times a week, and monitored the status of the Ravenclaw Seeker, <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a>. Knowing that she had a lot of trouble with injuries, he hoped that she would not be fit to play, and was disappointed to learn that she was able to. During the match, Oliver told Harry to stop being a gentleman and knock Cho off her broom if he had to.
</p>

<p>Gryffindor beat Ravenclaw, and other results went their way, meaning that, in the final game of the season against <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, if they could win by two hundred and ten points, they would win the Cup. Oliver took to constantly reminding Harry that he must <i>not</i> catch the Snitch unless they were ahead by at least sixty points, and ordering that Harry should be accompanied everywhere he went, to prevent anyone from harming him before the game, which numerous Slytherins made it clear was a clear and present danger.
</p><p>During the match against Slytherin, Oliver took two <a href="/wiki/Bludger" title="Bludger">Bludgers</a> to the stomach early in the game. Harry, though, managed to capture the Snitch when Gryffindor were ahead by at least sixty points, resulting in Gryffindor winning the <a href="/wiki/Inter-House_Quidditch_Cup" title="Inter-House Quidditch Cup">Quidditch Cup</a>. Oliver, overjoyed, ecstatic and finally having achieved his dream, watched Harry lift the Cup aloft.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>After leaving Hogwarts, Oliver became the Keeper for <a href="/wiki/Puddlemere_United" title="Puddlemere United">Puddlemere United</a>'s reserve team. In the summer of <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, he attended the <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a>, where he saw Harry and introduced him to his parents and told Harry of his new career.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF7_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF7-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, Harry Potter made a joke with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> about how the new Gryffindor captain, <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a>, seemed to be channelling Oliver's intense competitiveness, and that perhaps they should check with Puddlemere United that Oliver is still alive and hadn't been killed during a training session.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP13_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP13-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Oliver returned to Hogwarts along with former Gryffindor Quidditch players <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Spinnet" title="Alicia Spinnet">Alicia Spinnet</a> to fight in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> During a lull in the battle, which was granted to them by Lord Voldemort as time to gather the dead, he assisted <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> in carrying the dead bodies of <a href="/wiki/Colin_Creevey" title="Colin Creevey">Colin Creevey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fallen_Fifty" title="Fallen Fifty">others</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH34_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH34-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Following the defeat and <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> of Voldemort at the hands of Harry Potter at the end of the Battle, Wood survived the battle, as his name was not mentioned among the <a href="/wiki/Fallen_Fifty" title="Fallen Fifty">Fallen Fifty</a>. Afterwards, he likely continued with his career as a professional Quidditch player. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oliver was well built and burly, and had a commanding presence, especially to those in his team. He was known to get a maniacal look in his eyes when he was enthusing about Quidditch.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<dd>&#8212;Oliver's intense and slightly manic personality<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>Wood was described as an intensely driven, competitive personality. He was singularly focused and determined to creating the best <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_team" title="Quidditch team">Quidditch team</a> in the school. His ambition and dedication to the sport was such that he forced his players to train relentlessly, including early morning starts and all-weather training. Wood's obsession with winning occasionally became somewhat maniacal. 
</p><p>In 1992 he told Harry to "<i>catch the Snitch or die trying</i>" and in <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a> declared, with only mild exaggeration, that Wood was attempting to drown himself in the showers after a defeat as he refused to come out when they did. They also said in 1996 that "<i>Wood might have done the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherins</a> if he could have</i>," after someone asked who would try to kill off a Quidditch team. He regularly scheduled three practise sessions a week, rising to five a week before an important match. His overriding ambition was to win the <a href="/wiki/Inter-House_Quidditch_Cup" title="Inter-House Quidditch Cup">Quidditch cup</a> before he graduated, a dream which he finally achieved in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CS_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PA_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite this, Wood was remarkably humble concerning his own skill, putting the team's success down to the other members of the team. He would often be more concerned about his teammates than himself, like urging them to eat breakfast before a match, yet touching nothing himself. He was also extremely loyal to Gryffindor and saw the success of the Gryffindor team as <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a>'s success as a whole. He was also very fair; it was said by others that he didn't accept a replay after Harry fell off his broom against <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> in 1993 since he felt that Diggory had caught the Snitch fairly, although it is unknown if this is true. He also assured Harry that he didn't blame him for the loss as he respected that there had to be at least one game where Harry wouldn't win.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PA_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although he was fair-minded and approachable, Wood (which is an irony given his position as Captain of his team) appeared to struggle with interpersonal skills, as he often seemed oblivious to the way in which he came across to people. He never seemed to realise that his long-winded pep talks did not engage the rest of the team, and indeed frequently disgruntled them, possibly decreasing their motivation. After Harry's <a href="/wiki/Firebolt" title="Firebolt">Firebolt</a> was confiscated, Wood protested to <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> that he didn't care if it threw Harry off as long as he caught the Snitch on it first, apparently not understanding that this implied that he cared more about winning than about Harry's welfare. Despite this, he generally had a good relationship with the rest of the team.
</p><p>Aside from his Quidditch skills, Oliver had the trademark courage and bravery of a true Gryffindor as, despite having graduated and having no obligation to do so, Oliver returned to Hogwarts to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">battle</a> against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>, and led fellow former Gryffindor Quidditch players <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Spinnet" title="Alicia Spinnet">Alicia Spinnet</a> into the battle with their brooms.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The olive branch often is a symbol of peace. The olive tree's symbolism also includes a great harvest and long life. Wood is a place name (like Brooks or Field) and suggests ancestors who lived near or in a forest, or who were woodcutters. It is possible that Wood was meant to be the opposite of the name <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Flint" title="Marcus Flint">Flint</a>, the name of the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> Quidditch team captain and Wood's rival.
</p>

<li>Oliver was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Sean_Biggerstaff" title="Sean Biggerstaff">Sean Biggerstaff</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, although he did not match <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s description of Wood as "burly." Biggerstaff portrayed Wood as a <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scot</a>, although this is never mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">books</a>. Biggerstaff originally planned to audition using an English accent, but was encouraged by Chris Columbus to read the lines in his own Glaswegian dialect; thus Oliver Wood became Scottish<a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external autonumber" href="https://play.acast.com/s/honestactors/3.11-seanbiggerstaff">[1]</a>.</li>
<li>Though Oliver was not a member of <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, having left Hogwarts two years before its formation, Wood fought with <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">D.A.</a> members in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>. He was most likely alerted to the Battle of Hogwarts by former members of his Quidditch team who were members of Dumbledore's Army.</li>
<li>Oliver did not know what basketball was. This suggests that he comes from a purely magical family. However, Oliver used golf balls when first practising with Harry Potter, suggesting he may be a half-blood, or at least has a family member who knows about golf.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Oliver once borrowed <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages_(real)" title="Quidditch Through the Ages (real)">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i> from the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">Hogwarts Library</a> and was due to return the book on <a href="/wiki/9_April" title="9 April">9 April</a>.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>,<i> </i>Oliver can be seen leading an aerial attack on <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.</li>
<li>The hair and head pieces of the Oliver Wood minifigure in the <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter" title="LEGO Harry Potter">LEGO Harry Potter</a></i> sets are identical to those of the minifigure for <a href="https://indianajones.fandom.com/wiki/Mutt_Williams" class="extiw" title="w:c:indianajones:Mutt Williams">Mutt Williams</a>, a character from the <a href="https://indianajones.fandom.com/wiki/Indiana_Jones_(franchise)" class="extiw" title="w:c:indianajones:Indiana Jones (franchise)"><i>Indiana Jones</i> series</a>.</li></ul>
