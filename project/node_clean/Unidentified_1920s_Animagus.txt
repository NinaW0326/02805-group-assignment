<dd>&#8212;<i><a href="/wiki/Le_Cri_de_la_Gargouille" title="Le Cri de la Gargouille">Le Cri de la Gargouille</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts:_The_Crimes_of_Grindelwald" title="Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>This <b>individual</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> who lived in the early 20th century. In 1927, <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Cri_de_la_Gargouille" title="Le Cri de la Gargouille">Le Cri de la Gargouille</a></i> reported that he was somehow implicated in the "<i>affaire du <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">canard</a></i>".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_1920s_Animagus?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_1920s_Animagus?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The newspaper title that mentions this individual is probably a pun: "<a href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/canard#French" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:canard">canard</a>", which means "duck" in French, can also refer to an untrustworthy newspaper, or a false or misleading report.</li></ul>
