<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Faris_Spavin?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Faris_Spavin?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Faris Spavin was born around the year <a href="/wiki/1756" class="mw-redirect" title="1756">1756</a>. He became <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> on <a href="/wiki/1865" class="mw-redirect" title="1865">1865</a> at 109 years old, and was known for being the longest-ever serving and most long-winded Minister. He survived an assassination attempt (kicking) made by a <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaur</a>, who took offence to the punch line of his infamous 'a centaur, a ghost and a dwarf walk into a bar' joke.<sup id="cite_ref-PMministers_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMministers-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spavin was elected by the Wizarding population in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> at least six times.
</p><p>Minister Spavin's term in office saw the introduction of an important piece of wizarding legislation: the <a href="/wiki/Decree_for_the_Reasonable_Restriction_of_Underage_Sorcery" title="Decree for the Reasonable Restriction of Underage Sorcery">Decree for the Reasonable Restriction of Underage Sorcery</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1875" class="mw-redirect" title="1875">1875</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This Decree made it illegal for an <a href="/wiki/Underage_magic" title="Underage magic">underage wizard to do magic outside school</a>. The Ministry of Magic detects breaches of this Decree by means of <a href="/wiki/Trace" title="Trace">The Trace</a>.
</p><p>In the late 19th century, the Muggle government made plans to flatten the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a>, with the creation of <a href="/wiki/Charing_Cross_Road" title="Charing Cross Road">Charing Cross Road</a>. Faris Spavin made a melancholy seven-hour speech before the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> explaining why the Leaky Cauldron could not be saved. During the course of his tedious speech, however, the wizarding community rallied and performed a mass of <a href="/wiki/Memory_Charm" title="Memory Charm">Memory Charms</a> (some say, although it has never been conclusively proven, that the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> was additionally used on several Muggle town planners), so that the Leaky Cauldron was now accommodated in the revised plans for the new road. After his speech, his <a href="/wiki/Faris_Spavin%27s_secretary" title="Faris Spavin&#39;s secretary">secretary</a> presented him with a note describing the developments that had just invalidated his words.<sup id="cite_ref-PMleakycauldron_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMleakycauldron-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spavin's term was also notable for introducing some reforms to the game of <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> (and the commotion they originated): on the night of <a href="/wiki/21_June" title="21 June">21 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1884" class="mw-redirect" title="1884">1884</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Games_and_Sports" title="Department of Magical Games and Sports">Department of Magical Games and Sports</a> decreed the institutionalisation of the <a href="/wiki/Stooging" title="Stooging">Stooging</a> Penalty in <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>. This announcement caused widespread discontent among British Quidditch players and fans, who <a href="/wiki/1884_riot_at_the_British_Ministry_of_Magic_Headquarters" title="1884 riot at the British Ministry of Magic Headquarters">demonstrated profusely at the Ministry of Magic Headquarters</a>: the assembled crowd bombarded a <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Department_of_Magical_Games_and_Sports_representative" title="Unidentified Department of Magical Games and Sports representative">Departmental representative</a> with <a href="/wiki/Quaffle" title="Quaffle">Quaffles</a>, as well as threatened to stooge Minister Spavin himself. Wizards from the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a> were duly dispatched there and the crowd reluctantly dispersed.<sup id="cite_ref-QA6_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QA6-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This was not without precedent: just over a year before, <a href="/wiki/1883_riot_at_the_British_Ministry_of_Magic_Headquarters" title="1883 riot at the British Ministry of Magic Headquarters">another riot</a> had broken out at the Ministry as the Department of Magical Games and Sports had decided to get rid of "goal baskets" in favour of the modern <a href="/wiki/Goalpost" title="Goalpost">goalposts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-QA6_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QA6-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spavin attended <a href="/wiki/Victoria" title="Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>'s funeral in an admiral's hat and spats, at which point the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> suggested gently he step down as <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>. He was 147 when he resigned, in <a href="/wiki/1903" title="1903">1903</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PMministers_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMministers-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Faris_Spavin?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Faris_Spavin?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Faris_Spavin?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Faris_Spavin?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name "<i>Faris</i>" means "<i>knight</i>" in Arabic, but also "<i>stone</i>" in both Greek and English. Faris is also the name of a municipality in Laconia, Greece. It has a population of roughly 5,000.</li>
<li>Spavin is a word given to a disorder located in a horse's neck, usually a swelling. The word has French origins and comes from the word "<i>espavin</i>", meaning "<i>swelling</i>".</li></ul>
