<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Euan_Abercrombie?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Euan_Abercrombie?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Abercrombie" is a surname of Celtic or <a href="/wiki/Gaelic" title="Gaelic">Gaelic</a> origin. It comes from the name of a parish in Fife, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, on the northern shore of the Firth of Forth, whence the possessor took his surname; from <i>Aber</i>, marshy ground, a place where two or more streams meet; and <i>cruime</i> or <i>crombie</i>, a bend or crook. Aber, in the Celtic and Gaelic, and also in the Cornish British, signifies the confluence of two or more streams, or the mouth of a river, where it flows into the sea; hence it is often applied to marshy ground, generally near the confluence of two rivers. It also signifies, sometimes, a gulf or whirlpool. "Euan" is of Scottish and Gaelic origin, and it means "born of yew" or "youth". It also means "little swift one" in Irish Gaelic.
</p>
