<dd>&#8212;The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_Wizards_Unite" title="Harry Potter: Wizards Unite">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Hamish Steed</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was murdered on a murderous rampage by <a class="new" title="Flannery (page does not exist)" rel="nofollow" data-uncrawlable-url="L3dpa2kvRmxhbm5lcnk/YWN0aW9uPWVkaXQmcmVkbGluaz0x">Flannery</a>. Even though Steed was under the protection of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> at the time, the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> reported Flannery had used an ancient spell to track him down.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sage_Bragnam" title="Sage Bragnam">Sage Bragnam</a> thought this story was implausible and that the <i>Daily Prophet'</i>s report was a product of exaggeration.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, it seems that <a href="/wiki/Grim_Fawley" title="Grim Fawley">Grim Fawley</a> drew inspiration from this report to find his <a href="/wiki/Penelope_Fawley" title="Penelope Fawley">missing wife</a>.
</p>
