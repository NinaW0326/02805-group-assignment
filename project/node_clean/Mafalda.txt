<b>Mafalda</b> (born c. <a href="/wiki/1983" title="1983">1983</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. She was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_second_cousin" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s second cousin">Molly Weasley's second cousin</a> (most likely a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>) and a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Mafalda%27s_mother" title="Mafalda&#39;s mother">mother</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mafalda was born in around <a href="/wiki/1983" title="1983">1983</a> to an accountant <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley%27s_second_cousin" title="Molly Weasley&#39;s second cousin">father</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Mafalda%27s_mother" title="Mafalda&#39;s mother">mother</a>. Although her father was most likely a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>, he did not expect to become the parent of a witch.<sup id="cite_ref-mafalda_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mafalda-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mafalda started at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> on <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> house.<sup id="cite_ref-mafalda_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mafalda-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality">Personality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personality"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All in all, Mafalda was a rather unpleasant individual, even to her own parents. She liked to impress others and showed off as much as possible (this may have also been the root of her academic drive). She was also very nosy, eavesdropping often (even if this included breaking the rules), and was very loud-mouthed too, prone to gossip. There are noted parallels between the nature of Mafalda and those of <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> (in their academic prowess) and <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> (in their morbid curiosities).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_skills_and_abilities">Magical skills and abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical skills and abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Magical skills and abilities"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magical</a> aptitude</b>: Whilst explicit details about Mafalda's magical talent are not known, it is known that she was generally a very gifted witch, rivalling even the overachieving <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mafalda is an Italian form of Matilda, a Germanic name meaning, "strength in battle."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Her name coincides with that of <a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk" title="Mafalda Hopkirk">Mafalda Hopkirk</a>, a <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> employee, first introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>.
</p><p>Mafalda also is a very popular comic strip character by Argentinian writer Quino. Besides being greatly concerned about humanity, Mafalda's main characteristics are her constant asking about mature or complex topics, to the extent that she constantly has grown-ups baffled. According to her, no matter how pessimistic she may be, it is never reason enough to stop discussing a topic.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Mafalda was originally planned to be included in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, but was ultimately cut. <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> stated on her website that she was very fond of Mafalda – calling her a "match for <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a>" – and that she did not want to have to cut her from the book. Her role in the plot would have been to eavesdrop on conversations held between the children of <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> in her <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">house</a>, then repeat what she heard to <a href="/wiki/Trio" title="Trio">The Trio</a> in an attempt to impress them. Hermione would have discovered that, much to her own horror, Mafalda was her equal in many ways, and would have been caught between hating Mafalda's disregard for rules and longing to try to outdo her. However, Rowling found that Mafalda "wasn't doing the job she was supposed to do", as there were severe limitations to how much information an eleven-year-old girl stuck at Hogwarts could gather, and consequently developed <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> further to fill her role.<sup id="cite_ref-mafalda_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mafalda-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>J. K. Rowling jokingly promised to name a character after a person using the screen name "Icicle" in a September 1999 Barnes &amp; Noble webchat.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She also revealed sometime prior to the release of <i>Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</i> that she planned to include a "Weasley cousin" in the book.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This led to Internet rumours that the Weasley cousin would be named Icicle.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
