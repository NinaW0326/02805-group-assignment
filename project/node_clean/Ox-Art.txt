<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ox-Art?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ox-Art?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The coffee table featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> is one of a line of tile-inlaid tables (which included dining tables, coffee tables, and end tables) produced by the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denmark" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Denmark">Danish</a> furniture company Trioh in the <a href="/wiki/1970s" title="1970s">1970s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pamona_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pamona-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-furnitureetc_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-furnitureetc-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tennants_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tennants-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The tiles on this line of tables were typically signed "Ox-Art" and dated with the year of manufacture.<sup id="cite_ref-pamona_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pamona-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-furnitureetc_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-furnitureetc-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tennants_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tennants-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown whether "Ox-Art" was an individual artist, collective, or designer.</li></ul>
