<p><b>Minister Rufus Scrimgeour</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/1_August" title="1 August">1 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was a powerful ranking official in the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. He originally joined the Ministry of Magic as an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>, where he became a hardened veteran, spending most of his life fighting <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizards</a>. Eventually he rose to become Head of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Scrimgeour was appointed in 1996 to succeed <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> as <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, being the third ex-Auror to hold the post.<sup id="cite_ref-ministers_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ministers-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> While he appeared to provide a much tougher and resolute stance against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, he made the same mistake of creating the appearance of safety and security, while in truth Scrimgeour's efforts were waning in progress in the war.
</p><p>This policy of giving the appearance of false safety would ultimately cost Scrimgeour his life, as it left the Ministry open to infiltration by the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, who used several high-ranking Ministry officials, all of whom had been <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">subverted to their control</a>, to launch a successful coup and bring the Ministry under Voldemort's control. Voldemort, wanting the location of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, captured and brutally tortured Scrimgeour, though when Scrimgeour refused to talk, Voldemort murdered him.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince"><b>Horace Slughorn</b>: "<i>Now, you, Cormac. I happen to know you see a lot of your Uncle Tiberius, because he has a rather splendid picture of the two of you hunting Nogtails in, I think, Norfolk?</i>"</span></dd>

<p>Scrimgeour was born (possibly in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a><sup id="cite_ref-scotland_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotland-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>) into the wizarding <a href="/wiki/Scrimgeour_family" title="Scrimgeour family">Scrimgeour family</a> on a date no later than <a href="/wiki/1968" title="1968">1968</a>. It was highly likely Scrimgeour attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> during his youth, where he would have received his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> education. Scrimgeour joined the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, and completed a stringent series of character and aptitude tests to join the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Auror_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Auror-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> At some point before he became Minister, Scrimgeour joined <a href="/wiki/Bertie_Higgs" title="Bertie Higgs">Bertie Higgs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tiberius" title="Tiberius">Tiberius</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cormac_McLaggen" title="Cormac McLaggen">Cormac McLaggen</a> to hunt recreationally for <a href="/wiki/Nogtail" title="Nogtail">Nogtails</a> in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP7_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP7-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Head_of_the_Auror_Office_(1990s-1996)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Head_of_the_Auror_Office_.281990s-1996.29">Head of the Auror Office <span class="template-c">(1990s-1996)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Head of the Auror Office (1990s-1996)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Head of the Auror Office (1990s-1996)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">

<dd><b>Nymphadora Tonks</b>: "<i>Oh… yeah… well, we need to be a bit more careful, he's been asking Kingsley and me funny questions...</i>"</dd>

<p>Eventually, after serving as an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> for some time and having fought <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizards</a> for most of his working life, Scrimgeour rose to the post of Head of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>, which he occupied at least in the early-to-mid-1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-OP7_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP7-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In this capacity, Scrimgeour worked alongside <a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish" title="John Dawlish">John Dawlish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Proudfoot" title="Proudfoot">Proudfoot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savage" title="Savage">Savage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gawain_Robards" title="Gawain Robards">Gawain Robards</a>.
</p><p>When he was the Head of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>, he often questioned Tonks and Shacklebolt about <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, as they were in charge of the investigation of his whereabouts after his <a href="/wiki/Azkaban_escapes#1993" title="Azkaban escapes">escape</a> from <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP7_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP7-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>With Voldemort finally into the open and declaring <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">open war</a> on Britain's <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding</a> and <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> communities, it forced the wizarding community to unite and call for Cornelius Fudge's resignation. In the fortnight following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> and the Ministry's acknowledgement of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort's</a> return in June of 1996,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> was, as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i> went on to report, "<i>chased from his office</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-JKR_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKR-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Fudge left office in disgrace and was replaced by the ostensibly more proactive Scrimgeour.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gawain_Robards" title="Gawain Robards">Gawain Robards</a> replaced Scrimgeour as Head of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Soon after, Scrimgeour made a speech to the the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> and the Daily Prophet about "dark times", and trying to better protect Britain from dark wizards than the previous year,<sup id="cite_ref-DHF1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> as the Ministry under Fudge had denied any evidence of Voldemort's return and branded <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> liars for saying the opposite.<sup id="cite_ref-OP7_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP7-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Fudge resigned in summer of 1996, Fudge and Scrimgeour visited the <a href="/wiki/British_Prime_Minister_in_1996" title="British Prime Minister in 1996">Muggle Prime Minister</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister%27s_office" title="Prime Minister&#39;s office">office</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Floo_Network" title="Floo Network">Floo Network</a>, in which Fudge introducted Scrimgeour as his predecessor. Scrimgeour informed the Muggle Prime Minister of various strange happenings in the country, such as the Junior Minister <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Chorley" title="Herbert Chorley">Herbert Chorley</a> being bewitched with the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a>, who was subsequently hospitalised at <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungos</a>. He expressed the belief to the Prime Minister his security needed to be increased by the Ministry of Magic, in order to prevent similar events occurring, and the Prime Minister was also not too happy with two wizards entering his office so abruptly. He and Fudge bid the Prime Minister farewell and promised to keep him updated about ongoing wizarding affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Scrimgeour, though seemingly more capable than Fudge, decided to prioritise appearances over truth in his battle against Voldemort. Apparently, Scrimgeour distrusted Albus Dumbledore in a similar way to Fudge before him, as he assigned <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish" title="John Dawlish">John Dawlish</a> to follow Dumbledore during his frequent departures from Hogwarts, ultimately resulting in the Auror being jinxed by Dumbledore.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP17_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP17-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He ordered the arrest of <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Shunpike" title="Stanley Shunpike">Stan Shunpike</a>, a conductor on the <a href="/wiki/Knight_Bus" title="Knight Bus">Knight Bus</a>, to give the impression that the Ministry was actively seeking and arresting <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, even though it was apparent to Harry that Shunpike was innocent and merely foolish.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP11_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP11-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also quarrelled with Albus Dumbledore over the use of Harry Potter, the alleged "Chosen One", as a mascot for the Ministry to "boost morale".<sup id="cite_ref-HBP17_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP17-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On Christmas Day 1996, which Harry spent at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a> in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, Scrimgeour came to visit him, accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a>, whom he used as a pretext. Scrimgeour took Harry for a walk around the garden, during which he tried to get answers out of Harry about what Dumbledore was doing, and suggested that the Ministry would be grateful if Harry was to visibly align himself with them. Harry, revolted by the Ministry's hypocrisy, refused further assistance, saying that the new Ministry was as bad in its own way as the old one.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_death_of_Albus_Dumbledore">The death of Albus Dumbledore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The death of Albus Dumbledore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The death of Albus Dumbledore"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>After Dumbledore's death atop the <a href="/wiki/Astronomy_Tower" title="Astronomy Tower">Astronomy Tower</a>, in the summer of 1997, Scrimgeour and a delegation of Ministry officials came for a short stay at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>, so that they could attend <a href="/wiki/Funeral_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="Funeral of Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore's funeral</a>. Scrimgeour was seated in the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, looking "grave and dignified". After the service, Scrimgeour once more tried to convince Harry Potter to openly support the Ministry's endeavours, and Harry once again refused.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP30_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP30-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Scrimgeour spoke to Harry once more at the Burrow on 31 July, a month after Dumbledore's death, to release the contents of the late wizard's will to Harry, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, interrupting <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_seventeenth_birthday_party" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter&#39;s seventeenth birthday party">Harry's birthday party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH7_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Scrimgeour appeared to have aged considerably as a result of working to retain control of the wizarding community, which was in a state of open warfare. He had delayed releasing Dumbledore's personal bequests for thirty-one days, under the <a href="/wiki/Decree_for_Justifiable_Confiscation" title="Decree for Justifiable Confiscation">Decree for Justifiable Confiscation</a>, in order to inspect the items. Scrimgeour bombarded the trio with questions, attempting to discern Dumbledore's intentions, and was highly suspicious of the gifts he had left them: the <a href="/wiki/Deluminator" title="Deluminator">Deluminator</a> for Ron, a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a> for Harry, and an original copy of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">The Tales of Beedle the Bard </a></i> for Hermione. Scrimgeour refused to give Harry Dumbledore's final bequest, <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor%27s_Sword" class="mw-redirect" title="Godric Gryffindor&#39;s Sword">Godric Gryffindor's Sword</a>, believing it to be a historical artefact and public property, which Dumbledore therefore had no right to dispose of as he saw fit.<sup id="cite_ref-DH7_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Harry criticised Scrimgeour for wasting his time scrutinising Dumbledore's things, and for covering up the recent escape of dozens of Death Eaters from <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>, the two came close to a <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duel</a>, in spite of Scrimgeour's claim that he in fact sought to work together, in order to fight Voldemort. The arrival of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a> forced Scrimgeour to regain composure and then depart.<sup id="cite_ref-DH7_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Scrimgeour correctly guessed that there might be something hidden inside the Golden Snitch, although he never found out what, as Dumbledore had ensured that the Snitch would only open "at the close".
</p>

<h3><span id="Calamity_(2019)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Calamity_.282019.29"><a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a> (2019)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Calamity (2019)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Calamity (2019)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2019" title="2019">2019</a>, Scrimgeour appeared as a <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundable</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a>. Volunteer members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> were able to free him, allowing Scrimgeour to return to his original time.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 167px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e8/F_56015.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20140525081912" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="165" height="220" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="F 56015.jpg" data-image-key="F_56015.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e8/F_56015.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/165?cb=20140525081912" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e8/F_56015.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20140525081912" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e8/F_56015.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/165?cb=20140525081912" decoding="async" width="165" height="220" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="F 56015.jpg" data-image-key="F_56015.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e8/F_56015.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/165?cb=20140525081912" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:F_56015.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Rufus Scrimgeour</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Scrimgeour was a man with a certain rangy loping grace; he walked with a slight limp, and looked rather like an old lion with grey streaks in his mane of tawny hair and bushy eyebrows. He had keen yellowish eyes and wore wire-rimmed spectacles.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He sometimes used <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour%27s_walking_stick" title="Rufus Scrimgeour&#39;s walking stick">a walking stick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP30_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP30-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> With time, the stresses of the failing conflict reached him, and when last seen he appeared badly-shaven, scraggy, and grim.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows"><b>Scrimgeour</b>: "<i>Remembered you're not at school, have you? Remembered that I am not Dumbledore, who forgave your insolence and insubordination? You may wear that scar like a crown, Potter, but it is not up to a seventeen-year-old boy to tell me how to do my job! It's time you learned some respect!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Harry Potter</b>: "<i>It's time you earned it.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; An intense row regarding respect and responsibility <sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p>Scrimgeour was, according to Dumbledore, a "man of action", having fought against <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizards</a> throughout his career as an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> and climbed ambitiously through the Ministry.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He gave an impression of shrewd toughness and was more decisive and forceful than his predecessor, <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Scrimgeour did not underestimate Voldemort as much as Fudge did,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> which he demonstrated by taking more action for the Wizarding World to fight Voldemort and the Death Eaters, and cared a great deal about the Ministry's reputation.
</p><p>However, despite appearing more proactive, Scrimgeour possessed a more hypocritical, deceitful and shameless side as he prioritised appearances over truth and cared more for the Ministry's reputation than the safety of the wizarding community, just as his predecessor had done. As a result, he did not allow the reporting of devastating events which had occurred such as the death of renowned Auror <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Azkaban_escapes" title="Azkaban escapes">1997 mass breakout of Azkaban</a>, due to believing it would cause panic and ruin the Ministry's reputation, and to keep an appearance of false security and safety. At the expense of true justice, he even allowed the the arrest of innocent people — such as <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Shunpike" title="Stanley Shunpike">Stanley Shunpike</a> — to give the impression that the Ministry was making progress in the war, similar to what <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Bartemius Crouch Senior</a> had done.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This was most likely fueled by a belief that maintaining peace and giving false appearances of progress were important for the Ministry to regain trust from the wizarding community after they unanimously condemned Fudge's tenure as Minister, when in reality it only further endangered the wizarding community by keeping them more oblivious and unprepared of Voldemort's actions and intentions. Scrimgeour also appeared ignorant of corruption within the Ministry, as he was oblivious of <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>'s tyrannical crimes during her temporary rule over Hogwarts,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and the fact that <a href="/wiki/Pius_Thicknesse" title="Pius Thicknesse">Pius Thicknesse</a> and other Ministry employees were put under the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> to serve Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-DH1_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Along with his tendency to keep an appearance of false security, this would ultimately prove to be Scrimgeour's undoing.
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 257px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Scrimgeour_speech.JPG/revision/latest?cb=20110402120104" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="255" height="107" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Scrimgeour speech.JPG" data-image-key="Scrimgeour_speech.JPG" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Scrimgeour_speech.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/255?cb=20110402120104" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Scrimgeour_speech.JPG/revision/latest?cb=20110402120104" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Scrimgeour_speech.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/255?cb=20110402120104" decoding="async" width="255" height="107" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Scrimgeour speech.JPG" data-image-key="Scrimgeour_speech.JPG" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/84/Scrimgeour_speech.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/255?cb=20110402120104" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Scrimgeour_speech.JPG" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Unlike his predecessor, Scrimgeour sought to take action against Voldemort</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Under his shamelessness and hypocrisy, he was also willing to gain the trust of those who the Ministry had previously attempted to discredit in order to keep a false pretence of peace. He tried unsuccessfully at various times to convince Harry Potter to work for the Ministry as a "mascot" to improve morale: once during the Christmas holidays,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and another time after Dumbledore's funeral.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP30_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP30-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Like Snape, he thought of Harry as arrogant and conceited, and disliked him for it<sup id="cite_ref-DH7_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> while Harry disliked his hypocrisy and false pretences, claiming Scrimgeour's tenure as Minister was just as bad as Fudge's tenure was. When Dumbledore disagreed with his intended use of Harry as a mascot and refused to reveal his plans against Voldemort to him, Scrimgeour deeply distrusted Dumbledore to the point that he attempted to spy on him and control the matter of Dumbledore's will after his death rather than prioritising the fight against Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-DH7_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Despite his flaws, Scrimgeour in the end proved his bravery and strength of character when he refused, under torture, to tell Voldemort of Harry's whereabouts.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> skills</b>: An accomplished <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, Rufus Scrimgeour was more than proficient in at least <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a>, as all these subjects at Hogwarts are required at both <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a> level, at either "<a href="/wiki/Exceeds_Expectations" title="Exceeds Expectations">Exceeds Expectations</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Outstanding" title="Outstanding">Outstanding</a>" level.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Given his status as Head Auror, his skill presumably surpassed most of the highly trained <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a> capturers working under him, having fought many Dark Wizard and Witches during his career as an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>, which displayed his considerable <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duelling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">martial magic</a> abilities, the most essential ability to everyone working in his profession.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Auror_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Auror-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Scrimgeour was also noted to have put up a valiant fight against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH11_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH11-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> though it is unknown if this meant his duelling prowess or <a href="/wiki/Spell_resistance" title="Spell resistance">resilience</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">torture</a>, although it was likely a combination of both.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Nonverbal magic</a></b>: Scrimgeour was capable of casting <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a> without speaking their <a href="/wiki/Incantation" title="Incantation">incantations</a> aloud, a highly difficult and advanced <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> feat. He non-verbally <a href="/wiki/Locking_Spell" title="Locking Spell">locked</a> the door of the Muggle Prime Minister's office as well as closing its curtains in 1996,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which is an impressive use of magic.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a></b>: Like most accomplished adult wizards, Scrimgeour was capable of apparating at will.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP29_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP29-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Scrimgeour was capable of producing a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> a mark of superior <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability and talent, given the immense difficulty of performing the charm. Furthermore, he would have needed both an <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">subject</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> showing his accomplishment in this field. He was also capable of using the <a href="/wiki/Locking_Spell" title="Locking Spell">Locking Spell</a> to lock the door to the Muggle Prime Minister's office as well as closing the office's curtains both non-verbally, which is further testament to his magical skills.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Leadership skills</b>: Scrimgeour obviously had the necessary skills of leadership as the wizarding community together called for Scrimgeour to become <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> to take control over the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> A tried and tested veteran, his skill and competence was both such that he was eventually called upon to head up the Auror Office and command his colleagues, and spearheaded their efforts to round up Dark witches and wizards.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP4_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP4-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> While presumably a very capable leader of combatants, however, he made for a poor politician, and spent his short-lived career as head of state trying to improve public opinion, and put more effort into getting the cooperation of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in appeasing the masses than he did focusing on the upcoming war,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> which would prove to be a mistake that cost him his life.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rufus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Rufus">Rufus</a></i> is a Latin name meaning "red". It was a Roman cognomen as well as the nickname of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_II_of_England" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William II of England">King William II of England</a>, who had the reputation for being an effective soldier but a ruthless and unpopular ruler. <i>Scrimgeour</i> is a Scottish surname that is believed to probably mean "skirmish", from the French <i>escrimeur</i>, meaning "swordsman". His name therefore loosely translates to "Red Skirmish" or "Red Swordsman".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bill_Nighy" title="Bill Nighy">Bill Nighy</a> portrayed Scrimgeour in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Nighy was originally cast for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>;<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> however, Scrimgeour was cut from that film.</li>
<li>Although it is likely Scrimgeour was raised in Scotland, Nighy played the character with a Welsh accent.</li>
<li>In the film, Scrimgeour does not sport a mane-like beard, and he doesn't seem to be as tough and ruthless as he is in the book.</li>
<li>Due to his being cut in Half-Blood Prince, Scrimgeour's relationship with Dumbledore is not mentioned in the film, and Harry's relationship with him is far from antagonistic.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> once mentioned to Harry Potter that Scrimgeour was a <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a>, a story her father was supposed to publish in the magazine <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>. The authenticity of this claim is quite questionable, as <i>The Quibbler</i> has a reputation for publishing unlikely and outrageous stories. Interestingly, Bill Nighy also played Viktor, a vampire in the <i>Underworld </i>series.</li>
<li>It is possible that he is related to <a href="/wiki/Brutus_Scrimgeour" title="Brutus Scrimgeour">Brutus Scrimgeour</a>, the author of the book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beaters%27_Bible" title="The Beaters&#39; Bible">The Beaters' Bible</a></i>, and a reviewer of the book <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages" title="Quidditch Through the Ages">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i>.</li>
<li>Scrimgeour shares the same given name as his <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">predecessor's</a> <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Fudge" title="Rufus Fudge">nephew</a>.</li>
<li>Although stated in the book Scrimgeour sports glasses, in the film he does not — even though Nighy himself does.</li>
<li>In the film adaptations, Scrimgeour's negative personality aspects never come to light in the way and to the extent they do in the book.</li>
<li>Scrimgeour's anti-Death Eater propaganda, particularly the Ministry's attitude towards <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Shunpike" title="Stanley Shunpike">Stan Shunpike</a>, is similar to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_McCarthy" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Joseph McCarthy">Senator Joseph McCarthy</a>'s anti-communist ideas during the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Red_Scare" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Second Red Scare">Second Red Scare</a> in the 1950s.</li>
<li>In the books, Scrimgeour protects Harry's whereabouts when the Ministry has fallen. This shows his protection of Harry, even though he has argued with him many times in the books.</li></ul>
