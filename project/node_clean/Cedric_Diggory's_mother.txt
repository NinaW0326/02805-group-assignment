<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_marriage">Early life and marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life and marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and marriage"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mrs Diggory was a witch and an only child.<sup id="cite_ref-Act3Scene18_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Act3Scene18-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She married <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">Amos Diggory</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and had a son,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1977" title="1977">1977</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Cedric went to attend <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>&#160;and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire"><b>Harry</b>: "<i>You take this. It should've been Cedric's, he got there first, you take it-</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Mrs Diggory</b>: "<i>Oh, no, it's yours, dear, we couldn't... you keep it.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Harry arguing with Mrs Diggory over who should get the Triwizard winnings.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div><p>Although she was only seen and heard from for brief moments, Mrs Diggory seemed to be a compassionate, amiable, and understanding person. For instance, she prevented her husband from arguing with Molly Weasley over Harry's supposed "theft" of Cedric's glory in being a participant of the Triwizard Tournament, allowing Harry to leave peacefully with the Weasleys.
</p><p>It was also evident that she loved her son dearly, given how her grief was described to be "beyond tears" when she learned from Harry how Cedric had been killed, which was in direct contrast to her husband, who sobbed openly from grief. She demonstrated her compassion and understanding nature by thanking Harry for returning her son's body, declining Harry's offer of his Tournament winnings, and even urged him to look after himself.
</p><p>Given how Cedric also proved himself to be a compassionate and caring soul, it is possible that Mrs Diggory had passed some of her best characteristics to her son.
</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid%27s_birthday_celebration" title="Rubeus Hagrid&#39;s birthday celebration">birthday celebration</a>, Amos talked about his wife and described her as a pretty woman, implying she was in fact rather beautiful in real life. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Mrs Diggory does not make&#160;an appearance in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>. Her husband's personality in the film seems to more closely match her personality than that of his own novel counterpart.</li>
<li>Mrs Diggory is conspicuously absent from <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Cursed_Child" title="Harry Potter and the Cursed Child">Harry Potter and the Cursed Child</a></i>. This is perhaps an indication that she had passed away sometime before the time the play is set, in <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>.</li></ul>
