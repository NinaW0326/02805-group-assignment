<p>The <b>Unicorn</b> was a white <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horse</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_horn" title="Unicorn horn">horn</a> sticking out of its head. An iconic <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creature</a> of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, the existence of unicorns was one of the worst-kept secrets of the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, as unicorns were inextricably associated with magic in the <a href="/wiki/Muggle_world" title="Muggle world">Muggle world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> These majestic equines possessed potent magical properties, with their <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_hair" title="Unicorn hair">tail hairs</a> being designed by <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a> as one of the "<a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">Supreme Cores</a>," which was a distinction shared by only two other <a href="/wiki/Beast" title="Beast">beasts</a>.
</p>

<p>Unicorn foals were born pure gold in colour. They remained so until they were about two years old, at which time they turned silver in colour. At around four years old their horn grew in. They were fully grown at about seven years old, at which at this age they turned a shade of pure white that was so bright that it made freshly fallen <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">snow</a> looked grey in comparison. Their hooves were golden (remaining so from their gold stage, the first two years of their life), and their <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_blood" title="Unicorn blood">blood</a> was silver-blue and shone under the moonlight. It was not mentioned how long a Unicorn can live for.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unicorns inhabited the forests of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a> by <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF24_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF24-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unicorns preferred a woman's touch, but the young ones were more trusting and do not mind men as much. Unicorns are also so fleet of the hoof that they can rarely be caught by <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a>. They were also fast enough to outrun <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolves</a> with absurd ease. <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">Wandmakers</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Ollivander_family" title="Ollivander family">Ollivander family</a> also plucked <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_hair" title="Unicorn hair">hairs</a> from the tails of the creatures to use in <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> as <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">cores</a>. Not aggressive without cause, but should be treated with great respect.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unicorns were capable of moving faster than <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">Werewolves</a>. During the detention in the Forbidden Forest in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, Harry asked if a werewolf could be killing the unicorns and <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> replied that they were not fast enough to catch Unicorns.<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unicorns were considered pure and sacred creatures. Drinking their blood to preserve one’s life was considered a heinous and immoral crime.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Unicorn?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Unicorn?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Uses"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Various parts of the Unicorn — the <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_horn" title="Unicorn horn">horn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_hair" title="Unicorn hair">tail hair</a> in particular — were used in potions. <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_hair" title="Unicorn hair">Unicorn hair</a> in general was used for the <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">cores</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a>. Unicorn hair can be used in wandmaking; they produce the most consistent <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, least subject to fluctuations and blockages, most difficult to turn to the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> and the most faithful of wands. However, they did not make the most powerful of wands and were prone to melancholy if mishandled, although the <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wand wood</a> can compensate this. The <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">Wandmaker</a> <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a> made wands with unicorn hair cores.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The tail hair can also be used as binding in bandages due to its incredible strength.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_blood" title="Unicorn blood">blood of a unicorn</a> can be used to keep a person who is near death alive, thus granting the drinker <a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">immortality</a>, but "<i>you will have but a half-life, a <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">cursed</a> life, from the moment the blood touches your lips</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Unicorn horn is often used as a common <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potion</a> ingredient, in potions such as the <a href="/wiki/Antidote_to_Common_Poisons" title="Antidote to Common Poisons">Antidote to Common Poisons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Draught_of_Peace" title="Draught of Peace">Draught of Peace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wiggenweld_Potion" title="Wiggenweld Potion">Wiggenweld Potion</a>.
</p><p>The unicorn's single straight horn was used, in the <a href="/wiki/Study_of_Ancient_Runes" title="Study of Ancient Runes">Study of Ancient Runes</a>, to symbolise the number one as a <a href="/wiki/Rune" title="Rune">runic</a> number symbol.<sup id="cite_ref-JKRS_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A unicorn was one rare possible corporeal form of the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><a href="/wiki/Constance_Pickering" title="Constance Pickering">Constance Pickering</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a> took the form of a unicorn.<sup id="cite_ref-BabyBeasts_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BabyBeasts-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Volunteer members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> had to repel the giant serpents with the <a href="/wiki/Exploding_Charm" title="Exploding Charm">Exploding Charm</a> to stun them and liberate the baby Unicorns, allowing the Foundables to return to their rightful place.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>

<ul><li>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/unicorn" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:unicorn">unicorn</a> was a creature that the ancient Greeks believed resided in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. Though commonly depicted in modern fantasy (as in <i>Harry Potter</i>) as being a <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horse</a> with a single horn, unicorns in antiquity were also described as <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goats</a>, wild <a href="/wiki/Donkey" title="Donkey">donkeys</a>, or some form of a hybrid creature. The concept of them being more comfortable with <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches than wizards</a> possibly stems from <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval</a> beliefs that only a maiden could tame a unicorn. It also likely stems from how unicorns are often made to appeal to young girls.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> tries to sneak up on the unicorn with a knife and fork, but is scared off by <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> who shows him his fist. <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Hagrid</a> then proceeds to care for the sick unicorn like a child.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>It is possible the author was inspired to place Unicorns within the Forbidden Forest at Hogwarts, based on the fact that the unicorn is the national animal of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, where Hogwarts is located.</li>
<li>The unicorn is one of the nine possible Patronus forms for <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a>. It can be achieved by having a higher Empathy stat or choosing the option "protect friends" and later, choosing "Equine" as the Patronus form.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
