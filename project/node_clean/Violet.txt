<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Violet?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Violet?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> hung in a small chamber off of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>. Because of this, she witnessed when <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and the other <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> participants were given the information about the tournament. She was friends with the <a href="/wiki/Fat_Lady" title="Fat Lady">Fat Lady</a> and is known to go to her painting as well as going other places to find things to gossip about.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF17_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF17-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Violet and the Fat Lady were known to drink too much on special occasions such as <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF23_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF23-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She also gave <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> a wink during the Triwizard Tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF31_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF31-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Violet?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Violet?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Violet" comes from the English word violet for the purple flower, ultimately derived from Latin viola. It was common in Scotland from the 16th century, and it came into general use as an English given name during the 19th century.
</p>
