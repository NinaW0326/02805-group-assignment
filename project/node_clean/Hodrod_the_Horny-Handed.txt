<b>Hodrod the Horny-Handed</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a> activist who <a href="/wiki/Goblin_magic" title="Goblin magic">shrunk</a> three <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> and then tried to crush them.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> arrested him for these actions. This did not sit well with the goblin community, because when the <a href="/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Goblins" title="Brotherhood of Goblins">Brotherhood of Goblins</a> met with representatives from the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Regulation_and_Control_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures">Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chipping_Clodbury" title="Chipping Clodbury">Chipping Clodbury</a>, they demanded that he be released.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hodrod was also mentioned in a question on the third <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">W.O.M.B.A.T. test</a>, which gave his imprisonment as one of several possible motivations for the <a href="/wiki/Goblin_Rebellions" title="Goblin Rebellions">Goblin Rebellions</a> of the <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th</a> and <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th centuries</a>. This answer is obviously a red herring, because the <a href="/wiki/Chipping_Clodbury_riot" title="Chipping Clodbury riot">Chipping Clodbury Riot</a> occurred at least two centuries later, in <a href="/wiki/1999" title="1999">1999</a>.
</p>
