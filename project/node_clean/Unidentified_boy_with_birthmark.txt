<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_boy_with_birthmark?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_boy_with_birthmark?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, the boy went to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Salem_Church" title="Second Salem Church">Second Salem Church</a>, where poor children were fed in exchange for distributing leaflets. While he was waiting in line for soup, <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_poor_boy" title="Unidentified poor boy">another boy</a> asked <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">New Salem Philanthropic Society</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone" title="Mary Lou Barebone">Mary Lou Barebone</a> if the birthmark on his face was a "<a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">witch's</a> mark," to which she replied that it was not.<sup id="cite_ref-FBS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
