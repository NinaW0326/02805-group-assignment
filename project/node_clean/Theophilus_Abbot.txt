<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Theophilus_Abbot?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Theophilus_Abbot?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He studied <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> families which descended from <a href="/wiki/Scourers" title="Scourers">Scourers</a> who escaped justice following the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a>, and identified in each family a deep belief in the existence of magic and an intense hatred of it, convictions passed down from their vengeful ancestors.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermoreHistory2_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermoreHistory2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He authored the book <i><a href="/wiki/Scourers_and_the_Creation_of_MACUSA" title="Scourers and the Creation of MACUSA">Scourers and the Creation of MACUSA</a></i> sometime prior to <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cb_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cb-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
