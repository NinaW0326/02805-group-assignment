<p><b>Professor Igor Karkaroff</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/24_June" title="24 June">24 June</a> – <a href="/wiki/31_July" title="31 July">31 July</a>, <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-HBP6_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP6-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang Institute</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, he was a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> who served <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. After being captured and briefly imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> following his master's defeat, Karkaroff turned in several of his fellow Death Eaters to the <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> in exchange for his crimes being pardoned. 
</p><p>Karkaroff later went on to become Durmstrang's headmaster, leading the school's delegation to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. There, Karkaroff did everything he could to get his school's champion (<a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>) in the lead, and flew into a panic when his <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a> began to burn. When Voldemort returned, he fled. For his treachery, Karkaroff was hunted down and murdered by his former comrades in the summer of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>. 
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Life">Early Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early Life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Virtually nothing is known about Igor's early life. He did, however, refer <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> as "Dear old Hogwarts", suggesting he at least visited it in the past, or even was educated there during his time as a student.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF15_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF15-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Wizarding_War">First Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire"><b>Karkaroff</b>: "<i>You must understand that He-Who-Must-Not-Be-Named operated always in the greatest secrecy…we never knew the names of every one of our fellows — He alone knew exactly who we all were —</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Alastor Moody</b>: "<i>Which was a wise move, wasn't it, as it prevented someone like you, Karkaroff, from turning all of them in.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Karkaroff turns in his fellow Death Eaters<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p>In his younger days, Karkaroff was a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> and acquaintance of <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>. It was reported that Karkaroff helped <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a> <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">torture</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and non-supporters of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. He was eventually captured by <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> and spent a short period of time in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3a/Harry-potter-goblet-of-fire-igor.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20161129045916" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/3a/Harry-potter-goblet-of-fire-igor.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20161129045916" decoding="async" width="250" height="104" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Harry-potter-goblet-of-fire-igor.jpg" data-image-key="Harry-potter-goblet-of-fire-igor.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Harry-potter-goblet-of-fire-igor.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Karkaroff gives information about other Death Eaters</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Shortly after Voldemort's first downfall, Karkaroff appeared before the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Magical_Law" title="Council of Magical Law">Council of Magical Law</a>, offering information in exchange for leniency. He claimed to have felt great remorse for his crimes as a Death Eater, however Moody was sceptical. Among the names he gave were <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Dolohov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travers" title="Travers">Travers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulciber_(Marauder-era)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mulciber (Marauder-era)">Mulciber</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Snape</a>. He became increasingly desperate as it was revealed that these names were worthless; the Death Eaters he mentioned had already been captured, killed, or, in Snape’s case, had switched sides.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>However, Karkaroff was able to satisfy the Council when he submitted the name <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood" title="Augustus Rookwood">Augustus Rookwood</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Unspeakable" title="Unspeakable">Unspeakable</a> who was spying on the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> for Voldemort. This information was new to the Ministry, and he was sent back to Azkaban for a review of his case. From <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s comment that Karkaroff "put a load of other people into Azkaban in his place", it can be assumed that he named other Death Eaters in addition to Rookwood after his initial hearing or that the discovery of Rookwood helped the Ministry weed out other Death Eaters and other Voldemort supporters.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<p>At some point after his release from Azkaban, Karkaroff became Headmaster of <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang Institute</a>. During his time as head of the school, Professor Karkaroff was seen by both students and parents alike as unprincipled and egotistical, leading many parents to withdraw their children from Durmstrang during his administration since he encouraged a environment dominated by fear and intimidation from his students.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The school also became notorious for its detailed teaching of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">academic class</a>; however, it is possible that this reputation existed to some extent even before his headmaster-ship.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%931995_school_year" title="1994–1995 school year">1994–1995 school year</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> was held for the first time in many years at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Karkaroff ventured to Hogwarts to supervise his students and to prepare Durmstrang’s champion for the Tournament, as well as to serve as one of the five judges. From the moment of his arrival at Hogwarts, it was obvious that Karkaroff was heavily biased towards the champion from his own school, <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF15_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF15-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Karkaroff's first evening at Hogwarts, he encountered former <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> "<a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Mad-Eye Moody</a>" (who was actually <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> in disguise), who was Hogwarts’ new <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor. It was apparent that Karkaroff and "Moody" not only knew each other, but had an unpleasant history. Karkaroff unsuccessfully tried to avoid "Moody" after that first evening.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF16_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF16-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It is likely that Barty Crouch Jnr despised Karkaroff himself because of his betrayal of Lord Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

While he had to accept Harry’s presence in the competition, he consistently gave Harry lower scores on the tasks and blatantly favoured Krum over the other champions. He also sneaked around to gather information on the tasks in hopes of giving Krum an advantage. He also ruthlessly insulted Dumbledore when <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Crouch</a> attacked Krum in the shadows and accused him of foul-play, going as far as spitting on the ground at Dumbledore's feet. <p>About halfway through the school year, Karkaroff's actions became increasingly suspicious. At the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>, which he attended with <a href="/wiki/Aurora_Sinistra" title="Aurora Sinistra">Professor Aurora Sinistra</a>, and again after a <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a> lesson, he confronted <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Professor Snape</a> about a secret matter. His agitated and increasingly apprehensive state aroused the suspicions of Harry Potter, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, Harry’s godfather, told him to be wary of Karkaroff because he was a former <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

Snape admitted that Karkaroff had come to him, concerned about the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a> on his left forearm, a mark Snape also bore as a former <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>. The Mark had been growing darker throughout the school year, signalling Voldemort’s growing strength. Karkaroff feared Voldemort’s return because he had betrayed too many of the Dark Lord's servants to be allowed back into the fold. <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> doubted he would get far, as the Dark Lord had "ways of tracking his enemies" and no one who had ever betrayed the Dark Lord ever got far.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF35_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF35-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karkaroff was described to be tall and thin like <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, but with an unctuously cheerful demeanour, while his smile "didn't reach his eyes", which were blue and remained cold and unmoving.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This made him slightly unnerving. His weak chin was not completely covered by his goatee, which ended with a small curl. His teeth were yellow and he liked to dress in sleek, silver furs. As a former <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>, he bore the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a> on his left inner forearm.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>One, too cowardly to return... he will pay...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s low opinion of Igor<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Karkaroff was a ruthless and immoral man, having been a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> in his youth and a turncoat when it suited him, as well as showing blatant favouritism as a judge of the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> to increase the chances that his <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">school</a>'s champion, <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>, would win. Karkaroff’s betrayal of his Death Eater comrades to avoid <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>, as well as his going into hiding following <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s return, indicate that he was not at all a brave nor loyal man, somewhat similar to <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>, who betrayed the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> out of fear as well. During his trial, Igor feigned regret and flattery to the judges, showing him to be a talented actor and liar, a skill <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> informed <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> of. Despite his position as headmaster of Durmstrang, he showed neither responsibility nor care for his students, as he encouraged an atmosphere of fear and intimidation at Durmstrang while it was under his stewardship, forcing his students to steer the ship while he stayed in the cabin, harshly scolding <a href="/wiki/Poliakoff" title="Poliakoff">Poliakoff</a> in contrast to doting on the famous Krum, and then ultimately abandoned all of them to protect his own life, showing his immense selfishness, egotism and cowardice.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>His membership in the Death Eater organisation and implementation — or, at least, continuation — of a policy of refusing to admit <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a> to <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang</a>, indicated that Karkaroff was prejudiced against those of different <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood status</a> (though if so he had did not seem to have the level of sadistic, genocidal hatred possessed by other Death Eaters such as <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corban_Yaxley" title="Corban Yaxley">Yaxley</a>). Nevertheless, Karkaroff was (at least during his time with the Death Eaters), something of a sadist, as he was noted to have assisted Dolohov in torturing Muggles, and even after he "reformed" during his headship of Durmstrang, he encouraged an environment where intimidation and fear rule over the school, leading parents to see him as unprincipled and egotistical. Even his warm greeting to Albus Dumbledore when he came to Hogwarts was less proof of a civil nature, and more proof of his ability to play at civility when it suited him. This is supported by his later yelling at Dumbledore after Harry Potter's name came out of the Goblet of Fire, and even spitting at his feet.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Although Karkaroff proved himself to be a skilled actor and liar, he was also poor with controlling his emotions, as his short-temper led him to become increasingly angry and rude at Dumbledore and the British Ministry of Magic for allowing Harry to participate in the Triwizard Tournament that culminated to him spitting in front of Dumbledore's feet. His lack of bravery led him to continuously bombard Snape about the Dark Mark's increasing colour, constantly expressing a worried tone and expression.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>Karkaroff seemed to have been a reasonably skilled <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, as it was said that it was impressive how he remained hidden from Voldemort for so long after the latter's rebirth, although Voldemort was keeping a low profile during the Ministry's denial, and that Karkaroff's death occurred shortly after the Ministry finally saw and accepted Voldemort's return further enforces this as a reason for his year-long survival. He was able to gain knowledge of a good number of Death Eaters' identities, despite Voldemort's tight secretive operations, including the otherwise undiscovered spy <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood" title="Augustus Rookwood">Augustus Rookwood</a>, showing that he was at least skilled in intelligence gathering.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>While the extent of his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> skills is unknown, Karkaroff must have been a skilled wizard, given that he was the Headmaster of <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang</a> as well as a former <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>. His skills were such that he was able to evade the likes of <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> himself for extended periods of time.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Being a former Death Eater, Karkaroff would have been highly skilled in the Dark Arts. According to <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>, Karkaroff was proficient with the <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">Unforgivable Curses</a>. He was also able to evade the aforementioned veteran <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> for six months. More impressive still was that he successfully managed to stay hidden from <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> himself for an entire year, presumably using various spells and enchantments (although whether these spells were Dark Magic is unknown) to make it harder for the Dark Lord to track him. Furthermore, whilst he was the Headmaster of Durmstrang, he encouraged the teaching of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts</a> as an academic subject, heavily implying that despite his abandonment of the Death Eaters and their cause, he was still a dedicated <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Acting skills</b>: Karkaroff was a very skilled actor and was able to convince others about his apparently benevolent convictions deceptively. During his trial before the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Magical_Law" title="Council of Magical Law">Council of Magical Law</a>, he successfully feigned flattery to the judges as well as remorse for his crimes, which could have significantly helped his lenient sentencing and eventual release from Azkaban. This shows he was able to successfully and very likely dishonestly persuade very accomplished individuals of his supposed good will, although he failed to convince more cynical and observant individuals such as <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>, who suspected he was being disingenuous. His notable acting ability was however noted by Sirius Black, who warned Harry Potter of his deceptive and cunning nature. In truth he did not remotely see the errors of his ways. He was able to put on the facade of a civil professional during his visit to Hogwarts, though this became mitigated by his rising short-temper of Harry's selection and fear of Lord Voldemort, causing him to throw tantrums at Dumbledore or annoyingly bombard Snape.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<p>In his younger days, Karkaroff was a friend and comrade of <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>. After Voldemort's first downfall, Karkaroff was caught by Aurors and briefly imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. He betrayed many Death Eaters in order to avoid remaining in Azkaban. One of them was Snape, which might have indicated a strained relationship after Snape's betrayal. Karkaroff learned that Snape had abandoned the Death Eaters and become a member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, and therefore Karkaroff was dismayed to hear yet another one of his names was useless.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>      
</p><p>Karkaroff met Snape again in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. When his <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a> began to burn, Karkaroff told Snape about it, because he knew that Snape had felt it too. Snape told him that he himself had nothing to be afraid of, and asked him if he (Karkaroff) could say the same. Snape certainly seemed more and more irritated by Karkaroff's constant bombardment on the issue, and continued to evade him more and more.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>, Snape also told <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a> that Karkaroff was planning to escape if Voldemort returned. Snape added that he wasn't "such a coward", and Dumbledore replied that Snape was "a braver man by far than Igor Karkaroff".<sup id="cite_ref-DH33_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH33-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> After Voldemort's return, Karkaroff, unlike Snape, escaped <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> and went into hiding. He was killed by Death Eaters some time in the next year. It is unknown how Severus Snape reacted when he learned of this.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_Eaters">Death Eaters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death Eaters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Death Eaters"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Karkaroff betrayed many <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> during his attempt to avoid imprisonment in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. These Death Eaters included <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travers" title="Travers">Travers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mulciber_(Marauder-era)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mulciber (Marauder-era)">Mulciber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Rookwood" title="Augustus Rookwood">Augustus Rookwood</a>; he became desperate when he heard some of these Death Eaters were already caught or killed, worried they did not contribute to his own pardon as opposed to actually mourning for his comrades, as he feigned congratulations for the judges for these arrests. It is most likely that he had betrayed many other Death Eaters, as <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> stated that Karkaroff had "put a load of other people into Azkaban in his place". <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Bartemius Crouch Junior</a> came to hate Igor for his disloyalty to Voldemort, and repeatedly intimidated him while disguised as <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>, even musing on how Voldemort would easily track down a fleeing Karkaroff. On the other hand, fellow rogue Death Eater <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> apparently retained a positive relationship with Karkaroff, as he intended to send his son <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a> to Durmstrang, partially due to knowing the headmaster personally.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Dark_Lord" class="mw-redirect" title="Return of the Dark Lord">Voldemort's rebirth</a>, Karkaroff didn't return to Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF33_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF33-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He was eventually caught and killed by Death Eaters in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP6_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP6-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p><a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> was the <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> who captured Karkaroff after Voldemort's downfall, so naturally hated Karkaroff for being a Death Eater and a coward. He also participated in Karkaroff's trial, and told Dumbledore that they should take information from Karkaroff and then send him back to the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>. However, Dumbledore refused.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_11-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Moody and Karkaroff met again in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. In fact, he was not the real Alastor Moody, but <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a>, a fellow Death Eater. Nevertheless, Igor became very shocked and seemingly frightened upon seeing the face of the one who arrested him.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF16_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF16-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> was chosen for the Tournament, Karkaroff got very angry, because Hogwarts had two champions. When Barty (disguised as Moody) told him how the Goblet of Fire might have been fooled, Karkaroff told him that he thought like a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>. Barty answered that it was his job to capture Dark Wizards, as Karkaroff should know, implying that Moody was, in part at least, responsible for Karkaroff's capture. Not knowing it was really Crouch, who hated Karkaroff as much as the real Moody, though for very different reasons, Karkaroff was careful around him not to give Moody a solid pretext for attacking him, backing off when the Auror showed signs of readying for battle, and pointing out Moody's paranoia to others around.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Karkaroff was the Headmaster of the <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang Institute</a>. He treated his students like servants, making them steer the <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Ship" title="Durmstrang Ship">Durmstrang Ship</a> while he stayed in his cabin.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He even encouraged an environment where intimidation and fear ruled over the institute, leading parents to see him as unprincipled and egotistical, and many of them withdrawing their children from the school as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The only student Karkaroff seemed to respect and care about was <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>, most likely because Krum was a famous professional <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> player prodigy, and Karkaroff thought that he would win the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> for him. Karkaroff doted on Krum when he felt unwell after their journey to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, although he expressed no such concern for his other students. He constantly gave Viktor full marks on the tournament while giving the other champions less than what they deserve, and even provided his own champion information as part of the tournament's tradition of cheating. Karkaroff also refused to allow <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> students to study at Durmstrang, and seemed to disapprove of Krum's relationship with Muggle-born <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Disregarding how much he desired Viktor win the Triwizard Tournament, Karkaroff felt his own life was far more important, as the moment he felt Voldemort summoning all Death Eaters, he abandoned his care for the tournament's results and his students and fled faraway. They did not appear to miss him much after this.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>Igor</i> is a Slavic name derived from the Scandinavian name <i>Ingvar</i>, meaning "protected by Ing"; Ing the name of the norse goddess of fertility. Igor is also a stock character of horror literature and films, usually acting as a servant to the villain.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Karkar</i> is a name which was common among Kipchaks<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>, a Turkic tribe which inhabited today's Kazakhstan during ca. 10-12c.; <i>"-off"</i> is a possessive suffix meaning <i>"[son] of"</i> in Slavic languages turning a given name into a surname which was also attached to non-Slavic names as part of Russification policies in the former USSR.</li>
<li>"<i>Kark</i>" also means "<i>the unsettled</i>" in Turkish.</li>
<li>Also, in several Slavic languages such as Russian <i>"kar</i>" is an onomatopoeia word, which represents the sound crows make (an equivalent to English "<i>caw</i>").</li>
<li>Karkaroff is also an alternate name for <a href="https://lotr.fandom.com/wiki/Carcharoth" class="extiw" title="lotr:Carcharoth">Carcharoth</a> the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> from <a href="https://lotr.fandom.com/wiki/J.R.R._Tolkien" class="extiw" title="lotr:J.R.R. Tolkien">J.R.R Tolkien</a>'s <i><a href="https://lotr.fandom.com/wiki/The_Silmarillion" class="extiw" title="lotr:The Silmarillion">The Silmarillion</a></i>. It translates as "The Red Maw".</li></ul>

He worked with <a href="/wiki/Warwick_Davis" title="Warwick Davis">Warwick Davis</a> in the 2008 film <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_Prince_Caspian"><i>The Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian</i></a>, which he was also credited as "Pedja".</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, Karkaroff also turned in <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> for <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">torturing</a> <a href="/wiki/Alice_Longbottom" title="Alice Longbottom">Alice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Longbottom" title="Frank Longbottom">Frank Longbottom</a>.</li>
<li>Karkaroff's murder is never mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, neither by the characters nor the headlines of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>. However, there is an image in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_Limited_Edition" title="Harry Potter Limited Edition">Harry Potter Limited Edition</a></i> of a <i>Daily Prophet</i> article talking about Karkaroff's murder, possibly hinting that it was originally designed for the film before getting scrapped through unknown reasons, possibly as Karkaroff's mention wasn't relevant to the story.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>, Karkaroff cannot use <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark magic</a> despite having once been a Death Eater. However, this may be because he had defected prior to when the game takes place. In the DS version of <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>, however, his character is able to perform it.</li>
<li>In the book, Karkaroff's hair was silver, but in the film it was black and silver. His hair is shown to be black during his trial in <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a>-<a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">81</a> in both the book and the film. In the book his eyes were also blue, but in the film they are brown.</li></ul>
