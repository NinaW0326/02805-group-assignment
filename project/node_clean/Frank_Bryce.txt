<p><b>Frank Bryce</b> (<a href="/wiki/August" title="August">August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1917" title="1917">1917</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/16_August" title="16 August">16 August</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-deathday_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deathday-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> who lived in the village of <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. After serving in the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, he returned to Little Hangleton and became the gardener for the <a href="/wiki/Riddle_family" title="Riddle family">Riddle family</a>. He was the prime suspect of the family's murder, a crime he did not commit.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two unidentified families lived at the Riddle house, but neither stayed long. <a href="/wiki/Wealthy_man_who_owned_the_Riddle_House" title="Wealthy man who owned the Riddle House">A wealthy man</a> later owned the house during Frank's last days, although he did not live at the house, being said to keep it "for tax reasons". Shortly before his 77th birthday, Bryce was murdered by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> after <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> told Voldemort and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> that Bryce was overhearing them discussing their plans to murder <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>Horrible temper. I remember, when he was a kid...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Dot" title="Dot">Dot</a> regarding Frank<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>Frank was born in <a href="/wiki/1917" title="1917">1917</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> family. He lived in <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Dot" title="Dot">Dot</a> in his childhood: this makes it likely that Frank was born in the village. He was said to have been quite short-tempered as a child.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_World_War">Second World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Second World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second World War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>He had a hard war, Frank.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;A woman at <a href="/wiki/The_Hanged_Man" title="The Hanged Man">The Hanged Man</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1939" title="1939">1939</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> started, and Frank was called into military service. He fought well for three years of the war, but his leg was wounded and remained very stiff even after it healed, so he left the armed forces afterwards, presumably because the wound made him incapable of fighting.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<dd></dd></dl></div><p>By <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a>, Frank returned to <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a> with a great dislike of crowds and loud noises, and his leg was still stiff. Despite his injury, he started working as caretaker and gardener for the elderly and snobbish squire <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle" title="Thomas Riddle">Thomas Riddle</a>, who lived with his wife <a href="/wiki/Mary_Riddle" title="Mary Riddle">Mary</a> and adult son <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle_Senior" title="Tom Riddle Senior">Tom</a> in the luxurious <a href="/wiki/Riddle_House" title="Riddle House">Riddle House</a> on a hill overlooking the village, in which he was given a <a href="/wiki/Cottage_of_Frank_Bryce" title="Cottage of Frank Bryce">small cottage</a> on the Riddles' property.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

Upon learning of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle_Senior" title="Tom Riddle Senior">his father</a>'s actions toward <a href="/wiki/Merope_Riddle" title="Merope Riddle">Merope</a> and himself from his <a href="/wiki/Morfin_Gaunt" title="Morfin Gaunt">uncle</a>, he decided to murder his father. That same evening, Frank Bryce saw Riddle climbing the hill towards the <a href="/wiki/Riddle_House" title="Riddle House">Riddle House</a> but did not accost him or do anything, unaware of how his life would be forever changed by the boy.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The next morning, a <a href="/wiki/Riddles%27_maid" title="Riddles&#39; maid">maid</a> found all three Riddles dead in the Riddle House's drawing room. The <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a> arrested Frank as their main suspect, since he had the only key to the mansion and there had been no sign of forced entry, and he was taken to the neighbouring town of <a href="/wiki/Great_Hangleton" title="Great Hangleton">Great Hangleton</a> for questioning. Though Bryce explained that the last person he saw near the Riddle House was the dark-haired boy, nobody else had seen him and was sure that Bryce invented him. However, the report on the Riddles' bodies was unable to determine the cause of their death, and Frank was released due to lack of evidence, although he was still widely suspected by his neighbours.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

He continued to be paid as the gardener by the house's succession of owners. As he aged, he was plagued by the pain in his leg, and by the vandalism of local boys, which he suspected was motivated by the belief that he was a murderer. The boys would usually throw rocks into the Riddle House, trample or ride their bicycles on the flowerbeds that Frank worked hard to keep smooth or even break in the House for a dare, then flee when Frank tried to pursue them, unsuccessfully due to his injured leg.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Death"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">

<p>On <a href="/wiki/16_August" title="16 August">16 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Frank Bryce, now nearing his 77th birthday, woke from the pain in his leg, and saw a light in the <a href="/wiki/Riddle_House" title="Riddle House">Riddle House</a>, and he assumed the local boys had broken in and set fire to it. Not having a telephone or trusting the police since his arrest, Frank entered the house to investigate and was surprised to see not vandals, but <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, hidden from view, and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">servant</a> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Wormtail</a>, discussing on taking <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s life. Going very deaf from old age, Frank listened with his better right ear, and though confused with terms such as "<a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>," he thought they must be codes, and he figured enough to learn that the master had killed <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">a woman</a> with amusement and were plotting another murder, directed to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">a boy</a>. Frank thought he must report this to the police at once, but before he could successfully flee, he encountered <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>, the snake.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>When Nagini discovered Frank and reported him to Voldemort, the Dark Lord welcomed Frank into the room. In a failed attempt to bluff his way out, Frank said that his wife would notice his disappearance, but Voldemort <a href="/wiki/Legilimency" title="Legilimency">saw through</a> this deception and found that nobody knew that he was there. After challenging Voldemort to show his face "like a man", to which Voldemort replied that he was "much more than a man", Frank was horrified to see a mutilated body in the chair, which made him scream loudly. He screamed so loud that he never heard the <a href="/wiki/Incantation" title="Incantation">incantation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> that took his life, nor did he live to learn that this gruesome form was the "dark-haired boy" he saw that killed the Riddle family and caused all the distrust in Frank for half a century.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">home</a> in <a href="/wiki/Little_Whinging" title="Little Whinging">Little Whinging</a>, located two hundred miles from Little Hangleton, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> had a vivid dream in which he saw Bryce's assassination in the moment it actually happened.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As it usually occurred when he had such visions, his <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_scars" title="Harry Potter&#39;s scars">forehead scar</a> hurt considerably.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>Always thought he was odd. Unfriendly, like. I'm sure if I've offered him a cuppa once, I've offered it a hundred times. Never wanted to mix, he didn't.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The <a href="/wiki/Riddles%27_cook" title="Riddles&#39; cook">Riddles' cook</a> on Frank Bryce<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Frank was described to have a "horrible temper" since he was a child. He was rather unsociable and solitary; according to the <a href="/wiki/Riddles%27_cook" title="Riddles&#39; cook">Riddles' cook</a>, he "never wanted to mix". After his experiences during the War, he developed a great hatred of crowds and loud noises. When the time finally came to take action, he managed his fear much easier; in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, despite being terrified of the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">deformed murderer</a> who was sitting in a chair in the Riddle House, Frank confronted him when he was caught eavesdropping.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Upon his temporary return to Earth, during the duel between Harry and Voldemort, he seemed to have a dry acceptance of his <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>. Bryce supported the former, despite never meeting him before, while distracting the latter in revenge for killing him.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Little_Hangleton_residents">Little Hangleton residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Little Hangleton residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Little Hangleton residents"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ever since Frank returned from his military services, he preferred the quiet life, avoiding relationships with the residents of <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a>. He lived a disconnected career, refusing the <a href="/wiki/Riddles%27_cook" title="Riddles&#39; cook">Riddles' cook</a> offers for a drink. After the murder of the Riddle family, the townspeople suspected Frank to be the murderer, and despite his release due to lack of evidence, they nevertheless remained distrusting of him, looking down on him for daring to remain in the village. Children would often trample the garden he worked to keep clean, running away before he could catch up with them.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lord_Voldemort">Lord Voldemort</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Lord Voldemort">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Lord Voldemort"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Frank first saw the teenage <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> approaching the Riddle House during the summer of <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a>. He had no way of knowing that this boy would be the one to forever taint his reputation within Little Hangleton, for murdering the Riddles. The police did not believe Frank's alibi of seeing Voldemort approaching the house, but due to lack of evidence, they released the gardener.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over half a century later, Frank would come face-to-face with Voldemort inside the Riddle House, not knowing that he was the very same boy he saw approaching place. Overhearing Voldemort's discussion with his servant <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Wormtail</a>, Frank learned that Voldemort is a remorseless madman who killed <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">a woman</a>, that he plotted to kill <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">a boy</a>, and that he must be informed on to the police. However, Frank was caught by Voldemort's pet snake, <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>, who reported to her master for the gardener's presence. Invited into the drawing room where Voldemort and Wormtail reside, Frank courageously faced the murderer, trying to bluff that his wife would know of his absence. However, Voldemort easily saw through Bryce's bluff, knowing that no one knows of his whereabouts and that he had no wife. When Bryce challenged Voldemort to face him "like a man", he was utterly horrified at the <a href="/wiki/Rudimentary_body_potion" title="Rudimentary body potion">mutilated, rudimentary body</a> he saw, as Voldemort cast the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> to end Frank's life.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Frank later returned to earth temporarily due to a <i><a href="/wiki/Reverse_Spell" title="Reverse Spell">Priori Incantatem</a></i> from Harry and Voldemort's spells clashing, he supported Harry by haunting Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-GF34_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF34-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harry_Potter">Harry Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Harry Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Harry Potter"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although Bryce had no idea who <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> was, he knew the boy would be in danger when he overheard Voldemort's plot to kill him, and attempted to call the Muggle <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a> on the Dark Lord, in an attempt to protect the boy.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1_1-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The night Bryce was assassinated, Harry experienced a vision of the event which manifested through a dream, likely due to his connection to Lord Voldemort.
</p><p>During his temporary return, Bryce supported Harry in the duel against Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-GF34_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF34-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Frank" comes from a Germanic name which referred to a member of the Germanic tribe, the Franks. The Franks settled in the regions now called France and the Netherlands in the 3rd and 4th century. They derived their tribal name from the name of a type of throwing axe that they used. The name was brought to England by the Normans. Notable bearers include author L. Frank Baum (1856-1919) and architect Frank Lloyd Wright (1867-1959).
</p>

<ul><li>Frank was portrayed by the late <a href="/wiki/Eric_Sykes" title="Eric Sykes">Eric Sykes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>.</li>
<li>Frank is one of only five characters in the <i>Harry Potter</i> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">book series</a> whose thoughts have been disclosed by the narrator, the others being <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">Vernon Dursley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>, and the British <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a>. However, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>'s viewpoint is shown via Potter. Other scenes depict <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, but these scenes do not appear to be told from anyone's point of view in particular.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> incorrectly suspected that Frank's death was used by Voldemort to turn <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> into his final <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>, but it was officially stated that <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">Bertha Jorkins</a>' death was used instead. However, as Jorkins was omitted from the films (and she did not appear in the stream of people who appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Reverse_Spell" title="Reverse Spell">Priori Incantatem</a>), Frank's death might have been used instead in the film timeline. However, this still leaves in question how Voldemort knew about the Triwizard Tournament. So Bertha may have been present off-screen, but not killed with Voldemort's wand, perhaps by Nagini instead.</li>
<li>In the book, Frank is invited into the room and killed when he demands Voldemort to show himself. In the film, Voldemort does not tell Wormtail to beckon Frank, but tells him to stand aside and merely kills Bryce on the spot where he was standing.</li>
<li>In the film, Frank has only one line of spoken dialogue: "Bloody kids!". He also screams in fear before his death.</li>
<li>Frank Bryce's role in the story is replaced by a <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton_milkman" title="Little Hangleton milkman">Milkman</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
