<b>Lycoris Black</b> (<a href="/wiki/1904" title="1904">1904</a> – <a href="/wiki/1965" title="1965">1965</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> witch, the middle child of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black_II" title="Sirius Black II">Sirius Black II</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Hesper_Gamp" title="Hesper Gamp">Hesper Gamp</a> and sister of <a href="/wiki/Arcturus_Black_III" title="Arcturus Black III">Arcturus Black III</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black_I" title="Regulus Black I">Regulus</a>. Lycoris never married and never had children.<sup id="cite_ref-BFT_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lycoris_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lycoris_Black?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Lycoris Black's likeness on the <a href="/wiki/Black_Family_Tapestry" title="Black Family Tapestry">Black Family Tapestry</a>, as featured in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, seems to have been based on <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://media.vam.ac.uk/media/thira/collection_images/2006BF/2006BF7031_jpg_l.jpg">a depiction of a nobleman</a> from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devonshire_Hunting_Tapestries" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Devonshire Hunting Tapestries">Devonshire Hunting Tapestries</a>, currently in the collection of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a>, in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.</li>
<li>Based on a statement by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> that all members of the Black family were in his house, Lycoris may have been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin House</a>. However, she attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> c. <a href="/wiki/1915" title="1915">1915</a> - <a href="/wiki/1922" title="1922">1922</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Slughorn</a> may not have been <a href="/wiki/Potions_Master" title="Potions Master">Potions Master</a>.</li>
<li>Lycoris, on the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Black_Family_Tapestry" title="Black Family Tapestry">tapestry</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">the film adaption</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, is depicted like a man rather than a woman.</li>
<li>It can also be noted that Lycoris's name is similar to her distant relative <a href="/wiki/Licorus_Black" title="Licorus Black">Licorus Black</a> who appears only on the version of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Family_Tapestry" title="Black Family Tapestry">Black family tree tapestry</a> shown in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lycoris_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lycoris_Black?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lycoris" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lycoris">Lycoris</a></i> means "twilight" in Greek. This name loosely fits into the Black family tradition of naming people after stars, in the sense that twilight denotes the times of day when the stars first begin to appear, or to disappear. It is also a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lycoris_(genus)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lycoris (genus)">genus</a> of flowering plants in the <i>Amaryllis</i> family that is associated with death and reincarnation in Chinese and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> folklore; another member of family with a name of flower is <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>. Ovid and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgil" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Virgil">Virgil</a> also mention a mistress of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Antony" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Mark Antony">Mark Antony</a> called Lycoris. It is also possible that this name was derived from the plant and sweet <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Licorice" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Licorice">liquorice</a>. The name is also similar to that of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lycorias" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lycorias">Lycorias</a>, a Nereid (sea nymph) of Greek mythology.
</p>
