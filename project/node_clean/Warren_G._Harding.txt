<b>Warren G. Harding</b> (born <a href="/wiki/2_November" title="2 November">2 November</a> <a href="/wiki/1865" class="mw-redirect" title="1865">1865</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> politician who belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party" title="Republican Party">Republican Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He served as the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the United States of America">President of the United States of America</a> from <a href="/wiki/4_March" title="4 March">4 March</a> <a href="/wiki/1921" title="1921">1921</a> to an unknown date.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In its <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> issue, the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Chronicle" title="New York Chronicle">New York Chronicle</a></i> reported that President Harding had delivered an in-person address before the <a class="new" title="United States Congress (page does not exist)" rel="nofollow" data-uncrawlable-url="L3dpa2kvVW5pdGVkX1N0YXRlc19Db25ncmVzcz9hY3Rpb249ZWRpdCZyZWRsaW5rPTE=">U.S. Congress</a> that day, challenging opponents of the <a href="/wiki/Ship_Subsidy_Bill" title="Ship Subsidy Bill">Ship Subsidy Bill</a> to work to defeat it instead of ignoring it because "the failure of Congress to act decisively [would] be no less disastrous than adverse action."<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Warren_G._Harding?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Warren_G._Harding?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warren_G._Harding" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Warren G. Harding">Warren G. Harding</a> died on <a href="/wiki/2_August" title="2 August">2 August</a> <a href="/wiki/1923" title="1923">1923</a> of a cerebral haemorrhage while serving his first term as president.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This makes the multiple references to President Harding in articles in the <a href="/wiki/File:New_York_Chronicle_-_Monday_Dec_1926_(HQ).png" title="File:New York Chronicle - Monday Dec 1926 (HQ).png">prop issue</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Chronicle" title="New York Chronicle">New York Chronicle</a></i> featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(film)" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i> anachronistic, as the film is set in <a href="/wiki/December" title="December">December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, three years after Harding's death in office. However, it can be presumed that Harding did not die in 1923 in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_universe" title="Harry Potter universe"><i>Harry Potter</i> universe</a>, and that he was elected to a second term.</li>
<li>The text of the articles mentioning Harding in the prop issue of the <i>New York Chronicle</i> were taken from the real <a href="/wiki/21_November" title="21 November">21 November</a> <a href="/wiki/1922" title="1922">1922</a> issue of <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Evening_World" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Evening World">The Evening World</a></i> and document actual historical events (albeit anachronistically).<sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
