<ul><li>Harfang was not Featured on the Longbottom family tree shown on Pottermore, but he is Featured on the Black Family tree.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Harfang_Longbottom?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Harfang_Longbottom?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Harfang may be derived from <i>Harfang des neiges</i>, a French term for "snowy owl."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In <i>The Silver Chair</i>, the sixth book in the <i>The Chronicles of Narnia</i> series by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C.S._Lewis" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:C.S. Lewis">C.S. Lewis</a>, Harfang is a city of giants in the far north.
</p><p>J.R.R Tolkien, the author of The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings, gave the name Longbottom to one of the Hobbits' villages in the&#160;Shire, which is known for its pipe-weed.
</p>
