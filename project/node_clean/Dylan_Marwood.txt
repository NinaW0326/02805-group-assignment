<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dylan_Marwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dylan_Marwood?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fluent in <a href="/wiki/Mermish" title="Mermish">Mermish</a>, he wrote a book on the language and culture of <a href="/wiki/Merpeople" title="Merpeople">Merpeople</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Merpeople:_A_Comprehensive_Guide_to_Their_Language_and_Customs" title="Merpeople: A Comprehensive Guide to Their Language and Customs">Merpeople: A Comprehensive Guide to Their Language and Customs</a></i>. His <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> was made out of <a href="/wiki/Apple_(tree)" title="Apple (tree)">applewood</a>, a fact which may have been connected to his skill with language, as apple wands were reputed to be common among <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches and wizards</a> with a special talent for speaking to magical <a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">beings</a> in their own tongues.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dylan_Marwood?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dylan_Marwood?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>It is possible that Marwood studied at <b>Merge School of Under-Water Spellage</b> in the Red Sea in order to prepare for underwater expeditions to study the ways of the merpeople.</li></ul>
