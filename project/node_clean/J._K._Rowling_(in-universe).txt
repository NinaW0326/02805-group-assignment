<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling_(in-universe)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling_(in-universe)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>J. K. Rowling authored <i><a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Book" title="Wizards&#39; Book">Wizards' Book</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-JKRS_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/2008" title="2008">2008</a> edition of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">The Tales of Beedle the Bard </a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Along with <a href="/wiki/Comic_Relief_(in-universe)" title="Comic Relief (in-universe)">Comic Relief</a>, she set up a fund in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s name for proceeds from the sales of special copies of <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages" title="Quidditch Through the Ages">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i> to be used to help struggling children worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-FBE_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QTAE_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAE-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She would later associate with another charity, the <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_High_Level_Group" title="Children&#39;s High Level Group">Children's High Level Group</a>, to distribute royalties from the sale of <i>The Tales of Beedle the Bard</i>.
</p><p>Rowling was also apparently associated with the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_Examinations_Authority" title="Wizarding Examinations Authority">Wizarding Examinations Authority</a>, as her signature appeared on completion certificates for the <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">Wizards' Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test</a>.
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>J.K. Rowling is a world authority on Quidditch and has been writing about the sport since 1990. Royston Idlewild, former player and International Director of the ICWQC, described her as ‘perhaps the only person in the world who knows more about Quidditch than I do.’</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In addition to this, Rowling had access to a <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> on which she frequently used the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PPA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PPA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She was the Quidditch correspondent for  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i>, and provided information for them on the <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a> to share with <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>; in addition to this, she gave insight into Muggle sports from a wizarding perspective as well.<sup id="cite_ref-PPA_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PPA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She had knowledge of magical history, and wrote the book <a href="/wiki/Magic_in_North_America" title="Magic in North America">Magic in North America</a>.
</p>
