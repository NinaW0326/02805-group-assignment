<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Phillipus_von_Hohenheim?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Phillipus_von_Hohenheim?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his lifetime, Paracelsus contributed heavily to the field of medicine, with his bold theories challenging <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval</a> thought.<sup id="cite_ref-PSGC_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSGC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Paracelsus was credited with the discovery of <a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parseltongue</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSGPC_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSGPC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/4a/Paracelsus.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090623162246" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/4a/Paracelsus.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20090623162246" decoding="async" width="180" height="225" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Paracelsus.jpg" data-image-key="Paracelsus.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Paracelsus.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Paracelsus brewing a <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potion</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>For his contributions to multiple magical fields, he was featured on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> received this card from a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog" title="Chocolate Frog">Chocolate Frog</a> on his first ride aboard the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was a bust of Paracelsus in a corridor at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, while on his way to the <a href="/wiki/Owlery" title="Owlery">Owlery</a>, Harry Potter ran into <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nearly Headless Nick</a>, who warned him that <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> was planning to drop the bust on the head of the next person who walked down the corridor. Harry thus chose to take an alternate route to the Owlery.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP14_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP14-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Phillipus_von_Hohenheim?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Phillipus_von_Hohenheim?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The real life <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paracelsus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Paracelsus">Paracelsus</a> was born Phillip Von Hohenheim, in 1493 in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. He later changed his name to Philippus Theophrastus Aureolus Bombastus von Hohenheim, then to Paracelsus. He has made many contributions to medicine. He believed every human needed a balance of minerals and metals in their body and he was the first to use minerals and metals as medicine. Paracelsus also studied psychotherapy, and was the first to understand the subconscious.</li>
<li>Although he made several important contributions to the sciences of medicine and chemistry, he also wrote several treatises in <a href="/wiki/Astrology" title="Astrology">Astrology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>, which has led to him being portrayed as a magician, or a "pseudo-scientist" in later popular culture, in several works besides the Harry Potter universe.</li></ul>
