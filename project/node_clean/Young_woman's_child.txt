<p>The <b>young woman's child</b> was a character in the tale <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot" title="The Wizard and the Hopping Pot">The Wizard and the Hopping Pot</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Beedle_the_Bard" title="Beedle the Bard">Beedle the Bard</a>. The child was very ill, and when its <a href="/wiki/Young_woman" title="Young woman">mother</a> asked the <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)" title="Wizard (The Wizard and the Hopping Pot)">Wizard</a> for help, he slammed the door on her. Later, when the Wizard could bear no more of his father's <a href="/wiki/The_Hopping_Pot" title="The Hopping Pot">lucky cooking pot</a>, he doused the baby in <a href="/wiki/Dittany" title="Dittany">Dittany</a> and it woke "well and rosy".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
