<b>Rawya Zaghloul</b> was an <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who played as <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Egyptian_National_Quidditch_team" title="Egyptian National Quidditch team">Egyptian National Quidditch team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rawya_Zaghloul?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rawya_Zaghloul?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She played in the <a href="/wiki/2002_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="2002 Quidditch World Cup">2002 Quidditch World Cup</a> where she famously beat Bulgarian <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Snitch</a>, leading to the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_National_Quidditch_team" title="Bulgarian National Quidditch team">Bulgarian National Quidditch team</a>'s defeat. This was a "<i>crushing disappointment</i>" for Bulgaria and, coupled with the team's loss of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a> final, it led to <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>'s retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
