<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>However, a cunning charlatan with no magical power saw a chance of enriching himself.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Charlatan's description<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>The Charlatan</b> was the male antagonist in <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump" title="Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump">Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump</a>, a wizarding fairy tale written by <a href="/wiki/Beedle_the_Bard" title="Beedle the Bard">Beedle the Bard</a> and included in a collection of his stories known as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">The Tales of Beedle the Bard </a></i>. In the story, he tricked a foolish <a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)" title="King (Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump)">King</a> into thinking that the charlatan was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, while he was just a mere <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> who knew no <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> at all. The King appointed him as <a href="/wiki/Grand_Sorcerer" title="Grand Sorcerer">Grand Sorcerer</a> in Chief, the King's Private <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magic</a> Master, so he could teach him his "magic". The King was fooled until <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> made him confess that he was a fraud. He was then ordered to the dungeons. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Instructor_of_magic">Instructor of magic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Instructor of magic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Instructor of magic"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Wanted by King, an Instructor of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magic</a></i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The King's Proclamations<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The King wanted to learn how to use <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, so he had proclamations read in every village across the country, looking for someone with magical abilities to teach him. No real <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards or witches</a> responded, due to the King's earlier decree that all wizards and witches be hunted down and killed by the Brigade of Witch-Hunters. However, the cunning Charlatan pretended to be a wizard of enormous skill. He took the post as the King's Magic Master for purely selfish reasons of gaining money, rubies, a silver chalice and fame.
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c7/Babbityrabbity.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090404104534" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c7/Babbityrabbity.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20090404104534" decoding="async" width="250" height="290" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Babbityrabbity.jpg" data-image-key="Babbityrabbity.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Babbityrabbity.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">The King and the Charlatan practising "magic"</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>One day, while the King and the Charlatan were practising waving their twigs and chanting meaningless rhymes, they heard Babbitty cackling hysterically from her cottage. This enraged the King, who demanded that the Charlatan perform in front of his subjects to show off his new abilities. The Charlatan attempted to back out by saying he had to go out of town, and could not help him, but the King threatened to send the Brigade after him. And if he stayed, and anyone ridiculed the King, the Charlatan's head would be cut off.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Help_from_Babbitty">Help from Babbitty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Help from Babbitty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Help from Babbitty"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>If you fail to help me, I shall denounce you as a witch, and it will be you who is torn apart by the King's hounds!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Charlatan to <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The Charlatan headed to <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>'s house, from where she had been spying on the King and the Charlatan, and found out that she was a real <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>. He demanded that she helped him, threatening that should she refuse, he would turn her in to the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a>. Babbitty agreed to help out the Charlatan. He told Babbitty that she would hide in the bush tomorrow, and do anything in her power to help in this charade.
</p><p>The next day, with Babbitty's help, the King and the Charlatan performed their "<a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>". They astonished the crowd by making a hat <a href="/wiki/Vanishing_Spell" title="Vanishing Spell">disappear</a> and <a href="/wiki/Levitation_Charm" title="Levitation Charm">levitating</a> a horse, but when one of the brigade asked if the King could make his dead hound return to life, the King attempted in vain. Babbitty did nothing, because she knew no <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> can raise the dead. The crowd laughed at the King, and the King wanted to know why the spell wasn't working. The Charlatan pointed to the bush, and said that a wicked <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> was blocking them. Babbitty ran from the bush, and when the hounds chased after her, she <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">disappeared</a>, leaving the dogs barking at a tree.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_of_his_trickery">Discovery of his trickery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Discovery of his trickery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Discovery of his trickery"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>&#8230;as he raised the axe the charlatan fell to his knees, screaming for mercy and confessing all his wickedness.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Charlatan confesses when threatened<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The Charlatan told the crowd that <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> had turned into an tree, and that the tree must be cut down, because she was an "evil" <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>. The tree was then cut down. As the crowd started to leave, they heard a cackling coming from the stump. Babbitty told the crowd that real witches and wizards cannot be cut in half, and that they should cut the "Grand Sorcerer" in two to prove it. Begging for mercy, the Charlatan then confessed that he was a fraud and was sent to the dungeons.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_King">The King</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The King">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The King"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>The charlatan gave one of the twigs to <a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)" title="King (Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump)">the King</a> and assured him that it was a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> with tremendous power.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Charlatan<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)" title="King (Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump)">the King</a> called for <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards or witches</a> to teach him <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, the Charlatan came to the palace claiming to be a gifted wizard with great talents. He performed a few simple tricks, presumably a form of sleight-of-hand, which convinced the King of the Charlatan's magical powers. From then on, the Charlatan pretended to teach the King "magic," despite not knowing any magic himself. In order to get "supplies" for the "magic lessons," he asked for treasures to finance this venture. While the King gave his so-called mentor privileges and rewards out of teaching him, he would not hesitate in giving him death threats for failing to assist him in proving to the audience of his powers.
</p><p>Later, when the King found out that the Charlatan was a fraud, he was sent to the dungeons.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Babbitty">Babbitty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Babbitty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Babbitty"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Cut her down, Your Majesty, that is the way to treat evil witches.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Charlatan<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 202px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/68/Babitty.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20081205235647" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/68/Babitty.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20081205235647" decoding="async" width="200" height="281" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Babitty.jpg" data-image-key="Babitty.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Babitty.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>, the King's washerwoman</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p><a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> was the king's washerwoman. She thought the Charlatan and <a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)" title="King (Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump)">King</a>'s "lessons" were ridiculous and laughed at the King, putting the Charlatan in great danger due to the King's rage. When the Charlatan came to see her, he blackmailed Babbitty to perform the King's spells for him without the King's knowledge, or else he would denounce her as a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a>. Babbitty agreed to help him. The following day, with Babbitty's help, the King and Charlatan pretended to perform "magic". But when one of the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">brigade</a> asked if the King could bring his dead hound back to life, the King attempted to do so in vain. Babbitty did nothing to try to help.
</p><p>Being a true witch, she knew it was useless to attempt to <a href="/wiki/Necromancy" title="Necromancy">bring back the dead</a>. The crowd laughed at the King, and the enraged King asked the Charlatan why the "magic" was not working. The Charlatan betrayed Babbitty and pointed out the bush behind which Babbitty was hiding, claiming that an evil which was blocking the King's spells. Babbitty ran, and a chase began. The King's hounds chased Babbitty, until she "disappeared," while the dogs stood barking at a tree. The Charlatan convinced the King and the crowd that Babbitty had turned into an apple. He told the King to cut down the tree, for that was the way to treat evil witches. After the men eagerly cut down the tree, a loud cackling sound comes from the stump of the tree. Babbitty told the crowd that real wizards and witches cannot be cut in half. She suggested they cut the Charlatan in half to prove it. The terrified Charaltan admitted his trickery, and was sent to the dungeons.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlatan?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The Charlatan was a very cunning man, capable of coming up with the idea to trick the King, and using the King and <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> to gain money and fame. He was also extremely greedy, asking the King for various precious treasures, including a silver chalice, rubies, and a large sack of gold. He betrayed Babbitty to the King to get out of his own troubles, proving his selfishness. His greed and schemes turned against him as Babbitty persuaded the King to slice him down to prove whether he was a wizard or not, which led to the Charlatan to confess in cowardice.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
