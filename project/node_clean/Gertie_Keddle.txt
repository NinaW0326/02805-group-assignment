<p><b>Gertie Keddle</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who lived on the edge of <a href="/wiki/Queerditch_Marsh" title="Queerditch Marsh">Queerditch Marsh</a> in the <a href="/wiki/11th_century" title="11th century">11th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jkr-manchester_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkr-manchester-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QTA3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTA3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A rather uneducated witch by wizarding standards of the time, Gertie kept a <a href="/wiki/Gertie_Keddle%27s_diary" title="Gertie Keddle&#39;s diary">diary</a> in "badly spelled" <a href="/wiki/Saxon" title="Saxon">Saxon</a>, and only knew the name of one day of the week (Tuesday). In her diary, she chronicled the evolution of the <i>"stupid game"</i> the wizards from across the marsh played, that would later become <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>. She thought that the lot who played it, including her friend, <a href="/wiki/Gwenog" title="Gwenog">Gwenog</a>, were idiots. Gertie also owned a <a href="/wiki/Cabbage" title="Cabbage">cabbage</a> patch in which a leather ball, presumably the first ever <a href="/wiki/Quaffle" title="Quaffle">Quaffle</a>, landed.
</p>
