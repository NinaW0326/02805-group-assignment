<p><b>Parvati Patil</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>/<a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a>), was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> heritage, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Patil_twins%27_father" title="Patil twins&#39; father">Mr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patil_twins%27_mother" title="Patil twins&#39; mother">Mrs Patil</a>, and the identical twin sister of <a href="/wiki/Padma_Patil" title="Padma Patil">Padma</a>. She was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, but her sister Padma was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>. Parvati became best friends with <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>, with whom she shared many interests, such as a love for <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>. Parvati attended the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, and a year later, she joined <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, an organisation taught and led by Harry Potter. 
</p><p>During her seventh year in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, she continued her studies at Hogwarts, when it was under the control of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and the same year Parvati fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> against Voldemort and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. It can be assumed that she survived the battle, as her name was not listed among the <a href="/wiki/Fallen_Fifty" title="Fallen Fifty">Fallen Fifty</a>. If she did survive, what she did in her later life is unknown. 
</p>

<p>Parvati began attending Hogwarts in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> and was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> house,<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> while her identical twin sister <a href="/wiki/Padma_Patil" title="Padma Patil">Padma</a> was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Parvati shared her dormitory with her best friend, <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> and two other girls.
</p><p>During her first flying lesson, Parvati came to the defence of housemate Neville Longbottom when <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> threw away his <a href="/wiki/Remembrall" title="Remembrall">Remembrall</a>. <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> student <a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson" title="Pansy Parkinson">Pansy Parkinson</a> made fun of her for this. Later in the lesson, when Harry made a soft landing after recovering the Remembrall and subsequently found himself caught by Professor <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, she was quick to tell Professor McGonagall that the incident was not Harry's fault. Professor McGonagall told both Parvati and Ron Weasley to be quiet and escorted Harry away.<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Parvati attended the Gryffindor vs Hufflepuff Quidditch match in their first year, and joined an excited Hermione in a hug after Harry's speedy capture of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Parvati also told Lavender that Hermione was crying in the girls' bathroom after Ron made fun of her during a Charms class.<sup id="cite_ref-PS10_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>Parvati attended the Yule Ball with Harry Potter in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. She wore bright pink <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil%27s_dress_robes" title="Parvati Patil&#39;s dress robes">dress robes</a> and gold bracelets. She seemed to enjoy the attention she received for being the date of a famous wizard and was eager to dance. However, the dance she had with Harry went poorly. After being ignored by Harry, who was romantically interested in <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a> at the time and spent much of the ball looking at her, a by then annoyed Parvati was asked to dance by another and spent most of the evening with a <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Beauxbatons_students#Beauxbatons_boy" title="Unidentified Beauxbatons students">boy from</a> Beauxbatons, whom she dated for a time afterwards. It's unknown what his name is and how long they dated for, if it lasted through the summer. In the film, it was <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff%27s_aide" class="mw-redirect" title="Igor Karkaroff&#39;s aide">Igor Karkaroff‘s Aide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

She enjoyed the lesson on <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">unicorns</a>, as did the rest of the girls in class. After the Yule Ball, both Parvati and Padma acted "very cool" towards their dates, Harry and Ron, who had basically ignored them in favour of staring at Cho and Hermione and refused to dance.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Parvati could be seen as the less serious of the Patil twins; like her friend <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>, she enjoyed gossip and fashion, and the two were often seen whispering and giggling together. They also shared a great interest in <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> and are relatively close to <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Professor Trelawney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Both girls could also be insensitive to others at times, a Gryffindor trait according to <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefect</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hilliard" title="Robert Hilliard">Robert Hilliard</a> (albeit a stereotypically negative one). This was exemplified when they pointed and laughed at <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>'s radish earrings<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and when she did not hesitate to desert <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>, something her sister hypocritically frowned at.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Still, Parvati was loyal to those she considered her friends. <p>Lavender Brown was one of Parvati's roommates at Hogwarts and soon became her best friend. The two girls share many interests, including: <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>, gossip, fashion, and they were both close to <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Professor Trelawney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_17-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> They were also emotional and slightly girly, putting much stock in their physical appearances.
</p><p>They were often seen around school together, giggling and whispering. Parvati initially supported Lavender in her infatuation with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> in their <a href="/wiki/1996-1997_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1996-1997 school year">sixth year</a>, but eventually became slightly dismayed with how intensely Lavender threw herself into the relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_19-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Parvati was described as being bored and embarrassed by her friend's behaviour, however the two remained close. Sadly, Lavender was killed after being savaged by <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>. It is unknown how Parvati took the news, but it can be assumed she was devastated by the loss of her best friend.
</p>

</p><p>The girls do not seem to have disliked each other. In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, when Hermione was jealous of Lavender dating <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, Parvati was noted to feel guilty over having laughed at Ron's cruel teasing of Hermione in <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a>, and the two girls discussed Hermione's attendance of the <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a> <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club_Christmas_party" title="Slug Club Christmas party">Christmas party</a> with <a href="/wiki/Cormac_McLaggen" title="Cormac McLaggen">Cormac McLaggen</a>. Since Parvati looked "positively agog" at Hermione sharing this news with her<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_19-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>, it can be assumed that Hermione seldom confided in her roommates.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harry_Potter">Harry Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Harry Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Harry Potter"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Parvati Patil was, like most of her fellow <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> students, friendly with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. She defended him when an angry Professor <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> scolded Harry for the incident in their first flying class, doing so before even <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> did.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In fourth year, when Harry was Triwizard Champion and trying to find a date for the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>, he mustered the courage to ask Parvati following his rejection by <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a>. Parvati blushed furiously at being asked but agreed. Parvati seemed excited by having Harry as a date, though Harry had no romantic interest in her. When asked to dance by a boy from <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons</a>, she asked Harry whether he minded, but when it became clear Harry's attention was on Cho, she left without another word and was considerably cooler to Harry for the rest of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Parvati's attitude toward Harry mellowed by the <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%931996_school_year" title="1995–1996 school year">following year</a>, and she gave no indication as to doubting his story of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s return, unlike her fellow Gryffindors <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a> and Lavender Brown. She, her sister and Lavender all joined <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> that year. Parvati learned well from Harry and mastered the <a href="/wiki/Reductor_Curse" title="Reductor Curse">Reductor Curse</a> under his tutelage.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_17-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> She and Padma helped Harry to destroy the speakers <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> set up around school with <a href="/wiki/Babbling_Beverage" title="Babbling Beverage">Babbling Beverage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTPG_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTPG-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> She and Harry interacted regularly during their O.W.L.s (due to their proximity on the alphabetical roster, they frequently sat near each other). 
</p><p>In their <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">sixth year</a>, Parvati greeted Harry in a friendly manner and they shared mutual embarrassment at Ron and Lavender's exhibitionist romance.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_19-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Parvati remained loyal to Harry and Dumbledore's Army, and found herself in hiding during her seventh year, later fighting in <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>

<p>Parvati seemed to have a reasonably close relationship with <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> professor Sybill Trelawney; she had an unwavering faith in the reliability of Trelawney's predictions and was often seen comforting or supporting her when she was experiencing difficult times. She and <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender</a> became offended on the professor's behalf when Hermione made her disdain for Trelawney and the subject she taught clear in 1993. When she was temporarily sacked by <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, Lavender and Parvati brought Trelawney some nice daffodils. Furthermore, Trelawney had told Parvati that she had the makings of a true seer.
</p>

<p>Parvati became a member of Dumbledore's Army in her fifth year. It was an organisation founded by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, and led by Harry, in order to teach students <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, something that the new professor, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, did not, as she believed that the students would use their magic to oppose the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>.
</p><p>She was a friend of fellow members <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a>. Dean and Seamus seemed to believe that Parvati and her sister, Padma, were the best-looking girls of their year. Furthermore, the romance between Ron and Lavender made both Parvati and Harry feel uncomfortable in 1996. She was also a friend of <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> and defended him during their first year, when <a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson" title="Pansy Parkinson">Pansy Parkinson</a> insulted him.
</p><p>Other members of the Dumbledore's Army were <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Fred and George Weasley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jordan" title="Lee Jordan">Lee Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Spinnet" title="Alicia Spinnet">Alicia Spinnet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colin_and_Dennis_Creevey" title="Colin and Dennis Creevey">Colin and Dennis Creevey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Abbott" title="Hannah Abbott">Hannah Abbott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Macmillan" title="Ernest Macmillan">Ernie Macmillan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Susan_Bones" title="Susan Bones">Susan Bones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terry_Boot" title="Terry Boot">Terry Boot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Finch-Fletchley" title="Justin Finch-Fletchley">Justin Finch-Fletchley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Goldstein" title="Anthony Goldstein">Anthony Goldstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Corner" title="Michael Corner">Michael Corner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zacharias_Smith" title="Zacharias Smith">Zacharias Smith</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a>. Their relationship with Parvati is unknown.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil?veaction=edit&amp;section=22" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil?action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parvati" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Parvati">Parvati</a> is the name of a Hindu goddess of strength, consort of Shiva and daughter of the Himalayas. She was renowned for her beauty. <i>Parvati</i> translates into "she of the mountains" in Sanskrit. It may entirely be possible Parvati Patil's name, and her sister's, have been somewhat inspired by the model <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Padma_Lakshmi" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Padma Lakshmi">Padma Parvati Lakshmi</a>. Padma and Parvati are also characters in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midnight%27s_Children" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Midnight&#39;s Children"><i>Midnight's Children</i></a>, by Salman Rushdie, the latter known mostly as Parvati-the-Witch. Rowling has never stated if she has read <i>Midnight's Children</i> or not, and these are common female names in India, so it is unclear whether or not these characters are intended as an homage to Rushdie.</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patil" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Patil">Patil</a> is a common Marathi surname in the Indian state of Maharashtra, while the alternatively spelled <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patel" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Patel">Patel</a> is a common Gujarati surname in the state of Gujarat.</li></ul>

<ul><li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a> of the series, Parvati and Padma are portrayed as fraternal, rather than identical, twins. They are also shown to both be in <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>, whereas in the books, Padma is in <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, the way <a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson" title="Pansy Parkinson">Pansy Parkinson</a> addresses Parvati suggests that the two were already familiar with one another, but this is never explored.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns" title="Cuthbert Binns">Professor Binns</a> calls Parvati "Miss Pennyfeather".</li>
<li>In the films, Parvati is first seen portrayed by actress <a href="/wiki/Sitara_Shah" title="Sitara Shah">Sitara Shah</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>. In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">Goblet of Fire</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Half-Blood Prince</a></i> she has been portrayed by Welsh actress <a href="/wiki/Shefali_Chowdhury" title="Shefali Chowdhury">Shefali Chowdhury</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-patils-newsround_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patils-newsround-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, Parvati's <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a> is shown to be a giant <a href="/wiki/Cobra" title="Cobra">cobra</a>, rather than a mummy.</li>
<li>Also in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, Parvati was portrayed as left-handed, but shown to be right-handed in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>. This was because of the change of actors.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, Parvati is shown to spend a lot of time with her twin, Padma. However, in the books, she is seldom seen without her best friend, <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>. Additionally, she is not identified minus a mention of her by Hermione and McGonagall calling her 'Miss Patil', but moviegoers would have no way of knowing they were one and the same, whereas the name 'Padma' is never spoken aloud in the films.</li>
<li>In the film version of the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>, Parvati wears an Indian orange blouse and a pink skirt known in India as a "Lehnga", gold bangles (known in India as Chudiyan), and had gold braided into her hair (known in India as a Parandah Braid). She also seems to be closer to <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> than she seems to be in the books, and dances with the <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff%27s_aide" class="mw-redirect" title="Igor Karkaroff&#39;s aide">aide</a> of <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a> rather than a boy from <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Parvati Patil does not appear in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">final film adaptation</a>, but her twin sister Padma does. In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter" title="LEGO Harry Potter">LEGO Harry Potter</a></i>, she appears in Year 7.</li>
<li>In  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i>, the early names for Parvati and Padma are given with: Madhari Patel and Mati Patel.</li></ul>
