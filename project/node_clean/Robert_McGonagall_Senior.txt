<b>Reverend Robert McGonagall Senior</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> and Presbyterian minister who lived in <a href="/wiki/Caithness" title="Caithness">Caithness</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, during the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">twentieth century</a>. He <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married</a> <a href="/wiki/Isobel_Ross" title="Isobel Ross">Isobel Ross</a>, and together they had several children: <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_McGonagall" title="Malcolm McGonagall">Malcolm</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Jr" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert McGonagall Jr">Robert Jr</a>; all of them <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Robert was born to <a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior%27s_father" title="Robert McGonagall Senior&#39;s father">Mr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior%27s_mother" title="Robert McGonagall Senior&#39;s mother">Mrs McGonagall</a>, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Scottish_village" title="Scottish village">a village</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point during his teens, Robert became captivated by the high-spirited <a href="/wiki/Isobel_Ross" title="Isobel Ross">Isobel Ross</a>, who lived in the same village. Like his neighbours, Robert believed that Isobel attended a select ladies' boarding school in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. In fact, when Isobel vanished from her home in the school months, she was attending <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> as she was, unbeknownst to Robert, a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family">Marriage and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Marriage and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage and family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Deeply in love, Robert and Isobel (then aged eighteen) decided to elope, angering both sets of parents. They married and had a blissful honeymoon, on which Isobel could not bear to tell her Robert she was a witch, and so the fact remained a secret.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Robert and Isobel moved to <a href="/wiki/McGonagall_manse" title="McGonagall manse">a manse</a> on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Caithness" title="Caithness">Caithness</a>. She proved skilled at making the most of the small income he earned as a Presbyterian minster.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The couple's first child, a <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">daughter</a>, arrived on <a href="/wiki/4_October" title="4 October">4 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1935" title="1935">1935</a>. Isobel, missing her family and the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">magical world</a> she left behind in the name of love, named the baby Minerva in honour of <a href="/wiki/Minerva" title="Minerva">her own grandmother</a>, a powerful witch. The residents of Caithness found this name extremely unusual and Reverend McGonagall found it very difficult to explain to the members of his congregation why Isobel had chosen it. After Minerva's birth, Isobel became moody, alarming her husband. When Robert consulted his <a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior%27s_friends" title="Robert McGonagall Senior&#39;s friends">friends</a>, they told him that post-partum depression was not uncommon for new mothers to go through, and that it would soon pass. However, Isobel only grew more withdrawn, sometimes hiding away with Minerva for days on end.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>What Isobel was actually trying to do was keep Robert from catching sight of any of the <a href="/wiki/Underage_magic" title="Underage magic">subtle signs</a> of being magic that Minerva had displayed from her very first hours of life. One night, she finally broke down under her husband's gentle questioning, and, in tears, retrieved her <a href="/wiki/Isobel_McGonagall%27s_wand" class="mw-redirect" title="Isobel McGonagall&#39;s wand">wand</a> from where it was hidden and showed him she was a witch. Robert, while shocked by this revelation, did not love Isobel any less after it. Isobel also explained how she was bound to keep the existence of the wizarding world secret from Muggles by <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While the couple still loved each other afterward, they never regained the sense of trust they once shared, something which Minerva, an extremely bright, observant child, soon recognised.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Isobel and her husband eventually had two more children: <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_McGonagall" title="Malcolm McGonagall">Malcolm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Jr" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert McGonagall Jr">Robert Jr</a>. In time, both proved to be magical, and Minerva readily helped her mother teach them that they could not go around showing off their magical powers, and helped her cover up the accidents their magic sometimes caused.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Robert_McGonagall_Senior?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robert McGonagall was a serious, upright, and honest man. He seemed to be amiable and close to his parishoners.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His daughter <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva</a> was noted to be similar to the Reverend in temperament and personality. As such, one can presume he was strict, stern, and clever.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>Robert was also a lover of music and was known to play, as his daughter was noted for enchanting his bagpipes at times from a very young age much to her enjoyment.
</p>
