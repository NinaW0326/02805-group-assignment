<b>Hesper Black</b> was an early member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>. She was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, and it is possible that <a href="/wiki/Licorus_Black" title="Licorus Black">Licorus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexia_Walkin_Black" title="Alexia Walkin Black">Alexia Walkin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phoebe_Black" title="Phoebe Black">Phoebe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eduardus_Limette_Black" title="Eduardus Limette Black">Eduardus Limette</a> were her siblings. She never married or had any children.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hesper_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hesper_Black?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Hesper</i> traces back to <i>Hesperia</i>, which means "sunset". Sunsets and the night that follows it are often considered evil, like the colour black. It could also refer to the "setting of her bloodline" since she didn't have children.
</p><p>Black&#160;can be considered a colour, and is the result of the absence of, or complete absorption of, light.&#160;In Western culture, the colour black has typically been associated with evil, darkness, and witchcraft, but also with prestige and sophistication — appropriate for the House of Black. Black has also been associated with life and prosperity by other cultures. In ancient <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, black was the symbol of the element of water, which also corresponds to&#160;<a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, the&#160;<a href="/wiki/Hogwarts" class="mw-redirect" title="Hogwarts">Hogwarts&#160;house</a> of almost all members of the Black family. In English heraldry, black represents darkness and ignorance; a possible allusion to the prejudice common to members of the House of Black.
</p><p>However, the English word&#160;<i>black</i>&#160;is derived from either the Old English&#160;<i>blœc</i>, meaning "black", or&#160;<i>blac</i>, meaning "pale". Pallor&#160;is associated with sickness and the colour&#160;white, which is usually held to symbolise purity in Western and Indian culture, and death in East Asian cultures. This mirrors the typical colouring of members of the House of Black — dark hair and pale skin.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hesper_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hesper_Black?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Based on a statement by <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>, this character may have been a member of <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> house.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, she attended Hogwarts in the 1830s, when Slughorn wasn’t even born.</li>
<li>This character appears only on the version of the Black family tree shown in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>. She was not included on the version of the tree which <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> gave to a charity auction in 2006<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>. However, according to director <a href="/wiki/David_Yates" title="David Yates">David Yates</a>, Rowling provided the film-makers with a copy of the Black family tree going back eight generations<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>. The film makers presumably received a more complete version of the tree than the one auctioned off, which only goes back six generations.</li>
<li>On the Black Family tree depicted on the 2nd DVD of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, Hesper’s picture is depicted as a skull. <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus Black</a> is also depicted in the same way, and it is unknown why some people have skulls instead of pictures. It is possible that the skull denotes those Blacks who have died violent deaths, as opposed to natural causes.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The skull bears a striking resemblance to the Death’s Heads worn by the Nazis SS troopers during the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:World War II">Second World War</a>. This may simply be coincidence or it could be done to emphasise the support for repression of certain groups of people exhibited by several prominent Blacks (support of Muggle-hunting, et cetera).</li>
<li>It is interesting to note that there are no dates on the scroll of Hesper, where only her first name is written: it is possible that she was a stillborn child, as there would be no need for dates.</li>
<li>She shared her first name with <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black_II" title="Sirius Black II">Sirius Black II</a>'s wife&#160;<a href="/wiki/Hesper_Gamp" title="Hesper Gamp">Hesper Black née Gamp</a>.</li>
<li>She is one of the only Blacks not to bear the name of a star. However, a variant of her name, Hesperus, was the Greek name for the divine personification of planet Venus in the evening.</li></ul>
