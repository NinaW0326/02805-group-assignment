<b>President Emily Rappaport</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who served as <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America">President</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a> in the late <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Emily_Rappaport?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Emily_Rappaport?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rappaport was the fifteenth person to be elected to the office of <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America">President</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rappaport's term saw one of the greatest ever breaches of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Secrecy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees" title="Dorcus Twelvetrees">Dorcus Twelvetrees</a>, the daughter of Rappaport's <a href="/wiki/Keeper_of_Treasure_and_Dragots" title="Keeper of Treasure and Dragots">Keeper of Treasure and Dragots</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aristotle_Twelvetrees" title="Aristotle Twelvetrees">Aristotle Twelvetrees</a>, revealed the locations of both the MACUSA headquarters and <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Ilvermorny</a>, as well as information on how the wizarding community sought to hide itself, to a <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> with whom she was infatuated, <a href="/wiki/Bartholomew_Barebone" title="Bartholomew Barebone">Bartholomew Barebone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Barebone, a <a href="/wiki/Scourers" title="Scourers">Scourer</a> descendant, firmly believed in the existence of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, and that <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches and wizards</a> should be destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He thus stole Dorcus's <a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees%27s_wand" title="Dorcus Twelvetrees&#39;s wand">wand</a> and sought to publicise all of the details about the wizarding world which she had told him.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The fallout of this incident was profound.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The MACUSA was forced to relocate its headquarters because of all the No-Maj attention drawn to it and faced "humiliating" censure by the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards" title="International Confederation of Wizards">International Confederation of Wizards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Rappaport was required to testify before the ICW at a public inquiry, and admitted to them that she could not be certain that all No-Majs exposed to the leaked information had been <a href="/wiki/Memory_Charm" title="Memory Charm">Obliviated</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1790" class="mw-redirect" title="1790">1790</a>, Rappaport enacted <a href="/wiki/Rappaport%27s_Law" title="Rappaport&#39;s Law">Rappaport's Law</a>, which created total segregation between the magic and non-magic communities in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a> by banning wizards and witches from <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marrying</a> or befriending No-Majs, restricting them to interactions "necessary to perform daily activities."<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
