<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Benedita_Dourado?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Benedita_Dourado?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She went on an exchange visit to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> sometime during <a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet" title="Armando Dippet">Professor Armando Dippet</a>'s tenure as Head of Hogwarts. When Dippet complained of <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a>, the resident <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">poltergeist</a>, Professor Dourado laughed heartily and offered to send him some <a href="/wiki/Caipora" title="Caipora">Caipora</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a> "to show [him] what trouble really is." Professor Dippet did not take her up on this offer.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Benedita_Dourado?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Benedita_Dourado?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benedita" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Benedita">Benedita</a></i> is the feminine form of <i>Benedito</i>, which is the Portuguese form of <a href="/wiki/Benedict" title="Benedict">Benedict</a>. "Benedita" comes from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> "<i>bene</i>" (well) and "<i>dicere</i>" (to speak), and it means "(she who is) blessed"; the same etymon also originated the English word "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benediction" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Benediction">benediction</a>".</li>
<li>"<i>Dourado</i>" means "golden" in Portuguese, and it is not a fairly common surname in either Brazil or Portugal.</li></ul>
