<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>Peeves is the most notorious and troublesome poltergeist in British history. Unlike the overwhelming majority of his colleagues, Peeves has a physical form, though he is able to become invisible at will. His looks reflect his nature, which those who know him would agree is a seamless blend of humour and malice.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description of Peeves and his traits<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Peeves</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">poltergeist</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, since c. <a href="/wiki/First_millennium_A.D." title="First millennium A.D.">993</a>. Fond of mischief and chaos, he was a constant pain to the school and its inhabitants, especially <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Argus Filch</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Caretaker" title="Hogwarts Caretaker">caretaker</a>, as the only ones able to control him were the <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron" title="Bloody Baron">Bloody Baron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, Peeves, along with most of the Hogwarts residents, opposed <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, the High Inquisitor, and pranked her until she skedaddled at the end of the school year. In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Peeves fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Peeves continued making trouble at Hogwarts.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding_of_Hogwarts">Founding of Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Founding of Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding of Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Peeves has lived at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> since its founding in c. <a href="/wiki/First_millennium_A.D." title="First millennium A.D.">993</a>. He "came with the building",<sup id="cite_ref-LC_and_MN_interview_JKR_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LC_and_MN_interview_JKR-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and came into existence with the founding of an institution containing a large number of children. Whilst unconfirmed, it was guessed that Peeves is a tangible manifestation of the students' mischief. Peeves has wrought havoc upon Hogwarts for centuries, and was often the cause of ire for various teachers and staff. Starting with <a href="/wiki/Hankerton_Humble" title="Hankerton Humble">Hankerton Humble</a>, who was appointed by the Founders themselves, Peeves has traditionally been a pet peeve of every <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Caretaker" title="Hogwarts Caretaker">caretaker</a> the school has seen.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermorepeeves_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorepeeves-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1876">1876</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1876">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1876"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1876" class="mw-redirect" title="1876">1876</a>, caretaker <a href="/wiki/Rancorous_Carpe" title="Rancorous Carpe">Rancorous Carpe</a> devised an "<a href="/wiki/Rancorous_Carpe%27s_trap" title="Rancorous Carpe&#39;s trap">elaborate trap</a>" in an effort to remove Peeves from the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Castle</a>. The trap comprised a bait of assortment of weapons and a vast enchanted bell jar, reinforced by various <a href="/wiki/Containment_Charm" title="Containment Charm">Containment Charms</a>, which he intended to drop over the poltergeist once he was in place. This did not go as planned, as Peeves easily broke through the bell jar and was left armed with several cutlasses, crossbows, a <a href="/wiki/Blunderbuss" title="Blunderbuss">blunderbuss</a> and a miniature cannon. The Castle had to be evacuated for three days as Peeves amused himself by firing randomly and threatening the students with death.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermorepeeves_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorepeeves-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Eupraxia_Mole" title="Eupraxia Mole">Professor Eupraxia Mole</a>, Headmistress of the day, agreed to sign a <a href="/wiki/Peeves%27_contract" title="Peeves&#39; contract">contract</a> with Peeves, so that he relinquished the weapons in exchange for additional privileges (a once-weekly swim in the boys' toilets on the ground floor, first choice on stale bread from the kitchens for throwing purposes, and a new custom-made hat by <a href="/wiki/Bonhabille" title="Bonhabille">Madame Bonhabille</a>). This was the last and most disastrous attempt to get rid of Peeves. These events forced Mr Carpe to take early retirement "for health reasons".<sup id="cite_ref-pottermorepeeves_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorepeeves-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1980s"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Throughout the <a href="/wiki/1980s" title="1980s">1980s</a>, Peeves would frequently disrupt <a href="/wiki/Flying_(class)" title="Flying (class)">Flying classes</a> by mixing the brooms with the mops, which <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> in particular would help to rearrange.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991-1992_school_year">1991-1992 school year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1991-1992 school year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1991-1992 school year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>My dear <a href="/wiki/Fat_Friar" title="Fat Friar">Friar</a>, haven't we given Peeves all the chances he deserves? He gives us all a bad name and you know, he's not really even a ghost.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nearly Headless Nick</a> discussing Peeves with his fellow ghosts<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Peeves was a topic of discussion before the <a href="/wiki/Start-of-Term_Feast" title="Start-of-Term Feast">Start-of-Term Feast</a> of 1991. Hogwarts' ghosts debated whether or not Peeves should be allowed to attend the feast. While the <a href="/wiki/Fat_Friar" title="Fat Friar">Fat Friar</a> was willing to give Peeves another chance, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nearly Headless Nick</a> felt Peeves did not deserve to be even considered; ghosts had been given a bad name because of him.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the Start-of-Term Feast, Peeves appeared to torment prefect <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a> as he led the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> first years to <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a>. Peeves threw walking sticks at the <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefect</a>, disregarded Percy's threat to tell the Bloody Baron, responded by blowing a raspberry, dumped even more walking sticks onto <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>'s head and left.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Peeves wrote rude words on a classroom blackboard when <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> surprised Harry with the position of Gryffindor Seeker.<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/9c/Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif/revision/latest?cb=20171018234135" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="180" height="279" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif" data-image-key="Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/9c/Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20171018234135" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/9c/Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif/revision/latest?cb=20171018234135" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/9c/Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20171018234135" decoding="async" width="180" height="279" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif" data-image-key="Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/9c/Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20171018234135" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Peeves-PM-17-10-17.gif" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Peeves' physical form</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Unlike the overwhelming majority of his <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghostly</a> colleagues, Peeves had a physical form, though he was able to become <a href="/wiki/Invisibility" title="Invisibility">invisible</a> at will.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Peeves looked like a little man with wickedly slanted, orange eyes, dressed in loud, outlandish clothes including a bell-covered hat and an orange bow tie. He had black hair and compared to the Hogwarts ghosts, he was solid-looking, not pearly white and transparent.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF12_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF12-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He was capable of flight and intangibility, like a ghost, but could also affect solid objects and make himself invisible. Unlike the <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghosts</a>, he could be affected by <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, as Harry used <i><a href="/wiki/Langlock" title="Langlock">Langlock</a></i> to silence him.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP19_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP19-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Peeves was more of a chaotic <a href="/wiki/Spirit" title="Spirit">spirit</a> rather than a physical being, but highly different from the other school <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghosts</a>. Peeves was essentially an embodiment of disorder and took great pleasure in constantly causing it.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PS8_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pottermorepeeves_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorepeeves-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At times, Peeves could be legitimately dangerous. Hogwarts caretaker <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Argus Filch</a> waged a constant war with Peeves, and year after year requested the poltergeist be thrown out of the school.
</p><p>Peeves further annoyed and bothered the Hogwarts community by joining in with whatever current gossip was circulating among students. When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> was suspected of being the "Heir of <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Slytherin</a>" in 1992, Peeves taunted him in the hallways for "speaking in tongues."<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> If students were late to class, he would, among other things, drop wastepaper baskets on their heads, pelt them with <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a>, or pull the rugs out from under them. The worst case involved him sneaking up invisible, grabbing the person's nose and screeching "GOT YOUR CONK!".<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>Oooooooh! Ickle Firsties! What fun!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Peeves tormenting first years<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Peeves was shown to be able to create food at will out of thin air. This ability of his was shown when <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> was trying to get Peeves to give him the vault portrait. Since this breaks one of <a href="/wiki/Gamp%27s_Law_of_Elemental_Transfiguration" title="Gamp&#39;s Law of Elemental Transfiguration">Gamp's Law of Elemental Transfiguration</a>, this might mean that Peeves might have had otherworldly powers.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, despite this, he does not seem to have the power to oppose tremendously powerful wizards, as he was shown to be unwilling to challenge the notoriously powerful Dumbledore.<sup id="cite_ref-POA8_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA8-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>So sorry, your bloodiness, Mr Baron, sir. My mistake, my mistake  I didnt see you  of course I didnt, youre invisible  forgive old Peevsie his little joke, sir.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Peeves' fear of the Bloody Baron<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Peeves did not heed commands from Hogwarts <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefects</a> or professors, though Headmaster <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> was an exception. The one Hogwarts ghost that struck fear in Peeves, in addition to controlling him, was the <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron" title="Bloody Baron">Bloody Baron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS16_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS16-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His fear of the Baron was so well known that in his first year <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, under his <a href="/wiki/Cloak_of_Invisibility" title="Cloak of Invisibility">Invisibility Cloak</a>, was able to drive him away by pretending to be the Baron. Although Peeves did not usually heed commands, he did recognise authority of a sort. Though generally unimpressed by titles and badges, he is generally amenable to the strictures of the teachers, agreeing to stay out of their classrooms while they teach. He has also been known to show an affinity for rare students (notably <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-pottermorepeeves_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorepeeves-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, Peeves was loyal and devoted to Hogwarts and proved this twice: first by defying the hated Dolores Umbridge by assaulting her endlessly with trouble, and then in the Battle of Hogwarts where he dropped <a href="/wiki/Snargaluff" title="Snargaluff">Snargaluff</a> pods on Death Eaters in mid-air,<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> causing general chaos among their ranks and later showing evident joy when Voldemort was defeated.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Peeves also possessed a honourable side, as after Jacob's sibling had caused great chaos around Hogwarts, Peeves kept his end of the bargain.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Students were known to allow Peeves to take the blame for their own mischief, for which he accepted responsibility, as he never had any fear of repercussions.
</p>

<p>Peeves enjoyed taunting all the students and staff members of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> told Filch that he "<i>[doesn't] give a damn about that wretched poltergeist</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-GOF25_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF25-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Most people were infuriated by him but on one occasion, <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> told Peeves that he was unscrewing a chandelier the wrong way when Peeves attempted to create as much trouble for <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> as possible. Peeves obeyed <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a>'s command to give Umbridge hell and caused her a great deal of trouble. Most of the students and teachers were on Peeves' side on this occasion due to their hatred of Umbridge.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP30_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP30-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Umbridge had previously asked <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> to sign an <a href="/wiki/Order_for_the_Expulsion_of_Peeves" title="Order for the Expulsion of Peeves">order for the expulsion of Peeves</a>, but it is uncertain if he ever actually signed such an order, nor how the order could have been enforced had he done so.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP28_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP28-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Peeves also happened to enjoy tormenting <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>. He tried to get them in trouble with <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> when he found them out of bed late in their first year,<sup id="cite_ref-PS9_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> but was once beaten by them that same year (excluding Neville) when Harry impersonated the Bloody Baron, telling him to go away from the third floor so they could save the Philosopher's Stone without any interference (although he was unaware of this).<sup id="cite_ref-PS16_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS16-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The next year, Peeves made a foolish alarm that Harry had petrified two more people (Justin Finch-Fletchley and Nearly Headless Nick) and shouting that no humans or ghosts were safe.<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Peeves has directly clashed with other past Hogwarts caretakers, including <a href="/wiki/Hankerton_Humble" title="Hankerton Humble">Hankerton Humble</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rancorous_Carpe" title="Rancorous Carpe">Rancorous Carpe</a>. An attempt by Carpe to remove Peeves by luring him into a <a href="/wiki/Rancorous_Carpe%27s_trap" title="Rancorous Carpe&#39;s trap">trap</a> featuring weapons and trapping him with <a href="/wiki/Containment_Charm" title="Containment Charm">Containment Charms</a> instead resulted in Peeves seizing the weapons and then subjecting Hogwarts to a three-day standoff. The standoff was only ended when then Headmistress <a href="/wiki/Eupraxia_Mole" title="Eupraxia Mole">Eupraxia Mole</a> drew up a <a href="/wiki/Peeves%27_contract" title="Peeves&#39; contract">contract</a> that granted him special privileges, including a once-weekly swim in the boys' toilets on the ground floor, first refusal on stale bread from the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_kitchens" title="Hogwarts kitchens">kitchen</a> for throwing purposes, and a new hat  to be custom-made by <a href="/wiki/Bonhabille" title="Bonhabille">Madame Bonhabille</a> of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermorepeeves_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermorepeeves-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, despite all this, Peeves's surprisingly high loyalty to Hogwarts proved to allow him to work with both students and teachers alike when Hogwarts is truly in great danger, as he valiantly fought for Hogwarts during the Battle of Hogwarts and effectively coordinated with the fighters on Hogwarts's side; he taunted and attacked only the Death Eaters, and never once pranked any of those fighting for Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?veaction=edit&amp;section=21" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Peeves?action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>Peeve</i> means <i>little devil</i> or something that is personally annoying; a personal dislike (like a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pet_peeve" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Pet peeve">pet peeve</a>). Peevish means disagreeable and bad-tempered.</li></ul>

<ul><li>According to <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s website, Peeves was "an indestructible spirit of chaos."</li>
<li>Peeves has appeared in all of the books and the first three video games, but none of the films. A scene with Peeves was filmed for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, but was left on the cutting room floor and to date has never even been released in any form, even the <i>Ultimate Edition</i> box set. Peeves' role in the movies was replaced with his archenemy <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Argus Filch</a> instead. Peeves was played by <i>Drop Dead Fred</i>'s <a href="/wiki/Rik_Mayall" title="Rik Mayall">Rik Mayall</a>. According to <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_Limited_Edition" title="Harry Potter Limited Edition">Harry Potter Limited Edition</a></i>, Peeves was conceived as having a consistently devilish grin, though there was a question of whether or not he would have hair.</li>
<li>The only on-screen reference to Peeves occurs in the short film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Queen%27s_Handbag" title="The Queen&#39;s Handbag">The Queen's Handbag</a></i>, during which Harry Potter describes Peeves using the Queen's Birthday as an excuse to drop coloured eggs on Hogwarts students at breakfast but then he appears during breakfast anyway.</li>
<li>In the video games, Peeves is battled several times. For example, he randomly pops up out of boxes. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (video game)">PC game</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Peeves is defeated with the <a href="/wiki/Knockback_Jinx" title="Knockback Jinx">Knockback Jinx</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (video game)">PC game</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, he is defeated with the <a href="/wiki/Skurge_Charm" title="Skurge Charm">Skurge Charm</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (video game)">PC game</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, he is defeated with the <a href="/wiki/Tickling_Charm" title="Tickling Charm">Tickling Charm</a>. Also, in the DS versions of <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>, he is somewhat of a boss.</li>
<li>Although Peeves does not actually appear in the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a> </i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">game</a>, some <figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 222px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b8/Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20161218043223" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="220" height="237" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Jk rowling peeves.jpg" data-image-key="Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b8/Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20161218043223" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b8/Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20161218043223" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b8/Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20161218043223" decoding="async" width="220" height="237" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Jk rowling peeves.jpg" data-image-key="Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b8/Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20161218043223" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Jk_rowling_peeves.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">JK Rowling's original sketch of Peeves.</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>students make references to him, saying things like, "Want to go watch Peeves throw things?"</li>
<li>Peeves was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Crosby" title="Chris Crosby">Chris Crosby</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (video game)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone Video Game</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (video game)">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets Video Game</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (video game)">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban Video Game</a>.</li>
<li>Although in the third book Peeves shows a bit of respect for Albus Dumbledore, it is noted in the fourth book that the Bloody Baron is the only one really able to control Peeves.</li>
<li>Peeves is <a href="/wiki/James_and_Oliver_Phelps" title="James and Oliver Phelps">Oliver Phelps</a>'s favourite character in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-megacon_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-megacon-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
