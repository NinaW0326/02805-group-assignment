<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ciceron_Harkiss?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ciceron_Harkiss?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dd>"a local guide in Italy," 1726, from Italian&#160;cicerone, from Latin&#160;Ciceronem, from the name of the great Roman orator.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd>"eloquent," a reference to Roman statesman and orator Marcus Tullius&#160;Cicero&#160;(106-43 B.C.E.); also often known as&#160;Tully&#160;in early Modern English writers;&#160;Cicero&#160;being a cognomen of the genus&#160;Tullia.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
