<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>...had been deserted by a man whom she loved dearly, and she thought her heart would never mend</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Amata's description<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Amata</b> is one of the three female protagonists in the story of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">The Fountain of Fair Fortune</a></i> by the <a href="/wiki/Beedle_the_Bard" title="Beedle the Bard">Beedle the Bard</a>.
</p><p>She suffered from a broken heart due to her being deserted by a man whom she had loved dearly. Hence, she journeyed with two <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">knight</a> who had problems like herself to <a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">the Fountain of Fair Fortune</a>. When they reached the fountain, she realised that she no longer needed the fountain and let <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a> to bath in it instead and, after he did, he proposed to her and she accepted.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>The stream had washed away all her regret for her lover...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Amata's deserted lover<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Amata came to the Fountain because she had been jilted by the man she <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">loved</a>. She hoped that the fountain would heal her broken heart, grief, and longing.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quest_for_the_Fountain">Quest for the Fountain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Quest for the Fountain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Quest for the Fountain"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>And Amata became caught upon the armour of a dismal-looking knight who was seated on a bone-thin horse.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Amata<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>She met up with two <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a> who had problems like herself.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> One of the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a> was named <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a>, who was sick of "a malady no <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a> could cure" and hoped than the Fountain can restore her health. The other was named <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a>, who had been robbed and humiliated by a sorcerer. She hoped that the Fountain would relieve her feelings of helplessness and her poverty.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/66/Luckless_leading_witches.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090601135858" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/66/Luckless_leading_witches.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20090601135858" decoding="async" width="180" height="206" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Luckless leading witches.jpg" data-image-key="Luckless_leading_witches.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Luckless_leading_witches.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Amata, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> trying to reach for the Fountain</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>They soon decided that three heads are better than one and pooled their efforts to reach the Fountain together. At first light, a crack in the wall appeared and creepers from the garden reached through and wrapped themselves around <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, who grabbed onto <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a>, who then took hold of Amata.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> But Amata got tangled in the armour of a knight, and as the vines pulled <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> in, all three <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a> along with the knight were pulled through the wall and into the garden. Since only one person is permitted to bathe in the Fountain, the first two witches were upset that Amata had inadvertently invited another competitor.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Because he had no <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> power, he recognised the women as <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a>, and was well-suited to his name, "<a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a>," the knight announced his intention to abandon the quest.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Amata promptly chided him for giving up then asked him to join their group. They had to get past three challenges,<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the third of which is completed by Amata. The third task asked challengers to pay "the treasure of [their] past."<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Attempts to float or leap across failed. Finally, Amata thought to use her <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> to withdraw the memories of the lover who had abandoned her and drop the memories into the water. Stepping stones appeared in the water, and the four were able to cross to the Fountain, where they then had to decide who would bathe in the Fountain.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finding_True_Love">Finding True Love</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Finding True Love">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Finding True Love"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

The same went for <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a>, because she could cure this disease and use it as a means to earn money.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Amata realised that her former lover was a bad person, and that she was actually better off without him. Realising she no longer needed the Fountain's blessing, she told <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a> to bathe in it instead, and, after he did, he proposed to her, and she accepted.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>...at the feet of Amata, who was the kindest and most beautiful woman he had ever beheld.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Amata is a beautiful woman with wavy blonde hair and very white skin. Her beauty might have been the reason for her being able to attract men, such as her unknown lover and <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a>. At the end of the story, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a> thought Amata was the most beautiful woman he had ever beheld.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>She hoped that the Fountain would relieve her of her grief and longing.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Amata's grief and longing<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>In contrast to <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> heroines who wait to be rescued, Amata is a brave, intelligent, and true-hearted witch who takes her fate in her own hands.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the beginning of the story, she was already portrayed as an open and compassionate woman who could empathise with the misfortunes of others, as seen by how she, Asha, and Altheda became good friends through sharing their respective tales of woe, and how they even came to the agreement of uniting and trying to reach the Fountain if they had the chance to. Her initial flaw was that, before the end of the story, she suffered from the grief and longing of a broken heart due to her lover's desertion.
</p><p>Unlike Altheda and Asha, Amata could be generous to a fault, as demonstrated by how she convinced Sir Luckless to not give up and help them reach their goal, despite the obvious fact that an addition to the group meant increased difficulty of deciding exactly who should bathe in the Fountain if they ever reached it. Her method of conviction, however, was in the form of a good scolding of Sir Luckless' pessimism and apparent cowardice, which seems to testify that Amata was not one to give up easily, and hated seeing others do so without even attempting to try their very best. Later on, she proved her intelligence by solving the riddle of the third trial of the <a href="/wiki/Enchanted_garden" title="Enchanted garden">Enchanted Garden</a>.
</p><p>When the four companions finally reached the Fountain, one could also see that Amata was a true and selfless friend, given how she was perfectly willing to forgo her chance to bathe in the Fountain through giving that very chance to Asha, who had collapsed from exhaustion and was on the brink of death.
</p><p>At the very end of the story, Amata realised that she did not need to bathe in the Fountain after all, for she had grown wise enough to see that her former lover had been cruel and faithless, and it was actually a joy to her to be rid of him. She also realised that she had found a man worthy of her: Sir Luckless, whose proposal of marriage she accepted.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Her_Lover">Her Lover</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Her Lover">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Her Lover"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>...had been cruel and faithless, and that it was happiness enough to get rid of him...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Amata's lover<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Her lover deserted her, causing her grief and longing as she had <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">loved</a> him dear; she thought her heart would never mend. So she went on a journey to <a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">the Fountain of Fair Fortune</a> so he would <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> her again.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> But when she reached the fountain, she had realised that he had been cruel and faithless and she was happy to be rid of him.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asha_and_Altheda">Asha and Altheda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Asha and Altheda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Asha and Altheda"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>Pitying each other, the three woman agree that, should the chance befall them, they would unite and try to reach the Fountain Together</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Amata, <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Amata met up with two witches who had problems like herself. So they decided that together would be better and stronger than apart, and they pool their efforts to reach the Fountain together.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They when they are pulled by the creepers, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a> ends up coming with them.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On their journey to the Fountain, the motley band faces three challenges. First, they face a "monstrous white worm, bloated and blind" who demands "proof of your pain." After several fruitless attempts to attack it with <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and other means, <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a>'s tears of frustration finally satisfy the worm, and the four are allowed to pass. Next, they face a steep slope and are asked to pay the "fruit of their labours".<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They try and try to make it up the hill but spend hours climbing to no avail. Finally, the hard-won effort of <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a> as she cheers her friends on (specifically the sweat from her brow) gets them past the challenge.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At last, they faced a stream in their path and were asked to pay "the treasure of your past."<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Their attempts to float or leap across failed, until Amata thought to use her wand to withdraw the memories of the <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">lover</a> who abandoned her, and drop them into the water. Stepping stones appeared in the water, and the four were able to cross to the Fountain, where they must decide who gets to bathe.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> collapsed from exhaustion and was near death.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She was in such pain that she cannot make it to the Fountain, and she begged her three friends not to move her. <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a> quickly mixed a powerful <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potion</a> in an attempt to revive her, and the concoction actually cures her malady, so she no longer needed the Fountain's waters.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Because <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a> now knew how to cure the disease, she decided that she wouldn't need it either. Amata told <a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless" title="Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a> to bathe in the fountain and they set off "arm-in-arm." We then learn that the four friends live long, never realising that the Fountain's waters "carried no enchantment at all."<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amata?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amata?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amata is an Italian word meaning beloved.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Queen Amata is a minor character in the Aeneid, an epic by Roman poet Virgil, written between 29 and 19 BC. She is the wife of Latinus, a King in Italy. She is somewhat a tragic figure; she commits suicide after the poem's antagonist, Turnus, is defeated in battle. This could be compared to the grief suffered by Amata in Beedle the Bard's tale.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
