<p><b>Gervaise Ollivander</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who came from a <a href="/wiki/Ollivander_family" title="Ollivander family">long line</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmakers</a> who had <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">operated</a> in <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a> since his earliest ancestors arrived in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>  with the Romans, and set up stall (subsequently shop) to sell to ancient British wizards. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander" title="Gerbold Ollivander">Gerbold Octavius Ollivander</a>, and the father of <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gervaise Ollivander was born before <a href="/wiki/1890" class="mw-redirect" title="1890">1890</a> likely<sup id="cite_ref-birth_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birth-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>, somewhere in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. He was born into the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> <a href="/wiki/Ollivander_family" title="Ollivander family">Ollivander family</a>, who had been in the <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">wand-making business</a> since <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_a_wandmaker">Career as a wandmaker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gervaise_Ollivander?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>Early in my career, as I watched my wandmaker father wrestling with substandard wand core materials such as kelpie hair, I conceived the ambition to discover the finest cores and to work only with those when my time came to take over the family business. This I have done.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Gervaise took up the <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">family business</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, studying further into <a href="/wiki/Wandlore" title="Wandlore">wandlore</a> and becoming an accomplished wandmaker.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He and <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander%27s_mother" title="Garrick Ollivander&#39;s mother">his wife</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> witch, had at least one child, <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick</a> sometime in, or prior to, <a href="/wiki/1919" title="1919">1919</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to Gervaise, it was impossible to fool a wizard or witch who carried a wand of <a href="/wiki/Cedar" title="Cedar">cedar</a> wood, an opinion that <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">his son</a> shared.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As it was practise at the time, a customer would often present the wandmaker with a magical substance to which they were attached, or had inherited, or by which their family swore.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As such, Gervaise used a wide variety of <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">wand cores</a>. Early in his career, his son <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick</a> saw his father working with substandard wand core materials such as <a href="/wiki/Kelpie" title="Kelpie">kelpie</a> hair, <a href="/wiki/Kneazle" title="Kneazle">kneazle</a> whisker and <a href="/wiki/Troll_whisker" title="Troll whisker">Troll whisker</a>. It was then that he conceived the ambition to work with only the finest cores, and thus discover the ideal wand.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
