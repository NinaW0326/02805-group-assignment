<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fridwulfa?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fridwulfa?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1928" title="1928">1928</a>, Fridwulfa bore a child with <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid%27s_father" title="Rubeus Hagrid&#39;s father">Mr Hagrid</a>, but deserted him and her son, <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus</a>, when he was three years old. She returned to the <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">Giants</a>, and bore another child, <a href="/wiki/Grawp" title="Grawp">Grawp</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Grawp%27s_father" title="Grawp&#39;s father">another</a> <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP30_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP30-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She died years before the events of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP20_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP20-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The reason she deserted Rubeus was that he was "small" in size, and Giants prefer to produce large-sized children. The same disappointment went to her second son, Grawp. For her desertion, Mr Hagrid was heartbroken and Rubeus didn't remember her much except for not being a great mother.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP20_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP20-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fridwulfa?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fridwulfa?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> published an article on Hagrid's half-giant ancestry, she mentioned Fridwulfa by name, even though no one (aside from Hagrid, who had told her to leave) knew it. It is possible this may have been information that Skeeter somehow picked up while spying in her <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">animagus</a> form as a <a href="/wiki/Beetle" title="Beetle">beetle</a>.</li>
<li>Rita Skeeter seems to insinuate in her article that Fridwulfa was among those who fought for Voldemort's side during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, though there is no evidence to support this. It's unknown if this is true as Hagrid claims that she left when he was three, nearly four decades before the war even began.</li>
<li>Fridwulfa was the first giant named in the books, and is, to date, the only giantess named in canon.</li></ul>
