<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bridget Wenlock was born in the year <a href="/wiki/1202" class="mw-redirect" title="1202">1202</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Tinworth" title="Tinworth">Tinworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WBoS_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBoS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In her youth, she attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from around <a href="/wiki/1213" class="mw-redirect" title="1213">1213</a> to <a href="/wiki/1220" class="mw-redirect" title="1220">1220</a>, where she defied the stereotype of intelligent wizards only coming from Ravenclaw by being <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She presumably took and studied <a href="/wiki/Arithmancy_(class)" title="Arithmancy (class)">Arithmancy class</a> at Hogwarts from her <a href="/wiki/Third_year" title="Third year">third year</a> onwards.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adulthood">Adulthood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Adulthood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Adulthood"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After her graduation from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a>, Bridget Wenlock studied further into <a href="/wiki/Arithmancy" title="Arithmancy">Arithmancy</a>, becoming an accomplished and celebrated <a href="/wiki/Arithmancer" title="Arithmancer">Arithmancer</a>.
</p><p>Wenlock, like a number of other magical researchers, was very protective of her discoveries; her paranoia was so that she wrote exclusively in <a href="/wiki/Invisible_Ink" title="Invisible Ink">Invisible Ink</a>, upside-down, back-to-front and in atrocious handwriting. She was also noted for her absent-mindedness, managing to promptly lose scraps of paper in which she had scribbled invisible notes. Because of this, it was not an unusual sight to see her retracing her steps through her hometown, attempting to use the <a href="/wiki/Revealing_Charm" title="Revealing Charm">Revealing Charm</a> on every scrap of paper she came across, in search of a lost Arithmancy calculation.<sup id="cite_ref-WBoS_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBoS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 243px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d4/BridgetWenlockWriting.png/revision/latest?cb=20121117162658" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d4/BridgetWenlockWriting.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/241?cb=20121117162658" decoding="async" width="241" height="123" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="BridgetWenlockWriting.png" data-image-key="BridgetWenlockWriting.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:BridgetWenlockWriting.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Bridget Wenlock in her house in <a href="/wiki/Tinworth" title="Tinworth">Tinworth</a>, conducting her studies in Arithmancy</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>It was one day, during breakfast, that Wenlock wrote the immensely famous theorem that supported the magical properties of the number <a href="/wiki/Seven" title="Seven">seven</a>. She wrote it down on what she thought was the back of an envelope, in the usual <a href="/wiki/Invisible_Ink" title="Invisible Ink">Invisible Ink</a>. That same day, she posted a letter to <a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock%27s_cousin" title="Bridget Wenlock&#39;s cousin">her cousin</a>, using what she later realised was the envelope in which she had written the theorem. Wenlock seized her broom at once, and managed to overtake the <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a> carrying the letter mid-flight but the owl refused to give anyone the letter but its addressee. After several nips and scratches, Wenlock had to resort to simply following the owl to her cousin's house in <a href="/wiki/John_O%27Groats" title="John O&#39;Groats">John O'Groats</a>, around 600 miles away.<sup id="cite_ref-WBoS_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBoS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When she finally retrieved the envelope from her cousin, a <a href="/wiki/Revealing_Charm" title="Revealing Charm">Revealing Charm</a> revealed that it contained no more than a cake recipe. It was only when she returned to <a href="/wiki/Tinworth" title="Tinworth">Tinworth</a> that Wenlock found out that her calculations were scrawled on a sugar packet that was still sitting on her kitchen table.<sup id="cite_ref-WBoS_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBoS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was precisely this theorem that made her such a celebrated <a href="/wiki/Arithmancer" title="Arithmancer">Arithmancer</a> for centuries to come.
</p><p>At some point, Bridget presumably had children as she had a granddaughter by the name of <a class="new" title="Daphne Wenlock (page does not exist)" rel="nofollow" data-uncrawlable-url="L3dpa2kvRGFwaG5lX1dlbmxvY2s/YWN0aW9uPWVkaXQmcmVkbGluaz0x">Daphne Wenlock</a> who, as of <a href="/wiki/2020s" title="2020s">2020</a>, was working at an Inn at number seven, Aldwych Road in Tinworth.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Instead of a week of detentions, <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Professor Sprout</a> let him off with a warning and a box of <a href="/wiki/Pink_Coconut_Ice" title="Pink Coconut Ice">coconut ice</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Arithmancy" title="Arithmancy">Arithmancy</a></b>: Wenlock was a highly accomplished and celebrated <a href="/wiki/Arithmancer" title="Arithmancer">Arithmancer</a>, who notably discovered the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> properties of the number <a href="/wiki/Seven" title="Seven">seven</a>. She devised many impressive numerical calculations throughout her career and was highly protective of her theorems that she had created.<sup id="cite_ref-JKRS_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her knowledge and experience in this field of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> warranted her inclusion on her own <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSg_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSg-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Wenlock was also talented with charm-work, as she was capable of casting the <a href="/wiki/Revealing_Charm" title="Revealing Charm">Revealing Charm</a>, which she used to reveal the contents of many of her own personal mathematical notes and papers written in <a href="/wiki/Invisible_Ink" title="Invisible Ink">Invisible Ink</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WBoS_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WBoS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
