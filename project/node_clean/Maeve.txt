<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Maeve?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Maeve?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, she was involved in the training of young <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> in her native <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. She was immortalised on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-POAG_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Maeve?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Maeve?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Her title may indicate she was <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">muggle-born</a> or <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> mentioned there are no princes in the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a><sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and thus, probably no queens or it means that she married a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">muggle</a> king.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Maeve?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Maeve?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Maeve" is the anglicised form of the Gaelic name <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medb" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Medb">Medb</a> meaning "intoxicating". In Irish legend this was the name of a warrior queen of Connacht. Her fight against Ulster and the hero CÃºchulainn is told in the Irish epic 'The Cattle Raid of Cooley'.
</p>
