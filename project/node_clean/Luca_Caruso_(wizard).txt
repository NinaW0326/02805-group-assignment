<b>Luca Michali Caruso</b><sup id="cite_ref-filmwizardry_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-filmwizardry-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">student</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. Caruso joined <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, an organisation taught and led by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and was one of the people to sign their name on its <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army_parchment" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army parchment">parchment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At some point, Caruso also purchased <a href="/wiki/Diabolic_Dare_Devils" title="Diabolic Dare Devils">Diabolic Dare Devils</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wildfire_Whiz-bangs" title="Weasleys&#39; Wildfire Whiz-bangs">Weasleys' Wildfire Whiz-bangs</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley%27s_Basic_Blaze_Box" title="Fred Weasley&#39;s Basic Blaze Box">Basic Blaze Box</a> from <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wizard_Wheezes" title="Weasleys&#39; Wizard Wheezes">Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-filmwizardry_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-filmwizardry-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Luca_Caruso_(wizard)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Luca_Caruso_(wizard)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a male given name, <i>Luca</i> is the Italian and Romanian form of <i>Luke</i>, an English form of the Greek name <i>Loukas</i>, meaning "from Lucania."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucania" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lucania">Lucania</a> was an ancient region of <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Southern Italy</a> extending from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyrrhenian_Sea" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Tyrrhenian Sea">Tyrrhenian Sea</a> to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Taranto" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Gulf of Taranto">Gulf of Taranto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Caruso</i> is an Italian surname derived from the Italian word <i>caruso</i>, "close-cropped," itself from the Latin <i>cariosus</i>, "smooth" or "bald."<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The word took on the broader meaning of "boy" in Italian, since, during the Middle Ages, when most men wore their hair longer, it was fashionable for young men to keep theirs short.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <i>Caruso</i> also became an occupational name for sulphur pit workers in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Girgenti" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Girgenti">Girgenti</a> region of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sicily" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Sicily">Sicily</a> because their job required short hair.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
