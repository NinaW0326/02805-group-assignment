<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>The wealthy man who owned the Riddle House these days neither lived there nor put it to any use; they said in the village that he kept it for "tax reasons", though nobody was very clear what these might be. <dd>&#8212;Owner of the <a href="/wiki/Riddle_House" title="Riddle House">Riddle House</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>This <b>individual</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>) was a wealthy <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> man, who owned the <a href="/wiki/Riddle_House" title="Riddle House">Riddle House</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF1_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He did not live in it, nor put it to any use, although he still paid <a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce" title="Frank Bryce">Frank Bryce</a> to do the gardening before his death in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. Nobody in <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a> seemed to know why he kept the house, although it was said it was for "<i>tax reasons"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF1_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
