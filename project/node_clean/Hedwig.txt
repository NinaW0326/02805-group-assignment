<p><b>Hedwig</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/27_July" title="27 July">27 July</a> <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>) was <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s pet <a href="/wiki/Snowy_owl" title="Snowy owl">snowy owl</a>. Hedwig was an eleventh birthday gift from <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> to Harry. Hagrid purchased her at the <a href="/wiki/Eeylops_Owl_Emporium" title="Eeylops Owl Emporium">Eeylops Owl Emporium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to delivering mail, the typical use <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owls</a> are put to, Hedwig was also a close companion to Harry. She remained one of his closest friends right up to her death during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a> at the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>.
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, Hedwig was kept by the <a href="/wiki/Eeylops_Owl_Emporium" title="Eeylops Owl Emporium">Eeylops Owl Emporium</a>, until she was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> while he was helping <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> get school supplies. She was a <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_eleventh_birthday" title="Harry Potter&#39;s eleventh birthday">birthday</a> present for Harry when he turned eleven. Harry was obviously delighted with his new pet and was very grateful to Hagrid.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This was the first birthday present Harry had ever received, as the Dursleys never celebrated his birthday. He very quickly grew attached to her.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry decided to name her Hedwig after <a href="/wiki/Hedwig_(wizard)" title="Hedwig (wizard)">an individual</a> he saw mentioned in <i><a href="/wiki/A_History_of_Magic" title="A History of Magic">A History of Magic</a></i>. In the days before arriving for his first year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, Harry avoided the <a href="/wiki/Dursley_family" title="Dursley family">Dursleys</a> by staying in his room. Hedwig stayed with him during this time, his only friend at <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Throughout her life, she fulfilled the traditional function of an owl by delivering mail for Harry. She spent their first year at Hogwarts fulfilling her duty by getting and sending letters to <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> and even took ones to <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charles Weasley</a> in <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>.
</p><p>In the summer of <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, they stayed at the <a href="/wiki/Dursley_family" title="Dursley family">Dursleys</a> in Privet Drive again. <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">Vernon Dursley</a> forced Harry to lock Hedwig in her cage, sometimes for weeks at a time to prevent Harry from sending messages to his "freaky little friends". Hedwig started to grow bored and frustrated, a feeling Harry mirrored. He felt forgotten by his friends and mused that Hedwig was the only proof he had that the wizarding world was real. 
</p>

<p>When Ron, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a>, arrived to rescue Harry from Privet Drive in the <a href="/wiki/Flying_Ford_Anglia" title="Flying Ford Anglia">flying Ford Anglia</a>, he nearly ended up forgetting Hedwig when he was getting into the car. She noticed and hooted, waking Vernon up. Harry had to run to get her and put her in the car too. He later let her out, and Hedwig flew behind the car all the way to <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a>. In subsequent summers, Hedwig's frustrated hooting convinced the Dursleys to allow Harry to let her out to fly, on the condition that he didn't use her to send messages. <p>In the summer of <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, she took longer to get back to Privet Drive because she decided to fly to Hermione to get her birthday gift for Harry. On her way back, she had to help <a href="/wiki/Errol" title="Errol">Errol</a> get Ron's gift to Harry when he collapsed. She later went back to Hermione to deliver Harry's letter to her, and in her absence he abandoned Privet Drive and went to the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a>. Hedwig was very intelligent, and knew the place she needed to go. She arrived at the pub a few minutes earlier than he did. 
</p><p>In the build-up to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Hedwig was constrained in what she could do for Harry, due to the fact that snowy owls aren't native to Britain, which made her conspicuous; this could potentially raise suspicions if onlookers witnessed a Snowy owl always going back and forth from a specific location. A specific instance of this was during <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s evasion of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. Hedwig was keen to deliver messages, but Harry refused to put her at risk and this led to a minor falling out between them.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hedwig?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hedwig?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>No... </i>NO!<i> Hedwig... Hedwig...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Harry's grief due to her death<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Hedwig was killed in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>. While aboard <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Hagrid's</a> flying motorcycle with Harry she was struck by a <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a>, possibly aimed at Hagrid or Harry. She was killed instantly and fell like a rag doll to the bottom of her cage. To add insult to the injury of losing his much-loved companion, Harry was then forced to destroy the sidecar containing Hedwig's body in an effort to slow down the pursuing Death Eaters.<sup id="cite_ref-DH4_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Harry was devastated by the loss of Hedwig. His friends were naturally deeply sympathetic and tried to comfort him. Hagrid consoled him by saying that Hedwig had lived "a great old life".<sup id="cite_ref-DH5_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH5-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a> which happened across the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding world</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, a mysterious <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundable</a> of the memory of Hedwig appeared throughout the world, which was trapped in a <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_Charm" title="Atmospheric Charm">whirlwind</a> <a href="/wiki/Confoundable" title="Confoundable">Confoundable</a>. Volunteer <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> could free Hedwig by casting the <a href="/wiki/Counter-charm" title="Counter-charm">Counter-charm</a> <a href="/wiki/Meteolojinx_Recanto" title="Meteolojinx Recanto">Meteolojinx Recanto</a> to cancel the effects of the wind and have Hedwig's memory return to its rightful place.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hedwig?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hedwig?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>Hedwig's large round amber eyes gazed reproachfully at him over the dead frog clamped in her beak... In addition to every other miserable feeling, he now felt guilty that he'd been irritable with Hedwig; she was the only friend at he had at <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">number four, Privet Drive</a>.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Hedwig expresses her displeasure at Harry's treatment of her<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Hedwig was a very intelligent, loyal and affectionate companion, and tended to show her affection by gently nibbling Harry's ears or fingers. She also demonstrated that she was fully capable of understanding his wishes and would faithfully follow his orders, such as when he instructed her to find and peck <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> to prompt them to write to him during the summer holidays. Hedwig obeyed Harry's requests and even pecked Ron and Hermione on Harry's orders, in order to get them to write back to him.<sup id="cite_ref-OP5_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hedwig was a dignified and proud creature and was disapproving of less mature <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owls</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Pigwidgeon" title="Pigwidgeon">Pigwidgeon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF3_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF3-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She responded to the behaviour she considered inappropriate by staring or hooting reproachfully. She would sometimes show her displeasure physically, turning her back on Harry (showing him her tail), cuffing him with a wing, or even nipping him a little harder than usual if she was offended. In particular, she seemed disdainful of the hyperactive behaviour of <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>'s pet owl <a href="/wiki/Pigwidgeon" title="Pigwidgeon">Pigwidgeon</a>. Hedwig would also make a lot of noise if she was locked in her cage for too long.<sup id="cite_ref-csfilm_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-csfilm-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>She was very intelligent, once deciding to fly to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> to get Hermione's thirteenth birthday present to Harry while she was on holiday. During Hedwig's journey, Harry relocated to the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a> and she knew, arriving five minutes after him, according to Cauldron's landlord, <a href="/wiki/Tom" title="Tom">Tom</a>. Further evidence of Hedwig's resourcefulness was found in the fact that she didn't need a specific address to deliver messages - a name alone was sufficient. Hedwig was a keen hunter too and when possible, was allowed to fly outside free and catch food for herself, such as <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP3_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She would also eat <a href="/wiki/Eeylops_Premium_Owl_Treats" title="Eeylops Premium Owl Treats">Eeylops Premium Owl Treats</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hedwig?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hedwig?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/J.K._Rowling" class="mw-redirect" title="J.K. Rowling">J.K. Rowling</a> found the name St Hedwig in a book of medieval saints.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It's derived from Hedvig - a Scandinavian name meaning female warrior or battle.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> There are two female saints named Hedwig:
</p>
<ul><li>Saint <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hedwig_of_Andechs" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hedwig of Andechs">Hedwig of Andechs</a> (1174–1243), Duchess of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silesia" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Silesia">Silesia</a>, who is the patron saint of orphans.</li>
<li>Saint <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hedwig_of_Poland" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hedwig of Poland">Hedwig (Jadwiga)</a> (1373–1399), Queen (officially King) of Poland. A St Hedwig's Day dedicated to Hedwig of Andechs has been observed on <a href="/wiki/October" title="October">October</a> 16th every year since she was canonised in 1267.</li></ul>

<ul><li>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_Trading_Card_Game" title="Harry Potter Trading Card Game">Harry Potter Trading Card Game</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Hedwig_(Trading_Card)" title="Hedwig (Trading Card)">Hedwig</a> is listed as a <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>. <li>On 17 August, 2009, The <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/">Telegraph</a> <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/harry-potter/6042159/Owl-crop-circle-appears-in-Wiltshire-field.html">reports</a> that a new series of mysterious crop-circles (patterns cut in farmers fields) have appeared on that summer in fields in <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a>, and the most recent is the 400 foot pattern, they wrote it as a tribute to the late Hedwig from the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">Harry Potter series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The site Mugglenet.com released a book titled <i><a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.amazon.com/MuggleNet-coms-What-Happen-Harry-Potter/dp/B002ACPMF4">MuggleNet.com's What Will Happen in Harry Potter 7: Who Lives, Who Dies, Who Falls in Love and How Will the Adventure Finally End?</a></i> In the book, they gave odds as to which characters would live and which would be killed off, but did not include odds for Hedwig. After the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, they posted the results of the odds on their website, and also listed Hedwig with 1/1000 and the note "Just kidding."<sup id="cite_ref-scorecard_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scorecard-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
