<p><b>Half-breed</b> is a term given to <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> with at least one non-human parent, although those with more distant non-human ancestry are also referred to as half-breed. "Half-breed" may be an offensive, rather than proper, term, as it seems to appear as an insult. However, no non-offensive alternative is known to exist. There is a great deal of prejudice against half-breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, which also tends to discriminate against non-human beings in general.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traits">Traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Half-breeds share the attributes of both their parents, still being able to use <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, as the magic gene is dominant, while having traits of their non-human parent as well. It is also unknown for how many generations non-human traits will persist, although it seems that <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> with even one non-human great-grandparent will retain some of those characteristics. For example, <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Filius Flitwick</a> is only one-eighth <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a>, but retains a small stature as a result of this heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is unknown if <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> can interbreed with non-humans and if they can, this would probably result in non-magical half-breeds. Though it is very rare for a Muggle to even find out about the existence of those creatures, the existence of a non-magical half-breed is potentially possible but unlikely.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatment">Treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Treatment"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>Filthy half breeds!  Beasts!  Uncontrolled animals!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Dolores Umbridge insulting centaurs in the Forbidden Forest<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Many wizards and witches are prejudiced against non-human beings, from <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elves</a> to <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblins</a> to <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaurs</a>, as well as extend this prejudice to those with mixed heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> An example of this prejudice would be <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, as well as her decrees to continue to suppress beings whom she deems are too low for her esteem.
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>'s status as a <a href="/wiki/Half-giant" title="Half-giant">half-giant</a> was exposed by <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, many parents of his students at Hogwarts were alarmed, assuming that Hagrid must have been savage and dim-witted because of his ancestry.
</p><p>It is also possible that there is less prejudice toward different types of half-breeds, as <a href="/wiki/Half-Veela" title="Half-Veela">part-Veela</a> <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a> never seemed to suffer any discrimination based on her heritage. This may be because there is no clear physical difference between humans and <a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veelas</a> and because even part-Veelas possess stunning good looks and the supernatural ability to entrance the opposite sex.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In contrast, someone like Hagrid, with his <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a>-like appearance, would be far less attractive than the beautiful, Veela-like Fleur.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_half-breeds">Types of half-breeds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Types of half-breeds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Types of half-breeds"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Half-giant"><a href="/wiki/Half-giant" title="Half-giant">Half-giant</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Half-giant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Half-giant"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 161px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/cd/Rubeus_Hagrid.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160121163026" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/cd/Rubeus_Hagrid.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/159?cb=20160121163026" decoding="async" width="159" height="218" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Rubeus Hagrid.jpg" data-image-key="Rubeus_Hagrid.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Rubeus_Hagrid.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>A half-giant is the offspring of a <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a> and a human. Half-giants will be much taller and larger than ordinary humans, will have considerable <a href="/wiki/Spell_resistance" title="Spell resistance">spell resistant</a> skin, as well as possessing great physical strength, though not to the same extent as giants.
</p><p>Rubeus Hagrid is a half-giant, with a wizard father and giantess mother.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The exact parentage of <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Olympe Maxime</a> is unclear: whether she had a <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a> father or mother is also unknown. Maxime was very cautious about revealing her heritage, presumably due to fear of being persecuted for it. Half-giants are rare in the Wizarding world.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Half-goblin"><a href="/wiki/Half-goblin" title="Half-goblin">Half-goblin</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Half-goblin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Half-goblin"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A half-goblin is the child of a <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a> and a human. Half-goblins are much shorter than ordinary humans, as well as are likely more clever than most humans as well, a trait they could inherit from their goblin ancestry.
</p><p>There are no known half-goblins, but <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Filius Flitwick</a> has "<i>a dash of goblin ancestry</i>", meaning that one of his ancestors was a goblin.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>A half-Veela is the child of a wizard and a <a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veela</a>. Half-Veela are considerably more beautiful than average humans, as well as seem to possess the Veela power of entrancing men. It is unknown if part-Veelas are able to transform into bird-like creatures, as full Veelas can.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was, at one time, unknown if a being with Veela blood could produce a male, as all examples of part-Veelas were female. However, quarter-Veela Fleur Delacour and wizard <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> had a son named <a href="/wiki/Louis_Weasley" title="Louis Weasley">Louis</a>. It is unknown if he is able to entrance human females the way female part-Veelas entrance male humans.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Apolline_Delacour" title="Apolline Delacour">Apolline Delacour</a> is a half-Veela, making her daughters Fleur and <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Delacour" title="Gabrielle Delacour">Gabrielle</a> quarter-veelas and her grandchildren, <a href="/wiki/Victoire_Weasley" title="Victoire Weasley">Victoire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Weasley" title="Dominique Weasley">Dominique</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Weasley" title="Louis Weasley">Louis Weasley</a> one-eighth Veela.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Half-Vampire"><a href="/wiki/Half-Vampire" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-Vampire">Half-Vampire</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Half-Vampire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Half-Vampire"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A half-vampire is the child of a wizard and a <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">Vampire</a>. They supposedly resemble a human but with subtle vampire traits such as mildly exaggerated fangs and pale skin. It is unknown whether they will inherit any vampire tendencies, (e.g. an aversion to sunlight).
</p><p>Singer <a href="/wiki/Lorcan_d%27Eath" title="Lorcan d&#39;Eath">Lorcan d'Eath</a> was a part-vampire wizard. 
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theoretical_Half-humans">Theoretical Half-humans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Theoretical Half-humans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Theoretical Half-humans"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> once thought to himself that <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Flint" title="Marcus Flint">Marcus Flint</a> looked like he might have had <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">troll</a> blood in him, though he may have simply been insulting him, as Marcus was large and rather ugly, as well as why such an instance of cross-breeding may occur, is unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-PS_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> also once stated that a <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatcher</a> was “definitely part troll” based on his body odour, though he was intentionally being humorous and thus might not have meant this literally.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As crossbreeding between humans and goblins exists, it is likely that humans may also crossbreed with&#160;<a href="/wiki/Pukwudgie" title="Pukwudgie">Pukwudgies</a>&#160;and&#160;<a href="/wiki/Dark_Goblin" title="Dark Goblin">Dark Goblins</a>, as they are subtypes or close relatives of goblins. Following the same logic, if&#160;<a href="/wiki/Part_elf" title="Part elf">Part-elves</a>&#160;do indeed exist, it is very plausible there could also be Half-<a href="/wiki/Yumbo" title="Yumbo">yumboes</a>, Half-<a href="/wiki/Erkling" title="Erkling">erklings</a>&#160;and Half-<a href="/wiki/Red_Cap" title="Red Cap">redcaps</a>.&#160;It may also be theoretically possible that&#160;<a href="/wiki/Hag" title="Hag">hags</a>&#160;can cross-breed with humans, however, for obvious reasons (see&#160;<a href="/wiki/Hag" title="Hag">hag</a>), this is unlikely to occur.
</p><p>It is also likely that the other species that can cross breed with humans can supposedly cross breed with each other, as logic would dictate.
</p><p>Other human-like "creatures" include <a href="/wiki/Merpeople" title="Merpeople">merpeople</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manticore" title="Manticore">manticores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sphinx" title="Sphinx">sphinxes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaurs</a>. They are all distinguished from the above species by obvious non-human morphology in spite of having humanoid facial features and intelligence. It is unknown if cross-breeding with these people could be possible.&#160;
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-human">Non-human</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Non-human">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Non-human"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Magical creatures can interbreed with other creatures, both magical and not, aside from humans. Generally, it seems that similar creatures can produce successful offspring. For instance, half-breeds between <a href="/wiki/Crup" title="Crup">Crups</a> and normal <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dogs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kneazle" title="Kneazle">Kneazles</a> and normal <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cats</a> exist, breeding of the latter even being a hobby pursued by some people, such as <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a>. Offspring is also possible between wildly different creatures on occasion, a good example being <a href="/wiki/Blast-Ended-Skrewts" class="mw-redirect" title="Blast-Ended-Skrewts">Blast-Ended-Skrewts</a>, which are crosses between <a href="/wiki/Fire_crab" class="mw-redirect" title="Fire crab">fire crabs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manticore" title="Manticore">manticores</a>: whether magical intervention is required to successfully breed in such a case is unknown, though it is very probable.&#160;It is also likely that the other species that can cross breed with humans can cross breed with each other.
</p><p>In modern times, the creation of new half-breed <a href="/wiki/Beast" title="Beast">beasts</a> is illegal in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> due to the passing of the <a href="/wiki/Ban_on_Experimental_Breeding" title="Ban on Experimental Breeding">Ban on Experimental Breeding</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mistaken_for_half-breeds">Mistaken for half-breeds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Mistaken for half-breeds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Mistaken for half-breeds"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix"><b>Dolores Umbridge</b>: "<i>By the laws laid down by the Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures, any attack by half-breeds such as yourselves on a human-</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b><a href="/wiki/Bane" title="Bane">Bane</a></b>: "<i>What did you call us?</i>"</dd>
<dd><b>Hermione Granger</b>: "<i>Don't call them that!</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> offends a herd of <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">Centaurs</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div><p><a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">Centaurs</a> are their own non-human breed, even though they look to be half-human. They are not half-humans, half-horses, even though some might describe them that way because they possess a human-like head and great intelligence, but their body appears to be that of a horse. They find it extremely offensive to be referred to as half-breeds, as seen in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> when <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> insulted a herd of centaurs by calling them a half-breed.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Similarly, <a href="/wiki/Merpeople" title="Merpeople">Merpeople</a> are not half-breeds, despite their half-fish, half human appearance. Umbridge falsely believed them to be so, as well as suggested them to be rounded up and tagged.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">Werewolves</a> are not half-breeds: they are human victims of the magical affliction lycanthropy. It is unknown if "half-werewolves" exist: the only close example is <a href="/wiki/Edward_Lupin" title="Edward Lupin">Teddy Lupin</a>, the son of a werewolf and a witch. He is a <a href="/wiki/Metamorphmagus" title="Metamorphmagus">Metamorphmagus</a> like his <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">mother</a> and is not a werewolf. Teddy was the first known example of a werewolf's child. Because of the lack of a precedent, his <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">father</a> was extremely worried about passing lycanthropy to him. It is still unknown if the condition is hereditary.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Once again, Umbridge incorrectly believes them to be half-breeds, simply due to their human-animal dual attributes.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Half-breed?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Half-Breeds could be seen as similar to multiracal individuals in the real world. With the term “half-breed” being used in the past to describe someone of mixed-race. With their treatment being parallel to racism for those with notice non-human traits (such as those with notable traits from other races, such as being 3/4 European and 1/4 African). While those with known non-human ancestry but no non-human trains can “pass” as humans (such as some individuals passing as pure European descent during segregation in the Southern United States).</li></ul>
