<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She used a <a href="/wiki/Beautification_Potion" title="Beautification Potion">Beautification Potion</a> to transform herself into a beautiful woman, and married <a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm%27s_husband" title="Malodora Grymm&#39;s husband">a king</a>. She used a <a href="/wiki/Talking_mirror" title="Talking mirror">charmed mirror</a> to compliment her looks and reinforce her self-image. However, she became jealous of <a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm%27s_victim" title="Malodora Grymm&#39;s victim">the most beautiful girl in the kingdom</a> and plotted to get rid of her by using a <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoned</a> <a href="/wiki/Apple_(fruit)" title="Apple (fruit)">apple</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grymm's plot, despite being known as historical fact to the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, appeared to have directly inspired the Muggle fairy tale, <i><a href="/wiki/Snow_White_and_the_Seven_Dwarves" title="Snow White and the Seven Dwarves">Snow White and the Seven Dwarves</a>'<b>.</b></i>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In 2001, actress <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Richardson" title="Miranda Richardson">Miranda Richardson</a>, (who plays <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>), took part in the film, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow_White_(2001_film)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Snow White (2001 film)">Snow White: Fairest of Them All</a>, playing Queen Elspeth, who, like Malodora, was a hag turned into the beautiful and wicked Queen of the fairy tale.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malodora_Grymm?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Malodora consists of the noun "malus", "<i>apple</i>" in Latin (as in "malic acid", the acid which was named after apples and is found in many fruits) or adjective "malum", "<i>bad</i>" or "<i>evil</i>" in Latin; and "doron", "<i>gift</i>" in Greek (as in "Pandora" in Greek mythology, gift from all the gods). Thus, Malodora is "she who gifts apple" or "she who gives evil gift", which is a clear foreshadowing to her giving a poisoned apple to her victim.</li>
<li>Grymm is a nod to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brothers_Grimm" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Brothers Grimm">Brothers Grimm</a>. It can be also a reference to the word grim, meaning "ghastly" or "sinister".</li></ul>
