<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Circe?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Circe?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Circe was featured on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> obtained this card from a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog" title="Chocolate Frog">Chocolate Frog</a> on his first <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">trip</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> There was a portrait of Circe at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>. It guarded a secret shortcut between the fourth-floor landing of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Staircase" title="Grand Staircase">Grand Staircase</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Entrance_Hall" title="Entrance Hall">Entrance Hall</a> side room. The password required to gain access to said shortcut was <i>Piggywiggy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_PC_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG_PC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Circe?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Circe?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a></b>: Circe was proficient in the <a href="/wiki/Human_to_pig_spell" title="Human to pig spell">human to pig</a> <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spell</a>, as she turned lost sailors into said animals. <a href="/wiki/Human_transfiguration" class="mw-redirect" title="Human transfiguration">Human transfiguration</a> is stated as the most complex one, showing she was highly talented in this very difficult branch of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FWC_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FWC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Circe?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Circe?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Circe was a minor goddess in Greek mythology, said to transform sailors lost around her island of <a href="/wiki/Aeaea" title="Aeaea">Aeaea</a> into animals via the use of enchanted food. She is perhaps most well known as a figure in Homer's <i>Odyssey</i>, where the hero Odysseus spent a year on her island after freeing his shipmates from her spells.</li>
<li>Because of her appearance in said Greek texts, Circe is considered by scholars to be the first traditional witch to appear in literature.</li></ul>
