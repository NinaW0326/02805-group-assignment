<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Little is known about the origins of the "Ginger Witch". She was born in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a>, presumably sometime in the late <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a> or early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>, and was living in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> by <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1926_investigation">1926 investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1926 investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1926 investigation"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The "Ginger Witch" found herself under investigation by <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> authorities in <a href="/wiki/November" title="November">November</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>. The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> carried an exclusive report on the incident, calling the witch "mysterious," which suggests this might have been her first brush with the law.<sup id="cite_ref-FBF_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBF-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Wig_theft_prosecution">Wig theft prosecution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Wig theft prosecution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wig theft prosecution"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In late <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, an article by <a href="/wiki/M._Ralphous" title="M. Ralphous">M. Ralphous</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Ghost" title="The New York Ghost">The New York Ghost</a></i> reported that the "Ginger Witch," who was described as being from The Bronx, had been prosecuted for stealing a wig.<sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1927">1927</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1927">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1927"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, she was seen in <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, as reported by French newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Cri_de_la_Gargouille" title="Le Cri de la Gargouille">Le Cri de la Gargouille</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-COG_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Same year, the <a href="/wiki/Spellbound_(magazine)#No._0227" title="Spellbound (magazine)">No. 0227</a> edition of the magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Spellbound_(magazine)" title="Spellbound (magazine)">Spellbound</a></i> claimed to unveil the witch's identity.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bertie_Botts_recall">Bertie Botts recall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Bertie Botts recall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bertie Botts recall"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/August" title="August">August</a> <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, the "Ginger Witch" was implicated in a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/product_recall" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:product recall">product recall</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bertie_Bott%27s_Every_Flavour_Beans" title="Bertie Bott&#39;s Every Flavour Beans">Bertie Bott's Every Flavour Beans</a>. The <i>Daily Prophet</i> reported that the witch was imprisoned and sent to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solitary_confinement" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Solitary confinement">isolation wing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LeakyCon2013_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeakyCon2013-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Hooliganism_arrest">Hooliganism arrest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hooliganism arrest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Hooliganism arrest"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, the <i>Daily Prophet</i> reported that the "Ginger Witch" attended a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Football" title="Football">football</a> match, where she was arrested for hooliganism.<sup id="cite_ref-gofprop_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gofprop-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Flying_pigs_incident">Flying pigs incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Flying pigs incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Flying pigs incident"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In late <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> or early <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, an article by <a href="/wiki/M._Amerinus" title="M. Amerinus">M. Amerinus</a> in the <i>Daily Prophet</i> reported that the "Ginger Witch" was implicated in an incident involving two <a href="/wiki/Flying_pig" title="Flying pig">flying pigs</a>, which interfered with the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Aeroplane" title="Aeroplane">air traffic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Henna_explosion">Henna explosion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Henna explosion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Henna explosion"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In August <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, the "Ginger Witch" was caught in a <a href="/wiki/Henna" title="Henna">henna</a> explosion, but survived. The story made the front page of the <i>Daily Prophet</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fake_henna_scandal">Fake henna scandal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Fake henna scandal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Fake henna scandal"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">Quibbler</a></i> reported that the "Ginger Witch" was arrested in <a href="/wiki/Caxambu" title="Caxambu">Caxambu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> with fake henna.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The magazine later reported that this scandal lead to her being sent to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She was eventually released from the prison on an unknown date.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ginger_Witch?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Lima" title="Eduardo Lima">Eduardo Lima</a>, a graphic artist who worked on every <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_films" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter films"><i>Harry Potter</i> film</a> from <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i> onward, revealed that this character was inspired by Debbie, a red-haired friend of his and <a href="/wiki/Miraphora_Mina" title="Miraphora Mina">Miraphora Mina</a> who worked as a buyer for the set decoration department. The pair of graphic artists gave her the nickname "Ginger Witch" and inserted a series of funny headlines about "The Ginger Witch" into the wizarding publications they designed for the film, including the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
