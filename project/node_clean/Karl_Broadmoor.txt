<b>Karl Broadmoor</b>, along with his brother, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Broadmoor" title="Kevin Broadmoor">Kevin</a>, was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beater" title="Beater">Beater</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Falmouth_Falcons" title="Falmouth Falcons">Falmouth Falcons</a> from <a href="/wiki/1958" title="1958">1958</a> to <a href="/wiki/1969" title="1969">1969</a>. Karl and Kevin earned no less than fourteen suspensions for their violent play and constant rule-breaking.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Karl_Broadmoor?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Karl_Broadmoor?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Karl" is a German and Scandinavian form of <i>Charles</i>. This was the name of seven emperors of Austria. Other famous bearers include Karl Marx (1818-1883), the German philosopher and revolutionary who laid the foundations for communism, and Karl Jaspers (1883-1969), a German existentialist philosopher.
</p>
