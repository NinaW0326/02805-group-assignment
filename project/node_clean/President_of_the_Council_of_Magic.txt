<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Council_of_Magic?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Council_of_Magic?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Argentinian_Council_of_Magic" title="Argentinian Council of Magic">Argentinian Council of Magic</a> is the first magical government in <a href="/wiki/Canon" title="Canon">canon</a> not to be referred to as a "<a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>". It is unknown precisely what distinguishes a Council of Magic from a Ministry of Magic, but it would seem that the former does not answer to the Muggle cabinet, while the latter does (to some degree, at least formally).</li></ul>
