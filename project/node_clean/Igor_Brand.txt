<b>Igor Brand</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a> on the <a href="/wiki/German_National_Quidditch_team" title="German National Quidditch team">German National Quidditch team</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">422nd Quidditch World Cup</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Brand?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Igor_Brand?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Igor" is a Russian form of <i>Ingvarr</i>. It was brought to Russia by the Varangians in the 10th century. This name was borne by Igor Stravinsky (1882-1971), a Russian composer whose most famous work is <i>The Rite of Spring</i>.
</p>
