<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Cotton?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Cotton?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During a match with the <a href="/wiki/Pride_of_Portree" title="Pride of Portree">Pride of Portree</a>, he collided with the opposing team's Seeker, <a href="/wiki/Dougal_McBride" title="Dougal McBride">Dougal McBride</a>, while both were locked in a race to catch the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wizardsc_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wizardsm_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsm-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dp_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dp-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Cotton came out with the Snitch in his hand, but an altercation with McBride ensued, during which <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxes</a> were illegally exchanged, and McBride <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">transfigured</a> Cotton's head into a cabbage.<sup id="cite_ref-wizardsc_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dp_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dp-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> McBride defended his actions to the referee, <a href="/wiki/Josiah_Plunkett" title="Josiah Plunkett">Josiah Plunkett</a>, by claiming that Cotton had hit him with a <a href="/wiki/Jelly-Fingers_Curse" title="Jelly-Fingers Curse">Jelly-Fingers Curse</a> during the race for the Snitch, and thus deserved to be "cabbaged."<sup id="cite_ref-wizardsm_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsm-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wizardsp_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wizardsp-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Cotton?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gregory_Cotton?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Gregory" is the English form of Latin <i>Gregorius</i>, which was from the Late Greek name Γρηγοριος (<i>Gregorios</i>), derived from γρηγορος (<i>gregoros</i>) meaning "watchful, alert". This name was popular among early Christians, being borne by a number of important saints including Saint Gregory Thaumaturgus (3rd century), Saint Gregory of Nyssa (4th century), Saint Gregory of Nazianzus (4th century), and Saint Gregory of Tours (6th century). It was also borne by the 6th-century pope Saint Gregory I the Great, a reformer and Doctor of the Church, as well as 15 subsequent popes.
</p>
