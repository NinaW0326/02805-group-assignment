<dd>&#8212;Rita's horrid personality<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Rita Skeeter</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1951" title="1951">1951</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and journalist who specialised in writing poison-pen stories. These stories tended to be based on false information and misreported interviews while she worked for the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>, as well authoring a few tell-all biographies. Skeeter preferred writing for the sake of publicity and wrote what she thought people would "like to read" rather than what they "ought to read" and which was the truth.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> described her writing as "<i>enchantingly nasty</i>" after she wrote an unfavourable article about him.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Notable stories Rita covered include the trials of various <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> after the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, and (albeit reluctantly) <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s account of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">return to power</a> in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>. She also wrote biographies of: <a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet" title="Armando Dippet">Armando Dippet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, and Harry Potter.
</p><p>Rita's writing tended to be sensationalist and sometimes outright dishonest. Her <a href="/wiki/Biography" title="Biography">biography</a> of Harry Potter for instance, is only one-quarter factual with the remaining three-quarters being pure fabrication.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Her ability to acquire information was assisted by her status as an unregistered beetle <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Skeeter was born into a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> or <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> family in <a href="/wiki/1951" title="1951">1951</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Skeeter spent time training to become an <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> and, at some point prior to 1986, she was finally able to transform into a <a href="/wiki/Beetle" title="Beetle">beetle</a> at will. Skeeter aspired to be a journalist and an author, and used this ability to spy and eavesdrop for her articles.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During her early days as an author, Skeeter wrote a biography on <a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet" title="Armando Dippet">Armando Dippet</a>, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet:_Master_or_Moron%3F" title="Armando Dippet: Master or Moron?">Armando Dippet: Master or Moron?</a></i>. It was presumably an exaggerated history of him to gain controversy and by extension, popularity and fame. This book turned out to be a best-seller, with numerous copies sold. Also sometime in her early career, Skeeter provided a quote for the critical acclaim for <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages" title="Quidditch Through the Ages">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Kennilworthy_Whisp" title="Kennilworthy Whisp">Kennilworthy Whisp</a>, when she wrote "<i>I've read worse.</i>" as a review.
</p>

<p>Skeeter was a journalist at the time of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> when <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> was in power. In 1981, at the fall of Voldemort and end of the War, Skeeter reported on the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> trials before the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Magical_Law" title="Council of Magical Law">Council of Magical Law</a>, of people such as <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Bagman" title="Ludovic Bagman">Ludovic Bagman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Guest_speaker_at_Hogwarts">Guest speaker at Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Guest speaker at Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Guest speaker at Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 172px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/24/SkeeterHM.png/revision/latest?cb=20190627112845" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="SkeeterHM.png" data-image-key="SkeeterHM.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/24/SkeeterHM.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/170?cb=20190627112845" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/24/SkeeterHM.png/revision/latest?cb=20190627112845" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/24/SkeeterHM.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/170?cb=20190627112845" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="SkeeterHM.png" data-image-key="SkeeterHM.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/24/SkeeterHM.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/170?cb=20190627112845" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:SkeeterHM.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Rita Skeeter at Hogwarts during the <a href="/wiki/1986%E2%80%931987_school_year" title="1986–1987 school year">1986–1987 school year</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Rita was a guest speaker at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1986%E2%80%931987_school_year" title="1986–1987 school year">1986–1987 school year</a>. Prior to the reveal, the identity of the speaker generated quite a buzz throughout the school, with <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Khanna" title="Rowan Khanna">Rowan Khanna</a>'s guess being an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, or a random <a href="/wiki/Arithmancer" title="Arithmancer">Arithmancer</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> thought to perhaps be <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Curse-Breaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Unidentified Curse-Breaker">the person</a> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> had been searching for throughout the school year. Professor <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> introduced her to the school in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>, with Rita adding a bit more detail, stating herself to be "a widely popular writer for the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>." She then gave a speech about the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a> being curious about the happenings within Hogwarts; <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vaults</a> aside, there apparently were talks about Hogwarts having the most promising assemblage of students in years, and that, in order to find out who the brightest student was, Professor Dumbledore had given the Prophet permission to observe and conduct a friendly competition, with the winner featuring on a front page article in the Prophet to start. Rita then observed <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flying_(class)" title="Flying (class)">Flying</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a> classes, much to most of the instructors' annoyance.<sup id="cite_ref-HM3_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM3-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Book_researcher">Book researcher</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Book researcher">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Book researcher"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Subsequent to her guest speaker role, Rita returned to Hogwarts to interview the staff as research for a book she was writing. She was particularly interested in uncovering Hogwarts dark and dangerous secrets in the hopes her book would force the headmaster to step down. Jacob's sibling was assigned as a chaperone while she interviewed Hagrid, <a href="/wiki/Torvus" title="Torvus">Torvus</a> the <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">Centaur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silvanus_Kettleburn" title="Silvanus Kettleburn">Silvanus Kettleburn</a>, and inspected the <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Section" title="Restricted Section">Restricted Section</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">Hogwarts Library</a>. Jacob's sibling reported her questions and motivations to Albus Dumbledore, who revealed to him that Rita was an unregistered Animagus who was using her abilities to eavesdrop at the time. Dumbledore convinced her to return to human form and they all came to an agreement to not expose Rita's abilities in exchange for her not publishing her book critical of Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-HM4_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM4-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Reappearance_of_the_Dark_Mark" title="Reappearance of the Dark Mark">Reappearance of the Dark Mark</a> after the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a>, Skeeter penned a scathing report of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>'s handling over the riots, accusing the Ministry of incompetence over their failure to catch any of the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">culprits</a>. These articles particularly angered <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> for her obviously biased reporting, and he along with his son and fellow Ministry employee <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a> had to head to the Ministry to help sort out the controversy, as she had mentioned Arthur in her article.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF10_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>That year, Rita was given the opportunity to interview the Triwizard Tournament contestants for an article in the <i>Daily Prophet</i>. Under the guise of interviewing all four champions, she confronted Harry Potter in a broom cupboard where she took his "um"s and "er"s and invented her own quotes. Eventually, her "interview of the contestants" turned out to be a highly falsified story of Harry himself.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

<p><br />
The article painted Harry as a tragic hero who cried himself to sleep over his <a href="/wiki/Potter_family" title="Potter family">late parents</a> and who had a romantic relationship with <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>. The article was mostly taken negatively by Harry's schoolmates, who assumed he was craving attention, although <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a> seemed to take it very seriously, to the point that she was particularly cool towards Hermione for a time until Harry assured her that the article was lying and he and Hermione had never dated. At some point after the first task, Albus Dumbledore had banned Rita from entering <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF21_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Rita then interviewed <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>, asking many questions about Harry that Hagrid refused to answer.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF21_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF21-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Her subsequent article described Hagrid as a ferocious-looking man who used his authority to terrify his students, but was otherwise mostly true. The article also revealed to the world that Hagrid was actually <a href="/wiki/Half-giant" title="Half-giant">half-giant</a>, which prompted letters from parents frightened by the idea of having a dangerous giant teaching their children and greatly upset Hagrid, subjecting him to ridicule and fear, although his mood was rallied when Harry, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a>, Hermione, and Dumbledore visited him and assured him that they didn't care about his family being the vicious monsters everyone believed them to be. <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">Giants</a> really were vicious and brutal, but Hagrid obviously wasn't, so Professor Dumbledore insisted Hagrid deny any insults and return to work, as Harry and his friends didn't care about Hagrid's family due to their knowledge of him as a person.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF24_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF24-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>

<p>When Rita encountered Harry, Ron, and Hermione in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a>, Hermione insulted her for her libel. Rita, in retaliation, then wrote a nasty story about the "<i>devious Miss Granger</i>" based on false rumours provided by <a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson" title="Pansy Parkinson">Pansy Parkinson</a>, making her out to be a plain but skilled witch who used <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potions</a> to satisfy her <i>"taste for famous wizards.</i>" Rita implied that Hermione was toying with the affections of both Harry and Krum.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF27_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF27-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Although Hermione was more disdainful than upset about the article, she unfortunately started receiving angry mail from people who believed Rita's lies. Some of the hate mail even came with <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a>; even Molly Weasley was upset with Hermione until Harry set her straight.

<p>Rita's last defaming article stated that Harry was "<i>disturbed and dangerous</i>," and used comments from Draco and his <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> cronies as its basis.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF31_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF31-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Ultimately, Hermione discovered the means by which Rita spied on others and forced her to "<i>keep her quill to herself for a full year</i>" by catching her in her beetle form while she was perched on the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a> window and trapping her in a jar imbued with an <a href="/wiki/Unbreakable_Charm" title="Unbreakable Charm">Unbreakable Charm</a> so she couldn't transform. Hermione blackmailed Rita by threatening to report her to the authorities as an illegal <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> to the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, which would have severe legal consequences. Rita was forced to comply, and suffered financial strain as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF37_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF37-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Unfortunately her last article would discredit Harry and serve as the basis for <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> to refuse to believe Harry's story about Voldemort's return that would start the Ministry's <a href="/wiki/Campaign_to_discredit_Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter" title="Campaign to discredit Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter">smear campaign</a> against Harry and Dumbledore.

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, Harry Potter's story that Lord Voldemort had <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">returned</a> was not believed by much of the wizarding world, as the <i>Daily Prophet</i> portrayed him as either a delusional boy or an attention-seeking liar. Hermione Granger wanted to get the truth to the public, and thus instructed Rita to meet her and <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> on <a href="/wiki/14_February" title="14 February">14 February</a> <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Three_Broomsticks_Inn" title="Three Broomsticks Inn">Three Broomsticks Inn</a>. As it was Rita who laid the foundation for the <i>Prophet's</i> defamation of Harry, with her initial stories about him, Hermione wanted her to be the one to set the record straight. They were joined by Harry after his date with <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a>. Harry thought that Hermione and Rita were "<i>the unlikeliest pair of drinking mates he could ever have imagined.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-OP25_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP25-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rita was initially taken aback that Hermione wanted her to interview Harry, and then resentful, since Hermione demanded that she only write exactly what Harry said and not portray him as the rest of the press was. Rita claimed the <i>Prophet</i> would not buy the story, reluctantly admitting that the Ministry of Magic was influencing it. Hermione told her that the interview would be printed in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">the Quibbler</a></i>, which <a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood" title="Xenophilius Lovegood">Luna's father</a> edited, and although Rita responded with disdain, she "eyed Hermione shrewdly" for a few moments and then agreed. However, when she learned she was expected to conduct the interview free of charge, she was furious. Because Hermione was able to blackmail Rita with the threat of reporting her unregistered Animagus status to the Ministry, Rita grudgingly complied and wrote the article.<sup id="cite_ref-OP25_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP25-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The interview was subsequently printed in the <i>Quibbler</i>, and became its best-selling issue of all time. It was banned at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> by High Inquisitor <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, which seemed to only increase its popularity.<sup id="cite_ref-OP26_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP26-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Editor <a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood" title="Xenophilius Lovegood">Xenophilius Lovegood</a> sold the article to the <i>Prophet</i> after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a>, when Voldemort's return became public, and he and his daughter Luna used the money to travel to <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> in search of the <a href="/wiki/Crumple-Horned_Snorkack" title="Crumple-Horned Snorkack">Crumple-Horned Snorkack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP38_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP38-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Rita made a brief appearance at the end of the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">1996–1997 school year</a>, where Harry was infuriated to notice her clutching a notebook at <a href="/wiki/Funeral_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="Funeral of Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore's funeral</a> in <a href="/wiki/June" title="June">June</a> <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP30_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP30-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It was, perhaps, her being present at the funeral that prompted her to write a biography of Albus Dumbledore, as within four weeks Skeeter wrote a 900-page tell-all biography about him, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></i>, which also included authentic old photos of the famed Hogwarts headmaster. Rita was interviewed by <a href="/wiki/Betty_Braithwaite" title="Betty Braithwaite">Betty Braithwaite</a>, a fellow Daily Prophet reporter in her <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter%27s_home" title="Rita Skeeter&#39;s home">home</a>, whom she treated very warmly, serving her tea and <a href="/wiki/Pound_cake" title="Pound cake">Pound cake</a> and informing her of much gossip surrounding the book's release.<sup id="cite_ref-DH2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH2-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The book depicted the former headmaster in an extremely negative light but was mostly based on truth, much to Harry's horror. It was implied that she got some of her information in unethical or illegal ways, as much of the book's content was obtained through an interview Rita conducted with <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot" title="Bathilda Bagshot">Bathilda Bagshot</a>, a very elderly historian, where Skeeter used <a href="/wiki/Veritaserum" title="Veritaserum">Veritaserum</a> to gain information about Dumbledore’s childhood. Skeeter stole photographs from Bagshot as well, and the subsequent novel sensationalised everything, implying that Dumbledore had dabbled in the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> and contributed to the death of his sister.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It turned out that Rita got at least some of her facts straight for once, but had no idea of the true story, such as claiming that <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Dumbledore" title="Ariana Dumbledore">Ariana Dumbledore</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squib</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> when in reality she suffered from uncontrollable bursts of magic due to a highly traumatic experience when she was around six.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">

<p>Following Voldemort's death, Skeeter wrote a biography of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> that was only about one-quarter true,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> as well as one about <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Snape:_Scoundrel_or_Saint%3F" title="Snape: Scoundrel or Saint?">Snape: Scoundrel or Saint?</a></i>. The latter added to her series of hatchet jobs on former headmasters of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, along with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet:_Master_or_Moron%3F" title="Armando Dippet: Master or Moron?">Armando Dippet: Master or Moron?</a></i>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/April" title="April">April</a>, <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a>, just before the opening matches of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="2014 Quidditch World Cup">427th Quidditch World Cup</a>, Brazilian manager <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Barboza" title="José Barboza">José Barboza</a> called the Welsh Chasers "<i>talentless hags</i>" over drinks with Rita Skeeter. These remarks became public, and Welsh manager <a href="/wiki/Gwenog_Jones" title="Gwenog Jones">Gwenog Jones</a> promptly threatened to "<i>curse</i> [his] <i>face off</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-bra-v-wal_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bra-v-wal-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PM-QWC_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-QWC-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> This created a large tension between Jones and Barboza, culminating in a brawl between the two following the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_National_Quidditch_team" title="Brazilian National Quidditch team">Brazil</a> vs. <a href="/wiki/Welsh_National_Quidditch_team" title="Welsh National Quidditch team">Wales</a> match of the quarter-finals, on 4 June: Gwenog Jones attempted to keep her promise to curse off Barboza's face in full view of the packed stadium — the Welsh manager was dragged from the pitch by her own <a href="/wiki/Beater" title="Beater">Beaters</a>, and was later put in custody; and the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> later reported that <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healers</a> confirmed Barboza's skin had almost regrown.<sup id="cite_ref-bra-v-wal_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bra-v-wal-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rita Skeeter wrote a gossip column in <i>The Daily Prophet</i> about the Quidditch World Cup on <a href="/wiki/8_July" title="8 July">8 July</a>, <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a>, in which she mentioned that she was about to publish a new book about <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> on <a href="/wiki/31_July" title="31 July">31 July</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army:_The_Dark_Side_of_the_Demob" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army: The Dark Side of the Demob">Dumbledore's Army: The Dark Side of the Demob</a></i>.
</p><p>Rita Skeeter reported live from the final of the 2014 Quidditch World Cup in the <a href="/wiki/Patagonian_Desert" title="Patagonian Desert">Patagonian Desert</a>, on 11 July 2014. Instead of focusing on the players, she spent the match spying on VIP Box Two in which the most prominent members of Dumbledore's Army were watching the game. <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Potter</a>, with Rita at the journalists' enclosure, repeatedly made corrections to her reports, until, shortly after the match ended, Rita remarked that her husband Harry Potter was "<i>publicity-hungry</i>" — just as Ginny was approaching, Rita was taken "unaccountably ill" with what some called "<i>a jinx to the solar plexus</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-the-qwc2014-final_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-the-qwc2014-final-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Rita published a new book: <a href="/wiki/Man_or_Monster%3F_The_TRUTH_about_Newt_Scamander" title="Man or Monster? The TRUTH about Newt Scamander">Man or Monster? The TRUTH about Newt Scamander</a>; like the rest of her books, most of it was rubbish with one claim being that Newt Scamander broke <a href="/wiki/Seraphina_Picquery" title="Seraphina Picquery">Seraphina Picquery</a>'s heart in 1926, something which he revealed was untrue in his own book.
</p>

<p>Skeeter was described as having blonde hair set in elaborate curls that contrasted oddly with her heavy-jawed face. She wore jewelled spectacles studded with rhinestones, and had thick fingers ending in two-inch nails, painted crimson. Her blonde curls were curiously rigid, suggesting it was styled with the magical equivalent of hairspray. In addition, she had pencilled-on eyebrows and three gold teeth, as well as large, masculine hands. Her bright scarlet painted fingernails and toenails were usually likened to claws or talons.
</p><p>She was known to carry with her a crocodile-skin handbag, inside of which she carried her acid green Quick-Quotes Quill. She usually wore magenta-coloured robes but also wore clothes of green leather with maroon furs at the collar and sleeves.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Apparently, unemployment did not serve Rita well, as when she showed up during the <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">year</a> she was blackmailed by Hermione not to write, Rita's nails were chipped, she was missing fake stones in her glasses, and her hair was lank and unkempt.<sup id="cite_ref-OP25_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP25-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As in her unregistered <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> beetle form, her antennas were curled to resemble her curls in her hair and she had marking around her antennas resembling her jewelled glasses.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF37_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF37-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>Rita Skeeter goes out of her way to cause trouble, Amos! I would have thought you'd know that, working at the Ministry!</i>"</span></dd>

<p>Rita was an unregistered <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>, wherein she took the form of a beetle and was able to slip around undetected and spy on unsuspecting company so she could obtain gossip for her articles which she later exaggerated and exploited to gain the attention of the masses. Her ability to turn into a beetle also reflected how irritating and bothersome she was to other people, particularly <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, and it also displayed her dishonest and cunning characteristics. Rita also wrote primarily for the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>, though some of her articles have also appeared in other sources, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Witch_Weekly" title="Witch Weekly">Witch Weekly</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>. Skeeter worked closely alongside <a href="/wiki/Bozo" title="Bozo">Bozo</a>, her photographer.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>You don't want to go upsetting Rita Skeeter. I'm serious, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a>, she'll dig up something on you —</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Rita and her tendency for revenge<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Rita was extremely nosy and willing to do anything for a good story — from spying on people in her <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> form of a beetle to sensationalising or outright inventing stories. She also took advantage of elderly <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot" title="Bathilda Bagshot">Bathilda Bagshot</a>'s possibly fragile state of mind (even giving her <a href="/wiki/Veritaserum" title="Veritaserum">Veritaserum</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> in order to dig up dirt on the late <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> for her extremely critical <a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">biography</a>. She did this despite the use of such a powerful truth serum being heavily controlled by the Ministry. Rita tended to portray the people she wrote about poorly, presumably because it sold more stories.
</p><p>However, she could also be very vindictive and spiteful towards those she disliked. For example, after Hermione Granger insulted her, Rita printed a completely false story about Hermione using <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">love potions</a> and toying with the hearts of both <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>. This resulted in Hermione receiving <a href="/wiki/Witch_Weekly_readers%27_hate_mail_to_Hermione_Granger" title="Witch Weekly readers&#39; hate mail to Hermione Granger">hate mail</a>, but she eventually got her revenge and blackmailed Rita into refraining from writing for a year. After this time period was up, Rita returned to her writing with as much gusto and as little scruples as before.
</p><p>Despite her inaccuracies and biased articles, Rita seemed to be a fast writer and researcher, as she managed to finish a nine-hundred-page book in four weeks.
</p><p>Rita seemed to have a somewhat low opinion of the intelligence of her average reader, based on her style of reporting. In a previous article she once referred to Hermione Granger as being "<i>stunningly pretty</i>," only to then describe her in an <a href="/wiki/HARRY_POTTER%27S_SECRET_HEARTACHE" title="HARRY POTTER&#39;S SECRET HEARTACHE">article</a> published just a few months later as "<i>a plain, but ambitious girl</i>." She also stated regarding the <i>Daily Prophet</i> that it "<i>exists to sell itself</i>."
</p><p>While she had a huge disregard for her interviewee's reputations and journalistic integrity, Rita deeply feared her own status as an unregistered animagus being revealed to the world. She gave up a year's worth of article writing when blackmailed by Hermione Granger (her main means of making a living) as she knew the alternate consequences would be a sentence in Azkaban. However, while Hermione thought this one year of abstinence would curb Skeeter's ill-habit of writing hurtful articles, she was wrong, as once the deal has reached its end, she swiftly and shamelessly returned to her old ways of writing sensationalist and dishonest journalism.
</p>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a></b>: Rita had mastered an incredibly complex branch of <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a>, becoming an unregistered beetle <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>. <li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Rita had the ability to conjure at least a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> a highly advanced and powerful protective charm and a mark of superior magical skill. She could also effortlessly cast simple charms, such as the <a href="/wiki/Fire-Making_Spell" title="Fire-Making Spell">Fire-Making Spell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Levitation_Charm" title="Levitation Charm">Levitation Charm</a>, which she also could cast non-verbally.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>She unsnapped her crocodile-skin handbag and pulled out a handful of candles, which she lit with a wave of her wand and magicked into midair, so that they could see what they were doing.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Rita using non-verbal magic<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Nonverbal magic</a></b>: Skeeter <a href="/wiki/Fire-Making_Spell" title="Fire-Making Spell">lit</a> several <a href="/wiki/Candle" title="Candle">candles</a> from her handbag and <a href="/wiki/Levitation_Charm" title="Levitation Charm">levitated</a> them into the air, with her wand without saying the incantation.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_9-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This implies she was a powerful witch, given the difficulty of casting nonverbal <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a>. Dumbledore had acknowledged her skills in Nonverbal magic as 'ironic' given her irritatingly talkative personality.<sup id="cite_ref-HM4_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM4-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Journalistic skills</b>: As one of the main editors for the <a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a>, she was a very talented journalist and had years of experience in the field, having taken part in assessing the Death Eater trials after the first Wizarding War ended.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Her primary work, <i>the Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</i>, was mostly based on true information, much to the disdain of many such as Harry Potter,<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> showing her to be a skilled researcher and writer as well.</li></ul>

<p>Rita Skeeter first met Harry Potter in 1994 when <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> was housing the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, which Harry's name shot surprising from the <a href="/wiki/Goblet_of_Fire" title="Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a>. She immediately tried to get an interview with Harry Potter, but did not get the chance until the the <a href="/wiki/Wand_weighing_ceremony" title="Wand weighing ceremony">Weighing of the Wands</a>, which she did inside a small broom cupboard. After the interview she dedicated a whole page of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> for his interview, which greatly annoyed <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">Amos Diggory</a>, the father of <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric</a>, another Hogwarts champion.
</p><p>Shortly before the <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Challenge" title="Dragon Challenge">First Task</a> she tried to get another interview but was interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> and was told to leave, Skeeter ended up getting her revenge on Hermione several times after this in <i><a href="/wiki/Witch_Weekly" title="Witch Weekly">Witch Weekly</a></i> and the <i>Daily Prophet</i>. After Harry defeated the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Horntail" title="Hungarian Horntail">Hungarian Horntail</a> and retrieved his egg, Rita tried to get another interview to which Harry refused. Harry did not see Rita Skeeter again until the next year where he gave her an interview for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i> announcing the return of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, which she did bitterly.
</p>

<p>Rita Skeeter clashed with Hermione several times during both The Triwizard Tournament, and during the denial of Lord Voldemort's return. She publicly shamed Hermione for her relationship with Viktor Krum and her supposed relationship with Harry Potter. When Hermione confronted and insulted Rita for having written horrible untruths about her friends, Rita vindictively responded by writing an article that painted Hermione as a "scarlet woman" who used her intellect and charm to toy with the affections of famous wizards. This caused Hermione to receive a large amount of hate mail, at least two of which were intended to cause significant harm to her, and be treated coldly by Molly Weasley who otherwise was very nice to her.
</p><p>In return, Hermione furiously vowed to get revenge on Rita if it was the last thing she ever did. After researching for half a year about how Rita could possibly be getting her information despite being nowhere near, and an unintentionally helpful hint from Harry, Hermione deduced that she was an unregistered Anigmagus and blackmailed her into keeping quiet for a whole year under threat of being exposed, which would otherwise land her in severe trouble.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF37_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF37-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Hermione used this as leverage to get Rita to do an interview with Harry for <i>The Quibbler</i> without any charge. She called Hermione "Miss Prissy" and "Little Miss Perfect" due to her dislike of her.<sup id="cite_ref-OP25_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP25-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the death of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, Rita made her own biography about him called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">the Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></i>, which infuriated Hermione further.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?veaction=edit&amp;section=24" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rita" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Rita">Rita</a></i> is a short form of the name <i>Margarita</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> a Latinate form of the name <i>Margaret</i>. The name is derived from the Greek μαργαρίτης, <i>margarites</i>, meaning "pearl."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Skeeter</i> is a slang term for a "mosquito." This could be a reference to her <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> form, which is an insect, her irritating personality, or to her career in tabloid journalism. The word <i>paparazzi</i> is derived from an Italian word for "mosquito."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> "Skeet" is also an Anglo-Manx colloquialism for gossip or an act of nosiness.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Skeeter could also be a pun off of how annoying Skeeter's lies tend to be, as people tend to find mosquitoes annoying and attempt to swat them away.</li>
<li>The character is similar to the "poison pen" biographer <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kitty_Kelley" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Kitty Kelley">Kitty Kelley</a>. Both have a semi-rhyming name with an animal reference, and each is known for writing scandalous unauthorised biographies that largely consist of publishing every available, unsubstantiated rumour about an individual. There is an interesting similarity in phrases used to describe the two authors. Skeeter was called "enchantingly nasty" by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a></i>, and Kelley was called "encyclopedically vicious", by the <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/6179623.stm"><i>New York Times</i></a> while reviewing her biography of <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/6179623.stm">Nancy Reagan</a>.</li></ul>

<ul><li>According to Skeeter herself, the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> have told her exactly 514 times that it does not comment on the top-secret work of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DA_QWC_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DA_QWC-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Whether this is just under <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a> or throughout Skeeter's entire career is unknown.</li>
<li>Rita may have been a classmate of <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>, as both witches were born in <a href="/wiki/1951" title="1951">1951</a>. But even if they both went to Hogwarts, it cannot be guaranteed they were in the same year because of their unspecified birth months. It was also never confirmed if Rita attended Hogwarts.</li>
<li>As of the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Rita is the only known living unregistered <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>.</li>
<li>During an interview on BBC Radio 4, Rowling admitted that Rita was originally called Bridget and appeared in the first draft of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, trying to interview <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a> on <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_eleventh_birthday" title="Harry Potter&#39;s eleventh birthday">his 11th birthday</a>. She later cut the character from the final draft, wanting Rita to appear in Harry's fourth year, just when his fame starts to oppress him.<sup id="cite_ref-desertisland_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-desertisland-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Accio_Quote_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Accio_Quote-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the German audio books, Rita speaks with an American accent.</li>
<li>Rita's tendency to write sensationalised and untrue stories about high profile members of the wizarding community is similar to (and may be a direct parody of) tabloid magazines stirring up stories about celebrities, politicians, and the economic elite. This can be seen in her shock-jock style of writing and purposeful misrepresentation of people's character in order to garner readership that prove to be consistently untrue and slanderous, with her interview with Harry about Voldemort's return being the only verified and totally accurate publication of hers to date.</li>
<li>Rita is voiced by <a class="new" title="Annette Badland (page does not exist)" rel="nofollow" data-uncrawlable-url="L3dpa2kvQW5uZXR0ZV9CYWRsYW5kP2FjdGlvbj1lZGl0JnJlZGxpbms9MQ==">Annette Badland</a> in the audiobook version of <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages_(real)" title="Quidditch Through the Ages (real)">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-QTAaudio_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAaudio-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></li>

As <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a>'s house has been trailing in the House competition for some time, despite carrying the house all year, it is a good chance to praise the professors after the lesson to gain points in Charms and Transfiguration. In the duelling portion, you can choose to duel against Merula again, or against <a href="/wiki/Penny_Haywood" title="Penny Haywood">Penny Haywood</a> (the second-weakest Charms student after Rowan when they were in Year 3) or <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a>, the strongest duellist of the three.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_films">In films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?veaction=edit&amp;section=26" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: In films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter?action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: In films"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Rita Skeeter was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> actress <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Richardson" title="Miranda Richardson">Miranda Richardson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, who portrayed Mrs Melisha Tweedy in <i>Chicken Run</i>, Queen Elizabeth I in<i> Blackadder</i>, Mrs Rose Arbutnot in <i>Enchanted April</i>, Lady Mary Van Tassel in <i>Sleepy Hollow</i> (who is also a witch, albeit an evil one), and Madame Giry in <i>The Phantom Of The Opera</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, Rita uses a spiral-bound <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> legal pad to conduct Harry's "interview." She is also seen using a Muggle ink pen during <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a>'s trial. It is established in the literary <a href="/wiki/Canon" title="Canon">canon</a> that the wizarding world uses loose parchment and quills for writing.</li>
<li>Skeeter was not revealed to be an animagus in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, and thus it did not include Hermione's later blackmailing of her.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, when Harry points out that they're in a broom cupboard, Rita points out that he should "feel right at home", referring to the fact that Harry in his earlier life lived in the cupboard under the stairs at the <a href="/wiki/Dursley_family" title="Dursley family">Dursley</a> house, hinting she knew about his childhood. Alternatively, she could have been alerting to the fact that it contained brooms, referring to his love of flying and quidditch; or otherwise hinting that Harry, like a number of students before him, is used to spending time kissing in broom cupboards, despite the fact that he didn't have his first kiss until his fifth year in the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>.</li>
<li>Although Rita has three gold teeth in the books, she does not in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>. Miranda Richardson and director <a href="/wiki/Mike_Newell" title="Mike Newell">Mike Newell</a> both felt that they were not right for Rita, with Miranda pointing out that gold teeth would make Rita appear 'unapproachable'. Instead, she chose to have a diamond in one of her teeth, saying that it was "the perfect embellishment for... an amalgam of sparkling brilliance and total hardness".</li>
<li>Rita does not appear in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_Limited_Edition" title="Harry Potter Limited Edition">Harry Potter Limited Edition</a></i> it is described that in the films, it was conceived by <a href="/wiki/Jany_Temime" title="Jany Temime">Jany Temime</a> that Rita would always dress for the story that she was reporting on.</li></ul>
