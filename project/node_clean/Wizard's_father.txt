<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>There was once a kindly old wizard who used his magic generously and wisely for the benefit of his neighbours.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description of the wizard's father<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The <b>wizard's father</b> was the father of the <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot)" title="Wizard (The Wizard and the Hopping Pot)">young wizard</a> who despised <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot" title="The Wizard and the Hopping Pot">The Wizard and the Hopping Pot</a></i>. The father used all his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> to help his <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> neighbours' illnesses and woes. He died at a goodly age, leaving all his belongings to his only son, who had none of the qualities his father had. One of the belongings he left to his son was his <a href="/wiki/The_Hopping_Pot" title="The Hopping Pot">lucky cooking pot</a>, which he enchanted in the hope that it would give him a lesson by experiencing the troubles of the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>' misery. The pot made the son's conscience awaken, and the young wizard agreed to use his magic to help his <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> neighbours as his father had done before him.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The father used his pot to brew <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antidote" title="Antidote">antidotes</a> for the local <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> when they needed his help.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He brewed a potion to help an old woman's granddaughter's warts and he bade a young woman to come if she had any trouble with her baby.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, generous as he was, he was wise enough to know not to reveal the source of his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> to his neighbours and instead he pretended that all his <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charms</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antidote" title="Antidote">antidotes</a> sprang ready-made from the little <a href="/wiki/Cauldron" title="Cauldron">cauldron</a> he called his "lucky cooking pot".<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_father?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_father?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Your father used to mix a special poultice in that old cooking pot-</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The old wizard's generousity<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>The wizard was described as a very kind and generous man.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He seemed to have pro-<a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> leanings as he did all he could to help his Muggle neighbours. He shared this trait with Beedle the Bard. He was also described as a wise man. However his anti-<a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> son, often quarrelled with his father's habit of helping their neighbours, but eventually his son finally decided to help them.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Rather than reveal the true source of his power, he pretended that his <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charms</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antidote" title="Antidote">antidotes</a> sprang ready-made from the little cauldron he called his lucky cooking pot.</i>"</span></dd>

<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">Healing magic</a></b>: The wizard had a very notable talent in healing magic, as he was able to cure all his neighbours' illnesses with magical aid.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: The wizard was a very skilled <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">potioneer</a>, as he was able to brew healing potions to cure his neighbours' illnesses.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: The wizard had also the power to enchant <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> objects as he did with his little cauldron.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<p>He had a difficult relationship with his son as they had two different views of how things are.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The son thought that those who could not do magic were worthless, a thought shared by wizard fanatics some years later when Muggle persecution of wizards and witches began.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When his father only left him an old cooking pot and a slipper, he cursed his father's age-softened mind as he had expected gold.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, after he was given the taste of the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>' misery, his conscience awoke and he agreed to use his magic to help his Muggle neighbours as his father did before him.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The wizard enjoyed helping his Muggle neighbours with their dreadful woes.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, he did not confide them with the source of all his magic and instead told the that all his potions, charms, and antidotes sprang ready-made from a little cauldron.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
