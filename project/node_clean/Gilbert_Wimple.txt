<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Wimple?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Wimple?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gilbert Wimple was born well before <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, possibly in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. He eventually received an education in <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and worked for the <a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Experimental_Charms" title="Committee on Experimental Charms">Committee on Experimental Charms</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. Presumably as a result of working on the Committee, he acquired horns. Wimple attended the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a>. <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> pointed him out to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Wimple?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Wimple?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Gilbert" means "bright pledge", derived from the Germanic elements gisil "pledge, hostage" and beraht "bright". The Normans introduced this name to England, where it was common during the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>. It was borne by a 12th-century British saint, the founder of the religious order known as the Gilbertines.
</p><p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whimple" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Whimple">Whimple</a> is a village in Devon, near Exeter, where <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> went to university. This could be a link to Wimple, as there are other references to places in the area in the books.
</p>
