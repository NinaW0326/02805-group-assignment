<b>William Friend</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> man who worked for the auctioneering firm <a href="/wiki/J._%26_W._Friend" title="J. &amp; W. Friend">J. &amp; W. Friend</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Friend was arrested in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> on <a href="/wiki/18_November" title="18 November">18 November</a> <a href="/wiki/1916" title="1916">1916</a> for <a href="/wiki/Morphine" title="Morphine">morphine</a> possession.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The case went to the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Special_Sessions" title="Court of Special Sessions">Court of Special Sessions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Friend was arraigned in the <a class="new" title="Centre Street Police Court (page does not exist)" rel="nofollow" data-uncrawlable-url="L3dpa2kvQ2VudHJlX1N0cmVldF9Qb2xpY2VfQ291cnQ/YWN0aW9uPWVkaXQmcmVkbGluaz0x">Centre Street Police Court</a> with alleged accomplice <a href="/wiki/John_Haas" title="John Haas">John Haas</a> for the crime of defrauding the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Bank" title="Columbia Bank">Columbia Bank</a> of $121,000.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Haas worked for the bank as a bookkeeper prior to his <a href="/wiki/2_December" title="2 December">2 December</a> dismissal.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mayer" title="Mayer">Mayer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brown" title="Brown">Brown</a>, the detectives in charge of the case, stated they had never seen such a "slovenly, almost unbelievably simple" system of forging accounting records to hide fraud as that used by Haas and Friend.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Friend was released on $50,000 bail a few hours after his arrest.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He pleaded in court on the night of <a href="/wiki/5_December" title="5 December">5 December</a> that he had nothing to do with the theft of the money from Columbia Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Another man, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Friend" title="Joseph Friend">Joseph H. Friend</a>, was also sought by police in connection with the case.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-paper_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-paper-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The case was reported in the 6 December 1926 issue of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Chronicle" title="New York Chronicle">New York Chronicle</a></i>.
</p>
