<p><b>Madam&#160;Mafalda Hopkirk</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/19_February" title="19 February">19 February</a> - <a href="/wiki/20_March" title="20 March">20 March</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-sun_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sun-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in or before <a href="/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and an assistant in the <a href="/wiki/Improper_Use_of_Magic_Office" title="Improper Use of Magic Office">Improper Use of Magic Office</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. She issued notices to many of those who violated the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Decree_for_the_Reasonable_Restriction_of_Underage_Sorcery" title="Decree for the Reasonable Restriction of Underage Sorcery">Decree for the Reasonable Restriction of Underage Sorcery</a>. This included <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> on two occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-CS2_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OotP2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OotP2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mafalda was still working in the Ministry on <a href="/wiki/2_September" title="2 September">2 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>. She was incapacitated and impersonated by <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, by way of <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Trio" title="Trio">trio</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Infiltration_of_the_Ministry_of_Magic" title="Infiltration of the Ministry of Magic">Infiltration of the Ministry of Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH12_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH12-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>By <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, she was working in the <a href="/wiki/Improper_Use_of_Magic_Office" title="Improper Use of Magic Office">Improper Use of Magic Office</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>. When <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> the <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> used a <a href="/wiki/Hover_Charm" title="Hover Charm">Hover Charm</a>, it was Mafalda Hopkirk who sent a notice to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> regarding "his" illegal use of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> at <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a> in the presence of <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS2_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was still working in the <a href="/wiki/Improper_Use_of_Magic_Office" title="Improper Use of Magic Office">Improper Use of Magic Office</a>, when on <a href="/wiki/2_August" title="2 August">2 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, the Ministry received intelligence via <a href="/wiki/Trace" title="Trace">The Trace</a> that Harry Potter had once again used magic.<sup id="cite_ref-OotP2_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OotP2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Under the orders of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>, she sent a notice to Harry expelling him from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> for his use of the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a> in front of his cousin <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley</a>, a Muggle. Later that same evening, when cooler heads had prevailed, she sent Harry a notice withdrawing his expulsion and ordering him to attend a <a href="/wiki/Disciplinary_hearing_of_Harry_Potter" title="Disciplinary hearing of Harry Potter">hearing</a> ten days later.<sup id="cite_ref-OotP2_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OotP2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/1_August" title="1 August">1 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, having been infiltrated by the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, was <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Ministry_of_Magic" title="Fall of the Ministry of Magic">taken over</a> in a virtually silent coup d'etat. Hopkirk continued to work for the new regime, still as an assistant in the <a href="/wiki/Improper_Use_of_Magic_Office" title="Improper Use of Magic Office">Improper Use of Magic Office</a>. However, it was acceptable for her to be set aside for record-keeping.<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> A month later, when Hopkirk <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparated</a> to work, she was ambushed by <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> and subsequently <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">stunned</a> and locked away.<sup id="cite_ref-DH12_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH12-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Some of her hair was pulled to use in <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a>. Hermione took the potion and assumed the appearance of Hopkirk in order to <a href="/wiki/Infiltration_of_the_Ministry_of_Magic" title="Infiltration of the Ministry of Magic">gain entry</a> to the Ministry. The impostor Hopkirk was used as a stenographer for the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a> and helped free the innocent <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mafalda was described as being a "wispy" <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> with flyaway grey hair. She was also small in stature and wore spectacles.<sup id="cite_ref-DH12_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH12-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Given the courtesy shown in her warning notices,<sup id="cite_ref-CS2_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OotP2_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OotP2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and that Hopkirk's <a href="/wiki/Polyjuice_Potion" title="Polyjuice Potion">Polyjuice Potion</a> was described as having a "pleasant heliotrope colour",<sup id="cite_ref-DH12_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH12-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> it is presumed that she was kind and good-natured witch.
</p><p>However, she was assumed to be sent by <a href="/wiki/Travers" title="Travers">Travers</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> with pro-<a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacy</a> ideals, to keep record for the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Given her personality as shown on her Polyjuice Potion's colour, it is unlikely that she was a pure-blood supremacist herself. Given that Hermione Granger disguised as her became deeply frightened of Dolores Umbridge's and Yaxley's trial on Muggle-borns, and both of them didn't suspect anything given her reaction,<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> it can be presumed that she was forced against her own free will in aiding the Ministry of Magic under Voldemort.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a></b>: Like most accomplished adult wizards and witches, Mafalda was capable of apparating at will. She used apparition to travel to work at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH12_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH12-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Mafalda was capable of conjuring a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> a highly advanced piece of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and a mark of superior magical skill.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Mafalda" is the Portuguese form of Matilda. It means "strength in battle" or "mighty sword", from the Germanic <i>macht</i> (might; strength) and <i>hild</i> (battle; sword). This was the name of the wife of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_I_of_England" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William I of England">William the Conqueror</a>. In Argentina, "Mafalda" is better known as the name of a comic strip character created by famed cartoonist Quino.
</p>

<ul><li>Hopkirk was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Hynes" title="Jessica Hynes">Jessica Hynes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>. She was recast for <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, instead portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Sophie_Thompson" title="Sophie Thompson">Sophie Thompson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In a <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot_letter_to_Harry_Potter" title="Wizengamot letter to Harry Potter">prop letter</a> made for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i> (that went unused in the final film), Mafalda Hopkirk is referred to as the "Commander-in-Chief" of the <a href="/wiki/Improper_Use_of_Magic_Office" title="Improper Use of Magic Office">Improper Use of Magic Office</a><sup id="cite_ref-LE_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LE-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> — whereas <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i> gives her position as "<i>an assistant in the Improper Use of Magic Office</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-DH12_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH12-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li>It is possible, however, that Hopkirk was indeed head of the office in 1995, but that following the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Ministry_of_Magic" title="Fall of the Ministry of Magic">Fall of the Ministry of Magic</a>, the Death Eaters recognised her as an effective employee, so rather than taking her life, <a href="/wiki/Corban_Yaxley" title="Corban Yaxley">Corban Yaxley</a> used his newfound power invested in him to demote her in favour of putting <a href="/wiki/Travers" title="Travers">Travers</a>, a fellow Death Eater, in charge of the division upon his appointment to the office of Head of Magical Law Enforcement.</li></ul></li>
<li>In the book Mafalda's hair was grey, but in the film it was brown and greying.</li></ul>
