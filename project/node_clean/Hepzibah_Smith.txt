<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>Can you keep a secret, Tom? Will you promise you won't tell Mr Burke I've got it? He'd never let me rest if he knew I'd shown it to you, and I'm not selling, not to Burke, not to anyone!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Hepzibah Smith to Tom Riddle on Helga Hufflepuff's Cup<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Madam Hepzibah Smith</b> (died c. <a href="/wiki/1955" title="1955">1955</a>-<a href="/wiki/1961" title="1961">1961</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who claimed to be descended from <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff" title="Helga Hufflepuff">Helga Hufflepuff</a>. Being very rich, Hepzibah was an avid collector of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> antiquities, including objects that belonged to the founders of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>; her two most prized possessions were <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s cup">Helga Hufflepuff's cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s locket">Salazar Slytherin's locket</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Not much is known of Hepzibah's early life, but as an older woman, she befriended the young wizard <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> during one of her visits to <a href="/wiki/Borgin_and_Burkes" title="Borgin and Burkes">Borgin and Burkes</a>. After Hepzibah revealed her two greatest treasures during one of Tom's visits to her home, Tom killed her and used her death to make the cup into a <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>. Because her <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> <a href="/wiki/Hokey" title="Hokey">Hokey</a>, was very old and forgetful, no one doubted that she did the crime. She claimed to have accidentally put <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poison</a> into her mistress' evening cocoa when in reality Riddle had planted a false memory in her mind.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before Hokey died, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> realised the mistake and obtained a true memory from her, but it only showed that Riddle had enquired about Hepzibah's artefacts and was not enough to prove Hokey was innocent before she died.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Very little is known of Hepzibah's early life. Hepzibah claimed to be descended from <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff" title="Helga Hufflepuff">Helga Hufflepuff</a>, one of the founders of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. She was a very old and wealthy <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who collected magical antiques. Two of her most prized possessions were a <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">cup</a> that belonged to her (claimed) ancestor, Helga Hufflepuff, and a <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">locket</a> that belonged to <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>, the latter of which she bought at great expense from <a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke" title="Caractacus Burke">Caractacus Burke</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Meeting_Tom_Riddle_and_death">Meeting Tom Riddle and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Meeting Tom Riddle and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Meeting Tom Riddle and death"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 236px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/80/Riddle_Hepzibah.png/revision/latest?cb=20141222162612" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/80/Riddle_Hepzibah.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/234?cb=20141222162612" decoding="async" width="234" height="151" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Riddle Hepzibah.png" data-image-key="Riddle_Hepzibah.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Riddle_Hepzibah.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Hepzibah shows Slytherin's Locket to Tom Riddle. Hokey stands with the box containing Hufflepuff's Cup</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>While working at <a href="/wiki/Borgin_and_Burkes" title="Borgin and Burkes">Borgin and Burkes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> befriended Hepzibah, who doted upon him to the extent of having an apparent attraction to him. While Riddle was visiting her on behalf of Mr Burke, ostensibly to negotiate the sale of a suit of <a href="/wiki/Goblin-made_armour" class="mw-redirect" title="Goblin-made armour">goblin-made armour</a> in her possession, she imprudently showed him two of her greatest treasures: Hufflepuff's cup and Slytherin's locket.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When she took the locket from Tom's hands, she caught a glimpse of the greedy red gleam in his eyes, which caused her "foolish smile" and obsessive admiration of the boy to momentarily falter.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two days later, she was found dead; a lethal and <a href="/wiki/Little_known_poison" title="Little known poison">little known poison</a> was found mixed with her cocoa. Riddle was never suspected; Hepzibah's <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hokey" title="Hokey">Hokey</a>, confessed to having mistaken the poison for sugar, for which her fate is not revealed.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-mortem">Post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> tracked Hokey down several years later and extracted a memory from her that cast doubt on her guilt. The cup and the locket had since gone missing;<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> they subsequently became two of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort's</a> <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>, with Albus Dumbledore believing that Hepzibah's death was the murder that transformed the cup. This would be another step in his rise to power, and pursuit of immortality.
</p><p>Hepzibah's murder was ultimately avenged when Riddle was killed in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>...An immensely fat old lady wearing an elaborate ginger wig and a brilliant pink set of robes that flowed all around her, giving her the look of a melting iced cake. She was looking into a small jewelled mirror and dabbing rouge onto her already scarlet cheeks with a large powder puff, while the tiniest and oldest <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a> Harry had ever seen laced her fleshy feet into tight satin slippers</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description of Hepzibah Smith<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Dumbledore and Harry viewed Hokey's memory of Hepzibah's meeting with Tom Riddle where she revealed the cup and locket. In the <a href="/wiki/Pensieve" title="Pensieve">Pensieve</a>, Harry's assessment of Hepzibah was that she was an enormously fat, wealthy woman with an ornate ginger wig. She was wearing a set of lustrous, flashy pink robes that flowed all around her and made her look like a melting iced cake. She wore satin slippers that were too small for her pudgy feet, laced by her house-elf Hokey. Hepzibah appeared to be quite vain and excessive, as when Harry saw her she was looking at herself in a jewel-encrusted mirror while putting rouge on her cheeks. She was very interested in valuable objects and antiques, as demonstrated by her possession of Slytherin's locket and Hufflepuff's cup. Hepzibah Smith appeared to go to great lengths to appear attractive and much younger than she really was, and to try and impress Riddle during his visits to her home.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hearing Hokey praise her mistress's appearance as "lovely", <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> concluded that Hokey's contract said that she must "lie through her teeth" when asked this question, as she was a long way from lovely in Harry's opinion. She was described to have a "foolish smile" in <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>'s presence, which faltered when she caught a momentary glimpse of his true nature.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hepzibah was a hoarder of antiques, as her house was so full of objects that Harry wondered how she could move around. She was also very proud, to the point that she did not tell anybody where she had hidden her antiques. However, she was at times unintelligent, as she foolishly showed off her two most valuable treasures to Tom Riddle, not realising that he was someone who would harm her to take them for himself. Highly infatuated with Riddle's faÃ§ade of a polite and handsome young man, Hepzibah was completely oblivious to the dark, greedy look on his face, until the last moment she saw him, at which even she could not ignore the red gleam in his eyes. She was also very conceited and believed that she was a beautiful woman, something <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> found that she was far from, and despite giving the act of being pleasantly surprised that Tom brought her flowers, she already prepared an empty flower pot beforehand, as though expecting the flowers. Due to her naÃ¯vetÃ© and conceit, Dumbledore described her as a "poor and besotted old woman".<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite her vanity and conceit, Hepzibah was not a cruel or unkind woman and though she could be sharp with her house-elf servant, she did not appear to mistreat her in any way. This was somewhat unusual as house-elves were generally treated with disdain or indifference if not outright cruelty by most wizards.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tom_Riddle">Tom Riddle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Tom Riddle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tom Riddle"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/79/Tom_Marvolo_Riddle_%28HBP_promo%29_1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20161107195034" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/79/Tom_Marvolo_Riddle_%28HBP_promo%29_1.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20161107195034" decoding="async" width="180" height="248" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Tom Marvolo Riddle (HBP promo) 1.jpg" data-image-key="Tom_Marvolo_Riddle_(HBP_promo)_1.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Tom_Marvolo_Riddle_(HBP_promo)_1.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>, her murderer</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Hepzibah first met Tom Riddle when the latter worked at <a href="/wiki/Borgin_and_Burkes" title="Borgin and Burkes">Borgin and Burkes</a>. Tom was a very handsome and clever young man. At first, he appeared to be friendly with Hepzibah, flattering her and making her feel pretty. However, it was obvious that he was kind to her solely for the sake of business. When Hepzibah showed two treasures â€” <a href="/wiki/Slytherin%27s_Locket" title="Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Slytherin's Locket</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" title="Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Hufflepuff's Cup</a> â€” to Tom, she saw a red and greedy gleam in his eyes, as he saw such invaluable items being worthy of being his <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">horcruxes</a>; she initially thought it was just the light and felt genuinely afraid of him when she glimpsed his true nature, but by that point her fate had already been sealed. Two days later, Tom Riddle killed Hepzibah in order to take the two heirlooms by spiking her cocoa with a little-known poison. He had no remorse for killing her, and even used her death to transform the cup into his <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hokey">Hokey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hokey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Hokey"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 122px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d0/Hokey.png/revision/latest?cb=20141219192100" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d0/Hokey.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/120?cb=20141219192100" decoding="async" width="120" height="196" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Hokey.png" data-image-key="Hokey.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Hokey.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Hokey" title="Hokey">Hokey</a>, her loyal <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">House-elf</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Hokey was Hepzibah's house-elf, who was tiny and very elderly. Hokey is likely to have performed most tasks typical of a house-elf. Harry Potter observed that one of her duties was to flatter her mistress; he suggested that she must have been lying "through her teeth" when she called Hepzibah "lovely". Their relationship was one typical between a witch and her servant. After Hepzibah's death, Hokey claimed responsibility, and as she was "old and confused", the Ministry labelled Hepzibah's death an accident. It was through a memory of Hokey's that Harry Potter observed the events which took place between Hepzibah and Tom Riddle, secured by Dumbledore before Hokey passed away.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_2-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Hepzibah" is a variant of Heftsi-Vah or Heftsi-Bah, a Hebrew female name meaning "my delight is in her".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Heftsi-Vah was the wife of Hezekiah and the mother of Manasseh, kings of Judah, according to 2 Kings 21:1.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This name may allude to the nature of Hepzibah Smith, regarding her interest in ancient valuable artefact collections. It can also allude to the will of Lord Voldemort in killing her to split his soul and steal Hufflepuff's cup and Slytherin's locket.
</p>
