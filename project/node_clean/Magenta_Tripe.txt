<b>Magenta Black</b> (née <b>Tripe</b>) (fl. <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> married to the pure-blood wizard, <a href="/wiki/Licorus_Black" title="Licorus Black">Licorus Black</a>, who was the earliest known member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>. Her name appears on the <a href="/wiki/Black_Family_Tapestry" title="Black Family Tapestry">Black family tree tapestry</a> that hangs in the ancestral home of <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>. It is possible that she was <a href="/wiki/Misapinoa_Black" title="Misapinoa Black">Misapinoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_I" title="Cygnus Black I">Cygnus I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arcturus_Black_I" title="Arcturus Black I">Arcturus I</a>'s mother.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Magenta_Tripe?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Magenta_Tripe?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magenta" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Magenta">Magenta</a> is a bright pinkish purple colour. (Scientifically, it is a precisely equal mixture of red and blue; it is one of the three subtractive primary colours (CMY/CMYK colour spaces) and one of the three pure additive secondary colours (RGB colour space).) <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tripe" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Tripe">Tripe</a> (from French <i>tripe</i>) is a type of edible offal from the stomachs of various farm animals. It is also used in modern English as a fairly polite synonym for nonsense.
</p>
