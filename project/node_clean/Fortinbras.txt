<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fortinbras?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fortinbras?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fortinbras was a former <a href="/wiki/Charms_Master" title="Charms Master">Charms Master</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, who taught sometime vaguely between the <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">15th century</a> and <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Sir Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</a> knew her, and she sometimes talked about the <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vaults</a> while he was listening. Sir Nicholas told <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angelica_Cole" title="Angelica Cole">Angelica Cole</a> about her in <a href="/wiki/1987" title="1987">1987</a>. At some point in her teaching career, she came to believe (correctly) that the fabled <a href="/wiki/Cursed_Vaults" title="Cursed Vaults">Cursed Vaults</a> were real and wrote her fears and suspicions about them in a <a href="/wiki/Folio_of_Fortinbras" title="Folio of Fortinbras">folio</a> that she left behind in <a href="/wiki/Charms_Classroom" title="Charms Classroom">her old classroom</a>. At the time, teachers and students alike formed a 'circle of trust' that worked together in researching the vaults, with the <a href="/wiki/Potions_Master" title="Potions Master">Potions Master</a> of the day, Professor <a href="/wiki/Bartholomew" title="Bartholomew">Bartholomew</a>, being her closest confidant. Her loyalty to him was such that she even sought to conceal his identity so that her colleague would not be implicated in the conspiracy.<sup id="cite_ref-Y3SQ_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Y3SQ-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fortinbras?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fortinbras?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fortinbras" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Fortinbras">Fortinbras</a> is the name of a fictional character in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s tragedy <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamlet" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>; Fortinbras is a <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> crown prince who, at the end of the play, is to be crowned ruler of <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> of nearly all the major characters.</li></ul>
