<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>Caractacus Burke was not famed for his generosity.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> describing Burke's greedy personality<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Caractacus Burke</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1846" class="mw-redirect" title="1846">1846</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, who was one of the original founders of <a href="/wiki/Borgin_and_Burkes" title="Borgin and Burkes">Borgin and Burkes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-shop_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Caractacus was one of the people <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> interviewed to find out more about <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort's</a> past. Dumbledore learned from Caractacus' memory that <a href="/wiki/Merope_Riddle" title="Merope Riddle">Merope Gaunt</a> came to him when she was destitute, and sold him <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a> for ten <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_currency" title="Wizarding currency">Galleons</a>. Dumbledore apparently had a low opinion of him, while not directly calling Burke a greedy man, he strongly implied it by saying that it had been the kind of reputation Burke had earned.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP13_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP13-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> had graduated from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, Burke hired him to research and acquire items for him. Riddle had done a very good job at this, and Burke was very surprised when Tom Riddle disappeared out of the blue one day without tending any notice to him.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>What ultimately happened to Burke is unknown, but by <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, the shop seemed to be exclusively operated by Mr <a href="/wiki/Borgin" title="Borgin">Borgin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS4_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS4-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Burke was born in or before 1846.<sup id="cite_ref-DOB_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOB-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_shop-owner">As shop-owner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: As shop-owner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As shop-owner"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>Yes, we acquired it in curious circumstances. It was brought in by a young witch just before Christmas, oh, many years ago now. She said she needed the gold badly, well, that much was obvious. Covered in rags and pretty far along ... going to have a baby, see. She said <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s locket">the locket</a> had been Slytherin's. Well, we hear that sort of story all the time, 'Oh this was <a href="/wiki/Merlin" title="Merlin">Merlin</a>'s, this was, his favourite teapot,' but when I looked at it, it had his mark all right, and a few simple spells were enough to tell me the truth. Of course, that made it near enough priceless. She didn't seem to have any idea how much it was worth. Happy to get ten <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">Galleons</a> for it. Best bargain we ever made!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Caractacus Burke on his encounter with <a href="/wiki/Merope_Riddle" title="Merope Riddle">Merope Gaunt</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1863" class="mw-redirect" title="1863">1863</a>, Burke founded the shop <a href="/wiki/Borgin_and_Burkes" title="Borgin and Burkes">Borgin and Burkes</a> with his partner <a href="/wiki/Borgin" title="Borgin">Borgin</a>. The shop is located in <a href="/wiki/Knockturn_Alley" title="Knockturn Alley">Knockturn Alley</a>, an infamous street specialising in shops that sell <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> items When Burke ran the shop he was known for taking advantage of people and driving hard bargains. This was demonstrated when, in <a href="/wiki/December" title="December">December</a>, <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, he was visited by <a href="/wiki/Merope_Riddle" title="Merope Riddle">Merope Gaunt</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> descendant of <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>. Merope was destitute, and heavily pregnant with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>. She wished to sell an <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">artefact</a>, the locket that had belonged to her famous ancestor. Burke was hesitant to accept her claims, having heard many such tales, but a few spells were able to confirm that she was telling the truth. He took advantage of her ignorance and desperation and paid her much less than its true value, about 10 <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">Galleons</a> worth. Burke later sold the locket to <a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith" title="Hepzibah Smith">Hepzibah Smith</a>, though he was desperate to get it back due to the historical value.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP13_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP13-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1945" title="1945">1945</a>, Burke hired <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>, the son of the woman who sold Slytherin's locket to him (a fact Burke never knew). Burke tasked the young Riddle to persuade people to sell their valuable artefacts to the shop, a feat Tom was very skilled in. One such task was to persuade Hepzibah to sell a <a href="/wiki/Hepzibah_Smith%27s_goblin-made_armour" title="Hepzibah Smith&#39;s goblin-made armour">goblin-made armour</a>, which Burke had to make an improved offer at 500 galleons. Shortly after this, Hepzibah died from an alleged accidental poisoning and during the investigations Riddle disappeared without so much as a resignation notice, much to Caractacus' astonishment.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It's unknown what happened to Burke after Tom left, though by the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a> <a href="/wiki/Borgin" title="Borgin">Borgin</a> appeared to run the shop alone.<sup id="cite_ref-COS4_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS4-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It's also unknown if he recognised Lord Voldemort as his former employee or if he was still alive to see him wage war. Though Dumbledore's speaking about Burke in past tense in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, is an indication that he was deceased by then.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP13_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP13-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Caractacus_Burke?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caratacus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Caratacus">Caractacus</a> was a British hero who fought the Roman invaders. Burke is reportedly named after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burke_and_Hare_murders" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Burke and Hare murders">Irishman William Burke</a>, a bizarre serial killer who, with help from a partner, murdered dozens of people in Edinburgh in the late 1820s, selling the bodies as fresh cadavers to a local medical school. 'Burk' or 'berk' is also a British term for an annoying and stupid person, with an original meaning in Cockney Rhyming Slang which is far stronger. Caractacus was also the first name of the character played by Dick Van Dyke in the film <i>Chitty Chitty Bang Bang</i>.
</p>
