<p><b>Sir Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</b>, known after his <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> as <b>Nearly Headless Nick</b>, (d. <a href="/wiki/31_October" title="31 October">31 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1492" class="mw-redirect" title="1492">1492</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a>. He was a wizard of the royal court as an adult. He was executed by an improper decapitation, after he had a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> mishap with <a href="/wiki/Lady_Grieve" title="Lady Grieve">Lady Grieve</a>, in which he tried to <a href="/wiki/Teeth-straightening_spell" title="Teeth-straightening spell">straighten her teeth</a> but it backfired and she grew tusks. Since then, he became the resident <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.
</p>

<p>Little is known about Sir Nicholas's childhood. He lived in the <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">fifteenth century</a> and was born into the noble Mimsy-Porpington family<sup id="cite_ref-OP11_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP11-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> somewhere in the British Isles.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When he turned eleven, Nicholas attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, where he was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-house_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-house-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown at which subjects he excelled in, but he was apparently poor in <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-background_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-background-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his adulthood, Sir Nicholas openly associated with <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>. He was knighted and was a courtier at <a href="/wiki/Henry_VII" title="Henry VII">King Henry VII</a>'s royal court.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Sir Nicholas met Lady Grieve while strolling in the park on the evening of <a href="/wiki/30_October" title="30 October">30 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1492" class="mw-redirect" title="1492">1492</a>. He was certain he could straighten her crooked teeth, but his attempt to do so backfired, causing her to grow a tusk.<sup id="cite_ref-background_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-background-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sir Nicholas was immediately taken into custody and <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">sentenced to death</a> as a result of this mistake. Before being locked in a dungeon, his <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> was taken from him, thus preventing him from using <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> to escape.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Throughout the night he cried that he would soon undo the damage done to <a href="/wiki/Lady_Grieve" title="Lady Grieve">Lady Grieve</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-background_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-background-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

He became a "gibbering wreck" when the executioner told him to kneel before the block. However, the executioner's <a href="/wiki/Axe" title="Axe">axe</a> was blunt, as the grindstone used to sharpen it had been misplaced. Thus, it took forty-five hacks to kill Sir Nicholas and even then his head was only partially severed. Naturally, these acts of betrayal and idiocy alone were not enough to condemn Sir Nicholas to such a brutal death, but the rampant acts of previous juvenile encounters led to an unsympathetic public.<sup id="cite_ref-background_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-background-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span id="1985–1986_school_year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1985.E2.80.931986_school_year">1985–1986 school year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1985–1986 school year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1985–1986 school year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Sir Nicholas held a <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington%27s_four-hundred_and_ninety-third_Deathday_Party" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington&#39;s four-hundred and ninety-third Deathday Party">deathday party</a> on October 31st, 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-HM2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was preparing for the party when <a href="/wiki/Erling" class="mw-redirect" title="Erling">Erling</a> arrived at Hogwarts. Since they shared the same deathday, <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Fat_Friar" title="Fat Friar">Fat Friar</a> suggested the two celebrate together, but it got rejected by Erling.<sup id="cite_ref-HM2_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This collision gradually escalated to a strife in which Sir Nicholas attempted to sabotage Erling's party by preserving the food which was intended to be spoiled. This act, planned and committed by Sir Nicholas, Jacob's sibling and <a href="/wiki/Merula_Snyde" title="Merula Snyde">Merula Snyde</a>, was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Professor Sprout</a>, who later ordered Sir Nicholas to give his food to Erling and help with <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a> classes. However, upon seeing Erling's pompous attitude, he decided to carry on throwing his party despite the lack of food.<sup id="cite_ref-HM2_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Halfway through his party in the <a href="/wiki/Deathday_Ballroom" title="Deathday Ballroom">Deathday Ballroom</a>, the Fat Friar brought him to see Erling in the <a href="/wiki/Courtyard" title="Courtyard">courtyard</a>. Seeing as neither of their parties were going well, with Sir Nicholas' no food and Erling's requirements not fulfilled, the Fat Friar suggested once again that the two hold a party together. Sir Nicholas eventually agreed and cleared out space in the ballroom to allow Erling and his guests to join the party.<sup id="cite_ref-HM2_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Nicholas</i> is from the Greek Nikolaos, "victory of the people." Nick is a jokey, ironic nickname and pun -- one definition of nick is "to cut slightly," and he received more than just a little cut when he was beheaded. <i>Sir</i> indicates he was knighted by a British monarch when he was alive. <i>Porpington</i> is a name Rowling made up and is closest to porpentine, an obsolete name for a porcupine. "Like quills upon the fretful porpentine...": From Shakespeare's <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>. Nick can be a prickly-tempered person at times. <i>Mimsy</i> may have come from the same word coined by Alice in Wonderland author Lewis Carroll. It appears in his nonsense poem "The Jabberwocky": "All mimsy were the borogoves..."

<ul><li>In the film adaptations of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, Nearly Headless Nick is played by <a href="/wiki/John_Cleese" title="John Cleese">John Cleese</a>.</li>
<li>In the Welsh translation, Nicholas is called "Syr Goronwy ap Gwallter", which literally translated as "Sir Goronwy son of Gwallter", commonly Anglicised as "Walter".</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Nick says he had not eaten in nearly 400 years; however, he had, at the time, already been dead for nearly 500 years. In later editions this was changed to "in nearly five hundred years".</li>
<li>It should be noted that Sir Nicholas's clothes do not match the time period he's said to be from. The ruff, doublet and breeches did not come into fashion until the sixteenth century and are mostly associated with the reign of Elizabeth I. This would be more consistent with earlier versions of <i>Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</i> in which Sir Nicholas claims to have been dead for four hundred years.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Dating_conventions" title="Dating conventions">Harry Potter time-line</a> is originally based upon Nick's death-day party in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>. With him having died in <a href="/wiki/1492" class="mw-redirect" title="1492">1492</a> and celebrating his 500th death day, this ostensibly places the novels in the early to mid 1990s. The timeline was later reinforced in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Potter</a>'s graves.</li>
<li>In the first draft of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, Nick sang a <a href="/wiki/Nearly_Headless_Nick%27s_song" class="mw-redirect" title="Nearly Headless Nick&#39;s song">self-penned ballad</a> explaining how his head had (nearly) come off. There was a <a href="/wiki/Priest_who_comforted_Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Priest who comforted Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">priest</a> who comforted him.</li>
<li>While most of the Hogwarts students call him "Nearly Headless Nick", he prefers the more formal "Sir Nicholas de Mimsy Porpington" or "Sir Nicholas". <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a>, however, also calls him Nick. The fact that he prefers to be called Sir Nicholas is only mentioned in the first book and film.</li>
<li>Although Nick claims that he has never been guilty of cowardice in his life, he also claims to have broken down sobbing when they told him that he would die.</li>
<li>He also admits to not being as brave as Sirius Black; Nick chose to become a ghost because he feared death, but Sirius would have "gone on" in Nick's own words.</li>
<li>It is unknown as to how he recovered from petrification by the <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Basilisk</a>, since he is unable to drink <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a>.</li>
<li>Professor Dumbledore explains in his notes on "<a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_and_the_Hopping_Pot" title="The Wizard and the Hopping Pot">The Wizard and the Hopping Pot</a>" that Muggle executions of wizards such as Sir Nicholas are rarely successful, given that wizards can magically protect themselves and escape. Sir Nicholas had his wand taken away, however, thus was unable to leave his dungeon cell.</li>
<li>In the PS1 versions of the first two video games he is seen wearing a high hat and his head constantly falls off to the side.</li>
<li>In the German translation of the second game (PS1 version), Nicholas states that it is his 150th deathday. Which would incorrectly place his death in <a href="/wiki/1842" class="mw-redirect" title="1842">1842</a>.</li>
<li>Sir Nicholas was the first ghost named in the series.</li>
<li>Sir Nicholas is the only House Ghost who did not live during the founders' time.</li>
<li>The manner of Sir Nicholas's death may be a reference to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Ketch" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Jack Ketch">Jack Ketch</a>, an executioner in the service of King Charles II, who was infamous for taking multiple attempts to perform his executions.</li></ul>
