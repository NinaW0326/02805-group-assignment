<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wampus_cat?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wampus_cat?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Somewhat resembling the mundane mountain <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lion</a> or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/cougar" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:cougar">cougar</a> in size and appearance, the Wampus Cat is native to the Appalachian Mountains. It can walk on its hind legs, outrun arrows, and its yellow eyes are reputed to have the power of both <a href="/wiki/Hypnosis" title="Hypnosis">hypnosis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Legilimency" title="Legilimency">Legilimency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FB2017_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB2017-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Wampus cat is fast, strong, and almost impossible to kill.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Cherokee" title="Cherokee">Cherokee</a> have most extensively studied the Wampus Cat, with whom they share their native region, and only they have ever succeeded in procuring Wampus Cat hair for use as a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> core.<sup id="cite_ref-FB2017_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB2017-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Wampus_cat?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Wampus_cat?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatright"><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/1/14/Wampus_Cat_PM.JPG/revision/latest?cb=20170314224730" class="image" title="A Wampus Cat living in the wilderness"><img alt="A Wampus Cat living in the wilderness" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/1/14/Wampus_Cat_PM.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20170314224730" decoding="async" width="150" height="225" data-image-name="Wampus Cat PM.JPG" data-image-key="Wampus_Cat_PM.JPG" /></a></div>
<p>Isolt and the <a href="/wiki/Pukwudgie" title="Pukwudgie">Pukwudgie</a> <a href="/wiki/William_(disambiguation)" title="William (disambiguation)">William</a> took trips to observe these creatures and watched newborn Wampus kittens playing. One of the four houses of Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, founded in the 17th century, was named after the Wampus cat.<sup id="cite_ref-PM-ilvermorny_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-ilvermorny-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This name of the house was decided by <a href="/wiki/Webster_Boot" title="Webster Boot">Webster Boot</a>. <a href="/wiki/Isolt_Sayre" title="Isolt Sayre">Isolt Sayre</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Steward" title="James Steward">James Steward</a> also used the hair for some of their wands.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1832" class="mw-redirect" title="1832">1832</a>, wizard <a href="/wiki/Abel_Treetops" title="Abel Treetops">Abel Treetops</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a> claimed to have patented a method of taming Wampus Cats for use as guards over wizarding houses. Treetops was exposed as a fraud when <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a> raided his home and found him putting <a href="/wiki/Engorgement_Charm" title="Engorgement Charm">Engorgement Charms</a> on <a href="/wiki/Kneazle" title="Kneazle">Kneazles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FB2017_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB2017-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The famous <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmaker</a> <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Jonker" title="Johannes Jonker">Johannes Jonker</a> used the <a href="/wiki/Wampus_cat_hair" title="Wampus cat hair">hair</a> of this species as a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">core</a> in the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
