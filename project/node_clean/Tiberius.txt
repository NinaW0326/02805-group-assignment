<b>Tiberius</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1970" title="1970">1970</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and the uncle of <a href="/wiki/Cormac_McLaggen" title="Cormac McLaggen">Cormac McLaggen</a>. Tiberius worked at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, and according to <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> was highly influential there. He was close with the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a>, and would often go hunting <a href="/wiki/Nogtail" title="Nogtail">Nogtails</a> with him and <a href="/wiki/Bertie_Higgs" title="Bertie Higgs">Bertie Higgs</a>. When he attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, he was a member of <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP7_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tiberius?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tiberius?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Tiberius" is a Roman praenomen, or given name, meaning "of the Tiber" in Latin. The Tiber is the river that runs through Rome. Tiberius was the second Roman emperor, the stepson of emperor Augustus. He was a paranoid and sadistic emperor who ordered mass arrests whilst spending most of his time in his palace on the Isle of Capri, engaging in all manners of sexual immorality.
</p>
