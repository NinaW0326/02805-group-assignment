<p><b>Babayaga</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Hag" title="Hag">hag</a> who regularly ate <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> children for breakfast, and presumably lunch and tea as well. She lived in <a href="/wiki/Babayaga%27s_hut" title="Babayaga&#39;s hut">a hut that stood on giant chicken legs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Babayaga?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Babayaga?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name Babayaga is presumably based on the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> folkloric legend of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baba_Yaga" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Baba Yaga">Baba Yaga</a>.
<ul><li>In the <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> myth of Baba Yaga, she lures children in to her house - which stands on giant <a href="/wiki/Chicken" title="Chicken">chicken</a> legs - and feeds on them. When they least expect it, she then throws them in the <a href="/wiki/Oven" title="Oven">oven</a> to cook and eat them.</li></ul></li>
<li>She appears on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.</li></ul>
