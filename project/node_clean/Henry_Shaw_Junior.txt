<p><b>Senator Henry Shaw Junior</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a>, <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a><sup id="cite_ref-deathdate_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deathdate-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/No-Maj" class="mw-redirect" title="No-Maj">No-Maj</a> who served as a United States <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">senator</a> from <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SnitchSeeker_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SnitchSeeker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was the son of newspaper magnate <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Senior" title="Henry Shaw Senior">Henry Shaw Senior</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-description_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He had at least one younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Langdon_Shaw" title="Langdon Shaw">Langdon Shaw</a>.
</p><p>Henry Shaw Jr died on 6 December, 1926 after being attacked by <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">Obscurus</a>. This was the result of a demeaning comment he made in regards to Credence. He was killed at a <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior%27s_fundraising_dinner" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry Shaw Junior&#39;s fundraising dinner">fundraising dinner</a> for his supporters at <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Hall" title="New York City Hall">New York City Hall</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Henry Shaw Junior was born in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a> in or before <a href="/wiki/1890" class="mw-redirect" title="1890">1890</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-age_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-age-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Senior" title="Henry Shaw Senior">father</a> was a newspaper magnate, and he had at least one sibling: a younger brother named <a href="/wiki/Langdon_Shaw" title="Langdon Shaw">Langdon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-description_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Shaw was elected to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> in <a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-electiondate_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electiondate-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As such, he was his father's pride and joy, while his brother was seen as a disappointment.<sup id="cite_ref-description_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, his younger brother Langdon Shaw brought <a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone" title="Mary Lou Barebone">Mary Lou Barebone</a> from the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">New Salem Philanthropic Society</a> and her adopted children <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chastity_Barebone" title="Chastity Barebone">Chastity</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Modesty_Barebone" title="Modesty Barebone">Modesty</a> to his father Henry Shaw Senior's office in <a href="/wiki/Shaw_Tower" title="Shaw Tower">Shaw Tower</a>.
</p><p>Langdon laid out a portfolio filled with a series of photographic evidence of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">witchcraft</a> and told his father of his belief that "strange things were going on all over <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>" and that this was the work of witchcraft, certain that he was giving him the scoop of the century. Henry and his father both dismissed Langdon's claims as out of hand. Mary Lou was interested in garnishing the support and influence of his <a href="/wiki/Shaw_News" title="Shaw News">newspaper company</a> and his elder son's re-election platform. Henry Shaw Senior refused and asked the <a href="/wiki/Barebone_family" title="Barebone family">Barebone family</a> to leave. While they were departing, Mary Lou's adopted son Credence dropped a leaflet which Henry Shaw Jr handed back to him, unashamedly calling his family freaks.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">

Something huge and bestial, although invisible, is soaring down the hall — tables fly, people are thrown, lights smash, and people scream as it carves a line toward the stage. <p>While running for re-election, Henry Shaw Jr held a <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior%27s_fundraising_dinner" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry Shaw Junior&#39;s fundraising dinner">fundraising dinner</a> for his supporters at <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Hall" title="New York City Hall">New York City Hall</a>, which was also picketed by members of the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">New Salem Philanthropic Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-description_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fbmsite1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fbmsite1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fbmsite2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fbmsite2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bleedingcool_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bleedingcool-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> When Henry was in the middle of his speech, he was interrupted by a strange, haunting noise coming from the organ pipes at the end of the room, and as he recommenced, the strange noise got louder, prompting the guests to look around for the source of the disruption and worriedly start muttering to each other.
</p><p>Suddenly, the chandeliers shattered and the lights went out, and a huge and bestial rush of wind burst forth from beneath the organ. The <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">Obscurus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a> soared across the hall, sending tables flying and throwing people around as it carved a line toward the stage where Shaw was still standing. The force threw Shaw backwards against his poster and then raised him up high where he was suspended for a moment midair before being thrown violently back down onto the stage, dead.
</p><p>After the attack, a magical image of Henry Shaw Jr's body was displayed in the <a href="/wiki/Pentagram_Office" title="Pentagram Office">Pentagram Office</a> of <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a> during a meeting of the delegation from the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards" title="International Confederation of Wizards">International Confederation of Wizards</a>. <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newton Scamander</a> observed the body and realised that he was killed by an <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">Obscurus</a> from the marks covering his face.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them: The Original Screenplay">"<i>Here you go, freak — why don’t you put that in the trash, where you all belong.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Henry Shaw Jr cruelly insults Credence Barebone<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them:_The_Original_Screenplay" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them: The Original Screenplay">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Henry Shaw Jr was a charismatic and successful politician. He was often arrogant, and could also be quite cruel to those he considered "freaks," as shown by his treatment of the <a href="/wiki/Barebone_family" title="Barebone family">Barebone family</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">Second Salemers</a> as a whole. Henry was also quick to dismiss other people's beliefs, as shown when he ignored <a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone" title="Mary Lou Barebone">Mary Lou Barebone</a>'s claims of witchcraft in New York City. Moreover, he appeared to enjoy his success in politics, as Henry was running for re-election, much to his father's pride. Henry appeared to be fairly conservative and authoritarian in his political beliefs, as during his re-election campaign, he expressed support for the maintenance of Prohibition laws.&#160;
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Henry Shaw Jr is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Josh_Cowdery" title="Josh Cowdery">Josh Cowdery</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(film)" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-SnitchSeeker_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SnitchSeeker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the real world, the Class 3 (and, for that reason, up for re-election) Senator from New York in 1926 was <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Wolcott_Wadsworth_Jr" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:James Wolcott Wadsworth Jr">James Wolcott Wadsworth, Jr</a> of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a>, who had been in office since 1915 but was defeated for re-election by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_F._Wagner" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Robert F. Wagner">Robert F. Wagner</a>. Unlike his fictional counterpart, Wadsworth Jr was vocally against <a href="/wiki/Prohibition" title="Prohibition">Prohibition</a>, having even voted against the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eighteenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Eighteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution">Eighteenth Amendment</a> when it was before the Senate in 1919.</li>
<li>It is unclear if Shaw was a Democrat or Republican (his attitude and several hints given in the film suggests the latter).</li></ul>
