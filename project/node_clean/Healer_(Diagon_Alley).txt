<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Healer_(Diagon_Alley)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Healer_(Diagon_Alley)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An altruistic individual, she offered her services free of charge to all injured persons in Diagon Alley, seeing it as her duty. When not attending to customers, the Healer appeared to have a passion for <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>; she kept what appeared to be a <a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">crystal ball</a> on her desk, and consulted what were possibly <a href="/wiki/Tarot_cards" title="Tarot cards">tarot cards</a> in her free time.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Healer_(Diagon_Alley)?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Healer_(Diagon_Alley)?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Interestingly, both the Healer and her shop are correctly have "<a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a>" in their names, which was a term that wasn't introduced to the series until the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i> on 21 June, 2003, even though the game was released on 15 November,&#160;2002. The only term for a healing wizard introduced to the series would've been "<a href="/wiki/Mediwizard" title="Mediwizard">Mediwizard</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>. This is likely just a lucky coincidence that happens to line up with the lore.</li></ul>
