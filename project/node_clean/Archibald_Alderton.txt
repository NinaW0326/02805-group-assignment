<b>Archibald Alderton</b> (<a href="/wiki/1568" class="mw-redirect" title="1568">1568</a>–<a href="/wiki/1623" class="mw-redirect" title="1623">1623</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was known for blowing up the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Little_Dropping" title="Little Dropping">Little Dropping</a> while attempting to <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magically</a> mix a birthday <a href="/wiki/Cake" title="Cake">cake</a>. Archibald was immortalised on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FWC_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FWC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Archibald_Alderton?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Archibald_Alderton?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Archibald" is derived from the Germanic elements ercan "genuine" and bald "bold". The first element was altered due to the influence of Greek names beginning with the element αρχος (archos) meaning "master". This name was brought to England by the Normans. It first became common in Scotland in the Middle Ages.
</p>
