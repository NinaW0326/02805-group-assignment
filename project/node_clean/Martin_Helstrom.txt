<b>Martin "Tiny" Helstrom</b> was Nordic <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who played as <a href="/wiki/Keeper" title="Keeper">Keeper</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_National_Quidditch_team" title="Nordic National Quidditch team">Nordic National Quidditch team</a> during the four-hundred and twenty-second <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">1996–1997 school year</a>, Helstrom's <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> was able to be won by beating <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ronald Weasley</a> at a <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duelling</a> challenge.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Martin_Helstrom?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Martin_Helstrom?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name "Martin" comes from the Roman name Martinus, which was derived from Martis, the genitive case of the name of the Roman god MARS. Saint Martin of Tours was a 4th-century bishop who is the patron saint of France. According to legend, he came across a cold beggar in the middle of winter so he ripped his cloak in two and gave half of it to the beggar. He was a favourite saint during the Middle Ages, and his name has become common throughout the Christian world.</li>
<li>Helstrøm is a Danish surname, possibly indicating that Martin is Danish. It is also a surname of the Norwegian chef Eyvind Hellstrøm.</li></ul>
