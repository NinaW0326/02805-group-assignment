<p><b>Wizard</b><sup id="cite_ref-PS10_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><b> Baruffio</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> with a speech impediment.<sup id="cite_ref-PS10french_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10french-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On account of his condition, he did not seem to have been able to perform particularly satisfactory magic, as he would mispronounce the <a href="/wiki/Incantation" title="Incantation">incantations</a>: once he said 's' instead of 'f' and conjured a <a href="/wiki/Buffalo" title="Buffalo">buffalo</a> on top of him,<sup id="cite_ref-PS10_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
Baruffio presumably lent his name to <a href="/wiki/Baruffio%27s_Brain_Elixir" title="Baruffio&#39;s Brain Elixir">Baruffio's Brain Elixir</a>, a brain stimulant.<sup id="cite_ref-OP31_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP31-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Baruffio?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Baruffio?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Despite the fact that Flitwick mentions Baruffio's incident while teaching Harry and the other first year students <i><a href="/wiki/Levitation_Charm" title="Levitation Charm">Wingardium Leviosa</a></i>, this could not have been the spell to which he was referring as there is no "f" in the Levitation Charm's incantation.</li>
<li>However, <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">he</a> was probably mentioning a line in the introduction of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Standard_Book_of_Spells,_Grade_1" title="The Standard Book of Spells, Grade 1">The Standard Book of Spells, Grade 1</a></i> which mentions Baruffio.</li>
<li>On the French translation of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i> Baruffio's incident with a buffalo is slightly different. It introduces another character: Baruffio's wife. Quoting from the French translation: "<i>N'oubliez jamais le sorcier Baruffio qui avait un défaut de prononciation et dont la femme s'est retrouvée avec un bison sur les épaules au lieu d'un vison</i>". (Loosely translated: "<i>Remember wizard Baruffio who had a speech impediment and whose wife was left with a buffalo on her shoulders instead of a mink</i>".). The translator, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_M%C3%A9nard" title="Jean-François Ménard">Jean-François Ménard</a>, plays with the alliteration "<i>bison</i>"/"<i>vison</i>" ("buffalo"/"mink"). As it is usually women who wear furs, the introduction of a female character was necessary.</li></ul>
