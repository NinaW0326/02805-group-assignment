<b>Minister Maximilian Crowdy</b> was <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> from <a href="/wiki/1770" class="mw-redirect" title="1770">1770</a> to <a href="/wiki/1781" class="mw-redirect" title="1781">1781</a>, elected by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">Wizarding</a> population at least twice.  A father of nine, Crowdy was a charismatic leader who routed out several <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">extremist pure-blood</a> groups planning <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> attacks. His mysterious <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> in office has been the subject of numerous books and conspiracy theories.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Maximilian Crowdy was the sitting Minister for Magic in <a href="/wiki/1777" class="mw-redirect" title="1777">1777</a>, when <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> wizards argued for and against interventionism in the <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_War" title="Revolutionary War">Revolutionary War</a>. When <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_McGilliguddy" title="Elizabeth McGilliguddy">President Elizabeth McGilliguddy</a> sought the advice of the Ministry of Magic to find out whether they intended to intervene on their side, Minister Crowdy replied with only a four-word message — "<i>Sitting this one out</i>". McGilliguddy replied back with an even shorter message — "<i>Mind you do</i>". Neither <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a> or the Ministry of Magic intervened in the war.<sup id="cite_ref-PottermoreHistory3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PottermoreHistory3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
