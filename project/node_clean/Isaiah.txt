<p><b>Isaiah</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a> prophet who lived in the <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">8th century B.C.</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-oxford_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxford-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He gave his name to one of the books of the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>, which was believed to contain his writings, as one well those of at least one later prophet.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-oxford_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oxford-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A quote from the Book of Isaiah ("the eyes of the blind shall be opened") was included on the cover of a <a href="/wiki/Witches_Live_Among_Us!" title="Witches Live Among Us!">pamphlet</a> published by the anti-<a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">witchcraft</a> group the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">New Salem Philanthropic Society</a> in <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
