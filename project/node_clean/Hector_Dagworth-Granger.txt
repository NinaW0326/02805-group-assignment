<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Dagworth-Granger?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Dagworth-Granger?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was known to understand that no such <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">elixir</a> has been created to control and create <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> (as <a href="/wiki/Love_potion" title="Love potion">love potions</a> only induce powerful infatuations). Among many other leading potioneers, Hector Dagworth-Granger doubted that it was possible to create such a truly unbreakable and eternal affection.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> asked <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> if she was related to Dagworth-Granger during their first <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">potions</a> lesson in her sixth year. She replied that she did not believe so, since she was <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP9_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, Hermione's closest magical ancestors were probably <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squibs</a> and would have lost all knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> from their family. Hence, Hermione could be related to him.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Dagworth-Granger?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Dagworth-Granger?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: Dagworth-Granger was a highly accomplished and knowledgeable <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">potioneer</a>, and was the founder of the <a href="/wiki/Most_Extraordinary_Society_of_Potioneers" title="Most Extraordinary Society of Potioneers">Most Extraordinary Society of Potioneers</a>, showing his expertise in the field.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP9_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In particular, he had considerable knowledge on the nature of <a href="/wiki/Love_potion" title="Love potion">love potions</a> and their effects.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBE_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Dagworth-Granger?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hector_Dagworth-Granger?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name "Hector" is the Latinized form of Greek ‘Εκτωρ (<i>Hektor</i>), which was derived from ‘εκτωρ (<i>hektor</i>) "holding fast", ultimately from εχω (<i>echo</i>) meaning "to hold, to possess". In Greek legend Hector was one of the Trojan champions who fought against the Greeks and was the brother of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_(mythology)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Paris (mythology)">Prince Paris</a>. After he killed Achilles' friend Patroclus in battle, he was himself brutally slain by Achilles, who proceeded to tie his dead body to a chariot and drag it about. Paris then in turn slew Achilles with a poisoned arrow that he shot at Achilles' only vulnerable point, his ankle.</li>
<li>The name "Hector" also appears Arthurian legends belonging to King Arthur's foster father. Hector has occasionally been used as a given name since the Middle Ages, probably because of the noble character of the classical hero. It was historically common in Scotland, where it was used as an Anglicised form of Eachann.</li></ul>
