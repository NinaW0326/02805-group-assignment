<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Not much is known about Gunhilda de Gorsemoor's early life. She was born in <a href="/wiki/1556" class="mw-redirect" title="1556">1556</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and might have attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in her youth, from c. 1567- 1574.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_a_Healer">As a Healer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: As a Healer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As a Healer"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 177px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8a/Gunhilda_of_Gorsemoor_USA_Card.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20091111235903" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8a/Gunhilda_of_Gorsemoor_USA_Card.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/175?cb=20091111235903" decoding="async" width="175" height="207" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Gunhilda of Gorsemoor USA Card.jpg" data-image-key="Gunhilda_of_Gorsemoor_USA_Card.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Gunhilda_of_Gorsemoor_USA_Card.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Gunhilda, treating a sick <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>At some point in her life, Gunhilda became a <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a>. She was seen as a talented and hard-working <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a> by everyone who knew her. A gifted <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">potioneer</a>, Gunhilda became very famous for her development of the cure for <a href="/wiki/Dragon_pox" class="mw-redirect" title="Dragon pox">dragon pox</a>, a disease that in worst cases can and did kill many wizards, including <a href="/wiki/Chauncey_Oldridge" title="Chauncey Oldridge">Chauncey Oldridge</a><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and, more recently, <a href="/wiki/Abraxas_Malfoy" title="Abraxas Malfoy">Abraxas Malfoy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP9_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Although effective, Gunhilda's cure has not eradicated the disease.<sup id="cite_ref-A._Malfoy_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-A._Malfoy-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_legacy">Death and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death and legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After her death in <a href="/wiki/1639" class="mw-redirect" title="1639">1639</a>, everyone who knew her felt great sorrow. The great Healer might have died but she was not forgotten; her cure for <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Pox" title="Dragon Pox">Dragon Pox</a> continued saving lives many centuries after her death. For this, Gunhilda was featured in a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A statue of Gunhilda can be found in the <a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_of_Gorsemoor_Corridor" title="Gunhilda of Gorsemoor Corridor">Gunhilda of Gorsemoor Corridor</a> on the third-floor at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>. When one taps the statue's hump with a wand and casts the opening <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charm</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dissendium" title="Dissendium">Dissendium</a></i>, a secret passage is opened, leading to the basement of <a href="/wiki/Honeydukes" title="Honeydukes">Honeydukes Sweetshop</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade Village</a>, a passage which Harry Potter used.<sup id="cite_ref-POA10_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA10-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Healing_magic" title="Healing magic">Healing magic</a></b>: Gunhilda was a highly accomplished and gifted <a href="/wiki/Healer" title="Healer">Healer</a>, meaning she was highly knowledgeable and proficient with various <a href="/wiki/Healing_spell" title="Healing spell">Healing spells</a>, healing <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a> and other magical remedies. She famously found the <a href="/wiki/Dragon_pox_cure" title="Dragon pox cure">Dragon pox cure</a>, showing her expertise in the art of medical magic.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: Gunhilda was also a highly talented <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">Potioneer</a>, as she created the <a href="/wiki/Dragon_pox_cure" title="Dragon pox cure">Dragon pox cure</a>, a potent healing potion which works as the cure for the often lethal disease <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Pox" title="Dragon Pox">Dragon Pox</a>, a remarkable achievement in both the fields of potion-making and healing.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Gunhilda_de_Gorsemoor?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Gunhilda" is from the Old Norse name <i>Gunnhildr</i>, derived from the elements <i>gunnr</i> "war" and <i>hildr</i> "battle".
</p>
