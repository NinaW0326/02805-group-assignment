<b>Professor Eupraxia Mole</b> was <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmistress</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1870s" title="1870s">1870s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Eupraxia_Mole?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Eupraxia_Mole?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1876" class="mw-redirect" title="1876">1876</a>, caretaker <a href="/wiki/Rancorous_Carpe" title="Rancorous Carpe">Rancorous Carpe</a> devised an "<a href="/wiki/Rancorous_Carpe%27s_trap" title="Rancorous Carpe&#39;s trap">elaborate trap</a>" in an effort to remove <a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a>, the resident <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a>, from the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Castle</a>, which failed. The Castle had to be evacuated for three days as Peeves amused himself by firing weapons randomly and threatening the students with death.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Professor Mole agreed to sign a <a href="/wiki/Peeves%27_contract" title="Peeves&#39; contract">contract</a> with Peeves, so that he relinquished the weapons in exchange for additional privileges: a once-weekly swim in the boys' toilets on the ground floor, first refusal on stale bread from the kitchen for throwing purposes, as well as a new custom-made hat by <a href="/wiki/Bonhabille" title="Bonhabille">Madame Bonhabille</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
