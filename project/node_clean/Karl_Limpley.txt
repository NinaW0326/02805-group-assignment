<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Karl_Limpley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Karl_Limpley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, he agreed to trade <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> an issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Which_Broomstick%3F" title="Which Broomstick?">Which Broomstick?</a></i> for three <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Cards</a>, as Harry needed a magazine to give to <a href="/wiki/Jason_Huntingdon" title="Jason Huntingdon">Jason Huntingdon</a> for a pair of <a href="/wiki/Spectrespecs" title="Spectrespecs">Spectrespecs</a>. Karl said he thought Jason might be <i>"making some kind of fashion statement"</i> by wearing Spectespecs, and that he would never wear the glasses himself, even though they're free. Later in the year, he ran a <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Clock_Tower_Courtyard" title="Clock Tower Courtyard">Clock Tower Courtyard</a>, which included <a href="/wiki/Irfan_Mustaq" title="Irfan Mustaq">Irfan Mustaq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neil_Randall" title="Neil Randall">Neil Randall</a> as members.<sup id="cite_ref-HBPG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBPG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Karl_Limpley?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Karl_Limpley?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Karl" is the German and Scandinavian form of Charles. This was the name of seven emperors of <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. Other famous bearers include Karl Marx (1818-1883), the German philosopher and revolutionary who laid the foundations for communism, and Karl Jaspers (1883-1969), a German existentialist philosopher.
</p>
