<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifetime">Lifetime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Wilkins?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Lifetime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Wilkins?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lifetime"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-mortem">Post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Wilkins?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Wilkins?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After her <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>, sometime before 1943, her <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> was hung in the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's office</a>, in order to give advice to the current Head.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She also had portraits hung at the Grand Staircase.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Wilkins?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Wilkins?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliotropium" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Heliotropium">Heliotrope</a> is a term used to describe a plant, which turns to face the sun, derived from the Greek <i>helios</i>, "sun", and <i>tropein</i>, "to turn."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_of_flowers" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Language of flowers">Language of flowers</a>, heliotropes represent devotion.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Miss Heliotrope is a character in <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Little_White_Horse" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Little White Horse">The Little White Horse</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/1946" title="1946">1946</a> novel by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elizabeth_Goudge" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Elizabeth Goudge">Elizabeth Goudge</a>, which was a childhood favourite of <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wilkins is an English patronymic surname based on Wilkin,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> a nickname of the given names Will and William,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> which both derive from Wilhelm, a Germanic name made up of the elements <i>wil</i>, "will", and <i>helm</i>, "helmet".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This was also the surname <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> chose, when she invented new identities for her parents, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger%27s_father" title="Hermione Granger&#39;s father">Mr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger%27s_mother" title="Hermione Granger&#39;s mother">Mrs Granger</a>, and sent them into hiding for safety's sake, during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>.
</p>
