<b>Sir Herbert Varney</b> (<a href="/wiki/1858" class="mw-redirect" title="1858">1858</a>â€“<a href="/wiki/1889" class="mw-redirect" title="1889">1889</a>) was a short-lived <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a> who preyed upon <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> women in the <a href="/wiki/1880s" title="1880s">1880s</a>. He was eventually caught and killed by a specially-assigned group of <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Regulation_and_Control_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures">Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures</a>. He appears on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Famous Wizard Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Herbert_Varney?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Herbert_Varney?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This character probably references <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varney_the_Vampire" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Varney the Vampire">Varney the Vampire or the Feast of Blood</a></i>, a Gothic horror story written by James Malcolm Rymer in the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>, and one of the first vampire novels ever written (predating and influencing Bram Stoker's more popular Dracula).<sup id="cite_ref-W3_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W3-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Herbert Varney may have also been loosely based on the unidentified serial killer <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_the_Ripper" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Jack the Ripper">Jack the Ripper</a>, who in <a href="/wiki/1888" class="mw-redirect" title="1888">1888</a> brutally murdered at least five women in London, but was never caught or identified.<sup id="cite_ref-w0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-w0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
