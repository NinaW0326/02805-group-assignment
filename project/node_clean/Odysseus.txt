</p><p><b>Odysseus</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Ancient Greek</a>  hero who slew the one-eyed <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giant</a> <a href="/wiki/Cyclops" title="Cyclops">Cyclops</a> with the help of several <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Odysseus?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Odysseus?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Odysseus</i> was probably derived from the Ancient Greek verb <b>odussomai</b>, meaning "hate", suggesting that the name could be rendered as "the one who is wrathful/hated." This interpretation is reinforced by Odysseus's and <a href="https://community.fandom.com/wiki/Poseidon" class="extiw" title="w:Poseidon">Poseidon's</a> mutual wrath in Homer's <i>The Odyssey</i>.
</p>
