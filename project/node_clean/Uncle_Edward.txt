<b>Uncle Edward</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">poltergeist</a> who haunted a <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> family.<sup id="cite_ref-dec6sunrise_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dec6sunrise-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although usually calm in temperament, one day in <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> he had an argument with the family he haunted during a <a href="/wiki/Picnic" title="Picnic">picnic</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">Central Park</a>, and proceeded to throw baked goods at passing <a href="/wiki/Muggles" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggles">No-Majs</a>, disappearing afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-dec6sunrise_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dec6sunrise-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The incident was reported in the <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> 1926 morning edition of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Ghost" title="The New York Ghost">The New York Ghost</a></i> as possibly jeopardising <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">wizarding secrecy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dec6sunrise_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dec6sunrise-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
