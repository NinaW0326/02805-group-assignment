<p><b>Langdon Shaw</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/No-Maj" class="mw-redirect" title="No-Maj">No-Maj</a> who lived in the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>. He was the younger son of newspaper magnate <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Senior" title="Henry Shaw Senior">Henry Shaw Senior</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-featurette_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-featurette-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-description_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He had at least one sibling, an older brother <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior" title="Henry Shaw Junior">Henry Shaw Junior</a> who was elected to the United States Senate in <a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Langdon_Shaw?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Langdon_Shaw?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While his older brother, the senator <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior" title="Henry Shaw Junior">Henry Shaw Junior</a>, was Shaw Snr's pride and joy, Langdon was viewed as something of a failure and disappointment. This did not stop him from constantly trying to seek his father's approval.<sup id="cite_ref-description_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Langdon brought <a href="/wiki/Mary_Lou_Barebone" title="Mary Lou Barebone">Mary Lou Barebone</a> from the <a href="/wiki/New_Salem_Philanthropic_Society" title="New Salem Philanthropic Society">New Salem Philanthropic Society</a> and her adopted children <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chastity_Barebone" title="Chastity Barebone">Chastity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modesty_Barebone" title="Modesty Barebone">Modesty</a> to <a href="/wiki/Shaw_News" title="Shaw News">Shaw News</a> meet with his father. Barging into Shaw Snr's office, Langdon laid out a portfolio filled with a series of photographic evidence of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">witchcraft</a> and told his father of his belief that "strange things [were] going on all over" <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> and that the people behind these incidents were "<a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">not like [them]</a>," certain that he was giving him the scoop of the century.<sup id="cite_ref-description_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His father and brother both dismissed his claims.<sup id="cite_ref-description_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-description-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Henry Shaw Senior refused to give Mary Lou the support of his newspaper company and his elder son's re-election platform, and asked the <a href="/wiki/Barebone_family" title="Barebone family">Barebone family</a> to leave. While they were departing, Mary Lou's adopted son Credence dropped a leaflet, which Henry Shaw Jr handed back to him, while unashamedly and cruelly calling him a freak.<sup id="cite_ref-FBS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>

<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them: The Original Screenplay">"<i>Witches!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Langdon's reaction after when his brother gets killed<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them:_The_Original_Screenplay" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them: The Original Screenplay">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Langdon later attended his brother's <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior%27s_fundraising_dinner" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry Shaw Junior&#39;s fundraising dinner">fundraising dinner</a> with his father. While his brother was in the middle of his speech, an <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">invisible force</a> burst forth from beneath the organ. It soared across the hall, sending tables flying, and throwing people around as it carved a line toward the stage where Shaw Jr was still standing. Langdon watched as the invisible force threw his brother backwards against his poster, and then raised him up high where he was suspended for just a moment in midair before being thrown violently back down onto the stage, dead. In the midst of sounds of anguish and panic from the crowd, his father crouched beside his slain son. Langdon, who was slightly drunk, determinedly and triumphantly stated that this was the work of witchcraft.<sup id="cite_ref-FBS_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>

<dd></dd></dl></div><p>On the next night, <a href="/wiki/7_December" title="7 December">7 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Langdon was present when <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a> unleashed his <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">Obscurus</a> on New York, and <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a> began magically sealing off the subway entrance where Credence had taken refuge. </p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Langdon_Shaw?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Langdon_Shaw?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them: The Original Screenplay">"<i>Shaw Snr and the senator look dubious—all too used to Langdon’s harebrained little projects and interests.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Shaw Senior and the Senator reaction about Langdon<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them:_The_Original_Screenplay" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them: The Original Screenplay">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Langdon Shaw was an excitable young man with a proclivity for wild ideas. He was frustrated by living in his elder brother’s shadow, and constantly tried to seek his father's approval and attention. He was also excited to try to show he had authority or power. Langdon appeared to have a tendency to drink too much.

<p>Langdon's relationship with his father and brother seemed to be quite poor. His father <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Senior" title="Henry Shaw Senior">Henry Shaw Senior</a> saw him as a disappointment and was rather contemptuous of him, constantly dismissing Langdon and even refusing to listen to anything he said, including his belief in witchcraft, all the while favouring and praising his brother <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior" title="Henry Shaw Junior">Henry Shaw Jr</a>.
</p>
