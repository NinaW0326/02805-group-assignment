<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>You know <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s snake, Neville? He's got a huge snake... Calls it Nagini... It's got to be killed.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Harry Potter arranges for Nagini to be slain<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Nagini</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>) was a female <a href="/wiki/Maledictus" title="Maledictus">Maledictus</a> <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">cursed</a> to <a href="/wiki/Transformation" title="Transformation">transform</a> into a <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snake</a>. Originating possibly in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-origin_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-origin-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a> she was a performer at the <a href="/wiki/Circus_Arcanus" title="Circus Arcanus">Circus Arcanus</a> where she met <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a> and had the ability then to transform at will. By <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Nagini was seemingly trapped in snake form and belonged to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, with whom she had a special bond largely due to becoming a <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>, after her master had killed <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">Bertha Jorkins</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>.
</p><p>After Voldemort's initial downfall, he used <a href="/wiki/Nagini%27s_venom" title="Nagini&#39;s venom">Nagini's venom</a> as one of the ingredients for a <a href="/wiki/Rudimentary_body_potion" title="Rudimentary body potion">Potion</a> to regain strength, which eventually led to his <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">rebirth</a> in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. Later in the same year, she attacked <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> but he managed to survive. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, she had to be destroyed for Voldemort to finally be defeated. Nagini was killed by <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor" title="Sword of Gryffindor">Godric Gryffindor's Sword</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> and was the last Horcrux to be destroyed after <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw%27s_diadem" class="mw-redirect" title="Rowena Ravenclaw&#39;s diadem">Rowena Ravenclaw's diadem</a>.
</p>

<p>Originally, Nagini was a female <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> <a href="/wiki/Maledictus" title="Maledictus">Maledictus</a>, with the ability to transform into a <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snake</a>. By <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, she was a featured attraction in a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> <a href="/wiki/Circus_Arcanus" title="Circus Arcanus">circus</a> owned by <a href="/wiki/Skender" title="Skender">Skender</a> (who claimed to have found her in the 'jungles of Indonesia). At some point she befriended <a href="/wiki/Obscurial" title="Obscurial">Obscurial</a> <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a>, who travelled to Europe with the circus after surviving <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA's</a> attempt to destroy him in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>, after he <a href="/wiki/Obscurial_attack_on_New_York" title="Obscurial attack on New York">attacked</a> the city in <a href="/wiki/December" title="December">December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>.
</p>

However, Hermione came to Harry's assistance, using a <a href="/wiki/Blasting_Curse" title="Blasting Curse">Blasting Curse</a> against the snake; the two then jumped out the window and <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Disapparated</a> just before Voldemort arrived.<sup id="cite_ref-DH17_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Later, on <i><a href="/wiki/Potterwatch" title="Potterwatch">Potterwatch</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jordan" title="Lee Jordan">Lee Jordan</a> revealed that the remains of Bathilda's body had been found after being informed by the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH22_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH22-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With Nagini's death, Voldemort's final Horcrux was destroyed, thereby stripping him of his immortality. He furiously tried to kill Neville for it, but was stopped by Harry. Voldemort himself finally died when his Killing Curse rebounded onto himself once again.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an <a href="/wiki/Alternate_reality" title="Alternate reality">alternate reality</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scorpius_Malfoy" title="Scorpius Malfoy">Scorpius Malfoy</a>, by saving <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> which caused him much humiliation during the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, they turned him into a bitter man who would join the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. As a result, Cedric killed Neville during the Battle of Hogwarts, thus preventing him from killing Nagini. This allowed Voldemort to retain his <a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">immortality</a>, and thus kill Harry, which led to the Dark Lord to effectively conquer the world.<sup id="cite_ref-CC_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a> which affected the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding world</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundable</a> memories of Nagini mysteriously appeared in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/a7/Nagini_snake.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20181210173203" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="180" height="101" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Nagini snake.jpg" data-image-key="Nagini_snake.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/a7/Nagini_snake.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20181210173203" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/a7/Nagini_snake.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20181210173203" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/a7/Nagini_snake.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20181210173203" decoding="async" width="180" height="101" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Nagini snake.jpg" data-image-key="Nagini_snake.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/a7/Nagini_snake.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20181210173203" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Nagini_snake.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Nagini the Snake</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Prior to her transformation into a <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>, Nagini was a shy, benevolent, quiet, and kind woman, having been abused as a sideshow freak by the <a href="/wiki/Circus_Arcanus" title="Circus Arcanus">Circus Arcanus</a> for so long, that it had left the <a href="/wiki/Maledictus" title="Maledictus">Maledictus</a> insecure, and longing to escape to something better. She befriended <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a>, likely seeing a fellow outcast in the young <a href="/wiki/Obscurial" title="Obscurial">Obscurial</a>, and also wanting to no longer be alone in her life. Nagini was also principled and moral enough to recognise <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> for what he was, despite the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>'s persuasive charisma and adamantly urged Credence not to join him. Unfortunately, however, Nagini's pleas availed her not, and she was left heartbroken when Credence decided to fall back in with Grindelwald, being too desperate to know his true name and identity. After losing Credence, Nagini seemingly wished to help defeat Grindelwald and end the <a href="/wiki/For_the_Greater_Good_Revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="For the Greater Good Revolution">Global Wizarding War</a>, since she joined forces with <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theseus_Scamander" title="Theseus Scamander">Theseus Scamander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Tina Goldstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Kowalski" title="Jacob Kowalski">Jacob Kowalski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yusuf_Kama" title="Yusuf Kama">Yusuf Kama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Torquil_Travers" title="Torquil Travers">Torquil Travers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rudolph_Spielman" title="Rudolph Spielman">Rudolph Spielman</a>, subsequently going to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> with them all, to meet with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.
</p>

<p>By the time she had become a Horcrux and, it seemed, permanently stuck in Maledictus serpent form, Nagini's original kind and gentle personality had been entirely expunged - she was now completely loyal to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. While she remained either aloof or outright malevolent towards most others, she obeyed Voldemort completely and seemed to enjoy being close to him, as she often slithered up to the Dark Lord's shoulders to receive his affection. Nagini was also shown to be very intelligent, and was able to understand the concept of strategies and plans, as seen when she set up a trap for <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> (which may have been due to her possessing a portion of Voldemort's soul, or maintaining her human sentience). She was rather cunning and deceptive, and understood how humans behaved (likely owing to Nagini having once been human herself). Possessing a strong bond with Voldemort, Nagini alone did not fear his wrathful moments, as he never punished her for failures nor took it out on her when misfortune struck him.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>It seems there was some rather unusual kind of poison in that snake’s fangs that keeps wounds open....</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> regarding the strength of Nagini's venom<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li><b>Maledictus Transformation</b> (formerly): Nagini was originally able to transform into a huge snake at will, identical to the ability of an <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>. This was an ability the <a href="/wiki/Circus_Arcanus" title="Circus Arcanus">Circus Arcanus</a> took extreme advantage of. Ultimately, however, Nagini's <a href="/wiki/Maledictus" title="Maledictus">Maledictus</a> blood curse took over, which forced her to stay in her snake form permanently.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parseltongue</a></b>: Nagini, due to her Maledictus transformation into a snake, also gained the ability of a <a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parselmouth</a> to speak the language of serpents, while remaining in snake form.<sup id="cite_ref-DH17_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Combat skills</b>: Nagini, while in snake form, was extremely deadly in battle, just like her master <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. She was very fast, capable of striking her enemies with great dexterity and speed, and was able to hold her own against wizards and witches on several occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP21_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP21-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Indeed, even when she still had little combat training at the Circus Arcanus, Nagini was able to swiftly incapacitate <a href="/wiki/Skender" title="Skender">Skender</a>, thus successfully escaping from him. Nagini was also quite strong as a snake, being able to hold <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in place by wrapping him up in her thick, strong coils when she attacked him in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a>. If not for <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>'s Blasting Curse, Nagini's physical strength would have allowed her to hold Harry in her coils until Voldemort had arrived.<sup id="cite_ref-DH17_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nagini%27s_venom" title="Nagini&#39;s venom">Venom</a></b>: Nagini was an extremely <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">venomous</a> snake, so a single bite could, therefore, result in a wound that was slow to heal, though the extent to which this was due to her status as a Horcrux was unclear. While her venom was not always fatal, it made it difficult for wounds to close, and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>'s injuries continued to bleed long after Nagini had bitten him; it can even dissolve <a href="/wiki/Stitches" title="Stitches">stitches</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP22_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP22-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> If bitten at a vital location, it can result in fatal blood loss within a short amount of time, as <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> died at a bite to the neck.<sup id="cite_ref-DH36_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH36-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Bond with Voldemort</b>: Being one of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>, Nagini had a strong telepathic bond with her master and was capable of speaking to him over long distances. Voldemort could also possess Nagini at some circumstances. However, since <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> himself was an unintentional Horcrux, Nagini's status as one allowed him to see her perspective in dreams, notably her attack on <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP21_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP21-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<p>As a member of <a href="/wiki/Skender" title="Skender">Skender</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Circus_Arcanus" title="Circus Arcanus">Circus Arcanus</a>, Nagini the Maledictus had been abused by him as a sideshow freak, with this mistreatment lasting so long, that it left the young woman insecure, and longing to escape to something better. Hence, as soon as the opportunity to escape from Skender presented itself, she readily took it - going into snake form to bite Skender in the shoulder, and fleeing, with Credence's help. When Skender was later questioned by Auror <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Tina Goldstein</a> about Nagini and Credence, he sneered at how all of his "freaks" desired to "go home", and didn't seen interested in pursuing them. Due to her prolonged abuse by Skender, Nagini was able to see through <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>'s charming words, and wasn't at all tempted to join the Dark Wizard's forces.
</p>

<p>While at the <a href="/wiki/Circus_Arcanus" title="Circus Arcanus">Circus Arcanus</a>, Nagini met and befriended fellow outcast <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a>, with the two of them being drawn close together due to their similarly tragic pasts, and, wishing to stay together, they hatch a plan of escape. Nagini assisted Credence's quest to find out his true identity, and proceeded to hug and comfort the young <a href="/wiki/Obscurial" title="Obscurial">Obscurial</a> following the death of <a href="/wiki/Irma_Dugard" title="Irma Dugard">Irma Dugard</a>. When Credence was being tempted by Grindelwald, Nagini desperately implored her friend to stay with her but was ultimately unable to stop Credence from joining the Dark Wizard. However, Nagini's devastation over losing Credence induced her to officially join the <a href="/wiki/For_the_Greater_Good_Revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="For the Greater Good Revolution">Global Wizarding War</a> against Grindelwald.
</p>

<p>Nagini seemed fearful of the infamous Dark Wizard upon suddenly meeting him on a rooftop, as she didn't say a word while <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Grindelwald</a> invited Credence to his rally at the <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Mausoleum" title="Lestrange Mausoleum">Lestrange Mausoleum</a>. As such, Nagini greatly desired the downfall of Grindelwald, and officially joined the <a href="/wiki/For_the_Greater_Good_Revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="For the Greater Good Revolution">Global Wizarding War</a> effort against him, with her even visiting Grindelwald's former friend <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> shortly thereafter, together with <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a>.

<p>Voldemort had a special relationship with Nagini, as she was his pet and one of his Horcruxes. According to Dumbledore, Voldemort had strong feelings for Nagini that he had not for anyone else; she was the one living thing that he had ever cared about.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He was able to communicate with Nagini as a <a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parselmouth</a>, and he turned her into a Horcrux in 1994, after he murdered <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">Bertha Jorkins</a>, which accounted for the unusual amount of control that he had over her. By 1997, and likely before then, he was able to communicate with her silently and across great distances, as she was able to signal to him when Harry Potter arrived at Godric's Hollow. This may have been due to her nature as a Horcrux, which gave her a link to Voldemort like the one Harry possessed.<sup id="cite_ref-DH17_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Voldemort was also able to possess Nagini, which he did in December 1995, during the attack on Arthur Weasley. In 1994, Peter Pettigrew milked Nagini's venom to return Voldemort to a rudimentary body, and as a Horcrux, her very existence contributed to maintaining Voldemort's immortality. While Voldemort was known for his independence that makes him unable to feel any form of attachment or reliance to others, the affection he held for Nagini (and that she underlines his heritage to Salazar Slytherin) was the main reason he chose to make her into a container for his soul fragment in the first place; of course, holding a place in keeping her master alive made Nagini only increased her value to Voldemort's eyes. Voldemort usually sent her on missions, such as infiltrating the Ministry or holding Harry Potter in Godric's Hollow. He allowed her to consume the corpses of those he killed as a reward, like that of Charity Burbage, though this also served as a way for him to dispose of bodies. Voldemort apologised to Nagini when he could not feed Pettigrew to her, as Pettigrew's failure of keeping <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Crouch Snr</a> was rectified, and therefore, allowed him to escape the death penalty and not become Nagini's meal. Unlike many of his subordinates, Voldemort did not punish Nagini for failures, as when she failed to retrieve the prophecy or let Harry Potter escape from her grasp. In turn, Nagini did not fear Voldemort's wrathful moments, as when even high-ranking Death Eaters such as Lucius Malfoy and Bellatrix Lestrange fled the area when Voldemort was savagely murdering goblins for reporting his one of his Horcruxes being taken, Nagini remained next to her lord.
</p>

<p>Nagini was aloof and indifferent to the Death Eaters as a whole, but sometimes could be irritated by them, as she hissed angrily at their loud laughter at the Malfoy manor which none of them took notice until Voldemort ordered their silence for her sake.<sup id="cite_ref-DH1_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> She allowed Peter Pettigrew to milk the venom out of her fangs in order for it to be used for their master,<sup id="cite_ref-GOF1_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but otherwise would have been glad to devour any Death Eater that her master permitted, as a form of punishment for their failure; Pettigrew, Dolohov and Rowle were threatened to become Nagini's meal in lieu of torture.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF29_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF29-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DH9_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH9-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Under Voldemort's order, Nagini ruthlessly bit Snape in a fatal location, dealing him a slow, painful demise.<sup id="cite_ref-DH32_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH32-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Because of this, along with that she held more favour from their master than any of them did, many of the Death Eaters were terrified of Nagini for her deadly and ruthless behaviour.
</p>

<p>Nagini was frequently used by Voldemort to kill others or to dispose of their dead bodies. In 1995, he promised Nagini that she could devour Harry Potter after he had killed him.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF29_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF29-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GOF33_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF33-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, she was denied this when Harry escaped from the graveyard shortly after the promise was made.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF34_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF34-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It was likely that <a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce" title="Frank Bryce">Frank Bryce</a> met that intended fate, in August 1994. Voldemort threatened <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> with being fed to Nagini if Bartemius Crouch Snr was able to contact Albus Dumbledore about the plot behind the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. Nagini also attacked <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>, but he survived the bites after Harry had a vision of it, and he was quickly aided.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP21_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP21-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> During the meeting at Malfoy Manor, she consumed the body of <a href="/wiki/Charity_Burbage" title="Charity Burbage">Charity Burbage</a> after Voldemort killed her.<sup id="cite_ref-DH1_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Later, when <a href="/wiki/Antonin_Dolohov" title="Antonin Dolohov">Antonin Dolohov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thorfinn_Rowle" title="Thorfinn Rowle">Thorfinn Rowle</a> did not capture Harry Potter and his friends after the <a href="/wiki/Luchino_Caffe_Duel" title="Luchino Caffe Duel">skirmish</a> at <a href="/wiki/Luchino_Caffe" title="Luchino Caffe">Luchino Caffe</a>, Voldemort offered them a choice in the form of punishment: being tortured by <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus curse</a> or being killed and eaten by Nagini.<sup id="cite_ref-DH9_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH9-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> After the skirmish at Malfoy Manor, Voldemort possibly ordered Nagini to consume the dead corpse of Pettigrew after he was strangled to death by his own enchanted hand. During the Battle of Hogwarts, she attacked and killed <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Shrieking_Shack" title="Shrieking Shack">Shrieking Shack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH32_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH32-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?veaction=edit&amp;section=24" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nagini?action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Naga" is "<a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snake</a>" while "Nagini" is a "female snake" in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> and "Nagin" means "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/N%C4%81ga" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Nāga">female snake</a>" in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindi" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hindi">Hindi</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urdu" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Urdu">Urdu</a>. A reference to Rudyard Kipling's cobra characters Nag and Nagaina<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> from the book, Rikki-Tikki-Tavi is referred to as Nagini in some translations.</li>
<li>A "Nagini" was also a half-woman, half-snake creature that is found in some Indian mythology (possibly a half-snake, half-woman creature called "Lamia").</li></ul>

<ul><li>Nagini was based on Indonesian mythology.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Nagini's <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> form is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Kim" title="Claudia Kim">Claudia Kim</a> and is one of the main cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts:_The_Crimes_of_Grindelwald" title="Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald">Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-pmn_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmn-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li>A common pre-release theory predicted that the <a href="/wiki/Maledictus" title="Maledictus">Maledictus</a> was the Snake Girl advertised on a poster for the Circus Arcanus,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> with her Maledictus form being a snake. It was further hypothesised that the snake she would become was Nagini, which would solve the mystery of Nagini's origins. Her nature is such an important reveal would also explain why promotional material refused to give her name.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> This theory was confirmed when Claudia Kim stated that she was playing Nagini, along with the character being named in the final <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts:_The_Crimes_of_Grindelwald" title="Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald">Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald</a></i> trailer.</li>
<li>According to <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>, she had been sitting on Nagini's secret for around twenty years.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li>Along with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, Minerva McGonagall and <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, Nagini is one of four characters who appear in both the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)"><i>Harry Potter</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_(film_series)" title="Fantastic Beasts (film series)"><i>Fantastic Beasts</i></a> series to date (discounting the mentions of Newt Scamander and the Flamels in the <i>Harry Potter</i> series).</li>
<li>In <i>Goblet of Fire</i> and <i>Order of the Phoenix</i>, Nagini was likely portrayed as a Dumeril's Ground Boa, but in the last two films, she was portrayed as a Reticulated Python (the longest real-life snake species); both instances contradicted the book's description of her as a venomous snake. However, it was possible that she was a fictional species of venomous python within the film continuity. The filmmakers' intention was to model Nagini after a Burmese Python.<sup id="cite_ref-popup_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-popup-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Nagini is the only Horcrux of Voldemort that was a living creature as Harry Potter was a pseudo horcrux that was created by accident.</li>
<li>Nagini seems highly intelligent as a snake during the Second Wizarding War. Whether she retained her human intelligence and sense of self, or was possessed by the portion of Voldemort's soul in the <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a> (which would be a second example of a self-aware Horcrux after <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s diary">Tom Riddle's diary</a>), is unknown, though it's worth noting that mundane <a href="/wiki/Boa_constrictor_at_the_Zoo" title="Boa constrictor at the Zoo">boa</a> whom <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> encountered earlier in his life also demonstrated seemingly human-like intelligence, suggesting that snakes in the <i>Harry Potter</i> universe may simply be sentient as a matter of course, unbeknownst to Muggles.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, Albus Dumbledore believed that Nagini may have been made into a Horcrux through the murder of an "old Muggle man", most likely referring to <a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce" title="Frank Bryce">Frank Bryce</a>. However, J. K. Rowling said in an interview that the murder that Voldemort used to make Nagini into a Horcrux was <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">Bertha Jorkins</a>.</li>
<li>Nagini's history with Voldemort is difficult to piece together. It is unknown whether Voldemort owned Nagini before his fall in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, or if he had found her during his exile in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>. However, it was stated that in the woods of Albania, the phantom-like form that was the remnants of Voldemort had possessed many animals, yet only snakes could bear having him inhabit them, thus leading to the notion that Voldemort and Nagini's relationship had originally commenced in Albania.
<ul><li>It is worth noting that she knew Voldemort when he killed <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">Bertha Jorkins</a> in Albania.</li>
<li>As she had the name of Nagini before she permanently became a snake, it is possible that Voldemort met or acquired her before her permanent transformation, or found out her name through <a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parseltongue</a>.</li></ul></li>
<li>Nagini was killed by Neville using Gryffindor's Sword, which was an object that could destroy Horcruxes because it had been impregnated with <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_venom" title="Basilisk venom">Basilisk venom</a> in <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>. It was unknown if living Horcruxes could be destroyed by anything that cannot destroy an inanimate Horcrux; however, it was likely, because the Horcrux inside Harry was destroyed by Voldemort by using the Killing Curse. Nevertheless, it was still possible that living Horcruxes could not be destroyed by anything that would not destroy an inanimate Horcrux considering that the Killing Curse was a one-of-a-kind unblockable instant death spell. As a living container of a piece of Lord Voldemort's soul, it was unknown what would happen if she could have died naturally.</li>
<li>The killing of Nagini was very representative of the houses Gryffindor and Slytherin. The sword was originally Godric Gryffindor's, and Nagini was a snake, Salazar Slytherin's symbol, who was owned by a descendent of the Slytherin Family Line.</li>
<li>Nagini and the <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Basilisk</a> were both giant snakes who served Voldemort, and both were killed with Gryffindor's Sword when someone pulled it out of the Sorting Hat. Also, both their deaths contributed to Voldemort becoming mortal (as the Basilisk fangs were used to destroy two Horcruxes and the venom absorbed by Godric Gryffindor's Sword allowed the destruction of three more). Also, the Basilisk was related to the first appearance of a Horcrux in the series, while Nagini was the last Horcrux.</li>
<li>Nagini was usually sent on missions on Voldemort's behalf, and made her rather vulnerable, which was against the general purpose of a Horcrux: to protect the portion of the soul. Nagini shared this trait with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s diary">The Diary Horcrux</a>. However, it may be that Voldemort was sure that no one had discovered his Horcrux secrets and, as only a few things could destroy Horcruxes, he was fairly sure that both Nagini and the diary would be safe. However, it could have been that the primary purpose of creating these two items was not so much to provide for Voldemort's immortality, but rather to use the other properties of a Horcrux to create unique weapons. Dumbledore believed this to be the case for the diary.</li>
<li>There was a rumour that Nagini was the snake that Harry had released from the zoo in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>. This was proven false,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> as Nagini was some type of viper while the <a href="/wiki/Boa_constrictor_at_the_Zoo" title="Boa constrictor at the Zoo">Snake at the Zoo</a> was a boa constrictor. Furthermore, the snake was given a male voice in the film, while Nagini was female.</li></ul>

<ul><li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>, Nagini is a normal-sized snake. In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>, she is considerably larger. In real-life terms, this was so she could be more like a formidable boss battle, but in-universe, this could be due to her being a Horcrux.</li>
<li>Nagini's portrayed death was changed in the film just like her master's: in the novel, both of their corpses fell onto the floor when they received their respective deathblows; in the film, both of them disintegrated into nothingness instead. The film also adds the detail of the shard of Voldemort's soul inside Nagini escaping as smoke from the wound before dissolving.</li>
<li>There is no known species of venomous snake matching Nagini's description; therefore, she was most likely a species of snake that was found only in the magical world. It is unknown if this was directly related to her <a href="/wiki/Maledictus" title="Maledictus">Maledictus</a> identity, or if this species also exists in the wild in the Wizarding World.</li>
<li>When Nagini was first revealed to be in <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts:_The_Crimes_of_Grindelwald" title="Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald">Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald</a></i>, the revelation received some backlash as it indicates Nagini, played by a woman of East Asian descent, will eventually become the <i>pet</i> of Voldemort — a likely unintentional, but not altogether-tasteful implication.</li>
<li>According to MuggleNet, it is possible that Nagini's human and snake form will be separated in the sequel film of Fantastic Beasts.</li>
<li>The books have no history of Nagini being a human before.</li></ul>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, Nagini was not protected by the magical orb, but instead by what appeared to be an invisible force field that deflected all spells cast at her. This allowed her to participate in the second stage of the battle, as opposed to dying before it began, where she attempted to strike Harry from the back while Voldemort duelled him from the front. Ultimately, she was fighting against Hermione and Ron, both of whom tried to stab her with a Basilisk fang, before Neville decapitated her. Upon her death, she vanished into black smoke, as opposed to simply dying and falling onto the ground in the novel. Why her body perished was not known, and she was the only Horcrux to disappear in such manner; all the others did not disappear, but instead left behind fragmented pieces, except the Diadem when Ron kicked the remnants into the oncoming <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">Fiendfyre</a>. Also, Nagini was killed while Harry Potter and Lord Voldemort were duelling, when in the book, Neville Longbottom killed Nagini while Voldemort and his Death Eaters were standing outside the <a href="/wiki/Entrance_Hall" title="Entrance Hall">Entrance Hall</a>. Furthermore, Voldemort's and Snape's final meeting occurs in the <a href="/wiki/Boathouse" title="Boathouse">Boathouse</a> in the film, as opposed to the <a href="/wiki/Shrieking_Shack" title="Shrieking Shack">Shrieking Shack</a> in the novel, and thus Nagini murders Snape there instead of on her master's orders.    
</p>
