<b>Charles Demengeot</b> was a <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a> French heraldic engraver and typographer, who published <i><a href="/wiki/Dictionnaire_du_Chiffre-monogramme_dans_les_Styles_Moyen-age_et_Renaissance_et_couronnes_nobilaires_universelles" title="Dictionnaire du Chiffre-monogramme dans les Styles Moyen-age et Renaissance et couronnes nobilaires universelles">Dictionnaire du Chiffre-monogramme dans les Styles Moyen-age et Renaissance et couronnes nobilaires universelles</a></i> <a href="/wiki/1881" class="mw-redirect" title="1881">1881</a>, a work that famously includes a monogram of the entire alphabet.<sup id="cite_ref-CSf_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSf-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, there was an aged poster featuring the frontispiece of his work, posted on the walls on <a href="/wiki/Knockturn_Alley" title="Knockturn Alley">Knockturn Alley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSf_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSf-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charles_Demengeot?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charles_Demengeot?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Demengeot's work seems to have influenced the graphical typefaces in some of the logos and monograms featured in some of the props in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_films" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter films"><i>Harry Potter</i> films</a>. Good examples of this are, for instance, the <a href="/wiki/File:Beauxbatons_Crest.jpg" title="File:Beauxbatons Crest.jpg">Beauxbatons crest</a>, which seems to have been inspired in <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/btv1b86265651/f247.image">this monogram (top)</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/File:Advocates_to_the_Wizarding_World.jpg" title="File:Advocates to the Wizarding World.jpg">logo of Advocates to the Wizarding World</a>, which seems to have been based on <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/btv1b86265651/f291.image">this one (third one from the top, on the right)</a>.</li></ul>
