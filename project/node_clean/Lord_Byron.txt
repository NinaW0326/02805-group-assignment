<b>George Gordon Noel</b> (née <b>Byron</b>), <b>6th Baron Byron</b> (<a href="/wiki/22_January" title="22 January">22 January</a>, <a href="/wiki/1788" class="mw-redirect" title="1788">1788</a> – <a href="/wiki/19_April" title="19 April">19 April</a>, <a href="/wiki/1824" class="mw-redirect" title="1824">1824</a>), commonly known as <b>Lord Byron</b>, was an English poet and a leading figure in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanticism" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Romanticism">Romantic movement</a>. He is regarded as one of the greatest British poets and remains widely read.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> kept volumes of <i><a href="/wiki/Byron%27s_Works" title="Byron&#39;s Works">Byron's Works</a></i>, a collection of poems by Lord Byron, on his desk at the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's office</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MHP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MHP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
