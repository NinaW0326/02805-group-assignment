<b>Vlad the Impaler</b> was the son of Count <a href="/wiki/Vlad_Drakul" title="Vlad Drakul">Vlad Drakul</a>. He was the Prince of Wallachia, which later became <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>. Despite being the son of a known <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vlad_Drakul" title="Vlad Drakul">Vlad Drakul</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> it is unclear whether or not Vlad the Impaler was one as well.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vlad_the_Impaler?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vlad_the_Impaler?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Little is known of vampires in <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s work, but the existence of partial vampires hints that it is possible for a person to be born a vampire without the need of being "turned". Children of humans and vampires would otherwise always be human. This raises the question as to whether Vlad the Impaler is a half-vampire, vampire, or human, but nothing has been confirmed as of yet.</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vlad_III_the_Impaler" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Vlad III the Impaler">Vladislav III</a>, also known as Vlad the Impaler, was an actual historical figure. He was Prince of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallachia" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Wallachia">Wallachia</a> who reigned three times between <a href="/wiki/1448" class="mw-redirect" title="1448">1448</a> and <a href="/wiki/1476" class="mw-redirect" title="1476">1476</a>. His epithet, "Impaler", originated from his favoured method of executing those who opposed him. Vlad III is indeed widely speculated to have been the inspiration behind the title character of <a href="/wiki/Bram_Stoker" title="Bram Stoker">Bram Stoker</a>'s 1897 novel <i>Dracula</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gary_Oldman" title="Gary Oldman">Gary Oldman</a>, who plays <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_films" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter films"><i>Harry Potter</i> films</a>, played Vlad the Impaler in the <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a> film, <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bram_Stoker%27s_Dracula" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Bram Stoker&#39;s Dracula">Bram Stoker's Dracula</a></i>.</li></ul>
