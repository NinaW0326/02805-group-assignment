<b>Sendelina de la Felino</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who played as <a href="/wiki/Keeper" title="Keeper">Keeper</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_National_Quidditch_team" title="Spanish National Quidditch team">Spanish National Quidditch team</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-QWC_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QWC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">1996–1997 school year</a>, Felino's <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> could be won by beating <a href="/wiki/Alannis_Shepley" title="Alannis Shepley">Alannis Shepley</a> at a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> challenge.<sup id="cite_ref-HBPG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBPG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sendelina_de_la_Felino?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sendelina_de_la_Felino?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In Spanish, <i>felino</i> is a male gender word, and <i>la</i> is a female gender article. Technically, the correct writing of Sendelina's surname is <i>del Felino</i>, or just <i>Felino</i>.
<ul><li>However, this surname might refer to Sendelina's ancestors. <i>De la Felino</i> can mean "he/she who belongs to the Felino woman". This surname may have originated as a nickname for the child of a woman surnamed "Felino". Therefore, a son of hers would be called, for example, "José el de la Felino" (José, the son of that Felino woman), and through the generations, the nickname would have evolved to an official surname, "de la Felino".</li></ul></li></ul>
