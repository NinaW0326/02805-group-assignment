<b>Evangeline Orpington</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and a celebrated <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>, in office from <a href="/wiki/1849" class="mw-redirect" title="1849">1849</a> to <a href="/wiki/1855" class="mw-redirect" title="1855">1855</a>. She is remembered for being the one who first thought of creating a <a href="/wiki/Platform_Nine_and_Three-Quarters" title="Platform Nine and Three-Quarters">concealed platform</a> at <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_Station" title="King&#39;s Cross Station">King's Cross Station</a>, to house the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kingscross_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kingscross-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Orpington?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Orpington?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Orpington?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Orpington?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Evangeline Orpington was born no later than <a href="/wiki/1832" class="mw-redirect" title="1832">1832</a>, into a wizarding family.<sup id="cite_ref-blood_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blood-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In her younger years, she purchased <a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Orpington%27s_wand" title="Evangeline Orpington&#39;s wand">her wand</a>, made of <a href="/wiki/Poplar" title="Poplar">poplar</a> wood, from <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders Wand Shop</a> in <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wands_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wands-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, the famous <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmaker</a>, went on to write that the "tired old joke" among lesser wandmakers that no poplar <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> had ever chosen a politician was utterly unsubstantiated, given that two of the most accomplished <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Ministers for Magic</a> had fine, Ollivander-made poplar wands: Evangeline Orpington and <a href="/wiki/Eldritch_Diggory" title="Eldritch Diggory">Eldritch Diggory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wands_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wands-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Orpington?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Orpington?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 267px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/61/Platform_9%C2%BE.png/revision/latest?cb=20131026094512" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/6/61/Platform_9%C2%BE.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/265?cb=20131026094512" decoding="async" width="265" height="156" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Platform 9¾.png" data-image-key="Platform_9¾.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Platform_9%C2%BE.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">The concealed entrance to Platform 9¾</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Ms Orpington was appointed <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> in <a href="/wiki/1849" class="mw-redirect" title="1849">1849</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kingscross_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kingscross-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>. She was elected Minister at least once.
</p><p>When one of her predecessors, <a href="/wiki/Ottaline_Gambol" title="Ottaline Gambol">Ottaline Gambol</a>, authorised an <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic_operation_to_acquire_the_Hogwarts_Express" title="Ministry of Magic operation to acquire the Hogwarts Express">operation to acquire</a> the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a>, the Ministry had seen fit to build a <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade_station" title="Hogsmeade station">railway station</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a>. The Ministry was not so keen, however, on building another station, in plain sight, in <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, as it would be in all likelihood a serious breach of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kingscross_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kingscross-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_Station" title="King&#39;s Cross Station">King's Cross Station</a> was opened in <a href="/wiki/1852" class="mw-redirect" title="1852">1852</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Wikipedia_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wikipedia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> during Orpington's time in office. It was only after its opening that the Minister hit upon the idea of adding a concealed platform at <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_Station" title="King&#39;s Cross Station">King's Cross Station</a> that could only be accessed by <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches and wizards</a>: <a href="/wiki/Platform_Nine_and_Three-Quarters" title="Platform Nine and Three-Quarters">Platform Nine and Three-Quarters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kingscross_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kingscross-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Minister Orpington was also good friends with <a href="/wiki/Victoria" title="Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>, who never realised that she was a witch or Minister for Magic. She was also believed to have illegally intervened <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magically</a> in the Crimean War.
</p><p>Minister Orpington left the office in <a href="/wiki/1855" class="mw-redirect" title="1855">1855</a>, to be regarded as one of the most accomplished Ministers in British history.
</p>
