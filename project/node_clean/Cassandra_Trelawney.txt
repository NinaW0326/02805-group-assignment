<b>Cassandra Trelawney</b> was a famous, celebrated <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a>. She was the great-great-grandmother to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Sybill Trelawney</a>. She possessed the <a href="/wiki/Inner_Eye" title="Inner Eye">Inner Eye</a>, which was passed on into generations later.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP15_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP15-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a></b>: It is known that she was a "genuine <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a>" and that "Cassandra's gift has been much diluted over ensuing generations, although Cassandra's descendant, Sybill, had inherited more than she knew,"<sup id="cite_ref-WW_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> implying that Cassandra was a very powerful Seer.&#160;She was referred to as "Legendary" on more than one occasion. Indeed, when Dumbledore was preparing to scrap the Hogwarts Divination programme, Sybill Trelawney's heritage (being descended from Cassandra) was enough to get her a job interview for the post.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP37_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP37-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Trelawney" may have been Cassandra's married name, since her great-great-granddaughter shares this surname. It is, however, possible that Cassandra was a single mother or simply did not take her husband's name.
<ul><li>This being true, Cassandra Trewlaney may have been <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Vablatsky" title="Cassandra Vablatsky">Cassandra Vablatsky</a> before she married.</li></ul></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Trelawney?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Cassandra</i> is generally taken to mean "she who entangles men". This meaning came from the Greek culture and was translated to mean various other things as well; such as "oracle".
</p><p>In Greek mythology, Cassandra was a princess of Troy. Her beauty caused Apollo to grant her the gift of prophecy. However, when she did not return his love, Apollo placed a curse on her so that no one would ever believe her predictions.
</p>
