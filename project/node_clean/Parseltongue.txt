<p><b>Parseltongue</b> is the language of <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">serpents</a> (as well as other magical serpentine creatures, like the <a href="/wiki/Runespoor" title="Runespoor">Runespoor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basilisk" title="Basilisk">Basilisk</a>) and those who can converse with them. An individual who can speak Parseltongue is known as a <b>Parselmouth</b>. It is a very uncommon skill, and was known to be an almost exclusively hereditary trait.<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Not counting <a href="/wiki/Herpo_the_Foul" title="Herpo the Foul">Herpo the Foul</a>, who predated him by several centuries,<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> nearly all known Parselmouths are descended from <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> being a notable exception (although he gained his ability as a Parselmouth in an unorthodox manner).<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Parseltongue is, when spoken, a hissing sound, similar to that of a snake; as such, normal people cannot understand it<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (one known exception being <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-bloomsburychat_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomsburychat-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Apart from merely communicating with serpentine lifeforms, Parselmouths also seem able to influence the will of serpents to a certain extent.<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS17_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Aside from serpent-based creatures, Parselmouths can communicate with each other with the language, as Harry understood <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>'s commands to the <a href="/wiki/Serpent_of_Slytherin" title="Serpent of Slytherin">Serpent of Slytherin</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Gaunt" title="House of Gaunt">House of Gaunt</a> communicate with each other almost exclusively in Parseltongue.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP10_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP10-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> While inherited, Parseltongue usually requires the speaker to face a snake-based creature or object shaped like a snake (e.g. a carving); more proficient speakers may be able to speak it at will, as the Gaunt family was very proficient in the language <i>—</i> enough so for Morfin to speak to <a href="/wiki/Bob_Ogden" title="Bob Ogden">Bob Ogden</a> with it,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP10_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP10-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> while Voldemort could speak to the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Slytherin" title="Statue of Slytherin">Statue of Slytherin</a> to release the basilisk.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reputation">Reputation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Reputation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reputation"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">"<i>The boy can talk to snakes, Dumbledore, and you still think he's trustworthy?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>'s prejudice against Parselmouths<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The ability to actually speak Parseltongue — not just imitate it as Ron Weasley did — is considered an attribute of a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GOF31_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which is partly due to the fact that both <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> possessed this ability.<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS17_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Another notable Dark Wizard who was also a Parselmouth was <a href="/wiki/Herpo_the_Foul" title="Herpo the Foul">Herpo the Foul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, as Albus Dumbledore pointed out, there are Parselmouths among the good as well, the most well-known of which was <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. <a href="/wiki/Phillipus_von_Hohenheim" title="Phillipus von Hohenheim">Paracelsus</a>, a notable <a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">alchemist</a> and a "medical genius", is credited with the discovery of Parseltongue in the <a href="/wiki/16th_century" title="16th century">sixteenth century</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> despite the existence of speakers like <a href="/wiki/Herpo_the_Foul" title="Herpo the Foul">Herpo the Foul</a> or <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a> in the classical and medieval ages. Because of the uncommonness of Parselmouths, Voldemort came to believe it made him special.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP13_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP13-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An unknown member of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Force_Defence_League" title="Dark Force Defence League">Dark Force Defence League</a> once stated when asked regarding the matter by <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> — "<i>Personally, I would be highly suspicious of anybody who could converse with snakes, as serpents are often used in the worst kinds of Dark Magic, and are historically associated with evildoers</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-GOF31_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF31-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Harry_Potter_as_a_Parselmouth">Harry Potter as a Parselmouth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Harry Potter as a Parselmouth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Harry Potter as a Parselmouth"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">"<i>I spoke a different language? But — I didn’t realise — how can I speak a language without knowing I can speak it?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> after he speaks Parseltongue<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> passed the ability to speak and understand Parseltongue on to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> when he <a href="/wiki/Attack_at_Godric%27s_Hollow_(1981)" title="Attack at Godric&#39;s Hollow (1981)">attacked</a> him in infancy in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, inadvertently and unknowingly making him into a <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH35_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH35-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Harry first experienced conversation with <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a> at age ten when he was taken to the zoo with his cousin <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley Dursley</a> and found himself communicating with a snake while in the reptile exhibit. Harry <a href="/wiki/Underage_magic" title="Underage magic">inadvertently caused</a> the glass of the snake's tank to vanish, enabling it to escape.<sup id="cite_ref-PS2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 239px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c6/Parseltongue_snake.gif/revision/latest?cb=20161121223346" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c6/Parseltongue_snake.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/237?cb=20161121223346" decoding="async" width="237" height="127" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Parseltongue snake.gif" data-image-key="Parseltongue_snake.gif" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Parseltongue_snake.gif" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Harry speaking Parseltongue during the Duelling Club</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Harry was not consciously aware of his ability to speak Parseltongue until <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>; he was pitted against <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a> and was able to communicate with the snake Draco <a href="/wiki/Snake_Summons_Spell" title="Snake Summons Spell">conjured to attack him</a>, telling it not to attack <a href="/wiki/Justin_Finch-Fletchley" title="Justin Finch-Fletchley">Justin Finch-Fletchley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This caused hysteria among his fellow students because <a href="/wiki/Writing_on_the_Wall" title="Writing on the Wall">writing on the walls</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> foretold that the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Heir of Slytherin</a> would open the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>, unleashing a <a href="/wiki/Serpent_of_Slytherin" title="Serpent of Slytherin">monster</a> that would attack the school's <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> students.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Even <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, who was previously enjoying Harry's horror at the snake, became horrified himself when he learned of Harry's ability to communicate with snakes.<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Heir of Slytherin was in fact Voldemort, but Harry was able to gain access to the Chamber by speaking the <a href="/wiki/Password" title="Password">password</a> in Parseltongue, and subsequently killed the basilisk within. It was shortly after this incident that Harry learned how he acquired the ability.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the wizarding world discovered Harry's ability to speak Parseltongue, courtesy of <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>, people began to doubt his words, as <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> refused to believe Harry's claim of Voldemort's rebirth partially due to being prejudiced to Harry's ability to speak the language.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF36_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF36-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry also used his skill in Parseltongue to open <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH19_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH19-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> When Harry, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> returned to Hogwarts to search for <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem" class="mw-redirect" title="Rowena Ravenclaw&#39;s Diadem">Rowena Ravenclaw's Diadem</a>, Ron managed a weak imitation of Parseltongue to re-open the Chamber of Secrets so that he and Hermione could fetch basilisk fangs, which Hermione used to destroy <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Helga Hufflepuff's Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Lord Voldemort destroyed the fragment of his <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a> residing in Harry, Harry seemingly lost the ability to speak Parseltongue, about which he was glad and relieved.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomsburychat_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomsburychat-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry Potter never tried to use again the ability during the next twenty-two years, but in 2020, despite his previous belief that the ability was lost forever to him, Harry had somehow regained the ability to speak and understand Parseltongue again if he truly ever lost it in the first place. While initially reluctant to do so and only after Hermione insisted that he had to try, Harry successfully spoke Parseltongue to open the enchanted room of <a href="/wiki/Delphini" title="Delphini">Delphini</a> while searching for his missing son, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a>. Later, in order to impersonate the Dark Witch's father, Lord Voldemort, Harry again used his ability to speak Parseltongue.<sup id="cite_ref-cursedchild_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cursedchild-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>People who were not Parselmouths but were able to speak or understand Parseltongue through various means:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> was able to speak Parseltongue while she was possessed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_Diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s Diary">Tom Riddle's Diary</a>, which enabled her to open the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS17_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> was also able to open the Chamber of Secrets during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> by repeating a fragment of Parseltongue he had heard Harry Potter use to open <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a>, though it took him several tries to make it work.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> could understand Parseltongue and repeat sentences in it verbatim; he may have been able to speak it in full, although this is unconfirmed.<sup id="cite_ref-bloomsburychat_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloomsburychat-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a> mentioned in his magnum opus, <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i>, that information on <a href="/wiki/Runespoor" title="Runespoor">Runespoors</a>, three-headed snakes from <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, is based on the “writings of Parselmouths who have kept and conversed with these serpents”.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> There are likely to be African Parselmouths completely unrelated to the Gaunts.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot" title="Bathilda Bagshot">Bathilda Bagshot</a>'s carcass spoke in Parseltongue while it was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH17_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH17-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Isolt_Sayre" title="Isolt Sayre">Isolt Sayre</a>, a direct descendant of <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>, was able to understand a <a href="/wiki/Horned_Serpent" title="Horned Serpent">Horned Serpent</a> which she regularly visited but was not able to speak Parseltongue herself.<sup id="cite_ref-PottermoreIlvermorny_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PottermoreIlvermorny-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Parseltongue?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has stated that she took the name Parselmouth from an "old word for someone who has a problem with the mouth, like a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/hare_lip" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:hare lip">hare lip</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> went to <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a> in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> (inside the corpse of <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot" title="Bathilda Bagshot">Bathilda Bagshot</a>) tells Harry to "<i>Come!</i>" from the next room in Parseltongue. In reaction, Hermione jumps and clutches Harry's arm, and the two of them obey the command.<sup id="cite_ref-DH17_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH17-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown how Hermione seemed to understand this command, though it could be that she did not hear it well, and merely followed Harry. She could also have been frightened by the hissing sound she heard and then followed <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)"><i>Philosopher's Stone</i> film</a>, Harry converses with a Burmese python, rather than a Boa Constrictor.</li>
<li>Whenever it was spoken in the films, Parseltongue went untranslated except for one scene in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i> when Voldemort was speaking to Nagini, where it was given subtitles. It was also heard in English in the first film when Harry was talking in the zoo.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">films</a>, Parseltongue is depicted as sounding like a sibilant-filled language with readily discernible phonemes created by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Nolan" title="Francis Nolan">Dr Francis Nolan</a> (as listed in the credits for <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>). In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">novels</a>, it is only ever described as a low hissing noise, easily missed by non-Parselmouths — a far cry from the rather loud versions from the films.</li>
<li>In the last two films, as Harry draws near a Horcrux he can hear, in addition to a whining noise, a faint voice which seems to speak in Parseltongue.</li>
<li>It is possible that Parseltongue comes in variants for different languages, rather than being understood as the language spoken by the Parselmouth. The boa constrictor that Harry Potter converses with at the zoo uses the Portuguese word <i>amigo</i> rather than the English <i>friend</i>.</li></ul>
