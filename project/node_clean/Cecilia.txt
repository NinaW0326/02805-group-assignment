<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cecilia?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cecilia?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At some point&#160;during her youth, Cecilia began a romantic relationship with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle_Senior" title="Tom Riddle Senior">Tom Riddle</a>, the son of wealthy landowners <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Riddle" title="Thomas Riddle">Thomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary_Riddle" title="Mary Riddle">Mary Riddle</a>.
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Bob_Ogden" title="Bob Ogden">Bob Ogden</a>, in the course of his duties as Head of the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Law_Enforcement_Patrol" title="Magical Law Enforcement Patrol">Magical Law Enforcement Squad</a>, visited the <a href="/wiki/Gaunt_Shack" title="Gaunt Shack">Gaunt Shack</a> in <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a> to interview <a href="/wiki/Morfin_Gaunt" title="Morfin Gaunt">Morfin Gaunt</a> about hexing a local <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a>&#160;(her boyfriend), Cecilia and&#160;Tom rode on a grey horse past the house. The two gossiped at the ruined cottage, Cecilia asking Tom to tear down the hovel who explained an <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt" title="Marvolo Gaunt">old tramp</a> was the owner of the cottage and thus he couldn't tear it down. The two then talked about Marvolo's son, Morfin, who was believed to be insane by the town. The two were disgusted and horrified to see Morfin had nailed his <a href="/wiki/Morfin_Gaunt%27s_first_snake" title="Morfin Gaunt&#39;s first snake">snake</a> to the door. Later, when Ogden ran from the house to escape <a href="/wiki/Morfin_Gaunt" title="Morfin Gaunt">Morfin Gaunt</a>'s hexes, he knocked into Tom and Cecilia's horse, making the couple laugh at him for his bumbling and silly clothing.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP10_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Presumably, Cecilia lost contact with the <a href="/wiki/Riddle_family" title="Riddle family">Riddle family</a> after&#160;Tom married <a href="/wiki/Merope_Riddle" title="Merope Riddle">Merope</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cecilia?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cecilia?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Celia seemed to be snobbish and rude, more so than Tom Riddle, as&#160;she was very&#160;impolite and critical when talking about the Gaunt family house. He seemed to have affectionate relationship with her, calling her darling, and comforting her when seeing Morfin's dead snake nailed to the door.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cecilia?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cecilia?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Cecilia" is the Latinate feminine form of the Roman family name Caecilius, which was derived from Latin caecus "blind". Saint Cecilia was a semi-legendary 2nd- or 3rd-century martyr who was sentenced to die because she refused to worship the Roman gods. After attempts to suffocate her failed, she was beheaded. She was later regarded as the patron saint of music and musicians. The name may also be close to "cecaelia", a Greek mythological creature with the upper torso of a human and the bottom torso of an octopus.&#160;
</p>
