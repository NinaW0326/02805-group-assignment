<b>Marius Black</b> was the&#160;third child&#160;of <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_II" title="Cygnus Black II">Cygnus Black II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Violetta_Bulstrode" title="Violetta Bulstrode">Violetta Bulstrode</a>, and brother of <a href="/wiki/Pollux_Black" title="Pollux Black">Pollux Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassiopeia_Black" title="Cassiopeia Black">Cassiopeia Black</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dorea_Black" title="Dorea Black">Dorea Black</a>. Marius was disowned and removed from the <a href="/wiki/Black_Family_Tapestry" title="Black Family Tapestry">Black family tree tapestry</a> for being born a Squib, a wizard-born person with no magical abilities. The Blacks were likely ashamed of his status, as Squibs are considered second-class citizens in the wizarding world and disregarded.
</p><p>Marius's date of birth is unknown, but given when his siblings were born, Marius was likely born between <a href="/wiki/1915" title="1915">1915</a> and <a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Marius_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Marius_Black?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marius" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Marius">Marius</a> is a Roman name derived from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars_(mythology)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Mars (mythology)">Mars, the Roman god</a> of war. It was the name of several Roman generals, a philosopher, and an emperor. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaius_Marius" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Gaius Marius">One of those Roman generals</a> was renowned for his revolutionary military reforms and for being elected consul an unprecedented <a href="/wiki/Seven" title="Seven">seven</a> times. The name is also sometimes used as a masculine version of the name <i>Maria</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which is derived from the Hebrew name <i>Miriam</i>, the meaning of which is usually theorised to be "sea of bitterness",<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> or, alternatively, derived from the ancient egyptic <i>mry; </i>beloved, or <i>mr</i>; love. Marius is also the name of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marius_Pontmercy" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Marius Pontmercy">one of the main characters</a> in Victor Hugo's <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Les Misérables">Les Misérables</a>.</i>
</p><p>Marius is also the name of a <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marius_(crater)">moon crater</a> , which fits in with the Black family tradition of naming children after stars.
</p>
