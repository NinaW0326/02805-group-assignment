<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ulick Gamp likely was born sometime before <a href="/wiki/1690" class="mw-redirect" title="1690">1690</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ministry_career">Ministry career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Ministry career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ministry career"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduating, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Wizard&#39;s Council">Wizard's Council</a> and became a member of <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a>. In 1707. after the abolishment of the Council, he bacame the first British Minister of Magic. Gamp had the onerous job of policing a fractious and frightened community adjusting to the imposition of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Secrecy</a>. His greatest legacy was to found the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a>. During Gamp's term, the Ministry outlawed the use of the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curses</a>, dubbing them <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">"unforgivable"</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TBBR_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBBR-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> During the early years of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a>, when the fate of the popular wizarding pub The <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a> was uncertain, Gamp agreed to bestow the landlord the responsibility of letting people into <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>, being sensitive to the needs of the patrons. To honour Gampâ€™s protection of the pub, the landlord created a new brand of <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">beer</a> in his name, <a href="/wiki/Gamp%27s_Old_Gregarious" title="Gamp&#39;s Old Gregarious">Gamp's Old Gregarious</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-leaky_cauldron_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leaky_cauldron-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/1707" class="mw-redirect" title="1707">1707</a>, he was elected as the first <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. 
</p><p>Gamp was elected Minister at least twice before resigning or being voted out of office in 1718.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He&#160;was depicted in a portrait hung at the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a>'s office at <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister%27s_office" title="Prime Minister&#39;s office">10 Downing Street</a> and, most likely, at the office of the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> at the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. It is highly probable that a <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Sticking_Charm" title="Permanent Sticking Charm">Permanent Sticking Charm</a> was placed on it, as the Muggle Prime Minister could never get the portrait off his wall, even with a builder to help. It served as a messenger between the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was also a portrait of Ulick Gamp at the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FBC2.3_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBC2.3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> presumably as an honour for ensuring protection of the pub as a safe haven for wizards.<sup id="cite_ref-leaky_cauldron_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leaky_cauldron-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After a <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Troll" class="mw-redirect" title="Mountain Troll">mountain troll</a> that had been kept in <a href="/wiki/Percival_Shacklehorn" title="Percival Shacklehorn">Percival Shacklehorn</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Percival_Shacklehorn%27s_tent" title="Percival Shacklehorn&#39;s tent">charmed tent</a> escaped and smashed much of the pub, the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron_bartender" title="Leaky Cauldron bartender">bartender</a> mistakenly hit the portrait with a <a href="/wiki/Confundus_Charm" title="Confundus Charm">Confundus Charm</a>. The charred painting was subsequently retrieved and restored during the Ministry investigation that followed the disturbance, by <a href="/wiki/Mathilda_Grimblehawk" title="Mathilda Grimblehawk">Mathilda Grimblehawk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mathilda_Grimblehawk%27s_partner" title="Mathilda Grimblehawk&#39;s partner">her partner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FBC2.3_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBC2.3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ulick_Gamp?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ulick is the Anglicised form of the <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> names Uilleac and Uilleag. There have been <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ulick" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Ulick">several Irish politicians and noblemen named Ulick</a>.
</p>
