<b>Selina Sapworthy</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> alumna at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and, later in her life, an award-winning author in the fields of <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a> and plant-related <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>. She was also a loyal servant to the <a href="/wiki/International_Quidditch_Team" title="International Quidditch Team">International Quidditch Team</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> sometime after her graduation.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Selina Sapworthy was born somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British Isles</a> and, in her youth, attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, where she was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Houses" title="Hogwarts Houses">House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-portrait_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-portrait-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During this time, she presumably excelled in the studies of <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>, as she later wrote books on both subjects.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Once she graduated from Hogwarts, Sapworthy became a loyal servant to the <a href="/wiki/International_Quidditch_Team" title="International Quidditch Team">International Quidditch Team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She went on to write a <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_books" class="mw-redirect" title="List of books">book</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Winogrand%27s_Wondrous_Water_Plants" title="Winogrand&#39;s Wondrous Water Plants">Winogrand's Wondrous Water Plants</a></i>, focusing specifically on the <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> plants among <a href="/wiki/Winogrand" title="Winogrand">Sir Winogrand's</a> collection in the <a href="/wiki/African_Lakes" title="African Lakes">African Lakes</a>. It was part of the required reading at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> for at least a quarter of a century.<sup id="cite_ref-book_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-book-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She also published <i><a href="/wiki/Xylomancy_(book)" title="Xylomancy (book)">Xylomancy</a></i>, a book exploring the eponymous (and often overlooked) art of <a href="/wiki/Xylomancy" title="Xylomancy">Divination by twigs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> of her was hung at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> by the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, near the sixth-floor landing of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Staircase" title="Grand Staircase">Grand Staircase</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a></b>: Selina Sapworthy was an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Herbologist" title="Herbologist">Herbologist</a> with much knowledge of different <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> plants and fungi, having written the Herbology textbook <i><a href="/wiki/Winogrand%27s_Wondrous_Water_Plants" title="Winogrand&#39;s Wondrous Water Plants">Winogrand's Wondrous Water Plants</a></i>, a guide which specifically focuses on aquatic plant life, showing she was very knowledgeable and experienced in this field.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a></b>: Sapworthy was also very skilful and experienced in this rather esoteric and mysterious branch of magic, having authored the book <i><a href="/wiki/Xylomancy_(book)" title="Xylomancy (book)">Xylomancy</a></i>, which covers the art of <a href="/wiki/Xylomancy" title="Xylomancy">Xylomancy</a>, the form of divination which involves the usage of twigs, showing she had much knowledge of this subject.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Selina_Sapworthy?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Selina is the English variant of the name Celine. Celine is a French feminine name that either descends from the Roman name <i>Caelinus, </i>which is derived from the Latin word <i>caelum</i>, meaning "heaven," or serves a nickname for <i>Marceline, </i>which is also Latin in origin and is related to the name of the Roman god Mars.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Selina is also an alternate spelling of the name Selena. "Selena" is the Latinized form of "Selene", a Greek name meaning "moon." Selene was the Greek goddess of the moon, later conflated with the goddess Artemis.<sup id="cite_ref-BTN_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTN-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>"Sapworthy" may be a variation of the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> surname "Sapworth" and a pun on the word "sap," the fluid that circulates in the vascular system of a plant. Both Sapworthy's known published works involve plants in some way.</li></ul>
