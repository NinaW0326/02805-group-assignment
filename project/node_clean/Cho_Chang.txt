<p><b>Cho Chang</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/1978" title="1978">1978</a>/<a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>-<a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> and was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw House</a>. She was a <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Quidditch_team" title="Ravenclaw Quidditch team">Ravenclaw Quidditch team</a> and a popular student. In Cho's <a href="/wiki/Fifth_year" title="Fifth year">fifth year</a>, she began dating <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> while he was a Champion in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. Unfortunately, Cedric became one of the first casualties of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, when he was murdered by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> on <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s order in June of <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. His death greatly upset Cho and made her determined to fight against the recently returned <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Lord</a>.
</p><p>In her sixth year, against her parents' wishes, she joined <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, an organisation taught and led by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. In the same year she also began a romantic relationship with Harry. However, Cho's best friend <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a> betrayed the <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">D.A.</a>. After her friend's betrayal, Cho and Harry's relationship fell apart. She remained loyal to her school and to the D.A., returning after she had graduated to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p><p>Cho survived the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> and eventually married <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang%27s_partner" class="mw-redirect" title="Cho Chang&#39;s partner">a Muggle</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cho was born into a wizarding <a href="/wiki/Chang_family" title="Chang family">family</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-blood_status_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blood_status-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang%27s_father" title="Cho Chang&#39;s father">Mr</a>. and <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang%27s_mother" title="Cho Chang&#39;s mother">Mrs Chang</a> between September <a href="/wiki/1978" title="1978">1978</a> and August <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>. She became a great <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> fan at the age of six, and cheered for the <a href="/wiki/Tutshill_Tornados" title="Tutshill Tornados">Tutshill Tornados</a>.
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>, at the age of eleven, Cho began attending <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and was Sorted into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> house. She soon tried out for her <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Quidditch_team" title="Ravenclaw Quidditch team">house Quidditch team</a>, and attained the position of <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a>. She became friends with fellow Ravenclaw <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a>.
</p>

<p>During a <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> match in her fourth year, Cho first met <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, who was a year below her and a fellow <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a>. Harry noticed she was very pretty and she made his stomach "feel funny". Cho's impression of Harry was favourable, and when she later wished Harry good luck before Gryffindor had a Quidditch match against <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, Harry went bright red.
</p><p>During the match against Ravenclaw, Cho began to tail Harry rather than find the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a> herself. Harry soon caught on to her strategy and attempted to trick her into thinking he had spotted the Snitch. As he dove, he saw the Snitch for real. Cho then gasped at the sight of a trio of <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>. Harry managed to unleash a powerful <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a> as well as snag the Snitch; the dementors were actually: <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Flint" title="Marcus Flint">Marcus Flint</a> in disguise. Ravenclaw did not win the <a href="/wiki/Inter-House_Quidditch_Cup" title="Inter-House Quidditch Cup">Quidditch Cup</a> that year with Gryffindor winning it instead of Slytherin.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/25_August" title="25 August">25 August</a> <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Cho went to the <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup#Finals" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">Finals</a>, presumably with her family. While there, she exchanged greetings with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, who had a crush on Cho by this time, having been developing feelings for her since the previous year when he first saw her during a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> match against the <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Quidditch_team" title="Ravenclaw Quidditch team">Ravenclaw Quidditch team</a>. He considered her to be very pretty and a good <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a>. She was friendly towards Harry throughout the year, but probably did not return his romantic feelings. She had already accepted <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>'s request to be his date to the Yule Ball. Cedric was a year above Cho and a champion in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>.
</p>

<p>Despite this, Cho refused to treat Harry poorly, as the majority of the school did because the <a href="/wiki/Goblet_of_Fire" title="Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a> had also selected him; she refused to wear one of <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>'s <i>Support Cedric Diggory</i> badges, something Harry was grateful for. He asked Cho to attend the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> with him, and she let him down kindly, even apologising for being unable to accept his offer; he still spent most of the ball watching Cho, who soon began dating Cedric. She was the "thing" Cedric would miss most and thus became his hostage during the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament#Second_Task" title="Triwizard Tournament">Second Task</a> of the Tournament; he employed the <a href="/wiki/Bubble-Head_Charm" title="Bubble-Head Charm">Bubble-Head Charm</a> to save her from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_lake" class="mw-redirect" title="Hogwarts lake">Hogwarts' lake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>That June, Cedric was murdered by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> on <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s orders. Cho was devastated and openly cried at the memorial service at the end of the school year.
</p>

</div><p>Cedric's death made Cho believe Harry's claim that Voldemort had returned despite the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> branding him a liar or insane. She sought Harry out several times and attempted to engage him in conversation, probably motivated by both a desire to learn the circumstances of Cedric's death and a growing fondness for Harry. Unfortunately for Harry, these encounters did not go well. The first time Cho found him covered with <a href="/wiki/Stinksap" title="Stinksap">Stinksap</a> that was accidentally let off by <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mimbulus_mimbletonia" title="Mimbulus mimbletonia">Mimbulus mimbletonia</a>. The second time, she ended up arguing with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> about Quidditch teams. On the third try, when Cho entered the <a href="/wiki/Owlery" title="Owlery">Owlery</a> to send a letter to her mother for her birthday while Harry was there, she defended him from a suspicious <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Argus Filch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>When invited by <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> to join Dumbledore's Army, Cho went against the advice of her parents, who wished her to remain on <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>'s good side, as her mother worked at the Ministry. She also brought her best friend and fellow Ravenclaw <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a> along, despite the other girl's reluctance to join due to her mom's position at the Ministry as well. Cho joined because she was determined to fight against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> after what happened to Cedric in the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, as a D.A. student she had her ups and downs. At first, she got nervous and performed her spells poorly whenever Harry was nearby. This indicated that her romantic feelings for him had grown since the previous year. Later, though, she was able to conjure a corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a> in the shape of a <a href="/wiki/Swan" title="Swan">swan</a>. However, she did have some trouble with a <a href="/wiki/Disarming_Charm" title="Disarming Charm">Disarming Charm</a>, saying <i>Expellimellius</i> instead of the actual incantation, causing Marietta's sleeve to catch on fire. She also mentioned that she had never been able to stun anything before Harry taught her and commented that he was a good teacher.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Cho initiated a kiss with Harry under some <a href="/wiki/Mistletoe" title="Mistletoe">mistletoe</a> shortly before <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> after a D.A. session. However, she soon ended up in tears, confused and guilty because of her conflicting feelings for Harry and Cedric. This kiss was something that Harry did not enjoy much and described as being "wet", because of all the tears and a repeat kiss did not happen. Despite this, she and Harry went on a date in the wizarding village of <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> on <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day#1996" title="Valentine&#39;s Day">Valentine's Day</a> where the couple sent their time at <a href="/wiki/Madam_Puddifoot%27s_Tea_Shop" title="Madam Puddifoot&#39;s Tea Shop">Madam Puddifoot's Tea Shop</a>. The tea shop made Harry uncomfortable, as it reminded him of Umbridge's too pink office.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cho was described to be an extremely pretty girl with long, shiny dark hair, a freckled nose, and being a head shorter than <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. For her date with Harry, she tied her hair back into a ponytail, to which he described as very pretty. Because of her beauty, she attracted a considerable amount of male attention during her Hogwarts years, most notably: <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Davies" title="Roger Davies">Roger Davies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Corner" title="Michael Corner">Michael Corner</a>, and of course Harry.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Generally polite, sweet, and well-mannered, Cho was easy to get along with and was a popular student at Hogwarts, with a large group of friends and many boys who admired her for her extreme beauty (including Harry Potter himself).
</p><p>Having been sorted into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw House</a>, Cho was presumably intelligent, enforced by her mastery of all <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">defence magic</a> that Harry taught her, including the Patronus Charm, though she did admit that she was unable to stun anything before Harry taught her. She was also an avid <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> fan (having supported the <a href="/wiki/Tutshill_Tornados" title="Tutshill Tornados">Tutshill Tornados</a> since she was six years old) and a good athlete, playing <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> for her House's team. She was also loyal and brave, having been loyal to the Tutshill Tornados for many years as opposed to jumping the bandwagon, joining <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> in defiance with the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> both out of support of Harry and in order to avenge the death of her late boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric</a>, sticking up for her friend <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta</a> when the rest of the group ousted her for her treachery, and returning to Hogwarts in her <a href="/wiki/Seventh_year" title="Seventh year">seventh year</a> in order to fight alongside her comrades and defend the school from Death Eaters.
</p><p>For the most part, Cho was often found around Hogwarts with a gaggle of girlfriends, though after one of her emotional breakdowns over Cedric's death, they apparently left her during her depression. Despite this, Cho was a very loyal person (perhaps even to a fault); when she and her friend <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a> joined Dumbledore's Army, she insisted on bringing her friend along even when her friend expressed disinterest and cynicism to the club's effects. Later, even though Marietta eventually sold out the DA to Umbridge and the Ministry of Magic, Cho stood by her and insisted to the accusatory Harry that Marietta was a "lovely person" who "just made a mistake." Her loyalty and courage were shown again when she came back to Hogwarts to fight even after she had graduated, and offered to show Harry up to the <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a>, indicating that she harboured no hard feelings against them despite their previously fallen out romantic relationship.
</p><p>Cho was perceived by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> to be excessively emotional, though as Hermione stated, much of this characterisation was attributed to her behaviour and actions directly after her boyfriend's death, pushing her way through the trauma and grief that is only observed from the periphery. During her grief, she was prone to many crying spells and a difficult control over her emotions, leading to a prying sort of aggression when she dated Harry to get the details of Cedric's death, and also the irrational jealousy and paranoia that ensued when Harry would bring up <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> for what Cho saw to be flippant insensitivity from Harry's end. Hermione, for her part, did not seem to blame Cho at all, understanding her grief and depression and stating that it had interfered with many aspects of Cho's life, including Cho's grades and her position on the Quidditch team.
</p><p>Cho has also said that she and Roger Davies used to date; after Ravenclaw's loss of the House Cup in 1995, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> said that Michael Corner had gone to comfort Cho. Later in life, she married a Muggle.

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: Cho flew excellently, so much so that she was chosen to play the position of <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Quidditch_team" title="Ravenclaw Quidditch team">Ravenclaw Quidditch team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, Cho's flying skills suffered intensely due to her grief over Cedric's death, to the extent where she was actually afraid she would lose her position on the Ravenclaw Quidditch team.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Cho appeared to be proficient in charm-work, given how in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> she learned how to successfully conjure a fully corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a> in her sixth year, which is a very advanced piece of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and a sign of superior magical ability and talent. Her Patronus took the form of a <a href="/wiki/Swan" title="Swan">swan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Considering she trained with the <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">D.A.</a>, Cho would have been capable in defensive and offensive magic. However, her track-record with defensive learning spells was uneven, as she struggled to learn the <a href="/wiki/Disarming_Charm" title="Disarming Charm">Disarming Charm</a> effectively.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She seemed to have improved her abilities based on her fighting in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, and surviving both the battle and the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> (assuming her survival had not just been owing to simple good luck of course).<sup id="cite_ref-DH_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cho's relationship with her parents was unknown, but it was revealed that, during the <a href="/wiki/1995-1996_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1995-1996 school year">1995-1996 school year</a>, both her <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang%27s_mother" title="Cho Chang&#39;s mother">mother</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang%27s_father" title="Cho Chang&#39;s father">father</a> wanted her to stay on <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>'s good side. Though Cho went against their wishes by joining <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, she did send her mother a <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang%27s_letter_to_her_mother" title="Cho Chang&#39;s letter to her mother">parcel</a> for her birthday, showing affectionate care. Their relationship after the school year was unknown.
</p>

<p>Some time during Cho's life, she dated a fellow <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> — <a href="/wiki/Roger_Davies" title="Roger Davies">Roger Davies</a>, who also served as her <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Captain#Hogwarts_Quidditch_Captains" title="Quidditch Captain">Captain</a> on <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Quidditch_team" title="Ravenclaw Quidditch team">their house's Quidditch team</a> — but it didn't last long since he eventually got <a href="/wiki/Roger_Davies%27s_girlfriend" title="Roger Davies&#39;s girlfriend">a new girlfriend</a> while Cho moved on with either Cedric or Harry. Davies asked Cho out sometime after Cedric's death, though Cho rejected him in favour for Harry; regardless, she used this to gauge Harry's feelings for her, and looked at Roger and his girlfriend as though they were setting a standard to which Cho expected Harry to live up.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cedric_Diggory">Cedric Diggory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Cedric Diggory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Cedric Diggory"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In her <a href="/wiki/Fifth_year" title="Fifth year">fifth year</a>, Cho was asked to the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> by Cedric Diggory, a <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> student a year above her. The two subsequently began dating, and she was extremely taken with him. Because Cho was the person Cedric would miss most, she served as his hostage during the Second Task of the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Cho and Cedric also had a date at <a href="/wiki/Puddifoot" title="Puddifoot">Madam Puddifoot</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Madam_Puddifoot%27s_Tea_Shop" title="Madam Puddifoot&#39;s Tea Shop">Tea Shop</a>. Cho was devastated when Cedric was murdered that June.
</p><p>The following year was very difficult for her; in addition to missing Cedric, she was the subject of gossip regarding her burgeoning romance with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, the only witness to Cedric's murder who was widely regarded as being either a liar or a nutter for claiming that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> had returned. Cho's grief and depression led her to almost constantly crying and causing her Quidditch abilities to suffer.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This was probably compounded by a lack of closure, as Harry was the only person who knew what had happened to Cedric and did not want to discuss it with Cho.
</p><p>Given how, in spite of her genuinely intense grief, Cho managed to go on to date others, and eventually married a Muggle after the Second Wizarding War, it can be said that she learned to move on from the pain of losing Cedric.
</p>

<p>Harry Potter was drawn to Cho's good looks and developed a crush on her when she was in her fourth year,<sup id="cite_ref-POA_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and asked her to the Yule Ball in the next, though she had already agreed to attend with Cedric. She joined <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> and became nervous whenever Harry was nearby, something Harry also exhibited. They shared a kiss under the mistletoe around Christmas, but this was spoiled by Cho beginning to cry, and Harry being at a loss for how to respond.
</p>

<p>The two went on a date in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> on Valentine's Day, but Cho's feelings for Harry were confused because of her continuing grief over Cedric's death and Harry's discomfort with discussing it. She also became jealous of Harry's friendship with <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, convinced they had feelings for each-other, and in a moment of weakness and insecurity, ditched the date. Harry, who was inexperienced with girls, found Cho's behaviour very confusing, especially when she tried to make him jealous and to gauge his feelings for her by mentioning that <a href="/wiki/Roger_Davies" title="Roger Davies">Roger Davies</a> had asked her out, and ultimately leaving him in <a href="/wiki/Madam_Puddifoot%27s_Tea_Shop" title="Madam Puddifoot&#39;s Tea Shop">Madam Puddifoot's Tea Shop</a> for the other customers to stare at him astonishingly. Unimpressed with her attitude and for souring his experience of a first date, Harry grew angry at her. Though they eased up a bit after Cho read Harry's interview in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quibbler" title="The Quibbler">The Quibbler</a></i>, the strike that broke the relationship was when <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a> betrayed the D.A. and Cho stood up for her, angering Harry, who in turn again became angry with Harry and paranoid that he and Hermione were in a relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After that, their relationship simply “fell apart”.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Cho saw Harry on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> at the end of the 1995–1996 school year, and again at the start of the 1996-1997 school year, she avoided him both times, as she was too embarrassed (and possibly still angry), for them to look at each other, let alone talk to each other.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On the other hand, Harry found himself not caring about Cho anymore due to his godfather <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s death being far more devastating to him than their break-up,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> seemingly disregarding Cho with indifference as opposed to embarrassment. As for Marietta, he sneered at her limited success of hiding her disfigurement.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, by 1998, any negative feelings on Cho's part seemed to have vanished, as she offered to escort Harry to <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a> just before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>. She was disappointed when <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> fiercely suggested that <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> escort him instead, suggesting that Cho may have appreciated some alone time with Harry, whether because of any lingering feelings or just because she would've liked to talk with him since after their fallout. Harry, on the other hand, did not so much as hesitate in accepting Ginny's suggestion and Luna's escort, indicating that he was still angry and/or indifferent towards her and had no interest in mending their relationship (though it is also possible that the then tense and desperate situation with Voldemort and his forces left Harry with little time or emotional energy to devote to Cho).<sup id="cite_ref-DH_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Eventually, Harry implied that Cho's depression and crying were things that he had to "cope" with, leading her to storming away due to this insensitivity.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Cho was, until the beginning of her sixth year, very popular. She had an entire group of Ravenclaw girl friends, who were constantly giggling, Marietta being one of them. However, as because of Cho's depression and grief-induced mood swings, all the others fell by the wayside. Being a Quidditch star and very pretty, she was the centre of the group, having a handful of friends accompany her even to the bathroom, and up until her sixth year she was never seen without them. At the welcoming feast in her sixth year, Cho chatted animatedly with her friends but later on she was mostly by herself or with Marietta.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_5-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Cho became a member of Dumbledore's Army in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. It was an organisation led by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, in order to oppose <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>. Cho's relationship with the other members of the army is unknown, but it is possible that they were all friends. Some members of <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Michael_Corner" title="Michael Corner">Michael Corner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terry_Boot" title="Terry Boot">Terry Boot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Goldstein" title="Anthony Goldstein">Anthony Goldstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Padma_Patil" title="Padma Patil">Padma Patil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> were members of the army, as well. She may also have been friends with <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a> as the two girls were in the same year and were seen chatting to one another after the Battle of Hogwarts.
</p><p>Cho was not fond of <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> however; she did not trust Harry's closeness and friendship with Hermione, generally suspicious that Harry had feelings for her as well. This was one of the reasons that Cho and Harry fell apart. Ironically, Hermione was trying to give advice to Harry on building a successful relationship with Cho. As for Ron, he was tactless when Cho came to Harry, accusing her of being biased in supporting the <a href="/wiki/Tutshill_Tornados" title="Tutshill Tornados">Tutshill Tornados</a> while being ignorant of her desire to talk to Harry alone. Naturally this did little to endear her to Ron. Furthermore, as Harry would discuss Cedric's death with both Ron and Hermione, while refusing to do the same with Cho, this only increased Cho's dislike of Harry's best friends.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> seemed relatively neutral about Cho, (though for obvious reasons she was not supportive of her and Harry's relationship), mentioning to Harry out of suggestion that maybe he should talk to her about Marietta instead of the long periods of avoidance from his end.
</p><p>When the entire DA ostracised Marietta for betraying them, Cho alone stood up for her, putting her at odds with the rest of the group. This did not, however, appear to prevent her from retaining membership with the DA, and she fought with Dumbledore's Army during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, most of them surviving the war.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?veaction=edit&amp;section=25" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang?action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Cho is a common Sinitic and Korean name. Chang is a Chinese family name, depending on the character meaning “prosperous” [昌], "common" [常], "bright" [昶], "to sing/chant" [唱] or “unhindered” [暢]. In Chinese, <i>chóuchàng</i> [惆悵] means “melancholy”.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This may allude to Cho's fragile emotional state following <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>'s murder. It is, however, unlikely since 惆 is not a surname.
<ul><li>In the Chinese translations of Harry Potter, the name "Cho Chang" is translated into "Zhāng Qiū" [張秋] (in most Eastern languages, the surname precedes the given name), with "Zhāng" being a common Chinese surname and the official pinyin transcription of "Chang", with "Qiū" meaning "autumn". As the name Cho Chang is considered to be Chinese, this translation may give a hint as to what the name may mean. In Mandarin pinyin, the name is pronounced "Jah-ng Chee-oh". "Cho Chang" is also a possible direct romanisation (not a translation) of 張秋 in Hong Kong, due to its dual Cantonese and British influence.</li></ul></li></ul>

<ul><li>Over 3,000 girls turned up to audition for the role of Cho Chang.</li>
<li>Cho was portrayed in the fourth, fifth, and sixth films and the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (film)">two-part film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i> by Scottish actress <a href="/wiki/Katie_Leung" title="Katie Leung">Katie Leung</a>, who portrayed her as a Scot, although this is never mentioned in the books. Katie speaks with a pronounced Glaswegian accent.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, the character of <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a> does not appear, and instead it is Cho who betrays the D.A. under the influence of <a href="/wiki/Veritaserum" title="Veritaserum">Veritaserum</a>. This is also the reason for her break-up with Harry in the film, instead of her continuing grief over <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>, jealousy of <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, and defence of Marietta, as in the book. Harry, Ron, and Hermione don't know this until it is confirmed by <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Professor Snape</a>.</li>
<li>In the films, Cho is in the same year as Harry which was first brought up in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i> (similar changes were made for <a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane" title="Romilda Vane">Romilda Vane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Half-Blood Prince</a></i> film adaptation). This explains why she is still at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">last film</a>. In the books she is a year older than Harry and graduated at the end of the <i>Half-Blood Prince</i>.</li>
<li>Also in the film adaptation of the <i>Order of the Phoenix</i>, it was mentioned that Cho's mother worked for the Ministry, whereas in the book it was Marietta's mother that worked for the Ministry. This change was due to Marietta's removal from the film.</li>
<li>In the film adaptation of <i>Half-Blood Prince</i> Cho appears in several scenes and has no speaking lines in this film.</li>
<li>Cho's known <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> record against <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> is 0-3. This includes two straight defeats playing head to head against <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>.</li>
<li>Cho Chang is well known for being <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s first crush, first kiss, and first girlfriend in the Harry Potter books and films.</li>
<li>Ironically, Ginny's first boyfriend, Michael Corner, and Harry's first girlfriend, Cho, begin dating in <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">1997</a>.</li>
<li>Katie recently portrayed Cho again, for one of <a href="/wiki/Jessie_Cave" title="Jessie Cave">Jessie Cave</a>'s Pindippy video.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, Cho appears as one of the many students of Hogwarts suffering under the Death Eater regime run by Snape and the Carrows. When Harry returns, it seems that all hard feelings about her betrayal of Dumbledore's Army have been forgotten (or forgiven) when she is one of the many people to embrace him. She is also one of the many who stand up for Harry against Pansy Parkinson's claim that they should grab him after Voldemort's ultimatum and is seen with Neville and Seamus receiving instructions from Professor McGonagall to blow up the bridge. Finally, she is seen by Harry recovering from the battle in the Great Hall. They smile at each other.</li></ul>
