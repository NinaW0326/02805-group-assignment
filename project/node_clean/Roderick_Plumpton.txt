<p><b>Roderick Plumpton</b> (<a href="/wiki/1889" class="mw-redirect" title="1889">1889</a>-<a href="/wiki/1987" title="1987">1987</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was the <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Tutshill_Tornados" title="Tutshill Tornados">Tutshill Tornados</a> <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_team" title="Quidditch team">team</a> during the early 1900s. He invented the <a href="/wiki/Plumpton_Pass" title="Plumpton Pass">Plumpton Pass</a> during a match against the <a href="/wiki/Caerphilly_Catapults" title="Caerphilly Catapults">Caerphilly Catapults</a> in <a href="/wiki/1921" title="1921">1921</a>, where he caught the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Snitch</a> after three and a half seconds, setting the British record for the fastest capture of the Snitch during a game.<sup id="cite_ref-QTAR_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Plumpton was also accomplished in the arts, having painted the <a href="/wiki/Witch_and_Wizard_Couple" title="Witch and Wizard Couple">Witch and Wizard Couple</a>, a painting of a medieval couple, currently hanging at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Roderick_Plumpton?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Roderick_Plumpton?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Plumpton led the Tornados to five consecutive <a href="/wiki/League_Cup" title="League Cup">League Cups</a>, and played Seeker for the <a href="/wiki/English_National_Quidditch_team" title="English National Quidditch team">English National Quidditch team</a> twenty-two times. While critics allege his 'Plumpton Pass' was an accident, and the Snitch had merely flown up his sleeve, Plumpton insisted until his death that he meant to do it.<sup id="cite_ref-QTAR_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point before he died, he signed a souvenir programme, which later made its way into the hands of <a href="/wiki/Sage_Bragnam" title="Sage Bragnam">Sage Bragnam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Roderick_Plumpton?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Roderick_Plumpton?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: Plumpton was a highly accomplished and proficient <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> flyer, having played as <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Tutshill_Tornados" title="Tutshill Tornados">Tutshill Tornados</a> and the <a href="/wiki/English_National_Quidditch_team" title="English National Quidditch team">English National Quidditch team</a>. He famously caught the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a> three and a half-seconds into a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>, setting a new record and establishing himself as a legend in the sport's history.<sup id="cite_ref-QTAR_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTAR-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Artist</b>: Plumpton was also very talented in the visual arts, having painted a <a href="/wiki/Witch_and_Wizard_Couple" title="Witch and Wizard Couple">Witch and Wizard Couple</a> <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> which hung in <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Roderick_Plumpton?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Roderick_Plumpton?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Roderick" means "famous power" from the Germanic elements hrod "fame" and ric "power". This name was in use among the Visigoths; it was borne by their last king (also known as Rodrigo), who died fighting the Muslim invaders of Spain in the 8th century. It also had cognates in Old Norse and West Germanic, and it was introduced to England by Scandinavian settlers and the Normans, though it died out after the Middle Ages. It was revived in the English-speaking world by Sir Walter Scott's poem 'The Vision of Don Roderick' (1811).
</p>
