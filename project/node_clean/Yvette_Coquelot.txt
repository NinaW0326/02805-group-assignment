<p><b>Madame Yvette Coquelot</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1869" class="mw-redirect" title="1869">1869</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> who presumably ran an establishment in <a href="/wiki/69_Boulevard_des_Galantes" title="69 Boulevard des Galantes">69 Boulevard des Galantes</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. There was a plaque at <a href="/wiki/The_Seven_Swans" title="The Seven Swans">The Seven Swans</a> pub in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a> requesting patrons to "discharge their weapons before entering", crediting Mme Coquelot as its original owner.<sup id="cite_ref-Potterish_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Potterish-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Yvette_Coquelot?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Yvette_Coquelot?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>Yvette</b> is the female form of the name Yves meaning Yew tree.</li>
<li><b>Yvette</b> is also Quebec term for a docile obedient housewife who votes against sovereignty <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quebec_referendum,_1980#Brunch_des_Yvettes">[1]</a> <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Yvette">[2]</a>.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Coquelot is likely a contracted form of the word coquelicot or a variant of coquelet meaning small cockerel or male baby chick <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/coquelet">[3]</a>. The word coquelicot means "poppy" since poppies are red like a rooster's chest <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external autonumber" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/coquelicot">[4]</a>.  The French word for rooster is coq.</li></ul>
