<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rosalind_Bungs?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rosalind_Bungs?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Rosalind" is derived from the Germanic elements <i>hros</i> "horse" and <i>linde</i> "soft, tender". This name was introduced by the Normans to England, though it was not common. During the Middle Ages its spelling was influenced by the Latin phrase <i>rosa linda</i> "beautiful rose". The name was popularised by Edmund Spencer, who used it in his poetry, and by William Shakespeare, who used it for the heroine in his comedy <i>As You Like It</i> (1599).</li>
<li>A "bung" is a stopper especially for the hole through which a cask, keg, or barrel is filled or emptied.</li></ul>
