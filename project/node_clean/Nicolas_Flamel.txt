<p><b>Nicolas Flamel</b> (c. <a href="/wiki/1326" class="mw-redirect" title="1326">1326</a><sup id="cite_ref-birthyear_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthyear-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> â€“ c. <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a><sup id="cite_ref-death_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-death-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/French" title="French">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and famed <a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">alchemist</a> who was the only known maker of the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>, a legendary substance with incredible powers. He was a close friend and partner with former <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Headmaster" title="Hogwarts Headmaster">Hogwarts Headmaster</a> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>. He owed his considerable age to the <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a>, which was produced from the Stone, along with his wife <a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel" title="Perenelle Flamel">Perenelle</a>. He was well over six hundred years old as of 1992,<sup id="cite_ref-birthyear_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthyear-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> being one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_oldest_wizards" title="List of oldest wizards">oldest wizards</a> in history.
</p><p>Flamel was briefly mentioned on <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore_(Chocolate_Frog_Card)" title="Albus Dumbledore (Chocolate Frog Card)">Albus Dumbledore's Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="Early_life_(early_14th_century)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_.28early_14th_century.29">Early life <span class="template-c">(early 14th century)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life (early 14th century)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life (early 14th century)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nicolas was born in the early <a href="/wiki/14th_century" title="14th century">14th century</a>. In his youth, Nicolas attended <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons Academy of Magic</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">Pyrenees</a> Mountains of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and it was there that he met <a href="/wiki/Perenelle_Flamel" title="Perenelle Flamel">Perenelle</a>, his future wife. It is said that he later funded both the academy's chateau and grounds, and a <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_and_Perenelle_Flamel_Fountain" title="Nicolas and Perenelle Flamel Fountain">fountain</a> on the grounds was named after the couple.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Gaining_immortality_(late_14th_century-1926)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Gaining_immortality_.28late_14th_century-1926.29">Gaining immortality <span class="template-c">(late 14th century-1926)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Gaining immortality (late 14th century-1926)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gaining immortality (late 14th century-1926)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Flamel became a noted alchemist of considerable talent.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At some point, he created the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>, an item of immense <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> power. With the Stone, he created the <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a>, which extended his life. Although it is clear that he used the Elixir to greatly extend his life, it is not specified whether he used the Stone's powers to make himself greatly wealthy, as the Stone is also capable of turning any metal into gold.
</p><p>Nicolas was implied to have been in personal combat, but had become inactive in this field by the <a href="/wiki/1720s" title="1720s">1720s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1762 he was spotted at an opera house in Paris.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1890s" title="1890s">1890s</a>, Nicolas first came into contact with the brilliant teenager <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>When Albus Dumbledore began to take action to stop <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, Nicolas Flamel, as his friend, decided to help him in this, and agreed to let him use his <a href="/wiki/Flamel_House" title="Flamel House">home</a> as a safehouse to any potential allies of his should the situation require it. In <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Magizoologist" title="Magizoologist">Magizoologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newton Scamander</a> decided to take advantage of the invitation and arrived at the Flamel House alongside two <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">Americans</a> by the name <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Porpentina Goldstein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Kowalski" title="Jacob Kowalski">Jacob Kowalski</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Yusuf_Kama" title="Yusuf Kama">Yusuf Kama</a>, a wizard who had tried to take Newt and his friends captive so he was free to track down his intended victim unhindered, only for him to suddenly lose consciousness. Once they had arrived safely at the Flamel House, which by then was well protected by various <a href="/wiki/Protective_enchantment" title="Protective enchantment">protective enchantments</a>, Mr Scamander was able to remove the <a href="/wiki/Water_dragon_parasite" title="Water dragon parasite">Water dragon parasite</a> from the Kama's eye. While he recovered from the procedure and the parasite's venom, Newt and Tina went to the <a href="/wiki/French_Ministry_of_Magic_Headquarters" title="French Ministry of Magic Headquarters">French Ministry of Magic</a>, so when Nicolas returned home, he found only the inexorable and still unconscious Yusuf and a sleeping Jacob. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A kindly old man with a polite disposition, Nicolas Flamel warmly welcomed <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Kowalski" title="Jacob Kowalski">Jacob Kowalski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Tina Goldstein</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yusuf_Kama" title="Yusuf Kama">Yusuf Kama</a> when they arrived at his home in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, treating Dumbledore's "friends" like his own. In spite of his advanced age of over 600, Nicolas was happy to do what he could to help put an end to the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a> <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>'s seemingly unstoppable rise to power, and agreed to let his home be used as a safehouse for those who opposed him. Although he appeared to have had some doubts about his own abilities, Nicolas nevertheless proved himself to be a very brave and selfless individual, as he arrived to help Newt Scamander and the latter's allies to fight Grindelwald at the <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Mausoleum" title="Lestrange Mausoleum">Lestrange family Mausoleum</a> when called upon to do so by <a href="/wiki/Eulalie_Hicks" title="Eulalie Hicks">Eulalie Hicks</a> even though he had not been entirely certain that he was up to the task. Although he arrived too late to help fight Grindelwald himself, Nicolas nevertheless displayed great courage both by his timely arrival, and by his help in containing the incredibly powerful, rampant <a href="/wiki/Protego_Diabolica" title="Protego Diabolica">black fire</a> their foe conjured, and was, as such, instrumental to the efforts to save <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> from it.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite making efforts to hide his <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a> from the wrong hands (first in <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank" title="Gringotts Wizarding Bank">Gringotts Wizarding Bank</a>, then in <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>), Nicolas by the age of 690, much like his friend <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, ultimately viewed death as "but the next great adventure". Hence, after <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> very nearly succeeded in capturing his Philosopher's Stone, Nicolas willingly had it destroyed, having collected enough <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a> to set his and his wife's affairs in order.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Flamel was also well known for his love of <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Belying his frail exterior, Nicolas Flamel was a very powerful and accomplished wizard. Rich in learning and experience, testaments to Nicolas' considerable abilities were his proficiency in <a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">alchemy</a>, held to be some of the most difficult <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> known to <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizardkind</a>, and Nicolas helping to save the city of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> from the rampant, destructive black fire unleashed by <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, the most powerful <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a> of the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">Alchemy</a></b>: Nicolas Flamel was perhaps the greatest alchemist the world had ever seen, being the only known person to successfully create a <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>, the ultimate goal of alchemy. With this, Flamel was able to render himself and his wife Perenelle <a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">immortal</a> for centuries, until he chose to destroy the stone of his own volition in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: Flamel was also an exceptionally gifted <a href="/wiki/Potioneer" title="Potioneer">potioneer</a>, as by using his alchemical knowledge of the properties of the Philosopher's Stone, he created the <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a>, an incredible potion to make the drinker immortal. The effects of this potion were so extraordinary that by drinking it, he managed to remain alive and well for hundreds of years.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Although the exact extent of his skills is uncertain, it is evident that Nicolas Flamel had extensive knowledge of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> and was extremely adept at defending himself from them, as he instantly recognised and knew how to stop the incredibly dangerous and volatile <a href="/wiki/Protego_Diabolica" title="Protego Diabolica">fire</a> conjured by <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, and, along with <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Theseus_Scamander" title="Theseus Scamander">Theseus Scamander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Tina Goldstein</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yusuf_Kama" title="Yusuf Kama">Yusuf Kama</a>, successfully contained it by means of a combined powerful <a href="/wiki/General_Counter-Spell" title="General Counter-Spell">General Counter-Spell</a>, thus effectively preventing the destruction of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. Nicolas' home was also described by Dumbledore as being heavily warded by various powerful <a href="/wiki/Protective_enchantment" title="Protective enchantment">protective enchantments</a> after he turned his home into a safehouse for Dumbledore's friends and allies, showing he was very proficient in the casting of them. Furthermore, Nicolas Flamel claimed that he had not "seen action" in about two hundred years, suggesting that prior to <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, he used to be both an active and highly accomplished <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellist</a>. Despite his long absence from using <a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">martial magic</a>, however, and initial doubt that he would be of any help in fighting <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Ilvermorny</a> professor <a href="/wiki/Eulalie_Hicks" title="Eulalie Hicks">Eulalie Hicks</a> had great faith in his defensive abilities, and urged him to go to the <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Mausoleum" title="Lestrange Mausoleum">Lestrange family Mausoleum</a> to save Newt and his friends, which he did with great success, showing his impressive talents in this field.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a></b>: Nicolas Flamel apparently had some ability in Divination, as he could gaze into <a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">crystal balls</a> and see events unfold elsewhere, such as when he saw <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>, and the upcoming <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> rally at the <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Mausoleum" title="Lestrange Mausoleum">Lestrange family Mausoleum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">Wandless</a></b> and <b><a href="/wiki/Non-verbal_magic" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-verbal magic">Non-verbal magic</a></b>: Nicolas Flamel proved capable of using both wandless and nonverbal magic, both of which is advanced and highly difficult to perform even individually, yet Nicolas used both simultaneously when he unlocked the padlock on his <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel%27s_phoenix-embossed_book" title="Nicolas Flamel&#39;s phoenix-embossed book">phoenix-embossed book</a> by simply touching it.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Immortality" title="Immortality">Immortality</a></b>: Having drunk the Elixir of Life on a continuous basis, Nicolas remained alive for over 600 years.<sup id="cite_ref-PS13_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS13-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, while this extended his lifespan, his body continued to age, leading to a frail and ancient appearance. His life became completely reliant on the Elixir and the Stone used to brew it. Once he agreed to destroy the Stone and depleted the potion, Nicolas died.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Social networking</b>: Due to having lived over 600 years thanks to the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a>, Nicolas Flamel has a vast network of international contacts in the whole <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding world</a>, communicating with them via his <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel%27s_phoenix-embossed_book" title="Nicolas Flamel&#39;s phoenix-embossed book">phoenix-embossed book</a>. Notable among his contacts were <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eulalie_Hicks" title="Eulalie Hicks">Eulalie Hicks</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Ilvermorny</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Bilingualism:</b> Nicolas Flamel spoke his native French, as well as fluent English, with only a slight accent in the latter.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>Nicolas</i> is the French form of the Greek name <i>Nikolaos</i> meaning "victory of the people".</li>
<li>The name <i>Flamel</i> means in Old French "flame". Such meaning can be associated to Flamel's work as an alchemist.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Flamel's first name is spelt as Nicholas, adding an <i>h</i> in the subtitles and the book <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> reads.</li>
<li>Also in the film, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> discover the link to Nicolas Flamel and the stone through a book, rather than <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>. (In a deleted scene you see them discovering the connection through a chocolate frog card, but it was switched when edited.)</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_Flamel" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Nicolas Flamel">Nicolas Flamel</a> was a real French scribe believed in legend to have been an alchemist who created the Philosopher's Stone. He was born about <a href="/wiki/1330" class="mw-redirect" title="1330">1330</a> and died in <a href="/wiki/1418" class="mw-redirect" title="1418">1418</a>. There is no proof that the historical Flamel achieved immortality with his wife Perenelle, though a few have claimed to have seen him throughout the centuries, or that he was even involved in alchemy at all. Some treasure-hunters accidentally dug up his grave and discovered that his tomb was empty. Another goal of Nicolas Flamel according to the legend was to turn ordinary lead into pure gold. The reference to Flamel marks one of the few occasions in which a real person has been referenced as a character in the Potter books.</li>
<li><i>Nicolas Flamel</i> is the title of chapter 13 in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>.</li>
<li>In a deleted first draft of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Harry revealed that Nicolas Flamel was spotted in an <a href="/wiki/Parisian_opera_house" title="Parisian opera house">opera house</a> which was located in Paris in <a href="/wiki/1762" class="mw-redirect" title="1762">1762</a>. Whether this is <a href="/wiki/Canon" title="Canon">canon</a> is unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-early_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-early-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Nicolas was the first known Beauxbatons student introduced in the series, though the fact that he attended Beauxbatons was only revealed on Pottermore.</li>
<li>Nicolas Flamel appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts:_The_Crimes_of_Grindelwald" title="Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald">second film</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_(film_series)" title="Fantastic Beasts (film series)">Fantastic Beasts</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_(film_series)" title="Fantastic Beasts (film series)">series</a>, portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Brontis_Jodorowsky" title="Brontis Jodorowsky">Brontis Jodorowsky</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The pronunciation of Flamel's first name varies by canon source. While the s is pronounced in the Harry Potter films, Nicolas himself says it with a silent s in <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts:_The_Crimes_of_Grindelwald" title="Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald">Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald</a></i>, which is the French pronunciation.</li></ul>
