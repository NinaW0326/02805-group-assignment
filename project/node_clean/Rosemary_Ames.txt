<b>Rosemary Ames</b> was a respected <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> horticulturalist. She lived in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, where she kept a personal garden, which she described as "[her own] <a href="/wiki/Elysian_Fields" title="Elysian Fields">Elysian Fields</a>." In <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, she allowed <i><a href="/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Wall" title="Hadrian&#39;s Wall">Hadrian's Wall</a></i>, a British horticulture magazine, to tour her garden. The magazine published an article about the garden, proclaiming it "Devon heaven," and comparing it to <a href="/wiki/Eden" title="Eden">Eden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
