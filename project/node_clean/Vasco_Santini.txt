<b>Vasco Santini</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Beater" title="Beater">Beater</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_National_Quidditch_team" title="Spanish National Quidditch team">Spanish National Quidditch team</a>. He represented his country in at least two <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cups</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, he was included on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> for his achievements in <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vasco_Santini?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vasco_Santini?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Vasco" comes from the medieval Spanish name Velasco which possibly meant "crow" in Basque. A famous bearer was the 15th-century Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama, the first person to sail from Europe around Africa to India. <i>Vasco</i> is also the Spanish word for Basque, referring to the people of the Basque country, an autonomous community in Spain. 
</p>
