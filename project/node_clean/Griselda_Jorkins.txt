<b>Griselda Harmonia Jorkins</b> (born <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>) was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Jorkins" title="Albert Jorkins">Albert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primrose_Jorkins" title="Primrose Jorkins">Primrose Jorkins</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>. She had two older brothers called <a href="/wiki/Grimwold_Jorkins" title="Grimwold Jorkins">Grimwold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Granville_Jorkins" title="Granville Jorkins">Granville</a>. Her birth was announced in the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Jorkins?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Griselda_Jorkins?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Griselda" is possibly derived from the Germanic elements <i>gris</i> "grey" and <i>hild</i> "battle". It is not attested as a Germanic name. This was the name of a patient wife in medieval tales by <i>Boccaccio</i> and <i>Chaucer</i>.
</p>
