<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 147px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/7e/Wallofportraits.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20130512140920" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/7e/Wallofportraits.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/145?cb=20130512140920" decoding="async" width="145" height="246" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Wallofportraits.jpg" data-image-key="Wallofportraits.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Wallofportraits.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor" title="Godric Gryffindor">Godric Gryffindor</a>, the original owner of the Sword</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>The sword of Gryffindor was made in the 10th century by goblins, the magical world's most skilled metalworkers, and is therefore enchanted. Fashioned from pure silver, it is inset with rubies, the stone that represents Gryffindor in the hour-glasses that count the house points at Hogwarts. Godric Gryffindor's name is engraved just beneath the hilt.<sup id="cite_ref-POT_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The sword was made to Godric Gryffindor's specifications by <a href="/wiki/Ragnuk" title="Ragnuk">Ragnuk the First</a>, finest of the <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a> <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silversmiths</a>, and therefore King (in goblin culture, the ruler does not work less than the others, but more skillfully). When it was finished, Ragnuk coveted it so much that he pretended that Gryffindor had stolen it from him, and sent minions to steal it back. Gryffindor defended himself with his <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>, but did not kill his attackers. Instead he sent them back to their king bewitched, to deliver the threat that if he ever tried to steal from Gryffindor again, Gryffindor would unsheathe the sword against them all. The goblin king took the threat seriously and left Gryffindor in possession of his rightful property, but remained resentful until he died.<sup id="cite_ref-POT_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A legend persists, in some sections of the goblin community, that Gryffindor actually stole the sword from Ragnuk. This is partly a result of belief among some goblins that goblin-made items rightfully belong to goblins, rather than the heirs of those who may have purchased them.<sup id="cite_ref-POT_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DH_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/May" title="May">May</a> of <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, the sword materialised inside the old school <a href="/wiki/Sorting_Hat" title="Sorting Hat">Sorting Hat</a> and appeared to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, whom the hat was given to when he arrived in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> to save his <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">best friend</a>'s sister, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>, from being drained of her life force by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_Diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s Diary">Tom Riddle's Diary</a> and eaten by the <a href="/wiki/Basilisk" title="Basilisk">Basilisk</a>. Harry used the sword to kill the Basilisk, which was hiding inside the bowels of the Chamber.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This caused the sword to be imbued with basilisk <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_venom" title="Basilisk venom">venom</a> (since goblin-made items only imbibe what makes them stronger) and hence gained the ability to destroy Horcruxes, although Harry instead used one of the Basilisk's fangs to destroy the diary, which eliminated the sentient memory of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> it contained and restored Ginny's vitality. After the events of that year, the sword resided in the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster’s</a> <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_Office" title="Headmaster&#39;s Office">office</a> and was kept safe within a glass case.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hunt_for_Horcruxes">Hunt for Horcruxes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hunt for Horcruxes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hunt for Horcruxes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows"><b>Rufus Scrimgeour</b>: "<i>Was it because Dumbledore believed that only the sword of Godric Gryffindor could defeat the Heir of Slytherin? <dd><b>Harry Potter</b>: "<i>Interesting theory.  Has anyone ever tried sticking a sword in Voldemort?</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Scrimgeour and the trio discussing the contents of Albus Dumbledore's will<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div><p>During the summer of 1996, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> used the sword on the <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_Ring" title="Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s Ring">Gaunt family ring</a>. The sword cracked the stone in the ring, thus destroying it as a Horcrux. It still worked as the <a href="/wiki/Resurrection_Stone" title="Resurrection Stone">Resurrection Stone</a>. Dumbledore tried to use it to talk to his deceased relatives, but failed.

<p>Before he died, Dumbledore entrusted the sword to Harry in his <a href="/wiki/The_Last_Will_and_Testament_of_Albus_Percival_Wulfric_Brian_Dumbledore" title="The Last Will and Testament of Albus Percival Wulfric Brian Dumbledore">will</a>. The group was caught and punished as a result. Following that incident, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> passed the counterfeit sword to <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>. Bellatrix then stored it in her <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank" title="Gringotts Wizarding Bank">Gringotts</a> vault alongside <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s cup">Helga Hufflepuff's cup</a>, one of Voldemort's Horcruxes.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Calamity">The Calamity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Calamity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Calamity"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the mysterious <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a> which affected the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding world</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, the Sword of Gryffindor was one of the numerous <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> items and artefacts that appeared scattered across the world, called <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundables</a> for which volunteer <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> had to deal with in order to uphold the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>Fashioned from pure silver, it is inset with rubies, the stone that represents <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> in the hour-glasses that count the house points at Hogwarts. <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor" title="Godric Gryffindor">Godric Gryffindor</a>'s name is engraved just beneath the hilt</i>"</span></dd>

<p>The Sword of Gryffindor is made of pure silver, and its hilt is set with egg-sized rubies — the gemstone that is used to symbolise the House of Gryffindor at Hogwarts. The full name of Godric Gryffindor is also engraved just beneath the hilt.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_properties">Magical properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Magical properties"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Much like a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>, the sword of Gryffindor appears to be almost sentient, responding to appeals for help by Gryffindor's chosen successors. Whenever a "true <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>" needs it, the Sword will let itself be pulled out of another of <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor" title="Godric Gryffindor">Godric Gryffindor</a>'s artefacts - the <a href="/wiki/Sorting_Hat" title="Sorting Hat">Sorting Hat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Ron_Weasley" class="mw-redirect" title="Ron Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> saved him from drowning, Harry believed that since it was Ron who recovered the sword it was Ron who had to use it because "Dumbledore had at least taught Harry something about certain kinds of magic, of the incalculable power of certain acts." In addition, the portrait of Dumbledore told <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> that the sword must be taken under conditions of need and valour which was why Snape put it into the frozen pond in the first place.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As <a href="/wiki/Goblin%27s_silver" class="mw-redirect" title="Goblin&#39;s silver">Goblin's silver</a>, it imbibes only substances which strengthens it, which can then be used against enemies. This means that it never requires cleaning. Due to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> killing <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Salazar Slytherin's Basilisk</a> with it, the sword is imbued with <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_venom" title="Basilisk venom">Basilisk venom</a> and can destroy <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<ul><li>Gryffindor's sword owes something to the legend of Excalibur, <a href="/wiki/Arthur" title="Arthur">Arthur</a>'s sword, as it had to be drawn from a rock by the rightful king as only a rightful Gryffindor can hold Gryffindor's sword.</li>
<li>The role of the sword in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)"><i>Chamber of Secrets</i> film</a> is much the same as in the book; however in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(PC,_Mac)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (PC, Mac)">PC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(PlayStation)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (PlayStation)">PS1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(PlayStation_2,_Xbox,_GameCube)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (PlayStation 2, Xbox, GameCube)">PS2</a> versions of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (video game)">video game</a> based on the film, the sword has a long range attack; a ball of light (PC), lightning/a curving beam of light (PS2/Xbox and Gamecube) or <a href="/wiki/Basilisk_venom" title="Basilisk venom">basilisk venom</a> which can be launched from the sword, though it in the case of the PS2, Xbox and Gamecube versions, the sword flies out of Harry's hands once the Basilisk is properly damaged with it (and the boss music will stop until the sword is picked up again but only for the latter two console versions).
<ul><li>In the PS1 version, however, the instead sword repels the venom like how shiny metal or a mirror would repel a laser, and the player has to direct the shot onto the basilisk's fangs. It is only in the GBC version of <i>Chamber of Secrets</i> that the sword has a physical, non-magical attack used.</li></ul></li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 (video game)">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 (video game)</a></i>, Voldemort casts a spell at Neville as soon as he pulls out the sword from the Sorting Hat. Neville then deflects Voldemort's spell using the sword, which then hits Bellatrix's stomach, causing her to scream in pain.</li>
<li>The sword is featured in the hand of the goblin <a href="/wiki/Griphook" title="Griphook">Griphook</a> on the front cover of the UK version of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>. The sword is also featured on the front cover of the German version of the book.</li>
<li>An illustration of the sword can also be seen being held by Dumbledore before Chapter Eighteen in the American version of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>. In this image, the sword has a curved blade, like a scimitar, while in the film it is straight, like a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arming_sword" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Arming sword">knight's sword</a>. On the American dust jacket of Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets, Harry has a sword in his belt, and this sword has a cup-hilt which guards the fingers, unlike the cross-hilt sword of the movies and the Chapter Eighteen illustration.</li>
<li>The sword was used twice in the series (by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>) to kill snakes: <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Basilisk">Salazar Slytherin's Basilisk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>, respectively. This may symbolise <a href="/wiki/Godric_Gryffindor" title="Godric Gryffindor">Godric Gryffindor</a>'s enmity with <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>, who was closely associated with serpents. This may also have been an homage to the courtly tales of heroes "slaying dragons with swords," including <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_George_and_the_Dragon" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Saint George and the Dragon">Saint George</a> and is also a clear reference to Beowulf where the protagonist uses various swords against monsters and demons in the Anglo-Saxon epic saga Beowulf.</li>
<li>It is interesting to note that the sword is the only one of the main relics of the Hogwarts Founders that is not a Horcrux but one of the few things that can destroy a Horcrux. <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Slytherin's Locket</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Hufflepuff's Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem" class="mw-redirect" title="Rowena Ravenclaw&#39;s Diadem">Ravenclaw's Diadem</a> are the other relics, but are also <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>.<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 164px"> 	<a <li>In the series the sword's size changes often, on the cover of the seventh novel it looks more like a large dagger than sword, as mentioned above it appears like a scimitar in the American version illustrations. In the films the handle appears to have a one handed handle but when Neville wields it, its handle is relatively long and is more a hand and a half. In the PS2 version of the game, it is as short as a short sword, while the PS1, Xbox and Gamecube versions have its length and design akin to a normal broadsword. Some fans theorise that it was enchanted to adjust its size appropriately to the worthy wielder's size and style of combat, single or double-handed. This has yet to be confirmed or discredited.</li>
<li>The sword is Matthew Lewis (actor who portrayed Neville Longbottom)'s favourite prop from the series.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Twice, the sword presented itself to a Gryffindor who doubted that they belonged. Harry Potter pulled the sword out of the Sorting Hat, when he was worrying that he should have been in Slytherin, and Neville Longbottom had an argument with the Sorting Hat; The hat wanted to put Neville in Gryffindor but Neville was scared and wanted to be put in Hufflepuff. Later, he pulled the sword out of the hat.</li>
<li>The sword, along with the <a href="/wiki/Sorting_Hat" title="Sorting Hat">Sorting Hat</a>, are the only remaining relics of the Hogwarts Founders, as the others were turned into Horcruxes by Voldemort and subsequently destroyed in an effort to defeat Voldemort. Both belonged to Godric Gryffindor.</li>
<li>Ironically, though the sword is a Gryffindor relic, it is made of silver which is a Slytherin colour. However, this is likely out of necessity as a solid gold sword would be far too heavy to lift, let alone wield. In addition, gold is also a rather unsuitable metal for a sword: besides its weight being unruly for a weapon, it is also an incredibly soft metal with a very low melting point; a sword of ordinary steel could easily damage or cleave right through it. Gold is so soft that a brick of it the size of a match box can be hammered down to cover an area the size of the average tennis court.</li>
<li>Its composition of silver is fitting for a sword used to slay dark creatures and destroy dark objects as silver is often used in mythology as a metal that can kill monsters such as Vampires, Werewolves, and more.</li>
<li>Judging from the era which it was supposedly made, it could be argued that the sword should have taken the appearance of a pattern-welded Anglo-Saxon sword, perhaps not dissimilar to the description of the poison-ivy bladed sword <i>Hrunting</i> in the Anglo-Saxon epic poem Beowulf.</li>
<li>It is also fitting as a magical weapon as silver is an incredible conductor of electricity which, like magic, is a form of energy, making the comparison all the more fitting.</li>
<li>In the film adaptation, the design of the Sword of Gryffindor has a blatant resemblance to one of the ceremonial swords of Napoleon Bonaparte. The only few differences would be the silver hilt, a label of Godric Gryffindor on the handle and the rubies on the pommel and the guard.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Interestingly, the sword's quality of imbibing only that which strengthens it corresponds with muggle philosopher Frederich Nieztche's famous quote, "That which does not kill me only makes me stronger." And this is very much the principle that the Sword of Gryffindor exemplifies.</li>
<li>Ironically, it also has qualities mentioned by Snape during a lecture on the nature of the Dark Arts, that they are mutating, ever-changing, indestructible. Funny that a sword that was used to destroy the darkest and foulest of magical objects would in and of itself be similar in nature to the forces it was being used to fight against, much like the classic saying, "Fight fire with fire." And this fits the sword due to it being the legacy of Gryffindor, whose house corresponds with the element of fire.</li>
<li>The sword's rubies and silver blade and possession of a man whose emblem is a lion and possesses red hair and lion-like features is reminiscent of the character Lion-O from the 80's cartoon series, Thundercats and the Sword of Omens. This, and also the fact that only the worthy are able to wield or summon each sword, and that they are used against an enemy with a serpent as their insignia and mottif, Voldemort and Mumm-Ra for Gryffindor students Harry Potter and Neville Longbottom, and Lion-O respectively.</li>
<li>The issue of the sword's ownership is discussed frequently in the series. As it is indeed rightful wizard property, the goblins have no claim to it. Hermione states to Scrimgeour that it belongs to Harry since it appeared to him in the Chamber of Secrets (along with the popular but incorrect fan theory that Harry is a descendant of Godric Gryffindor which technically would make it his) while Scrimgeour himself viewed it as an important historical artefact which is known to present itself to worthy Gryffindors but is not theirs automatically. As Gryffindor has no known descendants, the sword would technically be the property of Hogwarts along with the Sorting Hat.</li>
<li>A functional replica of the sword was made by the Stagmer Brothers of Baltimore Knife and Sword on the popular web series, Man at Arms: Reforged.</li>
<li>A fan theory states that Voldemort had the Sword when going to the Potter's house intending to use Harry's death to turn the Sword into a Horcrux just as he'd done with the other Hogwarts Founders relics (the Locket, the Cup, and the Diadem). However as Voldemort was defeated when trying to kill Harry, he was not able to turn the Sword into a Horcrux. The theory also states that after Voldemort's defeat, the Sword returned to the sorting hat as it was not found in the Potter's house in the aftermath. However this theory is not confirmed or hinted at during any point in the series.</li></ul>
<h3><span id="Author's_comments"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Author.27s_comments">Author's comments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_Gryffindor?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>"<i>The question of why a wizard would need a sword, though often asked, is easily answered. In the days before the International Statute of Secrecy, when wizards mingled freely with Muggles, they would use swords to defend themselves just as often as wands. Indeed, it was considered unsporting to use a wand against a Muggle sword (which is not to say it was never done). Many gifted wizards were also accomplished duellists in the conventional sense, Gryffindor among them."</i></li>
<li>"<i>There have been many enchanted swords in folklore. The Sword of Nuadu, part of the four legendary treasures of Tuatha Dé Danann, was invincible when drawn. Gryffindor's sword owes something to the legend of Excalibur, the sword of King Arthur, which in some legends must be drawn from a stone by the rightful king. The idea of fitness to carry the sword is echoed in the sword of Gryffindor's return to worthy members of its true owner's house</i>. <i>There is a further allusion to Excalibur emerging from the lake when Harry must dive into a frozen forest pool to retrieve the sword in Deathly Hallows (though the location of the sword was really due to a spiteful impulse of Snape's to place it there), for in other versions of the legend, Excalibur was given to Arthur by the Lady of the Lake, and was returned to the lake when he died.</i>"</li></ul>
<ul><li>"<i>Within the magical world, physical possession is not necessarily a guarantee of ownership. This concept applies to the three Deathly Hallows, and also to Gryffindor's sword. I am interested in what happens when cultural beliefs collide. In the Harry Potter books, the most militant of the goblin race consider all goblin-made objects to be theirs by right, although a specific object might be made over to a wizard for his life-span upon a payment of gold. Witches and wizards, like Muggles, believe that once payment has been made, the object belongs to them and their descendants or legatees in perpetuity. This is a clash of values without a solution, because each side has a different concept of what is right. It therefore presents Harry with a difficult moral dilemma when Griphook demands the sword as payment for his services in Deathly Hallows.</i>"</li></ul>
