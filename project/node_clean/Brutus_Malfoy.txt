<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Nothing is a surer sign of weak <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> than a weakness for <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">non-magical company</a></i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Brutus Malfoy, in the <a href="/wiki/1675" class="mw-redirect" title="1675">1675</a> periodical <i><a href="/wiki/Warlock_at_War" title="Warlock at War">Warlock at War</a></i><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Brutus Malfoy</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1675" class="mw-redirect" title="1675">1675</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who lived during the <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th century</a>. He edited the anti-<a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> periodical <i><a href="/wiki/Warlock_at_War" title="Warlock at War">Warlock at War</a></i>, and advocated the notion that <a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">witches and wizards who associated with Muggles</a> lacked magical talent, writing in <a href="/wiki/1675" class="mw-redirect" title="1675">1675</a>, "<i>Nothing is a surer sign of weak magic than a weakness for non-magical company.</i>"
</p><p>In Brutus Malfoy's lifetime, persecution of <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> and witches by <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> was at its height. Under these circumstances, anti-<a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> sentiments were widespread in the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding community</a>. Brutus himself perpetuated the prejudice that <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> who associated with <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> lacked intelligence and <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability.
</p><p>Brutus's descendants include later members of the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Abraxas_Malfoy" title="Abraxas Malfoy">Abraxas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scorpius_Malfoy" title="Scorpius Malfoy">Scorpius Malfoy</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Brutus_Malfoy?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Brutus_Malfoy?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Brutus" is a Roman cognomen meaning "heavy" in Latin. Famous bearers include Lucius Junius Brutus, the traditional founder of the Roman Republic, and Marcus Junius Brutus, the statesman who conspired to assassinate Julius Caesar.
</p>
