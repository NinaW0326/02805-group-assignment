<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Celestina Warbeck was born on <a href="/wiki/18_August" title="18 August">18 August</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/1917" title="1917">1917</a><sup id="cite_ref-FWC_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FWC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> father and a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> mother. Her father, a minor functionary in the <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Liaison_Office" title="Muggle Liaison Office">Muggle Liaison Office</a>, met her mother, a failed actress, when the latter was attacked by a <a href="/wiki/Lethifold" title="Lethifold">Lethifold</a> disguised as a stage curtain.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Celestina's musical talent was apparent even at an early age; in <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, the wizarding magazine <a href="/wiki/Spellbound_(magazine)" title="Spellbound (magazine)"><i>Spellbound</i></a> published an article about her talents when she was around ten years old, referring her as a child prodigy.<sup id="cite_ref-COG_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COG-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Disappointed to learn that there was no such thing as a wizarding stage school, <a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck%27s_mother" title="Celestina Warbeck&#39;s mother">Mrs Warbeck</a> reluctantly let her daughter go to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> (which she attended from <a href="/wiki/1928" title="1928">1928</a>-<a href="/wiki/1936" title="1936">1936</a> and was Sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a>), but frequently bombarded the school with letters to form a theatre club, choir and <a href="/wiki/Tap-dancing" title="Tap-dancing">dancing</a> class to showcase her daughter's innumerable talents.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_a_singer">Career as a singer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career as a singer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career as a singer"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Frequently appearing with a chorus of backing <a href="/wiki/Banshee" title="Banshee">banshees</a>, Celestina's concerts were justly famous. Three devoted fans were involved in a three-broom pile-up over <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> on the last night of her '<a href="/wiki/Flighty_Aphrodite" title="Flighty Aphrodite">Flighty Aphrodite</a>' tour.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Her tickets often went on sale in the black market for vastly inflated prices (one reason why <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a>, who listened to her songs every <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> could never see her favourite singer live).<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Celestina's personal life provided much fodder for the gossip columns of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>. Warbeck became romantically involved with one of her backing dancers early in her career. They married, but ended up divorcing after only a year. Some time later, she married her <a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck%27s_second_husband" title="Celestina Warbeck&#39;s second husband">manager</a>, and they had a <a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck%27s_son" title="Celestina Warbeck&#39;s son">son</a> together. However, after ten years of marriage, Warbeck left him in order to marry <a href="/wiki/Irving_Warble" title="Irving Warble">Irving Warble</a>, a music composer.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Celestina sometimes lent her name and talents to good causes, such as raising funds for <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a> with a recording of <a href="/wiki/Puddlemere_United" title="Puddlemere United">Puddlemere United</a>'s anthem <i>Beat Back Those Bludgers, Boys, and Chuck That Quaffle Here</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-DPN_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DPN-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> More controversially, Celestina was vocal in her disagreement when the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> sought to impose restrictions on how the wizarding community was allowed to celebrate <a href="/wiki/Hallowe%27en" title="Hallowe&#39;en">Hallowe'en</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-QTtA_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QTtA-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some of Celestina's best-known songs include <i><a href="/wiki/You_Charmed_the_Heart_Right_Out_of_Me" title="You Charmed the Heart Right Out of Me">You Charmed the Heart Right Out of Me</a></i> and <i>A Cauldron Full of Hot, Strong Love</i>. Her fans are usually older people who love her grandstanding style and powerful voice.<sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The late 20th-century album <i>You Stole My Cauldron but You Can't Have My Heart </i>was a massive global hit. She also featured heavily on shows at the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_Wireless_Network" title="Wizarding Wireless Network">Wizarding Wireless Network</a>, one such being the <i><a href="/wiki/Witching_Hour" title="Witching Hour">Witching Hour</a></i> hosted by <a href="/wiki/Glenda_Chittock" title="Glenda Chittock">Glenda Chittock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2003" title="2003">2003</a> she allowed a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">dog</a>-walker who accidentally took a <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">Portkey</a> to one of her concerts to join her on the stage for a duet of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Cauldron_Full_of_Hot,_Strong_Love" title="A Cauldron Full of Hot, Strong Love">A Cauldron Full of Hot, Strong Love</a></i>; while the <a href="/wiki/Memory_Charm" title="Memory Charm">Memory Charm</a> cast on him by a harassed <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> official appeared at first to have erased his memory of the incident, he since has written a popular song that greatly resembles Warbeck's hit, something she was not amused about.<sup id="cite_ref-Portkey_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Portkey-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a>, Celestina Warbeck travelled to the <a href="/wiki/Patagonian_Desert" title="Patagonian Desert">Patagonian Desert</a> to watch the games of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="2014 Quidditch World Cup">427th Quidditch World Cup</a>. Her presence, alongside other wizarding celebrities, was noted by reporter <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> to have caused "<i>flurries of excitement</i>", with crowd members scrambling for autographs and even attempting to cast <a href="/wiki/Bridging_Charm" title="Bridging Charm">Bridging Charms</a> to reach the VIP boxes.<sup id="cite_ref-PottermoreDP_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PottermoreDP-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Celestina's latest album was entitled <i><a href="/wiki/You_Stole_My_Cauldron_But_You_Can%27t_Have_My_Heart" title="You Stole My Cauldron But You Can&#39;t Have My Heart">You Stole My Cauldron But You Can't Have My Heart</a></i>. The promotional tour included a show in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>; fans desperate to arrive at this concert were involved in a three-broom crash.<sup id="cite_ref-DPN_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DPN-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pottermore_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Warbeck scheduled concerts in <a href="/wiki/Exmoor_Quidditch_Stadium" title="Exmoor Quidditch Stadium">Exmoor</a>, sponsored by <a href="/wiki/The_Sorcerer%27s_Saucepot" title="The Sorcerer&#39;s Saucepot">The Sorcerer's Saucepot</a>. Tickets were on sale for five <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_currency" title="Wizarding currency">Galleons</a> each, and fans had to book their <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">Portkeys</a> early.<sup id="cite_ref-JKRS_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRS-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Care_of_Magical_Creatures" title="Care of Magical Creatures">Care of Magical Creatures</a></b>: In her free time, Celestina enjoyed breeding <a href="/wiki/Crup" title="Crup">crups</a>, meaning she had a level of talent in the handling of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> creatures.<sup id="cite_ref-hobbies_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hobbies-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Singing skills</b>: Celestina was a highly popular and talented vocalist, having had a very lucrative career as a pop singer with an impressive discography, along with a huge fan base throughout the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, proving her abilities as a singer to have been quite legendary.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Celestina</i> possibly comes from the word <i>celestial</i>, meaning "pertaining to the sky or visible heaven." <i>Warbeck</i> may come from <i>warbler</i>, which is a singing <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>.
</p>

<h3><span id="Author's_comments"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Author.27s_comments">Author's comments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<i>Celestina is one of my favourite 'off-stage' characters in the whole series, and has been part of the Potter world ever since its inception, making an early appearance in the short-lived 'Daily Prophet' series I produced for members of the equally short-lived fan club run by my British publisher, Bloomsbury. Although we never lay eyes on Celestina during the whole seven volumes of the Potter books, I always imagined her to resemble Shirley Bassey in both looks and style. I stole her first name from a friend with whom I worked, years ago, at Amnesty International's Headquarters in London; 'Celestina' was simply begging to be scooped up and attached to a glamorous witch.</i>"
</p>
