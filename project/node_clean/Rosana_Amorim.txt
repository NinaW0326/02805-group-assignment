<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Rosana_Amorim?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Rosana_Amorim?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Rosana Amorim sounds like a Brazilian given name as this name and surname are very common in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, which may indicate the nationality of the author. Rosana is the agglutination of names Rosa (means "rose") and Ana (variant of "Anna"). Amorim is a surname with Latin origin and means "a place of lovers".</li></ul>
