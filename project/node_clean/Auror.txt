<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter: Wizards Unite">"<i>Aurors are the Wizarding World's best defence against the practitioners of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>. We're well-versed in <a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">offensive magic</a>, because our very lives depend on it. It takes courage, wit and guile. Not many have the stomach for it. Do you?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> addressing members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">S.O.S.</a> wanting to pursue a career as an Auror.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_Wizards_Unite" title="Harry Potter: Wizards Unite">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>An <b>Auror</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard or witch</a> who acted as highly trained law enforcement officials for magical governments. Auror training was extremely difficult and intensive, so there were few qualified applicants. Aurors of different countries dealt with different high-risk situations that were most prominent to them. Their training and areas of responsibility varied greatly depending on the type of threats for which they were prepared, and the magical education they received beforehand. In Britain, for example, the Aurors were trained to investigate crimes related to the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>, and to apprehend or detain <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">dark wizards and witches</a>. As a result, they were often called "Dark Wizard catchers".<sup id="cite_ref-GOF11_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF11-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the United States however, they seemed to go up against any criminal posing a significant threat. When working for the <a href="/wiki/Major_Investigation_Department" title="Major Investigation Department">Major Investigation Department</a>, Aurors used a number of magical tools and devices such as the <a href="/wiki/MACUSA_Real-Time_Hex_Indicator_map" title="MACUSA Real-Time Hex Indicator map">Real-Time Hex Indicator map</a> and the <a href="/wiki/MACUSA_USA_Spell_Contraventions_map" title="MACUSA USA Spell Contraventions map">USA Spell Contraventions map</a> to track down illegal or especially flamboyant use of magic that could pose a threat to the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a>. Also, unlike their British counterpart, the American Aurors were divided into several divisions around North America to keep the peace, as opposed to all working within the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">same one</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FBS_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aurors were, essentially, the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a> equivalent of police officers and military (as they served in both roles for <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizardkind</a>).
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, no Auror had been taken on by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> for three years prior to <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, though <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> stated that she qualified the year before, meaning she was probably one of the last candidates taken on. The head of the British <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a> (as of <a href="/wiki/2007" title="2007">2007</a>) was <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, but he left to become Head of <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a> by <a href="/wiki/2019" title="2019">2019</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CC1-5_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC1-5-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<dl><dd><div class="noprint"><i>Main article&#58; <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a></i></div>The British Auror headquarters were on Level Two of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, which was the level that housed the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a>. The Ministry was located underneath <a href="/wiki/Whitehall" title="Whitehall">Whitehall</a>, a road in Westminster in the heart of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The Auror Office consisted of a series of open cubicles, each Auror being given a place to work. Pictures of known Dark wizards, maps, clippings from the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>, and various other things lined the cubicles. When <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> visited the office in 1995 he was impressed by it.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP7_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP7-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_an_Auror">Becoming an Auror</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Becoming an Auror">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Becoming an Auror"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>You'd need top grades for that... They ask for a minimum of five <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.s</a>, and nothing under '<a href="/wiki/Exceeds_Expectations" title="Exceeds Expectations">Exceeds Expectations</a>' grade, I see. Then you would be required to undergo a stringent series of character and aptitude tests at the Auror office.  It's a difficult career path...  they only take the best.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Minerva McGonagall regarding the process of becoming an Auror<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Requirements">Requirements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Requirements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Requirements"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was very difficult to fulfil the requirements to get into Auror training. Applicants had to have excellent academic credentials, before they were accepted into a rigorous training programme. It is also possible that the wizarding governing bodies looked into the criminal records of the applicants, and that those without a clean one would not make it through the cut.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In accordance with British standards, one had to have a minimum of five N.E.W.T.s in challenging subjects. Such highly recommended subjects included: <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a> with top grades of either '<a href="/wiki/Outstanding" title="Outstanding">Outstanding</a>' or '<a href="/wiki/Exceeds_Expectations" title="Exceeds Expectations">Exceeds Expectations</a>' in the students' chosen subjects.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is not known if Herbology was always required, though it is possible, as Harry Potter continued to take it to <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a>-level as his fifth subject.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP9_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP9-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP11_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP11-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> It is also possible that any other subject could be substituted as the five, as long as the grades met the requirements. It is also possible that <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> didn't do quite as well in Herbology, even though she did gain A or O at O.W.L. level, because she did not do so at N.E.W.T. level, and ended up replacing Herbology with <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The requirements for applicants to become Aurors were temporarily relaxed following the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. With many Aurors destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, the need to restrengthen the department to combat the remaining Death Eaters was great. The new <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> and former Auror <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>, permitted any of-age participant in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> to become an Auror immediately if they were interested. He did this on the grounds that having fought in and survived such a battle and the Second Wizarding War in general, and not changing sides against such odds, more than satisfied the character and skill requirements. Some of these recruits, including <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, did not achieve any N.E.W.T.s (they had not attended and completed their final year at Hogwarts).
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980s</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Talbott_Winger" title="Talbott Winger">Talbott Winger</a> aspired to become an Auror after he graduated, achieving at least an A in <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> also aspired to become an Auror.<sup id="cite_ref-HM_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Training">Training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Training"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>I got top marks in Concealment and Disguise during Auror training without any study at all, it was great... Nearly failed on Stealth and Tracking.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> regarding Auror training<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>It was incredibly difficult to fulfil Auror training. If accepted into Auror training, prospective Aurors were required to undergo a stringent series of character and aptitude tests. These tests were done in order to show how they react well under pressure. Applicants were then required to train extensively in advanced <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">magical combat</a> and other elements of practical defence, and most likely methods of criminal investigation. Given that Harry, Ron and Neville became Aurors immediately after the Second Wizarding War, it is likely that they improved their capabilities in advanced defensive magic during the work hands-on.
</p><p>The known courses included in training were <a href="/wiki/Concealment_and_Disguise" title="Concealment and Disguise">Concealment and Disguise</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stealth_and_Tracking" title="Stealth and Tracking">Stealth and Tracking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_Instinct" title="Battle Instinct">Battle Instinct</a>, <a href="/wiki/Best-Laid_Plans" title="Best-Laid Plans">Best-Laid Plans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duelling_in_the_Dark" title="Duelling in the Dark">Duelling in the Dark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Field_Training" title="Field Training">Field Training</a>, <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Dark_Arts" title="History of the Dark Arts">History of the Dark Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Method_in_the_Mad-Eye" title="Method in the Mad-Eye">Method in the Mad-Eye</a>, <a href="/wiki/Resilience_Training" title="Resilience Training">Resilience Training</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Auror_Advantage" title="The Auror Advantage">The Auror Advantage</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-WU_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antidote" title="Antidote">antidotes</a> were also essential studies. <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> also mentioned that Aurors often <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfigured</a> and <a href="/wiki/Untransfiguration" title="Untransfiguration">Untransfigured</a> things in their line of work.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 1995, Tonks stated that she passed training without any difficulties on the 'Concealment and Disguise' portion, but nearly failed the stealth tests. Training lasted for three years and was very hard work.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_job">The job</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The job">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The job"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter: Wizards Unite">"<i>The job of Auror isn't for everyone, but I can say from personal experience that it just may be the best job you'll ever have. Or the worst. One or the other.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, Head of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_Wizards_Unite" title="Harry Potter: Wizards Unite">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>An Auror's job was to investigate crimes related to the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> and to hunt down and capture <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizards</a>, often with help from <a href="/wiki/Hit_Wizard" title="Hit Wizard">Hit Wizards</a>. Once a criminal was captured they were generally handed over to the authorities. It was not uncommon for criminals to resist arrest; some chose to fight to the death rather than let themselves be captured, such as <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> <a href="/wiki/Evan_Rosier" title="Evan Rosier">Evan Rosier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF30_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF30-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Ministry also enlisted Aurors to guard high profile targets, such as Harry Potter during the <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>-<a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> school term.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP8_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP8-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The British <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> had an Auror bodyguard, posing as his secretary, to protect him against the possibility that Lord Voldemort might take control of him to gain access to various <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> resources at the highest level.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reputation">Reputation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Reputation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reputation"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix"><b>Ron Weasley</b>: "<i>Well, it'd be cool to be an Auror,</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Harry Potter</b>: "<i>Yeah, it would,</i>"</dd>
<dd><b>Ron Weasley</b>: "<i>But they're, like, the elite,... You've got to be </i>really<i> good.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Discussion of future careers<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div><p>While the job was seen as glamorous by some, such as <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, due to the amount of danger involved and because it was extremely difficult to join their ranks, others did not think very highly of Aurors, such as <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a>, who considered Aurors to be "careerist hypocrites", and during the rally of <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> in <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, a vast majority of the attendees reacted with disdain and fearful muttering upon realising there were Aurors in their midst, which Grindelwald and those who subscribed to his <a href="/wiki/For_the_Greater_Good" title="For the Greater Good">ideology</a> regarded as something almost akin to paramilitary special forces of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">powers that were</a> under the direction of the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards" title="International Confederation of Wizards">ruling class</a> that hid behind their duty of upholding Wizarding law to maintain their mandatory and forcibly imposed <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">totalitarian regime</a> while justifying the squashing of any individual or group that they felt threatened by.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> This latter view might have softened a little, however, following the <a href="/wiki/Global_wizarding_war" title="Global wizarding war">Global wizarding war</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s defeat of Grindelwald in <a href="/wiki/1945" title="1945">1945</a>, given the number of Aurors who laid down their lives to do the same thing.
</p><p>Though Auror training was very taxing, and meant an additional three years of studying even after graduation from school, by the time of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, they seemed to be very well respected in the wizarding community, at least in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>, where and higher-ranking members of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a> sometimes went on to become potential candidates for the job of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>. For example, <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a> was a leader of the Auror Office, and replaced <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Fudge</a> as Minister after the call of his resignation.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP3_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP3-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Another example of a respected Auror (though not Head) that became Minister was <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>. Harry Potter was eventually promoted to the Head of the Auror Office after serving nine years as an Auror.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="US_divisions">US divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: US divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: US divisions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d8/MACUSA_-_Always_Stay_Vigilant.png/revision/latest?cb=20161020203229" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/d8/MACUSA_-_Always_Stay_Vigilant.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20161020203229" decoding="async" width="250" height="199" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="MACUSA - Always Stay Vigilant.png" data-image-key="MACUSA_-_Always_Stay_Vigilant.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:MACUSA_-_Always_Stay_Vigilant.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">MACUSA - <i>Always Stay Vigilant</i></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>The American Aurors were also militarised to a greater extent than their British counterparts, carrying ranks such as "chief" and "Captain"<sup id="cite_ref-source_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-source-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>, which may give some insight into how these divisions went about their work and how it differed from that of the British <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>.
</p><p>An <a href="/wiki/US_Auror_Divisions" title="US Auror Divisions">Auror Division</a> was a field office of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Aurors" title="Department of Aurors">Department of Aurors</a> located throughout <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, specifically tasked with the capture of dangerous criminals within the jurisdiction of their designated areas. The divisions reported back to their home office at the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement_(MACUSA)" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement (MACUSA)">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Woolworth_Building" title="Woolworth Building">Woolworth Building</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>, the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a>.
</p><p>Each division presumably worked in collaboration with local offices of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Covert_Vigilance_and_No-Maj_Obliviation" title="Federal Bureau of Covert Vigilance and No-Maj Obliviation">Federal Bureau of Covert Vigilance and No-Maj Obliviation</a> across North America. If their splitting into multiple divisions is any indication, the Aurors of North America were a much more numerous, organised and meticulously structured group than those in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, likely out of necessity to keep order across the large continent. In other words, should a suspect or escaped convict manage to slip through the fingers of the Aurors in one place, the Auror Division in the area to which the criminal in question is believed to have fled would be contacted and would take over from there.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Aurors">British Aurors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: British Aurors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: British Aurors"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Minister <a href="/wiki/Eldritch_Diggory" title="Eldritch Diggory">Eldritch Diggory</a> first established an Auror recruitment programme for Great Britain in the <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>, and by <a href="/wiki/1752" class="mw-redirect" title="1752">1752</a> some of the earliest members of the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a> were already actively pursuing <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark witches and wizards</a>, prominent among them <a href="/wiki/Hesphaestus_Gore" title="Hesphaestus Gore">Hesphaestus Gore</a>, who would eventually be elected <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_to_mid-_20th_century">Early to mid- <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early to mid- 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Early to mid- 20th century"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although Aurors working for the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> would usually only work domestically and keep themselves within the boundaries of the Ministry's jurisdiction, they would occasionally be sent abroad during extraordinary circumstances, presumably after being granted prior permission to operate there from local magical authorities. Notably, some Aurors (including <a href="/wiki/Theseus_Scamander" title="Theseus Scamander">Theseus Scamander</a>) were sent into war zones to represent their country's interests at the field of battle, such as was the case with the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>. Scamander, who would later go on to be appointed Head of the British Auror Office, famously participated in the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> effort in the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>, carving out for himself a well-deserved reputation as a war hero through his courage and skill during the war<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>. He was later sent abroad by his superiors to assist in the manhunt for the dark wizard <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> during the early days of his <a href="/wiki/Global_wizarding_war" title="Global wizarding war">For the Greater Good Revolution</a>, a task that the whole Auror Office had been coveting at the time.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, Theseus corresponded with <a href="/wiki/Percival_Graves" title="Percival Graves">Percival Graves</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Director_of_Magical_Security" title="Director of Magical Security">Director of Magical Security</a> of <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a>. He corresponded with Graves regarding the events in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> due to his younger <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">brother's</a> involvement. The following year, he and other Aurors were present at the <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald%27s_Paris_rally" title="Gellert Grindelwald&#39;s Paris rally">rally</a> of Grindelwald in the <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Mausoleum" title="Lestrange Mausoleum">Lestrange Mausoleum</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Cimeti%C3%A8re_du_P%C3%A8re-Lachaise" title="Cimetière du Père-Lachaise">Cimetière du Père-Lachaise</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and fought Grindelwald himself after the rally was broken up, where many of them were killed. During Grindelwald's speech, it was revealed that Aurors had fought and killed many of his followers prior to that point in time, although it's uncertain how many of these, if any, were from the British Auror Office.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>

<dd></dd></dl></div><p>In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, after the destruction of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a> became <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> and led a massive reform of the Ministry. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, at age 17, and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, at age 18, joined the Auror Office and helped revolutionise the department. The office was thoroughly reshuffled and corruption was weeded out. In due time, Harry and Ron were considered to be "experts".<sup id="cite_ref-2007CHAT_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2007CHAT-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2007" title="2007">2007</a>, at the young age of 26 or 27, Harry became the Head of the Auror Office.<sup id="cite_ref-WOTM_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WOTM-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Harry was sporting a nasty cut over his right cheekbone. <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>'s requests for information as to its provenance merely produced the usual response from the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>: "<i>We do not comment on the top secret work of the Auror department, as we have told you no less than 514 times, Ms. Skeeter</i>".
</p><p>When, In <a href="/wiki/2019" title="2019">2019</a>, the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> became the World Centre for containing the threat of <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">The Calamity</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a> ministered the <a href="/wiki/Auror_training" title="Auror training">subscribed training programme</a> to the members of the then recently established <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> who were interested in pursuing a career as an Auror. Though undoubtedly tasked with tracking down and rounding up the reappearing <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-WU2_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU2-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> the Ministry Aurors also worked to help contain the current catastrophe, as Aurors, such as <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, were known to be working towards that end as well.<sup id="cite_ref-WU1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU1-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry remained Head of the office until he was promoted to Head of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a> by the summer of 2020. It is unknown who succeeded him.
</p><p>In August 2020, after hearing a rumour about an illegal <a href="/wiki/Time-Turner" title="Time-Turner">Time-Turner</a>, Harry and his team of Aurors from the Department of Magical Law Enforcement raided the Nott residence and confiscated it. Harry then turned it over to <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, the Minister for Magic, for safekeeping.
</p>

<p>The first President of MACUSA was <a href="/wiki/Josiah_Jackson" title="Josiah Jackson">Josiah Jackson</a>, a warlike wizard who was voted into post by his fellow representatives because he was considered tough enough to deal with the difficulties of the post-<a href="/wiki/Salem_Witch_Trials" title="Salem Witch Trials">Salem Witch Trials</a> era. President Jackson’s immediate priority was to recruit and train Aurors. The names of the first dozen volunteers to train as Aurors in the US had a special place in <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a>’ wizarding history. There were so few of them, and the challenges they faced so great, that they knew they might be required to lay down their lives when they took the job. The descendants of these witches and wizards were given particular respect in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-macusa_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macusa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>Of these twelve, only two survived into old age: <a href="/wiki/Charity_Wilkinson" title="Charity Wilkinson">Charity Wilkinson</a>, who would become MACUSA’s third President, and <a href="/wiki/Theodard_Fontaine" title="Theodard Fontaine">Theodard Fontaine</a>, whose direct descendant Agilbert is the present day Headmaster of <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Also of note are <a href="/wiki/Gondulphus_Graves" title="Gondulphus Graves">Gondulphus Graves</a>, whose family remains influential in American wizarding politics, and Abraham Potter, whose distant relationship to the famous <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> would be uncovered by eager genealogists centuries later.<sup id="cite_ref-macusa_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-macusa-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Aurors">European Aurors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=22" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: European Aurors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: European Aurors"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a> and throughout the entirety of the <a href="/wiki/Global_wizarding_war" title="Global wizarding war">Global wizarding war</a>, many Aurors all over Europe were involved in the effort to put an end to <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>'s seemingly unstoppable rise to power as the revolutionary endeavours of he and his <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald%27s_army" class="mw-redirect" title="Gellert Grindelwald&#39;s army">supporters</a> became increasingly aggressive and led to a number of much publicised attacks, and worked together with each other to track down Grindelwald. They also co-operated with the <a href="/wiki/International_Wizarding_Police" title="International Wizarding Police">International Wizarding Police</a> to accomplish this. By <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, numerous Aurors had already fought and killed many of his <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald%27s_army" class="mw-redirect" title="Gellert Grindelwald&#39;s army">followers</a>, but also suffered losses in the conflict as well, with a number of Aurors having been killed in duels with Grindelwald himself. Grindelwald's charisma and idealism also led some Aurors to become seduced by what he stood for and to become supporters of him as well.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=27" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Auror" may be derived from "aurora", meaning "the dawn", from the Latin word "aurum", meaning "gold", or from the Hebrew word "Arur" (ארור), meaning someone who is cursed.
</p><p>Most likely, however, it is derived from the Latin word for ear, 'auris' and the related adjective, 'aural'. Since aurors are detectives (among other policing and justicial roles), they are those who listen for and receive information. Alternatively, if it is derived from "aurora" the name is possibly a metaphor based on the fact that Aurors are notorious Dark Wizard hunters. Indeed, after the night and the darkness it brings always come the aurora/dawn which always end it and restore light.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Auror?veaction=edit&amp;section=28" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Auror?action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Aurors can easily be comparable to elite Muggle national law enforcement officials, intelligence operatives, or military units such as the MI5, MI6, SAS, SBS, RAF Regiment, etc. Some require excellent academic credentials (but not all), have an exhausting selection process to weed out the weak, undergo extensive advance training (sometimes up to a year or two to be operationally ready in the field), and are employed for the most dangerous missions/criminals. And like many units in real-life, the Auror office is very selective based on character traits. An example would be how the American agency known as the CIA has often taken into account a potential candidate's personal history and moral compass through a series of intense psychological screening processes. Given this, there is likely a Wizarding equivalent of such screening in the process of becoming an Auror.</li>
<li>Harry's goal to be an Auror is only briefly mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, despite the Careers Advice scene being omitted from the previous film.</li>
<li>In the films, Aurors tend to wear brown trench coats, possibly as a uniform. Kingsley Shacklebolt is the only Auror seen on-duty without one.</li></ul>
