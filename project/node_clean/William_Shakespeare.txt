<b>William Shakespeare</b> (<a href="/wiki/23_April" title="23 April">23 April</a>, <a href="/wiki/1564" class="mw-redirect" title="1564">1564</a>â€”<a href="/wiki/23_April" title="23 April">23 April</a>, <a href="/wiki/1616" class="mw-redirect" title="1616">1616</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> poet and playwright, best known for his works of comedy, tragedy and romance.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During his lifetime, he wrote <i><a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It" title="As You Like It">As You Like It</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night&#39;s Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Night" title="Twelfth Night">Twelfth Night</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Coriolanus" title="Coriolanus">Coriolanus</a></i>, among many others. He lived during <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elizabethan_period" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Elizabethan period">Elizabethan times</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> as a whole were prejudiced against <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> and often organised massive witchhunts, partly inspired by regal paranoia.
</p><p>There is a bust of Shakespeare on the pediment of <a href="/wiki/Wyndham%27s_Theatre" title="Wyndham&#39;s Theatre">Wyndham's Theatre</a>, depicting him flanked by muses and <a href="/wiki/Cherub" title="Cherub">cherubs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The lyrics of <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Trouble" title="Double Trouble">Something Wicked This Way Comes</a></i>, as heard in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, were taken from Shakespeare's <i>Macbeth</i>. Thus, Shakespeare is credited with the lyrics. Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> series</a> author <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has stated "I absolutely adore <i>Macbeth</i>. It is possibly my favourite Shakespeare play."<sup id="cite_ref-leakymugglenet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leakymugglenet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the 2011 film <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anonymous_(film)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Anonymous (film)">Anonymous</a></i>, Shakespeare is portrayed by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rafe_Spall" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Rafe Spall">Rafe Spall</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Spall" title="Timothy Spall">Timothy Spall</a> (<a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>). In this film Shakespeare is portrayed as a drunken, inarticluate buffoon, and a fraud. The film identifies <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_de_Vere,_17th_Earl_of_Oxford" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Edward de Vere, 17th Earl of Oxford">Edward de Vere, 17th Earl of Oxford</a>, as the true author of Shakespeare's alleged works. de Vere is portrayed in the film by <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Campbell_Bower" title="Jamie Campbell Bower">Jamie Campbell Bower</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>) as a young man and <a href="/wiki/Rhys_Ifans" title="Rhys Ifans">Rhys Ifans</a> (<a href="/wiki/Xenophilius_Lovegood" title="Xenophilius Lovegood">Xenophilius Lovegood</a>) as an older man. <a href="/wiki/David_Thewlis" title="David Thewlis">David Thewlis</a> (<a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a>) plays <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Cecil,_1st_Baron_Burghley" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William Cecil, 1st Baron Burghley">William Cecil, 1st Baron Burghley</a>. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Lloyd-Hughes" title="Henry Lloyd-Hughes">Henry Lloyd-Hughes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Roger_Davies" title="Roger Davies">Roger Davies</a>) appears as a bear baiter.</li>
<li>In 2011 <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" title="Ralph Fiennes">Ralph Fiennes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>) made his directorial debut with a modern-day adaptation of Shakespeare's <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coriolanus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Coriolanus">Coriolanus</a></i>, in which he also played the title role.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has referred to the <a href="/wiki/Encyclopaedia_of_Potterworld" title="Encyclopaedia of Potterworld">Harry Potter Encyclopedia</a> as "The Scottish Book" which is a reference to Shakespeare's play <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macbeth" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Macbeth">Macbeth</a></i>, which is sometimes referred to as "The Scottish Play".<sup id="cite_ref-J._K._Rowling.27s_Official_Site_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-J._K._Rowling.27s_Official_Site-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Branagh" title="Kenneth Branagh">Kenneth Branagh</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Emma_Thompson" title="Emma Thompson">Emma Thompson</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Sybill Trelawney</a>) both appear in Branagh's 1989 adaptation of <i>Henry V</i>, which also features <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Coltrane" title="Robbie Coltrane">Robbie Coltrane</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>) as Falstaff in a cameo appearance.</li>
<li>In&#160;the <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>&#160;film adaptation of Shakespeare's <i>Hamlet</i>, Branagh plays the title character Hamlet,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Ray_Fearon" title="Ray Fearon">Ray Fearon</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a>) plays Francisco, <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Spall" title="Timothy Spall">Timothy Spall</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Wormtail</a>) plays Rosencrantz, and <a href="/wiki/Julie_Christie" title="Julie Christie">Julie Christie</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Rosmerta" title="Rosmerta">Rosmerta</a>) plays Gertrude.&#160;Branagh, also, was portrays characters in other Shakespeare films -- <i>Much Ado About Nothing </i>(Benedick) and <i>Othello </i>(Iago), for example.</li>
<li>In <i>Much Ado About Nothing</i>, Thompson plays Beatrice, Benedick's love interest. This is ironic, as Branagh and Thompson were actually married at the time. <a href="/wiki/Imelda_Staunton" title="Imelda Staunton">Imelda Staunton</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>) also makes an appearance in this film as Margaret.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>&#160;film adaptation of <i>Hamlet</i>, <a href="/wiki/Helena_Bonham_Carter" title="Helena Bonham Carter">Helena Bonham Carter</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>) plays Ophelia.</li>
<li>Both Branagh and Spall return in <i>Love's Labour's Lost</i>, as Berowne and Don Armado respectively.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/1965" title="1965">1965</a>&#160;adaptation of <i>Othello</i>, where <a href="/wiki/Michael_Gambon" title="Michael Gambon">Michael Gambon</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>)&#160;made his&#160;film debut,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Maggie_Smith" title="Maggie Smith">Maggie Smith</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>) plays Desdemona. Gambon also made his TV debut in a Shakespeare work, <i>Much Ado About Nothng</i>. Smith returned to Shakespeare thirty years later, in <i>Richard III</i>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/2009" title="2009">2009</a>&#160;adaptation of <i>Hamlet</i>, <a href="/wiki/David_Tennant" title="David Tennant">David Tennant</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Bartemius Crouch, Jnr</a>) stars as the title character, Hamlet.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" title="Ralph Fiennes">Ralph Fiennes</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>) acted in several stage productions of Shakespeare's work, including <i>A Midsummer Night's Dream </i>and <i>Romeo and Juliet</i>.</li>
<li>In the TV miniseries adaptation of <i>Julius Caesar</i>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Harris" title="Richard Harris">Richard Harris</a>&#160;(Dumbledore) plays Lucius Sulla. It should be noted that this was one of the last few films of Harris before his death.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alan_Rickman" title="Alan Rickman">Alan Rickman</a>&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>) made his film debut in the <a href="/wiki/1978" title="1978">1978</a>&#160;film adaptation of <i>Romeo and Juliet</i>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "William" comes from the Germanic name Willahelm, which was composed of the elements wil "will, desire" and helm "helmet, protection".
</p>
