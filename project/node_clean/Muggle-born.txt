<p><b>Muggle-born</b> (<b>No-Maj-born</b> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> once known as <b>Magbobs</b> and also known by the pejorative <b><a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudblood</a></b>,<sup id="cite_ref-PM-pureblood_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-pureblood-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> are expressions referring to <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches or wizards</a> who are born to two <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">non-magical parents</a>. Their <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> abilities do not seem to be at all affected by their Muggle parentage. On the contrary, many Muggle-borns have been among the most talented witches and wizards of their age, such as <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Evans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>.
</p><p>The proportion of the wizarding population that is Muggle-born is on the rise as the <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> families shrink in size and number.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_magical_abilities">Origin of magical abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Origin of magical abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin of magical abilities"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a>-borns inherit <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> from a distant ancestor; they are descended from <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squibs</a> who have married <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and whose families had lost the knowledge of their wizarding legacy. The magic resurfaces unexpectedly many generations later.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because of the heritable traits of magic, Muggle-born siblings are possible, as in the case of <a href="/wiki/Colin_Creevey" title="Colin Creevey">Colin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Creevey" title="Dennis Creevey">Dennis Creevey</a>. However, this is not always the case, as Lily Evans' sister <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia</a> was a Muggle.
</p><p>When Muggle-born witches and wizards reach the age of eleven in the British wizarding community, their <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_acceptance_letter" title="Hogwarts acceptance letter">Hogwarts acceptance letters</a> are delivered in person by <a href="/wiki/Special_messenger" title="Special messenger">a member of the staff</a>, instead of by <a href="/wiki/Owl_Postal_Service" title="Owl Postal Service">owl post</a> (the usual postal system for wizards and witches). The purpose is to reassure the parents or guardians about the sudden news, and explain to them about the concealed magical society. They would also assist the family with the preparation for going to their desired wizarding school and for the buying of school supplies.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatment">Treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Treatment"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">"<i>No one asked your opinion, you filthy little Mudblood.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;An example of wizarding prejudice<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Muggle-born witches and wizards are often subjects of deeply prejudicial and discriminatory beliefs akin to racism. Derided by <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-bloods</a> and called disparaging names such as "<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudblood</a>", a term implying that they are somehow dirty and impure, or at the very least, common. Many pure-bloods believe that Muggle-borns are unworthy of magic and should not be allowed into the wizarding world. During the period of time where Muggle-borns were being persecuted, a news article from the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> stated that Muggle-borns could have only obtained magic by force or theft.
</p><p>Those who do not share these beliefs, such as the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a>, are often labelled as "<a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitors</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-CS_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> who was half-blood was marked as "Undesirable No.1" during the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> for his pro Muggle views and his stance against Lord Voldemort and his new regime.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> described the entire prejudice as being ridiculous: not only were most modern wizards half-blood, if Muggle-borns had not been accepted into the wizard community, wizardkind would have inevitably died out altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_persecution">History of persecution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History of persecution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History of persecution"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Muggle-borns are being rounded up as we speak... unless you can prove that you have at least one close wizarding relative, you are now deemed to have obtained your magical power illegally and must suffer the punishment.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Ministry of Magic's new regime<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>, one of the founders of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, argued that the school should only admit pure-bloods as students, as <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang Institute</a> does. Slytherin's views were considered unusual at the time; most wizards of his era not only accepted Muggle-borns but actually considered them especially gifted, and referred to them by the affectionate term "<a href="/wiki/Magbob" title="Magbob">Magbobs</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-PM-pureblood_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-pureblood-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The other three founders of Hogwarts favoured admitting all witches and wizards as students and opposed Slytherin on this point.
</p><p>As a result of the disagreement, Slytherin left the school. Before leaving, he created the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>, hoping that his true heir would one day release the Basilisk out of the Chamber and kill wizards and witches he thought didn't deserve to have access to magic (particularly Muggle-borns). About one thousand years later, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Marvolo Riddle</a>, having discovered his ancestry, went on to set the basilisk on his fellow Hogwarts students. He was finally successful in killing one fourteen year old Muggle-born student, named <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren" title="Myrtle Warren">Myrtle Warren</a>, in 1943. She later became to be known as Moaning Myrtle and lived in the girls bathroom for years after her death.Fifty years later in 1992, by means of his diary Horcrux, he possessed <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> and reopened the Chamber of Secrets. The monster petrified several people, including <a href="/wiki/Colin_Creevey" title="Colin Creevey">Colin Creevey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penelope_Clearwater" title="Penelope Clearwater">Penelope Clearwater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Finch-Fletchley" title="Justin Finch-Fletchley">Justin Finch-Fletchley</a>, and even the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_de_Mimsy-Porpington" title="Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington">Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington</a>, as well as Filch's cat, Mrs Norris.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tom Riddle, later known as Lord Voldemort, took this prejudice to an even greater extreme with his followers, the Death Eaters, despite the fact that he himself was a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 157px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b7/Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png/revision/latest?cb=20150623200416" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="155" height="249" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Mudbloods Pamplet Pottermore.png" data-image-key="Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b7/Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/155?cb=20150623200416" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b7/Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png/revision/latest?cb=20150623200416" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b7/Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/155?cb=20150623200416" decoding="async" width="155" height="249" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Mudbloods Pamplet Pottermore.png" data-image-key="Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/b7/Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/155?cb=20150623200416" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Mudbloods_Pamplet_Pottermore.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><i><a href="/wiki/Mudbloods_and_the_Dangers_They_Pose_to_a_Peaceful_Pure-Blood_Society" title="Mudbloods and the Dangers They Pose to a Peaceful Pure-Blood Society">Mudbloods and the Dangers They Pose to a Peaceful Pure-Blood Society</a></i></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>When Voldemort seized control of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> in 1997, Muggle-borns were required to register with the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a>. Political propaganda claimed that Muggle-borns were really Muggles who had stolen magic from "real" witches and wizards, supported by research supposedly carried out by the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a>. The Ministry continued to promote the belief with the distribution of agitprop such as the pamphlet <i>Mudbloods and the Dangers They Pose to a Peaceful Pure-Blood Society</i>. The Commission punished anyone who could not prove to have wizarding heritage for this alleged action, sentencing them to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. Anyone who resisted was threatened with, and perhaps given, the <a href="/wiki/Dementor%27s_Kiss" title="Dementor&#39;s Kiss">Dementor's Kiss</a>. They also issued two <a href="/wiki/Ministerial_Decree" title="Ministerial Decree">Ministerial Decrees</a> stating that it was any wizards' duty to report suspected "Mudbloods" to them and that failure to do so would result in imprisonment.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This led some Muggle-borns, such as <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Cresswell" title="Dirk Cresswell">Dirk Cresswell</a>, to forge their family trees. Those who refused to register, such as <a href="/wiki/Edward_Tonks" title="Edward Tonks">Ted Tonks</a>, were forced to go on the run and were pursued by <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatchers</a>, sometimes fatally.
</p><p>This was ended with Voldemort's final defeat in 1998 and the reform of the Ministry under new <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>. Hermione Granger would be crucial in eliminating pro-pure-blood laws as a high-ranking member of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subtler_prejudice">Subtler prejudice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Subtler prejudice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Subtler prejudice"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>There are also indications that a subtler, less virulent form of prejudice against Muggle-borns is relatively common in the wizarding world. For example, <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> discriminated more on the grounds of talent or fame, rather than <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood status</a>. Thus he included talented Muggle-borns such as <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, Lily Evans, and <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Cresswell" title="Dirk Cresswell">Dirk Cresswell</a> among his favourite students.
</p><p>However, Slughorn generally expected his more talented students to be <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-bloods</a>, and was surprised when Muggle-borns performed very well. When he remarked upon this to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in 1996 and Harry responded coldly, Slughorn was genuinely surprised, and insisted that he was not prejudiced.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This may indicate that even among those who do not believe Muggle-borns are inherently inferior or "dirty", there tends to be a false belief that most Muggle-borns are less <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magically</a> talented than those with a full or much recent wizarding heritage. However, Slughorn could have also meant that since Muggle-borns are not raised around magic, it is all the more astonishing when they do very well in school.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> also seemed to have mildly favoured pure-bloods for many years before it was reformed after the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, as there were still "pro-pure-blood laws" in existence, which Hermione later eliminated. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> also once accused <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> himself of placing too much importance on blood purity, as Fudge was sided more with old, wealthy and influential pure-blood families such as the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoys</a>, and looked down upon those who had less wealth and more affiliation with <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>, like the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasleys</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was a widely held belief in the wizarding world that Muggle-borns were more likely to produce Squib children and slower to show signs of magic in childhood than pure or half-blood wizards. These stereotypes were disproven by the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Muggle_Studies" title="Institute of Muggle Studies">Institute of Muggle Studies</a>, though they also disproved the positive beliefs that Muggle-borns have greater immunity to wizarding illnesses and a natural sense of rhythm.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Muggle-born?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Abbott" title="Hannah Abbott">Hannah Abbott</a> was originally listed as a Muggle-born on <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s draft list of students in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s class.<sup id="cite_ref-HPM_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPM-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> However, Rowling stated in an <a href="/wiki/J.K._Rowling_PotterCast_Interview" title="J.K. Rowling PotterCast Interview">interview</a> that she always considered Hannah to be a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a>. To compromise, she was officially made a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottercast_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottercast-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Another student mentioned as being Muggle-born on Rowling's draft class list, which is not considered <a href="/wiki/Canon" title="Canon">canon</a> due to several contradictions with the novels, is <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Entwhistle" title="Kevin Entwhistle">Kevin Entwhistle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HPM_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPM-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Terry_Boot" title="Terry Boot">Terry Boot</a> was listed as a Muggle-born on Rowling's draft class list,<sup id="cite_ref-HPM_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPM-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> but in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, he was shown to be attending his seventh year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> despite Muggle-borns being banned from the school while <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> was in power. This suggests that he is either a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>, though it is possible that he is a Muggle-born who faked his family tree.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a> was never certain whether he was Muggle-born or not, since his <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas%27s_father" title="Dean Thomas&#39;s father">father</a> left his <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas%27s_mother" title="Dean Thomas&#39;s mother">mother</a> when he was very young. His father was in fact a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>, thus Dean is a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>, but Dean was not able to prove it, which made him a target of the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snatchers" title="Snatchers">Snatchers</a> during the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. His Father was also killed for refusing to join the Death Eaters which would not have made him safe with the Death Eaters even if he could prove his heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A rumour on the <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">W.O.M.B.A.T.</a> was that Muggle-borns have a natural sense of rhythm.</li>
<li>According to J.K Rowling, a Muggle-born can become a Death Eater in rare circumstances.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
