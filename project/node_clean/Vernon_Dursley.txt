<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>I’m warning you, I’m warning you now, boy — any funny business, anything at all — and you’ll be in that cupboard from now until <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>.</i>"</span></dd>

<p><b>Vernon Dursley</b> was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a>, husband of <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia Evans</a>, father of <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley</a> and uncle-in-law of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. He used to live at <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a> along with his wife and son for twenty years until they were forced into hiding during the escalation of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. After Petunia's sister <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily</a> and brother-in-law, <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a>, were killed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> in their own home, Petunia remained the sole surviving blood relative of Harry's. Both Wizard and Muggle law demanded that Petunia and Vernon be the new legal guardians of Harry Potter, which they accepted (albeit reluctantly). However, they disliked Harry and treated him badly, while spoiling and fattening Dudley.
</p><p>When Harry was sent a letter from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, Vernon got very angry, as he detested anything to do with <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>. He was a former student of <a href="/wiki/Smeltings_Academy" title="Smeltings Academy">Smeltings Academy</a> and a director of the <a href="/wiki/Grunnings" title="Grunnings">Grunnings</a> drill manufacturing company. In <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, Vernon, Petunia and Dudley left their house in order to be hidden from Lord Voldemort and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> (by the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>.)
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Vernon had at least two <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_children" title="Dudley Dursley&#39;s children">grandchildren</a> through Dudley and his <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_wife" title="Dudley Dursley&#39;s wife">wife</a>. It is possible that he had a better relationship with Harry and that they remained in contact. Through the union of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginevra Weasley</a>, Vernon had two grandnephews-in-law, <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_II" title="James Potter II">James Potter II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a>, as well as a grandniece-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Lily_Potter" title="Lily Potter">Lily Potter</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Little is known about Vernon's childhood life. He was born into the Muggle <a href="/wiki/Dursley_family" title="Dursley family">Dursley family</a> and attended <a href="/wiki/Smeltings_Academy" title="Smeltings Academy">Smeltings Academy</a> as a secondary school.<sup id="cite_ref-PS3_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Vernon had an older sister named Marjorie (Marge to family members and friends) who made a living breeding bulldogs.<sup id="cite_ref-POA2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vernon started working for <a href="/wiki/Grunnings" title="Grunnings">Grunnings</a> in <a href="/wiki/1968" title="1968">1968</a> or earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-GrunningsAward_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrunningsAward-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vernon met <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia Evans</a> at an office job in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> where he was a junior executive.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He later married Petunia and settled with her at <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a> in <a href="/wiki/Little_Whinging" title="Little Whinging">Little Whinging</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a> in <a href="/wiki/1977" title="1977">1977</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Before their wedding, Vernon had taken Petunia on a series of dull dates during which he talked mainly about himself and his predictable ideas on the world. After some time Vernon proposed marriage to Petunia, very correctly on one knee in his mother's sitting room. His fiancée accepted at once. He wasn't aware of the fact that Petunia's sister Lily was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, who was then attending her seventh year at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Petunia eventually revealed to him that her sister was a witch after they stopped for a snack of battered sausage at a <a href="/wiki/Chip_shop" title="Chip shop">chip shop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family_life">Marriage and family life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Marriage and family life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage and family life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>Mr and <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Mrs Dursley</a>, of number four, Privet Drive, were proud to say that they were perfectly normal, thank you very much. They were the last people you'd expect to be involved in anything strange or mysterious, because they just didn't hold with such nonsense.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description of the Dursleys<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>}Vernon, along with his wife, always liked to be normal and never tolerated anything that they considered out of the ordinary. He knew that Petunia's sister was a witch, but kept it quiet. They had a baby boy on <a href="/wiki/23_June" title="23 June">23 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a>. This boy, <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley Dursley</a>, was highly spoiled by his parents.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Giving_a_home_to_Harry_Potter">Giving a home to Harry Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Giving a home to Harry Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Giving a home to Harry Potter"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/bc/Baby_Harry_Left_At_Privet_Drive.png/revision/latest?cb=20180606153618" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/b/bc/Baby_Harry_Left_At_Privet_Drive.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180606153618" decoding="async" width="250" height="104" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Baby Harry Left At Privet Drive.png" data-image-key="Baby_Harry_Left_At_Privet_Drive.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Baby_Harry_Left_At_Privet_Drive.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Albus Dumbledore leaves infant Harry Potter and a note on the doorstep of 4 Privet Drive</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>It was after <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s first downfall and the Potters' murder that Vernon first saw signs of wizards. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> brought Vernon's nephew <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> to Little Whinging. Although reluctantly, Vernon and Petunia allowed him into their home.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Harry's early life, Vernon treated him with indifference and cruelty. He forced Harry to live in the <a href="/wiki/Cupboard_Under_the_Stairs" title="Cupboard Under the Stairs">Cupboard Under the Stairs</a> and generally mistreated and neglected him while spoiling his own son <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley</a>. Vernon tried his hardest to stop Harry knowing about the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a> and his true <a href="/wiki/Magic_genes" title="Magic genes">magical heritage</a> and also ignorantly believing along with his wife that their harsh mistreatment of him would prevent his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> from surfacing. Before <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, Vernon told Harry his parents died in a car crash. On Dudley's birthday that year, Vernon drove the family to the local zoo for the day, ranting as usual about the various and innumerable things that annoyed him, motorbikes being the chosen topic. When Harry remarked that he'd had a dream about a <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black%27s_motorcycle" class="mw-redirect" title="Sirius Black&#39;s motorcycle">flying motorbike</a>, Vernon nearly crashed into the car in front of them before bellowing at his nephew for saying such a ridiculous thing. He yelled at him some more and told him there was no such thing as <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, after Harry unknowingly used <a href="/wiki/Underage_magic" title="Underage magic">underage magic</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Zoo" title="Zoo">Zoo</a>'s reptile house.<sup id="cite_ref-PS2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>When Harry got his first <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_acceptance_letter" title="Hogwarts acceptance letter">letter</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, Vernon tried everything to stop him getting it as more and more letters arrived via <a href="/wiki/Owl_Postal_Service" title="Owl Postal Service">Owl post</a>, resorting to increasingly desperate measures as the letters entered the house in variously creative ways and becoming more and more unstable. He finally cracked one Sunday when a veritable flood of letters came pouring down the chimney, and had the entire family leave the house to try and prevent contact from Hogwarts. However, his erratic attempts failed after driving the family several miles away from home, and upon staying in a gloomy Hotel, he was shocked to see letters turn up there even. Eventually, he took his whole family to a <a href="/wiki/Hut-on-the-Rock" title="Hut-on-the-Rock">Hut</a> on a small rock on the sea, in a final drastic attempt to prevent contact and even bought what was most likely an illegal firearm as a final defence.<sup id="cite_ref-PS3_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> But even this did not work, because it was found by <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>, who personally gave Harry the letter, despite Vernon's stubborn protests and threatening him with the gun, which Hagrid contemptuously bent into a knot.<sup id="cite_ref-PS4_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS4-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Hagrid told Harry about  <a href="/wiki/Attack_at_Godric%27s_Hollow_(1981)" title="Attack at Godric&#39;s Hollow (1981)">how his parents really died</a>, Vernon cynically responded by telling Harry that whatever happened to Harry's <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">mother</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">father</a> they probably deserved it, as well as that they were (in Vernon's opinion) freaks anyway and that the world was better off without them (also in Vernon's opinion). Hagrid threatened Vernon with his umbrella in response. The exchanges became more and more heated until Vernon angrily insulted <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> by calling him a "crackpot old fool". This would be a great mistake to Vernon: a livid Hagrid responded to Vernon's outburst by <a href="/wiki/Human_to_pig_spell" title="Human to pig spell">giving a pig tail</a> to Vernon's <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">son</a>, much to the Dursley's horror.<sup id="cite_ref-PS4_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS4-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After this incident, Vernon avoided Harry even more so than usual. Vernon reluctantly agreed to take Harry to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_Station" title="King&#39;s Cross Station">King's Cross Station</a> to board the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a>, but only because it was on the way to the <a href="/wiki/London_private_hospital" title="London private hospital">private hospital</a> in London to remove Dudley's tail before he started attending Smeltings.<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From then on Vernon only saw Harry in the summer holidays and even then treated him badly, although he reluctantly gave Harry Dudley's spare room.<sup id="cite_ref-CS1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Almost everything seemed to go wrong every time Vernon had guests over when Harry was present. In <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, he invited <a href="/wiki/Mason" title="Mason">Mr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mason%27s_wife" title="Mason&#39;s wife">Mrs Mason</a> over for dinner to try and secure a deal with Mr Mason about a stock of drills. He also forced Harry to stay in his room and pretend he did not exist.<sup id="cite_ref-COS1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Harry was willing to comply with this, arguably because he did not want to create unneeded problems for his uncle (and get himself in further dislike). Due to the appearance of <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a>, the deal was utterly ruined when Dobby destroyed Petunia's prized pudding and the frightening away of Mrs Mason by a Ministry of Magic Owl, due to her ornithophobia. The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> accused Harry of doing Dobby's <a href="/wiki/Hover_Charm" title="Hover Charm">levitation</a> of the pudding, and threatened expulsion from Hogwarts for violation of the Underage Magic Act, but elected to let Harry off with a warning.<sup id="cite_ref-CS2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Furious for his business deal failing and learning that Harry was forbidden to use magic outside of Hogwarts, Vernon had Harry locked in his room and even had bars fitted on his window to punish him, believing that Harry would be unable to escape without using magic. However, Harry was rescued by <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> using their <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">father</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Flying_Ford_Anglia" title="Flying Ford Anglia">Flying Ford Anglia</a>, and when Vernon, Petunia and Dudley tried to stop Harry escaping by pulling him back into his room, they were all left dangling out of his window when the Weasleys drove off with Harry. Then, in <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, Vernon's equally mean-spirited sister <a href="/wiki/Marjorie_Dursley" title="Marjorie Dursley">Marge</a> visited, much to Harry's discomfort. When Marge insulted Harry's parents, Harry used magic to <a href="/wiki/Inflating_Charm" class="mw-redirect" title="Inflating Charm">blow her up like a balloon</a>. Vernon insisted Harry return her to how she was, but was refused. The <a href="/wiki/Accidental_Magic_Reversal_Squad" title="Accidental Magic Reversal Squad">Accidental Magic Reversal Squad</a> later fixed her and <a href="/wiki/Memory_Charm" title="Memory Charm">modified</a> her memory of having witnessed magic.<sup id="cite_ref-POA2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Harry afterwards left Privet Drive, and as a result he did not sign Harry's <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade_permission_form" title="Hogsmeade permission form">Hogsmeade permission form</a>, which he had beforehand actually agreed to sign, should Harry behave during Marge's visit.

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Vernon, along with Petunia, decided to put Dudley on a diet, following his dismal report from Smeltings and upon learning that his son was too overweight to fit into the school's uniform. When it was not going so well, he threatened Dudley that he would cut his pocket money if he did not stop sneaking in sweets, which made Dudley furious and caused him to throw his <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation">PlayStation</a> out of a window.<sup id="cite_ref-GF2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a> sent an invitation to the family inviting Harry to attend the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a> with them, Vernon angrily asked his nephew about the letter, as the Molly's letter was covered in stamps which the postman found amusing. After Harry explained, he yelled at him initially but eventually decided that his nephew could go.<sup id="cite_ref-GF3_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF3-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He and Petunia were very displeased and discomforted with the idea that <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> would soon be visiting their home. When the Weasleys came to pick up Harry by the <a href="/wiki/Floo_Network" title="Floo Network">Floo Network</a> and accidentally destroyed their living room due to them trying to travel into their <a href="/wiki/Electric_fire" title="Electric fire">electric fireplace</a>, Vernon became very intimidated in the presence of actual wizards, including when <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> mentioned that he liked to collect batteries. When the Weasleys were leaving, Dudley ate a <a href="/wiki/Ton-Tongue_Toffee" title="Ton-Tongue Toffee">Ton-Tongue Toffee</a> deliberately left by Fred and George Weasley, causing his tongue to swell. This sent Vernon into a rage which had him throw several china ornaments at Mr Weasley, all the time bellowing like an injured <a href="/wiki/Hippopotamus" title="Hippopotamus">hippopotamus</a> while Arthur was trying to fix Dudley's swollen tongue; it ended up being four feet long before Vernon and Petunia let Arthur shrink it.<sup id="cite_ref-GF4_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF4-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, Vernon, Petunia and Dudley had learned that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> had returned to terrorise the both Wizarding and Muggle communities again. During the summer, Vernon and Petunia were concerned regarding Harry's overly attentive nature regarding his listening to the Muggle news, believing he was up to no good. Ironically, he and his wife were ignorantly unaware that Dudley was in fact terrorising the neighbourhood with his <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_gang" title="Dudley Dursley&#39;s gang">gang</a>, engaging in anti-social behaviour and bullying younger children, instead believing that Dudley was simply going round his friends' houses for tea.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, Dudley was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> and Harry had to rescue him via <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>. Upon coming across his demented son, Vernon erroneously believed that Harry had in fact attacked Dudley himself. He became extremely angry with his nephew, believing that he had caused permanent brain damage to his son, and even expressed the view that Harry should face <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">capital punishment</a>. However, even when Harry explained to him that he had in fact saved Dudley from the Dementors and they were likely sent by the recently resurrected Voldemort, Vernon decided to throw Harry out of the house, since he did not want Harry staying because he believed that Voldemort was after him. However, he was reluctantly forced by Petunia to allow him to stay, on the grounds the neighbours would notice, when in reality she had just received an angry <a href="/wiki/Howler" title="Howler">Howler</a> from <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, telling her to keep their promise of protecting Harry. When he heard that Harry was in trouble with the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> because of the letter from <a href="/wiki/Mafalda_Hopkirk" title="Mafalda Hopkirk">Mafalda Hopkirk</a>, Vernon was delighted at the concept of Harry being punished by the Ministry.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP2_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP2-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He locked Harry into his room for several days until leaving with his wife and son, to accept an award for the best kept lawn in England. In fact, it was a trick of <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, who had sent the Dursleys an invitation to a fake awards ceremony to get them temporarily out of their house. Tonks then escorted Harry to <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>, along with a <a href="/wiki/Advance_Guard" title="Advance Guard">group</a> of other <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP3_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP3-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It was highly likely that Vernon was indignant at being fooled by the very cunning ploy.
</p>

<p>During the summer of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Vernon and his family were visited by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> at their house, who had came to inform Harry and them of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black%27s_will" title="Sirius Black&#39;s will">will</a>. Vernon was horrified and offended with the sudden arrival of Dumbledore into his home, due to his hatred and fear of wizards. Vernon was surprised and greedily interested when he learnt that <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> had died, and that Harry had inherited <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>. He and his family were too fearful to drink Dumbledore's levitating drinks, which resulted in the drinks emptying themselves over their heads. Vernon was also horrified with the sudden arrival of <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a> into his living room, as he had never seen a <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">House-elf</a> before. Vernon and Petunia were both admonished by Dumbledore for abusing Harry growing up and for spoiling Dudley, which in Dumbledore's eyes had inflicted irrevocable emotional damage upon their son, before Dumbledore left with Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP3_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the war, Vernon had some <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_children" title="Dudley Dursley&#39;s children">grandchildren</a> by Dudley.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown if he remained in contact with his nephew, if even only on Christmas-card terms, like Dudley did: nevertheless, he had two great-nephews and a great-niece by Harry. It is also unknown whether Vernon returned to his home at <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a>, or if he stayed where he'd been hiding throughout the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2015" title="2015">2015</a>, Vernon signed an <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">online</a> petition to reinstate <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Clarkson" title="Jeremy Clarkson">Jeremy Clarkson</a> as host of the <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> programme <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear" title="Top Gear">Top Gear</a></i>, claiming it was the only reason he paid for a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/television_licence" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:television licence">television licence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point before <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>, Vernon’s wife Petunia died.<sup id="cite_ref-CC1-17_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC1-17-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It's unknown if Vernon was still alive by this point.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vernon was described as a big, beefy man with a large purple face. He had thick, dark hair, a bushy black moustache (half of which he tore out through frustration on two occasions), with hardly any neck and mean little eyes that narrowed to slits when he was angry.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley</a> much resembled him apart from his blond hair.
</p><p>Vernon was renowned for his physical reactions to magic or when attempting and/or failing to control his temper. In the case of the latter, a vein would throb very prominently in his temple. His face would also change to several different colours; a sickly porridge grey, red to green and various shades of purple. In one case, his face was said to resemble a "<i>gigantic beetroot with a moustache</i>."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Vernon was an arrogant, surly, combustible, blustering, abrasive, petty-minded bully who was completely intolerant of people who were different than himself. Like Petunia, he detested magic and anything unusual or out of the ordinary, which he labelled as "funny business" in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PS2_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Naturally, he despised his nephew Harry, who as a wizard was very much a symbol of both, and spitefully bullied him all throughout the latter's childhood. Vernon's disdain for Harry was so great that he forced him to sleep in the cupboard under the stairs and, after Harry discovered he was a wizard despite Vernon's best efforts to keep it from him, prohibited Harry from saying words such as "magic" or "wizard" inside the house.<sup id="cite_ref-COS1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to being a bully towards Harry, Vernon was absurdly vain, materialistic and unsympathetic to beggars and vagrants. He was very much concerned with status and wealth and tended to judge people based on how big their cars were or their clothes. He enjoyed ordering around his employees at Grunnings, enjoying his status and authority as director of his company, sitting above them in a <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley%27s_office" title="Vernon Dursley&#39;s office">high office</a> on the ninth floor.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Vernon liked to look big and impressive, yet was easily intimidated whenever he was put in situations that he was not used to (e.g.<i>,</i> dealing with certain individuals (i.e., wizards such as <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP3_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GF2_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP38_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP38-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-PS4_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS4-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> etc.).
</p><p>He was also shown to be quite oblivious to small things, particularly when cross, such as in 1992 when he asked Harry if he looked stupid to him when he became annoyed with the boy: he had a bit of fried egg sticking to his moustache.<sup id="cite_ref-COS1_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Sometimes when he was angry, Vernon would mumble, barely forming complete sentences.<sup id="cite_ref-PS4_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS4-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">"<i>When will they learn, that hanging’s the only way to deal with these people?</i>"</span></dd>

<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>He was also shown to be very conservative in his political views, as he read the <i>Daily Mail</i>, a British tabloid famed for its right-wing political stance. He also seemed to have a low opinion of the state of the UK, saying: "<i>No wonder the country's going to the dogs</i>!" upon finding out that there was a Ministry of Magic,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP2_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP2-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> a popular Daily Mail editorial stance. He advocated the death penalty, especially for murder; after seeing a mug shot of Sirius Black, he claimed that <i>"...hanging's the only way to deal with these people,"</i><sup id="cite_ref-POA2_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> showing his harsh view of criminal justice.
</p><p>Vernon enjoyed complaining about anything that did not fit in with his narrow-minded views of the world and his favourite subjects included people at work, the council, the bank, motorbikes, as well as most of all: Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-PS2_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He showed his disdain to Harry with nasty smiles and he was not afraid of physical confrontations, going as far as wrestling with his son, Dudley, to confiscate Harry's <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_acceptance_letter" title="Hogwarts acceptance letter">Hogwarts acceptance letter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS3_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS3-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He frequently became angry and he received brandy from his wife when he was rendered speechless by the "vanishing glass" incident at the <a href="/wiki/Zoo" title="Zoo">zoo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS2_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Despite his bad temper and blustering, bullying, dominant nature however, Vernon showed himself to be a coward when confronted by those he could not bully or intimidate, as shown by his terrified reaction when Hagrid furiously confronted him upon discovering that he had not told Harry the latter was a wizard, or who his parents were or that they had been murdered. All this Vernon had kept from Harry in order to "stamp the wizarding rubbish out of him."<sup id="cite_ref-PS4_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS4-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He was likewise scared by Alastor Moody when the latter confronted him over his bullying treatment of Harry,<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP38_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP38-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and would immediately back down whenever Harry threatened him with magic.<sup id="cite_ref-POA2_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dursley_family">Dursley family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Dursley family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Dursley family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Vernon married <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia Evans</a> after having met her at work and likely learned of the wizarding world through her. He was deeply shocked, though, when Petunia confessed the truth, however, Vernon told Petunia solemnly that he would never hold it against her that she had a freak for a <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">sister</a>. Then Petunia threw herself upon him in such violent gratitude that he dropped his battered sausage.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Together they did everything they could to keep their nephew Harry from learning the truth about his heritage and abilities and bossing him in any sort of ways. They also endlessly pampered and spoiled their only son, Dudley.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PS2_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Vernon spoiled Dudley in a similar manner to his wife: they appeared to buy Dudley anything he wants, often apparently to waylay a tantrum and indulged him with treats and activities, particularly those which Harry was not allowed.<sup id="cite_ref-PS1_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PS2_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, there is evidence that Vernon and Petunia were indulgent of their son's behaviour and contributed to his obesity before <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> was introduced to the household.
</p><p>They were frequently shown to be oblivious to his bullying and at times, criminal behaviour, Vernon showing pride in his son and interpreting some of these characteristics as manly. Vernon had a similar physique to Dudley, also being very large and when convinced to make Dudley diet by the school nurse, was forced disapprovingly to join in the family's regime, whereas previously he and his immediate family considered Dudley merely to have a healthy appetite. He became very proud of Dudley's resulting sporting achievements when this enforced exercise and diet helped him become a competing boxer, believing Dudley gave the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> the "ol' one-two" when they attacked him in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. He was, however, oblivious to the fact that Dudley used his new skills to terrorise neighbourhood children.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP1_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Vernon saw Harry as nothing more than a waste of space. Throughout his life, he tried to keep Harry as downtrodden as possible, in hopes of squashing the magic out of him. He lived in fear of anyone finding out about Harry's "<a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">abnormality</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-PS4_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS4-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Vernon rarely referred to Harry by name, instead referring to him as "[the] boy" or "You", both to Harry directly and in conversation. Vernon would have a tendency to throw fits whenever Harry (either accidentally or intentionally) used words such as "magic", "wand", "Hogwarts", or "broomstick" in the house.<sup id="cite_ref-COS1_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Both guests and neighbours believed Vernon's explanation that Harry attended <a href="/wiki/St_Brutus%27s_Secure_Centre_for_Incurably_Criminal_Boys" title="St Brutus&#39;s Secure Centre for Incurably Criminal Boys">St Brutus's Secure Centre for Incurably Criminal Boys</a>, rather than the local comprehensive.<sup id="cite_ref-POA2_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Vernon even told Mr Mason and his wife that Harry was "disturbed" when explaining his sudden presence in the house in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS2_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Though Petunia felt some moral obligation to let Harry stay (however grudgingly and bitterly), Vernon felt nothing towards his nephew. He had no trouble throwing him out of the house, despite full knowledge that doing so would put his life in grave danger. Petunia, however, intervened when Dumbledore sent her a <a href="/wiki/Howler" title="Howler">Howler</a>, reminding her of the agreement she made when they took in Harry. Additionally, when Harry was forced to go to court for underage magic, Vernon openly admitted that he hoped that Harry would get the death penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP2_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP2-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> However, despite his dislike of Harry, he would still go to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_Station" title="King&#39;s Cross Station">King's Cross Station</a> to collect him from the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> at the end of each school year.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS18_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS18-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-POA22_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA22-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GOF37_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF37-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP38_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP38-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vernon would later become an uncle-in-law to Harry's wife, <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginevra Weasley</a> and a great-uncle to Harry and Ginny's children, <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_II" title="James Potter II">James Sirius Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Severus Potter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lily_L._Potter" title="Lily L. Potter">Lily Luna Potter</a>.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="James_Potter">James Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: James Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: James Potter"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Vernon Dursley's first meeting with <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a> went badly. Vernon asked James what car he drove, but James described his racing <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broom</a>, thus Vernon started patronising him and finally offended James by supposing out loud that <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> had to live on unemployment benefit. Having heard from the wizard that he had a fortune in solid gold, he grew angry and stormed out of restaurant, where they had met with their wives. After that Vernon refused to talk with James, but described him, within James’ earshot, as "<i>some kind of amateur <a href="/wiki/Magician" title="Magician">magician</a></i>".<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vernon is from a French last name for "alder tree".
</p><p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dursley" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dursley">Dursley</a> is a town in Gloucestershire, England. <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has commented that she visited the place as a child and hated it, which likely affected her choice of surname for the awful family, remarking "I don't imagine I'm very popular in Dursley".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The town is known for its Victorian history in coal mining. Rowling chose the name because it sounded "dull and forbidding".
</p>

<ul><li>Vernon Dursley was the first character introduced in the <i>Harry Potter</i> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">series</a>.</li>
<li>Vernon is also one of only four characters in the <i>Harry Potter</i> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)">book series</a> whose thoughts have been disclosed by the narrator, the others being <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce" title="Frank Bryce">Frank Bryce</a>, as well as the British <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a>.</li>
<li>Vernon is portrayed by the late <a href="/wiki/Richard_Griffiths" title="Richard Griffiths">Richard Griffiths</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film series</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Christopher_Wilson" title="Christopher Wilson">Chris Wilson</a> was stand-in for Richard Griffiths as Uncle Vernon<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the book series, Vernon is depicted as very unpleasant and downright unlikable. In the films, however, while he remains the same, Vernon is portrayed in a much more humourous light, possibly due to Richard Griffiths' natural comedic approaches.</li>
<li>In an <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://atrossbooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Harry-Potter-and-the-Order-of-the-Phoenix-2nd-draft.pdf">earlier draft</a> of the fifth film, Vernon and Petunia notice the darkness caused by the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> and come running out in time to see Harry's Patronus: Consequently, they do not blame him. Also in that draft, Vernon <i>can</i> see the Dementors and stares after them, dumbfounded, finally finding the strength to ask "<i>What the hell are those</i>?".</li>
<li>In the books, Vernon is described as a "big, beefy man" with black hair and a matching moustache and being in his mid-thirties. In the films, however, he is a middle aged man who has greying hair and moustache. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, his hair is blonde-grey and in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, it is grey-white.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> said the character in the series she most dislikes is probably Uncle Vernon.</li>
<li>Vernon appeared in five of the eight Harry Potter films. He did, however, appear in all seven books.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> had originally planned for one of Dudley's children to have magic and for Dudley to be at Platform 9 3/4 seeing them off to Hogwarts during the epilogue of <i>Deathly Hallows</i>. However, she later decided against this, saying that no magical genes could survive contact with Uncle Vernon's DNA.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the books, Vernon appears to be so afraid of magic that he forbids Harry to say any word related to magic (e.g. "wizard", "Hogwarts" "Hogsmeade", "Hogwarts Express" and the word "magic" itself). When he discusses magic, he usually uses euphemisms like "you-know-what" (magic), "that school" (Hogwarts), "that abnormality" (magic or magical ability), "[Harry]'s lot" (wizards and witches), or simply "it" to refer to magic. In the films, he actually utters the word "magic". Ironically, he's not afraid of the name <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>. Vernon also cannot remember the proper name of <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>, referring to them as "dismembers", "dementy-whatsits" and similar names.</li>
<li>The scene in which the Dursleys leave the house in the seventh book is shortened down to a scene at the beginning of the seventh film where Vernon and Dudley are leaving their house and are hastily packing away their furniture. Petunia is seen waiting in the car already. Vernon has two lines in this scene while Dudley has one and Petunia doesn't speak.</li>
<li>Vernon appears also in a deleted scene from <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>, in which Dudley asks Vernon why Harry is not coming with them and Vernon says he does not want to. Vernon also enthusiastically tells Harry "This isn't just goodbye, boy, is it? This is farewell!" something at which Harry nods.</li>
<li>Richard Griffiths, along with the actor of Harry Potter, Daniel Radcliffe, was shown on a poster in the films as an obvious reference to him. The show the poster was for is Equus. The production did star both Griffiths and Radcliffe in real life, but happened later in the real world than in-universe.</li>
<li>Vernon is based on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_Ector" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Sir Ector">Sir Ector</a> from Arthurian legends (and particularly Sir Ector's portrayal in Disney's <i>The Sword in the Stone</i>) because of his intolerance both of magic and of his ward. Sir Ector calls Arthur "wart" or "you" and Vernon calls Harry "boy" or "you." Likewise, Dudley is based on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_Kay" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Sir Kay">Sir Kay</a>, Sir Ector's son who mistreats Arthur.</li>
<li>Unlike his magic-hating character, Richard Griffiths was very interested in the idea of a boy attending a school for wizards. Throughout the film series, he was hopeful that he could film a scene where Uncle Vernon is summoned to Hogwarts; however, this was firmly vetoed by J.K. Rowling.</li>
<li>One of Vernon's favourite television shows is said to be <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top_Gear_(1977_TV_series)">Top Gear</a>, a motoring show, matching his like for cars.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the trailer for the film adaptation of <i>Harry Potter and the Philosophers' Stone</i>, the beginning has Harry approaching the vent and it opening up while Vernon is heard offscreen yelling at Harry that magic doesn't exist before shutting it forcefully enough to leave a quaking motion as the Warner Bros logo pops up. In the actual film, it shows Vernon after tossing Harry into the cupboard under the stairs opening the vent and admonishing Harry before cutting to the inside just prior to Vernon shutting the vent. On a similar note, Vernon's delivery for "There's no such thing as magic" slightly differed in the trailer and in the film.</li></ul>
