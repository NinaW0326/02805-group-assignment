<b>Daisy Dodderidge</b> (<a href="/wiki/1467" class="mw-redirect" title="1467">1467</a>–<a href="/wiki/1555" class="mw-redirect" title="1555">1555</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-WOTM_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WOTM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CFC_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CFC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who built the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a> in a successful attempt to create a gateway between the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding worlds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CFC2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CFC2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She was the original landlady of the pub<sup id="cite_ref-WOTM_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WOTM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>, and the Leaky Cauldron soon became the primary entrance into <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CFC2_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CFC2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Daisy_Dodderidge?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Daisy_Dodderidge?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Daisy" is simply from the English word for the white flower, ultimately derived from Old English <i>dægeseage</i> meaning "day eye". It was first used as a given name in the 19th century, at the same time many other plant and flower names were coined.
</p>
