<b>Arturo Cephalopos</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmaker</a> in the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th century</a>. During this period, <a href="/wiki/Silver_lime" title="Silver lime">silver lime</a> was fashionable as a <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wand wood</a>. He claimed that its alleged association with <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">divination</a> was "a falsehood circulated by merchants like <a href="/wiki/Gerbold_Ollivander" title="Gerbold Ollivander">Gerbold Ollivander</a>, who have overstocked their workshops with it and hope to shift their surplus." However, according to Gerbold's grandson <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, Cephalopos was a "slipshod wandmaker" and "an ignoramus," who, to no one's surprise, went out of business.<sup id="cite_ref-wands_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wands-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Arturo_Cephalopos?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Arturo_Cephalopos?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cephalopos might be of Greek origin (at least in his ancestry), as his surname's&#160;etymology (as well as surname formation)&#160;suggests.
</p>
<ul><li>"<i>Arturo</i>" is the Italian/Spanish form&#160;of "Arthur", whose meaning is unknown. It could be derived from British <i>art</i> "bear" combined with <i>viros</i> "man", or it could be related to Irish <i>art</i> "stone". Alternatively it could be related to an obscure Roman family name <i>Artorius</i>. Arthur is the name of the central character in Arthurian legend, a 6th-century king of the Britons who presided over the knights of the Round Table.</li>
<li>"<i>Cephalopos</i>" is a&#160;likely Greek compound word (though it&#160;resembles nothing like a modern Greek surname):
<ul><li>"<i>cephalo</i>" from the Ancient Greek κεφαλή ‎(<i>kephalḗ</i>), meaning "head" (in biology, the "brain"), and is the origin of the English prefix&#160;"cephalo-" &amp; suffix "-cephalic"&#160;.</li>
<li>"<i>pos</i>" from either:
<ul><li>πούς ‎(<i>poús</i>), meaning "leg/foot" (and thru its derivative&#160;"ποδός" (<i>podós</i>), the origin of the English prefix "-pod")</li>
<li>πως ‎(<i>pōs</i>), Greek adverb meaning "how?" or "in what way?"</li></ul></li>
<li>Put together, "<i>cephalopos</i>" suggests an individual with a low intelligence, essentially a foreshadowing of his character ("ignoramus") &amp; fate (his wand&#160;business came under).</li></ul></li></ul>
