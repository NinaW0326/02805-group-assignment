<p>Romilda was part of a group of giggling fourth-year girls who went to Harry's compartment on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a>. They stood outside for a while, arguing about who should go in and invite Harry to sit with them. Romilda was the only one brave enough, remarking he didn't have to sit with the unpopular <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>. Harry, having fought alongside the pair during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a> and considering Neville and Luna his friends, coldly denied her request, leaving Romilda very surprised when she departed with her friends. This made Luna and Neville think that they were uncool and maybe unworthy of sitting with the famous Harry Potter.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP7_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She also tried out for the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Quidditch_team" title="Gryffindor Quidditch team">Gryffindor Quidditch team</a>. When she and her group of friends were asked to fly once around the pitch, they simply fell over one another and giggled. Harry quickly asked them to leave; they left the pitch, but remained in the stadium to heckle the other applicants.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP11_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP11-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> of that year, when Harry was invited to <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Professor Slughorn</a>'s lavish <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club_Christmas_party" title="Slug Club Christmas party">Christmas party</a>, Romilda plotted with her friends in the girls' bathroom to somehow give Harry a <a href="/wiki/Love_potion" title="Love potion">love potion</a> from <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wizard_Wheezes" title="Weasleys&#39; Wizard Wheezes">Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes</a>, which would make him ask one of them to be his guest to the party. <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> overheard this and warned Harry of her intentions. When he returned to the common room, Romilda initially tried to give Harry <a href="/wiki/Gillywater" title="Gillywater">gillywater</a>, but he refused. Instead, she forced a package of <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Cauldrons" title="Chocolate Cauldrons">Chocolate Cauldrons</a>, both of which were spiked with love potion, into his hands. She also made hints about wanting to go to the party with him, which Harry ignored.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry proceeded to stow the love potion-spiked Chocolate Cauldrons away in his trunk until <a href="/wiki/March" title="March">March</a>, when, while he was looking for the <a href="/wiki/Marauder%27s_Map" title="Marauder&#39;s Map">Marauder's Map</a>, he tossed them on the floor. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, believing they were one of his birthday presents that had fallen off his bed, picked them up and ate them. Ron suddenly became obsessively infatuated with Romilda, to the point that he declared his love for her in front of Harry, then punched him when the former asked if it was a joke, and attempted to wrestle his way into Slughorn's office, believing she was in there. This led to <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> giving Ron an <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion_Antidote" title="Love Potion Antidote">antidote</a>. Believing it was a "<i>tonic for the nerves</i>", Ron drank it and the potion's effects ceased. Soon afterwards, he was <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoned</a> by drinking from a bottle of oak matured <a href="/wiki/Mead" title="Mead">mead</a> that was meant for <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP18_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Harry publicly kissed <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> after a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> victory, Romilda looked as if she wanted to throw something.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, when the two subsequently began dating, Romilda gave up her efforts to win Harry's heart. She was nonetheless incessantly curious about their relationship and bombarded Ginny with questions, such as if Harry had a tattoo of a <a href="/wiki/Hippogriff" title="Hippogriff">hippogriff</a> on his chest.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP25_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP25-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Fifth_year">Fifth year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Fifth year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fifth year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In her <a href="/wiki/Fifth_year" title="Fifth year">fifth year</a> Romilda was present on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> on the <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> when <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> <a href="/wiki/Raid_on_the_Hogwarts_Express" title="Raid on the Hogwarts Express">stormed</a> the train in search of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHF1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>When Death Eaters gained control of Hogwarts she became a member of <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, an organisation founded by the trio that fought the tyranny within the school. They were a radical and rebellious group of students dedicated to making things as difficult as possible for Death Eater professors <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow" title="Amycus Carrow">Amycus Carrow</a>, as well as Headmaster <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, when Harry Potter returned to the school, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> broke out, which Romilda bravely fought in alongside her fellow D.A. members. She ultimately survived the battle.<sup id="cite_ref-DHF2_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Later_life_(2000-?)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_.282000-.3F.29">Later life <span class="template-c">(<a href="/wiki/2000" title="2000">2000</a>-?)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life (2000-?)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Later life (2000-?)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eventually, a <a class="new" title="Music box (page does not exist)" rel="nofollow" data-uncrawlable-url="L3dpa2kvTXVzaWNfYm94P2FjdGlvbj1lZGl0JnJlZGxpbms9MQ==">music box</a> topped with a <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a> <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">troll</a> for which Romilda owned as an adult was displaced by the events of the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a>. It was later returned to her by the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Romilda had large, dark eyes, long, curly black hair, and a prominent chin.<sup id="cite_ref-haireyes_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haireyes-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-haireyes2_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haireyes2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She was described as having an air of extreme boldness about her and often spoke in a loud, dramatic voice.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP7_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>Why don't you join us in our compartment? You don't have to sit with </i>them<i>.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Romilda invites <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> to ditch <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Like <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a>, Romilda seemed to represent all the stereotypical aspects of <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> house, both positive and negative. She was domineering, conceited, and overly self-confident, though she is also genuinely perseverant, courageous, noble, and loyal to friends. Her honourable side was shown by her membership in <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, participation in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> showing she was genuinely heroic and brave.<sup id="cite_ref-DHF2_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She seemed to be the leader of her clique and was possibly the boldest among her friends, being the only one to offer a boy company or use a <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potion</a> on him,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP18_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and may have supplanted <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a> in her leadership.
</p><p>On the other hand, she could also be sneaky and manipulative, as was evidenced by the lengths to which she was willing to go in her obsessive, single-minded pursuit of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP18_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, she apparently stopped trying to win him over when he began dating <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>. Her character was also marked by a tendency toward gossip and cliquish snobbery.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP25_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP25-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry Potter once pointed out the similarities between himself and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, and incidentally, the Love Potion that Romilda attempted to use on him was likely the same method that <a href="/wiki/Merope_Gaunt" class="mw-redirect" title="Merope Gaunt">Voldemort's mother</a> likely used to trick <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle_Senior" title="Tom Riddle Senior">his father</a> into marrying her. Furthermore, Romilda's obsession with Harry was vaguely similar to the obsessive "<a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a>" that Voldemort's mother had had for his father. But, Romilda differed from Merope Gaunt in that she easily gave up her pursuit of Harry after he became involved with Ginny while Merope resorted to conceiving a child with Tom Riddle Snr while he was under the mind-altering effects of a love potion. In spite of her bad qualities, she was reasonable to a fault and capable of behaving sensibly and pragmatically when she chose to.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harry_Potter">Harry Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Harry Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Harry Potter"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Romilda was obsessively in <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, mostly because she thought he was the Chosen One.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP7_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, she let him go after he started dating <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>, narrowly restraining herself from reacting violently when she saw him kissing Ginny Weasley.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Harry was far less charitable in his regard for her. He considered her to be obnoxious and had little patience for her. He coldly rebuffed her invitation to sit with her and her friends.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP7_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ironically, it is possible that Romilda laughed at him when he would have asked Cho out, before learning he was the Chosen One. This said, he might have remembered her while Romilda might have forgotten all about that. However, she later showed her immense loyalty and respect towards Harry and his cause by joining <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> and by fighting bravely in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHF2_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leanne_and_Katie_Bell">Leanne and Katie Bell</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Leanne and Katie Bell">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Leanne and Katie Bell"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Romilda got along well with <a href="/wiki/Leanne" title="Leanne">Leanne</a> as well as the latter's best friend <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a>. Romilda sat with Leanne on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> during her fifth year,<sup id="cite_ref-DHF1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and much later they kept each other company and rested either during or after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHF2_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Hogwarts_students">Other Hogwarts students</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Other Hogwarts students">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Other Hogwarts students"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Romilda's regard for <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> was clearly negative, as she was envious of Harry's close and platonic relationship with them, clearly considering herself a superior witch.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP7_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> She was also jealous when Harry invited Luna to the <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP15_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP15-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Romilda was even more jealous of <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a>, as Harry chose her over the former, as she learned upon watching them make out, nearly goading her into throwing something.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, she was still a loyal and committed member of <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> and was fiercely determined to fighting alongside other <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> student to protect their school from <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and his army.<sup id="cite_ref-DHF2_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHF2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Romilda is derived from the Germanic <i>hrom</i>, "fame" and <i>hild</i>, "battle."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> This may allude to Romilda's aggressive personality. The surname <i>Vane</i> is likely a play on the adjective <i>vain</i>, meaning "excessively proud of or concerned about one's own appearance, qualities, achievements,"<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> given Romilda's character traits.
</p><p>Her surname may also be a reference to Sibyl Vane, the alluring actress in Oscar Wilde's novel <i><a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Picture_of_Dorian_Grey">The Picture of Dorian Gray</a></i>. In the novel, Sibyl is infatuated with Dorian, much in the same way that Romilda is infatuated with Harry, only to be rejected by Dorian when he no longer finds her pleasing.
</p>

<ul><li>Romilda Vane was played by actress <a href="/wiki/Anna_Shaffer" title="Anna Shaffer">Anna Shaffer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>. Also in the film adaptations, Romilda is a <a href="/wiki/Sixth_year" title="Sixth year">sixth year</a> instead of a <a href="/wiki/Fourth_year" title="Fourth year">fourth year</a>.</li>
<li>In the first part <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, Romilda can be briefly seen on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> sitting next to <a href="/wiki/Leanne" title="Leanne">Leanne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cormac_McLaggen" title="Cormac McLaggen">Cormac McLaggen</a>.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i> Romilda participates in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>. However, in the novel's canon, she would have only been a <a href="/wiki/Fifth_year" title="Fifth year">fifth year</a> at the time (though in movie canon she would have been a seventh year), and if she had been at Hogwarts she would have been sent away before the battle with the other underage students, although she could have come back like <a href="/wiki/Colin_Creevey" title="Colin Creevey">Colin Creevey</a>.</li>
<li>It is unclear how she learned to cast a <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>, but she probably learned it from the D.A. It's unusual for fifth-years to be capable of such a feat. It's also unclear if she shot a Patronus at Harry's assailants or simply watched, as she lowered her wand rather quickly.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)"><i>Half-Blood Prince</i> film</a>, it appears that Harry is flattered by Romilda's <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">interest</a> in him, until <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a> reminds him that Romilda is only interested in him because she, like most of the wizarding world, believes him to be the <i>"Chosen One"</i>, to which he replies <i>"but I am the Chosen One"</i>. Then Hermione gets irritated by it and swats him with a copy of the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>. This contrasts the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">book</a>, in which Harry dislikes, and is disconcerted by, Romilda's attention from the start.</li>
<li>In the film adaptation of <i>Deathly Hallows</i> she survived the battle. After the camera is on <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth</a>, on the right one can see <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leanne" title="Leanne">Leanne</a> and Romilda talking, and possibly <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho</a> as well. She and Leanne also sat together on the Hogwarts Express.
<ul><li>This said, it is very likely Romilda was one of the members of Cho's posse. While this statement is contradicted by Romilda being the leader, it is not mentioned until <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, a year after Cho's friends left her minus <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a>. After Cho went into a depression following <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>'s death, it would add up if she became the leader in her place despite being younger than Cho.</li>
<li>This would mean the two girls who argued over inviting Harry to sit with them were the same people who laughed at him when he tried to ask Cho out, which may explain why they both hesitated prompting Romilda herself to step forward.</li></ul></li>
<li>Nothing much is known about her personality in the films partly due to the fact that she has no lines in any of the films she appears in. She appears to be much less obnoxious than in the book.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>, Romilda is omitted from the console versions of the game. Due to this, Ron, after consuming the <a href="/wiki/Love_Potion" class="mw-redirect" title="Love Potion">Love Potion</a>, is made to fall in love not with her, but anyone or anything he first sees.</li>
<li>The actress who plays Romilda, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Shaffer" title="Anna Shaffer">Anna Shaffer</a>, also plays another witch character in Netflix's series, <i><a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Witcher_(TV_series)">The Witcher</a></i>.</li></ul>
