<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mungo_Bonham?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mungo_Bonham?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In reality, Saint Mungo is the commonly used name for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_Mungo" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Saint Mungo">Saint Kentigern</a> of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Catholic">Catholic church</a> who lived in the sixth century <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. He was also known as a founder of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glasgow_Cathedral" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Glasgow Cathedral">Glasgow Cathedral</a>. A Feast day for St Mungo, also known as St Mungo's Day,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is observed on the <a href="/wiki/13_January" title="13 January">13th of January</a> every year after he was canonised.</li>
<li>Interestingly, Lula Landry, the victim in <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">Robert Galbraith</a>'s first Cormoran Strike novel <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Cuckoo%27s_Calling" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Cuckoo&#39;s Calling">The Cuckoo's Calling</a></i>, lives in the fictional Kentigern Gardens, in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mayfair" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Mayfair">Mayfair</a>. Kentigern, as noted above, in the real name of the real-life Saint Mungo.</li>
<li>Mungo also shares part of his name with <a href="/wiki/Helena_Bonham_Carter" title="Helena Bonham Carter">Helena Bonham Carter</a>, the actress who portrays <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">Harry Potter films</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mungo_Bonham?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mungo_Bonham?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Mungo" is possibly derived from Welsh <i>mwyn</i> "gentle, kind". This was a nickname of the 6th-century Saint Kentigern.
</p><p>The surname "Bonham" is an Anglicisation of the French surname "Bonhomme", which itself is derived from the Old French "bon homme" meaning "good man".
</p>
