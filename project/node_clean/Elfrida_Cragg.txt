<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Elfrida_Cragg?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Elfrida_Cragg?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Elfrida" is from the Old English name <i>Ælfþryð</i>, meaning "elf strength" from the element <i>ælf</i> combined with <i>þryð</i>, "strength". <i>Ælfþryð</i> was a 10th-century queen of England. This name was rare after the Norman conquest, but it was revived in the 19th century.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Elfrida_Cragg?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Elfrida_Cragg?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Elfrida Cragg is possibly a misspelling of <a href="/wiki/Elfrida_Clagg" title="Elfrida Clagg">Elfrida Clagg</a>. This is likely, as Clagg was a celebrated Chieftainess of the <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Council" title="Wizards&#39; Council">Wizards' Council</a>, the organisation that preceded the Ministry of Magic in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>.</li></ul>
