<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Catullus_Spangle?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Catullus_Spangle?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his book, Professor Spangle set forth certain principles on the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a> that is, even today, widely accepted as true. He proposed that the Patronus was a hidden, but inherent part of one's personality, that only comes to light when the able caster needs it, such as when facing something as inhumanly evil as a <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementor</a> (this, according to Spangle, allows us to explain why Patronuses appear in forms that their casters may not expect, or for which they have never felt a particular affinity, such as why Hermione's Patronus was very frisky and moved around a lot, paralleling her).<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also suggested that witches and wizards whose Patronus took the form of their favourite animal were obsessive or eccentric, as they were wizards who could not hide their essential self in common life, in effect parading tendencies that others would prefer to conceal.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Catullus_Spangle?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Catullus_Spangle?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Professor Spangle was highly knowledgeable in the field of charms, having been a renowned researcher in these types of <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a>. His famous work <i><a href="/wiki/Charms_of_Defence_and_Deterrence" title="Charms of Defence and Deterrence">Charms of Defence and Deterrence</a></i> covered the theory of defensive charms with great detail, such as with the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>. He was presumably highly accomplished with the casting of charms himself, due to the extent of his knowledge of them.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Catullus_Spangle?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Catullus_Spangle?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catullus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Catullus">Gaius Valerius Catullus</a> (ca. 84–54 BC) was a Latin poet of the late Roman Republic who wrote in the neoteric style of poetry, and whose surviving poems are still widely read.</li>
<li>"<a href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/spangle" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:spangle">Spangle</a>" is a word used to refer to a sparkling metallic object or, as a verb, meaning "to adorn with small, brilliant bodies".</li></ul>
