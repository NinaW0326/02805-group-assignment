</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Baruffio%27s_wife?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Baruffio%27s_wife?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>N'oubliez jamais le sorcier Baruffio qui avait un défaut de prononciation et dont la femme s'est retrouvée avec un bison sur les épaules au lieu d'un vison.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Filius Flitwick</a>.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li>In the original English version of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Baruffio mispronounces "s" instead of "f" and ends up with a buffalo on his chest. On the French translation, Baruffio's incident is slightly different. It introduces another character: Baruffio's wife. Quoting from the French translation: "<i>N'oubliez jamais le sorcier Baruffio qui avait un défaut de prononciation et dont la femme s'est retrouvée avec un bison sur les épaules au lieu d'un vison</i>". (Loosely translated: "<i>Remember wizard Baruffio who had a speech impediment and whose wife was left with a buffalo on her shoulders instead of a mink</i>".). The translator, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_M%C3%A9nard" title="Jean-François Ménard">Jean-François Ménard</a>, plays with the alliteration "<i>bison</i>"/"<i>vison</i>" ("buffalo"/"mink"). As it is usually women who wear furs, the introduction of a female character was necessary. This change in translation is maintained on the French language version of  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> when Baruffio is mentioned in the entry for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Standard_Book_of_Spells,_Grade_1" title="The Standard Book of Spells, Grade 1">The Standard Book of Spells, Grade 1</a></i>.</li></ul>
