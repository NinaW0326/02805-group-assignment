<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Known_Arithmancers">Known Arithmancers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Arithmancer?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Known Arithmancers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Arithmancer?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Known Arithmancers"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The well-known Arithmancer, <a href="/wiki/Bridget_Wenlock" title="Bridget Wenlock">Bridget Wenlock</a>, was the first to discover the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> properties of the number <a href="/wiki/Seven" title="Seven">seven</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Septima_Vector" title="Septima Vector">Septima Vector</a> is known to have taught <a href="/wiki/Arithmancy" title="Arithmancy">Arithmancy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> from <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> to <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, quite possibly longer.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Arithmancer?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Arithmancer?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Arithmancer</i> and <i>Arithmancy</i> come from the Greek αριθμός (<i>arithmos</i>), meaning "<i>number</i>", and μαντεία (<i>manteia</i>), meaning "<i><a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">divination</a></i>".
</p>
