<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_pamphlet_maker_witch?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_pamphlet_maker_witch?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was described as being young, and she seemed to dislike <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> as she referred to her as an "old <a href="/wiki/Hag" title="Hag">hag</a>". She could possibly have been prejudiced towards <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a>, as she referred to them as "<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudbloods</a>", but as the term was so common during <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s reign at the Ministry it is possible she harboured no strong feelings about <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a> and was simply throwing out the term casually. She was puzzled when <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> (disguised as <a href="/wiki/Albert_Runcorn" title="Albert Runcorn">Albert Runcorn</a>) used a <a href="/wiki/Decoy_Detonator" title="Decoy Detonator">Decoy Detonator</a> to sneak into <a href="/wiki/Senior_Undersecretary_of_The_Minister_of_Magic%27s_Office" class="mw-redirect" title="Senior Undersecretary of The Minister of Magic&#39;s Office">Dolores Umbridge's Office</a>. She later blamed it on <a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Experimental_Charms" title="Committee on Experimental Charms">Experimental Charms</a>, asking her colleague if he remembered the <a href="/wiki/Poisonous_duck" title="Poisonous duck">poisonous duck</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
