<p>A <b>House-elf</b> (sometimes also referred to as just <b>Elves</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> <a href="/wiki/Being" title="Being">being</a> which is immensely devoted and loyal to the one designated as their master.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>House-elves serve <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> and are usually found under the employment of old wizarding families taking residence in elaborate establishments, like mansions, and must do everything that their masters command unless they are freed. A house-elf can only be freed when their master presents them with clothes.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>House-elves have <a href="/wiki/House-elf_magic" title="House-elf magic">their own brand of wandless magic</a> which, despite their small physical size, is very powerful.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> House-elves also presumably produce <a href="/wiki/Elf-made_wine" title="Elf-made wine">elf-made wine</a>, given its name.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magic">Magic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Magic"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dd><div class="noprint"><i>Main article&#58; <a href="/wiki/House-elf_magic" title="House-elf magic">House-elf magic</a></i></div></dd></dl>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Fight, fight for my master, the defender of the house-elves! Fight for master <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus</a>! Fight!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a> leading the house-elves into battle<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 243px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8a/DobbyDisapperates.gif/revision/latest?cb=20140422144534" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8a/DobbyDisapperates.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/241?cb=20140422144534" decoding="async" width="241" height="118" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="DobbyDisapperates.gif" data-image-key="DobbyDisapperates.gif" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:DobbyDisapperates.gif" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Dobby using his magic to <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparate</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>All house-elves have their own brand of <a href="/wiki/House-elf_magic" title="House-elf magic">powerful magic</a>, which allows them to perform tasks, such as Apparating, where <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> cannot.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> More than just a mere housekeeper, a house-elf is a ruthless protector of those to whom they give their allegiance. In <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> did not hesitate to protect <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, by using his magic on his former master <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Malfoy" title="Lucius Malfoy">Lucius Malfoy</a> to blast him away after he attempted to physically assault Harry on the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Staircase" title="Grand Staircase">Grand Staircase</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 240px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif/revision/latest?cb=20090628045537" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="238" height="104" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Dobby protecting Harry Potter.gif" data-image-key="Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/238?cb=20090628045537" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif/revision/latest?cb=20090628045537" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/238?cb=20090628045537" decoding="async" width="238" height="104" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Dobby protecting Harry Potter.gif" data-image-key="Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/73/Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/238?cb=20090628045537" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Dobby_protecting_Harry_Potter.gif" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Dobby blasting Lucius away in order to protect Harry Potter</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Their magic is limited by the lack of a wand. However, unlike some other magical beings, the house-elf is actually quite happy not to own a wand, as they do not require one for their daily activities. Apart from routine domestic tasks, house-elves usually have to obtain permission from their master before they would use their own brand of magic for other things. Although a house-elf may rarely act without permission, they would have to punish themselves for these acts.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is implied that with a wand and the proper knowledge, a house-elf may be able to do other magic, as <a href="/wiki/Winky" title="Winky">Winky</a> was falsely accused of using the <i><a href="/wiki/Morsmordre" title="Morsmordre">Morsmordre</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curse</a> to summon the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF9_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF9-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> once remarked that House-elves were better at performing advanced magic than <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which is testament to the impressive strength of their magical power.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_Wizardkind">Relationship with Wizardkind</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationship with Wizardkind">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Relationship with Wizardkind"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>House-elves are subject to the laws of their country's wizarding government, known as <a href="/wiki/Elf_Legislation" title="Elf Legislation">Elf Legislation</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Guidelines_on_house-elf_welfare" title="Guidelines on house-elf welfare">guidelines on house-elf welfare</a> were a set of regulations passed by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> regarding the treatment of house-elves. Despite their existence at the time, these guidelines were not enforced by the Ministry of Magic by <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>, and therefore many <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> households such as the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoys</a> and the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Blacks</a> mistreated their elves. This led wizards such as <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> to personally work to improve the lives of these creatures.
</p>

<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>GET OUT!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s fateful order to <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/2b/DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190128092906" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="250" height="128" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="DH1 HQ still Catching the crook.jpg" data-image-key="DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/2b/DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190128092906" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/2b/DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190128092906" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/2b/DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190128092906" decoding="async" width="250" height="128" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="DH1 HQ still Catching the crook.jpg" data-image-key="DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/2b/DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190128092906" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:DH1_HQ_still_Catching_the_crook.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Kreacher obeying Harry Potter's request</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>A house-elf must obey any commands given to them by their masters. At times, they seem to desire to disobey these orders, but as their master's orders are "the house-elf's highest law," they cannot, and are forced to punish themselves if they do. However, a house-elf may find loopholes within their master's or mistress's orders. For example, despite his loathing for Sirius Black, Kreacher could not disobey his direct orders. However, when Sirius yelled at him to "get out," which he meant to get out of the kitchen, Kreacher was able to interpret the command in a way that enabled him to leave <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">number 12 Grimmauld Place</a> and go to Sirius' cousins, Narcissa Malfoy and Bellatrix Lestrange.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%931997_school_year" title="1996–1997 school year">1996–1997 school year</a>, Harry Potter ordered Kreacher to follow <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> in order to find out what he was doing and where he was going, and carefully phrased his command to prevent Kreacher from contacting or alerting Draco. Kreacher, still reluctant to be owned by Harry, was unable to find a loophole in the orders about contacting Draco, but instead merely told Harry only about Draco's mundane daily activities.
</p>

<p>In the past in part due to their absolute obedience, house-elves have been treated very brutally by their owners. House-elves have no rights of their own and are viewed as servants without feeling or emotions.
</p><p>For instance, Kreacher had to sleep in a cupboard under a boiler, where he made himself a den and was given no attention while <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> was his master.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Before this, Voldemort forced Kreacher to drink <a href="/wiki/Emerald_Potion" title="Emerald Potion">the potion</a> in which his Horcrux was hidden to test its effectiveness, and was then left to die.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In addition, Voldemort framed the house-elf Hokey for the murder of Hepzibah Smith.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In some <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pureblood</a> families, such as the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Blacks</a>, the house-elves would be beheaded when they were too old to carry tea trays. This practice was invented by <a href="/wiki/Elladora_Black" title="Elladora Black">Elladora Black</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP6_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The house-elf <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> was so mistreated and under-appreciated by the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoy family</a> that he independently sought out to aid and protect <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, Dobby was slain by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> in the act of protecting Harry's life.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Also, <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> made a house-elf test of his bottles of mead for poison (although he likely had antidotes on hand).
</p><p>When a house-elf is mistreated, they will typically work just enough to obey their master's commands. However, if even the slightest loophole can be found in a master's orders, house-elves that are unhappy with their master will exploit that loophole to their advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The best way for a master to ensure full loyalty and compliance with their orders is to simply treat them properly. This was evidenced in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> convinced <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> to be nicer to <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a>. Soon, Kreacher began to accept his new master, and started serving him much more enthusiastically.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/House-elves" class="mw-redirect" title="House-elves">House-elves</a> are so intensely loyal to their masters that they will not allow themselves to be set free unless their master presents them with clothes and this loyalty, in and of itself, is something akin to a code of honour among their kind such that it is not uncommon for a house-elf to carry out their imperatives to the best of their ability, and at great personal risk, even if at the cusp of death. Unfortunately, this could also mean that they are left utterly helpless if ordered to be by their masters, even if the elf wishes otherwise, as seen when Kreacher apparently wasn't able to prevent Regulus' sacrifice after the latter's explicit orders for Kreacher to let him die through inaction and not interfering. To symbolise this, they usually wear makeshift clothes made with found objects such as pillowcases and rags. These clothes can become quite filthy, and yet the house-elf will not clean their clothes to further express that they have no needs which are not specifically commanded to them by the master. House-elves will torture and maim themselves if they think it will please the master (or to punish themselves.)<sup id="cite_ref-CoS_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CoS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>House-elves not only serve one specific master, but the whole family of the master and whoever the house-elf is ordered by the master to serve; however, if an order or instruction from a specific master they serve has been given and worded specifically enough, then not even the other members of the family would be able to circumvent it as seen when Regulus told Kreacher not to divulge the circumstances concerning the former's demise to the rest of the Blacks, with that knowledge resurfacing only after Harry was given ownership over the house-elf and ordering Kreacher to reveal what he knows, not violating Regulus' final command seeing that Harry wasn't a Black. When <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> was killed in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> inherited the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>'s servant <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Although Kreacher was first unwilling to enter Harry's service, and only did so as a result of the house-elf's enslavement, after he was treated kindly, he eventually formed a fierce loyalty to Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite the seemingly horrid lifestyle that house-elves endure, house-elves seem to actually <i>enjoy</i> being enslaved. With few exceptions (<a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> being one of them), house-elves will feel insulted if their master attempts to pay them, give them pensions, or reward their service with anything except kindness. In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> began hiding clothes in <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a> in an attempt to free the house-elves of Hogwarts, the house-elves felt rather insulted, and everyone except Dobby refused to clean the Gryffindor common room in protest.
</p><p>Despite Hogwarts having had a large number of house-elves for centuries, it was only in their fourth year that Harry, Ron, and Hermione (rather shockingly) discovered their existence at Hogwarts, which happened inadvertently when the ghost of Sir Nicholas casually mentioned them being scared by Peeves the Poltergeist's tantrums. Sir Nicholas explained to them that the house-elves work in hiding, and it is considered the mark of a good house-elf that he or she does all the work but his/her existence is not even noticed.
</p>

<p>It should be noted, however, that there are elements of coercion in some house-elves' loyalty. <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a> disliked <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> even when he was his master, and helped to sabotage Sirius by assisting <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narcissa_Malfoy" title="Narcissa Malfoy">Narcissa Malfoy</a>, though this may have been easier for him since both witches had been born <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Blacks</a>. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> described Kreacher as "<i>forced to do Sirius's bidding, because Sirius was the last of the family to which he was enslaved, but he felt no true loyalty to him</i>."<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Kreacher also initially despised serving Harry, only coming to be willingly loyal to him after Harry showed him kindness and respect and pointed out that by cooperating with him and his friends, Kreacher would be honouring the dying actions of <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus Black</a>, the late master he was very fond of. <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> pointed out that Kreacher was accustomed to "<i>bad, even brutal treatment</i>" and that he was "<i>loyal to people who are kind to him.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The house-elves of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a> were, in old age, beheaded and their heads were sickeningly stuffed and mounted on the house walls. This was, no doubt, considered by the house-elves as the very highest honour.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Winky was absolutely loyal to the <a href="/wiki/Crouch_family" title="Crouch family">Crouch family</a>, and when she was dismissed for failure to keep Crouch Jnr under control, she suffered a mental breakdown, thinking that her release was the ultimate disgrace to her family.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While some house-elves were treated abysmally by their masters, some were genuinely loved and cared for. Some wizards treated their house-elves like adored pets or dolls, lavishing praise on them for doing their duties. An example of this is Hepzibah Smith, who was very talkative and pleasant with her house-elf Hokey about her mundane affairs while Hokey was attending to her own tasks.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>A veritable legion of house-elves are enlisted by <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. They work the kitchens, preparing feasts for the entire school. They also move trunks and baggage to and from rooms, clean dormitories, and presumably other areas of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">castle</a> as well.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff" title="Helga Hufflepuff">Helga Hufflepuff</a> is credited with bringing the house-elves to Hogwarts. Though technically still bound to servitude, bringing them to a place with good working conditions was the most moral choice available at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-PC_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PC-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They were likely treated extremely well by Professor Dumbledore. In <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, they became angry with <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> as she made attempts to free them. <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Winky" title="Winky">Winky</a>, who came under Hogwarts' employ at the time, were considered disgraces to the rest of their colleagues due to Dobby being paid and receiving a vacation while Winky getting drunk out of self-pity. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, the house-elves fought against the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a> leading them. They defended their masters by using kitchen knives to stab at the attackers' ankles.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, in outrage for the inhumane treatment of house-elves, created the student organisation <a href="/wiki/Society_for_the_Promotion_of_Elfish_Welfare" title="Society for the Promotion of Elfish Welfare">S.P.E.W.</a>—Society for the Promotion of Elfish Welfare—in an attempt to win rights for house-elves. She managed to get several students to join, paying a fee of two Sickles, though they only did so to stop her from badgering them. These students included <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

Hermione even tried to trick house-elves into picking up little hats and socks she had knitted and left around the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> dormitory so as to grant them freedom. The house-elves did not appreciate this gesture; on the contrary, they took it as an insult and refused to clean the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">common room</a>, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GoF_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GoF-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inspiration">Inspiration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Inspiration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Inspiration"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Elves or similar creatures doing menial tasks around a home, and leaving when rewarded for their tasks with money or clothes, is a common motif in European folklore.
</p><p>The most well-known example of this in the English-speaking world is the Brownie, a small fairy-like creature who helps around a home in exchange for daily food and drink (in the form of hot milk, honey and gruel) but will depart forever if it is paid in human money. In some of the legends, rather than departing, the Brownie would instead turn malicious and antagonise their owners, much like a Harry Potter <a href="/wiki/Poltergeist" title="Poltergeist">Poltergeist</a>; such a "wicked Brownie" would be called a <a href="/wiki/Boggart" title="Boggart">Boggart</a>, though the name, of course, refers to an entirely different creature in <i>Harry Potter</i>.
</p><p>Finally, one last possible source of inspiration for <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s House-Elves is French folklore's "farfadet". Originating in the southern areas, one specific legends about farfadets is that they were "wrinkled, brown-skinned midgets who went around either naked or wearing dirty rags", a description that is almost identical to Rowling's elves. The Farfadets would help around a farming home, completing tasks not done in the day by the human servants. They would leave forever if the master of the house gave them new clothes to replace their shabby old ones; once again, the parallels are obvious, though in this case, the reasoning is that Farfadets are proud creatures who are offended by the implication that their clothes <i>needed</i> replacing.
</p><p>To have the creatures specifically called 'elves' may be an allusion to the fairy tale of <i>The Elves and the Shoemaker</i>, where a group of elves worked for a shoemaker until his wife, in a fit of generosity, sewed them little clothes and gave them to the elves.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/House-elf?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, two house-elves are briefly seen wearing green and red and riding llamas at the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a>. <span class="template-c">(See <a href="/wiki/File:House_elves_on_llamas-Quidditch_World_Cup-GOF.jpg" title="File:House elves on llamas-Quidditch World Cup-GOF.jpg">image</a>)</span></li>
<li>According to the <a href="/wiki/Wizards%27_Ordinary_Magic_and_Basic_Aptitude_Test" title="Wizards&#39; Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test">WOMBAT</a> test, it is possible that house-elves have an average life expectancy of 200 years, cannot be ordered to kill themselves, breed infrequently and only with their master's permission, can override wizard enchantments, and have an allegiance to their home rather than its inhabitants. The answer key of the test<sup class="noprint">&#91;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Wikipedia:Citing sources"><i><span title="The material in the vicinity of this tag needs references to reliable sources." style="white-space: nowrap;">citation needed</span></i></a>&#93;</sup> awards five points for counting "cannot be ordered to kill themselves" as false, and one point for counting "A house-elf's allegiance is foremost to its house (rather than to the inhabitants of the house)", likely implying that this is only partially true. Perhaps house-elves are loyal to the inhabitants of a house only so long as they inhabit the house. The other statements are presumably true, and a house-elf can likely be ordered to kill itself, although most owners would have little reason to use this order.</li>
<li>House-elves are never identified as "beings" in <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i>, though presumably they are.</li>
<li>No other creature in the series is referred to as an "elf", making the precision that they are <i>House</i>-Elves seem relatively pointless. On the other hand, <a href="/wiki/Erkling" title="Erkling">Erklings</a> are referred to by <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newton Scamander</a> as "elvish creatures", implying that "elf" may be the name for a general class that includes several species including House-Elves, Erklings, and (presumably) <a href="/wiki/Yumbo" title="Yumbo">Yumboes</a>.</li>
<li>It may be possible for elves to breed with humans as one <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> student stated that the students in the house thought <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Filius Flitwick</a> was part elf, but had never been rude enough to ask (in fact Flitwick was <a href="/wiki/Half-goblin" title="Half-goblin">part goblin</a>). <a href="/wiki/Irma_Dugard" title="Irma Dugard">Irma Dugard</a> was also described as a <a href="/wiki/Part_elf" title="Part elf">half-elf</a>.</li></ul>
