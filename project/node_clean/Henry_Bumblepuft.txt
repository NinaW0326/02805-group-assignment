<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Bumblepuft?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Henry_Bumblepuft?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Henry" is from the Germanic name <i>Heimirich</i>, which means "home ruler", composed of the elements <i>heim</i>, "home" and <i>ric,</i> "power", "ruler". It was later commonly spelled Heinrich, with the spelling altered due to the influence of other Germanic names, like Haganrich, in which the first element is <i>hagan</i>, "enclosure".
</p>

<ul><li>This portrait was commissioned specifically for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_films" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter films"><i>Harry Potter</i> films</a>, his face being inspired on the likeness of <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Craig" title="Stuart Craig">Stuart Craig</a>, production designer for all the eight <i>Harry Potter</i> films.<sup id="cite_ref-PTS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PTS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The painting was based on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Hogarth" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William Hogarth">William Hogarth</a>'s 1745 self-portrait work <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.tate.org.uk/art/images/work/N/N00/N00112_10.jpg"><i>The Painter and His Pug</i></a>.</li></ul>
