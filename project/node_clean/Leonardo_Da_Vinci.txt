<p><b>Leonardo da Vinci</b> (<a href="/wiki/15_April" title="15 April">15 April</a>, <a href="/wiki/1452" class="mw-redirect" title="1452">1452</a>—<a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1519" class="mw-redirect" title="1519">1519</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymath" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Polymath">polymath</a> who lived during the Renaissance era.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was a contemporary of <a href="/wiki/Phillipus_von_Hohenheim" title="Phillipus von Hohenheim">Paracelsus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicolaus_Copernicus" title="Nicolaus Copernicus">Copernicus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSpc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSpc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Da Vinci is perhaps the most diversely talented person ever to have lived, with interests and pursuits ranging from painting, sculpting, architecture, music, science, mathematics, engineering, invention, anatomy, geology, cartography, botany and writing.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He is widely considered to be one of the greatest painters of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> One of his most famous works is his <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">Portrait</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mona_Lisa" title="Mona Lisa">Mona Lisa</a>, a copy of which hung at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSpc_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSpc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li>Da Vinci died 479 years to the day before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> shows a number of images of <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolves</a> during his lecture as a temporary <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> teacher. One of these images is a variant of the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitruvian_Man" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Vitruvian Man">Vitruvian Man</a></i>, a famous sketch by Leonardo da Vinci.</li></ul>
