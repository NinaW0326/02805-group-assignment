<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amanda?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amanda?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Amanda did not appear in any of the books, and appeared solely in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i> film.</li>
<li>She was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Christina_Petrou" title="Christina Petrou">Christina Petrou</a> in the film.</li>
<li>A shortened form of the name <i>Amanda</i> is <i>Mandy</i>. Also in her first year in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> was <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Brocklehurst" title="Mandy Brocklehurst">Mandy Brocklehurst</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> like Amanda.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is a possibility therefore, that Amanda and Mandy are the same person, just addressed in different ways.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amanda?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amanda?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Amanda</i> is a feminine form of <i>Amandus</i>. However, it was not used during the Middle Ages. In the 17th century it was recreated by authors and poets who based it directly on Latin amanda "lovable, worthy of love". Notably, it was used by the playwright Colley Cibber for a character in his play 'Love's Last Shift' (1696). It came into regular use during the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
