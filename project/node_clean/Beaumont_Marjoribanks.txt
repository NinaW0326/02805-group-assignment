<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was the first person to have discovered <a href="/wiki/Gillyweed" title="Gillyweed">Gillyweed</a>. For his contributions to the field of Herbology, he was featured on a bronze <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/1993-1994_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1993-1994 school year">1993-1994 school year</a>, there was a bust of Marjoribanks on the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic_Classroom" title="History of Magic Classroom">History of Magic Classroom</a>, next to <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns" title="Cuthbert Binns">Cuthbert Binns</a>'s desk.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a></b>: Marjoribanks was a well-renowned and prominent professional <a href="/wiki/Herbologist" title="Herbologist">Herbologist</a>, who would have been exceptionally knowledgeable in the study of magical and mudnane plants and fungi, and very skilled in the handling and cultivation of them. He collected and studied many rare magical plants and flowers throughout his lifetime, earning him recognition on a bronze <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> for his superb achievements in this field.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>His first name "Beaumont" comes from a French surname meaning "beautiful mountain."</li>
<li>His surname comes from the Scottish Lowlands <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clan_Marjoribanks">Clan Marjoribanks</a>, whose name is (according to legend) derived from the area originally called "Marjorie's Banks" in honour of Princess <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marjorie_Bruce">Marjorie Bruce</a>, who owned the territory alongside her husband. The name "Marjorie" is derived in part from the name of the herb <i>marjoram.</i></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Marjoribanks?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Despite the fact that it is said Majoribanks discovered Gillyweed, <a href="/wiki/Elladora_Ketteridge" title="Elladora Ketteridge">Elladora Ketteridge</a> is said to have discovered its effects about a century earlier. This error would be fixed if the dates were swtiched, or if Majoribanks was alive before, or at the same time as Ketteridge, as he only discovered the existence of Gillyweed, while Ketteridge discovered its magical effects.</li>
<li>There appear to be inconsistencies in the spelling of this individual's surname: in the video games, it appears to be spelt "Majorbanks". In the real-life <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Cards</a> once released in the United States (which were penned by <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>), it is spelt "<a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.hp-lexicon.org/images/wizcards/03-beaumont-marjoribanks.jpg">Marjoribanks</a>".</li>
<li>The Chocolate Frog Card portrait of Marjoribanks featured in the video games appears to be a direct homage to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rembrandt_Peale" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Rembrandt Peale">Rembrandt Peale</a>'s 1801 oil painting <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubens_Peale_with_a_Geranium" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Rubens Peale with a Geranium"><i>Rubens Peale with a Geranium</i></a> in both pose and setting.</li></ul>
