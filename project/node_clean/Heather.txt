<b>Heather</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> student at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a> she became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a> and was ranked eight on the Club's top-ten <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duellists</a> list.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Heather?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Heather?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Heather" comes from the English word <i>heather</i> for the variety of small shrubs with pink or white flowers which commonly grow in rocky areas. It is derived from Middle English <i>hather</i>. It was first used as a given name in the late 19th century, though it did not become popular until the last half of the 20th century.
</p>
