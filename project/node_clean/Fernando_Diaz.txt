<b>Fernando Diaz</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014" title="2014">2014</a> <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_National_Quidditch_team" title="Brazilian National Quidditch team">Brazilian National Quidditch team</a>. He, along with fellow Chaser <a href="/wiki/Alejandra_Alonso" title="Alejandra Alonso">Alejandra Alonso</a>, was described as one of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>'s finest by <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Potter</a> for her article in the <a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_National_Quidditch_team" title="American National Quidditch team">USA</a>-Brazil game.<sup id="cite_ref-Insider_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Insider-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/BRAZIL_VERSUS_HAITI" title="BRAZIL VERSUS HAITI">Brazil vs Haiti</a>&#160;match, him and his fellow Brazil Chasers, <a href="/wiki/Alejandra_Alonso" title="Alejandra Alonso">Alejandra Alonso</a>&#160;and&#160;<a href="/wiki/Gon%C3%A7alo_Flores" title="Gonçalo Flores">Gonçalo Flores</a>, made as many as thirty attempts to score during the beginning of the game, although they only made ten goals. <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Potter</a> also commended all three Brazilian Chasers&#160;for keeping their heads during the <a href="/wiki/BRAZIL_VERSUS_WALES" title="BRAZIL VERSUS WALES">Brazil vs Wales</a>&#160;match that was deemed the most contentious match of the tournament.&#160;
</p><p>In the semifinal match between Brazil and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a>, Diaz and Alonso&#160;both dropped the <a href="/wiki/Quaffle" title="Quaffle">Quaffle</a> twice, something that was common in that match due to the nerves associated with the possibility of Brazil's reaching the World Cup final for the first time since <a href="/wiki/1982" title="1982">1982</a>.
</p>
