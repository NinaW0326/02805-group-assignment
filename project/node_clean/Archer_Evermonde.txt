<b>Minister Archer Evermonde</b> was <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> from <a href="/wiki/1912" title="1912">1912</a> to <a href="/wiki/1923" title="1923">1923</a>. He was ultimately elected at least twice.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Minister Evermonde's term, the <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> world became involved in a large-scale military conflict that became known as the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>. Evermonde was responsible for passing emergency legislation forbidding wizards and witches to get involved, in an attempt to prevent mass breaches of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statue of Secrecy</a>. This did not prevent thousands of wizards from aiding Muggles discreetly and where they could.<sup id="cite_ref-WW_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> For these actions, Minister Evermonde earned <a href="/wiki/Wizengamot" title="Wizengamot">Wizengamot</a> member <a href="/wiki/Henry_Potter" title="Henry Potter">Henry Potter</a>'s public condemnation, which caused a minor stir at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-PotterFamilyPottermore_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PotterFamilyPottermore-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
