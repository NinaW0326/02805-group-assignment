<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dominique_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dominique_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominique_(given_name)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dominique (given name)">Dominique</a></i> is the French feminine form of the masculine name <i>Dominic</i>, derived from the Late Latin <i>Dominicus</i>, meaning "of the Lord". It was traditionally given to a child born on Sunday in Catholic countries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is also the name of a popular French <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominique" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dominique">song</a> by <i>Soeur Sourire</i>, the Belgian "Singing Nun", about Saint Dominic and his fight against the Albigensians.
</p>
