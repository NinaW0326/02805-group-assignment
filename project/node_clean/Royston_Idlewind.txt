<p><b>Royston Idlewind</b> was a former <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> player in the <a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">1960s</a>, the star <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_National_Quidditch_team" title="Australian National Quidditch team">Australian team</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a>, he was appointed <a href="/wiki/International_Director" title="International Director">International Director</a> of the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards_Quidditch_Committee" title="International Confederation of Wizards Quidditch Committee">International Confederation of Wizards Quidditch Committee</a>, but later resigned after the draconian regulations regarding crowd control he imposed were flouted <i>en masse</i> by the spectators of the final of the <a href="/wiki/1974_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1974 Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/1974" title="1974">1974</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Royston_Idlewind?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Royston_Idlewind?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quidditch_career">Quidditch career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Royston_Idlewind?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Quidditch career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Royston_Idlewind?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Quidditch career"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Royston Idlewind was born in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, sometime before <a href="/wiki/1949" title="1949">1949</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">1960s</a>, he was "Australia's star <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a>" — in which capacity he was noted to have endured many <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxes</a>, something that fuelled his dislike of spectator crowds later in his life.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was also part of the winning <a href="/wiki/Australian_National_Quidditch_team" title="Australian National Quidditch team">Australian</a> squad during the <a href="/wiki/1966_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1966 Quidditch World Cup">1966 Quidditch World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_International_Director_of_the_ICWQC">As International Director of the ICWQC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Royston_Idlewind?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: As International Director of the ICWQC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Royston_Idlewind?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As International Director of the ICWQC"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a>, Royston Idlewind was named <a href="/wiki/International_Director" title="International Director">International Director</a> of the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards_Quidditch_Committee" title="International Confederation of Wizards Quidditch Committee">International Confederation of Wizards Quidditch Committee</a>. Upon his contentious appointment, he immediately issued the statement that the only thing he disliked about <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> was the crowds, thus gaining the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>'s dislike and distrust — this petty dislike soon turned into outright hostility when Idlewind passed a number of draconian regulations on crowd control, the worst of which was the ban of all wands from the stadium except those carried by <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards_Quidditch_Committee" title="International Confederation of Wizards Quidditch Committee">ICWQC</a> officials.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many fans threatened to boycott the <a href="/wiki/1974_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1974 Quidditch World Cup">1974 Quidditch World Cup</a> in protest, but seeing as empty stands were Idlewind's secret ambition, their strategy was not effective. The World Cup duly commenced and the crowds that attended carried with them not their <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a>, but a new style of musical instrument called a <a href="/wiki/Dissimulator" title="Dissimulator">Dissimulator</a>: a tube-like object that issued bright lights and colourful puffs of smoke in national colours and emitted loud cheers for the teams being supported. For the first few matches of the tournament, very few wizards showed up, but those that did brought along Dissimulators, enlivening the match considerably. The Dissimulator craze grew, as did the crowds.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the day of the final match between <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, the stands were packed with a record crowd of wizards, each carrying his own Dissimulator. When Royston Idlewind appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Top_Box" title="Top Box">Top Box</a>, a hundred thousand Dissimulators emitted loud raspberries turned back instantly into the wands they had been concealing all along. Humiliated by the mass flouting of his pet law, Royston Idlewind resigned immediately, among celebrations.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
