<p><b>Propaganda</b> is information, especially of a biased or misleading nature, used to promote or publicise a particular political cause or point of view. The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> was notorious for using propaganda against those that held a differing opinion from their own or as a means of increasing newspaper sales. <a href="/wiki/Muggle-borns" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle-borns">Muggle-borns</a>, Harry Potter, and <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> have all been victims of propaganda.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Propaganda?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Propaganda?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Propaganda is information that is not objective and is used primarily to influence an audience and further an agenda, often by presenting facts selectively to encourage a particular synthesis or perception, or using loaded language to produce an emotional rather than a rational response to the information that is presented. Propaganda is often associated with material prepared by the magical and Muggle governments, but activist groups, companies, and the media can also produce propaganda.
</p><p>In the twentieth century, the term propaganda has been associated with a manipulative approach, but propaganda historically was a neutral descriptive term. A wide range of materials and media are used for conveying propaganda messages including: posters, pamphlets, radio broadcasts, and newspaper printouts.
</p><p>For example, the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> has a somewhat negative reputation for being incredibly biased, corrupt, and deceptive. As there is strong government control of the newspaper the facts can be misleading, if not outright fabrications, and that certain means of gathering information appear to be somewhat unethical. Government control of the Ministry is shown in the way that the <i>Daily Prophet</i> appears to pressure the government and go around official sources, and several people in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s circles feel that the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> "leans heavily" on <i>Prophet</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Propaganda?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Propaganda?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Campaign_to_discredit_Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter" title="Campaign to discredit Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter">campaign to discredit</a> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> was a prime example of propaganda.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF36_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF36-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Due to paranoia, Minister for Magic <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Fudge</a> believed that Dumbledore was trying to usurp his position of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>. He not only used the <i>Daily Prophet</i> to make it look as though Dumbledore and Potter were liars and "nutters," but also stripped Dumbledore of many of his titles and statuses.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP5_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 146px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/1/1d/Mudbloods.PNG/revision/latest?cb=20150623205823" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/1/1d/Mudbloods.PNG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/144?cb=20150623205823" decoding="async" width="144" height="231" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Mudbloods.PNG" data-image-key="Mudbloods.PNG" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Mudbloods.PNG" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><i><a href="/wiki/Mudbloods_and_the_Dangers_They_Pose_to_a_Peaceful_Pure-Blood_Society" title="Mudbloods and the Dangers They Pose to a Peaceful Pure-Blood Society">Mudbloods and the Dangers They Pose to a Peaceful Pure-Blood Society</a>, </i>propaganda published by the Ministry of Magic</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Harry, for supporting Dumbledore and being the one who claimed <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> returned, also suffered from this campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP5_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Fudge planted <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_High_Inquisitor" title="Hogwarts High Inquisitor">Hogwarts High Inquisitor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> in order to further destabilise Dumbledore and Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP11_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP11-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP12_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP12-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP15_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP15-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Fudge also made it clear that anyone siding with Dumbledore should clear out their desks in the Ministry's office. Due to the great influence the Ministry had, the majority of the wizarding community ceased supporting Dumbledore and Harry and lost a chance to prepare themselves against Voldemort. The campaign also pressed the position that Harry was a deluded attention-seeker that could not be trusted.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP5_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Ministry also ran a smear campaign against <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> during the 1995-1996 school year. When <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> suffered a mass breakout Fudge blamed the incident on "<i>notorious mass murderer Sirius Black</i>", as Sirius had previous experience breaking out of Azkaban. Fudge also blamed other strange happenings that occured around this time on Sirius as well.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP25_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP25-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Mudbloods and the Dangers They Pose to a Peaceful Pure-Blood Society</i> is a pamphlet that was printed and distributed enmasse by the Ministry of Magic while it was under Lord Voldemort's control during the height of the Second Wizarding War. It was written by Dolores Umbridge, head of the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a>, and contained propaganda against <a href="/wiki/Muggle-borns" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle-borns">Muggle-borns</a>, disparagingly referred to as "<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudbloods</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Propaganda?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Propaganda?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Propaganda</i> is a modern Latin word, the gerundive form of <i>propagare</i>, meaning <i>to spread</i> or <i>to propagate</i>, thus <i>propaganda</i> means <i>that which is to be propagated</i>. Originally this word derived from an administrative body of the Catholic church (congregation) created in 1622, called the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congregation_for_the_Evangelization_of_Peoples" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Congregation for the Evangelization of Peoples">Sacra Congregatio de Propaganda Fide</a></i> ("Sacred Congregation for the Propagation of the Faith"), or informally simply <i>Propaganda</i>. Its activity was aimed at "propagating" the Catholic faith in non-Catholic countries.
</p>
