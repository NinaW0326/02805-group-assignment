The <b>Unidentified Magical Statue</b> was a bronze statue depicting a woman sitting on a pedestal, located in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. It served as entrance to <a href="/wiki/Montmartre" title="Montmartre">Montmartre</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding</a>&#160;part of Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-Le_Point_Pop_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Point_Pop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Magical_Statue?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Magical_Statue?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">non-magical individuals</a>, the statue would appear as any ordinary statue, but when a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> individual was near, the statue would become animated, providing entrance to Paris' counterpart of <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>. The statue came&#160;to life when approached by <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a>&#160;— the statue would move its leg and dress away from the pedestal it was set on, opening the passage.<sup id="cite_ref-Le_Point_Pop_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Point_Pop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Magical_Statue?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Magical_Statue?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Porpentina Goldstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newton Scamander</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Kowalski" title="Jacob Kowalski">Jacob Kowalski</a> used the statue to gain entrance to <a href="/wiki/Montmartre" title="Montmartre">Montmartre</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding</a> part of Paris for their missions.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li>The statue is based on a real world statue named <i>Pythia</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> sculpted by <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> artist <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ad%C3%A8le_d%27Affry" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Adèle d&#39;Affry">Marcello</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> located in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Opera" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Paris Opera">Paris Opera</a>.
<ul><li>"Pythia," also known as the Oracle of Delphi,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is a representation of the Delphi pythoness.</li></ul></li></ul>
