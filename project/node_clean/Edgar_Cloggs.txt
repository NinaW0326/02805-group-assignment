<b>Edgar Cloggs</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a> who was often found hanging around the <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_pitch" title="Quidditch pitch">Quidditch pitch</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> for as long as anyone could remember. He was willing to help the <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_team" title="Quidditch team">Quidditch teams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> practise.<sup id="cite_ref-QWC_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QWC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By the year of <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, he was also included on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>, for his achievements in the sport.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Edgar_Cloggs?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Edgar_Cloggs?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Derived from the Old English elements <i>ead</i> "rich, blessed" and <i>gar</i> "spear". This was the name of a <a href="/wiki/First_millennium_A.D." title="First millennium A.D.">10th century</a> English king who is regarded as a saint. The name did not survive long after the Norman conquest, but it was revived in the 18th century, in part due to a character by this name in Sir Walter Scott's novel 'The Bride of Lammermoor' (1819). Famous bearers include author and poet Edgar Allan Poe (1809-1849), French impressionist painter Edgar Degas (1834-1917), and author Edgar Rice Burroughs (1875-1950).
</p>
