<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malecrit?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malecrit?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A playwright, Malecrit wrote the <i><a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9las,_Je_me_suis_Transfigur%C3%A9_Les_Pieds" title="Hélas, Je me suis Transfiguré Les Pieds">Hélas, Je me suis Transfiguré Les Pieds</a></i>, a play which included a reference to the game of <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>, showing that the game had spread to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> by the early <a href="/wiki/1400s" title="1400s">1400s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As reported by French wizarding newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Cri_De_La_Gargouille" class="mw-redirect" title="Le Cri De La Gargouille">Le Cri De La Gargouille</a></i>, in <a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a>, some unpublished works by Malecrit were found in a <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragon</a> cave.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Malecrit?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Malecrit?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Malecrit" presumably derives from the French "<i>mal écrit</i>," which means "<i>poorly written</i>". Interestingly, the name was translated into French as "Forbien Narré" (pronounced "<i>fort bien narré</i>", which means "<i>well narrated</i>").
</p>
