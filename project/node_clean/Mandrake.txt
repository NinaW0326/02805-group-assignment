<p>A <b>Mandrake</b>, also known as <b>Mandragora</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> and sentient plant which has a root that looks like a <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> (like a baby when the plant is young, but maturing as the plant grows). When matured, its cry can be <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">fatal</a> to any person who hears it.<sup id="cite_ref-CS6_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mandrake?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mandrake?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Venusia_Crickerly" title="Venusia Crickerly">Venusia Crickerly</a> died in <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1912</a>, following a freak Mandrake-related gardening accident.<sup id="cite_ref-PMministers_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMministers-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mandrakes are part of the <a href="/wiki/Second_year" title="Second year">second-year</a> <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a> curriculum at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS6_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/1992-1993_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1992-1993 school year">1992-1993 school year</a>, the school's growth of Mandrakes served the additional purpose of making a <a href="/wiki/Mandrake_Restorative_Draught" title="Mandrake Restorative Draught">Restorative Draught</a> to revive all those who had been <a href="/wiki/Petrification" title="Petrification">Petrified</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> openings.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Professor Sprout</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, and other students, lobbed Mandrakes over the Castle's battlements to attack the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a> which affected the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, Mandrakes were amongst the various <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> items caught up as <a href="/wiki/Foundable" title="Foundable">Foundables</a> which had to be dealt with by members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Whenever unearthed, the root screams. The scream of a mature Mandrake when it is unearthed will kill any person who hears it, but a young Mandrake's screams will usually only knock a person out for several hours. When <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> students study Mandrakes in <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a> class, <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Pomona Sprout</a> had the students wear <a href="/wiki/Earmuffs" title="Earmuffs">earmuffs</a> to protect their ears from the Mandrake's cries.<sup id="cite_ref-CS6_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS6-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Mandrakes not only resemble humans, but also have similar behaviours to them. In the <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%931993_school_year" title="1992–1993 school year">1992-1993 school year</a>, the Mandrakes, at one point of time, became moody and secretive, which indicated that they were reaching adolescence.<sup id="cite_ref-COS13_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Later on, they threw a loud and raucous party, which is comparable to humans when they are teenagers, which made Pomona Sprout very happy indeed.<sup id="cite_ref-COS14_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS14-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> also mentioned the Mandrakes having acne. Mandrakes are fully matured when they start moving into each others' pots. When matured, Mandrakes can be cut up to serve as a prime ingredient for the <a href="/wiki/Mandrake_Restorative_Draught" title="Mandrake Restorative Draught">Mandrake Restorative Draught</a>, which is used to cure those who have been <a href="/wiki/Petrification" title="Petrification">Petrified</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS16_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS16-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>

<p>A Mandrake's scream is very similar to (if not the same as) a <a href="/wiki/Banshee" title="Banshee">banshee</a>'s scream, which is also fatal.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Dugbog" title="Dugbog">Dugbog</a>'s favourite food is Mandrakes, which leads to Mandrake-growers finding nothing but a bloody mess when pulling their plants out.<sup id="cite_ref-FBR_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FBR-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Flesh-Eating_Slug" title="Flesh-Eating Slug">Flesh-Eating Slugs</a> are known to favour eating Mandrakes as well.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mandrake?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mandrake?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The (<a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a>) Mandrake plant has been used since <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">ancient times</a> as a medicinal plant and has a tradition associated with <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> activities and witchcraft. It is a member of the nightshade family. It contains hyoscyamine, scopolamine, and mandragorin. Medically, it has been used as a pain killer and a sedative. It was used in ancient times for surgery. An overdose, though, can be fatal.
</p><p>Because of its roots' shapes resembling <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a>, it has been used in magical operations, and as a supposed aphrodisiac. There are variations on the plant, <i>Mandragora Offininarum</i> being the most usual form, with the smaller <i>Mandragora autumnalis</i> having the same properties. Some folk traditions call the latter (<i>M. autumnalis</i>) variety as "Womandrake" in distinction to <i>M. Officinarum</i> as "Mandrake." A third variant, <i>Mandragora turcomanica,</i> is a nearly extinct and very rare variety, found mostly in Turkey and a few areas of Iran. <i>M. turcomanica</i> is distinguished from <i>M. autumnalis</i> by having larger fruit.
</p><p>In the Western Hemisphere, another plant, <i>Podophyllum petaltum</i>, is called the American Mandrake. The American Mandrake is unrelated to the European variety, and has fewer medical applications. It, too, is poisonous.
</p>
