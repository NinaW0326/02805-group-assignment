<p><b>Cedric Diggory</b> (<a href="/wiki/September" title="September">September</a>/<a href="/wiki/October" title="October">October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1977" title="1977">1977</a> – <a href="/wiki/24_June" title="24 June">24 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was the son of <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">Amos Diggory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother" title="Cedric Diggory&#39;s mother">his wife</a>. He started attending <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in <a href="/wiki/1989" title="1989">1989</a>, and was sorted into the  <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff House</a>. During his time at school he was a <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">prefect</a> and captained the <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff_Quidditch_team" title="Hufflepuff Quidditch team">Hufflepuff Quidditch team</a>, playing as <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a>. In his sixth year Cedric put his name forward to compete in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>.
</p><p>He was selected as the Hogwarts Triwizard Champion and, although overshadowed in the press by the entry of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, Cedric enjoyed almost overwhelming support from the student body, and after the first two tasks, tied with Harry for first place. Cedric and Harry completed the Third Task together. Both took hold of the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Cup" title="Triwizard Cup">Triwizard Cup</a>, which <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">transported</a> them to the <a href="/wiki/Little_Hangleton" title="Little Hangleton">Little Hangleton</a> graveyard. In the graveyard, Cedric was murdered by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a>, on the order of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>.
</p><p>During the following duel between Harry and Voldemort, Cedric's echo reappeared during <i><a href="/wiki/Reverse_Spell" title="Reverse Spell">Priori Incantatem</a></i>, and helped the echoes of Harry's parents, <a href="/wiki/Bertha_Jorkins" title="Bertha Jorkins">Bertha Jorkins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Bryce" title="Frank Bryce">Frank Bryce</a> hold off Voldemort long enough for him to escape. His only request was for Harry to take his body back to his parents, which Harry honoured.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>, Diggory's participation in the Triwizard Tournament and subsequent death was the subject of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scorpius_Malfoy" title="Scorpius Malfoy">Scorpius Malfoy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Time-Turner" title="Time-Turner">time-travel</a> antics at the request of Amos. They sought to save Cedric and created an alternate timeline where Cedric survived and became part of the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, because of the humiliation they caused him when they tried to save his life. Cedric's original and heroic life and death was later restored.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_life_(1977-1989)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_.281977-1989.29">Early life <span class="template-c">(1977-1989)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life (1977-1989)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life (1977-1989)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cedric was born in either <a href="/wiki/September" title="September">September</a> or <a href="/wiki/October" title="October">October</a> of <a href="/wiki/1977" title="1977">1977</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">Amos Diggory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_mother" title="Cedric Diggory&#39;s mother">his wife</a>. He grew up near <a href="/wiki/Ottery_St_Catchpole" title="Ottery St Catchpole">Ottery St Catchpole</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> a small town located in <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. When he was seven years old, his father enjoyed bragging about him.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Hogwarts_years_(1989-1995)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Hogwarts_years_.281989-1995.29">Hogwarts years <span class="template-c">(1989-1995)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hogwarts years (1989-1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hogwarts years (1989-1995)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">Amos Diggory</a> bragging about Cedric<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="First_year">First year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Cedric started attending <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> on <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1989" title="1989">1989</a> and was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff House</a>. Soon after starting Hogwarts, Cedric became a popular student at the school, and people talked about him constantly.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>He was present when <a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey" title="Poppy Pomfrey">Madam Pomfrey</a> was <a href="/wiki/Petrification" title="Petrification">petrified</a>, and recounted the event to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Penny_Haywood" title="Penny Haywood">Penny Haywood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_Khanna" title="Circle of Khanna">Circle of Khanna</a> was founded, Cedric's friend <a href="/wiki/Ben_Copper" title="Ben Copper">Ben Copper</a>, one of the founders of the group, invited him to join. Cedric spent much of his time in the group training <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duelling</a> with Ben and <a href="/wiki/Diego_Caplan" title="Diego Caplan">Diego Caplan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM620_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM620-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> One time during training, his arm was broken as a result of Ben casting a spell Cedric was not expecting, and he had to be sent to the Hospital Wing. He was soon healed by <a href="/wiki/Chiara_Lobosca" title="Chiara Lobosca">Chiara Lobosca</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>He distinguished himself as both an academic and athletic student. At some point, he became <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> of his house's Quidditch team.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, Cedric became a <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">Prefect</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Captain" title="Quidditch Captain">Captain</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff_Quidditch_team" title="Hufflepuff Quidditch team">Hufflepuff Quidditch team</a>. On <a href="/wiki/7_November" title="7 November">7 November</a>, he led his team against <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> in the first match of the school year.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Originally, the match should have taken place between Gryffindor and <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> played on the fact that his arm had been injured by the <a href="/wiki/Hippogriff" title="Hippogriff">hippogriff</a> <a href="/wiki/Buckbeak" title="Buckbeak">Buckbeak</a> to get out of playing in stormy conditions. During the match, Cedric and Harry Potter contested the Golden Snitch. However, Harry was overcome by the presence of <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> and fell from his broom. Cedric caught the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a>, but as soon as he realised what had happened to Harry, he immediately offered to replay the match. <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Wood" title="Oliver Wood">Oliver Wood</a> was said to have declined the offer, feeling that Cedric had won the match fair and square.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Harry got back his <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter%27s_Firebolt" title="Harry Potter&#39;s Firebolt">Firebolt broomstick</a>, Cedric was among those who congratulated Harry on his ownership of the broomstick.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sixth_year_and_Death">Sixth year and Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Sixth year and Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sixth year and Death"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In the summer of 1994, Cedric attended the <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a> with his father, travelling to the game with the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasleys</a>, Harry Potter, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> as he and his father lived close to <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">the Burrow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cedric started his sixth year in September 1994, the same year that the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> was reinstated. Being of age, Cedric submitted his name to the <a href="/wiki/Goblet_of_Fire" title="Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a> for the chance to become the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> Champion. On 31 October, the Goblet chose the champions to represent the three schools; <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a> for <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a> for <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang</a>, and Cedric himself for Hogwarts.
</p>

<p>In the weeks that followed his selection, Cedric was supported by the majority of the school, even the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherins</a>. Enchanted badges became popular around the school that read <i>Support CEDRIC DIGGORY — the REAL Hogwarts Champion!</i>, then changed to read <i>POTTER STINKS</i>. Cedric, however, was not fond of the badges and asked his friends not to wear them. As part of the Tournament, Cedric took part in the <a href="/wiki/Wand_weighing_ceremony" title="Wand weighing ceremony">Wand weighing ceremony</a> conducted by <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, who deemed <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_wand" title="Cedric Diggory&#39;s wand">his wand</a> "<i>pleasantly springy</i>", and posed for photos for the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i>. However, his entry into the Tournament was completely overlooked by <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>'s article on the event in favour of Harry, a situation about which <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">his father</a> was most displeased.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>In the months before to the First Task, Cedric was informed by Harry that the first task was to get past a <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragon</a>. Cedric was the only Champion not to be aware of them, and Harry felt it was only fair to tell him so everyone would be on an even footing.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/24_November" title="24 November">24 November</a>, the Champions gathered and randomly selected miniature versions of a dragon with a number on them to indicate the order in which they would face them. Cedric drew the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Short-Snout" title="Swedish Short-Snout">Swedish Short-Snout</a> and the number one slot, so entered the arena first to attempt to retrieve the <a href="/wiki/Golden_egg" title="Golden egg">golden egg</a> it was guarding. Facing the dragon, Cedric <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">transfigured</a> a rock into a Labrador to distract the creature. While the dragon turned to chase the dog, Cedric went for the Golden egg. Although he retrieved it, the dragon lost interest in the dog, preferring to go after Cedric. The dragon burned Cedric on the side of his face, and he barely managed to get away from it. He was tended to by <a href="/wiki/Poppy_Pomfrey" title="Poppy Pomfrey">Madam Pomfrey</a>, who applied an orange paste to his face to heal the wound.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Like all the Champions, Cedric had to work out the clue hidden in the <a href="/wiki/Golden_egg" title="Golden egg">golden egg</a> to prepare for the Second Task. He was aided by <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a>, disguised as the new <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor "Mad-Eye" Moody</a>, who told him to take it into the bath and listen to it underwater. He took it into the prefects' bathroom on the fifth floor, and opened the egg underwater. He heard the clue, but it took him a while to figure out that it referred to the <a href="/wiki/Merpeople" title="Merpeople">merpeople</a> in the lake.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At Christmas, Cedric was required to attend the Yule Ball, a traditional part of the Triwizard Tournament. He asked <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a> to attend the ball with him, and she accepted. Towards the end of the ball, Cedric found Harry and told him to take the egg into the bath and "<i>mull things over in the hot water</i>" as he had done. As repayment for warning him about the <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a>, he gave Harry access to the Prefect's Bathroom to work out the clue.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/24_February" title="24 February">24 February</a>, the Second Task took place. Having deciphered the clue in the golden egg, Cedric knew he had to breathe underwater for at least an hour and retrieve something that had been stolen from him. He effectively employed the <a href="/wiki/Bubble-Head_Charm" title="Bubble-Head Charm">Bubble-Head Charm</a>, and reached the merpeople village shortly after Harry. He found that his something that he would "<i>sorely miss</i>" was Cho, and rescued her. He warned Harry — who was worried about the other "hostages" — to hurry up, then swam to the surface. He arrived one minute outside the hour time limit, and received forty-seven points. After two tasks, he was tied for first place with Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Prior to the Third Task, Cedric, along with the other Champions, joined <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Bagman" title="Ludovic Bagman">Ludo Bagman</a> to view the maze that was being grown on the Hogwarts <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> pitch. Along with Harry, Cedric was dismayed to see the Quidditch pitch overgrown, but was assured by Bagman that the pitch would be returned to normal following the conclusion of the Tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 24 June, Cedric was joined by his parents, who had been invited to watch the Third Task. During the meeting, his father confronted Harry over the coverage he had been receiving in the press at the expense of his son. Cedric tried to calm him down and explain that it was not Harry's fault, then apologised to Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Because they were tied for first place, Cedric entered the maze with Harry. They split up, each hoping to be the first to get to the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Cup" title="Triwizard Cup">Triwizard Cup</a> placed in the centre of the maze. Soon after, Cedric encountered one of <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>'s now gigantic <a href="/wiki/Blast-Ended_Skrewt" title="Blast-Ended Skrewt">Blast-Ended Skrewts</a>, and narrowly escaped being burned by it. After the Skrewt, Cedric came across <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a>. Krum — who was under the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> at the time — attacked Cedric with the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a>. Despite being briefly united, he and Harry split apart again to seek the cup.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Assisting_Harry_Potter">Assisting Harry Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Assisting Harry Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Assisting Harry Potter"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Although dead, Cedric still had a role to play in the night's events. After Voldemort was <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">reborn</a>, he and Harry engaged in a <a href="/wiki/Duel_in_Little_Hangleton" title="Duel in Little Hangleton">duel</a>, during which their wands — which shared a wand core from <a href="/wiki/Fawkes" title="Fawkes">the same phoenix</a> — connected, creating the effect known as <i><a href="/wiki/Reverse_Spell" title="Reverse Spell">Priori Incantatem</a></i>, the backward spell effect. Voldemort's wand began regurgitating the last spells it had cast in reverse.
</p>

The crowd that had gathered for the Third Task erupted into screams when they realised what had happened, and Cedric's parents were grief-stricken by the fate that had been befallen their son (although his mother attempted to console herself by reflecting that Cedric had not suffered in death, and that he had died after winning the Tournament, believing that the victory would have made him happy).<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Cedric's death was widely felt throughout the school, and on <a href="/wiki/26_June" title="26 June">26 June</a> the Leaving Feast was turned into a memorial for him, with black banners replacing the house colours. Dumbledore delivered a tribute to Cedric, and relayed the events that had led to his death to the student body, telling them, against the explicit wishes of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, that the young Hufflepuff had been murdered by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> himself.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<p>The Minister of Magic Cornelius Fudge refused to accept Harry Potter's claims that Lord Voldemort had returned, and tried to hush up Cedric's death, claiming that it was a freak accident during the Tournament that killed him. He also slandered Harry Potter as a liar and questioned <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s sanity.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In death, Cedric proved to be an inspiration to many students at Hogwarts. His death was heartfelt and was tragic enough to cause even those who disliked Harry Potter to believe his claims of Voldemort's return. During the school year after Cedric's death, Hogwarts was under the watchful eyes of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> with <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, a high-ranking Ministry official being employed as <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> teacher. She constantly told students that Harry Potter lied about Cedric's death and refused to teach any practical magic.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>This started a rebellion amongst the students, and they formed <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, an alliance against Umbridge that taught students how to prepare themselves for the day that Lord Voldemort announced his return. During the D.A's meetings, Cedric was a key point of interest, with students wanting accounts of how he died, and his former girlfriend, Cho, seeking comfort from Harry, by asking whether Cedric would have survived if he had known what they were being taught. Harry, in turn, assured her that Cedric had known what he (Harry) was teaching the other students, but Cedric was simply unable to match Voldemort's experience and evil, and that Harry himself had made it back through good luck, rather than greater talent.<sup id="cite_ref-OPF_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OPF-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Later on that year, only when incontrovertible proof of the Dark Lord's return was presented, did Fudge believe Voldemort had indeed returned and that Cedric was actually murdered.<sup id="cite_ref-OP_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

Harry continued to honour him by keeping a badge that read "Support Cedric Diggory".<sup id="cite_ref-DH_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternate_timelines">Alternate timelines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Alternate timelines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Alternate timelines"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>, Cedric's father, Amos, was still greatly grieving for his son's death. When he found out Harry Potter had retrieved a <a href="/wiki/Time-Turner" title="Time-Turner">Time-Turner</a>, he begged Harry to use it to save Cedric. Harry stated that it was dangerous to meddle with time, but his own son, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a>, secretly agreed to it along with his friend, <a href="/wiki/Scorpius_Malfoy" title="Scorpius Malfoy">Scorpius Malfoy</a>. Although they managed to create <a href="/wiki/Alternate_reality" title="Alternate reality">alternate realities</a> (including one where Cedric lived), it changed time too much and they had to restore the original timeline. Indeed, the one timeline where Albus and Scorpius succeeded their goal of saving Cedric actually humiliated him in public, causing him to join the Death Eaters in bitter hatred for society; he ended up killing <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, thus preventing <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>'s death, which in turn <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">prevented</a> Voldemort from being killed, and Harry was killed in turn, thus leading to an age of darkness where Voldemort ruled. Albus and Scorpius prevented an attempt at changing time a third time by Delphi during the third task with the help of Cedric himself who rescued the two from the Dark witch, thinking that it was part of the task.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2021" title="2021">2021</a>, Harry visited <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory%27s_grave" title="Cedric Diggory&#39;s grave">Cedric's grave</a>, which was placed on a beautiful hill. He shared this experience with his son, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus Potter</a>, explaining that he came to say sorry for a life that was stolen and that he visits every year on the anniversary of Cedric's death. He tells Albus that Cedric was a great wizard and that he did not deserve his fate.<sup id="cite_ref-CC415_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC415-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Cedric was a tall and extremely handsome young man with chiselled features, dark hair, and bright grey eyes. Although he was a <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> (a position usually taken by small and light players) he was well-built and burly.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because of his handsomeness, Cedric caught attention from numerous girls - <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a> commented that he was <i>"tall and good-looking"</i>, while <a href="/wiki/Katie_Bell" title="Katie Bell">Katie Bell</a> agreed that he was <i>"strong and silent"</i>. Even <a href="/wiki/Pansy_Parkinson" title="Pansy Parkinson">Pansy Parkinson</a>, who usually had something negative to say about everyone's appearance, acknowledged his good looks. The fact that <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a> tried to use her Veela allure on him to charm him into becoming her date for the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> was further proof of how attractive he was to the opposite sex.
</p>

<p>Cedric was said to be an embodiment of the qualities of <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff House</a>. He had a sense of fair play: during the 1993 Gryffindor/Hufflepuff match, despite his capture of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a> (which ensured his team's victory), Cedric immediately offered to replay the match once he found out that his opponent, Harry Potter, had fallen from his broom due to the influence of the <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Later on, he provided Harry with information as to how to decipher the <a href="/wiki/Golden_egg" title="Golden egg">golden egg</a> clue for the Second Task. After Harry saved him in the maze, Cedric realised that Harry was the one who truly deserved to take the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Cup" title="Triwizard Cup">Triwizard Cup</a>, and had no qualms with openly acknowledging it, which actually resulted in an argument of sorts between them. This sense of fair play and his ability to accept there were those who were better than him is a definite testament to his nobility and his grace.
</p><p>He also appeared to possess a strength of character that was not unlike Harry's. This came into play when Fleur's turning the force of her <a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veela</a> charm on him while asking him to be her date for the Yule Ball. He refused, for he already had a date. Hence, it could be said that Cedric was a man of his word with remarkable inner strength, especially given how he was able to successfully resist the allure of Veela charm (which could be overwhelming) and keep his previously made promise.
</p><p>Besides all this, Cedric was genuinely modest, telling <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">his father</a> that his victory in the Gryffindor/Hufflepuff Quidditch match had not been a truly fair one. All the female Gryffindor Chasers described him as being <i>"strong and silent"</i>, suggesting that he was sensible enough to speak only when it was absolutely necessary. He was also courteously polite to foreigners: though he supported Ireland during the 1994 Quidditch World Cup final, he was still civil to Viktor Krum (who was from Bulgaria). The fact that his spirit's last request to Harry had only been to take his body back to his parents also proved that he loved his parents dearly.
</p><p>While this side of him was never seen in the original timeline, he appeared to have a sense of honour and importance for his title at the school. This was proved by the fact that in one of the alternate timelines, in which he was humiliated during the Triwizard Tournament, this feeling of embarrassment eventually led him to join the Death Eaters. This means that if his pride or status was to ever been shot down enough, this feeling of anger would have turned him over to the Death Eaters.
</p>

<p>Given Cedric's relatively smooth and successful participation in the Triwizard Tournament, he was a skilful and competent wizard in his own right. <ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: As the Captain and <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff_Quidditch_team" title="Hufflepuff Quidditch team">Hufflepuff Quidditch team</a>, Cedric was a skilful and accomplished flyer and <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> player, especially since the position of Seeker was generally considered to be the hardest position to play. In particular, he was noted to have a very fast swerve.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He even defeated <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> once in a match, although this had been due to the presence of <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> causing Harry to fall off his broom, but many still considered it an impressive display of his flying abilities.</li></ul>

<li><b>Indomitable willpower</b>: Cedric was shown to possess an exceptionally strong willpower that could rival that of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a>'s. For instance, he was able to successfully resist <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veela</a> charm and honour his previous arrangement with <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho</a>. This was no easy feat, as Veela charm could be particularly powerful and Fleur seemed to be turning on the full force of it upon him.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<p>Amos Diggory was immensely proud of his son and his achievements, especially when he beat <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> during a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> match in 1993, dismissing Cedric's insistence that Harry had fallen off his broom because of <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> just before Cedric caught the Snitch. During the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, Amos was outraged that the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> completely overlooked the fact that Cedric was also a Hogwarts champion in favour of Harry Potter, and made no mention of him at all. Despite his son trying to point out that it wasn't Harry's fault, Amos countered by remarking that Harry hadn't done anything to correct them.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Cedric's death, Amos was overcome with grief. Along with his wife, he visited Harry in the hospital wing, and thanked him for bringing his son's body back, and assured Harry that he wasn't to blame for his son's death.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Taken beneath the waves of the lake to the Merpeople's village, Cedric rescued her. During the awarding of points for the Task, she gave him a glowing look. <p>Cedric and Harry had a relationship of mutual respect and friendliness, though they were not close. In <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> played against each other in <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>, Cedric offered to replay the match after Harry was incapacitated by <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>, though the Gryffindors refused, believing that Cedric caught the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Snitch" title="Golden Snitch">Golden Snitch</a> fair and square.<sup id="cite_ref-PA_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">following year</a>, when Harry was selected as <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>' champion along with Cedric, Harry felt Cedric was sceptical of Harry's claims that he was not to blame, but he still disapproved of students wearing <i>Support Cedric Diggory/Potter Stinks</i> badges and discouraged it.
</p><p>They assisted each other multiple times during the Tournament out of good sportsmanship, agreeing in the end that they were equally winners. Both of them touching the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Cup" title="Triwizard Cup">Triwizard Cup</a> led to Cedric's murder, an event which haunted Harry for years. During Harry's duel with Voldemort, his spirit appeared to assist Harry when the two wands connected. He asked Harry to bring his body back to his parents. When the connection was broken, he and the other spirits that appeared distracted Voldemort to buy Harry enough time to escape to safety with his body.
</p>

<p>Harry risked his own life to take Cedric's body back. He nearly died as the Portkey was too far away to reach, forcing him to use a <a href="/wiki/Summoning_Charm" title="Summoning Charm">Summoning Charm</a>. Harry would later use the death of Cedric (along with the deaths of his <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">mother</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">father</a>) as added motivation when fighting Lord Voldemort.<sup id="cite_ref-GF_2-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Cedric's death allowed Harry to see thestrals at the beginning of his fifth year. Harry avenged Cedric when he killed Voldemort at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?veaction=edit&amp;section=25" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory?action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cedric" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Cedric">Cedric</a> was unintentionally created by Sir Walter Scott in his <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivanhoe" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Ivanhoe">Ivanhoe</a></i> character Cedric the Saxon when he misspelt the name <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cerdic" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Cerdic">Cerdic</a>. Cerdic was a sixth-century Saxon leader and the first king of Wessex.</li>
<li>It is commonly believed that <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> borrowed Cedric's last name from Digory Kirke in C.S. Lewis's <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Chronicles of Narnia">Narnia</a></i> series, as he is said to be one of her favourite authors.</li></ul>

<ul><li>Cedric was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Pattinson" title="Robert Pattinson">Robert Pattinson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Joe_Livermore" title="Joe Livermore">Joe Livermore</a> portrayed Cedric in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tom_Milligan" title="Tom Milligan">Tom Milligan</a> portrays Cedric in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Cursed_Child_(play)#Original_West_End_production" title="Harry Potter and the Cursed Child (play)">original West End production</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Cursed_Child" title="Harry Potter and the Cursed Child">Harry Potter and the Cursed Child</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Cedric's death is the first death Harry witnesses in the book series. Harry was in his cot when his mother died, but he would not remember this due to his age at the time, and he passed out before he sees <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirrell</a> die. Yet, Harry is unable to see the <a href="/wiki/Thestral" title="Thestral">Thestrals</a> until <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, because the reality of Cedric's death has not fully sunk in. Technically, however, Harry did not witness the event at all in the books, as he was described to have his eyes closed with agony during Cedric's actual death.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, Cedric, the <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> <a href="/wiki/Seeker" title="Seeker">Seeker</a> is struck by lightning during the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>-<a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> match just as he is about to catch the Snitch, causing him to fall back. The player is not named in the film's dialogue, but "Diggory" is embroidered above the Hufflepuff logo on his Quidditch robes, and the costume is identified as "Cedric Diggory's Quidditch robes" in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_The_Exhibition" title="Harry Potter: The Exhibition">Harry Potter: The Exhibition</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> However, it may have not been intended to be Cedric at the time, since in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, Harry, along with Fred and George Weasley, do not show signs of knowing Cedric previously, as the film implies that they are first introduced to him on the way to the Quidditch World Cup; while in the novel they only knew him from the aforementioned Quidditch match against Hufflepuff in the previous school year.
<ul><li>When Harry is cleaning out his school trunk in 1997, he finds a "Support Cedric Diggory!" badge in there. Considering the badges were only created by <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a> to get a rise out of Harry in 1994, it seems out of place that Harry has it, although he may have it by accident, or out of respect for Cedric. He could also have kept one as a memento from the Tournament.</li></ul></li>
<li>Cedric once borrowed <i><a href="/wiki/Quidditch_Through_the_Ages_(real)" title="Quidditch Through the Ages (real)">Quidditch Through the Ages</a></i> from the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">Hogwarts Library</a>, and was due to return the book on <a href="/wiki/3_July" title="3 July">3 July</a>.</li>
<li>Although <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> and Dumbledore say it was <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> who killed Cedric, it was actually <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>, under Voldemort's orders, who killed him. It could be that they meant that Voldemort <i>ordered</i> Pettigrew to kill Cedric. It also should be noted that the wand Peter used to kill Cedric with was Voldemort's. In addition, they were likely simplifying the facts to address the relatively greater issue of Voldemort's return, and not to waste time explaining Pettigrew's defection and faking his own death.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>, Cedric is not killed right away like in the novel and film. He is stuck to the statue with Harry, and participates in the ensuing battle. Rather, he is killed when Voldemort shoots a Killing Curse just as they grab the cup, which strikes Cedric and kills him. Despite Cedric's death in the graveyard, his <a href="/wiki/Amos_Diggory" title="Amos Diggory">father</a> gets instructions on how to rebuild him from Dumbledore. However, in the <a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">sequel</a>, it is implied that Cedric is still dead, most likely to avoid creating a plothole surrounding the aftermath of his death and the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a>'s denial of Voldemort's return.</li>
<li>Robert Pattinson has openly stated that he would rather want to play Cedric Diggory than playing <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Cullen" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Edward Cullen">Edward Cullen</a> from the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twilight_(series)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Twilight (series)">Twilight</a></i> series.</li>
<li>Despite being dead at the time, Cedric Diggory is an opponent in the <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a> minigame in the Nintendo DS version of <i>LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</i>.</li>
<li><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Man_of_Steel_(film)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Man of Steel (film)">Man of Steel</a></i> star <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Cavill" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Henry Cavill">Henry Cavill</a> auditioned to play Cedric, but lost out to Robert Pattinson. He later lost out to Pattinson on playing <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Cullen" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Edward Cullen">Edward Cullen</a> in <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twilight_(series)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Twilight (series)">Twilight</a></i>, despite being author <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephanie_Meyer" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Stephanie Meyer">Stephanie Meyer</a>'s first choice for the role.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Hufflepuff is a house that is said to have produced the fewest Dark wizards. In an alternative timeline Cedric joined the Death Eaters and murdered Neville Longbottom. It's possible that due to the unique circumstances of Cedric's humiliation during the Triwizard Tournament and the overwhelming presence of the Death Eaters within the British Wizarding community created a situation where Cedric was easily influenced by the Dark Arts and the benefits that joining Lord Voldemort's elite could bring.</li></ul>
