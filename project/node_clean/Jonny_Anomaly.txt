<b>Jonny Anomaly</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> magician in the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-poster-lego2_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poster-lego2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-poster-lego_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poster-lego-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, posters were put up around <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>'s harbour promoting a series of twelve performances staged by <a href="/wiki/G._Forbes_Productions" title="G. Forbes Productions">G. Forbes Productions</a>, which were slated to begin on Saturday <a href="/wiki/11_December" title="11 December">11 December</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-poster-lego_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poster-lego-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This included a show featuring the "amazing skills" of Jonny Anomaly.<sup id="cite_ref-poster-lego_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poster-lego-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Another poster was put up advertising a performance by the "magnificent" Anomaly at the <a href="/wiki/Packer_Theatre" title="Packer Theatre">Packer Theatre</a> on the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-poster-lego2_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-poster-lego2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
