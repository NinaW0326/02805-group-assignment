<b>Tabitha Tabella</b>, also known by the nickname <b>La Bella</b>, was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> criminal who operated in the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-itm_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tabitha_Tabella?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tabitha_Tabella?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a> issued a wanted poster for Tabella, which stated that she was wanted for "<a href="/wiki/Dragot" title="Dragot">Dragot</a> laundering," "defenestration of wizards," and "<a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> murder."<sup id="cite_ref-itm_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It warned that she was "bearing [a] <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> and extremely dangerous."<sup id="cite_ref-itm_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The poster promised a reward of 2538 Dragots for information leading to her capture.<sup id="cite_ref-itm_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This poster was hanging in <a href="/wiki/The_Blind_Pig" title="The Blind Pig">The Blind Pig</a> when <a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a> visited in December 1926.<sup id="cite_ref-itm_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
