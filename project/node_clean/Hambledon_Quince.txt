<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hambledon_Quince?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hambledon_Quince?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his lifetime, he proposed the rather <a href="/wiki/The_Evolution_of_Muggles_From_Mushrooms" title="The Evolution of Muggles From Mushrooms">controversial theory</a> that <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> originally originated from the planet <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a>, and that <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> originated from mushrooms. It is unknown if he was a <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_supremacy" title="Wizarding supremacy">Wizard supremacist</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacist</a>, however his controversial theories were readily accepted by both groups as proof of the inferiority of Muggles.<sup id="cite_ref-JKRS_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Hambledon_Quince?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Hambledon_Quince?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The idea of Wizards being from Mars and Muggles being from mushrooms may be referential of the phrase "Men are from Mars, women are from Venus," which was used as the title of a best-selling relationship-advice <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Men_Are_from_Mars,_Women_Are_from_Venus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Men Are from Mars, Women Are from Venus">book</a> by John Gray, and has since passed into popular culture. There is also a wizarding book titled <i><a href="/wiki/Wizards_Are_from_Neptune,_Witches_Are_from_Saturn" title="Wizards Are from Neptune, Witches Are from Saturn">Wizards Are from Neptune, Witches Are from Saturn</a></i>.</li></ul>
