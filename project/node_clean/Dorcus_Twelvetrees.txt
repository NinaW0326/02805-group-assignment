<b>Dorcus Twelvetrees</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who lived during the <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The daughter of <a href="/wiki/Aristotle_Twelvetrees" title="Aristotle Twelvetrees">Aristotle Twelvetrees</a>, she was responsible for one of the greatest ever breaches of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Secrecy</a>, which led to the passage of <a href="/wiki/Rappaport%27s_Law" title="Rappaport&#39;s Law">Rappaport's Law</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dorcus was born into a <a href="/wiki/Twelvetrees_family" title="Twelvetrees family">wizarding family</a> sometime in the <a href="/wiki/18th_century" title="18th century">18th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Ilvermorny_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Ilvermorny School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, but did not do well academically, and apparently did not go on to pursue a career afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the time her father, <a href="/wiki/Aristotle_Twelvetrees" title="Aristotle Twelvetrees">Aristotle Twelvetrees</a>, rose to the office of <a href="/wiki/Keeper_of_Treasure_and_Dragots" title="Keeper of Treasure and Dragots">Keeper of Treasure and Dragots</a> under <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Emily_Rappaport" title="Emily Rappaport">Emily Rappaport</a>, Dorcus was still living with her parents, and rarely used <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Described as "pretty" but "dim", her primary interests included fashion, styling her hair, and organising parties.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bartholomew_Barebone_affair">Bartholomew Barebone affair</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Bartholomew Barebone affair">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bartholomew Barebone affair"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dorcus met <a href="/wiki/Bartholomew_Barebone" title="Bartholomew Barebone">Bartholomew Barebone</a>, a handsome <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> at a neighbourhood <a href="/wiki/Picnic" title="Picnic">picnic</a> one day. She was instantly smitten and performed "<a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">little tricks</a>" for him.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Interpreting his interest as nothing more than politeness and curiosity, she duly answered his "artless" queries.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the course of these interactions, she revealed to him the locations of Ilvermorny and the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a>. She also provided him information about the <a href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Wizards" title="International Confederation of Wizards">International Confederation of Wizards</a> and the manners in which the wizarding community endeavoured to hide itself from the <a href="/wiki/Muggle_world" title="Muggle world">No-Maj world</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unbeknownst to her, Bartholomew was in fact descended from a <a href="/wiki/Scourers" title="Scourers">Scourer</a> line, meaning that he vehemently believed in the existence of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, and that all witches and wizards were evil.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Having manipulated Dorcus into revealing as much about the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a> as he could, he proceeded to steal her <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He showed the wand to as many newspaper reporters as possible, and a few were motivated to publish pictures of it, describing it as having a "kick like a <a href="/wiki/Mule" title="Mule">mule</a>" when waved.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also printed and distributed leaflets listing the wizarding addresses Dorcus had revealed to him, and sent letters to important No-Majs, prompting some to investigate whether the "evil occult parties" he described were actually occurring.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After shooting at a group of No-Majs he wrongly assumed to be <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a> employees, Bartholomew was arrested by No-Maj authorities, but the damage had already been done.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The MACUSA was left struggling to cope with one of the most massive breaches of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Secrecy</a> in history.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They attempted to <a href="/wiki/Memory_Charm" title="Memory Charm">modify</a> the memories of all No-Majs exposed to the leaked information, but, as President Rappaport testified at a public inquiry, there was no way to be certain they had obliviated everyone.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Imprisonment_and_later_life">Imprisonment and later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Imprisonment and later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dorcus_Twelvetrees?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Imprisonment and later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many within the magical community advocated imprisoning Dorcus for life for her actions.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Some even called for her to be executed.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, she ultimately spent only a year in prison, emerging a traumatised and thoroughly disgraced woman.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She lived out the rest of her days in isolation, with only a mirror and a <a href="/wiki/Parrot" title="Parrot">parrot</a> to keep her company.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Twelvetrees-Barebone affair prompted the passage of <a href="/wiki/Rappaport%27s_Law" title="Rappaport&#39;s Law">Rappaport's Law</a> in <a href="/wiki/1790" class="mw-redirect" title="1790">1790</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This law banned American wizards and witches from <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marrying</a> or befriending No-Majs, and led to the total segregation of the No-Maj and wizarding communities in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-rappaport_2-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-rappaport-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
