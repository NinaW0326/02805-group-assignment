</p><p><b>Sulzeer Jeremiah "Sol" Campbell</b> was a professional <a href="/wiki/Football" title="Football">football</a> player who played for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England national football team</a> by <a href="/wiki/July" title="July">July</a> of <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>. By that time, he had played seventy games for the team, culminating in a win against <a href="/wiki/Estonia_national_football_team" title="Estonia national football team">Estonia</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley" title="Wembley">Wembley</a> Stadium. When team captain <a href="/wiki/John_Terry" title="John Terry">John Terry</a> showed signs of a reoccuring knee injury, manager <a href="/wiki/Steve_McClaren" title="Steve McClaren">Steve McClaren</a> decided to have Campbell stand in for him at an upcoming game against <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a>. As noted by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, this would be the first time since <a href="/wiki/January" title="January">January</a> of that year that Campbell made two appearances in that short of a time frame.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
