<p><b>Fleur Isabelle Weasley</b><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> (née <b>Delacour</b>) (b. c. <a href="/wiki/1976" title="1976">1976</a>-<a href="/wiki/1977" title="1977">1977</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> quarter-<a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veela</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> daughter of <a href="/wiki/Apolline_Delacour" title="Apolline Delacour">Apolline Delacour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delacour_sisters%27_father" title="Delacour sisters&#39; father">her husband</a>, and elder sister of <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Delacour" title="Gabrielle Delacour">Gabrielle</a>. Fleur attended <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons Academy of Magic</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%931995_school_year" title="1994–1995 school year">1994–1995 school year</a>, she was Beauxbatons' champion in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. Fleur competed bravely, but the Tournament ended in tragedy with the murder of <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> and the return of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>.
</p><p>In the following summer, Fleur took a part-time job at <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank" title="Gringotts Wizarding Bank">Gringotts Wizarding Bank</a> to improve her English, and began dating her co-worker <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a>. The pair soon became engaged and Fleur stayed at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a> in the summer of <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> to get to know Bill's family better, a fact that <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> greatly disliked, nicknaming her 'Phlegm'.
</p><p>Fleur arrived in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Astronomy_Tower" title="Battle of the Astronomy Tower">Battle of the Astronomy Tower</a> in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> during which her fiancé was badly injured by the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>. The pair were <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">married</a> in August of that year; however the wedding was disrupted by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> following the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>. Fleur and Bill took up residence at <a href="/wiki/Shell_Cottage" title="Shell Cottage">Shell Cottage</a>, and later assisted <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Dumbledore</a>'s quest following their escape from <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>, offering them room and board. Fleur fought alongside her husband in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, she and Bill had three children; <a href="/wiki/Victoire_Weasley" title="Victoire Weasley">Victoire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Weasley" title="Dominique Weasley">Dominique</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Weasley" title="Louis Weasley">Louis Weasley</a>. She was awarded <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour%27s_medals_of_bravery" title="Fleur Delacour&#39;s medals of bravery">medals of bravery</a> by both the <a href="/wiki/French_Ministry_of_Magic" title="French Ministry of Magic">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministries of Magic</a> for her participation in the Battle of Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fleur was quarter-<a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">veela</a>, who grew up in France. She grew up with her parents <a href="/wiki/Delacour_sisters%27_father" title="Delacour sisters&#39; father">Monsieur Delacour</a> and her half-veela mother <a href="/wiki/Apolline_Delacour" title="Apolline Delacour">Apolline Delacour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She also had a much younger sister, Gabrielle. Fleur's grandmother was full Veela and at some point contributed a hair, that would later become the core element of Fleur's wand.
</p>
<h3><span id="Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic_(?-1995)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic_.28.3F-1995.29"><a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons Academy of Magic</a> <span class="template-c">(?-1995)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Beauxbatons Academy of Magic (?-1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beauxbatons Academy of Magic (?-1995)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At an unknown point in time Fleur began attending Beauxbatons Academy of Magic.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Triwizard_champion">Triwizard champion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Triwizard champion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Triwizard champion"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>During her last school year in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a> she, along with Beauxbatons' Headmistress Madame <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Olympe Maxime</a> and a dozen other Beauxbatons students travelled to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Her school did this to enter in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. They arrived in a huge, powder-blue chariot pulled by roughly a dozen flying horses on <a href="/wiki/30_October" title="30 October">30 October</a> at around 6:00 pm.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF15_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF15-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She laughed derisively during <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s speech during the <a href="/wiki/Welcoming_Feast" title="Welcoming Feast">Welcoming Feast</a>. Part way through the feast, she walked over to the Gryffindor table to ask <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Bouillabaisse" title="Bouillabaisse">bouillabaisse</a> in front of him. On her way back, she caught the eyes of most of the other boys in the hall.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF16_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF16-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Fleur placed her name in the <a href="/wiki/Goblet_of_Fire" title="Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a>, and during the <a href="/wiki/Hallowe%27en_Feast" title="Hallowe&#39;en Feast">Hallowe'en Feast</a>, was selected to be Beauxbatons' student representative and Triwizard Champion. While awaiting further instructions in a small room off the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>, she learned that the Goblet of Fire had selected a fourth Champion — <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. When <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Bagman" title="Ludovic Bagman">Ludo Bagman</a> told her what happened, she first thought he was joking, then was surprised and outraged that they were to let a <i>"little boy"</i> compete in the Tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF16_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF16-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Challenge" title="Dragon Challenge">First Task</a>, Fleur took part in the <a href="/wiki/Wand_weighing_ceremony" title="Wand weighing ceremony">Wand weighing ceremony</a> presided over by <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>. After being handed the wand, it produced pink and gold sparks and was confirmed, by Fleur, to contain a <a href="/wiki/Veela_hair" title="Veela hair">Veela hair</a> as a core; a gift from <a href="/wiki/Apolline_Delacour%27s_mother" title="Apolline Delacour&#39;s mother">her grandmother</a>. Ollivander described <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour%27s_wand" title="Fleur Delacour&#39;s wand">her wand</a> as "<i>inflexible</i>", and in great working condition when it produced a bouquet of flowers without problem.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF18_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF18-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Fleur's entrance into the Tournament was overshadowed in the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Prophet" title="Daily Prophet">Daily Prophet</a></i> due to Harry Potter's participation; she was only mentioned in the last line of <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>'s article, with her name misspelled.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF19_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF19-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Forewarned by <a href="/wiki/Olympe_Maxime" title="Olympe Maxime">Madame Maxime</a> that the First Task would involve <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a>, Fleur tackled the task on <a href="/wiki/24_November" title="24 November">24 November</a> <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF17_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF17-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> To eliminate all advantages, the champions were made to choose a dragon at random. Fleur extinguished the fire with <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> from her wand, then managed to retrieve the <a href="/wiki/Golden_egg" title="Golden egg">golden egg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF20_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF20-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>

<p>In the run-up to the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a>, Fleur attempted to attract Cedric as a date, but he had already asked <a href="/wiki/Cho_Chang" title="Cho Chang">Cho Chang</a>. Suddenly aware of what he had done he ran away before she could answer him.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF22_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF22-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> This left Ron feeling incredibly embarrassed for several days to come and word spread around the Gryffindor common room.

Fleur and Roger, along with the other Champions and their partners opened the dancing at the Yule Ball. As the night wore on Fleur and Roger left the Great Hall and headed for the gardens, sneaking into the rose bushes for a romantic engagement of some kind.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF23_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF23-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/24_February" title="24 February">24 February</a>, Fleur participated in the Second Task. Having figured out the clue in the golden egg and understanding the need to breathe underwater for an hour, she chose to use the <a href="/wiki/Bubble-Head_Charm" title="Bubble-Head Charm">Bubble-Head Charm</a>. During the Task, she was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Grindylow" title="Grindylow">Grindylows</a> and was forced to retreat. Unaware that the hostages were not in any real danger, she was quite hysterical and fought so hard to return to the water that even Madame Maxime had difficulty restraining her. She was overjoyed when Harry, determined to rescue all the hostages, not just his own, returned to the surface with both Ron and her sister, <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Delacour" title="Gabrielle Delacour">Gabrielle</a>. She thanked him and Ron profusely by giving them a kiss on each cheek, and after the incident became friendly toward the two. From then on she came to view Harry as a respected friend. Upon learning that she had been awarded twenty-five points, she remarked that she deserved zero, as she had failed to retrieve her "hostage." After the first two tasks, Fleur was in last place going into the Third Task.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF26_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF26-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<p>On <a href="/wiki/1_August" title="1 August">1 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, Fleur and Bill <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">were married</a> at The Burrow. The ceremony and reception took place in a marquee tent which "<i>revealed rows and rows of fragile golden chairs set on either side of a long purple carpet.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>White and gold flowers and golden balloons were some of the many unique elements added to the occasion. <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Delacour" title="Gabrielle Delacour">Gabrielle</a> were both bridesmaids. Fleur wore a simple white dress and Muriel's tiara, and looked so beautiful that Bill did not look as though he had ever met Fenrir Greyback. The celebration was overshadowed, though, by the news that the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> had fallen to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, and the death of <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a>. Death Eaters, with the full power of the Ministry behind them, broke the protective spells surrounding the Burrow and <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparated</a> into the reception.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Fleur was questioned by them, along with the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a> on the whereabouts of Harry Potter, but was later free to go.
</p>

<p>Fleur and Bill relocated to <a href="/wiki/Shell_Cottage" title="Shell Cottage">Shell Cottage</a>, and spent their first Christmas there together, along with Bill's younger brother <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a>, who had secretly come to stay with them after abandoning <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione</a> on their <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a> hunt.<sup id="cite_ref-DH20_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH20-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following Harry, Ron, and Hermione's escape from <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_Manor" title="Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>, Fleur helped to look after them and their fellow escapees: <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Griphook" title="Griphook">Griphook</a>. She helped Hermione recover from the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">torture</a> she suffered at the hands of <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>, and healed Griphook's legs with <a href="/wiki/Skele-Gro" title="Skele-Gro">Skele-Gro</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH24_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH24-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Over the course of the next few weeks, she looked after her unexpected guests, although she disliked Griphook. When Ollivander was well enough to be transferred to Aunt Muriel's, she asked him to take Muriel's tiara back with him as she never had a chance to return it after the wedding. Fleur expressed concern over Harry's plans to leave Shell Cottage on his quest, telling him it was better for him to stay where it was safe.<sup id="cite_ref-DH25_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH25-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the war, Fleur and Bill had three children; <a href="/wiki/Victoire_Weasley" title="Victoire Weasley">Victoire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-MSNBC_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MSNBC-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Weasley" title="Dominique Weasley">Dominique</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Weasley" title="Louis Weasley">Louis Weasley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WFT_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WFT-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She was granted <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour%27s_medals_of_bravery" title="Fleur Delacour&#39;s medals of bravery">medals</a> of honour from the <a href="/wiki/French_Ministry_of_Magic" title="French Ministry of Magic">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministries of Magic</a> for her actions in the war.<sup id="cite_ref-PM_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PM-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>A young woman was standing in the doorway, a woman of such breathtaking beauty that the room seemed to have become strangely airless. <p>Due to her part-<a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veela</a> heritage, Fleur was a breathtakingly beautiful witch, and hence caught a good deal of attention from many males. She was tall and willowy, with an air of grace that made her seem like she was gliding when she was walking, and her very presence seemed to "<i>emanate a faint, silvery glow</i>". She had long, silvery-blonde hair that fell almost to her waist, which was also shiny, large, deep blue eyes, fair skin, and very white, even teeth.<sup id="cite_ref-appearance_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-appearance-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Her beauty "<i>usually dimmed everyone else by comparison</i>," but on her wedding day, "<i>it beautified everybody it fell upon.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>to the point where one could hardly even tell that her husband had his face scarred by Fenrir Greyback.
</p><p>Ron Weasley (who would later become Fleur's brother-in-law) usually made a fool of himself in attempts to get Fleur to kiss him on the cheek. Ginny Weasley and Hermione Granger usually remarked on this being "pathetic."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire"><b>Fleur Delacour</b>: "<i>It is too ’eavy, all zis ’Ogwarts food. I will not fit into my dress robes!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Hermione Granger</b>: "<i>Oooh there’s a tragedy. She really thinks a lot of herself, that one, doesn’t she?</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Fleur's high opinion of herself<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p>In her youth, Fleur was arrogant and conceited. Because of her beauty, she saw herself as a cut above most others. For instance, when <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> asked her to go to the Yule Ball with him, she "<i>looked at him as though he were a sea slug</i>." Her only redeeming feature seemed to be her genuine concern for her sister, <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Delacour" title="Gabrielle Delacour">Gabrielle</a>, as seen when she fought to return to the water to save her, despite having previously suffered an attack by <a href="/wiki/Grindylow" title="Grindylow">Grindylows</a> in the Second Task of the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>.
</p><p>Fleur also had a very blunt nature, always speaking her mind, and therefore often came off as rude and snobbish. She once made insulting comments about the state of the Hogwarts Christmas decorations and later even about Mrs Weasley (her future mother-in-law)'s home.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP16_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP16-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> This snobbish attitude was what made most other women, especially Molly and Ginny Weasley, dislike her.
</p><p>However, despite her high opinion of herself, Fleur's softer side was warm and caring. She fretted over the safety of those she loved, and if someone she cared about was in trouble she would always try to help them.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF26_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF26-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She was also not a superficial person. In <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Astronomy_Tower" title="Battle of the Astronomy Tower">Battle of the Astronomy Tower</a> — when her fiancé, <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a>, was in the hospital wing, Fleur insisted on treating his wounds herself, taking the ointment from <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a>. When Molly lamented over how the wedding was to be cancelled because of Bill's wounds, Fleur took offence at the implication of her being a shallow person and retorted that she loved Bill for more than his good looks, even stating that his acquired scars were — to her — proof of her husband's bravery. This genuine demonstration of her valuing true worth over surface glitter was what made those who initially disapproved of her (such as Molly and Ginny) finally begin to accept her.
</p><p>After being married in the summer of <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, one could see more of Fleur's positive qualities: true courage and fierce loyalty. She had never forgotten that Harry once saved her sister's life. This caused her to volunteer to escort <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> from <a href="/wiki/4_Privet_Drive" title="4 Privet Drive">4 Privet Drive</a>, risking both her life and that of her fiance's. She also allowed Harry to stay at her <a href="/wiki/Shell_Cottage" title="Shell Cottage">home</a> and even honoured his request that the <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Griphook" title="Griphook">Griphook</a>, be allowed to stay as well. While she played host to Harry, Hermione, Ron, and the others at her home, Harry noticed Fleur had become a lot more like her mother-in-law, Molly Weasley, as she displayed traits such as, worrying and fussing in a maternal fashion over him and his friends.<sup id="cite_ref-DH24_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH24-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Fleur was a talented and able <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, which was shown by her selection as the Beauxbatons <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Champion</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Goblet_of_Fire" title="Goblet of Fire">Goblet of Fire</a>. Though circumstances in the Tournament conspired against her, and she was unable to fully showcase her talents.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF31_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF31-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> She proved her considerable <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> skills by her participation and survival in subsequent battles.
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veela</a> <a href="/wiki/Veela_Magic" title="Veela Magic">attraction</a></b>: As a quarter-<a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">Veela</a>, Fleur had the ability to attract men with her beauty. Although her ability was not as strong as that of a full Veela, she was still able to make herself ravishing enough to completely overwhelm unsuspecting individuals if she wanted to.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF15_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF15-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> When in an elated or euphoric state, Fleur's Veela attraction enhanced the physical attractiveness of all within a given radius of her, to the point where one could hardly even tell that her husband had his face scarred after he fought <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP29_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP29-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Fleur was shown to have been highly proficient with charm-work. During the First Task, she used both a <a href="/wiki/Bewitched_Sleep_(spell)" title="Bewitched Sleep (spell)">bewitched sleep</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Water-Making_Spell" title="Water-Making Spell">Water-Making Spell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF20_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF20-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In the Second Task, she used a <a href="/wiki/Bubble-Head_Charm" title="Bubble-Head Charm">Bubble-Head Charm</a> — all to good effect.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF26_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF26-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In addition by adulthood, she was capable of producing a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOHP_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOHP-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> a mark of superior <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability and testament to her talent in charms.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Nonverbal magic</a></b>: Fleur was capable of performing <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a> non-verbally, which is a mark of superior magical ability as non-verbal magic is very difficult.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: Fleur was also a highly skilful duellist, as proven during the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a><sup id="cite_ref-DH4_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH4-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> all of which she both entered and finished in good health.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Household_magic" title="Household magic">Household magic</a></b>: At the time Harry, Ron, and Hermione stayed at her and Bill's house, Fleur seemed to have developed a talent for household spells: cleaning and making dinner with magic, just like her mother-in-law.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Bilingualism:</b> Fleur spoke her native French, as well as fluent English, albeit the latter with a strong French accent.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<p>Fleur seemed to have a pleasant relationship with her parents when they arrived at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">the Burrow</a> for <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">her wedding</a> with Bill; her <a href="/wiki/Veela" title="Veela">half-Veela</a> mother, <a href="/wiki/Apolline_Delacour" title="Apolline Delacour">Apolline</a>, proved to be very helpful and had a knack for household spells, and her father was a jovial man who seemed to find everything at the Burrow charming. After the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, they had three grandchildren through Fleur.
</p><p>Fleur had a very close relationship with her sister, <a href="/wiki/Gabrielle_Delacour" title="Gabrielle Delacour">Gabrielle</a>. During the Second Task of the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, Gabrielle was taken into the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lake" title="Great Lake">lake</a> as the thing Fleur would sorely miss. After <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> appeared to have saved Gabrielle's life by risking a high score to rescue her from the lake, Fleur warmed to him quite a bit, and took to kissing his cheeks when she saw him. Gabrielle also developed a crush on Harry. At her wedding, Fleur made Gabrielle one of her bridesmaids, along with <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> (Much to Ginny's dislike, as at the time she did not much like Fleur and had taken to calling her phlegm behind her back).

<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/7d/Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png/revision/latest?cb=20110718061348" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="250" height="105" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Fleur and Bill embracing.png" data-image-key="Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/7d/Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110718061348" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/7d/Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png/revision/latest?cb=20110718061348" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/7d/Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110718061348" decoding="async" width="250" height="105" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Fleur and Bill embracing.png" data-image-key="Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/7d/Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110718061348" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Fleur_and_Bill_embracing.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Fleur and Bill share an intimate moment at their wedding</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>They were married on <a href="/wiki/1_August" title="1 August">1 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, the same day that the Ministry of Magic fell to Lord Voldemort. Although their wedding was disrupted by Death Eaters, Fleur and Bill were allowed to go free and set up home in <a href="/wiki/Shell_Cottage" title="Shell Cottage">Shell Cottage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH25_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH25-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Following the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Fleur bore him three beautiful children, (eldest to youngest), <a href="/wiki/Victoire_Weasley" title="Victoire Weasley">Victoire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Weasley" title="Dominique Weasley">Dominique</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Weasley" title="Louis Weasley">Louis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WFT_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WFT-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Children">Children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=23" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Children"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the events of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Fleur bore three children; Victoire, who was named after the victory of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, because she was born on the anniversary of the battle; Dominique and Louis. In <a href="/wiki/2017" title="2017">2017</a>, it was implied that Victoire was in a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Edward_Lupin" title="Edward Lupin">Teddy Lupin</a>. Since Fleur began to emulate her mother-in-law, it is likely she was very warm and caring towards her children and loved them dearly.
</p>

<p>Fleur treated Harry Potter patronisingly at first, partly because she felt he was too young to participate in the Triwizard Tournament, and also because she believed that he must have cheated his way in for personal glory. At one point she even referred to him as a "<i>little boy</i>". However, after he rescued her sister from the <a href="/wiki/Great_Lake" title="Great Lake">Black Lake</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament#The_Second_Task" title="Triwizard Tournament">Second Task</a> of the tournament, her attitude and treatment towards him grew considerably warmer and she came to respect him very much.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF26_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF26-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> By <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, they considered themselves friends and recognised each other as valuable allies.
</p><p>While Fleur stayed at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">The Burrow</a> in the summer of 1996, Harry seemed to be the only male who was unaffected by Fleur's beauty. This was possibly due to the platonic relationship that developed between them over the course of the tournament. At the Burrow, Fleur showed affection for Harry and treated him in a similar way as Mrs Weasley. She once brought him a breakfast tray while he was in bed and would often kiss him on both cheeks twice as a form of greeting and farewell. Like <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>, Harry appeared to like Fleur and would defend her, much to the dismay of Molly, Hermione, and Ginny.
</p>

<p>Eventually, Fleur joined the Order of the Phoenix and in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> she put herself in great danger during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>, acting as one of Harry's decoys. She was also very loyal to Harry. When her wedding was gatecrashed by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, she did not tell them that Harry had been present.
</p><p>After Harry and several others came to her home at Shell Cottage the same year, Fleur tended to their wounds and generally took care of all of them. Having so many people at her home tested her patience, but she was still kind to Harry, of whom she still had a high opinion because of his efforts to save her sister. Through his marriage to Ginny Weasley, Harry became Fleur's brother-in-law and uncle to her three children. It's quite possible that Harry became close to Fleur's children and in return she became close to his. Due to their common ground as surviving Triwizard Champions, Harry and Fleur share a unique sense of camaraderie. This camaraderie reinforced their familial bond. Harry, as a frequent guest lecturer on <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> at Hogwarts, likely gave Fleur's children a chance to learn from their esteemed uncle in a non-familial perspective.
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasleys</a>' reaction to Fleur, with the exceptions of <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur</a>, was initially lukewarm. They became more annoyed with Fleur when she and Bill became engaged, but tolerated her presence for his sake.
</p><p>Ron always had a soft side for Fleur, he always stuck up for her. Once he asked her to go to the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> but ran away before she had a chance to answer him. However, she became his sister-in-law in 1997 and they started having a friendly relationship. While Ron, Harry, and Hermione stayed at her house, Fleur cared about them in a mother-like way.
</p><p>Molly and Ginny Weasley in particular did not like Fleur, considering her snooty and snobbish. Molly was irked by Fleur's criticism of her household and her favourite singer, <a href="/wiki/Celestina_Warbeck" title="Celestina Warbeck">Celestina Warbeck</a>, and Ginny called her "<i>Phlegm</i>", due to her throaty, French accent, and made fun of her behind her back. However, when Fleur did not break off their engagement following Bill's injuries, the Weasleys saw how deeply she loved Bill, and became much more accepting of her. Molly convinced her Aunt <a href="/wiki/Muriel" title="Muriel">Muriel</a> to lend Fleur her tiara for the wedding, and Ginny served as one of Fleur's bridesmaids.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP29_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP29-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Muriel herself remarked that Fleur was indeed beautiful, which considering her usual personality was impressive, but of course also expressed disappointment with her French blood.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Hermione Granger met Fleur during the Triwizard Tournament. She almost immediately grew to dislike her, as the beautiful French witch had a tendency to complain frequently and loudly about most aspects of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. Fleur often compared Hogwarts unfavourably with her own <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">school</a>. When Hermione met Fleur again at The Burrow, she found the latter to be arrogant and self-obsessed. This opinion shared by Ginny and <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Molly Weasley</a>. The fact that Ron had a very obvious crush on Fleur also didn't do much to improve her standing in Hermione's eyes.
</p><p>However, Hermione saw Fleur in a much different light after Bill was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>, and Fleur made it clear that Bill's now heavily scarred appearance didn't change her feelings for him in the slightest. Fleur later risked her life along with Hermione to act as one of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Harry's decoys</a> in 1997. After Hermione was tortured by <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> at <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_at_Malfoy_Manor" title="Skirmish at Malfoy Manor">Malfoy Manor</a>, Fleur tended to her injuries. She also tended the injuries sustained by the others who had been detained there, and generally fussed over all of them in a motherly fashion. The two finally fought alongside one another during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, and became sisters-in-law through Fleur's marriage to Bill, and Hermione's marriage to Ron.
</p>

<p>Fleur also had many nieces and nephews, through the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a>. Percy and <a href="/wiki/Audrey_Weasley" title="Audrey Weasley">Audrey</a>'s daughters, <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley_II" title="Molly Weasley II">Molly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Weasley" title="Lucy Weasley">Lucy</a>; George and <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a>'s children, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley_II" title="Fred Weasley II">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roxanne_Weasley" title="Roxanne Weasley">Roxanne</a>; Ron and Hermione's children, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Granger-Weasley" title="Rose Granger-Weasley">Rose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Granger-Weasley" title="Hugo Granger-Weasley">Hugo</a>; Ginny and Harry's children, <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_II" title="James Potter II">James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Potter" title="Albus Potter">Albus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_Potter" title="Lily Potter">Lily</a>. It is likely that Fleur was close with all of them. It's unknown if she eventually had any nieces or nephews through her sister Gabrielle.
</p>

<p>Olympe Maxime was the headmistress of <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons Academy of Magic</a> during Fleur's time at the school. Madam Maxime was indignant at the inclusion of Harry Potter in the Triwizard Tournament, and supported Fleur's efforts to win completely; when Harry saw Maxime when Hagrid showed him the First Task's <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a>, he had no doubts that Maxime would have told Fleur all about them. Fleur presumably remained in close contact with her headmistress, as she invited her to the wedding.
</p>

<p>During <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Fleur attended the <a href="/wiki/Yule_Ball" title="Yule Ball">Yule Ball</a> as the partner of <a href="/wiki/Roger_Davies" title="Roger Davies">Roger Davies</a>. He was apparently too smitten by her <a href="/wiki/Part-Veela" class="mw-redirect" title="Part-Veela">part-Veela</a> charm to take note of anything she said, most of which was criticism of Hogwarts, while constantly missing his own mouth with the fork during dinner. The two apparently enjoyed their time together, as they had a romantic night within the bushes during the later parts of the Ball. However, they did not keep in contact after the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a> ended, as Fleur married Bill Weasley.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Viktor_Krum">Viktor Krum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=30" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Viktor Krum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Viktor Krum"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krum" title="Viktor Krum">Viktor Krum</a> and Fleur were both the Triwizard champions for their schools, <a href="/wiki/Durmstrang_Institute" title="Durmstrang Institute">Durmstrang Institute</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beauxbatons_Academy_of_Magic" title="Beauxbatons Academy of Magic">Beauxbatons</a> respectively, they viewed each other as competition during the Triwizard Tournament. They maintained their friendship and she invited him to her wedding to William Weasley in 1997.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Order_of_the_Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=31" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Order of the Phoenix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Order of the Phoenix"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fleur became a member of the Order of the Phoenix during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. Her relationship with most fellow members is unknown; it is possible that she and the rest of the order had a good relationship.
</p><p>She was at least somewhat friendly with <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a>, though she seemed to think little of Tonks's efforts in terms of her appearance. She fought alongside both in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>, she was also thrilled to learn Tonks had given birth to a baby boy. By <a href="/wiki/2017" title="2017">2017</a>, Tonks and Remus's son, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Lupin" title="Edward Lupin">Teddy</a>, was seen kissing Bill and Fleur's daughter, Victoire.
</p><p>Fleur was also clearly fond of <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>, as she was visibly distraught by his death in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, and was seen crying.
</p>

<p>Fleur cared for a number of people at Shell Cottage following the <a href="/wiki/Skirmish_at_Malfoy_Manor" title="Skirmish at Malfoy Manor">Skirmish at Malfoy Manor</a>: she became friendly with <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, seeming especially fond of the last. Fleur asked the <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmaker</a> to return the tiara she had worn for <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">her wedding</a> to Bill's Aunt <a href="/wiki/Muriel" title="Muriel">Muriel</a>.
</p><p>Fleur disliked the <a href="/wiki/Goblin" title="Goblin">goblin</a> <a href="/wiki/Griphook" title="Griphook">Griphook</a>, who treated her as if she was a lowly maid; though it was clear to Harry that Griphook's behaviour was frustrating Fleur, she mostly kept her feelings to herself, as Griphook was still in poor health.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?veaction=edit&amp;section=34" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour?action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>According to <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>, her name comes from the French phrase <i>fleur de la cour</i>, meaning "flower of the court."<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><strong class="mw-selflink selflink">Fleur</strong>'s middle name, Isabelle, comes from the French word belle, meaning beautiful.</li>
<li>Her full name, <i>Fleur Isabelle Delacour</i>, can be roughly translated as beautiful flower of the court.</li></ul>

<ul><li>Fleur is portrayed by French actress <a href="/wiki/Cl%C3%A9mence_Po%C3%A9sy" title="Clémence Poésy">Clémence Poésy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> However, Fleur doesn't appear in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> Fleur is seen wearing a lavish black and white <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_William_Weasley_and_Fleur_Delacour" title="Wedding of William Weasley and Fleur Delacour">wedding</a> dress. Whereas in the book she is stated to be wearing a simple plain white dress.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Radcliffe" title="Daniel Radcliffe">Daniel Radcliffe</a> also portrayed Fleur in the Seven Potters scene, but Clémence did the voice for the transformed Fleur herself.</li>
<li>Fleur in the books is said to have silvery-blond hair. However, <a href="/wiki/Cl%C3%A9mence_Po%C3%A9sy" title="Clémence Poésy">Clémence Poésy</a> has darker hair.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, Fleur is <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">stunned</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperiused</a> Viktor Krum. Harry finds her immobilised and being taken away by vines, and sends <a href="/wiki/Periculum" title="Periculum">red sparks</a> into the air. The book does not show Harry finding her, and, at the time, Harry is portrayed as content with the knowledge of having one less source of competition.</li>
<li>Also, in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, Fleur and her sister, Gabrielle, both say goodbye to Ron, Gabrielle kisses him on the cheek and Fleur gives him a kiss on the forehead. In the book, Fleur shakes Harry's hand and tells him that she hopes to see him again.</li>
<li>In the film, Fleur appears to already be something of a stand-out student and star pupil at Beauxbatons, doing a solo dance as Beauxbatons enters Hogwarts along with her sister, whereas the others dance in a pack.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i> Fleur spoke with a thick French accent, however in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> she spoke with a normal UK English accent. A possible explanation is that she improved her English in the time between the events of <i>Goblet of Fire</i> and the events of <i>Deathly Hallows</i>. Despite being French herself, actress Clémence Poésy speaks English with almost no accent, and actually had to fake Fleur's thick accent in her first appearance.</li>
<li>In the video game Fleur slaps Ron on the cheek when they say goodbye.</li>
<li>According to houseofnames.com, the Delacour family originated in the Brittany region in France.</li></ul>
