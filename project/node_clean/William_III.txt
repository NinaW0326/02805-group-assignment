<b>William III</b> (<a href="/wiki/14_November" title="14 November">14 November</a> <a href="/wiki/1650" class="mw-redirect" title="1650">1650</a> – <a href="/wiki/8_March" title="8 March">8 March</a> <a href="/wiki/1702" class="mw-redirect" title="1702">1702</a>) was <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">King</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, ruling jointly with his wife <a href="/wiki/Mary_II" title="Mary II">Queen Mary II</a> from <a href="/wiki/1689" class="mw-redirect" title="1689">1689</a> until her <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> in <a href="/wiki/1694" class="mw-redirect" title="1694">1694</a>, and thereafter as sole monarch until his own death in 1702.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> was begging for recognition and protection under <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> law, William and Mary failed to send a delegation to them, which contributed to the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy</a> in <a href="/wiki/1692" class="mw-redirect" title="1692">1692</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/William_III?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/William_III?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "William" is from the Germanic name Willahelm, which was composed of the elements wil "will, desire" and helm "helmet, protection".
