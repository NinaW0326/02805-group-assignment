<p><b>Lavender Brown</b> (c. <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>/<a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a> – <a href="/wiki/2_May" title="2 May">2 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a><sup id="cite_ref-PageToScreen_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PageToScreen-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a><sup id="cite_ref-blood_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blood-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown%27s_father" title="Lavender Brown&#39;s father">Mr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown%27s_mother" title="Lavender Brown&#39;s mother">Mrs Brown</a>, and sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>. She started her education at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, the same year as the famous <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.
</p><p>While at Hogwarts Lavender began studying <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>, which she was fascinated by. In her fifth year she joined <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>. This was an organisation taught and led by Harry Potter, despite initially doubting Harry's claims that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> had <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">returned</a>. The following year she started a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, though they soon broke up.
</p><p>In her final year of schooling Lavender joined the reconstituted D.A. to oppose <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> control of Hogwarts and fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>. During the battle she was savaged by <a href="/wiki/Lycanthropy" title="Lycanthropy">werewolf</a> <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> and died of her injuries.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lavender was born in <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a> or <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a> to <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown%27s_father" title="Lavender Brown&#39;s father">Mr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown%27s_mother" title="Lavender Brown&#39;s mother">Mrs Brown</a>. Since she was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a>, all four of her grandparents must have been <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a>.
</p><p>She received her <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_acceptance_letter" title="Hogwarts acceptance letter">acceptance letter</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> at age eleven, and purchased her equipment from <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a> before the start of her first year.
</p>

<p>Lavender began attending Hogwarts in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, the same year as famous wizard <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, at the age of eleven and was the first of the <a href="/wiki/First_year" title="First year">first years</a> to be <a href="/wiki/Sorting_Hat" title="Sorting Hat">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Houses" title="Hogwarts Houses">House</a>, much to the delight of her peers.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She shared a dormitory with <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a>, and other two girls and soon became best friends with Parvati. However, she did not get along as well with Hermione as she did with the other three girls. Hermione was believed by most to be a loner and made friends with boys rather than girls. There were also personality clashes between Hermione and the other Gryffindor girls; Hermione, being rather studious, may have been better suited with <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> girls.
</p>

<p>In her second year, Lavender was one of the students present in <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns" title="Cuthbert Binns">Professor Binns</a>' <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a> class when Hermione asked about the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>; like the rest of the class, Lavender paid much more attention at this point, even rising her head off her arms.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lavender attended <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Professor Lockhart</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a>, and when Lockhart was <a href="/wiki/Disarming_Charm" title="Disarming Charm">disarmed</a> by <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Professor Snape</a>, she handed him back his <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS11_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS11-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It is likely that Lavender, given her common nature had a crush on Professor Lockhart, as did many of the other female students even including Hermione Granger, and would be shocked to discover he was a fraud at the end of the year.
</p>

When Divination Professor <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Sybill Trelawney</a> saw a <a href="/wiki/Grim" title="Grim">Grim</a> (a death omen in the form of a spectral dog) while seeking divinatory insight in the dregs of tea in the bottom of Harry's tea cup, Lavender was amongst the very few students in the class who, like Harry and <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a> (both students who grew up with Muggles and unaware of magic until their letters arrived), did not initially know what Grims were.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<p>She soon developed a good relationship with Professor Trelawney, who accurately predicted — or guessed — the death of Lavender's pet rabbit named <a href="/wiki/Binky" title="Binky">Binky</a>. The professor said that what Lavender was dreading would occur on the <a href="/wiki/16_October" title="16 October">sixteenth of October</a>, and on that day, Lavender received news that her rabbit, who had been a baby at the time, had been mauled to death by a fox. Hermione pointed out that Lavender could not possibly have dreaded something unexpected and that the death must have happened days before the news arrived, but Lavender had faith in Trelawney's abilities and was upset by Hermione's rather tactless attempt at comforting her.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During her fourth year, Hogwarts hosted the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>, a series of challenges for wizards to enter if they believed themselves capable. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, who was in Lavender's year, was selected as a champion. As one of the traditions, the tournament held a ball and Lavender attended the Yule Ball with fellow Gryffindor student <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a>, likely just as friends. Her best friend <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati</a> ended up going as Harry's date.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>That same year, when Care of Magical Creatures professor <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> attempted to see if <a href="/wiki/Blast-Ended_Skrewts" class="mw-redirect" title="Blast-Ended Skrewts">Blast-Ended Skrewts</a> hibernate (which they did not), she stayed to help collect the runaways instead of fleeing into the hut like the <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherins</a>, and singed her robes in the process. Later that year, Barty Crouch Jnr, in disguise as <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a>, used the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> on her in class, thus forcing her to humiliate herself publicly by imitating a squirrel. Hermione Granger was not impressed that <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Barty Crouch Jnr</a> had the nerve to teach the three Unforgivable Curses in school and was appalled when he demonstrated them on students. She didn't come to the aid of Lavender when she was being humiliated, but she did stick up for <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> when he was horrified by the demonstration of the Cruciatus Curse on a spider.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of the year, Lavender would attend the leaving feast, which was held as a memorial service to the late <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a> who was murdered during the Triwizard Tournament. During this service, Albus Dumbledore took the liberty upon himself to inform the students of Hogwarts that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> was at large once again.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

On the second day of the school year, <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Macmillan" title="Ernest Macmillan">Ernie Macmillan</a>'s public of support of Harry and his claims seemed to force her to begin to question her own beliefs. It is possible that <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a>, a fellow <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> may have been putting doubts into her head also as he strongly supported the Ministry and Lavender was close with Seamus. The same year, <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> was appointed the new <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">DADA</a> professor as a way for the Ministry at large to keep tabs on Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>

<p>However, despite the claims that Harry had been lying, Lavender was one of the first students to arrive at the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head" class="mw-redirect" title="Hog&#39;s Head">Hog's Head</a> for the first meeting of what would become Dumbledore's Army. She learned many different defensive and offensive charms from Harry. When the D.A. practised casting the highly advanced <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>, Lavender was only able to achieve puffs of silver vapour, and was becoming increasingly frustrated that she could not do it.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Later on in the year, High Inquisitor Dolores Umbridge began inspecting the teachers of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> and Lavender was saddened when Professor Trelawney was temporarily sacked by Umbridge after her inspection, and even brought her some nice daffodils, though she continued to take the class under the <a href="/wiki/Centaur" title="Centaur">centaur</a> <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a>, upon whom both she and Parvati had a crush, despite him being half-man and half-horse. This year Lavender took her <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">O.W.L.</a> exams and practised basic <a href="/wiki/Locomotion_Charm" title="Locomotion Charm">Locomotion Charms</a> with Parvati the week leading up to the exams.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Lavender achieved a high grade on her O.W.L. exams for: <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a>, as she attended <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a>-level classes in these subjects. However, she did not achieve a high enough O.W.L. score to continue on with <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a>. Harry Potter, Ron Weasley, and Hermione Granger were the only <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindors</a> able to attend N.E.W.T.-level class for that subject.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is also likely that she achieved a high mark in <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">Divination</a> as well, given her passion for the subject.
</p>

<p>By the beginning of her sixth year, Lavender had developed a crush on Ron Weasley. She complimented him, flirted with him, and wished him luck in his <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> games. After a particularly good performance in one of those games, Ron and Lavender ended up kissing. This greatly upset <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, as she had hoped Ron would attend <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Professor Slughorn</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club_Christmas_party" title="Slug Club Christmas party">Christmas party</a> with her and she had expected a more than platonic relationship to develop between them. It is unclear if Lavender was aware of her room-mate's feelings for Ron, though it seems likely that she would not have gotten involved with Ron if she realised that he was dating her largely to make Hermione jealous.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The two dated for a few months, and were often seen kissing enthusiastically in public. For Christmas, Lavender sent Ron a gaudy <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley%27s_sweetheart_necklace" title="Ronald Weasley&#39;s sweetheart necklace">necklace</a> which read 'My Sweetheart' dangling from a chain. He disliked it immensely and after the holidays, their relationship began to go downhill. Lavender was very upset when Ron was poisoned, and when he increasingly pulled away from her. He feigned sleep when she came to visit him in the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a>, avoided her, and became visibly annoyed with her affections.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Worried that her relationship with Ron was fading, she sought out the advice of his best friend, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. She would talk to Harry about Ron's feelings, making him feel uncomfortable. Ron also reconciled with Hermione and spent increasing amounts of time with her, making Lavender suspicious. After she saw Ron and Hermione leave the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> dormitory seemingly alone (since Harry was under his <a href="/wiki/Cloak_of_Invisibility" title="Cloak of Invisibility">Invisibility Cloak</a>) she ended their relationship, much to Ron's relief. She attended <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_funeral" class="mw-redirect" title="Dumbledore&#39;s funeral">Dumbledore's funeral</a> at the end of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During her final year at Hogwarts, Lavender joined the second <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> to oppose <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> control of the school. Like others, she eventually took to hiding in the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>, and may have been responsible for it forming a bathroom with washing facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lavender fought in the Battle of Hogwarts, during which she fell from a balcony and was attacked by the <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> Fenrir Greyback. Rushing to her aid, Hermione blasted Greyback away from her with a powerful spell, and Professor Trelawney dropped a <a href="/wiki/Crystal_ball" title="Crystal ball">crystal ball</a> on his head. Immediately after the attack, she was seen feebly stirring, but later died from her injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PageToScreen_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PageToScreen-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Lavender was a somewhat silly and sentimental girl,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> though she was also brave, as she was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> house and fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> She was lively and loved to be the centre of attention, but tended to react in an extreme way to everyday situations, either laughing or crying hysterically and could be easily offended or emotional.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She was also known to squeal and giggle at certain things and did so frequently. She could also be insensitive and childish, acting very possessive and over-protective of Ron when she was his girlfriend, even going so far as to say he looked "interesting" when he was poisoned. She frequently expressed her feelings for him in odd ways, such as calling him "Won-Won", sending him a necklace that made him cringe, writing his name in smog on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a>, and snogging him on a continuous basis, not infrequently in public view. Inevitably, her heart was always in the right place, but she had a tendency to offend or annoy other people, if not always deliberately.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<dd>&#8212;Lavender's belief in Divination<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>She had a great interest in Divination despite the subject being "woolly" and had reverence for Professor <a href="/wiki/Trelawney_family" title="Trelawney family">Trelawney</a>, even though she was something of a fraud.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> This implied that she was gullible, often taking Trelawney's words to heart. Additionally, she initially believed the Ministry's propaganda against <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, but she eventually began to doubt the Ministry's claims, hugely because of her friends' influence.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Much of her personality heavily implies that her social skills may have been poor; this is supported by the fact that she only had one known friend at school, <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a>, and they often had awkward interactions at the expense of others. However, even Parvati was uncomfortable by the fact that her relationship with Ron was becoming increasingly unhealthy. She stood quite in contrast with the more pragmatic and socially stable <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a></b>: Lavender was very interested in the field of Divination, and shared a close relationship with <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Professor Trelawney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It was implied that she was good at Divination, and most likely took the <a href="/wiki/Divination_(class)" title="Divination (class)">subject</a> to N.E.W.T.-level, given her keen enthusiasm expressed in class.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a></b>: Lavender advanced to <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a>-level <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a>, showing aptitude in the subject. She must have achieved either an '<a href="/wiki/Exceeds_Expectations" title="Exceeds Expectations">Exceeds Expectations</a>' or an '<a href="/wiki/Outstanding" title="Outstanding">Outstanding</a>' on her O.W.L. exam.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Lavender attended N.E.W.T. <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a>, meaning she achieved a high grade on her O.W.L. exam.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She was also able to produce a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>, an incredibly advanced and powerful piece of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and was able to cast the simple <a href="/wiki/Locomotion_Charm" title="Locomotion Charm">Locomotion Charm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Lavender was a committed and loyal member of <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> which she joined in her fifth year, and therefore learned how to cast numerous defensive and offensive <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a> under <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s masterful teaching.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She also recieved a high mark in her O.W.L. in the subject, which allowed her to study it further at N.E.W.T.-level, showing her proficiency in this field.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: Lavender bravely fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>, showing she was a skilled duellist to an extent with talent in <a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">martial magic</a>, but she was unfortunately not strong enough to fend off <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> who killed her in the battle.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown%27s_wand" title="Lavender Brown&#39;s wand">Wand</a></b>: Lavender's wand was of an unknown length, <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">core</a> material. <p>Parvati Patil was Lavender's best friend and room-mate, and was often seen in her company. The two seemed to share many interests and character traits, including a keen interest in <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>, as well as a fondness for more appealing types of magical creatures, like <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">unicorns</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> They were also brave as they were both sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> house.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The two were often seen giggling and gossiping together during their school days, and shared a friendship with <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Professor Trelawney</a>. Parvati became very uncomfortable with how seriously Lavender took her relationship with <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> in their sixth year. Parvati was often embarrassed by the behaviour of her friend in regards to Ron, though despite this the two remained close.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Her reaction to Lavender's death is unknown, although she was likely devastated at the death of her closest friend.
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, during her sixth year, Lavender developed a crush on fellow Gryffindor <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>. She took to complimenting him and flirting with him, which Ron seemed surprised and flattered by, though he was interested in <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> at the time. However, after Ron became angry with Hermione, he entered into a romantic relationship with Lavender. The romance consisted mostly of snogging, often in public, much to the disgust of Hermione, who was jealous and hurt, and of Ron's younger sister <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny</a>, who considered him a "<i>filthy hypocrite</i>" because he insulted her for being caught kissing <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">her boyfriend</a> earlier in the year.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>

<p>Lavender was very affectionate with, and possessive of, Ron, adopting the name of "Won-Won" for him and sending him a <a href="/wiki/Ron_Weasley%27s_sweetheart_necklace" class="mw-redirect" title="Ron Weasley&#39;s sweetheart necklace">necklace</a> for <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> saying "<i>My Sweetheart</i>" on it, which he greatly disliked and was shocked that she would think it was to his taste. The romance began to cool on Ron's end after the <a href="/wiki/Christmas_holidays" title="Christmas holidays">Christmas holidays</a>, which Lavender noticed and was very upset by. She repeatedly pressed <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> for details of Ron's feelings for her, and alarmed even her best friend <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati</a> with how obsessively she threw herself into the relationship. Lavender was infuriated when no one thought to inform her after Ron was poisoned, especially since Ron took to feigning sleep whenever she came to visit him in the infirmary, and reconciled with Hermione. After being released, Ron took to avoiding his girlfriend.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Ron was spared the difficult task of breaking up with Lavender when she ended things with him, convinced that there was something going on between him and Hermione. Afterwards, Ron was clearly content, but Lavender was upset for some time. For example, when Ron brushed fake snow off Hermione's shoulders soon after the break-up, Lavender burst into tears. Ron did show genuine guilt for breaking up with her.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown how Ron reacted when Lavender was killed by <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>, although when he saw her being savaged, Ron was clearly horrified and concerned but Hermione beat him to saving Lavender. Considering this, it is likely Ron at least was saddened by her death.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Lavender and Parvati were not nearly as close to their other room-mate, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, as they were to one another. Though the girls did not appear to dislike each other, they had very different interests and sometimes clashed as a result. For example, Hermione scorned <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a>, Lavender and Parvati's favourite subject.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She also became irked with Lavender for doubting <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s assertion that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> had returned in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a><sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Though Hermione was jealous of Ron and Lavender's relationship, she directed her anger mostly at Ron, perhaps indicating that Lavender was initially unaware of Hermione's feelings for him. Eventually, however, Lavender started to become jealous and suspicious of the time Ron and Hermione spent together, while her boyfriend was increasingly pulled away from her.
</p><p>Although Hermione was glad when they broke up, she tried to spare Lavender's feelings by not being openly affectionate with Ron.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP_4-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Hermione spotted Lavender being savaged by <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a>, blasting him away immediately, it was too late as Lavender was already dead at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Lavender seemed to have a reasonably close and caring relationship with Divination professor <a href="/wiki/Sybill_Trelawney" title="Sybill Trelawney">Sybill Trelawney</a>; she had an unwavering faith in the reliability of Trelawney's predictions and was often seen comforting or supporting her when she was experiencing difficult times. She and Parvati became offended on the professor's behalf when Hermione made her disdain for Trelawney and the subject she taught clear in <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> When she was temporarily sacked by <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, Lavender and Parvati brought Trelawney some daffodils.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the Battle of Hogwarts, Trelawney prevented <a href="/wiki/Fenrir_Greyback" title="Fenrir Greyback">Fenrir Greyback</a> from savaging Lavender any further after Hermione Granger blasted him away by dropping a crystal ball on his head. However, during the course of the fighting, Lavender succumbed to her wounds.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_14-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Lavender became a member of <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> during her <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%931996_school_year" title="1995–1996 school year">fifth year</a>. Dumbledore's Army was an organisation founded by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, Ron and Hermione, and led by Harry, in order to teach the students <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, something that professor <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> did not; thinking that the students would use their magic against the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lavender was a friend of fellow members <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Thomas" title="Dean Thomas">Dean Thomas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a>. Lavender may have been a friend of <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>, too, although, when the two girls met each other, Lavender made fun of Luna for her strange earrings.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_8-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown?veaction=edit&amp;section=21" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown?action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lavender" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lavender">Lavender</a></i> is a type of flower, as well as a name for the pale shade of purple of the blossoms. In flower symbolism, the lavender can symbolise affection, cleansing, or concealment. The Ancient Romans used lavender to scent baths, and its name is derived from the Latin verb <i>lavāre</i>, "to wash". In Western culture, the colour <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lavender_(color)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lavender (color)">lavender</a> can sometimes be used to symbolise decadence and the enjoyment of sensuality. Interestingly, "Lavender" is a colour and her last name is "Brown", which is also a colour.
</p>

<ul><li>Lavender Brown was played by <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Cauley" title="Kathleen Cauley">Kathleen Cauley</a> in the second film, and then in a non-speaking role by <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Smith" title="Jennifer Smith">Jennifer Smith</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>. She did not have any lines and was never mentioned by name, but appeared in several scenes. She is played by <a href="/wiki/Jessie_Cave" title="Jessie Cave">Jessie Cave</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Part 2</a></i>.
<ul><li>Many fans speculate as to why Lavender Brown's actress was changed for the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, but the reasoning is quite simple. Before <i>Half-Blood Prince</i>, Lavender Brown played no major role, she was portrayed by two separate actresses in the second and third film and was a background character. Background characters have no specific casting, which is why multiple people can portray them. Usually they are given character names just to keep order on a film set. Lavender Brown, as a major character role, was not cast until the sixth film. The filmmakers had no way of knowing she would play such a role when they assigned her name to the background actresses from previous films, which is why her actress changed in the sixth film.</li>
<li>Casting a white actress in a role that had previously been held by black actresses, however, earned the film-makers some criticism for white-washing the cast. Although, in the book, when she and Ron are cuddling, it is said that "it was impossible to tell whose hands were whose", implying she is the same race as Ron, as otherwise their hands could have been clearly distinguished.</li>
<li>Jessie Cave recently portrayed Lavender again for one of her Pindippy videos.<sup class="noprint">&#91;<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Wikipedia:Citing sources"><i><span title="The material in the vicinity of this tag needs references to reliable sources." style="white-space: nowrap;">citation needed</span></i></a>&#93;</sup></li></ul></li>
<li>In the novel <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, Lavender Brown's fate after Fenrir Greyback's attack is left uncertain: she is last mentioned "feebly stirring" after the attack. The film <i>Deathly Hallows: Part 2</i> is more clear about her fate, appearing to show her death outright, a fact confirmed in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_Page_to_Screen:_The_Complete_Filmmaking_Journey" title="Harry Potter Page to Screen: The Complete Filmmaking Journey">Harry Potter Page to Screen: The Complete Filmmaking Journey</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-PageToScreen_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PageToScreen-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> was inconclusive about the matter, originally listing her as "<i>Presumed dead, 2 May 1998</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> though this was removed sometime prior to 25 January 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>While the additional rendition never made it onto the  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> editorial article where Artist <a href="/wiki/Owen_Davey" title="Owen Davey">Owen Davey</a> was commissioned,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> it is interesting to note that, on the portfolio page for said commission on Davey's official website, Lavender's portrait fades away, being treated the same way as most other confirmed deceased individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li>On <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s draft class list, Lavender was listed as a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and as she was attending Hogwarts <a href="/wiki/1997-1998_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1997-1998 school year">1997-1998</a>, she must have been a pure-blood, half-blood, or she faked her blood status, as she would have been taken by the Death Eaters otherwise.</li>
<li>Lavender was the fifth student to be sorted in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, and she was the first Gryffindor.</li>
<li>Lavender is incorrectly referred to as a male in the <a href="/wiki/German" title="German">German</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>.</li>
<li>Lavender is the only original member of Dumbledore's Army not to at least be mentioned in the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">video game</a>. However, when <a href="/wiki/Padma_and_Parvati_Patil" title="Padma and Parvati Patil">Padma and Parvati Patil</a> are told to meet in the Room of Requirement, Lavender's name is ticked on the Dumbledore's Army parchment.</li>
<li>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i> novel, Lavender either did not qualify for or did not select <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a>-level <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a> as one of her classes. However, in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">film adaptation</a>, she appears to be attending a Potions class.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">first part of the film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, Lavender can be briefly seen on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Express" title="Hogwarts Express">Hogwarts Express</a> sitting next to <a href="/wiki/Romilda_Vane" title="Romilda Vane">Romilda Vane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leanne" title="Leanne">Leanne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cormac_McLaggen" title="Cormac McLaggen">Cormac McLaggen</a>.</li></ul>
