<b>Lord Stoddard Withers</b> (<a href="/wiki/1642" class="mw-redirect" title="1642">1642</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/1769" class="mw-redirect" title="1769">1769</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magizoologist" title="Magizoologist">Magizoologist</a> who bred <a href="/wiki/Winged_horse" title="Winged horse">Winged horses</a>. He created a sport that combined elements of polo and <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>, which was played on Winged horses instead of <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomsticks</a>. The sport never became popular though.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Stoddard_Withers?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Stoddard_Withers?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The English word "withers" refers to the back of a horse, specifically between the shoulder-blades (at which place winged horses' wings can be expected to be found).
</p>
