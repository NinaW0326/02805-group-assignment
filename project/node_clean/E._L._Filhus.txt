<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/E._L._Filhus?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/E._L._Filhus?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1_December" title="1 December">1 December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> issue, Filhus wrote an editorial about the threat <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> posed to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a>, titled "MACUSA... is the USA ready?".<sup id="cite_ref-Featurette_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Featurette-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FB-dec1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB-dec1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An editorial of theirs, entitled "<a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Grindelwald's</a> Stratagem? World Domination?", appeared in the <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> sunrise edition.<sup id="cite_ref-dec6sunrise_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dec6sunrise-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the day's sunset edition, Filhus wrote a report about an address that <a href="/wiki/Seraphina_Picquery" title="Seraphina Picquery">Seraphina Picquery</a>, the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America">President</a> of <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a>, was slated to give to the "fearful" American <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> community.<sup id="cite_ref-nyghost_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyghost-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> An editorial of theirs on an unknown subject was published in the same issue.<sup id="cite_ref-nyghost_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyghost-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They wrote a report, published in the <a href="/wiki/7_December" title="7 December">7 December</a> 1926 sunset edition, about how President Picquery was calling for <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a> to be on "full alert" because foreign wizarding powers had not yet committed to helping deal with the recent <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">secrecy</a> breaches in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nyghost-dec7_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyghost-dec7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This issue also featured an editorial by them on an unknown subject.<sup id="cite_ref-nyghost-dec7_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyghost-dec7-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
