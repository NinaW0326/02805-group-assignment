<b>C. Roemmele</b><sup id="cite_ref-hppts_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hppts-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> woman who was headmistress of <a href="/wiki/St._Grogory%27s_Primary_School" title="St. Grogory&#39;s Primary School">St. Grogory's Primary School</a> in the early <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/C._Roemmele?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/C._Roemmele?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She once sent a letter to <a href="/wiki/Vernon_Dursley" title="Vernon Dursley">Vernon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Petunia_Dursley" title="Petunia Dursley">Petunia Dursley</a> informing them that their nephew and ward, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, had been "climbing school buildings," when he had in fact <a href="/wiki/Underage_magic" title="Underage magic">unintentionally</a> <a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">flown</a> onto the school roof while trying to hide from his cousin <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">Dudley</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley%27s_gang" title="Dudley Dursley&#39;s gang">gang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/22_June" title="22 June">22 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, Roemmele presented Dudley with <a href="/wiki/Headteacher%27s_Award" title="Headteacher&#39;s Award">an award</a> for "always eating up his lunch."<sup id="cite_ref-hppts_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hppts-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
