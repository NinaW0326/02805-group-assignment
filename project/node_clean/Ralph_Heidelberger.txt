<b>Ralph Heidelberger</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who played as <a href="/wiki/Keeper" title="Keeper">Keeper</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_National_Quidditch_team" title="American National Quidditch team">American National Quidditch team</a> during the four-hundred and twenty-second <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_World_Cup" title="Quidditch World Cup">Quidditch World Cup</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. He wore a helmet with a brown <a href="/wiki/Pumpkin" title="Pumpkin">pumpkin</a> design on it.<sup id="cite_ref-QWC_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QWC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By the year <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, he was immortalised on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> for his achievements in the sport of <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBPG_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBPG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is unknown whether his ancestors came from <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg" title="Heidelberg">Heidelberg</a> home of the <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg_Harriers" title="Heidelberg Harriers">Heidelberg Harriers</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ralph_Heidelberger?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ralph_Heidelberger?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name "Ralph" is contracted form of the Old Norse name <i>Rádúlfr</i> (or its Norman form <i>Radulf</i>). It was introduced into England by Scandinavian settlers before the Norman conquest, though afterward it was bolstered by Norman influence. In the Middle Ages it was usually spelled Ralf, but by the 17th century it was most commonly Rafe, reflecting the normal pronunciation. The Ralph spelling appeared in the 18th century. A famous bearer of the name was Ralph Waldo Emerson (1803-1882), an American poet and author who wrote on transcendentalism.</li>
<li>See <a href="/wiki/Radulf" title="Radulf">Radulf</a>, its Latin form Radolphus <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external autonumber" href="https://harrypotter.fandom.com/wiki/Radolphus_Pittiman">[1]</a><a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external autonumber" href="https://harrypotter.fandom.com/wiki/Radolphus_Lestrange">[2]</a>) and its Spanish form <a href="/wiki/Raul_Almeida" title="Raul Almeida">Raul Almeida</a>.</li></ul>
