<b>Leopoldina Smethwyck</b> (<a href="/wiki/1829" class="mw-redirect" title="1829">1829</a> - <a href="/wiki/1910" title="1910">1910</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, who was well known for her career as a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_referee" title="Quidditch referee">Quidditch referee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Leopoldina_Smethwyck?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Leopoldina_Smethwyck?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smethwyck was born in <a href="/wiki/1829" class="mw-redirect" title="1829">1829</a>, somewhere in the British isles, into the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> <a href="/wiki/Smethwyck_family" title="Smethwyck family">Smethwyck family</a>. She was the first witch in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> to ever <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_referee" title="Quidditch referee">referee</a> a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> match. For this reason, she was featured on a silver <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Famous_Witches_and_Wizards_Cards" title="Famous Witches and Wizards Cards">Famous Witches and Wizards Cards</a> series. Smethwyck <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">died</a> in <a href="/wiki/1910" title="1910">1910</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%931994_school_year" title="1993–1994 school year">1993–1994 school year</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> of her guarded a secret passage to a <a href="/wiki/Bertie_Bott%27s_Every_Flavour_Beans" title="Bertie Bott&#39;s Every Flavour Beans">Every Flavour Beans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pumpkin_Pasty" title="Pumpkin Pasty">Pumpkin Pasties</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cauldron_Cake" title="Cauldron Cake">Cauldron Cakes</a> bonus room, with the password was <i>Polyglot</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Leopoldina_Smethwyck?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Leopoldina_Smethwyck?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The first time she refereed occurred some time before the Women's Rights Movement, although this might not matter in the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>.</li>
<li>She may be related to <a href="/wiki/Elliot_Smethwyck" title="Elliot Smethwyck">Elliot Smethwyck</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hippocrates_Smethwyck" title="Hippocrates Smethwyck">Hippocrates Smethwyck</a> or both, as they share the same surname.</li></ul>
