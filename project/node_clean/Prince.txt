<p>A <b>prince</b> is a title usually given to a son of the <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a>. A female equivalent is a <b>princess</b>.
</p><p>Many kings were once princes, such as English kings <a href="/wiki/Richard_I" title="Richard I">Richard I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII" title="Henry VIII">Henry VIII</a>.
</p><p>Although there are usually no princes amongst the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizardkind</a>, there was at least one princess who was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>; this was <a href="/wiki/Mary_I" title="Mary I">Mary</a>, daughter of Henry VIII, who became <a href="/wiki/Queen" title="Queen">Queen</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Her half-sister, Princess <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I" title="Elizabeth I">Elizabeth</a>, never <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married</a>, but became queen, too.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> once claimed that <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell%27s_turban" title="Quirinus Quirrell&#39;s turban">his turban</a> was given to him by one <a href="/wiki/African_prince" title="African prince">African prince</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Prince?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Prince?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although there are usually no princes in the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, there are persons surnamed like that. There is a <a href="/wiki/Prince_family" title="Prince family">Prince family</a>. The only known family member, <a href="/wiki/Eileen_Prince" title="Eileen Prince">Eileen Prince</a>, married a <a href="/wiki/Tobias_Snape" title="Tobias Snape">Muggle</a> and had a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> son, <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> who was known as The Half-Blood Prince.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Prince?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Prince?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Prince">Prince</a></i> is a general term for a monarch or a member of a monarch's family, and is a hereditary title of some <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> royalty, likely alluding to the aristocratic status of <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> families in the wizarding world. The word is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Princeps" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Princeps">princeps</a></i>, meaning "first", which was one of the titles of the Roman Emperor, in which case it was generally translated as "First Citizen". <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Prince" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Prince">The Prince</a></i> is also the title of a famous political treatise by Niccol√≤ Machiavelli; an interesting point considering that the adjective derived from the author's surname, <i>Machiavellian</i>, is defined as "attempting to achieve goals by cunning, scheming and unscrupulous methods", which may allude to the ideal traits of <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> house.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
