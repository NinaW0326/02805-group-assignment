<p><b>Mundungus "Dung" Fletcher</b><sup id="cite_ref-OP1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was involved in many illegal activities, having a notoriously disreputable reputation as a petty criminal.<sup id="cite_ref-OP1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However, despite his illicit dealings, he was one of the original members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>, a group dedicated to fighting <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP5_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, many members of the Order had mixed feelings about him; however, he was very loyal to the Order's head, <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, who once got Fletcher out of serious trouble. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Very little is known of Mundungus's early life, although it is known that he was born in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a><sup id="cite_ref-birthplace_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthplace-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> to the <a href="/wiki/Fletcher_family" title="Fletcher family">Fletcher family</a><sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> sometime before <a href="/wiki/1962" title="1962">1962</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-birthday_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birthday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When he was eleven years old, he received <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher%27s_wand" title="Mundungus Fletcher&#39;s wand">a wand</a>, and attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> for seven years.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown even to the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> which <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Houses" title="Hogwarts Houses">House</a> he was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into, for reasons unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a> of <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, all of Mundungus's family had died, and he never married or had any children.<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="First_Wizarding_War_(1970s-1981)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Wizarding_War_.281970s-1981.29">First Wizarding War <span class="template-c">(1970s-1981)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War (1970s-1981)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War (1970s-1981)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dd><div class="noprint"><i>Main article&#58; <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a></i></div></dd></dl>
<p>Sometime around <a href="/wiki/1970" title="1970">1970</a>, Mundungus joined the newly-founded <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP5_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was disliked and mistrusted by most of the other members due his very poor reputation as a criminal, but was fiercely loyal to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> after Dumbledore had helped him out of a bit of trouble.<sup id="cite_ref-OP5_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His acquaintances in the figurative underworld proved to be quite useful in gaining information for the Order.<sup id="cite_ref-OP5_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Sometime in <a href="/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a>, Mundungus offended <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a> and was subsequently permanently banned from the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head_Inn" title="Hog&#39;s Head Inn">Hog's Head Inn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP17_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP17-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Between_the_Wars_(1981-1995)"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Between_the_Wars_.281981-1995.29">Between the Wars <span class="template-c">(1981-1995)</span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Between the Wars (1981-1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Between the Wars (1981-1995)"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At some point by <a href="/wiki/1988" title="1988">1988</a>, Mundungus Fletcher got the <a href="/wiki/Marauder%27s_Map" title="Marauder&#39;s Map">Marauder's Map</a> by trading it with someone at Hogwarts. He was later attacked and the map got stolen from him by the attacker. He was also attacked by someone in white robes, although this was a separate attack.<sup id="cite_ref-HMY5ch11_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HMY5ch11-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a> was investigating <a href="/wiki/Knockturn_Alley" title="Knockturn Alley">Knockturn Alley</a> with another student, <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Rakepick" title="Patricia Rakepick">Patricia Rakepick</a> managed to capture Mundungus.<sup id="cite_ref-HMY5ch11_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HMY5ch11-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She tied him up in the <a href="/wiki/Three_Broomsticks_Inn" title="Three Broomsticks Inn">Three Broomsticks Inn</a>, where she and Jacob's sibling investigated him about the Marauder's Map.<sup id="cite_ref-HMY5ch11_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HMY5ch11-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/3_August" title="3 August">3 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> conducted a raid with the <a href="/wiki/Misuse_of_Muggle_Artefacts_Office" title="Misuse of Muggle Artefacts Office">Misuse of Muggle Artefacts Office</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>, and when his back was turned, Mundungus tried to <a href="/wiki/Hex" title="Hex">hex</a> him, although perhaps luckily he was unsuccessful.<sup id="cite_ref-CS3_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS3-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/26_August" title="26 August">26 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>, Mundungus attended the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a>, where he slept under a <a href="/wiki/Cloak" title="Cloak">cloak</a> propped up on sticks.<sup id="cite_ref-GF10_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF10-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Nonetheless, after the <a href="/wiki/Reappearance_of_the_Dark_Mark" title="Reappearance of the Dark Mark">Death Eater riot</a> that took place, he attempted to input an absurdly inflated compensation claim for damages to a twelve-bedroom tent with a built-in <a href="/wiki/Jacuzzi" title="Jacuzzi">jacuzzi</a>, though <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a>, who was working at the Ministry at the time and who had seen Mundungus under the cloak-on-sticks, reported it and Mundungus did not receive anything.<sup id="cite_ref-GF10_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF10-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> learned on <a href="/wiki/24_June" title="24 June">24 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">Return of the Dark Lord</a>, he demanded of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> that the "old crowd" be alerted to the incident, specifically naming Mundungus, <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF36_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF36-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

<p>With the return of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> in <a href="/wiki/June" title="June">June</a> of <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, Mundungus rejoined the Order of the Phoenix, but preferred to carry out business deals on stolen goods.<sup id="cite_ref-OP1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mundungus was supposed to be watching <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> on <a href="/wiki/2_August" title="2 August">2 August</a>, <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, but instead decided to go off to buy some stolen <a href="/wiki/Cauldron" title="Cauldron">cauldrons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP2_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> When he returned to his post, he was shouted at and attacked by <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a> for deserting his duties as Harry had <a href="/wiki/Attack_in_Little_Whinging" title="Attack in Little Whinging">been attacked</a> by <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> and forced to defend himself and <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Dursley" title="Dudley Dursley">his cousin</a> using magic when underage.<sup id="cite_ref-OP2_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p><p>He got into trouble with Molly Weasley when he tried to bring his load of stolen <a href="/wiki/Cauldron" title="Cauldron">cauldrons</a> to Headquarters for storage. He also provided a number of controlled magical ingredients to <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a>, which helped them set up their magic joke shop. However, he slightly redeemed himself in <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Mrs Weasley</a>'s eyes while cleaning out Grimmauld Place, when he saved <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">her son</a> from a set of purple cloaks that attempted to strangle him.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP6_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP6-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>

<p>When the initial meeting of <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> was held in the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head_Inn" title="Hog&#39;s Head Inn">Hog's Head Inn</a>, Mundungus was present, unbeknown to Harry, and reported back to the Order. Sirius Black, revealing this to Harry, said that Mundungus was heavily veiled and disguised as a witch because he had previously been barred from the Hog's Head, and admitted that Mundungus had been tailing Harry.<sup id="cite_ref-OP17_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP17-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>

Mundungus also provided a <a href="/wiki/Christmas_tree" title="Christmas tree">Christmas tree</a> for the house, which he also likely stole.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP23_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP23-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Imprisonment_and_escape">Imprisonment and escape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Imprisonment and escape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Imprisonment and escape"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Apparently, since the death of Sirius in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, Mundungus had been clearing valuables out of the headquarters of the Order and selling them. Harry caught Fletcher red-handed in <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> selling Black family silver to <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">the bartender</a> of the Hog's Head, which had been stolen from Sirius' house; these items and the entire residence at <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a> were left to Harry in Sirius' will. Harry confronted him using physical force before Mundungus <a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Disapparated</a> and went into hiding.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> His exact whereabouts were a mystery, but it was later noted in the <i>Daily Prophet</i> that he had been arrested and sent to Azkaban for impersonating an <a href="/wiki/Inferius" title="Inferius">Inferius</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP21_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP21-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He likely escaped during the mass breakout described by Kingsley Shacklebolt in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>.
</p>

<p>Later, shortly before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, Harry found himself in the Hog's Head and happened to see a magic mirror, twin to the one given to him by Sirius Black. The bartender, <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>, said that it had been sold to him by Mundungus, and he had been using it from time to time to keep an eye on what Harry was doing.<sup id="cite_ref-DH28_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH28-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Harry relived <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>'s memories, he saw the portrait of Albus Dumbledore, in conference with Snape, developing the plan of having multiple mock Potters to allow the escape from Privet Drive. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fletcher presumably went into hiding after his encounter with the trio at Grimmauld Place. He was not known to have taken part in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, but it is possible that he did, without being noticed by Harry Potter. Mundungus survived the war and returned to his affairs as a disliked dealer of artefacts.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Calamity">Calamity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Calamity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Calamity"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a>, Mundungus often came into the field of view of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">SOS Task Force</a>. He taught volunteers several "subtleties he learned from spotting vulnerabilities in his marks", and at <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head_Inn" title="Hog&#39;s Head Inn">Hog's Head Inn</a> he revealed "the art of surprise".<sup id="cite_ref-WU_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mundungus attempted to sell a load of "found" classics to the school. He also flooded the market with substandard <a href="/wiki/Re%27em" title="Re&#39;em">Re'em</a> blood - cut with common Ox blood. <a href="/wiki/Calamity_Investigator" title="Calamity Investigator">Calamity Investigators</a> shut down this operation. Mundungus attempted to steal from the Task Force a crate of ill-gotten <a href="/wiki/Leaping_Toadstool" title="Leaping Toadstool">Leaping Toadstools</a>, but fled after a fierce but brief duel.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By at least the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, Mundungus claimed to have created song named <a href="/wiki/Solowithmio" title="Solowithmio">Solowithmio</a>, and gave a performance of it to a crowd that contained a Calamity Investigator for the SOS Task Force. Unable to actually play the guitar, Fletcher was able to convince the entire crowd that he was a skilled guitarist by slipping a <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher%27s_potion" title="Mundungus Fletcher&#39;s potion">potion</a> into the crowd's drinks. The Calamity Investigator eventually discovered this and forced Fletcher to return all of the money he had made from the "performance" to the attendees, and to teach the Calamity Investigator the brewing secrets that he used to brew the potion.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mundungus was a short wizard with bandy legs and long, straggly ginger hair.<sup id="cite_ref-OP2_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He had bloodshot, baggy<sup id="cite_ref-OP2_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> brown<sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> eyes and rarely shaved.<sup id="cite_ref-OP2_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His hands were quite grubby, and he was noted to have a particularly strong bodily odour of tobacco and <a href="/wiki/Alcohol" title="Alcohol">alcohol</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP5_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix"><b>Mundungus</b>: "<i>Sirius? This solid silver, mate?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Sirius Black</b>: "<i>Yes. Finest fifteenth- century goblin-wrought silver, embossed with the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black family</a> crest.</i>"</dd>
<dd><b>Mundungus</b>: "<i>That'd come off, though...</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; Mundungus polishing a goblet to rid it of the Black family crest.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>
</div>
<p>Mundungus was a petty and unscrupulous criminal, specialising in committing petty crimes to make as much profit as he could. He always took valuable things from wherever he went, such as <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s death, Mundungus stole his possessions, including <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a>. He was also somewhat cowardly, as he abandoned <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>; Moody was killed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> immediately afterwards. It is possible that Mundungus fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, because, if he did, he most likely survived the war and continued his work as an disreputable artefact dealer.
</p><p>However, Mundungus appeared to be braver than people such as <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>. Though he had a tendency to flee when the situation seemed grim, he never actually turned against the Order of the Phoenix. Also, his crimes mainly included stealing and robbing, never directly harming or killing others, meaning he was capable of some amount of compassion and decency. He was capable of loyalty and repaying debts, if only to Dumbledore.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a></b>: Like most accomplished adult wizards, Mundungus Fletcher was capable of apparating at will successfully, although he did sometimes create a loud bang whilst doing so.<sup id="cite_ref-OP2_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Mundungus was able to conjure a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> a highly advanced piece of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and a mark of superior magical ability and talent given the immense difficulty of casting it.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">Martial magic</a></b>: While not a particularly brave man and thus prone to avoid dangerous confrontations whenever possible, be it by disguising himself in order to avoid people he would rather not cross,<sup id="cite_ref-HM69_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM69-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> or when he fled the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>, Mundungus was a capable duellist, but not as skilled as some. Although he was once defeated by an <a href="/wiki/Wizard_in_white_robes" title="Wizard in white robes">assassin</a> working for <a href="/wiki/R" title="R">a dangerous cabal</a>, and was unwilling to contend with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Rakepick" title="Patricia Rakepick">Patricia Rakepick</a>, he was nevertheless one of the members of the Order on rotation that were entrusted the task of guarding <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> following the <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">Return of Lord Voldemort</a>, speaking for the confidence the Order had in his abilities to hold his own when it mattered.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">Nonverbal magic</a></b>: Mundungus was able to perform a non-verbal version of the <a href="/wiki/Revulsion_Jinx" title="Revulsion Jinx">Revulsion Jinx</a> to escape the grasp of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> after Harry caught him red-handed after stealing some of Sirius' belongings outside the <a href="/wiki/Three_Broomsticks_Inn" title="Three Broomsticks Inn">Three Broomsticks Inn</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Given the high-level of difficulty required in the casting of spells non-verbally, this implies he was in fact a rather powerful and skilful wizard.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potions</a></b>: Mundungus possessed a degree of talent in the field of potion-making, as he invented a <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher%27s_potion" title="Mundungus Fletcher&#39;s potion">potion</a> to make the drinker believe poorly played music sounded better than what it truly was.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Asset acquisition</b>: A skill that Mundungus possessed in great enough aptitude that he was kept in the Order for his talent at attaining physical or informational assets. Be it an enchanted car or simply detailed information on the black market, Mundungus was a rather resourceful in his ability get his hands on whatever the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> required of him. Both as a thief and informant, Mundungus was not the kind of wizard to let something useful to him or his allies out of his reach. This tendency, however, has made him a character of ill repute despite his contributions to his allies.</li>
<li><b>Music</b>: Mundungus had some skills as an amateur musician and songwriter, having written a song titled "<a href="/wiki/Solowithmio" title="Solowithmio">Solowithmio</a>". However, he lacked the ability to play the guitar well, and had to use a <a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher%27s_potion" title="Mundungus Fletcher&#39;s potion">potion</a> to convince a crowd of people that he was in fact a skilled guitarist.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Order_of_the_Phoenix_members">Order of the Phoenix members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Order of the Phoenix members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Order of the Phoenix members"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Mundungus did not have a very good relationship with the most of the Order of the Phoenix members. They did not like him, as he was a criminal, but put up with him as his criminal contacts could give him intelligence about <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">Mrs Weasley</a> seemed to dislike him, as she thought he would be a bad influence on <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George</a> by telling them about his "jobs" (probably because she was afraid of her children following his example). She also told him not to smoke in the kitchen of <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>. However, she stopped disliking him so much when Mundungus saved <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> from a magic cloak that attempted to strangle him. <a href="/wiki/Arabella_Figg" title="Arabella Figg">Arabella Figg</a> disliked him too, when he abandoned his mission to watch <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, who was attacked by <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a>. Mundungus was regarded by many as a coward. He seemed to be intimidated by <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> and protested having to partner with him during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>. While he seemed to get along well with <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>, this didn't stop him from stealing Black family heirlooms following Sirius's death.
</p><p>In 1997, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> kidnapped Mundungus in order to learn about <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a>. Mundungus told them that he had given the locket to <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>. This led to Harry, Ron, and Hermione infiltrating the Ministry in order to find the locket and destroy it.
</p><p>The members of the Order learned about Mundungus's cowardice when he abandoned <a href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Seven_Potters" title="Battle of the Seven Potters">Battle of the Seven Potters</a>; Moody was killed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. It is unknown if Mundungus fought along with other members during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>.
</p><p>Mundungus also has a mixed relationship with the bartender of the <a href="/wiki/Hog%27s_Head" class="mw-redirect" title="Hog&#39;s Head">Hog's Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth Dumbledore</a>. While Aberforth banned Mundungus for life from the bar for offending him twenty years ago, the two remained in touch, with Mundungus selling stolen goods to Aberforth. It was also mentioned that Mundungus was loyal to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, who had helped him at some point in the past. Albus scared Mundungus a bit but nevertheless, Mundungus never betrayed him.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fred_and_George_Weasley">Fred and George Weasley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Fred and George Weasley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Fred and George Weasley"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mundungus seemed to have a good relationship with <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a>. He smuggled them items for their joke shop, <a href="/wiki/Weasleys%27_Wizard_Wheezes" title="Weasleys&#39; Wizard Wheezes">Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>, behind their <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">mother's</a> back, including <a href="/wiki/Venomous_Tentacula" title="Venomous Tentacula">Venomous Tentacula</a> seed, for which he charged them ten <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">Galleons</a>. Fred and George nicknamed him "Dung".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?veaction=edit&amp;section=18" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mundungus_Fletcher?action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Mundungus is an obsolete term for a malodorous tobacco.</li>
<li>A fletcher is an arrow-maker; one of his ancestors in the male line may have made arrows.</li></ul>

<ul><li>Mundungus Fletcher was played by <a href="/wiki/Andy_Linden" title="Andy Linden">Andy Linden</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-AndyasMundungus_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AndyasMundungus-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Radcliffe" title="Daniel Radcliffe">Daniel Radcliffe</a>, of course, played Mundungus during the "Seven Potters" scene. On the DVD, Andy pointed out that he was amazed by how well Dan pretended to be him.</li>
<li>Mundungus's appearance in the seventh film is greatly altered from his description in the books. He is described as having shaggy hair, bandy legs and bloodshot eyes. In the film he is bald, has normal legs and his eyes are not bloodshot.</li>
<li>Mundungus doesn't ever appear in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">fifth film</a>. It is unknown why J.K. Rowling never told the filmmakers to do so when she told them to include <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a>, as Mundungus, like Kreacher, has a big part to play in the adventure with the locket by giving it to Umbridge. One possible explanation is that Mundungus's character was much easier to introduce later on.</li>
<li>It is possible that Mundungus appears only for a brief second in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>. His possible appearance is the bald man behind Bill Weasley when he is being comforted by <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur</a> because they and the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a> are mourning <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred</a>'s death.</li>
<li>Elizabeth Tassel, a major character in <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Silkworm" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Silkworm">The Silkworm</a></i>, is mentioned as having had a Doberman named Fletcher, who died before the start of the novel.</li></ul>
