<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argo_Pyrites?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argo_Pyrites?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name Argo derives from the Greek word <i>argos</i>, meaning “swift”.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
In Greek mythology, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argo" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Argo">Argo</a> was the ship on which <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jason" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Jason">Jason</a> and his companions, the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argonaut" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Argonaut">Argonauts</a>, sailed on their journey to find the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Fleece" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Golden Fleece">Golden Fleece</a>. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrite" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Pyrite">Pyrite</a> is a shiny yellow mineral, which is also called Fool's Gold, due to its resemblance to the precious metal.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Argo_Pyrites?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Argo_Pyrites?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This character is mentioned only on a page from an early draft of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i> which <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> posted on her <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling%27s_official_site" title="J. K. Rowling&#39;s official site">website</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lex_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lex-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He may have been related to <a href="/wiki/Pyrites" title="Pyrites">Pyrites</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> who was included in early drafts of the same book, but was also discarded.</li>
<li>It is also possible that the Death Eater in question was supposed to be Argo Pyrites, and that the two are one and the same.</li></ul>
