<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lysander_Scamander?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lysander_Scamander?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Lysander means "liberator of mankind" or simply "liberator" in Greek.</li>
<li>Lysander is the name of a principal character in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:William Shakespeare">Shakespeare's</a> <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:A Midsummer Night&#39;s Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>. The play takes place&#160;largely at&#160;night, <i>"lit by moonlight"</i> as Oberon states, which hearkens back to Lysander's mother, Luna.</li></ul>
