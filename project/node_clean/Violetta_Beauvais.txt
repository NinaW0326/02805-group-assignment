<b>Violetta Beauvais</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She, along with <a href="/wiki/Shikoba_Wolfe" title="Shikoba Wolfe">Shikoba Wolfe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thiago_Quintana" title="Thiago Quintana">Thiago Quintana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Jonker" title="Johannes Jonker">Johannes Jonker</a>, was one of four <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmakers</a> who served <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> in the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beauvais was born in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> All of the <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> she produced were made from <a href="/wiki/Swamp_mayhaw" title="Swamp mayhaw">swamp mayhaw</a> wood.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> For many years, she kept the <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">core</a> she used in her wands a closely-guarded secret, but it was eventually discovered to be <a href="/wiki/Rougarou_hair" title="Rougarou hair">rougarou hair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Beauvais's wands were often accused of having a natural affinity for <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, many great American wizards of the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a> used Beauvais-made wands, including <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Seraphina_Picquery" title="Seraphina Picquery">Seraphina Picquery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Violetta</i> is a form of the name Violet, after the flower of the genus <i>Viola</i>. They share their name with a shade of purple, a popular colour among wizards
</p><p><i>Beauvais</i> is a French surname, from <i>beau</i> ("beautiful") + <i>vais</i> ("to go (away)"). Violetta may be named after Ren√© Beauvais, a carpenter and master woodcarver from Lower Canada known for his extensive work on churches.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since <a href="/wiki/Rappaport%27s_Law" title="Rappaport&#39;s Law">Rappaport's Law</a> required  a <a href="/wiki/Wand_permit" title="Wand permit">Wand permit</a>, it is unknown how users of these wands would apply for a permit as the <a href="/wiki/Wand_permit" title="Wand permit">wand Permit application</a> requires the applicant to tell the <a href="/wiki/Wand_Permit_Office" title="Wand Permit Office">Wand Permit Office</a> details of the wand. It might be that she was required to inform the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Wand_Permit_Officer" title="Federal Wand Permit Officer">Federal Wand Permit Officer</a>.
</p>
