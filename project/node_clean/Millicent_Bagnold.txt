<p><b>Minister Millicent Bagnold</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who served as the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> from <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a> to <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wombat_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wombat-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Her tenure is most remembered for taking place during <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s first downfall, in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wombat_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wombat-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bagnold was born in or prior to <a href="/wiki/1963" title="1963">1963</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-birth_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birth-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British Isles</a>. In her youth, she attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, where she was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Minister_for_Magic">As Minister for Magic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: As Minister for Magic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As Minister for Magic"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>Famous Ravenclaw Ministers for Magic include Millicent Bagnold, who was in power on the night that Harry Potter survived the Dark Lord's curse, and defended the wizarding celebrations all over Britain with the words, 'I assert our inalienable right to party'.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Robert_Hilliard" title="Robert Hilliard">Robert Hilliard</a>.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1980" title="1980">1980</a>, at the height of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, Bagnold rose to the position of <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wombat_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wombat-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Bartemius Crouch Snr</a> was Head of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Law_Enforcement" title="Department of Magical Law Enforcement">Department of Magical Law Enforcement</a> during Ms. Bagnold's term.<sup id="cite_ref-GF27_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF27-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was in power when, on the evening of <a href="/wiki/31_October" title="31 October">October 31</a>, <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> led an <a href="/wiki/Attack_at_Godric%27s_Hollow_(1981)" title="Attack at Godric&#39;s Hollow (1981)">attack</a> in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a> that resulted in the murders of <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Potter</a>, the destruction of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s corporeal body, as well as the inadvertent creation of a <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. The night that followed turned out to be the major upheaval of Bagnold's term in office since <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> up and down the country started <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Lord_Voldemort_celebrations" title="Fall of Lord Voldemort celebrations">celebrating Voldemort's downfall</a> and the end of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> in what became "a night of large-scale breaches of the <a href="/wiki/International_Statute_of_Wizarding_Secrecy" title="International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy">International Wizarding Statute of Secrecy</a>"<sup id="cite_ref-wombat_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wombat-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PS1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Bagnold defended these celebrations by famously stating that she asserted the wizards' "inalienable right to party."<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 302px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e8/Wizengamot.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190228140853" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/e/e8/Wizengamot.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20190228140853" decoding="async" width="300" height="125" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Wizengamot.jpg" data-image-key="Wizengamot.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Wizengamot.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">The <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> trials before the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Magical_Law" title="Council of Magical Law">Council of Magical Law</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>It was also during her term in office that <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> <a href="/wiki/Igor_Karkaroff" title="Igor Karkaroff">Igor Karkaroff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rodolphus_Lestrange" title="Rodolphus Lestrange">Rodolphus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rabastan_Lestrange" title="Rabastan Lestrange">Rabastan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Bartemius Crouch Jnr</a> were rounded up and tried before the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Magical_Law" title="Council of Magical Law">Council of Magical Law</a> and sent to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF30_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF30-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> was falsely accused to be one of Voldemort's supporters and one of those responsible for the murders of <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Potter</a> and was sent to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> without a trial.<sup id="cite_ref-GF27_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF27-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Bagnold's retirement in <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>, it was unclear who would succeed her. In the early <a href="/wiki/1980s" title="1980s">1980s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Bartemius Crouch Snr</a> was very likely to succeed Bagnold, but his popularity was tarnished after the scandal in which he sent <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">his own son</a> to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GF27_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF27-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> also had a lot of popular support to be her successor, but he never expressed any desire to take the Minister's job, so <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> took the post instead.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OP5_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP5-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">September 1</a>, <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">Prefect</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hilliard" title="Robert Hilliard">Robert Hilliard</a> welcomed the <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> <a href="/wiki/First_year" title="First year">first years</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a> with a short speech in which he mentioned former Minister Millicent Bagnold as one of the many notable Hogwarts students who had been <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a>, like them, into Ravenclaw.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Bagnold's term in office was almost the same as <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Margaret_Thatcher" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Margaret Thatcher">Margaret Thatcher</a>'s (<a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a>-<a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>).</li>
<li>She shares the same first name and last initial as <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> student <a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bulstrode" title="Millicent Bulstrode">Millicent Bulstrode</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Millicent_Bagnold?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The name "Millicent" is from the Germanic name <i>Amalasuintha</i>, composed of the elements <i>amal</i> "work, labour" and <i>swinþ</i> "strength".</li>
<li>Her last name is probably derived from the English author and playwright <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enid_Bagnold" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Enid Bagnold">Enid Bagnold</a>.</li></ul>
