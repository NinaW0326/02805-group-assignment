<p><b>Miss Boyle</b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">No-Maj</a> woman who was part of the upper echelons of <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> society in the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NewYorkStar_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewYorkStar-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was one of the attendees of the <a href="/wiki/Fundraising_Dinner_for_the_Re-election_of_Senator_Henry_Shaw" title="Fundraising Dinner for the Re-election of Senator Henry Shaw">fundraising dinner</a> for the re-election of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Shaw_Junior" title="Henry Shaw Junior">Senator Henry Shaw</a> in December 1926. When the function was interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Credence_Barebone" title="Credence Barebone">Credence Barebone</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Obscurus" title="Obscurus">Obscurus</a>'s attack on the Senator, Miss Boyle was at a loss as to what to do as chaos ensued. She later was quoted in a report on the incident published in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Star" title="The New York Star">The New York Star</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-NewYorkStar_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewYorkStar-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
