</p><p><b>Luca Caruso</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> musician. He collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Alice_Tolipan_(Muggle)" title="Alice Tolipan (Muggle)">Alice Tolipan</a> to provide the <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a> for the adapted production of <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i> that was staged at the <a href="/wiki/Regency_Playhouse" title="Regency Playhouse">Regency Playhouse</a> in the summer of <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Luca_Caruso_(Muggle)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Luca_Caruso_(Muggle)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a male given name, <i>Luca</i> is the Italian and Romanian form of <i>Luke</i>, an English form of the Greek name <i>Loukas</i>, meaning "from Lucania."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucania" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lucania">Lucania</a> was an ancient region of <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Southern Italy</a> extending from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyrrhenian_Sea" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Tyrrhenian Sea">Tyrrhenian Sea</a> to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Taranto" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Gulf of Taranto">Gulf of Taranto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Caruso</i> is an Italian surname derived from the Italian word <i>caruso</i>, "close-cropped," itself from the Latin <i>cariosus</i>, "smooth" or "bald."<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The word took on the broader meaning of "boy" in Italian, since, during the Middle Ages, when most men wore their hair longer, it was fashionable for young men to keep theirs short.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <i>Caruso</i> also became an occupational name for sulphur pit workers in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agrigento" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Agrigento">Girgenti</a> region of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sicily" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Sicily">Sicily</a> because their job required short hair.<sup id="cite_ref-ancestry_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ancestry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
