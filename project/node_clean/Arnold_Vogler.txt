<b>Arnold Vogler</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> commentator, who might have worked for the <a href="/wiki/German_Ministry_of_Magic" title="German Ministry of Magic">German Ministry of Magic</a>. He was a former player of the <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg_Harriers" title="Heidelberg Harriers">Heidelberg Harriers</a>, for which he played in an unknown position.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Arnold_Vogler?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Arnold_Vogler?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Arnold" comes from a Germanic name meaning "eagle power", derived from the elements arn "eagle" and wald "power". It was brought by the Normans to England, where it replaced the Old English cognate Earnweald.
</p>
