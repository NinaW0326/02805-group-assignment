<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">"<i>He's the most intelligent of his kind I've ever met.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s acknowledgement of Crookshanks' advanced intelligence<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p><b>Crookshanks</b> was <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>'s pet <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cat</a>. He was half-<a href="/wiki/Kneazle" title="Kneazle">Kneazle</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-kneazle_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kneazle-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kneazle2_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kneazle2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as evidenced by his <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lion</a>-like appearance, ability to solve problems on his own without aid or teaching, and clear dislike of and ability at recognising untrustworthy persons (even if they are <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">transfigured</a>).
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a> Crookshanks was purchased from <a href="/wiki/Magical_Menagerie" title="Magical Menagerie">Magical Menagerie</a> by Hermione Granger. The <a href="/wiki/Magical_Menagerie_saleswoman" title="Magical Menagerie saleswoman">saleswoman</a> from whom she had purchased him stated that he had been in the shop for a long time as "nobody wanted him". Crookshanks played an important role in the events that took place on <a href="/wiki/6_June" title="6 June">6 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. During the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Crookshanks stayed at the <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">the Burrow</a>. It is unknown what happened to him after the war, but it can be assumed that he was returned to Hermione.
</p>

<p>Crookshanks was purchased on <a href="/wiki/31_August" title="31 August">31 August</a> <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Menagerie" title="Magical Menagerie">Magical Menagerie</a>, who had originally gone there seeking an <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a>. The proprietor informed Hermione that he had been there for quite some time, and that "nobody wanted him".<sup id="cite_ref-POA4_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA4-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Hermione took Crookshanks to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry with her and he was present on the train when <a href="/wiki/Dementor" title="Dementor">Dementors</a> boarded in search of Sirius Black. Perhaps because he was half-<a href="/wiki/Kneazle" title="Kneazle">Kneazle</a>, the newly-adopted cat demonstrated the ability to detect frauds masquerading as animals, most notable of which being <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagi</a>. Before the start of the <a href="/wiki/1993-1994_school_year" class="mw-redirect" title="1993-1994 school year">1993-1994 school year</a>, Crookshanks attacked <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> in his Animagus form of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Scabbers</a>, Ron's beloved pet <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">rat</a> when Ron took him in as he was looking unwell after the <a href="/wiki/Weasley_family" title="Weasley family">Weasley family</a>'s trip to <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA4_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA4-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
Afterwards, Crookshanks sought Scabbers out even attacking him in Ron's presence and causing the rat to seek hiding places.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 251px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/9f/Crookshanks_and_scabbers.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20180108200426" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/9/9f/Crookshanks_and_scabbers.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/249?cb=20180108200426" decoding="async" width="249" height="107" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Crookshanks and scabbers.jpg" data-image-key="Crookshanks_and_scabbers.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Crookshanks_and_scabbers.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Crookshanks seeking out Scabbers in the Great Hall</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Crookshanks would even try to sneak into the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Boys%27_Dormitory" title="Gryffindor Boys&#39; Dormitory">Gryffindor Boys' Dormitory</a> to seek out Scabbers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Crookshanks got another chance at Scabbers during <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> when Hermione brought him into the boys' dormitory.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> These constant attacks were cause for Ron and Hermione to argue. Early in second term, when Ron discovered blood on his bed sheet and Scabbers gone, he was convinced that Crookshanks had eaten him.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> This caused a row with Hermione until the rat was found at Hagrid's. But Crookshanks was not the only one after Scabbers. <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s attempts to enter <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> that year, after escaping from the wizard prison <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>, was to procure Pettigrew, who framed Sirius for the betrayal of Harry's parents <a href="/wiki/James_and_Lily_Potter" title="James and Lily Potter">James and Lily</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> and murder of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew%27s_unidentified_victims" title="Peter Pettigrew&#39;s unidentified victims">twelve Muggles</a> on a crowded London street during the first War.
</p><p>At some point during the year, Crookshanks met <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> in his Animagus form, the stray dog mistaken by Harry for the <a href="/wiki/Grim" title="Grim">Grim</a>. He immediately took a trusting of Sirius, meeting with him on the Hogwarts grounds several times and stealing the passwords to <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Tower" title="Gryffindor Tower">Gryffindor Tower</a> from <a href="/wiki/Neville_Longbottom" title="Neville Longbottom">Neville Longbottom</a> to help him get into the castle. Crookshanks helped Sirius lure Harry, Ron, and Hermione into the <a href="/wiki/Shrieking_Shack" title="Shrieking Shack">Shrieking Shack</a>. During the scuffle between Harry and Sirius, Crookshanks attempted both to steal Harry's wand and attack Harry, and when both failed, set himself over Sirius' heart. The hesitation that Harry felt gave enough time for <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> to arrive.
</p>

<p>Crookshanks led the way back through the tunnels. However, when Lupin transformed into a <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Pettigrew</a> stole Lupin's wand and stunned both <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> and Crookshanks, escaping. After all this, Ron accepted Crookshanks as a remarkable animal companion, asking for his 'judgement' of the small owl that Sirius had offered to him as compensation for the loss of Scabbers to confirm that the owl wasn't another <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Crookshanks did not react to the tiny owl, therefore it was decided that there was nothing wrong with it.
</p>

<p>Crookshanks soon became more friendly to others; for example, he once curled up in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s lap when Harry was waiting to hear from Sirius. However, he still remained unusually perceptive and disapproving of bad behaviour, judging by how he stared at Harry and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron</a> while they made up answers for their <a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a> homework.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> (who Ron claimed was a great lover of cats<sup id="cite_ref-CS_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>) seemed fond of Crookshanks; she enjoyed playing with him and was able to coax him out in the kitchen of <a href="/wiki/Grimmauld_Place" title="Grimmauld Place">Grimmauld Place</a> during an <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> meeting, rolling butter beer corks for him to catch.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He had a fondness of chasing Wizard Chess pieces and had to occasionally be restrained in their presence. He was wary of fanged frisbees and hissed when one came near him. At a Gryffindor party, he took to following Ginny around, his yellow eyes fixed upon her new Pygmy Puff Arnold.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Wizarding_War">Second Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Crookshanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Crookshanks?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, Hermione and Crookshanks came to stay at <a href="/wiki/The_Burrow" title="The Burrow">the Burrow</a>, but Hermione left with Harry and Ron after <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fleur_Delacour" title="Fleur Delacour">Fleur Delacour</a>'s wedding reception was interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is unknown what became of Crookshanks after Hermione was forced to abandon him during the <a href="/wiki/Attack_at_the_Burrow" title="Attack at the Burrow">attack at the Burrow</a> in 1997. Hermione did not bring him along on the <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore_and_Harry_Potter%27s_Horcrux_Hunt" title="Albus Dumbledore and Harry Potter&#39;s Horcrux Hunt">Horcrux Hunt</a>. Most likely, Crookshanks stayed in the Burrow under the Weasleys' care until he could be reunited with Hermione after the war.
</p>

<div class="quote">

However, he could be moody towards those who threw him off their lap, no matter if it was accidental or deliberate.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Crookshanks?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Crookshanks?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Crookshanks</i> seems to refer to the cat's bandy legs, from <i>crook</i>, meaning "a bend", and <i>shank</i>, a term for the lower part of the legs. It is likely to be derived from the Scottish surname 'Cruikshanks', which is pronounced in exactly the same way. <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Cruikshank#Charles_Dickens">George Cruikshank</a> was a well-known British illustrator in the 1800s and illustrated <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Dickens" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Charles Dickens">Charles Dickens</a>' novel <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oliver_Twist" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Oliver Twist">Oliver Twist</a></i>, the first of the famous "orphan novels" that influenced <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> as she came up with the character <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.
</p>

<li>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i> book, Crookshanks was bought by Hermione while Harry and Ron were looking for <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Scabbers</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">film</a>, this scene was omitted and he was first seen chasing Scabbers when Harry left his room in <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">The Leaky Cauldron</a>. Ron later referred to him as a "bloody beast" and a "pig with hair".</li>
<li>Crookshanks' role in the film version <i>Prisoner of Azkaban</i> is considerably diminished compared to his role in the book; he is never shown as friends with Sirius Black nor does he help Sirius in any way.</li>
<li>Hermione's ownership of Crookshanks may be reminiscent of her transformation to a cat about one year prior to his purchase.</li>
<li>In the films, Crookshanks is played by <a href="/wiki/Crackerjack" title="Crackerjack">Crackerjack</a>, as said on the second disc of the DVD of <i>Prisoner of Azkaban</i>. He is also played by <a href="/wiki/Prince_(cat)" title="Prince (cat)">Prince</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pumpkin_(cat)" title="Pumpkin (cat)">Pumpkin</a>. To make Crackerjack's hair look tatty like Crookshanks's, his trainer stuck bits of his already shed fur onto his body.</li>
<li>A plush version of Crookshanks is available at <a href="/wiki/The_Wizarding_World_of_Harry_Potter" title="The Wizarding World of Harry Potter">The Wizarding World of Harry Potter</a> and at <i><a href="/wiki/The_Making_of_Harry_Potter" title="The Making of Harry Potter">The Making of Harry Potter</a></i>.</li>
<li>Crookshanks' type of cat's face is known as a "pansy face." Based on these markings, Crookshanks would have the characteristics of a hansa Himalayan, or ginger Himalayan cat. He could also be a Persian, but due to his face shape the former is more probable. Many people get these breeds confused. The Persian has larger, buggier eyes and rounder upper lips than a Himalayan.</li></ul>
