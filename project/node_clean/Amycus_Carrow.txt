<p><b>Professor Amycus Carrow</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>, brother of <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">Alecto Carrow</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>. Amycus fought in the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a> along with his sister. They did not search for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> after his first downfall but were forgiven after Voldemort's <a href="/wiki/Return_of_Lord_Voldemort" title="Return of Lord Voldemort">return</a> in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a> and joined the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> ranks in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. 
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Ministry_of_Magic" title="Fall of the Ministry of Magic">fall of the Ministry of Magic</a> in <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, Amycus was made <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor, which for all intents and purposes simply became "<a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts</a>" during his tenure. His sister was stationed at Hogwarts as well. Alecto was the new <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a> professor. He and his sister were also made <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Deputy Headmasters</a>, serving under Headmaster <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a>. Amycus liked to cruelly punish and torture the students who opposed the new regime. 
</p><p>In the evening of <a href="/wiki/1_May" title="1 May">1 May</a> <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, shortly before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>, he was <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">tortured</a> by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> when he spat in <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall's</a> face. He was then <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperiused</a> and tied up in <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a> by McGonagall herself. After the battle, it can be assumed he was sent to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> along with his sister, where presumably they carried out life-long sentences for their crimes.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Amycus Carrow was born sometime before <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a><sup id="cite_ref-HBP2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> into the <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">Pure-blood</a> or <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">Half-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Carrow_family" title="Carrow family">Carrow family</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-blood_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blood-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British Isles</a>. At some point before his instruction in the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> arts, he acquired a wand.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP27_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP27-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in his youth, and was a member of <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> house.<sup id="cite_ref-LEGOY2_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LEGOY2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He seemed to have been acquainted with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP27_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP27-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Wizarding_War">First Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Amycus and <a href="/wiki/Alecto_Carrow" title="Alecto Carrow">his sister</a> supported <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>, and became <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Attack_at_Godric%27s_Hollow_(1981)" title="Attack at Godric&#39;s Hollow (1981)">Voldemort's downfall</a> in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, they did not search for their master, believing that Voldemort was gone forever, and managed to avoid imprisonment in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP2_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-carrowsjkr_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carrowsjkr-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>His sister was keeping watch when Harry arrived with <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>. Alecto immediately touched her <a href="/wiki/Dark_Mark" title="Dark Mark">Dark Mark</a>, letting <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> and all the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> know that they had got him. Alecto was immediately <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">Stunned</a> by Luna, but Amycus demanded <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> to let him into the Tower. Once inside, he saw his unconscious sister but no sign of Harry, as both he and Luna were hidden beneath his <a href="/wiki/Cloak_of_Invisibility" title="Cloak of Invisibility">Invisibility Cloak</a>. Aware that they were going to feel Voldemort's wrath if they did not hand him Harry, Amycus planned to pass it off on the students, and when Professor McGonagall objected calling Amycus a coward in the process, he spat in her face. This show of disrespect for both sentient life and McGonagall enraged Harry to the point of disregarding stealth and revealing himself by throwing a Cruciatus Curse at Amycus with such force that he slammed into the wall. <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> then bewitched him with the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> to carry his unconscious sister, then used a spell to <a href="/wiki/Conjuration" title="Conjuration">conjure</a> a net, and the siblings were tied up and hoisted into the air, where they were left dangling beneath the ceiling.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-war">Post-war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Post-war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-war"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Neither Amycus nor his sister fought in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> as both of them were kept tied-up at <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a> and after the battle and the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>. Amycus was most likely imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>, for his crimes committed as a Death Eater under <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a>'s de-corrupted <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, along with his sister. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amycus Carrow was squat and lumpy, with a lopsided leer and a wheezy giggle, much like his sister.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP27_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP27-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Hunched, he had a pallid, doughy face and tiny eyes.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He also had a pig-like face.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As a Death Eater, he bore the Dark Mark on his left inner forearm.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Amycus was accomplished in dark magic as he was a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP27_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP27-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He could cast the <a href="/wiki/Cruciatus_Curse" title="Cruciatus Curse">Cruciatus Curse</a> effectively<sup id="cite_ref-HBP28_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP28-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and, apparently, the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a>. It is also likely that he could summon <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">Fiendfyre</a> and presumably had the means to control it, though he was never shown doing so.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He was skilled enough to teach students in <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a> and was placed as a professor of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts class</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">Duelling</a></b>: Amycus was skilled in duelling as he seemed too much for <a href="/wiki/Ginevra_Weasley" title="Ginevra Weasley">Ginny Weasley</a> to overpower during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Astronomy_Tower" title="Battle of the Astronomy Tower">Battle of the Astronomy Tower</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP27_5-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP27-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Although, he was unable to stand up to more powerful duellists such as <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, as both he and his sister retreated with their arms covering their heads as she sent blast after blast after them.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Apparition" title="Apparition">Apparition</a></b>: Like most adult wizards, Amycus was capable of apparating at will. Amycus disapparated away from beyond Hogwarts grounds following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Astronomy_Tower" title="Battle of the Astronomy Tower">Battle of the Astronomy Tower</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP28_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP28-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Teaching Skills</b>: Amycus taught the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts_(class)" title="Dark Arts (class)">Dark Arts</a> to students during his time as a Professor at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. He did have some noticeable skill in teaching, particularly to receptive students such as <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a>. According to Neville Longbottom Crabbe and Goyle loved the class, and it was the only subject in which they had ever shown any talent. It is known that Amycus was talented enough to teach students to successfully perform both the Cruciatus Curse and the Killing Curse.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amycus">Amycus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Amycus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Amycus"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>In Greek mythology, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amycus_(disambiguation)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Amycus (disambiguation)">Amycus</a> was the son of the sea god, Poseidon, and the nymph, Melia. He was a boxer as well as a soldier in the Trojan War. He eventually turned evil and was killed by Polydeuces, a son of Zeus and one of Jason's Argonauts.</li>
<li><i>"Amycus"</i> is also genus of South American spiders.</li>
<li><i>"Amycus"</i> may come from the Latin "<i>amicus"</i>, meaning "<i>friend</i>" (however this is ironic, as Amycus is obviously not a 'friend' to the cause of good).</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carrow">Carrow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Carrow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Amycus_Carrow?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Carrow"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>In English, the word; "<i>Carrow</i>", can refer to two places. "<i>Carrow</i>" in Norfolk, or "<i>Carraw</i>" in Northumberland. The first is assumed to have its name derived from the Old English word "<i>carr</i>", meaning "<i>rock</i>", and a word of Celtic origin; "<i>hoh</i>", meaning "<i>spur of a hill</i>". While the second appears to have been named either after the plural of the Old English word "<i>carr</i>", or after a mixture of a form of "<i>carr</i>", and another Old English word; "<i>raw</i>", meaning "<i>row</i>".</li></ul>
<ul><li>It is also possible, that the surname was originally a shortened version of the Cornish surname; "<i>Nancarrow</i>".</li></ul>
