<p><b>Walburga Black</b> (<a href="/wiki/1925" title="1925">1925</a> – <a href="/wiki/1985" title="1985">1985</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark witch</a>, the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Pollux_Black" title="Pollux Black">Pollux Black</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irma_Crabbe" title="Irma Crabbe">Irma Crabbe</a>, the sister of <a href="/wiki/Alphard_Black" title="Alphard Black">Alphard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_III" title="Cygnus Black III">Cygnus</a>, the wife and second cousin of <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black" title="Orion Black">Orion Black</a>, and the mother of <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Sirius Black III">Sirius Black III</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus Black</a> II. She was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black family</a>, an old wizarding family and one of the <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Twenty-Eight" title="Sacred Twenty-Eight">Sacred Twenty-Eight</a>.
</p><p>Walburga lived in <a href="/wiki/Grimmauld_Place" title="Grimmauld Place">Grimmauld Place</a> starting at an unknown point in her life, presumably after she married her second cousin Orion. She died in <a href="/wiki/1985" title="1985">1985</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black%27s_portrait" title="Walburga Black&#39;s portrait">her portrait</a> still remains at <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>. Walburga's portrait makes anyone not in the <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood elite</a> quite uncomfortable in her home, by constantly shouting insults and epithets at them.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix"><b>Harry Potter</b>: "<i>Were — were your parents <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> as well?</i>"</span></dd>
<dd><b>Sirius Black</b>: "<i>No, no, but believe me, they thought <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> had the right idea, they were all for the purification of the wizarding race, getting rid of <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a> and having <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-bloods</a> in charge… I bet my parents thought Regulus was a right little hero for joining up at first.</i>"</dd>
<dd>&#8212; <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a> regarding his family's views<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd></dl>

<p>Walburga was a member of the traditionalist <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>, who looked down on any except other "respectable" pure-blood wizards and believed in <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood_supremacy" title="Pure-blood supremacy">pure-blood supremacy</a>. She was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Pollux_Black" title="Pollux Black">Pollux Black</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irma_Crabbe" title="Irma Crabbe">Irma Crabbe</a>, and sister of <a href="/wiki/Alphard_Black" title="Alphard Black">Alphard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_III" title="Cygnus Black III">Cygnus</a>. Shortly after graduating Hogwarts, Walburga waited for her second cousin <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black" title="Orion Black">Orion</a> to finish his education and married him at some point in the <a href="/wiki/1950s" title="1950s">1950s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Walburga gave birth to a son, <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius</a>, on <a href="/wiki/3_November" title="3 November">3 November</a>, <a href="/wiki/1959" title="1959">1959</a>. However, Sirius was a severe disappointment to her because he did not agree with the ideology of <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood purity</a> and was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor House</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, rather than the family's traditional house, <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-slytherin_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-slytherin-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Growing up, he ticked off his parents (particularly his mother) by decorating his room with posters from the muggle world depicting bikini-clad women and motorcycles, and had stuck those to the walls with irreversible sticking charms that infuriated her.
</p><p>Sirius was disinherited and removed from the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black Family tree</a> when he left home at the age of sixteen to live with the <a href="/wiki/Potter_family" title="Potter family">Potter family</a>. Walburga personally blasted her son from the tapestry. She maintained a habit of blasting people from the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black family tree</a> which stood in the hall of the family home at <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a> if they displeased her; she did so to her brother <a href="/wiki/Alphard_Black" title="Alphard Black">Alphard</a> simply because he left gold to Sirius despite being disowned. According to <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a>, Walburga was heartbroken by this abandonment, but nonetheless disowned both of them to cleanse the tapestry.
</p><p>Luckily for her, Walburga gave birth to another son, <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus</a>, around <a href="/wiki/1962" title="1962">1961</a>. He followed the family traditions and joined the Slytherin House, and eventually became his mother's favourite, as well as heir to the wealth, position, and traditions of the Black family. Regulus became a <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>; although neither Walburga nor Orion were followers of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> themselves, according to Sirius, they thought he "<i>had the right idea</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP6_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>, until they saw that Voldemort was ruthless in his pursuit for power, at which point they got cold feet. Regulus was killed in <a href="/wiki/1979" title="1979">1979</a> after trying to destroy Voldemort's <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>, and died childless without an heir. As Regulus forbade Kreacher from telling this to anyone, Walburga thought Regulus died trying to leave the organisation.<sup id="cite_ref-DH10_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH10-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Walburga's husband <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black" title="Orion Black">Orion</a> also died in the same year from an unknown cause. Walburga was obviously devastated when her husband and son died.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a>, Walburga's disowned son, Sirius was framed for the murder of his friend, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew%27s_unidentified_victims" title="Peter Pettigrew&#39;s unidentified victims">12 Muggles</a> and was thus sent to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA10_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA10-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It is possible that if Walburga heard of this, she would have been pleased.
</p><p>She died in 1985, leaving her house-elf <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a> in <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">her now abandoned house</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BFT_1-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Portrait_of_Walburga_Black_at_12_Grimmauld_Place" class="mw-redirect" title="Portrait of Walburga Black at 12 Grimmauld Place">Walburga's portrait</a> remained magically fixed to the wall in the hallway of her family home, using a <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Sticking_Charm" title="Permanent Sticking Charm">Permanent Sticking Charm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP5_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The picture, which was covered in canvas when inactive, showed an insane old woman, prone to screaming insults at anyone who disturbed the portrait, particularly that of non-pure-bloods. This happened several times when the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> used Grimmauld Place as their headquarters after her death, and they tried unsuccessfully to remove the portrait for quite sometime. 
</p><p>Walburga survived the deaths of her family, but the destruction of it may account for her final madness. After their deaths, she was alone in the house except for the family <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">house-elf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a>, who seemed equally deranged due to his parroting her beliefs, up until <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> came to know and treat him better one year after inheriting him.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>Stains of dishonour, filthy half-breeds, blood traitors, children of filth!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black%27s_portrait" title="Walburga Black&#39;s portrait">Portrait of Walburga Black</a> screaming at members of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Walburga Black, like most members of her family, was obsessed with blood purity, and <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black%27s_portrait" title="Walburga Black&#39;s portrait">her portrait</a> would often scream insults at blood traitors, <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-bloods</a> or <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">muggle-born</a> wizards. She had little affection for her son <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius</a>, as he was viewed as a <a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitor</a> by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Black family</a> but appeared to have a fairly good relationship with Sirius' younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Regulus_Black" title="Regulus Black">Regulus</a>, and was proud of him when he joined the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. Although she was not a Death Eater herself, she was convinced that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> was doing the right thing by trying to eliminate <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and bringing <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> out of hiding for them to rule both the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">Wizarding</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muggle_world" title="Muggle world">Muggle</a> worlds. Despite this she is known to have become disillusioned with the Death Eaters toward the end of the <a href="/wiki/First_Wizarding_War" title="First Wizarding War">First Wizarding War</a>, showing that even she believed that they were going too far.  
</p><p>Walburga was kind to her house elf, <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a>, who shared her views on blood purity, and as such Kreacher was very loyal to her. Based on her portrait's tendencies of screaming uncontrollably, she seems to have had a very violent temper, similar to her elder son when angered. Sirius justified this by claiming that his mother was an intensely spiteful and heartless woman, though Kreacher did say Walburga was heartbroken by Sirius' defection.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Whilst the true extent of her skill in this field is unknown Walburga Black was proficient enough to have placed a <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Sticking_Charm" title="Permanent Sticking Charm">Permanent Sticking Charm</a> on the back of <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black%27s_portrait" title="Walburga Black&#39;s portrait">her portrait</a> to prevent anyone from taking it off the wall.﻿ The charm held in place for more than a decade after her death.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP5_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She could not, however, cast the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LHP5-7_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LHP5-7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Walburga was proficient in dark magic, being able to cast both the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Imperius_Curse" title="Imperius Curse">Imperius Curse</a> successfully and was also skilled in the handling <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">cursed</a> objects.<sup id="cite_ref-LHP5-7_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LHP5-7-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Black_family">Black family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Black family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Black family"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Walburga and Orion had a happy marriage and relationship, especially since they shared blood purity ideology. Walburga was likely devastated when her husband died. Walburga's eldest son, Sirius was a huge disappointment to her and the rest of the family, since he disagreed with the Black family's beliefs in blood purity and was sorted into <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>. When Sirius left home at the age of 16, she blasted his name off the tapestry. If she had heard about his being sent to Azkaban, she was likely pleased. Though she hated him, Kreacher did state Walburga was heartbroken by Sirius' departure.
</p><p>Walburga greatly admired and favoured her younger son, Regulus because he remained loyal to the Black family traditions and was sorted into Slytherin. Walburga was obviously devastated when her son died.This event may have finally made her to begin doubting the cause of the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> and begin to think that they were taking things to far due to the death of her favoured son.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Walburga is a variation of the name <i>Walpurga</i>, who was a saint for whom <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walpurgis_Night" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Walpurgis Night">Walpurgis Night</a> is named. Historically, Walpurgis Night was considered to be a time when the barrier between the living and the dead was weak and when witches held celebrations. Also, the Knights of Walpurgis (later known as the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>) were named after Walpurgis Night.</li>
<li>Walburga was also the given name of the mother of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josef_Mengele" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Josef Mengele">Josef Mengele</a>, a Nazi doctor who conducted horrific experiments on prisoners in concentration camps. She was said to have been extremely cruel and a Nazi supporter, rather like Walburga Black's views on retaining blood purity.</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/256_Walpurga" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:256 Walpurga">256 Walpurga</a> is a large Main belt asteroid. It was discovered by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Palisa" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Johann Palisa">Johann Palisa</a> on April 3, 1886 in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vienna" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Vienna">Vienna</a> and was named after <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_Walpurga" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Saint Walpurga">Saint Walpurga</a>. This loosely fits in with the Black family tradition of using celestial names for their children.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Walburga Black's likeness on the <a href="/wiki/Black_Family_Tapestry" title="Black Family Tapestry">Black Family Tapestry</a>, as featured in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, seems to have been based on <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://media.vam.ac.uk/media/thira/collection_images/2006BF/2006BF7035_jpg_l.jpg">a depiction of a noblewoman</a> from the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devonshire_Hunting_Tapestries" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Devonshire Hunting Tapestries">Devonshire Hunting Tapestries</a>, currently in the collection of the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a>, in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Portrait_of_Walburga_Black_at_12_Grimmauld_Place" class="mw-redirect" title="Portrait of Walburga Black at 12 Grimmauld Place">Walburga's portrait</a> was not heard screaming, but she could be heard muttering behind her curtain, and <a href="/wiki/Kreacher" title="Kreacher">Kreacher</a> still talked about the "scum" in <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a>, such as "<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudbloods</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">Blood traitors</a>".</li>
<li>Walburga's father was only thirteen when Walburga was born, although it is possible this is a mathematical error on Rowling's part (Rowling has admitted she lacks skills with mathematics).</li>
<li>On the tapestry from the film, Walburga and <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black" title="Orion Black">Orion</a> are depicted together, and their heads looking in different ways, although they are in marriage. This is likely because both Walburga and <a href="/wiki/Orion_Black" title="Orion Black">Orion</a> were born into the House of Black, and their marriage may have been arranged.</li>
<li>Walburga was probably educated at Hogwarts as <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> mentions that he has taught the whole Black Family.</li>
<li>Since Walburga and <a href="/wiki/Lucretia_Black" title="Lucretia Black">Lucretia Black</a> were born in the same year, it is more likely that they were classmates at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>.</li>
<li>Walburga may have known <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>, as he was one or two years behind her, so she could have encountered him at Hogwarts.</li>
<li>It's unknown why Walburga and Orion never joined the Death Eaters, though it's likely that they merely hoped to reap the benefits of their cause, especially because they, as Sirius claims, were very pleased when Regulus became a Death Eater.</li></ul>
