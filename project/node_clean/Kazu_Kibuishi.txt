<dl><dd><span>"<i>The </i>Harry Potter<i> covers by Mary GrandPré are so fantastic and iconic. When I was asked to submit samples, I initially hesitated because I didn't want to see them reinterpreted! However, I felt that if I were to handle the project, I could bring something to it that many other designers and illustrators probably couldn't, and that was that I was also a writer of my own series of middle grade fiction. As an author myself, I tried to answer the question, ‘If I were the author of the books - and they were like my own children - how would I want them to be seen years from now?' When illustrating the covers, I tried to think of classic perennial paperback editions of famous novels and how those illustrations tend to feel. In a way, the project became a tribute to both </i>Harry Potter<i> and the literary classics.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Kazu Kibuishi on being asked to illustrate the <i>Harry Potter</i> series<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Kazu Kibuishi</b>, a 4th-generation <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a>-<a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a>, is an author and illustrator. Best known for his graphic novel <i>Amulet</i>, Kibuishi was commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Scholastic" title="Scholastic">Scholastic</a> to illustrate special edition <a href="/wiki/Cover_art" title="Cover art">cover art</a> for the fifteenth anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(book_series)" title="Harry Potter (book series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> series</a>. These special editions&#160;were released on&#160;<a href="/wiki/27_August" title="27 August">27 August</a> <a href="/wiki/2013" title="2013">2013</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-amazon_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amazon-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The cover art all of the titles was released in advance online, with that for the final title, <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i> released on <a href="/wiki/31_July" title="31 July">31 July</a> (<a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s birthday), 2013. These special editions retain the original interior illustrations by <a href="/wiki/Mary_GrandPr%C3%A9" title="Mary GrandPré">Mary GrandPré</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-amazonsorc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amazonsorc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Kazu_Kibuishi?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Kazu_Kibuishi?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kazu was born in Tokyo, Japan on <a href="/wiki/April" title="April">April</a> <a href="/wiki/8_April" title="8 April">8</a> <a href="/wiki/1978" title="1978">1978</a>. He and his family moved to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a> in <a href="/wiki/1981" title="1981">1981</a> and grew up near <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He began drawing comics at the age of 5. Despite his passion for drawing, Kazu decided not to attend art school and enrolled at the University of California Santa Barbara, where he graduated with a degree in Film Studies in <a href="/wiki/2000" title="2000">2000</a>. He pursued a career in the animation industry but found his way back to drawing comics. He currently works as a full-time graphic novelist, living in the Pacific Northwest with his wife, Amy Kim Kibuishi, and their children. He works alongside her and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Caffoe" title="Jason Caffoe">Jason Caffoe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
