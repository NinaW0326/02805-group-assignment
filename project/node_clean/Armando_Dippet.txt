<p><b>Professor Armando Dippet</b> (<a href="/wiki/1637" class="mw-redirect" title="1637">1637</a> – <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a><sup id="cite_ref-HPLE-PA_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-PA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>)  was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, and the predecessor of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> in the post. 
</p><p>Dippet served as <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1940s" title="1940s">1940s</a> into the <a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">1960s</a> (and possibly the very early 70s). It was during his tenure that the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> was first opened by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> since its creation by <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a> in around <a href="/wiki/First_millennium_A.D." title="First millennium A.D.">A.D. 993</a>. Unknowingly, Dippet was indirectly responsible for the end of the attacks by telling <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Riddle</a> that the school would have to be <a href="/wiki/Closure_of_Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Closure of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">closed</a> if said attacks continued. Dippet was also responsible for the expulsion of <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> after Riddle framed him with the attacks.
</p><p>Dippet left office between March <a href="/wiki/1965" title="1965">1965</a> and March <a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a><sup id="cite_ref-dumbledoretakesover_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dumbledoretakesover-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and was succeeded by Albus Dumbledore. He was a "nearly bald and a somewhat feeble" man in his elderly years and died in late <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Armando Dippet was born in <a href="/wiki/October" title="October">October</a> <a href="/wiki/1637" class="mw-redirect" title="1637">1637</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Birth_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birth-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and it is likely that he attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. If so, he must have been <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into either <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>, but certainly not <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH16_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH16-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>If he went through his magical education at Hogwarts, he would have graduated at the age of eighteen in 1654. What he did after his graduation is unknown, although Armando Dippet seemed to have been held in high regard in his old age, since he was eventually chosen and offered the job of being Headmaster of Hogwarts, although it is unknown whether he ever took up a teaching post prior to this appointment.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_Headmaster_of_Hogwarts">As Headmaster of Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: As Headmaster of Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: As Headmaster of Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/0e/Dippet_and_friends.png/revision/latest?cb=20110603184608" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/0e/Dippet_and_friends.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20110603184608" decoding="async" width="250" height="91" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Dippet and friends.png" data-image-key="Dippet_and_friends.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Dippet_and_friends.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Dippet speaking with some of his employees</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Professor Dippet became <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">twentieth century</a><sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>, at over 200 years old<sup id="cite_ref-Birth_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birth-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>. In his position, it is possible he was responsible for the employment of <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-HBP5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-CS13_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Beery" title="Herbert Beery">Herbert Beery</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Silvanus_Kettleburn" title="Silvanus Kettleburn">Silvanus Kettleburn</a><sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Galatea_Merrythought" title="Galatea Merrythought">Galatea Merrythought</a><sup id="cite_ref-HBP17_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP17-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> as teachers at Hogwarts. It is also known that he was responsible for hiring <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>, having offered her "<i>a job in the Transfiguration department, under Head of Department, Albus Dumbledore</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-PMmcgonagall_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMmcgonagall-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He may have immediately succeeded Phineas Nigellus Black as Headmaster, after his death in <a href="/wiki/1925" title="1925">1925</a>. If so, Dippet would have sent students home early in the following year and increased the security of Hogwarts in response to the threat from <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>

<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>The night's entertainment concluded with a packed hospital wing...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>During Dippet's tenure as Headmaster, <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Beery" title="Herbert Beery">Herbert Beery</a>, then-Herbology master, proposed an adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">The Fountain of Fair Fortune</a></i> as a Christmas treat for both staff and students. The show, however, was a fiasco: the students <a href="/wiki/Girl_who_played_Amata" title="Girl who played Amata">playing Amata</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boy_who_played_Sir_Luckless" title="Boy who played Sir Luckless">Sir Luckless</a> had been boyfriend and girlfriend until one hour before the show, at which point <a href="/wiki/Boy_who_played_Sir_Luckless" title="Boy who played Sir Luckless">"Sir Luckless"</a> dumped her for <a href="/wiki/Girl_who_played_Asha" title="Girl who played Asha">"Asha"</a>. During the play, the <a href="/wiki/Engorgement_Charm" title="Engorgement Charm">Engorged</a> <a href="/wiki/Ashwinder" title="Ashwinder">Ashwinder</a> portraying the Worm (provided by the reckless Professor <a href="/wiki/Silvanus_Kettleburn" title="Silvanus Kettleburn">Silvanus Kettleburn</a>) exploded in a shower of hot sparks and dust, filling the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a> with smoke and fragments of the scenery. While the Ashwinder's eggs set fire to the floorboards, <a href="/wiki/Girl_who_played_Amata" title="Girl who played Amata">"Amata"</a> and <a href="/wiki/Girl_who_played_Asha" title="Girl who played Asha">"Asha"</a> started <a href="/wiki/Duelling" title="Duelling">duelling</a> fiercely. <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Beery" title="Herbert Beery">Professor Beery</a> was caught in the crossfire and his head assumed unusual proportions. While the staff evacuated the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall" title="Great Hall">Great Hall</a>, the fire raging inside it threatened to engulf the place.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Of course, there were several people sent to the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a> and Professor Dippet had to put Professor Kettleburn in one of his sixty-two periods of <a href="/wiki/Probation" title="Probation">probation</a>. In response to this dramatic fiasco, Professor Dippet imposed a blanket ban on future pantomimes, a tradition that is still followed by the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>' staff and students to this day.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Under <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s request, Hagrid was trained as <a href="/wiki/Keeper_of_Keys_and_Grounds" title="Keeper of Keys and Grounds">Gamekeeper</a> and was allowed to remain at the school.<sup id="cite_ref-CS17_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS17-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_post-mortem">Death and post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death and post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Death and post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">

<p>Professor Dippet's term as headmaster ended between March <a href="/wiki/1965" title="1965">1965</a> and March <a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dumbledoretakesover_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dumbledoretakesover-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His immediate successor in the post was <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Professor Albus Dumbledore</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-CS13_6-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> who had been Dippet's <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_department_(academic_department)" title="Transfiguration department (academic department)">Head of Transfiguration</a>.
</p><p>The prominence of Dippet's tenure as <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> is highlighted by the fact that he even got a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> made in his effigy, due to his work as chief administrator of Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-PAvg_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAvg-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At some point, <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a> published her bestselling biography titled <i><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet:_Master_or_Moron%3F" title="Armando Dippet: Master or Moron?">Armando Dippet: Master or Moron?</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH13_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH13-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> which, given its title and Skeeter's own brand of sensationalist writing (elsewhere described as "<i>one quarter truth to three quarters rubbish</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>), it can be reasonably assumed that it contains false defamatory information, or otherwise facts taken out of context.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1_September" title="1 September">1 September</a> <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a> edition of the <a href="/wiki/Evening_Prophet" title="Evening Prophet">Evening Prophet</a>, he was being suspected of causing an accident only a month before his <a href="/wiki/Ancient_age_flying_test" title="Ancient age flying test">Ancient age flying test</a> on his 355th birthday. Dippet died in late <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DOD_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>After his death,<sup id="cite_ref-DOD_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOD-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> the late Headmaster was allowed to leave behind an imprint of himself in the form of a <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portrait</a> mounted on the wall of the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's office</a> along with other revered Headmasters and Headmistress.<sup id="cite_ref-OP37_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP37-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In doing this, he effectively ensured that he in some capacity could continue to posthumously serve the school by sharing his knowledge with his successors in office as a final service to his beloved school.
</p><p>The portrait he left behind presents him as very dignified and solemn-looking while attired in long, majestic robes of blue and bronze.<sup id="cite_ref-CSf_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSf-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, he scorned the portrait of <a href="/wiki/Phineas_Nigellus_Black" title="Phineas Nigellus Black">Phineas Nigellus Black</a>, who was feigning tiredness in order to avoid going to <a href="/wiki/12_Grimmauld_Place" title="12 Grimmauld Place">12 Grimmauld Place</a> like Dumbledore asked him to. He was devoted to serving whoever was <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP22_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP22-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_description">Physical description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Physical description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Professor Dippet had pale skin, brown eyes and, in his youth, a thick brown beard.<sup id="cite_ref-PAvg_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAvg-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his elderly years, he became frail and feeble-looking. He was balding and had only a few wisps of white hair left.<sup id="cite_ref-CS13_6-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As made evident by his portrait in the Headmaster's Office, he was apparently a very proud man who for the most part held himself with dignity and solemnity at all but the most trying of times, in which case he would, over time, look very feeble and tired after having to cope with such troubles at his age. 
</p><p>Very loyal to Hogwarts, Professor Dippet gave a certain impression of holding the school in higher regard than he did even his staff, as he was very distrusting of others, and Professor Albus Dumbledore, his Head of Transfiguration at during the later parts of Professor Dippet's tenure, noted that he was one of the few people that Professor Dippet confided in. He was also a man of tradition, having trained his portrait to scold anyone who showed lack of respect or loyalty to the school, which was shown when it reprimanded the portrait Phineas Nigellus's for attempting to defy a request from Professor Dumbledore, expressing that they were honour-bound to give advice to the current Headmaster or Headmistress, regardless of their personal feelings, showing that the living Professor Dippet had taught his portrait to share his unyielding desire to serve the school with professional integrity.
</p><p>During his time as Headmaster, Professor Dippet was apparently a disciplinarian. Perhaps somewhat harshly old-fashioned in his methods, the school was marked by an acceptance of severe punishment under his leadership, as he allowed the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Caretaker" title="Hogwarts Caretaker">Caretaker</a> of the day, Mr <a href="/wiki/Apollyon_Pringle" title="Apollyon Pringle">Apollyon Pringle</a>, to continue the traditionally accepted use of corporal punishment on students for misbehaviour or rule-breaking. However, he was not feared by his students, but was seen as revered and very capable in his own right, and ultimately a man dedicated to the protection and preservation of the school and all its inhabitants who also did not take the safety of his students lightly, and his success as head of the school is shown by his depiction on a Famous Witches and Wizards Card solely in homage to his long and faithful service to the school as Headmaster.
</p><p>Professor Dippet was by no means above severely punishing anyone who might pose a potential breach in the security of the castle, be it student or staff: <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Professor Horace Slughorn</a> once commented, perhaps in jest, that Dippet would not hesitate to put him in detention if he found out that he had been <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">encouraging students to be up and about past curfew</a>. He also placed Professor <a href="/wiki/Silvanus_Kettleburn" title="Silvanus Kettleburn">Silvanus Kettleburn</a>, whom he always considered to be quite reckless, on no fewer than sixty-two periods of <a href="/wiki/Probation" title="Probation">probation</a> (including once after the chaos that ensued during a performance of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">The Fountain of Fair Fortune</a></i>, for which Kettleburn provided an <a href="/wiki/Engorgement_Charm" title="Engorgement Charm">Engorged</a> <a href="/wiki/Ashwinder" title="Ashwinder">Ashwinder</a> to play the part of the <a href="/wiki/Gigantic_white_worm" title="Gigantic white worm">Worm</a>), since the catastrophe endangered the students.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His concern for the well-being of his students was shown once more during the 1942-1943 school year, when he, like all his employees, did everything in his power to apprehend the perpetrator and protect the students after the opening of <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>. During that year, it is known that he instructed a student by the name of <a href="/wiki/Olive_Hornby" title="Olive Hornby">Olive Hornby</a> to go look for fellow student <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren" title="Myrtle Warren">Myrtle Warren</a> the moment he realised that no one had seen her for some time.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He also seemed to favour status, at least to some extent. This is demonstrated by the fact that he was fully prepared to believe the word of a Prefect over the word of a younger student, and when Rubeus Hagrid were declared a suspect in the case of who opened the Chamber of Secrets, Dippet promptly expelled him without putting much effort in investigating the matter. However, as the school at the time was dangerously near to being closed down by the Ministry of Magic after the murder on Myrtle, he was likely to have done so on account of having been under a lot of pressure from parents.  
</p><p>As Hagrid was allowed to stay after his expulsion, Professor Dippet might have been a wise, well-meaning if somewhat strict administrator under pressure rather than mercilessly expelling one student on the word of another. The fact that Hagrid's father passed away that year can also have been a factor in Professor Dippet permitting him to remain at Hogwarts, due to sympathising with the young boy's tragic situation and recognised his need for a home since there no longer was anyone to look after him, which proves that Professor Dippet, behind his solemn exterior, did indeed care about the students of Hogwarts, and was capable of looking past whatever wrongdoings one of the pupils was accused of and focus on their needs in times of great trouble and loss.
</p><p>It is known that during his tenure at Hogwarts, Professor Dippet grew rather fond of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> and was perhaps somewhat biased towards him, partly due to sympathising with the boy who had grown up orphaned and friendless and partly due to the young man's charisma. However, this did not blind him from keeping their safety his number one priority, nor did it stop him for keeping up certain standards. This is shown when he rejects the request of a recent graduate to become a teacher on the basis of being too young, as well as rejecting <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>'s request of staying at school over the summer holidays due to the situation while unaware that Riddle was the one behind the danger Dippet wanted to protect him from. However, being fully aware that Riddle despised the orphanage where he was raised, he did comment that he would have given him special permission if the school was more safer at the time.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magical</a> aptitude</b>: As he became the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> of Hogwarts, a highly prestigious and esteemed <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_school" title="Wizarding school">wizarding school</a>, Dippet was therefore a highly accomplished, powerful and experienced <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who would have possessed wide-reaching knowledge of and much proficiency in many different branches of magic.</li>
<li><b>Leadership skills</b>: While he never achieved the level of admiration and reverence of his immediate successor, Professor Dippet was nonetheless a very capable Headmaster of the school, and remembered as such by his tenure having been the theme of his <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Possessions">Possessions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Possessions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Possessions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>Wand</b>: Professor Dippet's wand was of unknown manufacturer, length, core and wood. He was in possession of it during the <a href="/wiki/1940s" title="1940s">1940s</a>. He left it on his desk during his meeting with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> in <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a> concerning the summer holidays and the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> openings.<sup id="cite_ref-CSgbc_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSgbc-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Headmaster's office</b>: When Professor Dippet presided in the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">office</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_Tower" title="Headmaster&#39;s Tower">Headmaster's Tower</a>, there was a single bookcase, a desk, and several paintings there, including the <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">portraits</a> of his <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">predecessors</a>. His passwords are unknown, though it can be presumed that he told one of them to <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> in <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CS13_6-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS13-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Strawberry and ling plantations</b>: According to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, Professor Dippet planted some <a href="/wiki/Strawberry" title="Strawberry">strawberries</a> and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calluna" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Calluna">ling</a> in the castle grounds while he was Headmaster. However, it is likely that Albus was simply trying to distract <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walden_Macnair" title="Walden Macnair">Walden Macnair</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PAf_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAf-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tom_Riddle">Tom Riddle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=15" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Tom Riddle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Tom Riddle"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>I had advised Armando against the appointment — I did not give the reasons I have given you, for Professor Dippet was very fond of Voldemort and convinced of his honesty...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Dippet's fondness of Riddle<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Dippet was very fond of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a>, whom he made several "special arrangements" for. His relationship with him was strong enough to make Dumbledore decide not to tell Dippet about why he thought Riddle should not be given a job at the school.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hogwarts_staff">Hogwarts staff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hogwarts staff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Hogwarts staff"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Professor Dippet was, apparently, distrusting of his employees, confiding only in a few <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professors</a>, which included the <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a> teacher <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP20_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP20-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As headmaster, Dippet placed <a href="/wiki/Silvanus_Kettleburn" title="Silvanus Kettleburn">Silvanus Kettleburn</a> on at least one, but presumably many more, of his sixty-two periods of probation. The one known time was after he provided an <a href="/wiki/Ashwinder" title="Ashwinder">Ashwinder</a> with an <a href="/wiki/Engorgement_Charm" title="Engorgement Charm">Engorgement Charm</a> on it instead of a worm for the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">The Fountain of Fair Fortune</a></i> pantomime. Dippet, after the chaos, placed a permanent ban on pantomimes from there on.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Another of Dippet's employees was <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Beery" title="Herbert Beery">Herbert Beery</a>, who was the one who adapted <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">The Fountain of Fair Fortune</a></i> into a play. Beery, who had been teaching <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a>, eventually left to become a <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_Academy_of_Dramatic_Arts" title="Wizarding Academy of Dramatic Arts">Wizarding Academy of Dramatic Arts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_10-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> also worked under Headmaster Dippet. One evening, during one of his <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club" title="Slug Club">Slug Club</a>'s supper parties, Slughorn joked that if the student group would be found up and about after hours, Professor Dippet would put them all in detention, including himself.<sup id="cite_ref-HBPf_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBPf-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Armando_Dippet?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"<i>Armando</i>" is the Spanish and Italian variant of the name Armand, meaning "of the army". It is likely that, given his name, Armando Dippet is at least partially of Spanish or Italian heritage.</li>
<li>"<i>Dippet</i>" is not a word in English, but "dip" has many meanings, including: to put something into something else, often a liquid; to take something out of something, as in dipping out <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>; to lower and raise something; a fool.</li></ul>
