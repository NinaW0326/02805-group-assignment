<b>George III</b> (full name <b>George William Frederick</b>; <a href="/wiki/4_June" title="4 June">4 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1738" class="mw-redirect" title="1738">1738</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/29_January" title="29 January">29 January</a>, <a href="/wiki/1820" class="mw-redirect" title="1820">1820</a>), was <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">King</a> of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> from <a href="/wiki/25_October" title="25 October">25 October</a>, <a href="/wiki/1760" class="mw-redirect" title="1760">1760</a> to <a href="/wiki/29_January" title="29 January">29 January</a>, <a href="/wiki/1820" class="mw-redirect" title="1820">1820</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He ruled the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">United Kingdom</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_War" title="Revolutionary War">Revolutionary War</a>, a notable military conflict between the non-magical peoples of Britain and (what would eventually become) the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States</a>; neither wizarding government (<a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_Crowdy" title="Maximilian Crowdy">Minister Crowdy</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">MACUSA</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_McGilliguddy" title="Elizabeth McGilliguddy">President McGilliguddy</a>) officially intervened.
</p><p>King George suffered from <a href="/wiki/Insanity" title="Insanity">mental illness</a> in his later years.<sup id="cite_ref-wikipedia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikipedia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pm-ministers_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pm-ministers-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/1782" class="mw-redirect" title="1782">1782</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> at the time, <a href="/wiki/Lord_North" title="Lord North">Lord North</a>, confidentially reached out to then-<a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Porteus_Knatchbull" title="Porteus Knatchbull">Porteus Knatchbull</a> to see if he could help with the king's condition.<sup id="cite_ref-pm-ministers_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pm-ministers-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, this resulted in a rumour spreading that Lord North believed in <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a>, triggering a motion of no confidence that forced his resignation.<sup id="cite_ref-pm-ministers_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pm-ministers-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
