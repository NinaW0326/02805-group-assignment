<b>Roxanne Weasley</b> (b. <a href="/wiki/2000s" title="2000s">2000s</a>) is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Weasley</a> (née Johnson). She has an older brother, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley_II" title="Fred Weasley II">Fred</a>, who was named after her <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">late uncle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WFT_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WFT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She may have shared her parents' love of <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>, playing as either a <a href="/wiki/Beater" title="Beater">Beater</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Roxanne_Weasley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Roxanne_Weasley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roxanne" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Roxanne">Roxanne</a> is an English spelling of the French name <i>Roxane</i>, which in turn comes from the Greek name <i>Roxana</i>, derived from the Persian <i>Roshanak</i>, meaning "bright" or "dawn". Roxana was the name of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>'s first wife.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Also, Roxanne is the name of a main character in Edmond Rostand's play <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyrano_de_Bergerac" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Cyrano de Bergerac">Cyrano de Bergerac</a></i>, who is the love interest of the eponymous hero.
</p>
