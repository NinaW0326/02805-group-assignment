<b>Stewart</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">student</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a> he became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a> and was ranked sixth on the Club's top-ten duellists list.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Stewart?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Stewart?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Stewart" comes from an occupational surname originally belonging to a person who was a steward. It is ultimately derived from Old English stig "house" and weard "guard". As a given name, it arose in 19th-century <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> in honour of the Stuart royal family, which produced several kings and queens of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a> between the 14th and 18th centuries.
</p>
