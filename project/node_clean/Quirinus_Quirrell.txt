<p><b>Professor Quirinus Quirrell</b> (<a href="/wiki/26_September" title="26 September">26 September</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/1967" title="1967">1967</a> or earlier<sup id="cite_ref-DOB_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DOB-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> – <a href="/wiki/4_June" title="4 June">4 June</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and was Sorted into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was the <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> at Hogwarts, though he later became the <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%931992_school_year" title="1991–1992 school year">1991–1992 school year</a>.
</p><p>Before teaching <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, Quirrell went out in search of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>, believing he could achieve recognition for finding him and could learn things that would ensure that no one laughed at him again. However, Voldemort, after finding out that he taught at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, took over his body, though Quirrell put up some feeble resistance. He died in vain while trying to murder <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> at <a href="/wiki/Underground_Chambers" title="Underground Chambers">underground Chambers</a>.
</p>

<p>Quirinus Quirrell was born presumably before <a href="/wiki/26_September" title="26 September">26 September</a>, <a href="/wiki/1967" title="1967">1967</a>, somewhere in the British Isles,to a magical and a Muggle parent.<sup id="cite_ref-HM2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hogwarts_Years">Hogwarts Years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hogwarts Years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hogwarts Years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Quirinus attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, where he was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is likely that he acquired <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell%27s_wand" title="Quirinus Quirrell&#39;s wand">his wand</a> from <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders</a> when he was eleven.
</p><p>As a boy, Quirinus was gifted but also delicate, teased for his timidity and nerves during his school life. Feeling inadequate and wishing to prove himself, he developed an (initially theoretical) interest in the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>. The feelings of insignificance and worthlessness that this bullying fostered embittered the boy, forging a latent desire to make the world sit up and notice him.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> was a professor during his this time and was very kind and helpful to the young Quirrell, encouraging him to value knowledge. He was very grateful towards her and admired her bravery, wisdom, thirst for knowledge and kindness.

<p>After teaching <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a> for some time, he took a year-long sabbatical in <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a> in order to "gain first-hand experience",<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> though in reality he had taken a 'Grand Tour' around the world in order to hopefully find whatever remained of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> after his first defeat, partly out of curiosity, partly out of that unacknowledged desire for importance from his childhood. His journey proved successful as he did indeed find Voldemort, though he was no match for the Dark Lord (even in such a weakened state) and when he realised that Quirinus had a position at Hogwarts, Voldemort latched onto Quirrell’s body and went back to Hogwarts with him.

<p>Quirinus Quirrell was a young man with a pale white skin.<sup id="cite_ref-skin_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skin-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Quirrell returned from his sabbatical in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, one of his eyes developed a twitch and he appeared quite pale and anxious and frequently stuttered. At the start of the term, he was wearing a purple turban.<sup id="cite_ref-PS7_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS7-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He was usually trembling and prone to fainting.<sup id="cite_ref-PS10_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Near the end of his life, his reason for wearing the turban became clear. Quirrell had joined with Voldemort and agreed to allow Voldemort's fledgling body to latch on to the back of his head. It was only able to grow into a face, and used Quirrell for transportation and sustenance.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>He didn't realise that love as powerful as your mother's for you leaves its own mark... to have been loved so deeply, even though the person who loved us is gone, will give us some protection forever. It is in your very skin. Quirrell, full of hatred, greed, and ambition, sharing his soul with Voldemort, could not touch you for this reason. It was agony to touch a person marked by something so good.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> explains Quirrell's demise<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>In his youth, Quirrell was gifted, but emotionally delicate. He became interested in travelling as well as pressing wild flowers, stemming from his early enthusiasm in academia, and continued to do so in his adult years. By his own admittance, he was "full of ridiculous ideas about good and evil". As a young boy, he was teased and neglected for his timid nature, and came to develop a latent desire for recognition and greatness, cynical feeling for society, and was seduced by the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>, no matter how subtle the lure appeared at first.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This led him to foolishly search out for Voldemort's remains in the hopes of fame and knowledge, while exposing his impressionable and naive side to the fallen Dark Lord. Quirinus' journey to capture Voldemort showcases his own overestimation of his own skills, possibly a slight arrogance, as Voldemort easily overpowered him. When Quirrell met Voldemort, he arrived at the conclusion that "there is no good and evil, there is only power, and those too weak to seek it". Meeting Voldemort made Quirrell admit himself as a "foolish young man [he] was then", apparently looking down on his past self, showing his gullibility, and weak will, and was easily corrupted. Quirrell generally did his best to serve Voldemort, though he admitted that his attempts were not always successful, seeing as he was being given rather tall orders. Dumbledore considered Quirrell to be greedy, hateful and ambitious, a reason why Quirrell could not understand the powerful magic of <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a>, which was what heavily injured him during the battle with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>. Despite Quirrell's continuous descent into corruption, he did not completely submit his existence to Voldemort as he attempted to resist the Dark Lord's soul of possessing his body, feeble and futile though it may have been.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Throughout the school year, Quirrell appeared nervous and timid. He frequently stuttered and was mostly an incompetent teacher. Though his nervousness was probably very real due to the enormous pressure being put upon him by Voldemort, Quirrell had a chillingly sinister side that most people never saw. He was extremely adept at conniving and manipulation; at one point, he disguised himself and tricked Hagrid into revealing crucial information on how to get past one of the obstacles protecting the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>. Another time he let a fully-grown <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Troll" class="mw-redirect" title="Mountain Troll">Mountain Troll</a> into the school on <a href="/wiki/Hallowe%27en" title="Hallowe&#39;en">Hallowe'en</a> as a diversion while he headed off to the third-floor corridor and tried to penetrate its defences.<sup id="cite_ref-PS10_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He even managed to deceive the great <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> by forging a <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a> letter to him to lure him away from school, although Dumbledore was quick to realise that Quirrell had fooled him and returned to Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Presumably because of his constant failures, Quirrell eventually became obsessed with pleasing Voldemort and whenever he looked into the Mirror of Erised he would see himself with the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>, the object Voldemort desired above all else. Quirrell became very desperate, being willing to slay <a href="/wiki/Unicorn" title="Unicorn">unicorns</a> in order to drink their <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_blood" title="Unicorn blood">blood</a> to keep himself alive, a crime that is noted by <a href="/wiki/Firenze" title="Firenze">Firenze</a> only those who have nothing to lose and everything to gain would commit.<sup id="cite_ref-PS15_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS15-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">"<i>As it was, I saw only greedy and unworthy Quirrell attempting to steal the stone and, I admit, I did all I could to thwart him.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Snape's scathing opinion of Quirrell<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Many people considered Quirrell to be foolish, avaricious, worthless, and tended to look down on him. <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> confided to <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> that he considered Quirrell as a mediocre wizard who was greedy and unworthy;<sup id="cite_ref-HBP2_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP2-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> many students saw his lessons to be a bit of a joke undeserving of respect;<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and Voldemort deemed Quirrell as a foolish and gullible man who he only allied with for his position.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF33_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF33-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Even Dumbledore himself, who had retained a level of pity and compassion even for his <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">much more incompetent successor</a>, openly acknowledged that Quirell's fate was well-deserved due to his greed, hatred and ambitions without any care.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Quirrell was known to have had an excellent aptitude for theoretical learning, particularly when it came to Defensive Magic.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Even so, as Professor <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">McGonagall</a> later pointed out that teachers of Hogwarts generally speaking were rather good at <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, it can be assumed that he also possessed a considerable amount of magical ability.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Intellectual giftedness</b>: Quirrell was known while still a student for being highly intelligent, possessing a brilliant intellectual mind.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His intelligence was great enough to locate Voldemort, which the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> had failed to do. He was also intelligent and cunning enough to successfully forge a convincing <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell%27s_fake_letter_to_Albus_Dumbledore" title="Quirinus Quirrell&#39;s fake letter to Albus Dumbledore">letter</a> to <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>, supposedly being from the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry</a>, in order to deceive him and send him away from the school.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Whilst the true extent of Quirrell's expertise in this area is unknown,  he was able to conjure a non-corporeal <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Patronus_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patronus-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> a very advanced piece of magic, which means he must have possessed a considerable amount of ability in charms. He also was able to skilfully perform and thoroughly teach on how to cast many other charms such as the <a href="/wiki/Verdimillious_Charm" title="Verdimillious Charm">Verdimillious Charm</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wand-Lighting_Charm" title="Wand-Lighting Charm">Wand-Lighting Charm</a>,, giving his students in his <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> classes full understanding on how to use them effectively. He was also capable of casting the <i><a href="/wiki/Deprimo" title="Deprimo">Deprimo</a></i> charm, which is capable of collapsing an entire room. Furthermore, Quirrell was capable of <a href="/wiki/Piertotum_Locomotor" title="Piertotum Locomotor">animating</a> objects, such as a gargoyle statue and several suits of armour, and conjuring a barrier around himself.<sup id="cite_ref-PSvg_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSvg-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a></b>: Quirrell had at least a reasonable amount of knowledge and ability in dark magic, and could adequately use it and thoroughly explain the function of <a href="/wiki/Jinx" title="Jinx">jinxes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hex" title="Hex">hexes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curse" title="Curse">curses</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Knockback_Jinx" title="Knockback Jinx">Knockback Jinx</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curse_of_the_Bogies" title="Curse of the Bogies">Curse of the Bogies</a>, teaching his first-years the knowledge of how to perform both spells effectively.<sup id="cite_ref-PSvg_16-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSvg-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  He was able to put a <a href="/wiki/Broom_jinx" title="Broom jinx">jinx</a> Harry Potter's Nimbus 2000 to go out of control in an attempt to kill him, something that Hagrid noted required considerable skill in the Dark Arts.<sup id="cite_ref-PS11_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS11-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He nearly succeeded - even Severus Snape was unable to <a href="/wiki/Counter-curse" title="Counter-curse">counter</a> the jinx completely - and was only thwarted when Hermione Granger indirectly made him lose focus. He was also capable of locking the exit to the Dungeons with four set of <a href="/wiki/Floating_eye_curse" title="Floating eye curse">curses</a>, making so that the only way to exit the Dungeons were to undo all the curses with a <a href="/wiki/Curse-dependent_locking_spell" title="Curse-dependent locking spell">curse-dependent locking spell</a>. He also possessed considerable skill in the handling and presumably also the understanding of dark magical artefacts and cursed objects.<sup id="cite_ref-legohp14_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-legohp14-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He also displayed the ability to <a href="/wiki/Homing_spell" title="Homing spell">shoot balls</a> of destructive green energy, and cast an unknown destructive curse that he charged in his wand before unleashing it.<sup id="cite_ref-PSvg_16-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSvg-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<dd>&#8212;Quirrell's impressive use of non-verbal and wandless conjuration<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a></b>: Quirrell was apparently proficient in this notably complex and <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">scientific</a> branch of magic, as he displayed a rather high amount of skill in <a href="/wiki/Conjuration" title="Conjuration">conjuration</a>, a highly difficult and complex branch of transfiguration. He could <a href="/wiki/Incarcerous_Spell" title="Incarcerous Spell">conjure ropes</a>, both <a href="/wiki/Nonverbal_spell" title="Nonverbal spell">non-verbally</a> and without his wand.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He could also cast <a href="/wiki/Gytrash-conjuring_spell" title="Gytrash-conjuring spell">a spell which conjured green Gytrashes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSvg_16-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSvg-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a></b>: Quirrell was noted to be particularly gifted in the theory of Defensive Magic and would gain an extensive amount of knowledge and experience on how to defend from the Dark Arts, some of which were attributed from his journeys,<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to the point where the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> considered him to have been a good teacher.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP15_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP15-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The thruth of this was exemplified by his ability to effectively teach his first year classes how to use the <a href="/wiki/Defensive_Charm" title="Defensive Charm">Defensive Charms</a> like the <a href="/wiki/Verdimillious_Charm" title="Verdimillious Charm">Verdimillious Charm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wand-Lighting_Charm" title="Wand-Lighting Charm">Wand-Lighting Charm</a> to defend from the Dark Forces. He also taught the <a href="/wiki/Curse_of_the_Bogies" title="Curse of the Bogies">Curse of the Bogies</a><sup id="cite_ref-PS9_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS9-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and could quickly deduce that Harry Potter was under the aforementioned curse's influence immediately upon seeing him entering his class coughing and he also was able to thoroughly explain to his class how to use the <a href="/wiki/Knockback_Jinx" title="Knockback Jinx">Knockback Jinx</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSvg_16-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSvg-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Despite his expertise, however, his nervous nature and constant stutter led many of his students to view his classes as a bit of a joke, giving him very little respect in return.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">Wandless magic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Non-verbal_magic" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-verbal magic">Non-verbal magic</a></b>: Quirrell was capable of effectively performing intricate magic <a href="/wiki/Non-verbal_magic" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-verbal magic">nonverbally</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">wandlessly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Incarcerous_Spell" title="Incarcerous Spell">conjuring ropes</a> <a href="/wiki/Wandless_magic" title="Wandless magic">without a wand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He could also <a href="/wiki/Spell_to_conjure_a_circle_of_fire" title="Spell to conjure a circle of fire">conjure fire</a> just by raising his hands.<sup id="cite_ref-PSF_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSF-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Magizoology" title="Magizoology">Magizoology</a></b>: He was known to be experienced and capable in handling dangerous magical <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creatures</a> from his travels, but had a particular uncommon gift with <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">trolls</a>. He was able to bring <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell%27s_first_mountain_troll" title="Quirinus Quirrell&#39;s first mountain troll">a fully grown mountain troll</a> into Hogwarts Castle on <a href="/wiki/Hallowe%27en" title="Hallowe&#39;en">Hallowe'en</a>, as well as easily subdue the notably bigger and presumably more ferocious <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell%27s_second_mountain_troll" title="Quirinus Quirrell&#39;s second mountain troll">mountain troll</a> that guarded the Philosopher's Stone.<sup id="cite_ref-PS10_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS10-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was also able to acquire a <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Ridgeback" title="Norwegian Ridgeback">Norwegian Ridgeback</a> <a href="/wiki/Dragon_egg" title="Dragon egg">dragon egg</a> to give to Hagrid,<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> implying he was sufficiently familiar with both the illegal trafficking of magical beasts and the local black market scene to know how to acquire <a href="/wiki/Non-Tradeable_Material" title="Non-Tradeable Material">Non-Tradeable Material</a> without discovery. Furthermore, he would have been very knowledgeable in understanding and handling dark creatures, considering his profession as the Defence Against the Dark Arts instructor, and was at least able to explain to Harry Potter how to deal with <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">Ghosts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gytrash" title="Gytrash">Gytrashes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Imp" title="Imp">Imps</a>, for example.<sup id="cite_ref-PSvg_16-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSvg-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He was also known to have an interest in <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">Vampires</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PS8_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> meaning he would have had a very good understanding of them as well, and proved capable of roaming the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_Forest" title="Forbidden Forest">Forbidden Forest</a> in order to consume <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_blood" title="Unicorn blood">unicorn blood</a> as means of strengthening Lord Voldemort without being mauled to death by the dark, dangerous creatures that dwelled therein, suggesting that he was more than able to keep such beasts at bay. When <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> intercepted Quirrell on the third floor the first time he tried to break into the chambers guarding the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>, they saw themselves confronted by <a href="/wiki/Fluffy" title="Fluffy">Fluffy</a>, and while Snape was injured, Quirrell emerged from the situation unschathed.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a></b>: Quirinus had considerable knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a>, Muggle technology and society considering that he was a professor of <a href="/wiki/Muggle_Studies" title="Muggle Studies">Muggle Studies</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>; he would have achieved a high score on his <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.</a> exam.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a></b>: Quirrell clearly had some interest and skill in Herbology and with magical and mundane plants, going as far as having a liking for pressing wild flowers in his free time.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Indeed, he was capable of passing through the <a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Snare" title="Devil&#39;s Snare">Devil's Snare</a> in the Underground Chambers presumably with relative ease, implying he was rather knowledgeable in this field.</li>
<li><b>Acting skills</b>: Despite his timid and nervous nature, Quirrell was also a talented actor as he was able to hide his true nature from most of the Hogwarts staff members and students, with the notable exceptions of Albus Dumbledore and Severus Snape, enough so that even Harry Potter and his friends suspected Snape instead of him, and only revealed his true nature and intentions to Harry at the very end. Additionally, even though Snape saw him for what he really was, Quirrell did not openly show his dark nature to Snape when he was threatened by him, keeping the facade of a nervous, helpless man in order to fool the other Hogwarts residents.</li></ul>

<p>Quirinus Quirrell first met <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, in the forests of <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, where Voldemort subjugated Quirrell into servitude. Quirrell brought him back to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>, and eventually to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>. The Dark Lord planned to obtain the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a> and fabricate the <a href="/wiki/Elixir_of_Life" title="Elixir of Life">Elixir of Life</a> to acquire a body.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF33_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF33-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Quirrell attempted to infiltrate <a href="/wiki/Vault_713" title="Vault 713">Vault 713</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank" title="Gringotts Wizarding Bank">Gringotts Wizarding Bank</a>, in order to steal the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>. Quirrell managed to break in, but found the vault had been emptied by <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> earlier that day.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Following this failed attempt, Voldemort began a parasitic relationship with Quirrell, in which he attached himself to the back of Quirrell's head, to ensure his plans were being followed precisely; throughout the year, Quirrell attempted to resist the possession, feeble and futile though his efforts were. Voldemort ordered Quirrell to kill <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, though Quirrell's attempts to do so were thwarted by <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>. Harry penetrated the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone_Chambers" class="mw-redirect" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone Chambers">Philosopher's Stone Chambers</a> in pursuit of <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Snape</a>, or so he believed, only to find it was Quirrell in front of the <a href="/wiki/Mirror_of_Erised" title="Mirror of Erised">Mirror of Erised</a>. Quirrell, under Voldemort's orders, demanded Harry draw the Stone from the Mirror; though Harry was successfully able to retrieve the stone, he attempted to conceal the fact by lying about what he saw in the mirror. Quirrell revealed Voldemort, who tried to manipulate Harry into handing over the Stone by offering him the chance to see his parents, and upon Harry's refusal, ordered Quirrell to take the stone by force. Quirrell, sharing his body with Voldemort, found himself unable to touch "a person marked by something so good" (referring to <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Potter</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sacrificial_protection" title="Sacrificial protection">sacrificial protection</a>). Voldemort separated his <a href="/wiki/Soul" title="Soul">soul</a> from Quirrell's body to escape the pain, and left Quirrell to die, as <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> noted, showing him as little mercy as he did his enemies.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Three years later, Voldemort told his <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> that Quirrell was a fool, and that he only allied with him for his position.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF33_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF33-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> It should be noted that despite his fear of Voldemort and that he was not a Death Eater, Quirrell was one of Voldemort's only followers who referred to him by name,<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the others being <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Bartemius Crouch Junior</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>.
</p>

<p>Quirrell first met Harry Potter in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>. Quirrell greeted Harry in the same way most of the wizarding community did, seeming thrilled to meet "The Boy who Lived". Though Harry found Quirrell's twitchy behaviour odd, he was generally pleased to meet Quirrell, as a friend of Hagrid's and as his soon-to-be <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> professor at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Harry eventually came to believe that <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> was attempting to threaten Quirrell into helping him penetrate the defences surrounding the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a>, for Voldemort. As it seemed like the only thing standing between Snape and a means of restoring Voldemort to power was Quirrell's will, Harry did what he could to encourage Quirrell and help build his confidence.<sup id="cite_ref-PS14_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS14-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As it turned out, Quirrell was a servant of Voldemort, and it was he, not Snape, who was aiming to steal the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a> for the Dark Lord, and, if possible, kill Harry along the way. When they met for the last time in the final of the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a> Chambers, Quirrell's connection to Voldemort rendered him unable to touch Harry without experiencing unbearable pain, due to the enduring power of <a href="/wiki/Lily_Evans" class="mw-redirect" title="Lily Evans">Lily Potter's</a> <a href="/wiki/Sacrificial_protection" title="Sacrificial protection">sacrificial protection</a>, and he died attempting to steal the stone from Harry by force.<sup id="cite_ref-PS17_4-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>About four years after this, when Dolores Umbridge commented positively on Quirrell's teaching methods, Harry sarcastically agreed before pointing out the deceased professor's duplicity.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP15_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP15-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> A year after that, when Snape finally took over as the Defence teacher, Harry made a snide comment of Quirrell dying on the job, and hoped that Snape would also die.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP8_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP8-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>With the exception of Severus Snape, Quirrell generally got along well with most of the faculty at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Filius Flitwick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pomona_Sprout" title="Pomona Sprout">Pomona Sprout</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a>. Hagrid seemed to think highly of Quirrell, and considered him to be a very clever and talented <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> for his extensive world travels and experience fighting the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS5_6-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, as Quirrell didn't show his true personality, it is possible he did not like all of them, and it is possible they were all disgusted by Quirrell's actions once his true nature was revealed. Quirrell additionally took advantage of Hagrid's love for magical creatures through getting him drunk and giving him an illegal Dragon egg so that he revealed the secret of getting past Fluffy. Hagrid afterwards was horrified when he learnt of Quirrell's true nature and the fact that he manipulated him for his own ends, calling him an "evil git" when he visited Harry in the Hospital Wing after Quirrell's death. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell?veaction=edit&amp;section=23" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell?action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Quirinus" is possibly derived from the Sabine word <i>quiris</i> meaning "spear". Quirinus was a Sabine and a Roman god who was later identified with Mars. The name was also borne by several early saints.
</p><p>Quirinus is also used as an epithet for the Roman god Janus, a god with two faces similar to Quirrell after he was possessed by Voldemort. This could be a clue to the malevolence of his character. Janus was the god of beginnings, and Quirrell was the first one to find Voldemort in Albania, thereby beginning the Dark Lord's return to power, in a way, and was the first Dark Wizard that Harry ever directly confronted.
</p><p>'Quirrell', which is so nearly 'squirrel' — small, cute and harmless — also suggested 'quiver', is a nod to the character's innate nervousness.

<ul><li>Quirinus Quirrell was played by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Hart" title="Ian Hart">Ian Hart</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>. Archive footage of Quirrell from <i>Philosopher's Stone</i> appears in the "Prince's Tale" sequence of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/David_Thewlis" title="David Thewlis">David Thewlis</a>, who went on to play <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Professor Lupin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">film adaptations</a>, was at one point considered for the role of Professor Quirrell.</li>
<li>Quirinus Quirrell served as a temporary <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a> when <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s soul possessed his body in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a>. However, the piece of the soul survived Quirrell's demise, and was destroyed six years later when Voldemort was killed.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>To make Quirrell's turban not seem out-of-place, many of the actors who played wizards in the Leaky Cauldron were told to wear turbans.</li>
<li>Quirrell's story about encountering the prince and being gifted with the turban is an early hint that he is not what he makes himself out to be.
<ul><li>It is remarked in the story that the turban smells foul, and most presume it is to protect Quirrell from a vampire he encountered in the Black Forest. It is later revealed that Voldemort was possessing Quirrell, and the smell was possibly owing to him.</li>
<li>Ironically, Quirrell's story about a vampire is in some respect true, as Voldemort existed in a state between life and death, and himself admitted that he was living parasitically off of Quirrell and forced him to do parasitic actions like drinking the blood of the unicorns in the Forbidden Forrest to keep him alive, likewise to how a vampire survives by drinking the blood of living beings.</li></ul></li>
<li>He is similar to <a href="https://villains.fandom.com/wiki/Mr_Helliwell" class="extiw" title="w:c:villains:Mr Helliwell">Mr Helliwell</a> from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_Horowitz" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Anthony Horowitz">Anthony Horowitz's</a> <i>Return to Groosham Grange</i>. Mr Helliwell and Quirrell are both dark sorcerers, both are skilled in advanced magic, both are traitors to the school and are conspiring to overthrow their schools, both are charismatic and have a corrupting influence they use to manipulate the protagonists and corrupt them, both seek a prized item - the Unholy Grail and the Philosopher's Stone, respectively - and both have their treachery revealed when it is almost too late.
<ul><li>Anthony Horowitz wrote <i>Groosham Grange</i> before Harry Potter, and Rowling admitted the use of a train in Groosham Grange to reach the school was one of her influences.</li></ul></li>
<li>On the student list shown by <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_Me" title="Harry Potter and Me">Harry Potter and Me</a></i>, a student named "Quirrell" was listed, but the name was crossed out with "<a href="/wiki/Oliver_Rivers" title="Oliver Rivers">Rivers</a>" written above it.</li>
<li>Quirrell was the first known Ravenclaw introduced in the series, though his house was only revealed on Pottermore.</li>
<li>He is also the first Harry Potter character to be directly involved with Voldemort. Snape, who also appeared in the first book, is not at that point revealed as a former <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>.</li>
<li>Given that he seemed familiar with the rivalry between <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a> it's possible that he was at Hogwarts at the same time as them, though it's unconfirmed.</li>
<li>Had Quirrell succeeded in restoring Voldemort to power, he likely would have been made an official <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a>.</li>
<li>In the novel of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Quirrell does not appear to have his turban until after his failed attempt to steal the <a href="/wiki/Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Philosopher's Stone</a> from <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank" title="Gringotts Wizarding Bank">Gringotts</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (film)">film</a> however, Quirrell has his turban before the robbery. This change is indicated by the fact that he shakes Harry's hand at the <a href="/wiki/Leaky_Cauldron" title="Leaky Cauldron">Leaky Cauldron</a> in the novel, but does not wish to in the film. However, not wanting to shake Harry's hand cannot be due to Voldemort sharing Quirrell's body, because as Quirrell dies, he does not understand what is happening to him (he yells, "What <i>is</i> this magic?!?").</li>
<li>In the film, Quirrell and Harry combated for possession of the stone. Quirrell is defeated by Harry upon physical touch, which burns him and allows Harry to break the chokehold. Harry cuts off his access to the stone and touches him, forcing him back, and he disintegrates, crumbling to ash and dust at Harry's feet, dead, and becoming Harry's first kill and victory. Voldemort dispossesses his body immediately afterwards and his vengeful disembodied soul lunges at Harry, leaving him unconscious. In the book, however, even though his death is not shown, it is implied by Voldemort in the fourth book that he died rather because Voldemort left his body, as opposed to Harry's love burning him.</li>
<li>Quirrell is notable as being one of few <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> capable of performing intricate magic, like Dumbledore, without a wand; he can fly without a wand, conjure ropes by snapping his fingers, etc. It is possible that he had this ability because of Voldemort's possession of him, although Hagrid's high opinion of Quirrell suggests that Quirrell was a talented wizard before Voldemort's possession.</li>
<li>Quirrell is unique among Voldemort's followers as one of the few who use Voldemort's name. While the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> call Voldemort "The Dark Lord" when referring to him (with the exceptions of <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">Bartemius Crouch Junior</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>), Quirrell openly uses Voldemort's name when talking to Harry.</li>
<li>Quirrell is a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i>. Despite dying in the first year, he can still be played in the other years and is seen at the end of Year 1, armless and heavily bandaged in the hospital wing. He is capable of using <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Magic</a> in the game. It is also possible to play both professor Quirrell in Voldemort form and his normal form with turban. However, he can only use the <a href="/wiki/Unforgivable_Curses" title="Unforgivable Curses">Unforgivable Curses</a> when he is in his Voldemort form. Quirrell's character can also be found walking around Diagon Alley even after finishing the Philosopher's Stone, along with other wizards, despite canonically being dead.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (video game)">PC version</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, Voldemort twists Quirrell's head 180 degrees to face Harry. Quirrell can also <a href="/wiki/Homing_spell" title="Homing spell">shoot green fireballs</a> and create a <a href="/wiki/Ghostly_head_spell" title="Ghostly head spell">ghostly green head</a> that can hurt Harry.</li>
<li>In all versions of <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i> and some of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone (video game)">video game adaptations</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i>, <i>Quirrell</i> is misspelled <i>Quirrel</i>.</li>
<li>Quirinus Quirrell was listed on the  <i><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">Pottermore</a></i> Feature "Top 5 Death Eaters you'll never forget." This is, to date, the only source identifying Quirrell as a Death Eater.</li></ul>
