<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Myrtle was a witch born circa late <a href="/wiki/1928" title="1928">1928</a> to early <a href="/wiki/1929" title="1929">1929</a><sup id="cite_ref-age_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-age-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> <a href="/wiki/Myrtle%27s_mother" class="mw-redirect" title="Myrtle&#39;s mother">mother</a> and <a href="/wiki/Myrtle%27s_father" class="mw-redirect" title="Myrtle&#39;s father">father</a>, making her a <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COS13_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS13-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hogwarts_years">Hogwarts years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Hogwarts years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hogwarts years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She started her education at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> in <a href="/wiki/1939" title="1939">1939</a> or <a href="/wiki/1940" title="1940">1940</a><sup id="cite_ref-age_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-age-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> When she arrived at Hogwarts she was <a href="/wiki/Sorting_ceremony" title="Sorting ceremony">Sorted</a> into the house of <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-house_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-house-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> While at Hogwarts she was unable to make any friends and was constantly teased and bullied because of this and her physical appearance, which included glasses and acne.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a>, sometime into Myrtle's education, the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> was opened for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-COS13_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS13-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Chamber of Secrets was a legendary chamber built by <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a> and inside was a beast that only his <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">heir</a> could control.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It was believed that <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor" title="Gryffindor">Gryffindor</a> student, <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> had opened the Chamber because he was caught raising <a href="/wiki/Aragog" title="Aragog">Aragog</a> (an <a href="/wiki/Acromantula" title="Acromantula">Acromantula</a>), but it was actually <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Marvolo Riddle</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Slytherin" title="Slytherin">Slytherin</a> student. <p>On her final day of life, <a href="/wiki/13_June" title="13 June">13 June</a> <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a>, Myrtle was teased by student <a href="/wiki/Olive_Hornby" title="Olive Hornby">Olive Hornby</a>. She had made fun of Myrtle's <a href="/wiki/Glasses" title="Glasses">glasses</a> and, as a result, left Myrtle sobbing. Myrtle then ran into one of the bathroom's stalls and started crying. Shortly after Myrtle had entered the bathroom, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Riddle</a> entered and started speaking in <a href="/wiki/Parseltongue" title="Parseltongue">Parseltongue</a> in order to open the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a>, the entrance of which was hidden behind the sink in front of the very stall occupied by Myrtle at the time. The <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_basilisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s basilisk">Monster of Slytherin</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Basilisk" title="Basilisk">basilisk</a>) then emerged from the entrance to the Chamber of Secrets. The monster had been using the pipes as a means of transportation, as to not be seen and therefore never being caught.<sup id="cite_ref-COS16_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS16-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>

Since looking into a Basilisk's eyes is a fatal act, Myrtle was killed instantly and her body fell to the bathroom floor, becoming Tom's first victim.<sup id="cite_ref-COS16_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS16-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Tom (later known as Lord Voldemort) used her murder to make his first <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle%27s_diary" class="mw-redirect" title="Tom Riddle&#39;s diary">Diary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP23_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP23-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Myrtle's body was later found by Hornby, something that Hornby would not soon forget.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF25_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF25-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>

<p>During the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%931995_school_year" title="1994â€“1995 school year">1994â€“1995 school year</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> hosted the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. The secret was that the noise became speech when heard underwater. One champion, <a href="/wiki/Cedric_Diggory" title="Cedric Diggory">Cedric Diggory</a>, decided to meditate upon the clue in the <a href="/wiki/Prefects%27_bathroom" class="mw-redirect" title="Prefects&#39; bathroom">prefects' bathroom</a>. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren?veaction=edit&amp;section=24" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren?action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The myrtle (<i>Myrtus</i>) is a type of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/evergreen" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:evergreen">evergreen</a> <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/shrub" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:shrub">shrub</a> that is often overlooked because of its plainness, native to southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. In Greek mythology, it was sacred to Aphrodite, goddess of <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> and beauty, and to Demeter, goddess of grain and fertility; Pausanias relates it to the tragedy of Adonis. The Roman poet Virgil relates the myrtle to Venus and used them in wedding rituals, something that Jewish liturgy, which deemed it sacred, also used. Myrtle has also uses in Wiccan rituals, and was considered medicinal by the ancients, including Hippocrates, Pliny, Galen and Arabian writers.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Myrtle</i> is a variety of the colour <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/green" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:green">green</a>. It is immoderately dark, slightly more so than the colour <a href="/wiki/Spinach" title="Spinach">spinach</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<ul><li>Myrtle was the first person to be killed by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>.</li>
<li>Apparently she never informed anyone about her death until she told Harry, Ron and <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Lockhart</a>. It's unknown why she never told Armando Dippet or Albus Dumbledore about the cause of her death. It is possible that shortly after her death she was too distraught or traumatised to talk about it and was not asked for fear of upsetting her.</li>
<li>Depending on the month she was born in, she may have been in the same year as Gryffindor Rubeus Hagrid, who was accused of killing her with his Acromantula Aragog.</li>
<li>Hermione and Myrtle, both Muggle-born, shared similar experiences: both Hermione and Myrtle run to the girls' lavatory upon being teased by classmates, a move that puts both in mortal danger (from a <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell%27s_first_mountain_troll" title="Quirinus Quirrell&#39;s first mountain troll">troll</a> and basilisk, respectively.)
<ul><li>However, while Hermione is saved by a repentant Ron and Harry, Myrtle is killed by the unfeeling Riddle.</li></ul></li>
<li>Moaning Myrtle was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Henderson" title="Shirley Henderson">Shirley Henderson</a> in the film adaptations of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>.

<li><a href="/wiki/Annabel_Baldwin" title="Annabel Baldwin">Annabel Baldwin</a> played Moaning Myrtle in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Cursed_Child_(play)#Original_West_End_production" title="Harry Potter and the Cursed Child (play)">original West End production</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Cursed_Child" title="Harry Potter and the Cursed Child">Harry Potter and the Cursed Child</a></i>.</li>

Rowling</a>'s early drafts for <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, this character was supposed to have been called "Wailing Wanda".<sup id="cite_ref-pottermoreghosts_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermoreghosts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Though never seen or mentioned, Ron and Hermione may have seen Myrtle when they went back down the Chamber of Secrets to destroy Hufflepuff's Cup in <i>Deathly Hallows</i>.</li>
<li>Myrtle's surname was never mentioned in the novels; however, in <a href="/wiki/2015" title="2015">2015</a>, after being asked by a fan over Twitter, J.K. Rowling announced it was Warren and that Myrtle also had a middle name (Elizabeth). Rowling also referred to the middle name of Elizabeth as "just one of those classic British middle names". The name was later used in the play <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Cursed_Child_(play)" title="Harry Potter and the Cursed Child (play)">Harry Potter and the Cursed Child</a></i>.</li>
<li>In one scene of <i>Chamber of Secrets</i>, Ron jokingly suggests that <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> earned his Special Award for Services to the School for killing Myrtle; this foreshadows the fact that Riddle did indeed kill her.</li>
<li>Myrtle is shown to have a crush on Harry throughout the series.

<li>Possible origins of her name and nickname include:
<ul><li>It is possible she was named "Myrtle" to continue J.K. Rowling's tradition of naming characters after flowers.</li>
<li>It is possible she was given the middle name Elizabeth and surname Warren as a reference to two characters from Arthur Miller's <i>The Crucible</i>: Elizabeth Proctor and her young servant Mary Warren, both of which are accused of being witches at some point.</li>
<li>The myrtle tree is a close relative of the willow tree. "Moaning Myrtle" is a similar phrase to "weeping willow."</li>
<li>Possibly related to the "Moaning Minnie," the British name for a German WWII artillery shell that made a distinctive moaning whine before it hit.</li></ul></li>
<li>Myrtle is similar to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanako-san" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hanako-san">Hanako-san</a>, a Japanese legend talking about a girl who haunts school bathrooms.</li>
<li>Shirley Henderson's first scene in the film <i>Bridget Jones's Diary</i> involves her character, Jude, crying in a women's toilets.</li>
