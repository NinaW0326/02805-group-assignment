<b>Josiah Jackson</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who served as the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America">President</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PottermoreHistory3_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PottermoreHistory3-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Josiah_Jackson?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Josiah_Jackson?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Described as "warlike," Jackson was elected into office by his fellow representatives in <a href="/wiki/1693" class="mw-redirect" title="1693">1693</a>, as he was considered tough enough to deal with the troubles wizarding America faced in the wake of the <a href="/wiki/Salem_Witch_Trials" title="Salem Witch Trials">Salem Witch Trials</a>. His top priority after taking office was the recruitment of new <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PottermoreHistory3_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PottermoreHistory3-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
