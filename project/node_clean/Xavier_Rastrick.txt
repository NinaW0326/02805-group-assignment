<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xavier_Rastrick?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xavier_Rastrick?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 202px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/23/XavierRastrick.png/revision/latest?cb=20120102203455" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/2/23/XavierRastrick.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20120102203455" decoding="async" width="200" height="196" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="XavierRastrick.png" data-image-key="XavierRastrick.png" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:XavierRastrick.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A younger Rastrick during one of his dance routines</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/1750" class="mw-redirect" title="1750">1750</a>, Xavier Rastrick became a performer in the early <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">nineteenth century</a>. By the <a href="/wiki/1830s" title="1830s">1830s</a>, his exuberant personality attracted a relatively large audience to his shows.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1836" class="mw-redirect" title="1836">1836</a>, a crowd of three hundred <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches and wizards</a> gathered in the village of <a href="/wiki/Painswick" title="Painswick">Painswick</a> to watch one of his performances. The show, however, became infamous, as Rastrick unexpectedly vanished halfway through a tap-dance routine and was never seen or heard from again. Never to be seen again, he was declared dead, with his true fate unknown.
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, Rastrick was featured on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a> for his mysterious disappearance.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xavier_Rastrick?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xavier_Rastrick?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The reason why Rastrick suddenly disappeared is completely unknown, let alone whether or not he truly <a href="/wiki/Vanishment" title="Vanishment">vanished</a> into non-being. It might a case of <a href="/wiki/Vanishing_sickness" title="Vanishing sickness">vanishing sickness</a>. Alternatively, Rastrick might have become <a href="/wiki/Un-born" title="Un-born">un-born</a> due to some time-travelling mishap (as his disappearance was before <a href="/wiki/1899" class="mw-redirect" title="1899">1899</a>, when the Ministry stopped conducting time-travelling experiments).</li>
<li>Based on the fact that his sudden and unexpected disappearance is similar to the reoccuring disappearances of <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_referee" title="Quidditch referee">Quidditch referees</a>, a similar situation may have happened, where an object in contact with Rastrick, such as his hat, was <a href="/wiki/Portus" title="Portus">transformed into a Portkey</a>, whisking him away from the stage to somewhere else where he may have died or turned up far into the future.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Xavier_Rastrick?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Xavier_Rastrick?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Xavier" is derived from the Basque place name Etxaberri meaning "the new house". This was the surname of the Jesuit priest <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Xavier" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Francis Xavier">Saint Francis Xavier</a> (1506â€”1552). He was a missionary to India, Japan, China, and other areas in eastern Asia, and he is the patron saint of the Orient and missionaries. His surname has since been adopted as a given name in his honour, chiefly among Catholics.
</p>
