<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Part_elf?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Part_elf?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, some <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> students, including <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hilliard" title="Robert Hilliard">Robert Hilliard</a>, speculated that <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Filius Flitwick</a> was part elf because of his stature, although they never asked him about it.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In fact, he was <a href="/wiki/Half-goblin" title="Half-goblin">part-goblin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Half-goblin" title="Half-goblin">Half-goblin</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Part_elf?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Part_elf?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>It is also unknown if they inherited the special form of <a href="/wiki/House-elf_magic" title="House-elf magic">elf magic</a> from their non-human ancestor. Irma Dugard possessed a weak form of magic, though it is unclear if it was of the human or house-elf kind.</li>
<li>It is likely interbreeding between humans and&#160;<a href="/wiki/Erkling" title="Erkling">erklings</a>&#160;or&#160;<a href="/wiki/Yumbo" title="Yumbo">yumboes</a>&#160;is possible as well, as both species are described as "elvish" (and thus presumably closely related to normal elves). Moreover, it would also be possible that humans could cross-breed with&#160;<a href="/wiki/Red_Cap" title="Red Cap">Red Caps</a>,&#160;<a href="/wiki/Leprechaun" title="Leprechaun">Leprechauns</a>&#160;and&#160;<a href="/wiki/Imp" title="Imp">Imps</a>, as all three, while not explicitly stated to be related to elves, are markedly similar to them.</li>
<li>Students confusing Flitwick for a part-elf may have been inspired by&#160;<a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s earlier comment that she thought the early movies' version of Flitwick "looked like an elf" (and thus far more non-human than she initially intended).</li></ul>
