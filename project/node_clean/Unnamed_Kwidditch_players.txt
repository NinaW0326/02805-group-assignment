<p>There were <b>unnamed Kwidditch players</b>, probably living on the edge of <a href="/wiki/Queerditch_Marsh" title="Queerditch Marsh">Queerditch Marsh</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a><sup id="cite_ref-jkr-manchester_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jkr-manchester-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/11th_century" title="11th century">11th century</a>. They were playing <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Kwidditch</a>, and their game took place over the marsh, between two trees, serving as a primitive form of modern <a href="/wiki/Goalpost" title="Goalpost">goal posts</a>, and was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Gertie_Keddle" title="Gertie Keddle">Gertie Keddle</a> in her <a href="/wiki/Gertie_Keddle%27s_diary" title="Gertie Keddle&#39;s diary">diary</a>. Each of them had his own flying <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a>. Their leather ball (an early equivalent of today's <a href="/wiki/Quaffle" title="Quaffle">Quaffle</a>) accidentally fell into Keddle's bowl of cabbage and through her window. She was so angry she hexed one of the players, who flew for it.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> These people are the first documented <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a> to play the oldest form of contemporary broomstick game called <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>.
</p>
