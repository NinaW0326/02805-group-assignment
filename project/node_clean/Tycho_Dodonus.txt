<p><b>Tycho Dodonus</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> of unknown nationality and an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Seer" title="Seer">Seer</a>, who prophesied in verse.<sup id="cite_ref-COG45_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COG45-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At some point, many, if not all of Dodonus's <a href="/wiki/Prophecy" title="Prophecy">prophecies</a> were compiled in a book called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Predictions_of_Tycho_Dodonus" title="The Predictions of Tycho Dodonus">The Predictions of Tycho Dodonus</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Apparently, Tycho Dodonus's prophecies were not universally accepted; <a href="/wiki/Porpentina_Goldstein" title="Porpentina Goldstein">Porpentina Goldstein</a> dismissed his work as nothing more than poetry, a notion that <a href="/wiki/Yusuf_Kama" title="Yusuf Kama">Yusuf Kama</a> did not seem to challenge<sup id="cite_ref-COG45_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COG45-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> even though he evidently was a firm believer in the prophecies.<sup id="cite_ref-COG103_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COG103-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tycho_Dodonus?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tycho_Dodonus?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Tycho" is derived from the Greek "Τυχων" meaning "hitting the mark". It is also derived from "Tyche", the Greek goddess of luck.</li>
<li>"Dodonus" is derived from "Dodona", a location in Greece that was the seat of one of Zeus' oracles.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Tycho_Dodonus?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Tycho_Dodonus?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Tycho Dodonus may be reference to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nostradamus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Nostradamus">Nostradamus</a>, a 16th-century French physician and reputed seer who published <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Les_Propheties" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Les Propheties">Les Propheties</a></i>, a collection of quatrains that, allegedly, accurately predicted many major world events.</li>
<li>The name Tycho may be taken from the Danish astronomer, Tycho Brahe, who in his lifetime also had been a astrologer. Brahe had once made out a horoscope for King Frederick II of Denmark's son, the future King Christian IV.</li></ul>
