<b>Andrew</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw</a> student at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School</a> during the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1992" title="1992">1992</a>, he became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a> and was ranked four on the Club's top-ten duellists list.<sup id="cite_ref-COSG_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Andrew?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Andrew?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andrew is from the Greek name Ανδρεας (Andreas), which was derived from ανηρ (aner) "man" (genitive ανδρος (andros) "of a man"). This name has been common (in various spellings) throughout the Christian world, and it became very popular in the Middle Ages. Saint Andrew is regarded as the patron of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>.
</p>
