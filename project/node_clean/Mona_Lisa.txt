<b>Lisa del Giocondo</b>, née <b>Gherardini</b> (<a href="/wiki/15_June" title="15 June">15 June</a>, <a href="/wiki/1479" class="mw-redirect" title="1479">1479</a> – <a href="/wiki/15_July" title="15 July">15 July</a>, <a href="/wiki/1542" class="mw-redirect" title="1542">1542</a> or c. <a href="/wiki/1551" class="mw-redirect" title="1551">1551</a>), better known as <b>Mona Lisa</b> (archaic Italian for <b>Lady Lisa</b>) was a member of the Gherardini family of Florence and Tuscany in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mona_Lisa?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mona_Lisa?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Little is known about Lisa's life. She was born in Florence and married as a teenager to a cloth and silk merchant who later became a local official. She was mother to six children and led what is thought to have been a comfortable and ordinary middle-class life.
</p><p>She was depicted in an <a href="/wiki/Portrait" title="Portrait">oil portrait</a> by the Florentine artist <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Da_Vinci" title="Leonardo Da Vinci">Leonardo da Vinci</a> completed circa <a href="/wiki/1503" class="mw-redirect" title="1503">1503</a>-<a href="/wiki/1519" class="mw-redirect" title="1519">1519</a>. A copy of her portrait was hung in the <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Wing" title="Hospital Wing">Hospital Wing</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CSpc_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSpc-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a> the newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> reported that "famous painting the Mona Lisa is to appear in latest Tango ads".<sup id="cite_ref-HBPF_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBPF-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
