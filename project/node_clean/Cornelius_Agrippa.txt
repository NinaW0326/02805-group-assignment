<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cornelius Agrippa was born in <a href="/wiki/1486" class="mw-redirect" title="1486">1486</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adulthood">Adulthood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Adulthood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Adulthood"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Chocolate Frog Cards">"<i>Cornelius Agrippa lived from 1486 to 1535. He wrote books about magic and wizards. Some important people thought his books were evil, so they put him in gaol for writing them.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Cards" class="mw-redirect" title="Chocolate Frog Cards">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 202px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/00/Cornelius_Agrippa.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20091103124637" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/0/00/Cornelius_Agrippa.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/200?cb=20091103124637" decoding="async" width="200" height="272" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Cornelius Agrippa.jpg" data-image-key="Cornelius_Agrippa.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Cornelius_Agrippa.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Agrippa whilst imprisoned</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>A celebrated author, Agrippa authored numerous <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa%27s_books" title="Cornelius Agrippa&#39;s books">works</a> about <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizardkind</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>. However, the persecution of witches and wizards gathered pace all over <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> in the <a href="/wiki/15th_century" title="15th century">fifteenth century</a>, and Agrippa was imprisoned by <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> who claimed his works were <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">evil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FWC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FWC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For undisclosed reasons, Cornelius Agrippa was the namesake of <a href="/wiki/Agrippa" title="Agrippa">a substance with magical properties</a>, used in <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">Potion-making</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_post-mortem">Death and post-mortem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Death and post-mortem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death and post-mortem"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cornelius Agrippa died in <a href="/wiki/1535" class="mw-redirect" title="1535">1535</a>, aged 49. By the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, he was featured on a <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Card</a>. This card was one of only two that were missing from <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a>'s collection when he met <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991" title="1991">1991</a> (the other being <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-PS6_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS6-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Agrippa?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Cornelius_Agrippa" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa">Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa von Nettesheim</a> (<a href="/wiki/1486" class="mw-redirect" title="1486">1486</a> – <a href="/wiki/1535" class="mw-redirect" title="1535">1535</a>) was a German soldier, physician and author whose major works include <i>Declamatio de nobilitate et praecellentia foeminei sexus</i> (<i>Declamation on the Nobility and Preeminence of the Female Sex</i>), a book first published in <a href="/wiki/1529" class="mw-redirect" title="1529">1529</a> that asserts that women are morally and theologically superior to men, and <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_occulta_philosophia_libri_tres" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:De occulta philosophia libri tres">De occulta philosophia libri tres</a></i> (<i>Three Books on Occult Philosophy</i>), a trilogy of books covering a wide variety of occult topics first published between <a href="/wiki/1531" class="mw-redirect" title="1531">1531</a> and <a href="/wiki/1533" class="mw-redirect" title="1533">1533</a>. He was an adept in alchemy, astrology, and magic. His interest in the occult lead to criticism, and caused him to lose several jobs, but he is not known to have been imprisoned for it.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
