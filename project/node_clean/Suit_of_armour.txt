<b>Suits of armour</b> come in all shapes and sizes, and can be <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">bewitched</a> to move and speak. There were many suits of armour at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> animated several suits of armour to defend Hogwarts against the oncoming <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The shields of those suits of armour in the front row were immune to spells, making them ideal for castle protection. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a> the Death Eaters tried to destroy the armour by using the <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">Killing Curse</a>, but their shields deflected all oncoming spells. The <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> could not defeat them easily, and they maintained the defensive lines of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During the <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%931992_school_year" title="1991–1992 school year">1991–1992 school year</a> and <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%931993_school_year" title="1992–1993 school year">1992–1993 school year</a> some suits were bewitched by <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dobby" title="Dobby">Dobby</a> respectively to attack <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Peeves" title="Peeves">Peeves</a> once hid inside a suit of armour over <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> break during the <a href="/wiki/Triwizard_Tournament" title="Triwizard Tournament">Triwizard Tournament</a>. As the armours were singing Christmas Carols, Peeves began to fill the gaps between lyrics with crude phrases and words. <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Argus Filch</a> had to extract Peeves from the suits on occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-GOF23_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GOF23-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape" title="Severus Snape">Severus Snape</a> was forced to take refuge behind one during his duel with <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> when she transfigured his fiery serpent into daggers. At this point he could do nothing but force a suit of armour in front of him.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> after being animated via the <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">charm</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Piertotum_Locomotor" title="Piertotum Locomotor">Piertotum Locomotor</a></i> cast by <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH30_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH30-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DH31_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown if they survived the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a>. If the suits were destroyed in the Battle, it is possible that they were rebuilt.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Suit_of_armour?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Suit_of_armour?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The suits of armour seem to have a degree of sentience, as a suit's head turned to follow Harry when he was going to <a href="/wiki/Umbridge%27s_Office" class="mw-redirect" title="Umbridge&#39;s Office">Umbridge's Office</a>, and another suit laughed as Neville tripped on the stairs.</li>
<li>While the suits of armour are harmless in the books/films, in some of the video games the Knights also serve as obstacles. In some versions, they attempt to attack Harry with their swords. In the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(PlayStation_2,_Xbox,_GameCube)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (PlayStation 2, Xbox, GameCube)">home console version</a> of the <i>Prisoner of Azkaban</i>, the player must sneak past them and avoid their line of vision, else they will stomp and creak loudly on detection.</li>
<li>The knights that appear in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i> are not, indeed, suits of armour; they appear to be either stone or <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/terracotta" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:terracotta">terracotta</a> statues and do not come in shapes and sizes like gargoyles and different animals in the books. However, they have different helmets and it is possible that some of them are suits because in the book, statues also protected the castle.
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2_(soundtrack)" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i> that is used to present the Suits of Armour is called <i>Statues</i>.</li>
<li>In <i>Deathly Hallows: Part 2</i>, the shields of the suits of armour in the front row have the House crests and Hogwarts crests on them: the far left a Gryffindor Crest, the second from the left a Hufflepuff Crest, the middle knight the Hogwarts Crest, the second from the right a Ravenclaw Crest, and the far right a Slytherin Crest.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_5-7" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 5-7</a></i>, Suits can be summoned to kill anything, including <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">Giants</a> and, despite what we see in the films, they are invincible.</li></ul></li></ul>
