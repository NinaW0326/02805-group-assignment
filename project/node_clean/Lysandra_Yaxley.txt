<b>Lysandra Black</b> (née <b>Yaxley</b>) (fl. 1915-1919) was a <a href="/wiki/Pure-blood" title="Pure-blood">pure-blood</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who married <a href="/wiki/Arcturus_Black_II" title="Arcturus Black II">Arcturus Black II</a>. The couple had three children together: <a href="/wiki/Callidora_Black" title="Callidora Black">Callidora</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cedrella_Black" title="Cedrella Black">Cedrella</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charis_Black" title="Charis Black">Charis Black</a>. Through Cedrella, who was disowned for marrying "<a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitor</a>" <a href="/wiki/Septimus_Weasley" title="Septimus Weasley">Septimus Weasley</a>, she is the maternal grandmother of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a>. Lysandra may be related to the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> <a href="/wiki/Corban_Yaxley" title="Corban Yaxley">Corban Yaxley</a>, and, through her daughters, the <a href="/wiki/Longbottom_family" title="Longbottom family">Longbottom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crouch_family" title="Crouch family">Crouch</a> families.<sup id="cite_ref-BFT_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BFT-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Lysandra_Yaxley?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Lysandra_Yaxley?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lysandra is the feminine form of the Greek name <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lysander_(disambiguation)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Lysander (disambiguation)">Lysander</a>, meaning "a release of man" or "liberty".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was the name of Spartan general and a character in William Shakespeare's <i>A Midsummer Night's Dream</i>.
</p>
