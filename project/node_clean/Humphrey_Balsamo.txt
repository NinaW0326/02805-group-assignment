<b>Humphrey Balsamo</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and shop assistant working at <a href="/wiki/Slug_%26_Jiggers_Apothecary" title="Slug &amp; Jiggers Apothecary">Slug &amp; Jiggers</a>, a shop specialising in <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potions</a> and their ingredients in <a href="/wiki/Diagon_Alley" title="Diagon Alley">Diagon Alley</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Balsamo?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Balsamo?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the time of Balsamo's employment at Slug &amp; Jiggers, the apothecary was broken into and wrecked by what seemed to be a <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> <a href="/wiki/Beast" title="Beast">beast</a>, thus prompting investigation by <a href="/wiki/Mathilda_Grimblehawk" title="Mathilda Grimblehawk">Mathilda Grimblehawk</a> and her <a href="/wiki/Mathilda_Grimblehawk%27s_partner" title="Mathilda Grimblehawk&#39;s partner">partner</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Magic" title="Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Beast_Division" title="Beast Division">Beast Division</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Balsamo?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Balsamo?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In Latin, "<i><a href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/balsamo" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:balsamo">balsamo</a></i>" is a declension of the noun "<i><a href="http://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/balsamum" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:balsamum">balsamum</a></i>", meaning balsam or balm, a sweet-smelling oil or soothing ointment derived from plants; it's a rather fitting surname for someone who works in an apothecary.</li>
<li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alessandro_Cagliostro" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Alessandro Cagliostro">Giuseppe Balsamo</a> (1743â€“1795), who is more commonly known by the assumed pseudonym and self-conferred title of "Count Alessandro di Cagliostro", was a noted charlatan and impostor who infiltrated several royal courts of Europe as a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quackery" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Quackery">quack</a> and self-styled magician.</li></ul>
