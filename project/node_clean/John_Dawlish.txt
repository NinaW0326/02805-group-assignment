<p><b>John</b><sup id="cite_ref-pottercast_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottercast-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <b>Dawlish</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who worked for the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>. Noted by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> as an "excellent <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>", he accompanied <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> on assignments, and also worked under Ministers <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pius_Thicknesse" title="Pius Thicknesse">Pius Thicknesse</a>. During the course of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, however, Dawlish's powers were diminished somewhat as he was incapacitated and attacked quite frequently.<sup id="cite_ref-pottercast_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottercast-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>I seem to remember that you achieved 'Outstanding' in all your NEWTs...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Dawlish's impressive N.E.W.T. results<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>John was born somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British Isles</a>, into the wizarding <a href="/wiki/Dawlish_family" title="Dawlish family">Dawlish family</a> at some point between a hundred year period of 1875 and 1975. He attended <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>, where he would finish his education earning at least five <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.s</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Auror_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Auror-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> all of them with '<a href="/wiki/Outstanding" title="Outstanding">Outstanding</a>' marks.<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_an_Auror">Career as an Auror</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career as an Auror">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career as an Auror"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sometime after his seventeenth birthday, Dawlish joined the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, and completed a stringent series of character and aptitude tests to be accepted into the comprehensive, three year long programme subscribed to potential applicants for the <a href="/wiki/Auror_Office" title="Auror Office">Auror Office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He ultimately served directly for <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> and would accompany him on his travels.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Under_Minister_Cornelius_Fudge">Under Minister Cornelius Fudge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Under Minister Cornelius Fudge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Under Minister Cornelius Fudge"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>Enough of this rubbish! Dawlish! Shacklebolt! Take him!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Cornelius Fudge orders <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a> to seize Albus Dumbledore<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 238px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/30/Kingsleywithfudge.JPG/revision/latest?cb=20110901171500" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/3/30/Kingsleywithfudge.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/236?cb=20110901171500" decoding="async" width="236" height="157" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Kingsleywithfudge.JPG" data-image-key="Kingsleywithfudge.JPG" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Kingsleywithfudge.JPG" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">John Dawlish with Dolores Umbridge, Cornelius Fudge, and Kingsley Shacklebolt in Dumbledore's office</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/20_April" title="20 April">20 April</a>, <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Edgecombe" title="Marietta Edgecombe">Marietta Edgecombe</a> denounced the illegal <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a> to <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a>, Minister <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a> travelled to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> in an attempt to expel <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, who was leading the outlawed student group. <a href="/wiki/Kingsley_Shacklebolt" title="Kingsley Shacklebolt">Kingsley Shacklebolt</a> and John Dawlish escorted the Minister there, and were positioned on either side of the door to the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">Headmaster's office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Headmaster <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> took the blame instead, with a false confession. After Fudge accused him of treason and sedition against himself and the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>, Fudge made a small choking noise, and then looked round at Kingsley and Dawlish. Dawlish nodded reassuringly and moved forwards, his hand drifting towards the wand in his pocket. Dumbledore said he had to hurt him if Dawlish attacked him, and the <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> blinked rather foolishly, without a clue as to what to do next. Fudge ordered the Aurors to seize Dumbledore, but the <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" title="Headmaster">Headmaster</a> easily defeated the Ministry officials, leaving Fudge, Umbridge, Kingsley and Dawlish unconscious on the floor as he escaped. Dawlish was the first to come to, stirring on the floor. When the wizards noted Dumbledore's absence, Dawlish fruitlessly tried to intercept him wrenching the door to the office open and disappearing down the stairs, followed closely by Kingsley and Umbridge.<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 252px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/72/Auror_Dawlish%2C_Cornelius_Fudge_and_Percy_Weasley.JPG/revision/latest?cb=20180104182510" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/72/Auror_Dawlish%2C_Cornelius_Fudge_and_Percy_Weasley.JPG/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180104182510" decoding="async" width="250" height="106" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Auror Dawlish, Cornelius Fudge and Percy Weasley.JPG" data-image-key="Auror_Dawlish,_Cornelius_Fudge_and_Percy_Weasley.JPG" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Auror_Dawlish,_Cornelius_Fudge_and_Percy_Weasley.JPG" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Dawlish alongside Cornelius Fudge and <a href="/wiki/Percy_Weasley" title="Percy Weasley">Percy Weasley</a>, after the Battle of the Department of Mysteries</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>In the early minutes of <a href="/wiki/17_June" title="17 June">17 June</a>, just after midnight, Dawlish was among the number of Ministry officials who, led by Umbridge, attacked <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> in order to arrest him and sack him from his positions of <a href="/wiki/Keeper_of_Keys_and_Grounds" title="Keeper of Keys and Grounds">Gamekeeper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a>. While trying to <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">stun</a> him, Dawlish pleaded Hagrid to "be reasonable". It is unclear whether Dawlish was one of the Aurors physically knocked-out by Hagrid, or the one who, like Umbridge, was left unharmed, but tripped over one of his unconscious colleagues and fell over.<sup id="cite_ref-OP31_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP31-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Department_of_Mysteries" title="Battle of the Department of Mysteries">Battle of the Department of Mysteries</a>, Dawlish was one of the Aurors who arrived with Cornelius Fudge into the <a href="/wiki/Atrium" title="Atrium">Ministry Atrium</a>. Fudge sent Dawlish and <a href="/wiki/Williamson" title="Williamson">Williamson</a> down to the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Mysteries" title="Department of Mysteries">Department of Mysteries</a> to see if there were, as Albus Dumbledore told him, escaped <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Death_Chamber" title="Death Chamber">Death Chamber</a>, bound by an <a href="/wiki/Anti-Disapparation_Jinx" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-Disapparation Jinx">Anti-Disapparation Jinx</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OP36_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP36-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Wizarding_War">Second Wizarding War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Second Wizarding War"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Later life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>What happened to Dawlish after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hogwarts" title="Battle of Hogwarts">Battle of Hogwarts</a> is unknown. It is possible that he was arrested and tried for complying with the Death Eaters and for crimes against muggle-borns, and if he were and if he were found guilty, he would have been sentenced to a term of unknown length in Azkaban.
</p><p>However, a Confundus Charm had been placed on John Dawlish the previous July, and he continued to act "not quite right" for some time thereafter; it is possible that he was not loyal to Voldemort's Ministry of his own volition, in which case charges against him would have been dismissed. Whether Dawlish continued to work for the Ministry, as an Auror or otherwise, cannot be determined.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dawlish was described as looking very capable and self-assured, a "tough-looking wizard" with "very short, wiry" grey hair.<sup id="cite_ref-hair_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hair-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> When hesitant or unsure of himself, he would blink and look "rather foolish".<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>John Dawlish was a dedicated individual, performing exceptionally well at school and passing the stringent requirements to become an Auror. By the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, he served under <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Scrimgeour" title="Rufus Scrimgeour">Rufus Scrimgeour</a> and accompanied the <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> himself. Even <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> himself voiced his belief that John Dawish was an excellent Auror.<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dawlish served the Ministry and appeared willing to execute more or less any order handed to him by higher-ranking officals, even when such course of action was morally questionable, such as the <a href="/wiki/Ousting_of_Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Ousting of Rubeus Hagrid">attack</a> on <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Minerva McGonagall</a> on the behest of <a href="/wiki/Dolores_Umbridge" title="Dolores Umbridge">Dolores Umbridge</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, or his work following the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Ministry_of_Magic" title="Fall of the Ministry of Magic">Death Eater take-over of the Ministry</a> when he was tasked with capturing <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a><sup id="cite_ref-DH15_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH15-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and with attacking <a href="/wiki/Augusta_Longbottom" title="Augusta Longbottom">Augusta Longbottom</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-DH29_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH29-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> which he attempted without protest.
</p><p>He was unflappable during conflicts and remained calm and generally unfazed by threats from his criminal enemies and intrepidly confront them when such a course of action was necessary — this was particularly evident during the Ministry's attempt to arrest <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> in <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, when not only did he not hesitate in stepping forward to challenge him single-handedly with the intent of arresting him (when Dumbledore's presence alone was enough to make several <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> flee in fear),<sup id="cite_ref-OP35_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP35-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> but he also showed no sign of reluctance in attempting to pursue him upon the Professor's escape when he believed it was still possible to catch him.<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magical</a> mastery</b>: John Dawlish was a wizard of considerable ability, excelling in his studies and ultimately achieving an impressive academic record by earning top marks on all of his <a href="/wiki/Nastily_Exhausting_Wizarding_Test" title="Nastily Exhausting Wizarding Test">N.E.W.T.s</a>. Sometime after his graduation from Hogwarts, he went on to become an <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a>, which by all accounts served as a further testament of the fact that he was an unusually accomplished wizard, as only the best and brightest could ever have passed the highly demanding training programme. As an official <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a> catcher, he was more than proficient in at least <a href="/wiki/Potions_(class)" title="Potions (class)">Potions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_(class)" title="Transfiguration (class)">Transfiguration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charms_(class)" title="Charms (class)">Charms</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OP29_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP29-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> as well as a very capable investigator <a href="/wiki/Auror_training" title="Auror training">trained extensively</a> in specialised areas such as <a href="/wiki/Concealment_and_Disguise" title="Concealment and Disguise">Concealment and Disguise</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stealth_and_Tracking" title="Stealth and Tracking">Stealth and Tracking</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Auror</a> skills</b>: By the mid-<a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, Dawlish was regarded as an excellent Auror. Ultimately, he proved to be reputable enough to perform assignments of great responsibility: he served as bodyguard on at least one occasion to <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Fudge" title="Cornelius Fudge">Cornelius Fudge</a>, he was stationed in the village of <a href="/wiki/Hogsmeade" title="Hogsmeade">Hogsmeade</a> to guard <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> from potential <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eater</a> attacks during the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, and he was sent to tail <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> when he was under suspicion by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Martial_magic" title="Martial magic">Martial magic</a></b>: As a professional Dark Wizard catcher, Dawlish's ability in combative magic was considerable, but by the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, a combination of unfamiliar situations, far superior opponents, sheer bad luck, and <a href="/wiki/Confundus_Charm" title="Confundus Charm">Confundus Charms</a> effectively meant that Dawlish was prevented from exercising the full extent of his skill. His two encounters with <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> aside, both of which predictably enough ended in him being easily defeated, even when Dumbledore had already maimed his hand, which was also attributable to Dumbledore knowing and thus effectively exploiting Dawlish's weaknesses,<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the rest of his subsequent failures came about from various unforeseen factors rather than any actual ineptitude on Dawlish's part: during the attempted dismissal of <a href="/wiki/Rubeus_Hagrid" title="Rubeus Hagrid">Rubeus Hagrid</a> from Hogwarts, the magical resistance that came with Hagrid being a <a href="/wiki/Half-Giant" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-Giant">Half-Giant</a>, made the task significantly more difficult;<sup id="cite_ref-OP31_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP31-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> when he was knocked unconscious by <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Cresswell" title="Dirk Cresswell">Dirk Cresswell</a> while he transporting the former to Azkaban, Cresswell himself attributed his escape to Dawlish having been <a href="/wiki/Confundus_Charm" title="Confundus Charm">Confunded</a> beforehand; by the time he was sent to <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's</a> by <a href="/wiki/Augusta_Longbottom" title="Augusta Longbottom">Augusta Longbottom</a>, he had been the "favourite punching-bag" of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Order of the Phoenix">Order of the Phoenix</a> for some time and was somewhat "punch-drunk", in addition to being often put under the effects of the <a href="/wiki/Confundus_Charm" title="Confundus Charm">Confundus Charm</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Flight" title="Flight">Flying</a></b>: Dawlish was also shown be a competent <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> flyer, as whilst working for the corrupt <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a>, he transported falsely convicted <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> individuals to the prison <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> via broomstick flight, meaning he was accomplished with this method of <a href="/wiki/Magical_transportation" title="Magical transportation">wizarding transportation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH15_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH15-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Possessions">Possessions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Possessions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Possessions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">Wand</a></b>: Dawlish owned a wand, which was of unknown length, <a href="/wiki/Wand_wood" title="Wand wood">wood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">core</a> material. He used his wand in his career as an Auror.<sup id="cite_ref-OP27_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP27-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OP31_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP31-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=13" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "John" is the English form of <i>Iohannes</i>, the Latin form of the Greek name Ιωαννης (<i>Ioannes</i>), itself derived from the Hebrew name יוֹחָנָן (<i>Yochanan</i>) meaning "Yahweh is gracious".
</p><p>"<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dawlish" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dawlish">Dawlish</a>" is the name of a seaside town in Devon. It's name comes from a nearby stream, once called "Deawlisc", meaning "devil <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?veaction=edit&amp;section=14" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/John_Dawlish?action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;J.K. Rowling PotterCast Interview">"<i>Jo, </i>[John Noe]<i> idolises a man, an Auror, who got <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/pwn" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:pwn">pwnd</a> by an old lady wearing a dead bird on her head. You know, on her hat. Now, come on!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Sue_Upton" title="Sue Upton">Sue Upton</a> on John Noe's obsession with Dawlish.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/J.K._Rowling_PotterCast_Interview" title="J.K. Rowling PotterCast Interview">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<ul><li>Dawlish is portrayed in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i> by British actor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Leaf" title="Richard Leaf">Richard Leaf</a>.</li>
<li>Dawlish's frequent injuries and incapacitations are somewhat of a running joke in the last three instalments of the series. His role is largely omitted in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_films" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Potter films"><i>Harry Potter</i> films</a>, and his appearances are diminished to two background appearances with no lines in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix</a></i>, and a brief mention in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">first part of the film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> stated that "there's no dishonour to Dawlish", as anyone would have been in trouble going up against Dumbledore and that he was "punch drunk" by the time that he faced Augusta Longbottom, who was nevertheless a powerful witch.<sup id="cite_ref-pottercast_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottercast-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>He was named after <a href="/wiki/John_Noe" title="John Noe">John Noe</a>, co-host of <a href="/wiki/PotterCast" title="PotterCast">PotterCast</a> after a friend of John's asked J. K. Rowling what Dawlish's first name was, and displayed an obsession with the character throughout the podcast.<sup id="cite_ref-pottercast_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottercast-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>He was occasionally the subject of a joke saying that he would have some real significance in the last book. An example of this joke can be seen at the poll on <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://www.the-leaky-cauldron.org/">The Leaky Cauldron</a>.</li></ul>
