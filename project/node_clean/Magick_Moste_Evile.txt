<p><i><b>Magick Moste Evile</b></i>&#160;was a <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a> reference book, written in the early <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">middle ages</a> by <a href="/wiki/Godelot" title="Godelot">Godelot</a>. This book had the characteristic of letting out a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghostly</a> wail when slammed shut.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Magick_Moste_Evile?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Magick_Moste_Evile?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A copy of the book was kept in the <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Section" title="Restricted Section">Restricted Section</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Library" title="Hogwarts Library">library</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> checked it out in early <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> in an attempt to gain information on <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>, only to be thoroughly annoyed by its lack of information. That particular copy let out "a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghostly</a> wail" when Hermione slammed it shut in irritation.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP18_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP18-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2020" title="2020">2020</a>, Hermione Granger had a copy of this along with many other dangerous or banned books on her bookshelves in her <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic%27s_office" title="Minister for Magic&#39;s office">office</a> as <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Magic" title="Minister for Magic">Minister for Magic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CC119_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CC119-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contents">Contents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Magick_Moste_Evile?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Contents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Magick_Moste_Evile?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Contents"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Godelot wrote down this collection of dangerous spells with the help of his wand (according to Godelot in his notebook) which he claims is made of <a href="/wiki/Elder" title="Elder">Elder</a> (<a href="/wiki/Deathly_Hallows" title="Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a> believers consider this to be the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">Elder Wand</a>). This body of work greatly advanced the study of Dark Magic. <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>, however, receive only a brief mention in the book, which does not detail what they really are, their nature considered so vile that even a book on the Dark Arts was unwilling to speak of them.
</p>
