<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Trevor_Birch?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Trevor_Birch?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, he became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Duelling_Club" title="Duelling Club">Duelling Club</a>, and hoped <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Birch%27s_mother" title="Trevor Birch&#39;s mother">his mother</a> would give him a <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> for <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a>. He could usually be found at the <a href="/wiki/Clock_Tower_Courtyard" title="Clock Tower Courtyard">Clock Tower Courtyard</a>. Unlike most people who thought that <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Horace Slughorn</a> was a stakeholder, Trevor thought that he was a good teacher. One day he was duelling and beat him. The blow was so powerful that he needed two days recovering in the hospital wing. He was also an admirer of the author <a href="/wiki/Gilderoy_Lockhart" title="Gilderoy Lockhart">Gilderoy Lockhart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBPG_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBPG-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Trevor_Birch?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Trevor_Birch?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Trevor" is from a surname which was originally from a place name meaning "big village" from Welsh <i>tref</i> "village" and <i>mawr</i> "large".
</p>
