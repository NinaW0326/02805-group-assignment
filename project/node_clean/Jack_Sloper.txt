<b>Jack Sloper</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and substitute <a href="/wiki/Beater" title="Beater">Beater</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Gryffindor_Quidditch_team" title="Gryffindor Quidditch team">Gryffindor Quidditch team</a> in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>. Along with <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Kirke" title="Andrew Kirke">Andrew Kirke</a>, he took over for <a href="/wiki/Fred_Weasley" title="Fred Weasley">Fred Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Weasley" title="George Weasley">George Weasley</a>. Jack was unsuccessful in rejoining the team the next year.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Jack_Sloper?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Jack_Sloper?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He and Andrew were both described as incompetent by <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Johnson" title="Angelina Johnson">Angelina Johnson</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP21_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP21-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> this was particularly evident when Jack knocked himself unconscious with his own bat.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP29_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP29-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>He expressed interest in continuing to play for Gryffindor, but was replaced the following year. Jack later delivered a message for <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> with the details of their first lesson. He also asked Harry when tryouts would be held for Quidditch.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP9_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP9-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Jack_Sloper?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Jack_Sloper?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Jack </i>derives from <i>John, </i>which means "God (Herbrew) is gracious".
</p><p><i>Sloper </i>could come from the English word<i> slope,</i>a surface in which one side is higher than the other. This could relate to his inability to do things right, since slopes have been described as very hard to climb for some people.
</p>
