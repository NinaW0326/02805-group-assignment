<dd>&#8212;Janus' note to his family concerning a <a href="/wiki/Lethifold" title="Lethifold">Lethifold</a> attack<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(real)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Janus Thickey</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1973" title="1973">1973</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1973" title="1973">1973</a>, he faked his own death by pretending that a <a href="/wiki/Lethifold" title="Lethifold">Lethifold</a> had eaten him. He scribbled a note on his beside table that read "<i>oh no a Lethifold's got me I'm suffocating</i>," and his <a href="/wiki/Janus_Thickey%27s_wife" title="Janus Thickey&#39;s wife">wife</a> and <a href="/wiki/Janus_Thickey%27s_children" title="Janus Thickey&#39;s children">children</a> were so convinced by it, and the fact that his bed was still neatly made, that they entered a period of mourning. However, their mourning was cut short when Janus was found to be living five miles away, with the <a href="/wiki/Landlady_of_the_Green_Dragon" title="Landlady of the Green Dragon">landlady</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Green_Dragon" title="Green Dragon">Green Dragon</a></i> pub.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Janus_Thickey_Ward" title="Janus Thickey Ward">Janus Thickey Ward</a> at <a href="/wiki/St_Mungo%27s_Hospital_for_Magical_Maladies_and_Injuries" title="St Mungo&#39;s Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries">St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries</a> was named after him.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Janus_Thickey?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Janus_Thickey?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Janus" means "archway" in Latin. Janus was the Roman god of gateways and beginnings, often depicted as having two faces looking in opposite directions, which alludes to Thickey's two-faced personality.
</p>
