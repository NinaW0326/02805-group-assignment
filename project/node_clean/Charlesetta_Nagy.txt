<b>Charlesetta Nagy</b>, also known by the nickname <b>Mother</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> criminal who operated in the <a href="/wiki/1920s" title="1920s">1920s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wwmm_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwmm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Charlesetta_Nagy?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Charlesetta_Nagy?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="Magical Congress of the United States of America">Magical Congress of the United States of America</a> issued a wanted poster for Nagy, which stated that she was wanted for <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spell</a> forging and <a href="/wiki/House-elf" title="House-elf">elf</a> trafficking.<sup id="cite_ref-wwmm_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwmm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The poster warned that she was "unpredictable" and would "likely resist arrest."<sup id="cite_ref-wwmm_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwmm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A reward of 4,500 <a href="/wiki/Dragot" title="Dragot">Dragots</a> was offered for help that led to her arrest.<sup id="cite_ref-wwmm_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwmm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
