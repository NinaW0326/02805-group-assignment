<p>A <b>Quidditch referee</b> (also known as a <b>Quijudge</b> during the early days of the game) is the individual responsible for adjudicating a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> match. Referees are usually extremely brave <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> are selected by the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Magical_Games_and_Sports" title="Department of Magical Games and Sports">Department of Magical Games and Sports</a>. Each referee has to be an excellent flier, and capable of watching the antics of fourteen players at once. They are required to take rigorous flying tests, and written examination on the rules of the game, and prove — through a series of trials — that they will not jinx or curse offensive players even while under pressure.
</p><p>Referees have had to deal with broom-tampering over the centuries, with the most dangerous example being the turning of a referee's <a href="/wiki/Broomstick" title="Broomstick">broomstick</a> into a <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">Portkey</a>. The referee was subsequently found months later in the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a>.
</p><p>Despite the dangers involved with the job, only one referee, <a href="/wiki/Cyprian_Youdle" title="Cyprian Youdle">Cyprian Youdle</a>, was ever <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">killed</a> during a Quidditch match.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Quidditch referees own <a href="/wiki/Whistle" title="Whistle">whistles</a> as well, used to signal the ends of games, or any pauses or breaks needed.
</p>
