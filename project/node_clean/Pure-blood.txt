<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Pottermore">"<i>The term 'pure-blood' refers to a family or individual without Muggle (non-magic) blood. The concept is generally associated with Salazar Slytherin, one of the four founders of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, whose aversion to teaching anybody of Muggle parentage eventually led to a breach with his three fellow founders, and his resignation from the school.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Where the term 'pure-blood' originated from<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Pottermore" title="Pottermore">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Pure-blood</b> is the term for <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding</a> families who claim to have no <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> or <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-borns</a> in their family tree; however, when traced back far enough, nearly every family tree had some non-magical ancestors.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-malfoy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malfoy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TBB_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Most wizards and witches consider an individual to be a pure-blood if his or her parents and grandparents were not Muggles or Muggle-born.<sup id="cite_ref-harry_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harry-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a>, the number of pure-blood families were declining. Most pure-blood families were related by then in an effort, by some, to retain their status by marrying only other pure-blood families, but it had eventually led to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inbreeding" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Inbreeding">inbreeding</a>. While some families, such as the <a href="/wiki/Malfoy_family" title="Malfoy family">Malfoys</a>, had allowed for marrying half-bloods, others such as the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Gaunt" title="House of Gaunt">Gaunts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_family" title="Lestrange family">Lestranges</a>, and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">Blacks</a>, required their family to only marry pure-blood individuals, or those with no Muggle or Muggle-born ancestors.<sup id="cite_ref-malfoy_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malfoy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FAQ_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This refusal of marrying Muggles or Muggle-borns had led to difficulty in propagating their families.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">"<i>The pure-blood families are all interrelated. If you’re only going to let your sons and daughters marry pure-bloods, your choice is very limited; there are hardly any of us left.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>'s overview of blood purity<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Pure-blood individuals are people who have no <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> or Muggle-borns as parents or grandparents.<sup id="cite_ref-harry_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harry-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Traditionalist pure-bloods like to keep the generations "pure" by breeding with other pure-bloods and are generally the ones who use the term '<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudblood</a>', a term considered derogatory by the wizarding world. Families of pure-blood status often avoid marrying their heirs off to Muggle-Borns and <a href="/wiki/Half-Blood" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-Blood">Half-Bloods</a>, believing that the next generation could lose their capability to perform magic. Mudblood means Muggle-born, suggesting they have dirty blood and thus have no right to be a witch/wizard. Pure-bloods who do not share these prejudices are considered "blood traitors" by the ones that do.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1930s" title="1930s">1930s</a>, a wizard, commonly believed to be <a href="/wiki/Cantankerus_Nott" title="Cantankerus Nott">Cantankerus Nott</a>, wrote the <i><a href="/wiki/Pure-Blood_Directory" title="Pure-Blood Directory">Pure-Blood Directory</a></i> featuring <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Twenty-Eight" title="Sacred Twenty-Eight">twenty-eight families</a> he believed to be truly pure-blooded, though many objected to this designation.<sup id="cite_ref-pureblood_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pureblood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Maintaining_&quot;purity&quot;"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Maintaining_.22purity.22">Maintaining "purity"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Maintaining &quot;purity&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Maintaining &quot;purity&quot;"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>So-called pure-blood families maintain their alleged purity by disowning, banishing, or lying about Muggles or Muggle-borns on their family trees. Then they attempt to foist their hypocrisy upon the rest of us by asking us to ban works dealing with the truths they deny. There is not a witch or wizard in existence whose blood has not mingled with that of Muggles...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Pure-blood wizards are not so pure<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 247px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/de/Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090626131137" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="245" height="138" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg" data-image-key="Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/de/Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20090626131137" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/de/Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090626131137" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/de/Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20090626131137" decoding="async" width="245" height="138" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg" data-image-key="Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/d/de/Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20090626131137" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Blackfamilytreeootp5.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Black_Family_Tapestry" title="Black Family Tapestry">Black Family Tapestry</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Some pure-blood families can trace their pure-blood status through many generations of magical ancestors and deny ever having any Muggles within the family, such as the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>, the motto of which is "Toujours pur", meaning "Always (or Still) Pure".<sup id="cite_ref-JKRtree_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JKRtree-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HOB_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOB-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, the truth is that if they ever did exist in the past, true pure-blood wizards and witches do not exist today. They merely erase <a href="/wiki/Squib" title="Squib">Squibs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muggle-borns" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle-borns">Muggle-borns</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> from their family trees.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Half-bloods who consider <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood purity</a> very important also hide their Muggle ancestry, clinging to the magical heritage they do have.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP12_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP12-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Many <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a> are believed to have done this, and their leader himself took on the name <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> out of a desire to not keep the name of his "<i>filthy Muggle father</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-CS_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It is likely that Voldemort told his followers he was a pure-blood, or that most of the Death Eaters knew better than to question Riddle's blood status, given the reaction of <a href="/wiki/Bellatrix_Lestrange" title="Bellatrix Lestrange">Bellatrix Lestrange</a> to Harry's "accusation" that he was in fact a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many pure-blood families, such as the Blacks and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Gaunt" title="House of Gaunt">Gaunts</a>, practise marrying cousins in order to maintain their pure-blood status, and disown any members who marry someone who is not a pure-blood.<sup id="cite_ref-Disown_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Disown-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This practice has been known to cause problems as families often become inbred because of this; for example, the Gaunts showed signs of violent tendencies, mental instability, and being enfeebled.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-malfoy_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malfoy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The number of pure-bloods has diminished over time because of said <a href="/wiki/Inbreeding" title="Inbreeding">inbreeding</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FAQ_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FAQ-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attitude">Attitude</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Attitude">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Attitude"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>I really don't think they should let the other sort in, do you? They’re just not the same, they've never been brought up to know our ways. Some of them have never even heard of <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> until they get the letter, imagine. I think they should keep it in the old wizarding families.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>'s prejudice concerning Muggle-borns<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 152px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8d/SalazarSlytherin.png/revision/latest?cb=20131117024104" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="150" height="239" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="SalazarSlytherin.png" data-image-key="SalazarSlytherin.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8d/SalazarSlytherin.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20131117024104" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8d/SalazarSlytherin.png/revision/latest?cb=20131117024104" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8d/SalazarSlytherin.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20131117024104" decoding="async" width="150" height="239" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="SalazarSlytherin.png" data-image-key="SalazarSlytherin.png" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/8/8d/SalazarSlytherin.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20131117024104" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:SalazarSlytherin.png" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption"><a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin" title="Salazar Slytherin">Salazar Slytherin</a>, an infamous pure-blood supremacist</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>Some pure-bloods regard themselves as superior to those witches and wizards who are born to <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-bloods</a>. They believe that Muggles are little more than animals and that <a href="/wiki/Muggle-borns" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle-borns">Muggle-borns</a>, whom they derisively call '<a href="/wiki/Mudblood" title="Mudblood">Mudbloods</a>', are second-class citizens, inferior and unworthy of being allowed to practise <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>. Many also discriminate against <a href="/wiki/Half-breeds" class="mw-redirect" title="Half-breeds">half-breeds,</a> <a href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant">giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">magical creatures</a>.
</p><p>Salazar Slytherin believed that only pure-bloods should be allowed to attend <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and educated in magic; this was the cause of his conflict with the other Founders and his leaving the school. However, he left behind the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> in the hope that one day his heir would open the Chamber and release the basilisk within to target Muggle-born students. <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Riddle</a> was this heir, and the <a href="/wiki/Basilisk" title="Basilisk">basilisk</a> petrified several Muggle-born students in <a href="/wiki/1943" title="1943">1943</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%931993_school_year" title="1992–1993 school year">1992–1993 school year</a> and managed to kill one student, <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Warren" title="Myrtle Warren">Myrtle Warren</a>, in 1943.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Riddle, later known as Lord Voldemort, continued his persecution of those who were not pure-bloods (ironically being a half-blood himself) with his followers, the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>. They strove to place pure-bloods in control of the wizarding world and to rid it of Muggle-borns. When Voldemort succeeded in taking over the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">Ministry of Magic</a> during the height of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, his followers created the <a href="/wiki/Muggle-Born_Registration_Commission" title="Muggle-Born Registration Commission">Muggle-Born Registration Commission</a> in order to weed Muggle-borns out of the population and send them to <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a> for allegedly stealing magic. Pure-bloods were also blatantly favoured within the Ministry and, to a lesser extent, Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, although half-blood was sufficient to avoid prosecution.<sup id="cite_ref-DH_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, there are pure-bloods who do not have these beliefs, and instead treat all magical people and Muggles as equals. These pure-blood folk are often labelled "<a href="/wiki/Blood_traitor" title="Blood traitor">blood traitors</a>" by the more fanatical pure-bloods.<sup id="cite_ref-CS_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CS-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Possible_pure-bloods">Possible pure-bloods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Possible pure-bloods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Possible pure-bloods"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Some even become <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>On <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>'s draft list of students in <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s class, the following students were mentioned as pure-bloods:<sup id="cite_ref-HPM_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPM-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Lavender_Brown" title="Lavender Brown">Lavender Brown</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stephen_Cornfoot" title="Stephen Cornfoot">Stephen Cornfoot</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Isobel_MacDougal" title="Isobel MacDougal">Isobel MacDougal</a> <span class="template-c">(Possibly a precursor to Morag McDougal)</span></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Morag_MacDougal" title="Morag MacDougal">Morag MacDougal</a></li></ul></li>
<li>Coincidentally, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> are all the three types of <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards</a>; Harry being a <a href="/wiki/Half-blood" title="Half-blood">half-blood</a>, Hermione being <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a>, and Ron being a pure-blood.</li>
<li>Many characters mention throughout the series that blood purity doesn't matter, often using Hermione Granger in comparison to Ron Weasley and Neville Longbottom as an example. Ron had long struggled with living in the shadows of how much better students his older brothers were, and often would not try if he felt the situation was out of his control. or too difficult, preferring to goof off or play with Chocolate Frog cards (though he did ultimately emerge as a capable student and wizard in his own right). Similarly, despite Neville's pure-blood heritage, he was not initially an extremely talented wizard and also suffered from a lack of confidence, two things which made him a target for ridicule and difficulty, particularly from Professor Snape (but like Ron in his later life he better grasped his full potential). However, Hermione, by contrast, is a supremely gifted witch despite her Muggle-born status and, unlike Ron and Neville, performed very well right from the beginning.
<ul><li>Likewise, Vincent Crabbe and Gregory Goyle were both often thuggishly stupid and gave poor academic performances in spite of their Pure-Blood status, and Harry's mother Lily was one of her teachers', Horace Slughorn's, all-time favourite students, performing exceptionally well in his and other classes.</li></ul></li>
<li>The <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://harrypotter.scholastic.com/glossary/"><i>Harry Potter</i> Glossary</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scholastic" title="Scholastic">Scholastic</a>'s official website lists the term "Trueblood" as "Someone born to pure magical parents." This term, however, is not known to have appeared in any official <i>Harry Potter</i> media. The nuance with "pureblood" may be that while the child of a half-blood and a pureblood counts as a pureblood, a trueblood can only be the child of two purebloods. That is however unconfirmed.</li>
<li>The concept of pure-blood is also similar to the real-life situation of nearly all European royal families being related, especially the British and (now extinct) German monarchies. Royals were only allowed to marry another member of the aristocracy, so most of the families were interrelated through marriage. Similarly, the issue of in-breeding is something that ravaged many real life European royal lines.
<ul><li>Likewise, the concept of blood purity, and how if one goes back far enough it is clear there is no such thing, is strongly analogous to real life racial purity attitudes such as those held by White Supremacists and the Nazis (the latter of whom J.K. Rowling explicitly compared to the Death Eaters, as seen below), but also how it is theorised all human being's ancestry can be traced back to Africa (and specifically Ethiopia), thus precluding the idea that "wholly white" lineages could have ever existed.</li></ul></li></ul>
<h3><span id="Author's_comments"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Author.27s_comments">Author's comments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Pure-blood?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the subject of <a href="/wiki/Blood_status" title="Blood status">blood purity</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has said the following:
</p>
<blockquote><p><i>The expressions 'pure-blood', 'half-blood', and 'Muggle-born' have been coined by people to whom these distinctions matter, and express their originators’ prejudice. As far as somebody like Lucius Malfoy is concerned, for instance, a Muggle-born is as ‘bad’ as a Muggle. Therefore Harry would be considered only ‘half’ wizard, because of his maternal grandparents. If you think this is far-fetched, look at some of the real charts the Nazis used to show what constituted 'Aryan' or 'Jewish' blood...the Nazis used precisely the same warped logic as the Death Eaters. A single Jewish grandparent 'polluted' the blood, according to their propaganda.</i><sup id="cite_ref-harry_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harry-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>This statement indicates that all of a wizard or witch's grandparents must have magical heritage — i.e. that he or she cannot have a single <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> or <a href="/wiki/Muggle-born" title="Muggle-born">Muggle-born</a> grandparent — in order for him or her to be considered pure-blood. The detailed and lengthy family trees maintained by pure-blood families such as the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a> maintained a record of all the pure-bloods born and married into the family (as well documenting those who were blasted off to maintain the family's sense of purity).<sup id="cite_ref-OTP_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTP-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
