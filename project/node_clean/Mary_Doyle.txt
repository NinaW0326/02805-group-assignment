<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Doyle?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Mary_Doyle?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Mary" is the English form of <i>Maria</i>, which was the Latin form of the New Testament Greek names Μαριαμ (<i>Mariam</i>) and Μαρια (<i>Maria</i>) - the spellings are interchangeable - which were from the Hebrew name מִרְיָם (<i>Miryam</i>). The meaning is not known for certain, but there are several theories including "sea of bitterness", "rebelliousness", and "wished for child". However it was most likely originally an Egyptian name, perhaps derived in part from <i>mry</i> "beloved" or mr "love".
</p>
