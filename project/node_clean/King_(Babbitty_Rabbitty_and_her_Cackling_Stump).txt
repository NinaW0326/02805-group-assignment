<p><b>The King</b> was one of the antagonists in a story from <a href="/wiki/Beedle_the_Bard" title="Beedle the Bard">Beedle the Bard</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">stories</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump" title="Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump">Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump</a></i>. He was a foolish and gullible ruler who condemned magic, which led to his founding of the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a>. He is later revealed to have been jealous of <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> and their ability to use <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Brigade">The Brigade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The Brigade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Brigade"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>&#8230;commanded the head of his army to form a Brigade of Witch-Hunters, and issued them with a pack of ferocious black hounds.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> king in the story wanted to keep all the <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> in the world for himself. In order to do so, he decided to gather all the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> in the world, which led to him forming the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a> and giving them wild hounds.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="An_Instructor_of_Magic">An Instructor of Magic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: An Instructor of Magic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: An Instructor of Magic"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Wanted by King, an Instructor of Magic</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The King's Proclamations<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Soon after the foundation of the Brigade, the King wanted to learn how to use <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>. He issued proclamations throughout his kingdom that he required a teacher with <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability. Because they were hiding from the Brigade, no real <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches or wizards</a> responded. However, a cunning <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">Charlatan</a> came to the palace claiming to be a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> with enormous skill. The <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">charlantan</a> performed a few simple tricks, which convinced the King of his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> powers. The <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">charlantan</a> agreed to teach the king, despite having no <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> knowledge himself, in exchange for money, rubies, a silver chalice and fame.
</p><p>While the King and the <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">Charlatan</a> were practising waving their twigs, hopping in circles, and chanting meaningless rhymes, they heard <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> laughing hysterically from her cottage. This enraged the King, who demanded that the charlatan help him perform in front of his subjects to show off his new abilities. The charlatan attempted to back out by saying he had to go out of town and could not help him, but the King threatened to send the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">brigade</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">charlantan</a>. He added that should anyone laugh at the performance, the <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">Charlatan</a>'s head would also be cut off.
</p><p>The next day, with help of <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>, the King and the <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">Charlatan</a> performed "their <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>". They astonished the crowd by making a hat disappear, levitating a horse, but when the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Captain of the Brigade</a> asked if the King can make <a href="/wiki/Sabre" title="Sabre">Sabre</a>, his dead hound, return to life, the King attempted in vain. <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> does nothing, because she knows no <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> can raise the dead. The crowd laughed at the King, and the King wanted to know why the <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spell</a> wasn't working. The charlatan pointed to the bush, and said that a wicked <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> was blocking them. <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> ran from the bush, and when the hounds chased after her she "disappeared", leaving the dogs barking at a tree.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="The_King's_promise"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_King.27s_promise">The King's promise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The King&#039;s promise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The King&#039;s promise"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>You will erect a statue of Babbitty upon me, in memory of your poor washerwoman, and to remind you for ever of your foolishness!</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The charlatan told the king and the crowd that <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> had turned into an apple tree, and that the tree must be cut down, because she is an "evil" witch. The king was wild, and the tree was cut down. As the crowd and the king started to leave, they heard a cackling coming from the stump. <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> told the king that real <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> could not be cut in half, and that they should cut the charlatan in half to prove it. The charlatan confessed to being a fraud. The witch told them that the King was cursed, and that everytime a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch or wizard</a> was harmed, the King himself would feel physical pain as though he himself were being cut in half. <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> then demanded a statue be built of herself, to remind them of the harm they had done to <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a>. The King agreed to the promise and later disbanded the Brigade. Soon after, an old rabbit, who is assumed to be <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>, appears out of a hole in the stump, with a wand in its mouth, implying that <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a>. She then leaves the kingdom. Forever after, the statue of <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> remained on top of the stump.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Relationships"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Witches_and_Wizards">Witches and Wizards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=6" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Witches and Wizards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Witches and Wizards"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>No true witch or wizard dared volunteer for the post, for they were hiding from the Brigade of Witch-Hunters&#8230;</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The status of the King's Instructor of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">Magic</a> post<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">Wizards and witches</a> in general loathed the King for creating the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a>, an organisation that hunted <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a>. When the King wanted to learn how to use <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, he called for someone with <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> ability to teach him. No real <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards or witches</a> dared to respond for they were hiding from the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a>. Ultimately, the King was jealous of <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_charlatan">The charlatan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: The charlatan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The charlatan"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c7/Babbityrabbity.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20090404104534" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/c/c7/Babbityrabbity.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20090404104534" decoding="async" width="180" height="209" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Babbityrabbity.jpg" data-image-key="Babbityrabbity.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Babbityrabbity.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">The King and the charlatan practising their "magic"</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>The charlatan gave one of the twigs to the King and assured him that it was a wand with tremendous power</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Charlatan<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>When the King called for any <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards or witches</a> to teach him <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>, a charlatan came to the palace claiming to be a wizard with enormous skill. The charlatan performed a few simple tricks, which convinced the King of his <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> powers and he taught the King, despite not knowing any <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> himself. He accepted a post as the King's <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> instructor purely to benefit his own wealth and social standing.
</p><p>When ordered by the King to perform magic in front of the people, the Charlatan went to <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>, knowing that she was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>. The charlatan blackmailed her into concealing herself inside a bush while the King gave his magical display and to perform the spells for him, without the King's knowledge. Babbitty agreed. The next day, the charlatan and the King, with help from <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>, performed <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>. However, the King does not succeed in bringing a dead dog back to life, so the charlatan accuses Babbitty of blocking his spells, causing <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> to flee. When <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> turned into her <a href="/wiki/Animagus" title="Animagus">Animagus</a> form and hid under a tree, it got chopped in half. But when she told the king that no <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch or wizard</a> could be chopped in half, and they should prove this by chopping the charlatan in half, the charlatan confessed that he was a fraud and was sent to the dungeon by order of the King.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Babbitty">Babbitty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Babbitty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Babbitty"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>Very good, but you have not yet made amends to Babbitty</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> was the king's washer woman. She thought that the charlatan and King's "lessons" were ridiculous and laughed at the king, causing the charlatan to become endangered. When the Charlatan came to see her, he blackmailed <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> to perform the King's spells for him without his knowledge, or else he would denounce her as a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">Brigade of Witch-Hunters</a>. <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> smiled and agreed to do "anything in her power" to help the charlatan (leaving herself a very large loophole in the process). Despite Babbity's voiced concerns about what would happen if the king attempted an impossible spell, the charlatan merely shrugged it off. The next day, with the help of <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>, the King and charlatan performed "their magic". But when one of the <a href="/wiki/Brigade_of_Witch-Hunters" title="Brigade of Witch-Hunters">brigade</a> asked if the King could make his dead hound return to life, the King tries in vain. <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> does nothing, because she knew that no <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> could raise the dead. The charlatan told the King and the crowd that <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> had turned into a tree, and that the tree must be cut down, because she is an "evil" witch. After they cut the tree down, they heard the sound of loud cackling coming from the stump. <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> told the petrified King that by cutting a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> in half, he had unleashed a terrible curse upon his kingdom and every stroke of harm that he inflicted upon fellow <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> would feel like an axe stroke in his side. The King promised that he would issue a proclamation at once, protecting <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> of the kingdom and to erect a statue of <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a> on the stump.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/King_(Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump)?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">"<i>At that, the King fell to his knees too, and told that he would issue a proclamation at once, protecting all the witches and wizards of the kingdom, and allowing to practise their magic in peace</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The Foolish King<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard_(real)" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard (real)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>The King is a foolish <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> who both covets and fears <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a>. He is ignorant of the true nature of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a>. It is for this reason that he thinks that he could become a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> by waving a <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> and saying a few simple incantations. Being gullible is also a trait that the King expresses, that is why, in the <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbitty_and_her_Cackling_Stump" title="Babbitty Rabbitty and her Cackling Stump">story</a>, he swallows the preposterous suggestions of both the <a href="/wiki/Charlatan" title="Charlatan">Charlatan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Babbitty_Rabbity" title="Babbitty Rabbity">Babbitty</a>. He is quite selfish as this demonstrated in the story by his ability to not care about how many <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizards and witches</a> he has to kill to get want he wants.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
