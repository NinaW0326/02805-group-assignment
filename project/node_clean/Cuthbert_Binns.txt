<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">"<i>My subject is <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a>. I deal with </i>facts<i>, not myths and legends.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Professor Binns to his class<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p><b>Professor Cuthbert</b><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <b>Binns</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. He owned <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns%27s_office" title="Cuthbert Binns&#39;s office">an office</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">the main castle</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic_Classroom" title="History of Magic Classroom">Classroom 4F</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle#First_floor" title="Hogwarts Castle">first floor</a> to teach in. He taught until a very old age, when he fell asleep in the <a href="/wiki/Staffroom" title="Staffroom">staff room</a> and died in slumber.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Afterwards, he became a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a> who continued to teach, droning on about various <a href="/wiki/Goblin_Rebellions" title="Goblin Rebellions">Goblin Rebellions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giant_wars" title="Giant wars">Giant wars</a> to bored, sleeping students.
</p><p>Professor Binns's lessons were regarded as some of the most boring at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>, and only once in known history did he talk about something not strictly factual. During the <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%931992_school_year" title="1991–1992 school year">1991–1992 school year</a>, he taught his <a href="/wiki/First_year" title="First year">first year</a> students about numerous historical personalities such as <a href="/wiki/Uric_the_Oddball" title="Uric the Oddball">Uric the Oddball</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emeric_the_Evil" title="Emeric the Evil">Emeric the Evil</a>, whom the class always got confused with.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The following <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%931993_school_year" title="1992–1993 school year">academic year</a>, he relayed the legend of the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> to his <a href="/wiki/Second_year" title="Second year">second year</a> class, but only after every student in the class showed complete and utter interest, despite initially stating the legend of the Chamber to be unimportant.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He continued to teach <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> and his class for three further years, until most, if not every one of his students dropped <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a> after completing their <a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Wizarding_Level" title="Ordinary Wizarding Level">Ordinary Wizarding Levels</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (video game)">"<i>You're welcome. As my father always used to say, zzzzzz...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Cuthbert Binns to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (video game)">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Cuthbert was born into the <a href="/wiki/Binns_family" title="Binns family">Binns family</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns%27s_father" title="Cuthbert Binns&#39;s father">Mr Binns</a> as his father. It was said that his father always used to say something to him, but Cuthbert fell asleep in the middle of his sentence before he could say what it was.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_at_Hogwarts">Career at Hogwarts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career at Hogwarts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career at Hogwarts"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Earlier_years">Earlier years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Earlier years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Earlier years"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">"<i>Professor Binns opened his notes and began to read in a flat drone like an old vacuum cleaner until nearly everyone in the class was in a deep stupor, occasionally coming round long enough to copy down a name or date, then falling asleep again.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description.<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Cuthbert came to <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> and applied for the post of <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> of <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a>, which he was granted. Cuthbert - now known as Professor Binns - was given <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic_Classroom" title="History of Magic Classroom">Classroom 4F</a> on the first floor near a mossy courtyard to teach in, as well as <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns%27s_office" title="Cuthbert Binns&#39;s office">an office</a> in the fifth-floor corridor.
</p><p>His lessons were known to be the most boring in all of Hogwarts; he read from his never-ending collection of notes. His students, whom he never payed any attention to, would fall asleep listening to his droning, reedy voice, eventually waking up to copy down a name or date, but falling asleep again.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years_and_death">Later years and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Later years and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Later years and death"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">"<i>Professor Binns had been very old indeed when he had fallen asleep in front of the staff-room fire and got up next morning to teach, leaving his body behind him.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The story of Professor Binns's death<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher&#39;s Stone">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Professor Binns was still teaching well into his elderly years at Hogwarts, still lecturing students in History of Magic. Now so old that he looked like a wrinkled tortoise,<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Binns went down to the <a href="/wiki/Staffroom" title="Staffroom">staffroom</a> on the ground floor and went to sleep in front of the fire. However, he died in his sleep and, when he got up to teach his next class, was a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Regardless, he did not seem to notice, though he did begin to fly into his classroom through the blackboard, but his lessons were still just as boring as ever. It was said the most exciting thing that happened in his class was when he entered through the blackboard.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1970s">1970s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1970s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1970s"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/1970s" title="1970s">1970s</a>, Professor Binns taught <a href="/wiki/James_Potter_I" title="James Potter I">James Potter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pettigrew" title="Peter Pettigrew">Peter Pettigrew</a>, <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Remus Lupin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Black" title="Sirius Black">Sirius Black</a>. For unknown reasons, Binns gave one of his <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Frog_Card" title="Chocolate Frog Card">Chocolate Frog Cards</a>, that of <a href="/wiki/Circe" title="Circe">Circe</a>, to James Potter, which later fell into the hands of <a href="/wiki/Quirinus_Quirrell" title="Quirinus Quirrell">Quirinus Quirrell</a> who put it into his vault, number <a href="/wiki/Vault_998" title="Vault 998">998</a>.
</p>
<h4><span id="1988–1989_school_year"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1988.E2.80.931989_school_year">1988–1989 school year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: 1988–1989 school year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 1988–1989 school year"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1988%E2%80%931989_school_year" title="1988–1989 school year">1988–1989 school year</a>, one of the groups Binns taught had 16 <a href="/wiki/Fifth_year" title="Fifth year">fifth year</a> students which included <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_sibling" title="Jacob&#39;s sibling">Jacob's sibling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nymphadora_Tonks" title="Nymphadora Tonks">Nymphadora Tonks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Weasley" title="Charles Weasley">Charlie Weasley</a>. As always, these classes were seen as boring by most students. Sometimes Tonks used her <a href="/wiki/Metamorphmagus" title="Metamorphmagus">Metamorphmagus</a> ability to transform into Binns in order to make lessons more interesting. Most of the time, Binns did not pay attention to the class, leading to students doing activities such as tasting confectionery or playing <a href="/wiki/Exploding_Snap" title="Exploding Snap">Exploding Snap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When preparing to enter the Portrait Vault, Jacob's sibling went to Binns along with <a href="/wiki/William_Weasley" title="William Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Merula_Snyde" title="Merula Snyde">Merula Snyde</a> in order to learn about <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5ch27_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5ch27-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Later, the three, along with <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Rakepick" title="Patricia Rakepick">Patricia Rakepick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Copper" title="Ben Copper">Ben Copper</a> and either Charlie Weasley or <a href="/wiki/Penny_Haywood" title="Penny Haywood">Penny Haywood</a> went to Binns to learn about <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">Portkeys</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

As preparation for the school year, Professor Binns set his <a href="/wiki/Third_year" title="Third year">third year</a> students an essay titled <i><a href="/wiki/Witch_Burning_in_the_Fourteenth_Century_Was_Completely_Pointless_%E2%80%94_discuss" title="Witch Burning in the Fourteenth Century Was Completely Pointless — discuss">Witch Burning in the Fourteenth Century Was Completely Pointless — discuss</a></i>, over the <a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a> summer holidays.<sup id="cite_ref-POA1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> One night in the winter term, Binns allowed <a href="/wiki/Remus_Lupin" title="Remus Lupin">Professor Remus Lupin</a>, the new <a href="/wiki/Defence_Against_the_Dark_Arts" title="Defence Against the Dark Arts">Defence Against the Dark Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">teacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anti-Dementor_lessons" title="Anti-Dementor lessons">to teach</a> Harry how to cast the <a href="/wiki/Patronus_Charm" title="Patronus Charm">Patronus Charm</a> in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic_Classroom" title="History of Magic Classroom">History of Magic Classroom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POA12_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POA12-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_description">Physical description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=16" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Physical description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<dd>&#8212;A brief description of Professor Binns<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">[src]</a></sup></dd>

<p>Professor Binns, as being a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a>, was described as looking ancient and shrivelled, and looked a little like an old tortoise when he pursed his lips. He wore glasses, which were small and thick, and had a dry, reedy voice that sounded a lot like an old vacuum cleaner, droning on and on.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=17" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Professor Binns did not display much of a personality, as all he did was read his notes in a droning voice to his students, while oblivious or disinterested about how everyone simply dozed off from boredom.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, it can be seen that he was very impatient and did not take the liberty of learning his students' names, calling <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> "Miss Grant", <a href="/wiki/Parvati_Patil" title="Parvati Patil">Parvati Patil</a> "Miss Pennyfeather", <a href="/wiki/Seamus_Finnigan" title="Seamus Finnigan">Seamus Finnigan</a> "O'Flaherty" and even the famous <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> "Perkins". It's possible he was actually calling them by the names of previous students from years ago, possibly due to senility as he was very old at the time of <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a>. Binns was also shown to be a firm disbeliever in legends and mythology, dedicating his studies and lectures on "<i>solid, believable </i>fact".<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was said to have a drawling monotonous voice, which most of his students become quickly bored with.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=18" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a></b>: Being the <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> of History of Magic at Hogwarts, he was an authority in and thoroughly knowledgeable of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> history, teaching the subject for several decades at Hogwarts School, with his rich and varied historical knowledge spanning much of wizarding history.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Magizoology" title="Magizoology">Magizoology</a></b>: Binns was shown to be knowledgeable in the study of magical <a href="/wiki/Creature" title="Creature">creatures</a>, as he had knowledge about the traits of several different breeds of <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HM5ch27_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM5ch27-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Study_of_Ancient_Runes" title="Study of Ancient Runes">Study of Ancient Runes</a></b>: Binns was knowledgeable in the field of Runology, as he taught <a href="/wiki/Sixth_year" title="Sixth year">sixth year</a> History of Magic students about the history of ancient runes, during the <a href="/wiki/1989%E2%80%931990_school_year" title="1989–1990 school year">1989–1990 school year</a> at Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-HM628hm_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM628hm-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Teaching skills</b>: Binns must have proven himself to have been an adequate teacher to have acquired his position at Hogwarts as a <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> whilst he was alive. However, his teaching after his death had proven to be ineffective, as he simply read notes in a droning manner that lulled many students to fall asleep.<sup id="cite_ref-PS8_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> His unenthusiastic manner of communications, lack of interest in his students' attention, and his refusal to delve into what he considered to be myth, greatly hampered his skills in delivering information to his class and keeping them interested in his subject.<sup id="cite_ref-COS9_6-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COS9-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Possessions">Possessions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=19" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Possessions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Possessions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns%27s_office" title="Cuthbert Binns&#39;s office">Office</a></b>: While teaching at Hogwarts, Binns had an office on the fifth floor.<sup id="cite_ref-PSG_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PSG-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COSG_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COSG-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Books</b>: Binns owned several books, including a <a href="/wiki/Draconifors_spellbook" title="Draconifors spellbook">Draconifors spellbook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Wizarding_Events_of_the_Twentieth_Century" title="Great Wizarding Events of the Twentieth Century">Great Wizarding Events of the Twentieth Century</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns%27s_books" title="Cuthbert Binns&#39;s books">several other books</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=20" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Binns" is from "binoculars", a slang term for spectacles. Notice that Professor Binns wore very thick glasses.</li>
<li>A bin is a large storage container. A dustbin is British English for a garbage can. This boring professor could be described as a ghostly storage for information that many Hogwarts students view as rubbish.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=21" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 162px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/af/Cuthbert_Binns.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20100727195951" class="image"><img alt="" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" decoding="async" width="160" height="227" class="thumbimage lazyload" data-image-name="Cuthbert Binns.jpg" data-image-key="Cuthbert_Binns.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/af/Cuthbert_Binns.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20100727195951" /></a> 	<noscript><a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/af/Cuthbert_Binns.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20100727195951" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/af/Cuthbert_Binns.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20100727195951" decoding="async" width="160" height="227" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="Cuthbert Binns.jpg" data-image-key="Cuthbert_Binns.jpg" data-src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/a/af/Cuthbert_Binns.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20100727195951" /></a></noscript> 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:Cuthbert_Binns.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Professor Binns in <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<ul><li>Professor Binns does assign homework and essays, though as he is a ghost it's unknown how he is able to mark them. Given this, it is possible he has a student assistant, like the ones in <a href="/wiki/Music_classroom_monitor" title="Music classroom monitor">Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Male_Muggle_Studies_teacher%27s_student_assistant" title="Male Muggle Studies teacher&#39;s student assistant">Muggle Studies</a>. Alternatively, he might be in possession of a quill that he can tell what to write and therefore does not need to actually touch it to grade papers.</li>
<li>Binns has not appeared in any of the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">films</a> nor the PC games. His explanation in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i> of the legend of the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Chamber of Secrets">Chamber of Secrets</a> is instead given by <a href="/wiki/Minerva_McGonagall" title="Minerva McGonagall">Professor McGonagall</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (film)">film</a> and <a href="/wiki/Filius_Flitwick" title="Filius Flitwick">Professor Flitwick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets_(PC,_Mac)" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (PC, Mac)">PC game</a>.</li>
<li>In the videogame <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (video game)">Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban</a></i>, there is one <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a> classroom featured on the Fourth Floor corridor. Professor Binns, however, is never seen in the game.</li>
<li>The only video games Professor Binns has made an appearance (so far) are the Game Boy Color adaptations, <i><a href="/wiki/LEGO_Harry_Potter:_Years_1-4" title="LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4">LEGO Harry Potter: Years 1-4</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_Hogwarts_Mystery" title="Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery">Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery</a></i>, although he was mentioned in the <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(video_game)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (video game)">Order of the Phoenix</a></i> video game. On the Game Boy Color versions Binns has a tendency to fall asleep during lectures and to forget about things.</li>
<li>Professor Binns' first name only appeared on a list of the Hogwarts professors which was released on <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling%27s_official_site" title="J. K. Rowling&#39;s official site">J. K. Rowling's official site</a>.</li>
<li>Professor Binns is seen in the book series as a stereotyped old man; he forgets things seconds after hearing them, has a slow droning voice, and gets irritable at times.</li>
<li>As Binns became a <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghost</a>, he can no longer move beyond the <a href="/wiki/Veil" title="Veil">veil</a>, and will remain earthbound for all eternity.<sup id="cite_ref-OOTP38_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OOTP38-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> This means that unless he resigns or is sacked, he would be the History of Magic teacher forever.</li>
<li>Since Binns died in the <a href="/wiki/Staffroom" title="Staffroom">staffroom</a>, and continued on as if nothing happened, it can be assumed that he feared <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">death</a> (or what lies beyond the <a href="/wiki/Veil" title="Veil">veil</a>). Perhaps teaching was what Binns wanted to do, for literally all eternity.</li>
<li>In "Harry Potter and the Forbidden Journey" ride in Orlando Florida, Dumbledore mentions that Professor Binns will be giving the audience a lecture entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Through_the_Centuries" title="Hogwarts Through the Centuries">Hogwarts Through the Centuries</a></i> and that it will be "...long". Harry, Ron and Hermione sneak the audience out of the <a href="/wiki/Classroom_3C" title="Classroom 3C">Defence Against the Dark Arts Classroom</a> before Professor Binns shows up.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Chamber_of_Secrets" title="Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets">Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets</a></i>, Binns states, "<i>There is not a shred of evidence that Slytherin ever built so much as a secret broom cupboard!</i>" Ironically enough, there <i>is</i> a secret broom cupboard in <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Fred_and_George_Weasley" title="Fred and George Weasley">Weasley twins</a> told Harry that when they were running away from <a href="/wiki/Argus_Filch" title="Argus Filch">Filch</a> once, they took refuge in a broom cupboard, which was later revealed to be the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>.</li>
<li>According to the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Prisoner_of_Azkaban_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)">Prisoner of Azkaban</a> transcript on thepensieve.org, Professor Binns makes an appearance in the film, as a ghost who reports to Dumbledore shortly after "The Flight of the <a href="/wiki/Fat_Lady" title="Fat Lady">Fat Lady</a>." However, if this is true, none of Binns' more memorable traits are portrayed in the films.</li>
<li>Binns' recollection of his father strongly suggests that not only did he pick up his droning style of speech from him, but that like his own students, it put Binns himself to sleep from boredom.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/1989%E2%80%931990_school_year" title="1989–1990 school year">1989–1990 school year</a> as seen in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter:_Hogwarts_Mystery" title="Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery">Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery</a></i>, Binns asked his Sixth-Year students (aged 16 or 17) whether they have served in the First World War, despite that one must be 18 to enlist (although many boys in real life have served by lying about their age). This would indicate that Binns had been teaching since at least 1918, and his memory had not moved on since then.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span id="Author's_comments"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Author.27s_comments">Author's comments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?veaction=edit&amp;section=22" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns?action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Author&#039;s comments"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<i>The most productive ghost at Hogwarts is, of course, Professor Binns, the old History of Magic teacher who fell asleep in front of the staff-room fire one day and simply got up to give his next class, leaving his body behind. There is some debate as to whether or not Professor Binns realises he is dead. While his entrance to lessons through the blackboard is vaguely amusing the first time students see it, he is not the most stimulating teacher.</i>
</p><p><i>The inspiration for Professor Binns was an old professor at my university, who gave every lecture with his eyes closed, rocking backwards and forwards slightly on his toes. While he was a brilliant man, who disgorged an immense amount of valuable information at every lecture, his disconnect with his students was total. Professor Binns is only dimly aware of his living students, and is astonished when they begin asking him questions.</i>"
</p>
