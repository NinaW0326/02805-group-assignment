<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Matagot?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Matagot?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, they are used by the <a href="/wiki/French_Ministry_of_Magic" title="French Ministry of Magic">French Ministry of Magic</a> for handiwork, such as handling the correspondence and security of some departments. Normally, they are harmless, but when provoked, they turn into very ferocious creatures.<sup id="cite_ref-mugglenet_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mugglenet-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Matagots appear to be resistant to most forms of magic and <a href="/wiki/Spell" title="Spell">spells</a>, and can multiply themselves upon being hit with offensive spells, such as the <a href="/wiki/Stunning_Spell" title="Stunning Spell">Stunning Spell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Upon exiting the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>, Matagots will turn into ordinary black cats.<sup id="cite_ref-COGS_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COGS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Matagot?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Matagot?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 182px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/79/MatagotPOP.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20190607170957" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/7/79/MatagotPOP.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20190607170957" decoding="async" width="180" height="180" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="MatagotPOP.jpg" data-image-key="MatagotPOP.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:MatagotPOP.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">A Matagot as a <a href="/wiki/POP!_Vinyl" title="POP! Vinyl">POP! Vinyl</a></p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<ul><li>A <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matagot" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Matagot">Matagot</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Spirit" title="Spirit">spirit</a> in French folklore that tends to take the form of a black cat. While many Matagots are evil in nature, some can be helpful to humans. If a "wealth-bringing" Matagot is allowed to eat and drink first at every meal, it will give its owner a solid gold coin each morning.</li>
<li>The Matagot closely resembles the specially-bred Sphynx breed of domestic cat, known for its lack of coat (fur).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Newton_Scamander" title="Newton Scamander">Newt Scamander</a> mentions that they are spirit familiars, which is a subject of much debate and controversy in the fandom. This is because <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a> has previously stated that <i>"familiars, in the strictest sense, do not exist in the world of Harry Potter"</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> after years of fans debating whether or not <a href="/wiki/Hedwig" title="Hedwig">Hedwig</a> was <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s spirit familiar.
<ul><li>It is worth noting that the exact phrasing suggests that something one could call a familiar may exist, but nothing that rigidly adheres to the archetype of such a thing.</li></ul></li></ul>
