<b>Walter Parkin</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/1422" class="mw-redirect" title="1422">1422</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> and butcher. In <a href="/wiki/1422" class="mw-redirect" title="1422">1422</a>, his seven children - four sons and three daughters - formed the <a href="/wiki/Wigtown_Wanderers" title="Wigtown Wanderers">Wigtown Wanderers</a> <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> <a href="/wiki/Quidditch_team" title="Quidditch team">team</a>. Walter often attended matches holding his wand in one hand, and a <a href="/wiki/Meat_cleaver" title="Meat cleaver">meat cleaver</a> in the other. Opposing teams were often intimidated by his presence on the sidelines.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Walter_Parkin?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Walter_Parkin?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Walter" comes from a Germanic name meaning "ruler of the army", composed of the elements <i>wald</i> "rule" and <i>hari</i> "army". The Normans brought it to England, where it replaced the Old English cognate <i>Wealdhere</i>. A famous bearer of the name was Sir Walter Scott (1771-1832), a Scottish novelist who wrote 'Ivanhoe' and other notable works.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Ruler of the Army" probably being a reference to the "army of children" that made up the Wigtown Wanders team.
</p>
