<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Felicity_Eastchurch?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Felicity_Eastchurch?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1996" title="1996">1996</a>, she took a handbag belonging to <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lovegood" title="Luna Lovegood">Luna Lovegood</a>, intending to hide it from her as a prank. Luna wanted to take this handbag to <a href="/wiki/Horace_Slughorn" title="Horace Slughorn">Professor Slughorn's</a> <a href="/wiki/Slug_Club_Christmas_party" title="Slug Club Christmas party">Christmas party</a> as part of her outfit. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> had to give Felicity three <a href="/wiki/Stink_Pellets" title="Stink Pellets">Stink Pellets</a> and six <a href="/wiki/Wizard%27s_Chess" title="Wizard&#39;s Chess">chess</a> pieces to get her to return the handbag. She also competed in the school <a href="/wiki/Exploding_Snap" title="Exploding Snap">Exploding Snap</a> tournament during that year.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Felicity_Eastchurch?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Felicity_Eastchurch?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Felicity" is from the English word <i>felicity</i> meaning "happiness", which ultimately derives from Latin <i>felicitas</i> "good luck". This was one of the virtue names adopted by the Puritans around the <a href="/wiki/17th_century" title="17th century">17th century</a>.
</p>
