<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Caspar_Crouch%27s_son?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Caspar_Crouch%27s_son?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>It is possible that Caspar Crouch’s son was <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Senior" title="Bartemius Crouch Senior">Bartemius Crouch Snr</a>, who is described as an "elderly man" at the <a href="/wiki/1994_Quidditch_World_Cup" title="1994 Quidditch World Cup">1994 Quidditch World Cup</a>. This is supported by the fact that he and <a href="/wiki/Bartemius_Crouch_Junior" title="Bartemius Crouch Junior">his son</a> were the last of their family. However, Caspar Crouch’s wife, <a href="/wiki/Charis_Black" title="Charis Black">Charis</a>, was born in <a href="/wiki/1919" title="1919">1919</a>, so her son could be born no earlier than <a href="/wiki/1936" title="1936">1936</a>, which makes it unlikely that he would be described as "elderly" in <a href="/wiki/1994" title="1994">1994</a>. But Rowling wrote all but the first chapter of <i>Goblet of Fire</i> in the third-person limited point-of-view, which means that the description of Crouch as "elderly" actually represents <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter’s</a> perception. A 58-year-old man might appear "elderly" to a 14-year-old boy, and it is also quite plausible that the tribulations Crouch went through during the First War would have prematurely aged him. Also, terms like "elderly" can be loosely used, without kind of mathematical rigour, especially by young people; for instance, <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black" title="Walburga Black">Walburga Black</a> in <a href="/wiki/Walburga_Black%27s_portrait" title="Walburga Black&#39;s portrait">her portrait</a> was described as "old", despite not being more than sixty-years-old when the portrait was made<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>. It should also be taken into account that Rowling's maths on the Black family tree are characteristically bad; <a href="/wiki/Pollux_Black" title="Pollux Black">two</a> <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_Black_III" title="Cygnus Black III">people</a> become fathers at the age of thirteen. Charis and her son might have been intended to be older than they appear to be.</li>
<li>If Caspar Crouch's son is Barty Crouch Snr, then Barty Crouch and Arthur Weasley are first cousins and Crouch's personal assistant Percy Weasley is his first cousin once removed. This relationship is not mentioned at all in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire">Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire</a></i>, though neither Crouch nor Arthur would be proud to admit that their mothers were daughters of the notorious <a href="/wiki/House_of_Black" title="House of Black">House of Black</a>.</li></ul>
