<b>Heliotrope Willis</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> who lead the activist group <a href="/wiki/Troll_Rights_Movement" title="Troll Rights Movement">Troll Rights Movement</a><sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>. She claimed <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">trolls</a> have a non-violent nature and make excellent <a href="/wiki/Security_Troll" class="mw-redirect" title="Security Troll">guards</a>, but, when her group crashed an anti-troll rally, she was knocked unconscious by their indiscriminate club-swinging<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Willis?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Heliotrope_Willis?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliotropium" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Heliotropium">Heliotrope</a> is a term used to describe a plant which turns to face the sun, derived from the Greek <i>helios</i>, "sun", and <i>tropein</i>, "to turn."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_of_flowers" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Language of flowers">language of flowers</a>, heliotropes represent devotion.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Miss Heliotrope is a character in <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Little_White_Horse" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Little White Horse">The Little White Horse</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/1946" title="1946">1946</a> novel by {{w|Elizabeth Goudge|Elizabeth Goudge]], which was a childhood favourite of <a href="/wiki/J._K._Rowling" title="J. K. Rowling">J. K. Rowling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The surname Willis is derived from the given name William,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which means "will helmet", from the Germanic elements <i>wil</i>, "will," and <i>helm</i>, "helmet."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
