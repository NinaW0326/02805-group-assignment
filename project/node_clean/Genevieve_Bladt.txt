<p><b>Genevieve Bladt</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> and one of the <a href="/wiki/London_Five" title="London Five">London Five</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_trailer_img_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU_trailer_img-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Genevieve_Bladt?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Genevieve_Bladt?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She mysteriously vanished sometime during the mid-to-late <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>. Her disappearance occurred within a few hours of the other four <a href="/wiki/Auror" title="Auror">Aurors</a>. It was the first instance of people having gone mysteriously missing since the <a href="/wiki/Second_Wizarding_War" title="Second Wizarding War">Second Wizarding War</a>, and hence came as a great shock to the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizarding community</a> of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> at large. An investigation was launched by the <a href="/wiki/British_Ministry_of_Magic" title="British Ministry of Magic">British Ministry of Magic</a> to try and locate Bladt and their fellow victims. As time passed, however, the Ministry met a dead end when the trail went cold, and it was generally felt by many that the chance of finding Bladt grew increasingly smaller, causing the despair of their family to increase and turn into outrage when the case was closed. As <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Greengrass" title="Gareth Greengrass">Gareth Greengrass</a> indeed speculated, it is very likely that Bladt was deceased, as of <a href="/wiki/2018" title="2018">2018</a>, though there was a small chance they were still alive.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_trailer_img_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU_trailer_img-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By at least the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Calamity" title="Calamity">Calamity</a>, volunteer members of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Secrecy_Task_Force" title="Statute of Secrecy Task Force">Statute of Secrecy Task Force</a> investigated more into the Genevieve's life. They suspected her work with artefacts was related to her disappearance, but they didn't know why.<sup id="cite_ref-WU_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WU-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
