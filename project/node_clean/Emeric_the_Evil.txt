<p><b>Emeric the Evil</b> was a short-lived but exceptionally aggressive <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a>. He was once the master of the <a href="/wiki/Elder_Wand" title="Elder Wand">Elder Wand</a>. With it, he terrorised the south of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> in the early <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>. He was "<i>slaughtered</i>" in a ferocious <a href="/wiki/Duel_Between_Emeric_the_Evil_and_Egbert_the_Egregious" class="mw-redirect" title="Duel Between Emeric the Evil and Egbert the Egregious">duel</a> against <a href="/wiki/Egbert_the_Egregious" title="Egbert the Egregious">Egbert the Egregious</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During their <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a> lessons with <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns" title="Cuthbert Binns">Professor Binns</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> students often mixed up Emeric the Evil with <a href="/wiki/Uric_the_Oddball" title="Uric the Oddball">Uric the Oddball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Emeric_the_Evil?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Emeric_the_Evil?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Emeric" is a Germanic name, in which the second element is <i>ric</i> meaning "power". The first element may be <i>ermen</i> "whole, universal" (making it a relative of <i>Ermenrich</i>), <i>amal</i> "work, labour" (making it a relative of <i>Amalric</i>) or <i>heim</i> "home" (making it a relative of <i>Henry</i>). It is likely that several forms merged into a single name.
</p>
