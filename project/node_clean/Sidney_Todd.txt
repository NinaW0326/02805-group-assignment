<b>Sidney Todd</b> (born c. <a href="/wiki/1916" title="1916">1916</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a> fan who supported the <a href="/wiki/Montrose_Magpies" title="Montrose Magpies">Montrose Magpies</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1999" title="1999">1999</a>, at the age of eighty-three, he was quoted by the Daily Prophet supporting team manager <a href="/wiki/Cormack_McLeod" title="Cormack McLeod">Cormack McLeod</a>'s decision to let <a href="/wiki/Chaser" title="Chaser">Chaser</a> <a href="/wiki/Alasdair_Maddock" title="Alasdair Maddock">Alasdair Maddock</a> go<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> after he was repeatedly caught trying out <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> sports.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sidney_Todd?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sidney_Todd?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Sidney" comes from an English surname which was originally derived from various place names in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> meaning "wide island", from Old English sid "wide" and eg "island". Another theory holds that the surname comes from the name of town in Normandy called "Saint Denis". It was first used as a given name in honour of executed politician Algernon Sidney (1622-1683). Another notable bearer of the surname was the poet and statesman Sir Philip Sidney (1554-1586).
</p>
