<b>Lady Carmilla Sanguina</b> (<a href="/wiki/1561" class="mw-redirect" title="1561">1561</a>-<a href="/wiki/1757" class="mw-redirect" title="1757">1757</a>) was an infamous <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a> who bathed in the <a href="/wiki/Blood" title="Blood">blood</a> of her victims, believing it would retain her youth and beauty.<sup id="cite_ref-POAG_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-POAG-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Carmilla_Sanguina?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Carmilla_Sanguina?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The surname Sanguina is derived from the Latin <i>sanguineus</i>, which means "of blood," "bloody," or "bloodthirsty."</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Carmilla_Sanguina?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Carmilla_Sanguina?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Lady Sanguina may have been been inspired by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elizabeth_B%C3%A1thory" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Elizabeth Báthory">Erzsébet Báthory</a>, a 16th-century Hungarian countess who was accused of killing young women, then bathing in their blood.</li>
<li><i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carmilla" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Carmilla">Carmilla</a></i> is a Gothic 19th-century novel written by Sheridan Lefanu and starring a female vampire with that name. The story has been adapted to cinema and comic-books, with the character (or similar characters with the same name) appearing in other franchises.</li></ul>
