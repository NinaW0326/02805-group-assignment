<b>Ollivander</b> (fl. <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C.</a>) was the <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who arrived at <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a> from, apparently, a Mediterranean country, and set up <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders</a> in <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C.</a>. An expert <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmaker</a>, Ollivander sold to ancient British wizards whose wands were crude of construction and unreliable in performance.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ollivander was apparently an ancient Roman wizard. Based on the etymology of his name, which is said to mean "he who owns the olive wand", he may have been brought up in a <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a> country (olive trees not being native to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_a_wandmaker">Career as a wandmaker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career as a wandmaker"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It is believed by <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>, a descendant of this individual, that the founder of <a href="/wiki/Ollivanders" title="Ollivanders">Ollivanders</a> arrived with the Romans, setting up a stall (subsequently a shop) to manufacture and sell <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> to the ancient British wizards, whose wands were crudely-made and inferior in quality and performance.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ollivander_(382_B.C._era)?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Despite <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Ollivander" title="Garrick Ollivander">Garrick Ollivander</a>'s claims that the original Ollivander arrived to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> with the Romans, this individual started his wand-making business in <a href="/wiki/B.C._era" title="B.C. era">382 B.C.</a> but the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_conquest_of_Britain" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Roman conquest of Britain">Roman conquest of Britain</a> (<i>Britannia</i>) only began effectively in <a href="/wiki/First_millennium_A.D." title="First millennium A.D.">A.D. 43</a>, under Emperor Claudius.  An earlier attempt was made in 55-54 B.C. by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julius_Caesar" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>, as an adjunct of his Gallic campaign.  Prior to then, the Romans had only sporadic contact with Britain, to the point where, up until 124 B.C., many Romans did not believe the land actually existed.</li></ul>
