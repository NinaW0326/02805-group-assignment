<p><b>Ravenclaw's Diadem</b><sup id="cite_ref-HPLE_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HPLE-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (also known as the <b>Lost Diadem of Ravenclaw</b>) was the only known relic once belonging to <a href="/wiki/Rowena_Ravenclaw" title="Rowena Ravenclaw">Rowena Ravenclaw</a>, the founder of <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw" title="Ravenclaw">Ravenclaw House</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a>. Etched upon its surface was Ravenclaw's famous quote: <i>"Wit beyond measure is man's greatest treasure."</i>
</p><p>It was said to enhance the wisdom of its wearer, which is Ravenclaw House's most treasured attribute. It had a blue sapphire shaped like an oval. The diadem was later stolen by her daughter, <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena</a>, a fact Rowena kept a secret from the other founders 'till her death bed.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rowena Ravenclaw's Diadem, <a href="/wiki/Helga_Hufflepuff%27s_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Helga Hufflepuff&#39;s Cup">Helga Hufflepuff's Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">Salazar Slytherin's Locket</a>, made up three of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rowena_Ravenclaw">Rowena Ravenclaw</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Rowena Ravenclaw">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rowena Ravenclaw"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 152px"> 	<a href="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/4a/PR_007_007-e1313269741697.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20140615151812" class="image"><img alt="" src="https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/harrypotter/images/4/4a/PR_007_007-e1313269741697.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/150?cb=20140615151812" decoding="async" width="150" height="254" class="thumbimage" data-image-name="PR 007 007-e1313269741697.jpg" data-image-key="PR_007_007-e1313269741697.jpg" /></a> 	 	<figcaption class="thumbcaption"> 		 			<a href="/wiki/File:PR_007_007-e1313269741697.jpg" class="info-icon"><svg><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-info-small"></use></svg></a> 		 		 		 			<p class="caption">Rowena Ravenclaw with her diadem</p> 		 	</figcaption> </figure>
<p>As Rowena Ravenclaw was a very intelligent and powerful <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, it is most likely she <a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">enchanted</a> the diadem herself, charming the object to increase the wearer's intelligence. Rowena died shortly after her daughter, Helena, stole the diadem.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Helena_Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?veaction=edit&amp;section=3" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Helena Ravenclaw">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Helena Ravenclaw"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Main article:&#160;<a href="/wiki/Theft_of_Rowena_Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem" title="Theft of Rowena Ravenclaw&#39;s Diadem">Theft of Rowena Ravenclaw's Diadem</a></i>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a>, Rowena's daughter, greatly envied the attention her mother received. She stole her mother's diadem, in hope of using its power to make herself wiser, and fled Hogwarts. Her mother fell fatally ill and in spite of Helena's betrayal, wanted to see her daughter one last time. Rowena sent the <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Baron" title="Bloody Baron">Bloody Baron</a>, a man who once loved Helena, to find her. When he caught up with her in a forest in Albania (the same forest <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> would use as a hiding place during his exile), she hid the diadem in a hollow tree. Upon her refusal to return with him, the Baron stabbed her; horrified by what he had just done, the Baron then stabbed himself as well. The diadem was later found to be lost (hence the name "the lost diadem"), and was sought after by students for hundreds of years.<sup id="cite_ref-DH29_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH29-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tom_Riddle">Tom Riddle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?veaction=edit&amp;section=4" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Tom Riddle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tom Riddle"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>While the diadem bestows wisdom, I doubt that it would greatly increase your chances of defeating the wizard who calls himself Lord –’</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;The ghost of <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a> to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div>
<p>Centuries later, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Tom Marvolo Riddle</a> managed to charm the story out of Helena's spirit, then known as the <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Grey Lady</a>, ghost of the Ravenclaw house. Upon learning of the diadem's location, Riddle travelled to the Albanian forest shortly after he left school and acquired the diadem. He transformed it into his fifth&#160;<a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcrux</a> by <a href="/wiki/Killing_Curse" title="Killing Curse">killing</a> an <a href="/wiki/Albanian_peasant" title="Albanian peasant">Albanian peasant</a>, and later chose to return it to the very place it had originated from: Hogwarts.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Riddle was arrogant enough to believe that he alone had penetrated Hogwarts' most mysterious secrets, and thus he believed that only he had discovered the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>, where he intended to hide the diadem. The Room's manifestation was none other than the "Room of Hidden Things" inside which Harry Potter would hide the Half-Blood Prince's copy of "Advanced Potion-Making" many decades later. Riddle chose to hide the diadem in this room the night he returned to the castle to request a position to teach Defence Against the Dark Arts. Unlike the other Horcruxes, Riddle did not put up heavy magical protections for the diadem, due to his lack of time to do so, along with his arrogance.<sup id="cite_ref-DH29_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH29-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harry_Potter">Harry Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?veaction=edit&amp;section=5" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Harry Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw%27s_Diadem?action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Harry Potter"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The diadem remained undisturbed in the "Room of Hidden Things" for many decades, until it was sighted by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> during his sixth year as a "discoloured old tiara", though he did not realise what it was, grabbing it and placing on top of a chipped bust of an ugly old warlock as a means of landmarking where he had placed his <a href="/wiki/Severus_Snape%27s_copy_of_Advanced_Potion-Making" title="Severus Snape&#39;s copy of Advanced Potion-Making">Potions book</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBP24_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBP24-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, accompanied by Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, Harry returned to Hogwarts in search of an object belonging to Ravenclaw, having learned that through the mental connection he held with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a> that it was hidden at the school. He was told of her lost diadem by several Ravenclaw members of <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore%27s_Army" title="Dumbledore&#39;s Army">Dumbledore's Army</a>, and visited <a href="/wiki/Ravenclaw_Tower" title="Ravenclaw Tower">Ravenclaw Tower</a> with Luna Lovegood to see the statue of Rowena Ravenclaw wearing her diadem. Later, Harry managed to coax the story of the diadem's theft out of the Grey Lady before figuring out exactly where it was hidden.<sup id="cite_ref-DH29_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH29-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>He was confronted by <a href="/wiki/Draco_Malfoy" title="Draco Malfoy">Draco Malfoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a> before he could reach the diadem, and a battle ensued within the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a> (in its form as the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement#Room_of_Hidden_Things" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Hidden Things</a>), resulting in Crabbe's creation of <a href="/wiki/Fiendfyre" title="Fiendfyre">Fiendfyre</a> that then went out of his control and resulted in his death. Harry was able to acquire the diadem, but was saved the trouble of having to destroy it, since the Fiendfyre had accidentally incinerated it. The diadem crumbled to pieces in his hands upon their escape from the <a href="/wiki/Room_of_Requirement" title="Room of Requirement">Room of Requirement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH31_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH31-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<ul><li>In the books, Voldemort is unaware when one of his Horcruxes is destroyed; in the films, he "feels" when one is destroyed, which triggers physically painful visions of Voldemort's thoughts for Harry (as Harry is a proto-Horcrux/is host to a part of Voldemort's soul); this is how Harry is able to deduce that one of the Horcruxes is tied to Ravenclaw, since Voldemort's thoughts about the castle and Rowena Ravenclaw/[the safety of] the Ravenclaw artefact transferred to him.</li>
<li>In the novel, the diadem is inadvertently destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Crabbe" title="Vincent Crabbe">Vincent Crabbe</a>'s Fiendfyre inside the Room of Requirement. In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, Harry stabs the diadem with a basilisk fang and Ron kicks it to the Fiendfyre (then created by <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Goyle" title="Gregory Goyle">Gregory Goyle</a>). Right before the door of the room closes, Voldemort's face appears in the fire from the part of his soul in the diadem coming free and perishing.</li>
<li>Since the diadem was a piece of jewellery, it is highly likely Voldemort put curses on the diadem after turning it into a Horcrux that would affect anyone who tried to wear it, similar to the curses and side effects of wearing either the <a href="/wiki/Marvolo_Gaunt%27s_Ring" title="Marvolo Gaunt&#39;s Ring">ring</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Salazar_Slytherin%27s_Locket" class="mw-redirect" title="Salazar Slytherin&#39;s Locket">locket</a>.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, the Grey Lady <a href="/wiki/Helena_Ravenclaw" title="Helena Ravenclaw">Helena Ravenclaw</a> calls the Diadem an abomination due to it being tainted by the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Magic</a> of a Horcrux. Indeed, when Harry finds it, rather than making the chittering violent noises of the others it makes a wailing noise as if the Light and dark forces inside of it are not sitting well together. A woman's voice can also be heard to say <i>"Here she comes."</i> from it.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2</a></i>, Tom Riddle had falsely promised Helena that he would destroy the diadem if she told him its whereabouts, but instead turned it into a Horcrux and hid it inside the Room of Hidden Things. Helena would come to know and regret all of that, something she did not express in the novel.</li></ul>
