<b>Marius</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard</a> who was assigned to <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Guards" title="Gringotts Guards">guard the entrance</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank" title="Gringotts Wizarding Bank">Gringotts Wizarding Bank</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Death_Eaters" title="Death Eaters">Death Eaters</a>' reign over the <a href="/wiki/Wizarding_world" title="Wizarding world">wizarding world</a>. He and <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Gringotts_guard_(I)" title="Unidentified Gringotts guard (I)">another guard</a> used <a href="/wiki/Probity_Probe" title="Probity Probe">Probity Probes</a> to search anyone attempting to enter. Both guards were <a href="/wiki/Confundus_Charm" title="Confundus Charm">Confunded</a> by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> on <a href="/wiki/1_May" title="1 May">1 May</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a> when he and his friends <a href="/wiki/Break-in_of_Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank_(1998)" title="Break-in of Gringotts Wizarding Bank (1998)">broke into the bank</a> to steal one of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Voldemort</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Horcrux" title="Horcrux">Horcruxes</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Lestrange_Vault" title="Lestrange Vault">Lestrange Vault</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Marius?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Marius?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Marius" was a Roman family name which was derived either from Mars, the name of the Roman god of War, or else from the Latin root mas, maris meaning "male". Gaius Marius was a famous Roman consul of the 2nd century BC.
</p>
