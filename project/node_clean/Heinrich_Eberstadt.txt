<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Eberstadt?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Eberstadt?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He participated in an emergency meeting alongside fellow members of the Confederation in <a href="/wiki/New_York" title="New York">New York</a> in <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> to discuss the potential exposure of the wizarding world.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Magical_Congress_of_the_United_States_of_America" title="President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America">President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seraphina_Picquery" title="Seraphina Picquery">Seraphina Picquery</a>, rebuked his criticisms, noting that she would not be lectured by the man who let <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> slip through his fingers.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
