<p><b>Professor Bathilda Bagshot</b> (d. <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> historian and the author of <i><a href="/wiki/A_History_of_Magic" title="A History of Magic">A History of Magic</a></i> and approximately <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot%27s_books" title="Bathilda Bagshot&#39;s books">ten other books</a>. <i>A History of Magic</i> is used in the <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry</a> <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">class</a> of the same name, taught by <a href="/wiki/Cuthbert_Binns" title="Cuthbert Binns">Cuthbert Binns</a>. She lived in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a>, and was murdered around <a href="/wiki/December" title="December">December</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, after which her corpse was animated by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a> to contain his pet <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snake</a> <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>.
</p><p>Bathilda was also the great-aunt of <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, the infamous <a href="/wiki/Dark_Wizard" title="Dark Wizard">Dark Wizard</a> who was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Professor Bagshot was born to the wizarding <a href="/wiki/Bagshot_family" title="Bagshot family">Bagshot family</a> sometime in the early or mid <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">nineteenth century</a>.
</p>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1890s" title="1890s">1890s</a>, Bagshot was already an adult living in <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot%27s_home" title="Bathilda Bagshot&#39;s home">her house</a> in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Kendra_Dumbledore" title="Kendra Dumbledore">Kendra Dumbledore</a> moved there from <a href="/wiki/Mould-on-the-Wold" title="Mould-on-the-Wold">Mould-on-the-Wold</a> along with her young children <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberforth_Dumbledore" title="Aberforth Dumbledore">Aberforth</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ariana_Dumbledore" title="Ariana Dumbledore">Ariana</a>, after her husband <a href="/wiki/Percival_Dumbledore" title="Percival Dumbledore">Percival</a>'s well-publicised arrest and imprisonment in <a href="/wiki/Azkaban" title="Azkaban">Azkaban</a>. Bathilda tried to befriend Kendra after they arrived, but she was rebuffed.<sup id="cite_ref-DH11_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH11-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Within the next several years, Bathilda sent an owl to Albus Dumbledore, then a <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> student, having been favourably impressed by his paper on <a href="/wiki/Cross-Species_Transfiguration" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross-Species Transfiguration">trans-species Transfiguration</a> for the scholarly journal <i><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_Today" title="Transfiguration Today">Transfiguration Today</a></i>. This initial contact led to her being acquainted with the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Dumbledore_family" title="Dumbledore family">Dumbledore family</a>. At the time of Kendra's death, Bathilda was the only person in Godric's Hollow who was on speaking terms with her.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In the summer of <a href="/wiki/1899" class="mw-redirect" title="1899">1899</a>, after Albus Dumbledore graduated from Hogwarts, an orphan and head of the family, Bathilda's great-nephew, <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a>, came to live with her and she made a point of introducing Grindelwald to Albus. The two talented young men became friends.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is unknown how much or what Bathilda knew of her nephew's actions concerning Aberforth and Ariana Dumbledore, but she witnessed the <a href="/wiki/Muggle_duelling" title="Muggle duelling">fist-fight</a> that broke out between the Dumbledore brothers at Ariana's funeral.<sup id="cite_ref-DH8_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH8-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1947" title="1947">1947</a>, Bathilda's work on wizarding history was published under the title <i>A History of Magic</i> by <a href="/wiki/Little_Red_Books" title="Little Red Books">Little Red Books</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FB_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It was presumably around this time she authored her <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot%27s_books" title="Bathilda Bagshot&#39;s books">other works on magical history</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt1_DVD2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt1_DVD2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> She was deemed the most celebrated magical historian of the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">twentieth century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt1_7_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt1_7-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bathilda may have been a teacher at some point, possibly at Hogwarts, as <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Granger" title="Hermione Granger">Hermione Granger</a> referred to her as "Professor Bagshot" when referencing her work.<sup id="cite_ref-DH16_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH16-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</figure><p>In <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a> (after the death of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>) Bathilda was interviewed by Rita Skeeter through the use of <a href="/wiki/Veritaserum" title="Veritaserum">Veritaserum</a>. Rita then used the material she gathered from Bathilda in her only semi-true biography <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is implied that Skeeter took advantage of Bathilda's poor mental state to twist her material to be more sensationalist. She even referred to Bathilda as being "batty".
</p><p>Also while in Bathilda's cottage she took several of her pictures and letters. For example an image of Dumbledore and Grindlewald together. And one letter she stole was a correspondence from Dumbledore to <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Grindlewald</a>, regarding their ambition to obtain the <a href="/wiki/Deathly_Hallows" title="Deathly Hallows">Deathly Hallows</a> and their plan to start a global revolution. This global revolution would lead <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizardkind</a> out of hiding and cause Muggle servitude. Bathilda's pictures and letters caused tension in the wizarding world, especially among those whom were Dumbledore supporters. Harry Potter was one of the ones most affected by it.
</p>

<p>Sometime after her interview with Rita Skeeter, Bathilda was murdered during the height of the Second Wizarding War. She was murdered through the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Arts" title="Dark Arts">Dark Arts</a><sup id="cite_ref-DH22_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH22-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and her body was animated by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Riddle" title="Tom Riddle">Lord Voldemort</a>. It is possible that Bathilda's body could technically be considered as an Inferi. Voldemort did this using his snake, <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a>. He left Nagini in her cottage as a trap, reasoning that <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> may visit Godric's Hollow to see his parents' grave and their destroyed home, since he was no longer at Hogwarts and had never before had the opportunity to visit Godricâ€™s Hollow.

After determining Harry's identity, Nagini leapt from Bathilda's body to <a href="/wiki/Attack_at_Godric%27s_Hollow_(1997)" title="Attack at Godric&#39;s Hollow (1997)">attack him</a>, leaving the old woman for good. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_appearance">Physical appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?veaction=edit&amp;section=8" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Physical appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Now that he was beside her, Harry realised how tiny she was; bowed down with age, she came barely level with his chest. She closed the door behind them, her knuckles blue and mottled against the peeling paint... Her eyes were thick with cataracts and sunken into folds of transparent skin, and her whole face was dotted with broken veins and liver spots... she unwound a moth-eaten black shawl, revealing a head of scant white hair through which the scalp showed clearly through. </i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Description of her appearance<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>An elderly Professor Bagshot was a tiny woman, her head at the level of seventeen-year-old <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>'s chest. At the time that Harry Potter met her, she was stooped, her eyes were thick with cataracts, and her hair was scant and white, with her scalp clearly visible. Her face had some liver spots and broken veins.<sup id="cite_ref-DH17_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH17-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?veaction=edit&amp;section=9" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">"<i>Unfortunately, the brilliance that Bathilda exhibited earlier in her life has now dimmed. "The fire's lit, but the cauldron's empty," as <a href="/wiki/Ivor_Dillonsby" title="Ivor Dillonsby">Ivor Dillonsby</a> put it to me, or, in <a href="/wiki/Enid_Smeek" title="Enid Smeek">Enid Smeek</a>'s slightly earthier phrase, "She's nutty as squirrel poo.</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Little is known of Bathilda's personality. One can presume she was friendly and amiable, having tried to befriend <a href="/wiki/Kendra_Dumbledore" title="Kendra Dumbledore">Kendra Dumbledore</a> in the 1890s with a batch of homemade <a href="/wiki/Cauldron_Cake" title="Cauldron Cake">Cauldron Cakes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH11_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH11-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She regarded <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> as something like a protÃ©gÃ©<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and was friendly with <a href="/wiki/Lily_J._Potter" title="Lily J. Potter">Lily Potter</a> in the early 1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-DH10_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH10-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She was a brilliant witch and historian in her youth, but during her later years she&#160;was quite possibly becoming senile.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Magical_abilities_and_skills">Magical abilities and skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Magical abilities and skills"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">Historian</a></b>: An authority in <a href="/wiki/History_of_Magic" title="History of Magic">History of Magic</a>, Professor Bagshot was referred to by some as the most celebrated magical historian of the twentieth century,<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt1_7_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt1_7-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> having authored <i><a href="/wiki/A_History_of_Magic" title="A History of Magic">A History of Magic</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-PS5_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PS5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot%27s_books" title="Bathilda Bagshot&#39;s books">ten other books</a> on the subject.<sup id="cite_ref-DHpt1_DVD2_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHpt1_DVD2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a></b>: Professor Bagshot was knowledgeable in transfiguration as she was a reader of the scholarly journal <i><a href="/wiki/Transfiguration_Today" title="Transfiguration Today">Transfiguration Today</a></i> and had enough understanding in the subject to properly assess the quality of one of <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a>'s papers on the subject of <a href="/wiki/Cross-Species_Transfiguration" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross-Species Transfiguration">trans-species Transfiguration</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Charm" title="Charm">Charms</a></b>: Bathilda appeared to be good with charms, as she was able to successfully use the <a href="/wiki/Portus" title="Portus">Portus</a> charm to arrange a <a href="/wiki/Portkey" title="Portkey">Portkey</a> for <a href="/wiki/Gellert_Grindelwald" title="Gellert Grindelwald">Gellert Grindelwald</a> to leave <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH18_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Herbology" title="Herbology">Herbology</a></b>: Bathilda kept and grew <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> plants in her back garden, such as <a href="/wiki/Plangentine" title="Plangentine">Plangentines</a>, and knew what the proper time was to pick them.<sup id="cite_ref-DH11_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH11-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Divination" title="Divination">Divination</a></b>: Bathilda appeared to be skilled and knowledgeable in the art of divination, as she authored two books on the subject, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oracle_of_Palombo" title="The Oracle of Palombo">The Oracle of Palombo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Omens,_Oracles_%26_the_Goat" title="Omens, Oracles &amp; the Goat">Omens, Oracles &amp; the Goat</a></i>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Author" class="mw-redirect" title="Author">Author</a></b>: Bathilda was a highly accomplished and renowned author, having written and published multiple books on a number of different magical subjects.</li>
<li><b>Cooking</b>: Bathilda was able to bake batches of <a href="/wiki/Cauldron_Cake" title="Cauldron Cake">Cauldron Cakes</a> in her youth.<sup id="cite_ref-DH11_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH11-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Possessions">Possessions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Possessions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Possessions"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot%27s_home" title="Bathilda Bagshot&#39;s home">Home</a></b>: Bathilda owned a dwelling in <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a> which served as her home when she was alive. After her death and murder, it fell into disarray and ruin, as no one was around to look after it.</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/The_Mill_on_the_Floss" title="The Mill on the Floss">The Mill on the Floss</a></b></i>: Bathilda owned a copy of this novel written by <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> author <a href="/wiki/George_Eliot" title="George Eliot">George Eliot</a>, which could be found on the bookshelves in her home.</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Shrubs_%26_Trees_for_the_Garden" title="Shrubs &amp; Trees for the Garden">Shrubs &amp; Trees for the Garden</a></b></i>: Bathilda owned a copy of this gardening and floriculture book, which could be found on the shelves in her home.</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></b></i>: Bathilda was personally gifted a copy of this controversial autobiography by its author <a href="/wiki/Rita_Skeeter" title="Rita Skeeter">Rita Skeeter</a>, after Bathilda personally gave her the information necessary to write it (although somewhat unwillingly).</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Plangentine" title="Plangentine">Plangentines</a></b>: Bathilda grew and cultivated this species of <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> plant in her garden.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Bathilda_Bagshot?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>Bathilda</i> is an alternate spelling of the name of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balthild" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Balthild">Saint Balthild</a>, a seventh-century Anglo-Saxon noble and wife of King Clovis II of Burgundy who dedicated her later life to helping the ill and the poor. The name is derived from the Old English <i>Bealdhild</i>, meaning "bold battle".</li>
<li>Bathilda is a German name that means "heroine".</li>
<li>Bagshot is the name of a town in Surrey, England. The name is thought to be derived from a tribe (bacca) and the Anglo-Saxon word for "The place of" (sheatte) meaning "the place of Bacca's tribe" though Bag could also mean badger, "the place of the <a href="/wiki/Badger" title="Badger">Badger</a>", creating a reference to <a href="/wiki/Hufflepuff" title="Hufflepuff">Hufflepuff</a>.</li>
<li>Bathilda's last name, Bagshot, may also be a reference to <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Lord of the Rings">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>, in which the characters Bilbo and Frodo Baggins, and Hamfast and Samwise Gamgee lived on Bagshot Row.</li></ul>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Hazel_Douglas" title="Hazel Douglas">Hazel Douglas</a> portrayed Bathilda Bagshot in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1</a></i>.</li>
<li>It is possible that Bathilda's body was animated as an <a href="/wiki/Inferius" title="Inferius">Inferius</a>, and thus controlled by Voldemort's will. As Inferi are never shown to speak, this would account for why Nagini had to speak for her instead, but this is not necessarily the case because it was stated in the book that <a href="/wiki/Nagini" title="Nagini">Nagini</a> came out of her.</li>
<li>Bathilda was incredibly old when she died, as she was a <i>great</i> aunt of Grindelwald in the <a href="/wiki/1890s" title="1890s">1890s</a>, although her precise date of birth is not mentioned.</li>
<li>A picture by concept artist Peter Mckinstry shows <a href="/wiki/File:BathildaWithAcademics.png" title="File:BathildaWithAcademics.png">a photograph of Bathilda Bagshot and other academics sitting in a classroom at Hogwarts Castle</a>. This seems to be a reference to the famous <a target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener" class="external text" href="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6e/Solvay_conference_1927.jpg">picture of the 1927 Solvay Conference on Quantum Mechanics</a>, as both the clothes and the overall alignment of individuals are similar. In the picture, Bathilda seems to take the place of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marie_Curie" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Marie Curie">Marie Curie</a>: in both pictures they are the only featured women, and they are both in a similar position and in a similar pose.</li>
<li>In a <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Lies_of_Albus_Dumbledore" title="The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore">The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore</a></i> prop for <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows:_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1">the first part of the film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i>, Bathilda Bagshot's name is poorly transcribed as "Batholith". The same book referred to <a href="/wiki/Godric%27s_Hollow" title="Godric&#39;s Hollow">Godric's Hollow</a> as "Godlike's Hollow", <a href="/wiki/Kendra_Dumbledore" title="Kendra Dumbledore">Kendra</a> as "Canard" and <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_School_of_Witchcraft_and_Wizardry" title="Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry">Hogwarts</a> as "Hog warts".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Her name may have been inspired by <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Bagehot" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Walter Bagehot">Walter Bagehot</a>, who, in <a href="/wiki/1867" class="mw-redirect" title="1867">1867</a>, wrote the book that is considered the most important text on the British system of government.&#160; His work <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_English_Constitution" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The English Constitution">The English Constitution</a></i> had nearly the same status in British public schools as <i>A History of Magic</i> did at Hogwarts.</li></ul>
