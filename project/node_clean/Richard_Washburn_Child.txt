<b>Richard Washburn Child</b> (<a href="/wiki/5_August" title="5 August">5 August</a> <a href="/wiki/1881" class="mw-redirect" title="1881">1881</a> â€“ <a href="/wiki/31_January" title="31 January">31 January</a> <a href="/wiki/1935" title="1935">1935</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Muggle" class="mw-redirect" title="Muggle">No-Maj</a> author and diplomat.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He had been appointed as the U.S. ambassador to <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> by <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-chronicle_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-chronicle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/6_December" title="6 December">6 December</a> of that year, he spoke at the plenary session of the <a href="/wiki/Near_East_Peace_Conference" title="Near East Peace Conference">Near East Peace Conference</a>, explaining that the U.S. was "much interested" in observing settlement of the <a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a>, but was not authorised to join committees, sign reports, or vote on decisions.<sup id="cite_ref-fb-chronicle_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fb-chronicle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_the_scenes">Behind the scenes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Richard_Washburn_Child?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Richard_Washburn_Child?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behind the scenes"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Washburn_Child" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Richard Washburn Child">Richard Washburn Child</a> served as the U.S. ambassador to <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> from May <a href="/wiki/1921" title="1921">1921</a> to February <a href="/wiki/1924" title="1924">1924</a> in real life.<sup id="cite_ref-wiki1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wiki1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This makes the reference to him in an article in the <a href="/wiki/File:New_York_Chronicle_-_Monday_Dec_1926_(HQ).png" title="File:New York Chronicle - Monday Dec 1926 (HQ).png">prop issue</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Chronicle" title="New York Chronicle">New York Chronicle</a></i> featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Beasts_and_Where_to_Find_Them_(film)" title="Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)">Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</a></i> anachronistic, as the film is set in <a href="/wiki/December" title="December">December</a> <a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a>, two years after his ambassadorship ended. However, it can be presumed that Child's ambassadorship lasted longer in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_universe" title="Harry Potter universe"><i>Harry Potter</i> universe</a>, as did Harding's presidency.</li>
<li>The text of the article mentioning Child in the prop issue of the <i>New York Chronicle</i> was taken from the real <a href="/wiki/21_November" title="21 November">21 November</a> <a href="/wiki/1922" title="1922">1922</a> issue of <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Evening_World" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Evening World">The Evening World</a></i> and documents an actual historical event (albeit anachronistically).<sup id="cite_ref-eveningworld_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eveningworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
