<b>Shikoba Wolfe</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">wizard or witch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Wolfe, along with <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Jonker" title="Johannes Jonker">Johannes Jonker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thiago_Quintana" title="Thiago Quintana">Thiago Quintana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Violetta_Beauvais" title="Violetta Beauvais">Violetta Beauvais</a>, was one of the four <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmakers</a> who served <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> during the early <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th century</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Shikoba_Wolfe?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Shikoba_Wolfe?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Of <a href="/wiki/Choctaw" title="Choctaw">Chocktaw</a> descent, Wolfe was best known for crafting <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wands</a> that were intricately carved. Wands made by Wolfe contained <a href="/wiki/Thunderbird" title="Thunderbird">Thunderbird</a> <a href="/wiki/Thunderbird_tail_feather" title="Thunderbird tail feather">tail feathers</a> as <a href="/wiki/Wand_core" title="Wand core">cores</a> and were generally believed to be extremely powerful, but difficult to master. They were especially sought after by practitioners of <a href="/wiki/Transfiguration" title="Transfiguration">Transfiguration</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pottermore-1920s_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pottermore-1920s-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
