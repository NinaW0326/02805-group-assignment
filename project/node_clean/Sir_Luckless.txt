<p><b>Sir Luckless</b> is the male protagonist in the <a href="/wiki/Beedle_the_Bard" title="Beedle the Bard">Beedle the Bard</a> story <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">The Fountain of Fair Fortune</a></i>. He is an inept <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a> knight who finds courage and wins the heart of a <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a> named <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1998" title="1998">1998</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> mentioned being told the story of <a href="/wiki/The_Fountain_of_Fair_Fortune" title="The Fountain of Fair Fortune">The Fountain of Fair Fortune</a> by his <a href="/wiki/Molly_Weasley" title="Molly Weasley">mother</a> when he was a child.<sup id="cite_ref-DH21_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH21-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Life">Early Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Early Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early Life"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>...and the knight was dragged off his steed after him</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Sir Luckless<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>Being inept and incompetent, Sir Luckless decided to travel to the Fountain of Fair Fortune among millions of others. As the chink in the wall of the garden open, <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> gets tangled in the armour of the knight, and as the vines pull <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> in, all three <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a> along with the knight get pulled through the wall and into the garden.
</p><p>Since only one of them will be permitted to bathe in the Fountain, the first two witches are upset that <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> inadvertently invited another competitor. Because he has no magical power, he recognises the women as <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witches</a>, and is well-suited to his name, "Sir Luckless," the knight announces his intention to abandon the quest. <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> promptly chides him for giving up and asks him to join their group.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>Sir Luckless attempted to float across the stream on his shield, but it sank</i>"</span></dd>

Sir Luckless tried to float across the stream on his shield but it just sank. <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> thinks to use her <a href="/wiki/Wand" title="Wand">wand</a> to withdraw the happy memories of the lover who abandoned her, and drop them into the <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>. Stepping stones appear in the water, and the four are able to cross to the Fountain, where they must decide who gets to bathe.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="True_Love">True Love</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?veaction=edit&amp;section=7" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: True Love">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: True Love"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>So the knight clanked forth in the last rays of the setting sun, and bathed in the Fountain of Fair Fortune...</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Sir Luckless bathing in the Fountain of Fair Fortune<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p><a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> collapses from exhaustion and is near death. She is in such pain that she cannot make it to the Fountain, and she begs her three friends not to move her. <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a> quickly mixes a powerful <a href="/wiki/Potion" title="Potion">potion</a> in an attempt to revive her, and the concoction actually cures her malady, so she no longer needs the Fountain's waters, neither did <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a> as she could now make gold out of the cure. The third <a href="/wiki/Wizardkind" title="Wizardkind">witch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> realises that once she washed away her regret for her lover, she was able to see him for what he really was ("cruel and faithless"), and she no longer needs the Fountain. She turns to Sir Luckless and offers him his turn at the Fountain as a reward for his bravery. The knight, amazed at his luck, bathes in the Fountain and flings himself "in his rusted armour" at the feet of <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> and begs for her "hand and her heart." Each witch achieves their dreams for a cure, a hapless knight wins knowledge of his bravery, and Amata, the one witch who had faith in him, realises that she has found a "man worthy of her."<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asha_and_Altheda">Asha and Altheda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?veaction=edit&amp;section=10" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Asha and Altheda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Asha and Altheda"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i><a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a> were angry with <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a>, who had accidentally brought along the knight</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Altheda" title="Altheda">Altheda</a><sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>When <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> accidentally brought Sir Luckless with them, they were first upset that <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a> inadvertently invited another competitor.
</p><p>Though he attempted to succeed in all three tasks, his efforts accomplished nothing until he assisted Altheda in creating a powerful potion for Asha by giving her his water flask.
</p><p>The witches allow him to bathe in the fountain because they no longer needed it. The four set off "arm-in-arm" we then learn that the four friends live long, never realising that the Fountain's waters "carried no enchantment at all."<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_Appearance">Physical Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?veaction=edit&amp;section=11" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Physical Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Physical Appearance"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>... a dismal-looking knight who was seated on a bone-thin horse</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Sir Luckless's description<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>He is described as an dismal-looking knight whom had an bone-thin horse. He also is known to have rusted armour.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_traits">Personality and traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?veaction=edit&amp;section=12" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Personality and traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Sir_Luckless?action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personality and traits"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span title="Source:&#160;The Tales of Beedle the Bard">"<i>...astonished that he was chosen one of hundreds and was giddy with his incredible luck</i>"</span></dd>
<dd>&#8212;Sir Luckless<sup class="noprint"><a href="/wiki/The_Tales_of_Beedle_the_Bard" title="The Tales of Beedle the Bard">[src]</a></sup></dd>
<dd></dd></dl></div><p>At first, he was a man who was not brave or skilful, and he tried to withdraw himself from the journey to the fountain. He proved to be brave and determined later during the story, however, as he tried to fight an worm, give up his only coin, and tried to float across dangerous waters to get himself and the three witches to the fountain. In the end, he was quite helpful as he helped make a potion to revive <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> and let the witches decide among themselves who should bathe in the fountain. He became giddy and astonished as he was one of a hundred got to bathe in the fountain. He also begged for <a href="/wiki/Amata" title="Amata">Amata</a>'s hand and heart, as he had fallen in <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> with her.<sup id="cite_ref-TBB_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
