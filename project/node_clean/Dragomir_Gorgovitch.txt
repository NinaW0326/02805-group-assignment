<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dragomir_Gorgovitch?veaction=edit&amp;section=1" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dragomir_Gorgovitch?action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gorgovitch transferred to the Cannons in <a href="/wiki/1995" title="1995">1995</a> for a "<i>record fee,</i>" and held the record for the most <a href="/wiki/Quaffle" title="Quaffle">Quaffle</a> drops in a season. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Weasley" title="Ronald Weasley">Ron Weasley</a> tried not to think about him if he could, probably because his favourite team was the Cannons.<sup id="cite_ref-DH7_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/1997" title="1997">1997</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> had a vision about <a href="/wiki/Wandmaker" title="Wandmaker">wandmaker</a> <a href="/wiki/Mykew_Gregorovitch" title="Mykew Gregorovitch">Gregorovitch</a>, Ron wondered if he was referring to Gorgovitch instead; as, at the time, Harry didn't know who Gregorovitch was, the only hint he had to go on was that he may have been somehow connected to <a href="/wiki/Quidditch" title="Quidditch">Quidditch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DH7_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When selecting an alias for himself, during the <a href="/wiki/Break-in_of_Gringotts_Wizarding_Bank_(1998)" title="Break-in of Gringotts Wizarding Bank (1998)">Break-in of Gringotts Wizarding Bank (1998)</a>, Ron named himself Dragomir Despard, most likely an allusion to Dragomir Gorgovitch.<sup id="cite_ref-DH26_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DH26-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/wiki/Dragomir_Gorgovitch?veaction=edit&amp;section=2" class="mw-editsection-visualeditor" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-divider"> | </span><a href="/wiki/Dragomir_Gorgovitch?action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology"><svg class="wds-icon wds-icon-tiny section-edit-pencil-icon"><use xlink:href="#wds-icons-pencil-tiny"></use></svg>edit source</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Dragomir" means "precious and peaceful" from the Slavic element <i>dorgu</i> meaning "precious" combined with <i>mir</i> "peace".
</p>
