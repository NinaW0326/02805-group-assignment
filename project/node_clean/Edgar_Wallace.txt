<b>Richard Horatio Edgar Wallace</b> (<a href="/wiki/1_April" title="1 April">1 April</a>, <a href="/wiki/1875" class="mw-redirect" title="1875">1875</a>—<a href="/wiki/10_February" title="10 February">10 February</a>, <a href="/wiki/1932" title="1932">1932</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Non-magic_people" title="Non-magic people">Muggle</a>&#160;<a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a>&#160;crime writer, journalist, novelist, screenwriter, and playwright, who wrote 175 novels, 24 plays, and numerous articles in newspapers and journals.
</p><p>Edgar Wallace was also the author of <i>The Flying Squad</i>. <a href="/wiki/Albus_Dumbledore" title="Albus Dumbledore">Albus Dumbledore</a> kept a copy of the Swedish translation of this book (<i><a href="/wiki/Kokain_N%C3%A4stet" title="Kokain Nästet">Kokain Nästet</a></i>) in his <a href="/wiki/Headmaster%27s_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster&#39;s office">office</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts_Castle" title="Hogwarts Castle">Hogwarts Castle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chicagotribune_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicagotribune-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
